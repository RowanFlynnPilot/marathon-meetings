## Meeting Overview
The Wausau Plan Commission approved two key items: a conditional use permit for a 70-unit, 7-story apartment building at 731 North First Street for Beacon Resources LLC, and a transportation project plat for Grand Avenue signal replacements. A public hearing was held regarding a storage facility proposal at 218 South Fourth Street, with the applicants noting downtown's need for storage to serve new apartment residents.

## Key Discussions
### Minutes for February 18th
Motion to approve made by Bugamman, seconded by Balkan. Passed unanimously with voice vote.

### Public hearing on conditional use permit for 218 South Fourth Street (personal storage facility)
Jason Dunwy and Melinda Dunwy spoke in favor of the storage facility proposal. They argued that with over 400 new apartment units approved downtown, including the 153-unit Foundry on Third and 102-unit Evergreen Landing project, there is a need for convenient storage options to keep residents' spending within Wausau rather than sending them to surrounding areas. The public hearing was closed but no vote was taken on this item during this meeting.

### Conditional use permit for 731 North First Street (70-unit, 7-story apartment building)
Motion to approve made by Bornman, seconded by Bugamin. No questions or discussion from commissioners. Passed unanimously by voice vote, authorizing Beacon Resources LLC to construct the apartment building.

### Transportation project plat for Grand Avenue signal replacements at Sturgeon and Townline Road
Motion to approve made by Bugamin, seconded by Balkan. No discussion. Passed unanimously by voice vote.

### Next meeting date
Staff indicated the next meeting would normally be April 21st (third Tuesday), but noted it may need to be moved due to the election and council meeting schedule. Commissioners will be notified of any changes.

## Public Comment
One written comment was submitted via email by Linda Lawrence on March 12th supporting the Beacon Resources apartment proposal, stating housing of this capacity will be good for downtown small businesses and expressing confidence in the developer's track record. In-person public comment during the storage facility hearing was provided by Jason Dunwy and Melinda Dunwy, who spoke in favor of allowing storage facilities downtown to serve the growing apartment population.

## Action Items
- Conditional use permit approved for 731 North First Street 70-unit apartment building (Beacon Resources LLC)
- Transportation project plat approved for Grand Avenue signal replacements at Sturgeon and Townline Road
- Vice chair election postponed to April meeting
- Next meeting tentatively scheduled for April 21st at 5:00 PM, subject to change due to election/council schedule
