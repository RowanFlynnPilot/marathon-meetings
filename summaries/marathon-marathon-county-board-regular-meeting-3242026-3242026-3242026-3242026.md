## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Board of Supervisors adopted the Marathon County Comprehensive Plan 2026 after approving nine amendments addressing topics including renewable energy terminology, data centers, radon/lead remediation, AI technology, and energy policy. The board also approved salaries for elected officials, authorized phase 2 design for a new highway facility, approved engagement of outside counsel for PFAS litigation, and ratified a local state of emergency declaration for the recent blizzard.

## Key Discussions
### Marathon County Comprehensive Plan 2026
Administrator Leonard presented 10 proposed amendments compiled from supervisor feedback. The board voted on amendments individually after Supervisor Crawl successfully moved to separate amendments 2, 3, and 4. Amendment 1 (livability standards) passed unanimously. Amendments 2, 3, and 4 (alternative energy systems terminology changes proposed by Vice Chair Dickinson) each passed but not unanimously, with Supervisor Crawl voting no. Amendment 5 (data centers and battery storage background language) passed but not unanimously, with Supervisor Leur voting no citing ideological concerns. Amendment 6 (radon and lead remediation reference) passed unanimously. Amendment 7 (regulate energy projects when allowed by law) passed but not unanimously. Amendment 8 (AI and automation language proposed by Supervisor Leur) passed unanimously. Amendment 9 was significantly debated - Supervisor Sindellski's original language promoting clean coal, natural gas, and nuclear was amended by Supervisor Boots to read 'promote coal and natural gas until a long-term sustainable and reliable energy source for use in Marathon County can be found that does not adversely affect agricultural land.' Supervisors Robinson and Rosenberg opposed the amendment, with Rosenberg stating 'there is no such thing as clean coal' from his experience working at the Weston Power Plant. The amended version passed but not unanimously. A late amendment from Supervisor Sindellski regarding utility-scale wind, solar and battery facilities being considered industrial uses was debated extensively. Supervisor Jeang moved to refer it to committee, but after discussion about process requirements, both the referral motions and the amendment itself were defeated. The final comprehensive plan as amended passed but not unanimously.

### Salaries for Elected Department Heads (Resolution 12-26)
Resolution establishing salaries for clerk of courts, sheriff, and elected department heads for the upcoming term of office. Passed with no discussion.

### Highway Facility Phase 2 Design Services (Resolution 13-26)
Resolution authorizing staff to proceed with phase 2 design services for the new highway facility. Supervisor Soyber requested information on plans for the old facility, and Chair Gibbs noted that the HR Finance and Property Committee will address this in the future. Supervisor Sundowski asked about the $53 million cost estimate but was informed that cost approval was not part of this resolution. Passed unanimously.

### PFAS Litigation Outside Counsel (Resolution 14-26)
Resolution authorizing engagement of outside counsel on contingency basis for PFAS lawsuits. Two amendments were adopted: Supervisor Robinson's amendment directing the county administrator to evaluate past and present practices that may have resulted in PFAS exposure in county operations passed unanimously. Vice Chair Dickinson's amendment modifying language regarding airport property also passed unanimously. The resolution as amended passed unanimously.

### Ratification of Local State of Emergency (Resolution 22-26)
Administrator Leonard explained the ratification was needed to preserve opportunity for reimbursement after the governor's emergency declaration expired on March 16th during the blizzard. He praised county staff extensively, noting facilities and parks staff logged over 600 hours of additional time in 24 hours, highway workers worked 12-16 hour shifts sleeping on cots, and staff ensured dispatch and corrections shift changes could occur. Supervisor Fifer echoed thanks to staff. Passed unanimously.

### Administrator Performance Evaluation
Chair Gibbs explained the executive committee had finalized the administrator's evaluation using board input summarized at the previous meeting. Supervisor Robinson moved to accept the executive committee's recommendation on salary and performance evaluation without going into closed session. Passed unanimously.
