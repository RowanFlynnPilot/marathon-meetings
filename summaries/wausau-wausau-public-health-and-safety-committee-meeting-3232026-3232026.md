## Meeting Overview
The Wausau Public Health and Safety Committee approved a parklet permit for Westider Diner and Lounge after the owner presented detailed plans, held action on one bartender license denial pending police chief review, and approved a batch of licenses including several summer events and new business ownership transfers. The committee also passed ordinance updates regarding solid waste disposal and repealed the city's mobile phone driving ban as redundant with state law.

## Key Discussions
### Westider Diner and Lounge Parklet Permit
Owner Tyler Vote presented detailed mockups of a proposed parklet extending 4 feet into the street and 4 feet on the 11-foot sidewalk at 628 North Third Avenue. Vote explained the parklet would provide sun for breakfast customers and would be lit for visibility. Alder Larson initially expressed skepticism but changed position after seeing the layout. The committee approved the permit unanimously with a motion from Watson and second from Larson, with Vote asked to return in November to report on how the summer trial went.

### Theodore Davis Bartender License Denial
Davis appeared before the committee regarding his license denial recommendation. He acknowledged his record was accurate and explained he made a mistake 20 years ago as a minor that has followed him. His boyfriend Matthew Prieb spoke emotionally in support, noting Davis has not reoffended since. The committee held action pending review by Chief Barnes of rehabilitation evidence Davis submitted. Deputy Chief Baiton indicated he was unfamiliar with what materials the chief received.

### Joanna Gregory Bartender License Denial
Gregory did not appear for her scheduled denial hearing. The record noted she was not present.

### Batch License Approvals
The committee approved licenses as recommended including summer events (Wings over Wausau, Concerts on the Square, Chalkfest, Memorial Day parade, Hope on the Block, Jazz on the River), three establishments reviewed by the liquor license subcommittee (Oasis Arcade, Whiskey River Bar and Grill rebrand, new Hayawa ownership - all unanimously recommended), and Class A retailers. Motion from Larson, second from Watson, passed unanimously.

### Municipal Code Chapter 6.44 Solid Waste Disposal
Assistant City Attorney Vinnie Bonino presented the repeal and recreation of the solid waste ordinance to comply with evolved state-level changes. Motion from Larson, second from Watson, passed unanimously with no discussion.

### Handheld Mobile Phone Ordinance Repeal
Attorney Bonino explained the city's cell phone ban is now redundant as state inattentive driving statutes have been amended to regulate cell phone usage. The local ordinance filled a gap when enacted but is now obsolete. Motion from Watson, second from Larson, passed unanimously.

### MREA Solar Partnership
Carrie from the planning department presented the memorandum of understanding with Midwest Renewable Energy for a group solar purchasing program. The sustainability committee voted unanimously on March 5th to move it forward. Alder Sarah noted her positive personal experience with solar installation assistance. Motion from Watson, second from Larson, passed unanimously.

### Fire Department 2025 Annual Report
Fire Chief reported the department achieved ISO Class 2 status as of Friday, maintaining that rating for the next four years. The department set a new record with over 7,200 calls averaging 20 per day. Three upcoming listening sessions were announced: March 31st at 1pm at the tech, April 1st at 5pm at Station 2, and April 3rd at Station 1, related to the April 7th referendum.

### Shelter Transition to Bridge Street Mission
Tracy Durante reported 415 unduplicated guests served since opening, with over 740 volunteer hours in February. James Torensson, new director of homeless services at Bridge Street Mission, announced the transition from WMC shelter is expected around late April, pending contractor confirmation on April 1st. The WMC contract with First United Methodist Church was extended through April 19th to ensure no service gap. Catholic Charities reported 99 unduplicated guests since November 1st.

## Public Comment
Carrie Mor Everest of 1025 Everest Boulevard spoke during a delayed public comment period at the end of the meeting. She expressed concerns about treatment of unhoused individuals during emergency responses at the shelter, stating she has witnessed multiple incidents over 10 months where 911 responders did not treat guests ethically or professionally. She stated complaints she filed have not been addressed and criticized what she perceived as differential treatment compared to housed residents. The chair noted formal complaint processes exist through the Police and Fire Commission.
