## Meeting Overview
The Parks Committee approved Rettler Corporation to develop a master plan for Mock Mueller Park, discussed increasing park impact fees to align with neighboring communities, and reviewed the successful Yellow Banks kayak launch project which came in significantly under budget due to grant funding. The committee also discussed future plans for the Kennedy Park ice rink.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of minutes from February 23rd, 2026
A motion to accept the minutes was made and seconded. The motion carried with all in favor, no opposition.

### Review of parks and recreation impact fee discussion
Jennifer presented information on park impact fees, noting the village currently charges $300 for single family homes while neighboring communities charge $600-$650. A 2020 study allowed fees up to $761 but the village only raised them to $300 in 2022. Committee members expressed support for a moderate increase to align with neighbors like Kronenwetter ($603) and Rib Mountain ($650). Katrina stated she supported the moderate increase bracket, and Roger agreed fees should be consistent with neighboring communities. The matter will go to Plan Commission for final decision.

### Request for proposals for Mock Mueller Park master plan
Staff received seven proposals for the park master plan, reviewed by four staff members. The two lowest bids were JSD and Rettler Corporation, both firms with prior village experience. Roger made a motion to select Rettler Corporation, seconded by Katrina. The motion carried unanimously.

### Review of Yellow Banks kayak launch expenses
Jessica presented a comprehensive breakdown of the kayak launch project expenses and grant funding. The project received grants from DNR and Marathon County Transportation, with the county covering full expense for the ADA accessible feature. Lisa Beck praised the RFC document during public comment. Committee members commended Jessica and Dan Higginbotham for their grant writing work. Katrina suggested formal recognition for Dan Higginbotham who donated site planning work. No formal action was taken; this was informational only.

### Discussion on ice rink operations at Kennedy Park
Staff presented information on the Kennedy Park ice rink operations as requested by Katrina. The warming house has been unattended since 2020 due to COVID and subsequent staffing shortages. Sean noted Everest Youth Hockey remains interested in improvements including a potential roofed structure to extend ice season. Katrina expressed concern about not losing sight of hockey amid Kennedy Park baseball focus. Staff will bring back historical attendance numbers from 2018-19 seasons and additional user feedback for future discussion.

## Public Comment
Jim Pencil expressed frustration about not receiving responses to his previous questions regarding playground equipment installation, Kennedy Park fundraising updates, and ice rink costs. He argued the true cost of the ice rink is $20,000-$30,000 when factoring in staff hours, not the stated $1,320. Lisa Beck (1808 Cortez Lane) praised Michael for snow management during the recent blizzard and commended Sean and Jessica for the well-written Yellow Banks RFC. A written response to Jim Pencil's previous email was submitted and will be included in the minutes.
