## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Parks Committee discussed park impact fees, selected Rettler Corporation as the consultant for Mead Park master planning, and reviewed Yellow Banks kayak launch expenses which came in significantly under budget due to successful grant funding. The committee also discussed ice rink operations at Kennedy Park and potential future hockey facility improvements.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of minutes from February 23rd, 2026
A motion to accept the minutes was made and seconded. The motion carried with no opposition.

### Parks and recreation impact fee discussion
Jennifer presented information on park impact fees, noting the village currently charges $300 for single family homes while neighboring communities charge $600-$900. The 2020 study recommended fees of $761 for single family but only modest increases were adopted. Committee members expressed support for a moderate increase to be more in line with neighboring communities while remaining competitive. Katrina stated she would support the moderate increase bracket. The matter will go to Plan Commission for final decision.

### RFP for Mead Park master plan
Staff received seven proposals for the Mead Park master planning project. Four staff members reviewed and rated proposals based on firm experience, personnel experience, similar projects, scope understanding, and cost. JSD and Rettler Corporation were the two lowest bids and most qualified based on reviews. Roger made a motion to select Rettler Corporation, seconded by Katrina. The motion carried unanimously.

### Yellow Banks kayak launch expenses
Jessica presented a detailed breakdown of the kayak launch project expenses and grant funding. The project included some unforeseen costs including subgrade issues and an unknown well casing. Multiple grants were secured by Jessica and Dan Hagenbotham, with Marathon County Transportation covering the full expense for ADA accessibility components. Katrina praised the RFC as 'awesome' and 'super well written,' commending the transparency and successful grant work that significantly reduced out-of-pocket costs for the village.

### Ice rink operations at Kennedy Park
Staff presented information on ice rink operations as requested by committee member Katrina. The warming house has been unattended since 2020 due to COVID and subsequent staffing challenges. Everest Youth Hockey has expressed interest in improvements including a covered structure to extend ice season. Staff noted ongoing discussions about Marathon Park's hockey facilities may increase need for ice facilities. Katrina stated she wants to ensure hockey users are not forgotten amid baseball-focused Kennedy Park discussions. Staff will bring back historical attendance numbers from 2018-19 seasons at the next meeting.

## Public Comment
Jim Pencil (Street not specified) expressed frustration about receiving no response to his three pages of comments and questions submitted prior to the February meeting. He raised concerns about playground equipment installed negligently in 2024, lack of Kennedy Park fundraising updates, hockey facilities being forgotten, and criticized the ice rink cost analysis as incomplete, arguing true costs are closer to $20,000-$30,000 when staff hours are included rather than the stated $1,320.98. Lisa Beck (1808 Cortez Lane) thanked Michael for snow removal during the blizzard and praised Sean and Jessica for the well-written Yellow Banks kayak launch RFC. A written response to Jim Pencil's previous email was submitted and will be included in the minutes.
