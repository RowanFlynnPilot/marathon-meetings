## Meeting Overview
The Wausau Finance Committee approved multiple airport ground leases, denied a tax recovery claim related to Greenwood Hills litigation, and postponed decisions on opioid settlement participation and lead service line replacement funding. The committee also approved 2025-2026 budget carryover funds and reviewed 2025 financial results showing a general fund surplus of approximately $540,000 after transfers.

## Key Discussions
### Minutes of March 10, 2026
Watson moved to approve, Griner seconded. Motion passed unanimously with no discussion.

### Tax recovery claim for Green Acres at Greenwood Hills LLC
This claim is part of ongoing litigation with Greenwood Hills. The chair explained that a motion to approve required a 'no' vote to deny the claim. Watson moved to approve, Griner seconded. The motion failed when members voted 'no' to deny the claim.

### Airport ground lease transfers at 939 Woods Place
Three related items for transferring a hangar from Wino Jones to Owen Jones were approved. Watson moved to approve consent to transfer title, Griner seconded, passed unanimously. Tierney moved to terminate the lease with Wino Jones, Watson seconded, passed unanimously. Watson moved to approve the new lease with Owen Jones, Tierney seconded, passed unanimously.

### Airport ground lease with Cole Lundberg
Griner moved to approve, Watson seconded. Motion passed unanimously with no discussion.

### National opioid settlement agreement
Committee members expressed discomfort proceeding without more information. Alder Mallini asked where the proposal came from, and Assistant City Attorney Vincent explained law firms send notices to potential class members. Alder Tierney noted signing would waive future individual action against the six companies. The deadline to participate is May 4th. Griner moved to postpone to the next meeting, Tierney seconded. Motion to postpone passed unanimously.

### Budget amendment for lead service line replacement
Eric from Public Works explained the DNR changed terms after initial agreement, resulting in $709,672 in non-construction costs being ineligible for the subsidized 0.25% loan. Finance Director Marian outlined options including borrowing, using general fund reserves, or potentially using PFAS settlement money. Alder Tierney opposed adding more debt. Discussion included possibly splitting the $283,868 homeowner portion from general fund and $425,803 utility portion from PFAS settlement, though the committee noted PFAS spending requires broader discussion. Watson moved to postpone to the next meeting, Griner seconded. Motion passed unanimously.

### Budget amendment for carryover funds 2025 to 2026
The Finance Director noted the large carryover includes $10 million for transit buses funded by VW mitigation grants, plus airport projects awaiting state draw requests. Unstarted projects include city hall chimney liner, public safety roof, and DPW fence replacement. Watson moved to approve, Griner seconded. Motion passed unanimously.

### 2025 motorpool fund financial results
The Finance Director reported the motorpool fund would show approximately $150,000 net profit after transferring GMT money. The fund faces a $177,000 cash flow shortfall due to outstanding vehicle purchases from as far back as 2023 due to supply chain issues. Solomon from Motorpool explained two dump trucks are near the front of the line for delivery and a med unit was returned to AEV for issues. The shortfall may be covered by remaining ARPA savings. This was informational with no action required.

### 2025 general fund financial results
The Finance Director reported a surplus of approximately $1.2 million driven by strong building permits, GMT revenue, and investment income. After motorpool charges, departments including Public Works ($481,000 over), Fire ($527,000 over), and Police ($468,000 over) exceeded budgets primarily due to motorpool allocations. CCITC was over by $194,000 due to subscription miscommunications and Office 365 upgrade costs. After proposed transfers to recycling, airport, and parking funds, the surplus would be $539,000. Tierney moved to approve the transfers, Watson seconded. Motion passed unanimously.

### 2026 general obligation promissory note
The Finance Director presented the borrowing calendar for capital improvements including street projects (10-year), motorpool (5-year), and various TID projects. The debt utilization percentage will decrease slightly even with new issuance. Watson moved to approve the calendar, the chair seconded. Motion passed unanimously. Phil Cawson from Ehlers will present parameters resolution at the next meeting.

### Consideration of purchasing properties for DPW
Properties at 108 Adolf Street, 112 Adolf Street, 112.5 Adolf Street, and 233 Myron Street were scheduled for closed session discussion. Due to council starting at 6:30 and time constraints, Watson moved to postpone to the next meeting, Tierney seconded. Motion passed unanimously.

## Public Comment
No public comment was offered.
