## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Board of Supervisors held an adjourned organizational meeting where they approved committee appointments, elected Supervisors Hapa and Arstead to the Executive Committee, and unanimously approved a resolution reducing landfill tipping fees for disaster debris following the recent severe weather that impacted Ringle and other communities. Public speakers requested the county waive fees entirely for affected residents.

## Key Discussions
### Committee Appointments
Chair Gibbs delivered remarks emphasizing that 34% of the board is in its first or second term and explaining goals for committee assignments including increasing diversity of perspectives and mentoring opportunities. Vice chairs were tasked with shepherding the strategic plan. Motion by Supervisor Cavelli, seconded by Supervisor Crims. The motion carried but was not unanimous.

### Election of At-Large Executive Committee Members
Three supervisors were nominated: Supervisor Hapa (nominated by Supervisor Mask), Supervisor Leur (nominated by Supervisor Rosenberg, who praised her advocacy for 'smallest children and oldest elders'), and Supervisor Arstead (nominated by Vice Chair Boots). Paper ballots were distributed and counted. Supervisors Hapa and Arstead were elected to the Executive Committee.

### Consent Agenda
Items C10 through C14A were approved on consent. Motion by Supervisor Cavelli, seconded by Supervisor Drebeck. The motion carried unanimously.

### Resolution 24-26: Out of Home Placement Reserve Increase
Motion by Supervisor Morash, seconded by Supervisor Robinson. No discussion. The motion carried but was not unanimous.

### Resolution 25-26: Social Services Budget Amendment
Motion by Supervisor P, seconded by Supervisor Robinson. No discussion. The motion carried but was not unanimous.

### Resolution 26-26: Landfill Tipping Fee Modification
Administrator Leonard and Director Hagen Busher explained the proposed fee reduction for disaster debris from the April severe weather. The standard $65/ton rate would be reduced to approximately $36.94 for certified disaster debris, reflecting waivers from the state ($13.10) and Town of Ringle ($2.33), plus a county reduction to commercial rates. Leonard noted the county will not meet the $670,743 threshold for state disaster aid but expects Ringle to qualify. Public speakers Jessica Toasty and Matt Haybeck requested the county waive fees entirely and extend relief beyond 60 days for the approximately 44 affected homes. Supervisor Rosenberg supported the work but requested continued discussion. The board suspended rules unanimously to consider the resolution, then approved Resolution 26-26 unanimously.

## Public Comment
Special dispensation was granted for two speakers on the landfill tipping fee item. Jessica Toasty stated that the state, DNR, and Ringle are waiving fees and asked Marathon County to do the same, noting residents are self-reliant and private businesses have donated $40,000+ in cleanup efforts. Matt Haybeck spoke on behalf of approximately 44 homeowners facing demolition, noting many insurance adjusters have not yet arrived and requesting extended fee relief beyond 60 days.

## Action Items
- Committee appointments approved as presented by Chair Gibbs and Vice Chair Boots
- Supervisors Hapa and Arstead elected to Executive Committee at-large positions
- Resolution 24-26 approved increasing out of home placement reserve
- Resolution 25-26 approved amending social services budget
- Resolution 26-26 approved reducing landfill tipping fees for disaster debris through June 17, 2025
- Administrator Leonard to continue discussions with Community Foundation on potential reimbursement mechanism for uninsured costs
- Solid Waste Management Board to develop long-term policy on handling similar disaster situations within six months
- Board to revisit tipping fee relief at May meeting if extension beyond state declaration period is needed
