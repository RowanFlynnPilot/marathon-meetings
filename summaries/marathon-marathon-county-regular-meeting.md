## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Board of Supervisors held an adjourned organizational meeting where they approved committee appointments, elected Supervisors Hapa and Arstead to the Executive Committee at-large positions, and unanimously approved a resolution to reduce landfill tipping fees for storm debris following the recent tornado and flooding disaster. The board heard emotional public testimony from affected Ringle residents requesting fee relief.

## Key Discussions
### Committee appointments
Chair Gibbs outlined goals for committee appointments including increasing diversity of perspective, providing leadership development opportunities for newer supervisors (34% of the board is in first or second term), and honoring supervisor preferences. Vice Chair Boots assisted in developing the appointments. The motion by Supervisor Cavelli, seconded by Supervisor Crims, was carried but not unanimously.

### Election of at-large Executive Committee members
Three supervisors were nominated: Supervisor Hapa (nominated by Supervisor Mask), Supervisor Leur (nominated by Supervisor Rosenberg), and Supervisor Arstead (nominated by Vice Chair Boots). Supervisor Rosenberg explained her nomination of Supervisor Leur, citing her advocacy for 'our county's smallest children and our oldest elders.' Paper ballots were distributed for the vote, and Supervisors Hapa and Arstead were elected to the Executive Committee.

### Resolution 26-26: Landfill tipping fees modification for storm debris
Administrator Leonard and Solid Waste Director Hagen Busher presented a proposal to reduce tipping fees for debris from the recent tornado and flooding disaster. The state waived its $13.10 fee, Ringle Township waived its $2.33 fee, and the county proposed reducing its rate to $36.94 per ton (covering costs only). Jessica Toasty and Matt Haybeck from the affected Ringle community spoke, requesting the county fully waive fees and extend the timeline to six months. Multiple supervisors asked questions about implementation, certification processes, and potential costs. The board unanimously approved suspending rules to bring the resolution forward, then unanimously passed Resolution 26-26.

## Public Comment
Two residents were granted special dispensation to speak on the tipping fee resolution. Jessica Toasty argued Marathon County should waive fees entirely like other entities have done, noting private businesses have donated up to $40,000 in cleanup costs and residents are facing deductibles and uninsured losses. She requested a six-month fee waiver. Matt Haybeck spoke on behalf of homeowners whose houses must be demolished, requesting fee waivers for underinsured residents and noting some insurance adjusters haven't yet visited the approximately 44 affected homes.

## Action Items
- Committee appointments approved for the term
- Supervisors Hapa and Arstead elected to Executive Committee at-large positions
- Resolution 24-26 approved for out of home placement reserve increase
- Resolution 25-26 approved amending social services budget
- Resolution 26-26 approved reducing landfill tipping fees to $36.94/ton for certified storm debris through June 17th
- Administrator to continue discussions with Community Foundation about reimbursement mechanism for unreimbursed insurance costs
- Solid Waste Management Board directed to develop a formal policy within six months for handling similar disaster situations in the future
