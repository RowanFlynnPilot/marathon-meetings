## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Board of Supervisors held an adjourned organizational meeting where they approved committee appointments, elected Supervisors Hapa and Arstead to the Executive Committee at-large positions, and unanimously approved a resolution to reduce landfill tipping fees for disaster-related debris following severe weather that impacted Ringle and other communities. The board also approved consent agenda items and budget resolutions related to out-of-home placement and social services.

## Key Discussions
### Committee appointments
Chair Gibbs delivered remarks thanking Vice Chair Boots for assistance in developing committee appointments, emphasizing goals of increasing diversity of perspective, developing new supervisors as leaders, and respecting supervisor preferences. He noted over 34% of the board is in its first or second term. The motion to approve committee appointments by Supervisor Cavelli, seconded by Supervisor Crims, was carried but not unanimous.

### Election of at-large Executive Committee members
Three supervisors were nominated for two at-large Executive Committee positions: Supervisor Hapa (nominated by Supervisor Mask), Supervisor Leur (nominated by Supervisor Rosenberg), and Supervisor Arstead (nominated by Vice Chair Boots). Supervisor Rosenberg spoke in support of Supervisor Leur, noting she advocates for the county's smallest children and oldest elders. Paper ballots were used for voting. Supervisors Hapa and Arstead were elected to the Executive Committee.

### Resolution 26-26: Modification to landfill tipping fees
County Administrator Leonard and Solid Waste Director Hagen Busher presented a proposal from the Solid Waste Management Board to reduce tipping fees for disaster-related debris following severe weather that impacted Ringle and other communities. The state is waiving its $13.10 fee, Ringle is waiving its $2.33 fee, and the county proposed reducing fees to approximately $36.94 per ton (commercial rate minus state and local fees) to cover operational costs. Two residents, Jessica Toasty and Matt Haybeck, were granted special dispensation to speak, requesting the county wave fees entirely for affected residents. Administrator Leonard mentioned exploring a partnership with the community foundation to help reimburse unreimbursed costs. The board voted unanimously to suspend rules to consider the resolution, then approved Resolution 26-26 unanimously.

### Consent agenda
Items C10 through C14A were approved unanimously on motion by Supervisor Cavelli, seconded by Supervisor Drebeck.

### Resolution 24-26: Out-of-home placement reserve increase
Motion by Supervisor Morash, seconded by Supervisor Robinson. The motion carried but was not unanimous.

### Resolution 25-26: Social services budget amendment
Motion by Supervisor P, seconded by Supervisor Robinson. The motion carried but was not unanimous.

## Public Comment
Two residents were granted special dispensation to speak on the landfill tipping fee resolution. Jessica Toasty requested the county wave fees entirely for affected residents, noting private businesses have donated significant resources (one up to $40,000) and requesting a six-month waiver period. Matt Haybeck spoke on behalf of homeowners whose houses must be demolished, noting some residents are still waiting for insurance adjusters and requesting extended fee relief.

## Action Items
- Committee appointments approved as presented by Chair Gibbs and Vice Chair Boots
- Supervisors Hapa and Arstead elected to Executive Committee at-large positions
- Resolution 26-26 approved to reduce landfill tipping fees for disaster-related debris through June 17th
- Administrator to work with community foundation on mechanism to reimburse unreimbursed debris disposal costs
- Solid Waste Management Board to develop clear policy on handling similar disaster situations within six months
- Board to revisit tipping fee relief at May meeting if extension beyond state declaration period is needed
