## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Board held an adjourned organizational meeting where they approved committee appointments, elected Supervisors Hapa and Arstead to the executive committee at-large positions, and unanimously approved a resolution reducing landfill tipping fees for disaster-related debris following severe tornado and flood damage in the Ringle area. The board suspended its rules to bring the tipping fee resolution directly from the Solid Waste Management Board without going through a standing committee.

## Key Discussions
### Committee Appointments
Chair Gibbs outlined the guiding principles for committee appointments, including increasing diversity of perspectives, providing leadership development opportunities for newer supervisors (34% in first or second term), and honoring supervisor preferences. Vice Chair Boots assisted in developing the appointments. Motion by Supervisor Cavelli, seconded by Supervisor Crims. The motion carried but was not unanimous.

### Election of At-Large Executive Committee Members
Three supervisors were nominated: Supervisor Hapa (nominated by Supervisor Mask), Supervisor Leur (nominated by Supervisor Rosenberg), and Supervisor Arstead (nominated by Supervisor Boots). Supervisor Rosenberg spoke in support of Supervisor Leur, citing her advocacy for the county's youngest and oldest residents. Paper ballots were distributed, and Supervisors Hapa and Arstead were elected to the two at-large positions on the executive committee.

### Consent Agenda
Items C10 through C14A were approved on consent. Motion by Supervisor Cavelli, seconded by Supervisor Drebeck. The motion carried unanimously.

### Out of Home Placement Reserve Increase (Resolution 24-26)
Resolution from Health and Human Services Committee and Human Resources and Finance and Property Committee. Motion by Supervisor Morash, seconded by Supervisor Robinson. The motion carried but was not unanimous.

### Social Services Budget Amendment (Resolution 25-26)
Motion by Supervisor P, seconded by Supervisor Robinson. The motion carried but was not unanimous.

### Landfill Tipping Fees Modification (Resolution 26-26)
Administrator Leonard and Director Hagen Busher presented the resolution from the Solid Waste Management Board in response to severe weather damage in the Ringle area. The state waived $13.10 per ton, Ringle waived $2.33 per ton, and the county proposed reducing fees to $36.94 per ton (covering costs only). Two residents, Jessica Toasty and Matt Haybeck, were granted special permission to speak, requesting the county wave fees entirely for affected residents. Supervisors discussed insurance reimbursement challenges, timing concerns, and potential partnership with the community foundation for unreimbursed costs. The board first voted unanimously to suspend rules to bring the resolution directly from the Solid Waste Management Board, then approved Resolution 26-26 unanimously with motion by Supervisor Arstead and second by Supervisor Morash.

## Public Comment
Two residents were granted special dispensation to speak on the tipping fee resolution. Jessica Toasty asked Marathon County to wave fees entirely, noting the state, DNR, and Ringle were waiving their portions, and that businesses had donated up to $40,000 in cleanup efforts. She requested a six-month fee waiver. Matt Haybeck spoke on behalf of homeowners facing demolition, noting some of the approximately 44 affected homes are still waiting for insurance adjusters, and requested more time and relief from the county.

## Action Items
- Committee appointments approved for the term
- Supervisors Hapa and Arstead elected to executive committee at-large positions
- Resolution 26-26 approved reducing landfill tipping fees for disaster-related debris through June 17, 2025
- Administrator to work with Community Foundation on potential reimbursement mechanism for unreimbursed insurance costs
- Solid Waste Management Board to develop clear policy on handling similar disaster situations within six months
- Board to revisit tipping fee reduction at May meeting if extension is needed beyond state declaration period
