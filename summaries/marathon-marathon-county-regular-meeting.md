## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Board of Supervisors held an adjourned organizational meeting where they approved committee appointments, elected two at-large executive committee members (Supervisors Hapa and Arstead), and unanimously approved a resolution to reduce landfill tipping fees for disaster-related debris following the April tornado and flooding that impacted Ringle and other communities. The board suspended its rules to allow the emergency tipping fee resolution to come directly from the Solid Waste Management Board.

## Key Discussions
### Committee appointments
Chair Gibbs presented committee appointments developed with Vice Chair Boots, emphasizing goals of increasing diverse perspectives, developing new supervisor leadership, and respecting supervisor preferences. He noted 34% of the board is in its first or second term. The motion by Supervisor Cavelli, seconded by Supervisor Crims, carried but was not unanimous. Supervisor Nisa noted the system marked him absent but didn't allow him to vote.

### Election of at-large executive committee members
Three supervisors were nominated: Supervisor Hapa (nominated by Supervisor Mask), Supervisor Leur (nominated by Supervisor Rosenberg), and Supervisor Arstead (nominated by Vice Chair Boots). Supervisor Rosenberg explained she nominated Supervisor Leur because she has advocated for the county's smallest children and oldest elders. Paper ballots were distributed and supervisors selected two names. Supervisors Hapa and Arstead were elected to the executive committee.

### Consent agenda
Items C10 through C14A were approved unanimously on motion by Supervisor Cavelli, seconded by Supervisor Drebeck.

### Out-of-home placement reserve increase (Resolution 24-26)
Motion by Supervisor Morash, seconded by Supervisor Robinson. The resolution carried but was not unanimous.

### Social services budget amendment (Resolution 25-26)
Motion by Supervisor P, seconded by Supervisor Robinson. The resolution carried but was not unanimous.

### Landfill tipping fee modification (Resolution 26-26)
Administrator Leonard and Solid Waste Director Hagen Busher presented the emergency resolution responding to tornado and flood damage affecting Ringle and other communities. The proposal reduces tipping fees from $65/ton standard rate to approximately $36.94/ton for certified disaster debris by waiving the state fee ($13.10), town of Ringle fee ($2.33), and reducing the county portion. Supervisor Rosenberg requested special dispensation for public comment, and residents Jessica Toasty and Matt Haybeck spoke, asking the county to waive fees entirely for affected residents. Leonard noted he's in preliminary discussions with the Community Foundation about reimbursing uninsured out-of-pocket costs. After extensive discussion about fee structures, insurance reimbursement, and future policy needs, the board unanimously voted to suspend rules and then unanimously approved the resolution.

## Public Comment
Two residents were granted special dispensation to speak on the tipping fee resolution. Jessica Toasty asked the county to waive fees entirely, noting the state, DNR, and town of Ringle are waiving their fees, and emphasized community self-reliance in cleanup efforts with businesses donating up to $40,000 in hauling expenses. She requested a six-month waiver period. Matt Haybeck spoke on behalf of residents whose homes must be demolished, noting some insurance adjusters haven't arrived yet and requesting more time and relief for underinsured residents.

## Action Items
- Committee appointments approved for the term
- Supervisors Hapa and Arstead elected to at-large executive committee positions
- Resolution 26-26 approved reducing landfill tipping fees for disaster-related debris through June 17, 2025
- Administrator Leonard to continue discussions with Community Foundation regarding reimbursement mechanism for uninsured disaster costs
- Solid Waste Management Board directed to develop clear policy on handling similar disaster situations within six months
- Staff to track all certified disaster debris and associated costs for potential state disaster aid applications
