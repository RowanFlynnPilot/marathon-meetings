## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Board held an organizational meeting where committee appointments were approved, two at-large executive committee members were elected (Supervisors Hapa and Arstead), and a resolution was unanimously passed to reduce landfill tipping fees for debris related to the state-declared tornado and flood emergency affecting Ringle and other areas. The board suspended its rules to consider the emergency fee reduction, which allows affected residents to pay $36.94 per ton instead of the standard $65 rate through June 17th.

## Key Discussions
### Committee appointments
Chair Gibbs outlined goals for committee appointments including increasing diversity of perspective and ensuring supervisor development opportunities. Vice Chair Boots assisted in developing the appointments. The motion was made by Supervisor Cavelli and seconded by Supervisor Crims. The motion carried but was not unanimous. Supervisor Rosenberg noted the system marked them absent without giving them an option to vote.

### Election of at-large executive committee members
Three supervisors were nominated: Supervisor Hapa (nominated by Supervisor Mask), Supervisor Leur (nominated by Supervisor Rosenberg), and Supervisor Arstead (nominated by Vice Chair Boots). Supervisor Rosenberg explained she nominated Supervisor Leur because she had observed her advocating for the county's youngest and oldest residents. Paper ballots were distributed and supervisors selected two names. Supervisors Hapa and Arstead were elected to the executive committee.

### Consent agenda
Items C10 through C14A were approved on consent. Motion by Supervisor Cavelli, seconded by Supervisor Drebeck. The motion carried unanimously.

### Resolution 24-26: Out of home placement reserve increase
Motion by Supervisor Morash, seconded by Supervisor Robinson. No discussion was offered. The motion carried but was not unanimous.

### Resolution 25-26: Social services budget amendment
Motion by Supervisor P, seconded by Supervisor Robinson. No discussion was offered. The motion carried but was not unanimous.

### Resolution 26-26: Landfill tipping fee modification
Administrator Leonard and Director Hagen Busher presented the proposal from the Solid Waste Management Board to reduce tipping fees for storm-related debris from the standard $65/ton to $36.94/ton for Ringle residents (or $39.27 for other areas). The state waived $13.10 and the town of Ringle waived $2.33. Leonard noted Marathon County likely won't meet the $670,743 threshold for state disaster aid but expects Ringle will qualify. He mentioned exploring partnership with the Community Foundation to help residents with unreimbursed costs. After public comment from Jessica Toasty and Matt Haybeck requesting the county wave all fees, and extensive board discussion about timing, FEMA implications, and tracking costs, the board unanimously suspended its rules to consider the resolution directly. The resolution was then approved unanimously with motion by Supervisor Arstead and second by Supervisor Morash.

## Public Comment
Two residents were granted special permission to speak on the landfill tipping fee resolution. Jessica Toasty requested Marathon County wave all fees, noting the state, DNR, and town of Ringle waived their portions, and that one business has spent $40,000 on cleanup while the community has been largely self-reliant. She asked for a six-month waiver. Matt Haybeck spoke on behalf of residents whose homes need demolition, requesting fee relief for underinsured homeowners and noting some of the approximately 44 affected homes still await insurance adjusters, suggesting more time is needed beyond the 60-day window.

## Action Items
- Committee appointments approved as presented by Chair Gibbs
- Supervisors Hapa and Arstead elected to at-large executive committee positions
- Resolution 24-26 approved for out of home placement reserve increase
- Resolution 25-26 approved for social services budget amendment
- Resolution 26-26 approved reducing landfill tipping fees for storm debris to $36.94/ton through June 17th
- Administrator Leonard to continue discussions with Community Foundation about reimbursement mechanism for unreimbursed insurance costs
- Solid Waste Management Board to develop long-term policy on handling similar disaster situations within six months
- Board to revisit tipping fee extension at May meeting if needed
