## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Board of Supervisors held an adjourned organizational meeting where they approved committee appointments, elected Supervisors Hapa and Arstead to the at-large Executive Committee positions, and unanimously approved a resolution reducing landfill tipping fees for disaster debris from $65 to approximately $36.94 per ton following the April severe weather emergency. The board heard public comment from affected Ringle residents requesting further fee waivers.

## Key Discussions
### Committee appointments
Chair Gibbs presented committee appointments developed with Vice Chair Boots, emphasizing goals of increasing diverse perspectives, mentoring new supervisors, and noting 34% of the board is in their first or second term. The motion by Supervisor Cavelli, seconded by Supervisor Crims, carried but was not unanimous.

### Election of at-large Executive Committee members
Three supervisors were nominated: Supervisor Hapa (nominated by Supervisor Mask), Supervisor Leur (nominated by Supervisor Rosenberg, who cited her advocacy for children and elders), and Supervisor Arstead (nominated by Vice Chair Boots). Paper ballots were cast, and Supervisors Hapa and Arstead were elected to the Executive Committee.

### Resolution 26-26: Landfill tipping fee modification
Administrator Leonard and Solid Waste Director Hagen Busher presented a proposal to reduce tipping fees from $65 to approximately $36.94 per ton for disaster-related debris following severe weather that impacted Ringle and other communities. The state is waiving $13.10 and Ringle is waiving $2.33 of fees. Leonard noted the county will not meet the $670,743 damage threshold for state disaster aid but expects Ringle to qualify. Two residents, Jessica Toasty and Matt Haybeck, were granted special dispensation to speak, requesting the county waive all fees and extend the timeline beyond 60 days. Multiple supervisors discussed concerns about timing, insurance delays, and future policy. The board unanimously voted to suspend rules to hear the resolution, then unanimously approved Resolution 26-26.

### Consent agenda
Items C10 through C14A were approved unanimously on a motion by Supervisor Cavelli, seconded by Supervisor Drebeck.

### Resolution 24-26: Out of home placement reserve increase
Motion by Supervisor Morash, seconded by Supervisor Robinson. The motion carried but was not unanimous.

### Resolution 25-26: Social services budget amendment
Motion by Supervisor P, seconded by Supervisor Robinson. The motion carried but was not unanimous.

## Public Comment
Two residents were granted special dispensation to address the board regarding the landfill tipping fee resolution. Jessica Toasty requested Marathon County waive all fees for disaster debris for six months, noting the state, DNR, and Ringle have waived their portions and emphasizing the community has been self-reliant with limited county financial assistance. Matt Haybeck spoke on behalf of homeowners whose houses must be demolished, requesting fee waivers for underinsured residents and noting some insurance adjusters have not yet arrived, asking for more than 60 days of relief.

## Action Items
- Committee appointments approved as presented by Chair Gibbs and Vice Chair Boots
- Supervisors Hapa and Arstead elected to at-large Executive Committee positions
- Resolution 26-26 approved: Landfill tipping fees reduced to approximately $36.94 per ton for certified disaster debris through June 17, 2025
- Administrator Leonard to continue discussions with community foundation regarding reimbursement mechanism for uninsured debris disposal costs
- Solid waste management board directed to develop clear policy within six months for handling similar disaster situations in the future
- Resolution 24-26 approved: Out of home placement reserve increase
- Resolution 25-26 approved: Social services budget amendment
