## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Board of Supervisors held an adjourned organizational meeting where they approved committee appointments, elected Supervisors Hapa and Arstead to the Executive Committee at-large positions, and unanimously approved a resolution reducing landfill tipping fees for storm debris related to the recent tornado and flooding disaster affecting Ringle and other communities. Two affected residents spoke requesting the county waive all fees, but the board approved the Solid Waste Management Board's recommendation to reduce fees to $36.94 per ton (covering costs) rather than eliminating them entirely.

## Key Discussions
### Committee Appointments
Chair Gibbs presented committee appointments developed with Vice Chair Boots, emphasizing goals of increasing diversity of perspective, developing new supervisors as leaders, and honoring supervisor preferences. Gibbs noted 34% of the board is in its first or second term. Motion by Supervisor Cavelli, seconded by Supervisor Crims. The motion carried but was not unanimous.

### Election of At-Large Executive Committee Members
Three supervisors were nominated for two at-large Executive Committee positions: Supervisor Hapa (nominated by Supervisor Mask), Supervisor Leur (nominated by Supervisor Rosenberg, who praised her advocacy for children and elders), and Supervisor Arstead (nominated by Supervisor Boots). Paper ballots were distributed and counted. Supervisors Hapa and Arstead were elected to the Executive Committee.

### Consent Agenda
Items C10 through C14A were approved on consent. Motion by Supervisor Cavelli, seconded by Supervisor Drebeck. The motion carried unanimously.

### Resolution 24-26: Out of Home Placement Reserve Increase
Motion by Supervisor Morash, seconded by Supervisor Robinson. The motion carried but was not unanimous with no discussion.

### Resolution 25-26: Social Services Budget Amendment
Motion by Supervisor P, seconded by Supervisor Robinson. The motion carried but was not unanimous with no discussion.

### Resolution 26-26: Landfill Tipping Fees Modification for Disaster Debris
Administrator Leonard and Solid Waste Director Hagen Busher presented a proposal to reduce tipping fees for debris from the recent tornado and flooding. The state waived its $13.10 fee, Ringle waived its $2.33 fee, and the county proposed reducing fees to $36.94 per ton (covering costs only). Two residents, Jessica Toasty and Matt Haybeck, were granted special permission to speak, requesting the county waive all fees entirely for affected residents. Supervisor Rosenberg, Supervisor Gao, Supervisor Leur, Supervisor Robinson, and others asked questions about timelines, insurance coverage, and policy implications. Administrator Leonard mentioned exploring a partnership with the Community Foundation to help cover unreimbursed costs for landowners. The board first voted unanimously to suspend rules to consider the resolution directly from the Solid Waste Management Board. Motion on the resolution by Supervisor Arstead, seconded by Supervisor Morash. The resolution passed unanimously.

## Public Comment
No formal public comment period was held, but Supervisor Rosenberg requested special dispensation for two residents to speak on the landfill tipping fees resolution. Jessica Toasty spoke for three minutes, noting the state, DNR, and Ringle are waiving fees and asking Marathon County to do the same, stating one business has incurred $40,000 in hauling expenses and that residents are paying deductibles for items not covered by insurance. Matt Haybeck spoke on behalf of residents with demolished homes, noting some of the 44 affected homes are still waiting for insurance adjusters and requesting more time for fee waivers.

## Action Items
- Committee appointments approved for the new term
- Supervisors Hapa and Arstead elected to Executive Committee at-large positions
- Landfill tipping fees reduced to $36.94 per ton for certified disaster debris through June 17, 2025
- Administrator to work with Community Foundation on mechanism to reimburse unreimbursed debris disposal costs for landowners
- Solid Waste Management Board to develop clear policy on handling similar disaster situations within six months
- Solid Waste Management Board may revisit timeline extension at future meetings if needed
