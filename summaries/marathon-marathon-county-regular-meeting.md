## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Board of Supervisors held an adjourned organizational meeting where they approved committee appointments, elected two at-large executive committee members (Supervisors Hapa and Arstead), and unanimously approved a resolution reducing landfill tipping fees for storm debris following the recent tornado and flooding disaster. The board also heard from two affected residents requesting additional fee relief.

## Key Discussions
### Committee Appointments
Chair Gibbs presented committee appointments developed with Vice Chair Boots, emphasizing goals of increasing perspective diversity, developing supervisors as leaders, and respecting supervisor preferences. He noted 34% of the board is in their first or second term. The motion by Supervisor Cavelli, seconded by Supervisor Crims, included both county board chair and administrator appointments. The motion carried but was not unanimous. Supervisor Oldenberg noted he was marked absent but wanted to be recorded as voting aye.

### Election of At-Large Executive Committee Members
Three supervisors were nominated: Supervisor Hapa (nominated by Supervisor Mask), Supervisor Leur (nominated by Supervisor Rosenberg), and Supervisor Arstead (nominated by Vice Chair Boots). Supervisor Rosenberg explained she nominated Supervisor Leur because she had seen her advocate for the county's smallest children and oldest elders. Nominations were closed unanimously. Paper ballots were distributed with supervisors selecting two names. Supervisors Hapa and Arstead were elected to the executive committee.

### Consent Agenda Items C10-C14A
Motion by Supervisor Cavelli, seconded by Supervisor Drebeck. The consent agenda was approved unanimously by voice vote.

### Resolution 24-26: Out of Home Placement Reserve Increase
Motion by Supervisor Morash, seconded by Supervisor Robinson. The resolution was carried but was not unanimous.

### Resolution 25-26: Social Services Budget Amendment
Motion by Supervisor P, seconded by Supervisor Robinson. The resolution was carried but was not unanimous.

### Resolution 26-26: Landfill Tipping Fee Modification
Administrator Leonard and Solid Waste Director Hagen Busher presented the proposal from the Solid Waste Management Board to reduce tipping fees for storm debris following the recent tornado and flooding. The standard $65/ton fee would be reduced to $36.94 by removing the state fee ($13.10), Ringle's fee ($2.33), and reducing to commercial rates. Leonard noted the county will not meet the $670,743 threshold for state disaster aid, but expects Ringle to qualify. He discussed potential partnership with the Community Foundation for unreimbursed insurance costs. The board suspended rules unanimously to consider the resolution that hadn't gone through a standing committee. The resolution was then approved unanimously.

## Public Comment
Two residents were granted special dispensation to speak on the tipping fee resolution. Jessica Toasty asked Marathon County to wave all fees like the state, DNR, and Ringle were doing, noting they hadn't seen Marathon County assistance during cleanup and businesses had donated up to $40,000 in hauling expenses. She requested a six-month fee waiver. Matt Haybeck spoke on behalf of homeowners needing house demolition, noting 44 homes affected and some insurance adjusters hadn't yet arrived. He requested more time for fee waivers and relief for underinsured residents.

## Action Items
- Committee appointments approved as presented by Chair Gibbs and Vice Chair Boots
- Supervisors Hapa and Arstead elected to at-large executive committee positions
- Resolution 24-26 approved for out of home placement reserve increase
- Resolution 25-26 approved for social services budget amendment
- Resolution 26-26 approved reducing landfill tipping fees to $36.94/ton for certified storm debris through June 17, 2025
- Administrator Leonard to continue discussions with Community Foundation regarding unreimbursed insurance costs for disaster victims
- Solid Waste Management Board to develop clear policy within six months for handling similar disaster situations in the future
- Staff to review Ringle's fee waiver resolution to determine geographic scope of their $2.33 fee waiver
