## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Board of Supervisors held an adjourned organizational meeting where they approved committee appointments, elected Supervisors Hapa and Arstead to at-large executive committee positions, and unanimously passed a resolution reducing landfill tipping fees for disaster debris from recent severe weather affecting Ringle and other communities. The board suspended its rules to address the emergency tipping fee modification, which reduces fees from $65/ton to approximately $36.94/ton for certified storm-related debris through June 17th.

## Key Discussions
### Committee Appointments
Chair Gibbs explained the committee appointment process, noting goals included increasing diversity of perspectives, developing supervisor leadership, and honoring supervisor preferences. Vice Chair Boots assisted in developing appointments. Chair Gibbs emphasized that over 34% of the board is in first or second terms. Motion by Supervisor Cavelli, seconded by Supervisor Crims. The motion carried but was not unanimous.

### Election of At-Large Executive Committee Members
Three supervisors were nominated for two at-large executive committee positions: Supervisor Hapa (nominated by Supervisor Mask), Supervisor Leur (nominated by Supervisor Rosenberg), and Supervisor Arstead (nominated by Vice Chair Boots). Supervisor Rosenberg explained she nominated Supervisor Leur because she has advocated for the county's smallest children and oldest elders. Paper ballots were distributed and supervisors voted for two candidates. Supervisors Hapa and Arstead were elected to the executive committee.

### Consent Agenda
Items C10 through C14A were approved on consent. Motion by Supervisor Cavelli, seconded by Supervisor Drebeck. The motion carried unanimously.

### Resolution 24-26: Out of Home Placement Reserve Increase
Resolution from Health and Human Services Committee and Human Resources and Finance and Property Committee. Motion by Supervisor Morash, seconded by Supervisor Robinson. The motion carried but was not unanimous.

### Resolution 25-26: Social Services Budget Amendment
Motion by Supervisor P, seconded by Supervisor Robinson. The motion carried but was not unanimous.

### Resolution 26-26: Landfill Tipping Fee Modification
Administrator Leonard and Director Hagen Busher presented the emergency tipping fee reduction for disaster debris following severe weather affecting Ringle and other communities. The state declared an emergency allowing waiver of $13.10 state fee; Ringle is waiving its $2.33 fee. The solid waste management board proposed reducing the county rate to approximately $36.94/ton (covering costs) from the standard $65/ton gate rate. Supervisor Rosenberg requested public comment be allowed. Jessica Tolesie and Matt Haybeck spoke, requesting the county waive fees entirely and extend the timeframe to six months due to ongoing insurance delays. Multiple supervisors asked questions about the fee structure and timeline. The board first voted unanimously to suspend rules to bring the resolution forward, then voted unanimously to approve Resolution 26-26.

## Public Comment
By special dispensation requested by Supervisor Rosenberg, two residents were allowed to speak on the landfill tipping fee resolution. Jessica Tolesie asked Marathon County to completely waive its fees like other entities have done, noting businesses have donated up to $40,000 in cleanup efforts and requesting a six-month timeframe. Matt Haybeck spoke on behalf of homeowners needing house demolitions, noting 44 homes are affected and many insurance adjusters haven't yet arrived, requesting more time for the fee waiver.

## Action Items
- Committee appointments approved for the new term
- Supervisors Hapa and Arstead elected to at-large executive committee positions
- Resolution 26-26 approved reducing landfill tipping fees for certified disaster debris through June 17th
- Administrator to continue discussions with Community Foundation regarding reimbursement mechanism for uninsured debris disposal costs
- Solid waste management board to develop long-term policy for handling similar disaster situations within six months
- Staff to track certified disaster-related debris and apply reduced fee structure
