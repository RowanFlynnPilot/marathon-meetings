## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Board held its adjourned organizational meeting, approving committee appointments for the new term and electing Supervisors Hapa and Arstead to at-large executive committee positions. The board also unanimously approved a resolution to reduce landfill tipping fees for storm debris following the severe weather emergency declaration, dropping rates from $65/ton to approximately $36.94/ton for affected residents.

## Key Discussions
### Committee appointments
Chair Gibbs presented committee appointments developed with Vice Chair Boots, emphasizing goals of increasing diverse perspectives, developing leadership among newer supervisors (34% in first or second term), and honoring supervisor preferences. Motion by Supervisor Cavelli, seconded by Supervisor Crims. The motion carried but was not unanimous, with some technical issues noted where Supervisor Nisa was marked absent but unable to vote.

### Election of at-large executive committee members
Three supervisors were nominated: Supervisor Hapa (nominated by Supervisor Mask), Supervisor Leur (nominated by Supervisor Rosenberg), and Supervisor Arstead (nominated by Supervisor Boots). Supervisor Rosenberg spoke in support of Leur, citing her advocacy for children and elders. Paper ballots were distributed and supervisors selected two of the three nominees. Supervisors Hapa and Arstead were elected to the executive committee.

### Consent agenda
Items C10 through C14A were approved on consent. Motion by Supervisor Cavelli, seconded by Supervisor Drebeck. The motion carried unanimously.

### Resolution 24-26: Out of home placement reserve increase
Resolution from Health and Human Services and HR Finance and Property committees. Motion by Supervisor Morash, seconded by Supervisor Robinson. The motion carried but was not unanimous.

### Resolution 25-26: Social services budget amendment
Motion by Supervisor P, seconded by Supervisor Robinson. The motion carried but was not unanimous.

### Resolution 26-26: Landfill tipping fee modification for storm debris
Administrator Leonard and Solid Waste Director Hagen Busher presented the proposal from the Solid Waste Management Board to reduce tipping fees for debris from the recent severe weather emergency. The state is waiving $13.10/ton and Ringle is waiving $2.33/ton. The county proposed reducing fees from the standard $65/ton gate rate to approximately $36.94/ton (covering costs only) through June 17th. Two residents, Jessica Toasty and Matt Haybeck, were granted special permission to speak, requesting the county waive fees entirely. Multiple supervisors asked questions about coverage, timelines, and tracking. The board first voted unanimously to suspend rules to consider the resolution directly, then approved Resolution 26-26 unanimously.

## Public Comment
Two residents were granted special dispensation to speak on the landfill tipping fee resolution. Jessica Toasty requested Marathon County wave fees entirely for six months, noting the state, DNR, and Ringle had waived their portions and that residents are facing significant unreimbursed costs. Matt Haybeck spoke on behalf of approximately 44 affected homeowners, noting many are still waiting for insurance adjusters and requested extended fee relief.

## Action Items
- Committee appointments approved for the new term
- Supervisors Hapa and Arstead elected to at-large executive committee positions
- Resolution 26-26 approved reducing landfill tipping fees for storm debris to approximately $36.94/ton through June 17th
- Administrator Leonard to continue discussions with the Community Foundation regarding reimbursement mechanisms for unreimbursed insurance costs
- Solid Waste Management Board to develop a clear policy on handling similar disaster situations within six months
- Supervisors interested in attending Solid Waste Board meetings to be notified of future meetings
