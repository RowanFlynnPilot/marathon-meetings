## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Board of Supervisors held an adjourned organizational meeting where they approved committee appointments, elected Supervisors Hapa and Arstead to at-large executive committee positions, and unanimously passed a resolution reducing landfill tipping fees for storm debris following the recent tornado and flooding disaster. The board heard from affected residents requesting additional fee relief beyond the proposed reduction.

## Key Discussions
### Committee Appointments
Chair Gibbs delivered remarks on committee appointment philosophy, emphasizing diversity of perspectives, leadership development, and noting 34% of the board is in first or second terms. Vice chairs were assigned responsibility for shepherding the strategic plan. Supervisor Cavelli moved and Supervisor Crims seconded to approve all appointments. The motion carried but was not unanimous.

### Election of At-Large Executive Committee Members
Three supervisors were nominated: Supervisor Hapa (nominated by Supervisor Mask), Supervisor Leur (nominated by Supervisor Rosenberg), and Supervisor Arstead (nominated by Vice Chair Boots). Supervisor Rosenberg spoke in support of Supervisor Leur, citing her advocacy for children and elderly residents. Paper ballots were distributed and supervisors selected two names. Supervisors Hapa and Arstead were elected to the executive committee.

### Consent Agenda (Items C10-C14A)
Supervisor Cavelli moved and Supervisor Drebeck seconded. The motion carried unanimously.

### Resolution 24-26: Out of Home Placement Reserve Increase
Motion by Supervisor Morash, seconded by Supervisor Robinson. The motion carried but was not unanimous.

### Resolution 25-26: Social Services Budget Amendment
Motion by Supervisor P, seconded by Supervisor Robinson. The motion carried but was not unanimous.

### Resolution 26-26: Landfill Tipping Fees Modification
Administrator Leonard and Director Hagen Busher presented the proposal from the Solid Waste Management Board to reduce tipping fees for storm debris from the recent tornado and flooding. The state waived $13.10, Town of Ringle waived $2.33, and the county proposed reducing fees to $36.94/ton (commercial rate minus waivers) to cover operational costs. Leonard noted the county won't meet the $670,743 damage threshold for state disaster aid, but expects Ringle will. Supervisor Rosenberg requested public comment, and residents Jessica Toasty and Matt Haybeck spoke, asking the county to fully waive fees citing hardship and slow insurance responses. Multiple supervisors asked clarifying questions about fee structure, timeline, and future policy. The board unanimously suspended rules to consider the resolution, then passed it unanimously. Supervisor Arstead moved, Supervisor Morash seconded.

## Public Comment
Two residents were granted special dispensation to speak on the landfill tipping fee resolution. Jessica Toasty asked Marathon County to fully waive its portion of tipping fees, noting that the state, DNR, and Ringle have waived their fees. She described community self-reliance efforts, with one business incurring $40,000 in cleanup costs, and requested a six-month fee waiver. Matt Haybeck spoke on behalf of affected homeowners, noting that 44 homes need demolition, many insurance adjusters haven't yet arrived, and residents need extended relief.

## Action Items
- Committee appointments approved as presented by Chair Gibbs and Vice Chair Boots
- Supervisors Hapa and Arstead elected to at-large executive committee positions
- Resolution 26-26 approved: Landfill tipping fees reduced to approximately $36.94/ton for certified disaster debris through June 17, 2025
- Administrator Leonard to work with Community Foundation on potential reimbursement mechanism for uninsured debris disposal costs
- Solid Waste Management Board to develop long-term disaster debris fee policy within six months
- Resolution 24-26 approved: Out of home placement reserve increase
- Resolution 25-26 approved: Social services budget amendment
