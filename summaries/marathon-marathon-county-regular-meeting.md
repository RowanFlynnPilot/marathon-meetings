## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Board of Supervisors held an adjourned organizational meeting where they approved committee appointments, elected Supervisors Hapa and Arstead to at-large executive committee positions, and unanimously passed a resolution to reduce landfill tipping fees for storm debris following a tornado that devastated the Ringle area. The board suspended its rules to address the emergency tipping fee matter directly without committee review.

## Key Discussions
### Committee Appointments
Chair Gibbs presented committee appointments developed with Vice Chair Boots, noting goals including increasing representation, allowing supervisors to develop as leaders, and accommodating preferences. He noted 34% of the board is in their first or second term. The appointments were approved but not unanimously on a single motion that included both county board chair and administrator appointments.

### Election of At-Large Executive Committee Members
Three supervisors were nominated: Supervisor Hapa (nominated by Supervisor Mask), Supervisor Leur (nominated by Supervisor Rosenberg), and Supervisor Arstead (nominated by Vice Chair Boots). Supervisor Rosenberg explained she nominated Leur because she had seen her advocate for the county's smallest children and oldest elders. Paper ballots were distributed and supervisors selected two names. Supervisors Hapa and Arstead were elected to the executive committee.

### Resolution 24-26: Out of Home Placement Reserve Increase
Motion by Supervisor Morash, seconded by Supervisor Robinson. No discussion occurred. The motion carried but was not unanimous.

### Resolution 25-26: Social Services Budget Amendment
Motion by Supervisor P, seconded by Supervisor Robinson. No discussion occurred. The motion carried but was not unanimous.

### Resolution 26-26: Landfill Tipping Fees Modification for Storm Debris
Administrator Leonard explained the Solid Waste Management Board held a special meeting to address debris from severe weather including a tornado that devastated Ringle. The state waived $13.10 per ton, Ringle waived $2.33, and the county proposed reducing fees to $36.94 per ton (commercial rate minus waivers) to cover costs. Director Hagen Busher noted 400 tons of right-of-way debris was already received. The board suspended rules unanimously to consider the resolution without committee review. Two residents, Jessica Toasty and Matt Haybeck, were allowed to speak and requested the county fully waive fees. Multiple supervisors discussed tracking costs, insurance reimbursement issues, and potential future policy. The resolution passed unanimously.

## Public Comment
Two residents were granted special permission to speak on the tipping fee resolution. Jessica Toasty asked the county to fully wave fees for six months, noting the state, DNR, and Ringle were waving their fees and that businesses had donated over $40,000 in cleanup efforts. Matt Haybeck spoke on behalf of homeowners awaiting demolition, noting 44 homes affected and many still waiting for insurance adjusters, requesting more time and relief for underinsured residents.

## Action Items
- Committee appointments approved as presented by Chair Gibbs and Vice Chair Boots
- Supervisors Hapa and Arstead elected to at-large executive committee positions
- Resolution 24-26 approved for out of home placement reserve increase
- Resolution 25-26 approved for social services budget amendment
- Resolution 26-26 approved reducing landfill tipping fees to approximately $36.94 per ton for storm debris through June 17, 2025
- Administrator to continue discussions with Community Foundation regarding potential reimbursement mechanism for unreimbursed insurance costs
- Solid Waste Management Board to develop a clear policy on handling similar disaster situations within six months
- Staff to track all storm-related debris with certification forms for potential FEMA reimbursement
