## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Board of Supervisors held an adjourned organizational meeting where they approved committee appointments, elected Supervisors Hapa and Arstead to the executive committee, and unanimously approved a resolution to reduce landfill tipping fees for debris from the recent tornado and flooding disaster. The board heard emotional testimony from affected Ringle residents requesting fee waivers for disaster cleanup.

## Key Discussions
### Committee Appointments
Chair Gibbs thanked staff and Vice Chair Boots for assistance in developing committee appointments. He emphasized that 34% of the board is in its first or second term and that appointments were guided by goals including increasing diversity of perspectives, ensuring leadership development, and honoring supervisor preferences. The appointments were approved in a single motion by Supervisor Cavelli, seconded by Supervisor Crims. The motion carried but was not unanimous.

### Election of At-Large Executive Committee Members
Three supervisors were nominated: Supervisor Hapa (nominated by Supervisor Mask), Supervisor Leur (nominated by Supervisor Rosenberg), and Supervisor Arstead (nominated by Supervisor Boots). Supervisor Rosenberg spoke in support of Supervisor Leur, citing her advocacy for 'our county's smallest children and our oldest elders.' Paper ballots were distributed for supervisors to select two candidates. Supervisors Hapa and Arstead were elected to the executive committee.

### Consent Agenda (Items C10-C14A)
Motion by Supervisor Cavelli, seconded by Supervisor Drebeck. The consent agenda was approved unanimously with no discussion.

### Resolution 24-26: Out of Home Placement Reserve Increase
Motion by Supervisor Morash, seconded by Supervisor Robinson. The resolution was approved but not unanimously, with no discussion recorded.

### Resolution 25-26: Social Services Budget Amendment
Motion by Supervisor P, seconded by Supervisor Robinson. The resolution was approved but not unanimously, with no discussion recorded.

### Resolution 26-26: Landfill Tipping Fee Modification
Administrator Leonard and Director Hagen Busher presented this resolution from the Solid Waste Management Board regarding reduced tipping fees for disaster debris. The county received approximately 400 tons of storm debris, with the proposed fee reduced to $36.94/ton (from $65 standard rate) to cover costs while waiving the state fee ($13.10) and Ringle's fee ($2.33). Leonard noted Marathon County will not meet the $670,743 threshold for state disaster aid, but the town of Ringle likely will. The board suspended rules unanimously to consider this resolution directly. Following public testimony, the resolution was approved unanimously. Motion by Supervisor Arstead, seconded by Supervisor Morash.

## Public Comment
At Supervisor Rosenberg's request, two residents were granted special permission to speak. Jessica Toasty urged the county to fully waive fees, noting the state and Ringle are waiving their portions, and that private businesses have donated up to $40,000 in cleanup assistance. She stated 'We didn't see anyone from Marathon County' during cleanup and requested fee waivers for six months. Matt Haybeck spoke on behalf of homeowners facing demolition, noting approximately 44 homes were affected and many are still waiting for insurance adjusters, requesting extended relief time for underinsured residents.

## Action Items
- Committee appointments approved for the term
- Supervisors Hapa and Arstead elected to executive committee as at-large members
- Resolution 24-26 approved for out of home placement reserve increase
- Resolution 25-26 approved for social services budget amendment
- Resolution 26-26 approved reducing landfill tipping fees to approximately $36.94/ton for certified disaster debris through June 17th
- Administrator Leonard to work with Community Foundation on potential reimbursement mechanism for unreimbursed insurance costs
- Solid Waste Management Board to develop clear policy within six months for handling similar disaster situations in the future
- Board to revisit tipping fee extension at May meeting if needed
