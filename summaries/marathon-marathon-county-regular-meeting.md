## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Board of Supervisors held an adjourned organizational meeting where they approved committee appointments, elected Supervisors Hapa and Arstead to the Executive Committee at-large positions, and unanimously approved a resolution reducing landfill tipping fees for disaster-related debris following recent severe weather that devastated parts of the county, particularly the Town of Ringle.

## Key Discussions
### Committee Appointments
Chair Gibbs outlined goals for committee appointments including increasing diversity of perspectives, developing new supervisors as leaders, and honoring supervisor preferences. He noted 34% of the board is in first or second terms. The motion by Supervisor Cavelli, seconded by Supervisor Crims, included both county board chair and administrator appointments. The motion carried but was not unanimous.

### Election of At-Large Executive Committee Members
Three supervisors were nominated: Supervisor Hapa (nominated by Supervisor Mask), Supervisor Leur (nominated by Supervisor Rosenberg), and Supervisor Arstead (nominated by Vice Chair Boots). Supervisor Rosenberg explained her nomination of Supervisor Leur, citing her advocacy for the county's youngest and oldest residents. Paper ballots were used and Supervisors Hapa and Arstead were elected to the Executive Committee.

### Resolution 26-26 - Landfill Tipping Fee Modification
Administrator Leonard and Solid Waste Director Hagen Busher presented the emergency resolution following severe weather that devastated Ringle and other communities. The state waived $13.10 per ton, Ringle waived $2.33 per ton, and the county proposed reducing fees to $36.94 per ton (commercial rate minus waivers) to cover costs. Special public comment was allowed for Jessica Toasty and Matt Haybeck, who requested the county wave all fees for affected residents and extend relief beyond 60 days. The board unanimously suspended rules to consider the resolution and then unanimously approved it. Administrator Leonard committed to exploring partnerships with the Community Foundation to help residents with unreimbursed costs and developing a long-term policy within six months.

### Consent Agenda
Items C10 through C14A were approved unanimously on motion by Supervisor Cavelli, seconded by Supervisor Drebeck.

### Out of Home Placement Reserve Increase (Resolution 24-26)
Motion by Supervisor Morash, seconded by Supervisor Robinson. The motion carried but was not unanimous.

### Social Services Budget Amendment (Resolution 25-26)
Motion by Supervisor P, seconded by Supervisor Robinson. The motion carried but was not unanimous.

## Public Comment
Special dispensation was granted for two members of the public to speak on the landfill tipping fee resolution. Jessica Toasty, a Ringle resident, asked the county to completely wave its fees, noting the state, DNR, and Town of Ringle had waived their portions. She stated businesses have donated up to $40,000 in cleanup costs and asked for six months of relief. Matt Haybeck spoke on behalf of residents with demolished homes, requesting fee waivers for underinsured homeowners and noting some insurance adjusters haven't yet arrived. He mentioned approximately 44 homes were affected.

## Action Items
- Reduced landfill tipping fees approved for disaster-related debris through June 17, 2025 (duration of state emergency declaration)
- Administrator Leonard to work with Community Foundation on mechanism to reimburse residents for out-of-pocket disposal costs not covered by insurance
- Solid Waste Management Board directed to develop long-term policy for handling similar disaster situations within six months
- Committee appointments approved as presented by Chair Gibbs
- Supervisors Hapa and Arstead elected to Executive Committee at-large positions
- Board to revisit tipping fee relief timeline at May meeting if extension is needed
