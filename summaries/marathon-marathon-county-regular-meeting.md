## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Board of Supervisors held an adjourned organizational meeting where they approved committee appointments, elected Supervisors Hapa and Arstead to at-large executive committee positions, and unanimously approved a resolution reducing landfill tipping fees for disaster-related debris following severe weather that impacted the Ringle area. The board also approved budget amendments for social services and out-of-home placement reserves.

## Key Discussions
### Committee appointments
Chair Gibbs presented committee appointments developed with Vice Chair Boots, emphasizing goals of increasing diverse perspectives, developing new supervisors as leaders, and honoring supervisor preferences. Over 34% of the board is in their first or second term. The motion by Supervisor Cavelli, seconded by Supervisor Crims, carried but was not unanimous.

### Election of at-large executive committee members
Three supervisors were nominated: Supervisor Hapa (nominated by Supervisor Mask), Supervisor Leur (nominated by Supervisor Rosenberg), and Supervisor Arstead (nominated by Vice Chair Boots). Supervisor Rosenberg explained she nominated Leur because she 'advocates for our county's smallest children and our oldest elders.' Paper ballots were used and Supervisors Hapa and Arstead were elected to the executive committee.

### Landfill tipping fee modification (Resolution 26-26)
Administrator Leonard and Solid Waste Director Hagen Busher presented a proposal to reduce tipping fees for disaster-related debris following severe weather that impacted Ringle and other areas. The state waived $13.10 per ton, Ringle planned to waive $2.33, and the county proposed reducing fees to $36.94 per ton (commercial rate minus waivers) to cover operational costs. Two residents, Jessica Toasty and Matt Haybeck, were granted special dispensation to speak and requested the county waive fees entirely for affected residents. Supervisor Robinson suggested tracking costs and potentially reimbursing the community foundation if needed. The board first voted unanimously to suspend rules to bring the resolution forward without committee review, then approved Resolution 26-26 unanimously.

### Out-of-home placement reserve increase (Resolution 24-26)
Motion by Supervisor Morash, seconded by Supervisor Robinson. The resolution was approved but was not unanimous.

### Social services budget amendment (Resolution 25-26)
Motion by Supervisor P, seconded by Supervisor Robinson. The resolution was approved but was not unanimous.

## Public Comment
Two residents were granted special dispensation to address the board regarding the landfill tipping fee resolution. Jessica Toasty spoke about the community's self-reliance in cleanup efforts, noting businesses have donated up to $40,000 in hauling expenses, and requested the county waive fees entirely for six months. Matt Haybeck spoke on behalf of residents with demolished homes, noting 44 homes affected and that many insurance adjusters haven't yet arrived, requesting more time and relief from Marathon County.

## Action Items
- Committee appointments approved as presented by Chair Gibbs and Vice Chair Boots
- Supervisors Hapa and Arstead elected to at-large executive committee positions
- Resolution 24-26 approved for out-of-home placement reserve increase
- Resolution 25-26 approved for social services budget amendment
- Resolution 26-26 approved reducing landfill tipping fees to approximately $36.94 per ton for disaster-related debris through June 17th
- Administrator Leonard to pursue discussions with community foundation regarding reimbursement mechanism for unreimbursed insurance costs
- Solid waste management board directed to develop long-term policy on handling similar disaster situations within six months
- Supervisor Gao requested notification of future solid waste board meetings
