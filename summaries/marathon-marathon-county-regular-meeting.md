## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Board of Supervisors held an adjourned organizational meeting where they approved committee appointments, elected two at-large members to the Executive Committee (Supervisors Hapa and Arstead), and passed a resolution reducing landfill tipping fees for debris from the recent tornado and flood disaster. The board heard public comment from affected Ringle residents requesting fee waivers and approved a reduced rate of approximately $36.94 per ton for certified disaster debris.

## Key Discussions
### Committee Appointments
Chair Gibbs delivered remarks emphasizing that 34% of the board is in their first or second term and explained the goals guiding appointments, including increasing diversity of perspectives and mentoring opportunities. The motion by Supervisor Cavelli, seconded by Supervisor Crims, passed but was not unanimous. The appointments included both county board chair and administrator appointments.

### Election of At-Large Executive Committee Members
Three supervisors were nominated: Supervisor Hapa (nominated by Supervisor Mask), Supervisor Leur (nominated by Supervisor Rosenberg), and Supervisor Arstead (nominated by Vice Chair Boots). Supervisor Rosenberg explained she nominated Supervisor Leur because she had seen her advocate for the county's smallest children and oldest elders. Paper ballots were distributed and supervisors voted for two candidates. Supervisors Hapa and Arstead were elected to the Executive Committee.

### Resolution 26-26: Landfill Tipping Fees Modification
Administrator Leonard and Solid Waste Director Hagen Busher presented a proposal to reduce tipping fees for disaster-related debris following the April tornado and flooding. The state is waiving $13.10/ton, the Town of Ringle is waiving $2.33/ton, and the county proposed reducing fees to approximately $36.94/ton (commercial rate minus state and town fees). The board suspended rules to consider the resolution since it came directly from the Solid Waste Management Board without going through a standing committee. After extensive discussion about future policy development and potential extension beyond 60 days, the resolution passed unanimously.

### Consent Agenda (Items C10-C14A)
Motion by Supervisor Cavelli, seconded by Supervisor Drebeck. The consent agenda passed unanimously via voice vote.

### Resolution 24-26: Out of Home Placement Reserve Increase
Motion by Supervisor Morash, seconded by Supervisor Robinson. The resolution was carried but was not unanimous.

### Resolution 25-26: Social Services Budget Amendment
Motion by Supervisor P, seconded by Supervisor Robinson. The resolution was carried but was not unanimous.

## Public Comment
Two residents were granted special permission to speak on the landfill tipping fee resolution. Jessica Toasty, a Ringle resident, asked the county to waive fees entirely for six months, noting that the state, DNR, and Town of Ringle were all waiving their portions and that private businesses had donated over $40,000 in cleanup assistance. Matt Haybeck spoke on behalf of residents whose homes must be demolished, requesting fee waivers for underinsured homeowners and extended timelines since some insurance adjusters hadn't yet arrived. He mentioned approximately 44 homes were affected.

## Action Items
- Committee appointments approved as presented by Chair Gibbs and Vice Chair Boots
- Supervisors Hapa and Arstead elected to Executive Committee at-large positions
- Resolution 26-26 approved reducing landfill tipping fees to approximately $36.94/ton for certified disaster debris through June 17, 2025
- Administrator Leonard to work with Community Foundation on potential reimbursement mechanism for unreimbursed insurance costs
- Solid Waste Management Board directed to develop clear policy on handling similar disaster situations within six months
- Staff to track and certify all disaster-related debris brought to landfill for potential state disaster aid documentation
