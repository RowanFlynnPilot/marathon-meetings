## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Board held an organizational meeting where they approved committee appointments, elected Supervisors Hapa and Arstead to the Executive Committee, and unanimously approved a resolution reducing landfill tipping fees for storm debris from the recent tornado and flooding disaster. The board heard emotional testimony from affected Ringle residents requesting fee waivers for disaster cleanup.

## Key Discussions
### Committee Appointments
Chair Gibbs presented committee appointments developed with Vice Chair Boots, emphasizing goals of increasing diversity of perspectives, developing new supervisors as leaders, and respecting supervisor preferences. He noted 34% of the board is in their first or second term. The motion by Supervisor Cavelli and seconded by Supervisor Crims was carried but not unanimous. Supervisor Enzy noted he was marked absent but wanted to be recorded as voting aye.

### Election of At-Large Executive Committee Members
Three supervisors were nominated: Supervisor Hapa (nominated by Supervisor Mask), Supervisor Leur (nominated by Supervisor Rosenberg), and Supervisor Arstead (nominated by Vice Chair Boots). Supervisor Rosenberg explained she nominated Supervisor Leur because she had seen her advocate for the county's smallest children and oldest elders. Paper ballots were distributed and supervisors voted for two candidates. Supervisors Hapa and Arstead were elected to the Executive Committee.

### Resolution 26-26: Landfill Tipping Fees Modification
Administrator Leonard and Solid Waste Director Hagen Busher presented a proposal to reduce tipping fees for disaster debris following the tornado and flooding. The proposal reduces fees from $65/ton to approximately $36.94/ton by waiving the state fee ($13.10), town of Ringle fee ($2.33), and reducing to the commercial rate. Two residents, Jessica Toasty and Matt Haybeck, were granted special permission to speak, requesting the county wave all fees. Leonard mentioned exploring a partnership with the Community Foundation to help residents with unreimbursed costs. The board voted unanimously to suspend rules to consider the resolution, then approved Resolution 26-26 unanimously.

### Consent Agenda
Items C10 through C14A were approved on consent. Motion by Supervisor Cavelli, seconded by Supervisor Drebeck. Passed unanimously.

### Resolution 24-26: Out of Home Placement Reserve Increase
Motion by Supervisor Morash, seconded by Supervisor Robinson. The motion carried but was not unanimous.

### Resolution 25-26: Social Services Budget Amendment
Motion by Supervisor P, seconded by Supervisor Robinson. The motion carried but was not unanimous.

## Public Comment
Two residents were granted special permission to speak on the landfill tipping fee resolution. Jessica Toasty requested Marathon County wave all fees for disaster victims, noting the state, DNR, and Ringle are waiving their fees, and that private businesses have donated $40,000+ in cleanup efforts. She requested a six-month timeline. Matt Haybeck spoke on behalf of residents whose homes must be demolished, noting some insurance adjusters haven't arrived yet and requesting more time and fee relief for underinsured residents.

## Action Items
- Committee appointments approved as presented by Chair Gibbs and Vice Chair Boots
- Supervisors Hapa and Arstead elected to Executive Committee at-large positions
- Resolution 26-26 approved reducing landfill tipping fees for disaster debris through June 17, 2025
- Solid Waste Management Board directed to develop a long-term policy for handling similar disaster situations within six months
- Administrator Leonard to explore partnership with Community Foundation for reimbursing residents' out-of-pocket disposal costs
- Staff to track all disaster-related debris with certification forms for potential state disaster aid documentation
