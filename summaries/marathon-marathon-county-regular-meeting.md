## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Board of Supervisors held an adjourned organizational meeting where they approved committee appointments, elected Supervisors Hapa and Arstead to the executive committee, and unanimously approved a resolution reducing landfill tipping fees for disaster debris following severe weather that devastated parts of the county, particularly the town of Ringle.

## Key Discussions
### Committee appointments
Chair Gibbs presented committee appointments developed with Vice Chair Boots, emphasizing goals of increasing diversity of perspective, providing leadership development opportunities, and honoring supervisor preferences. He noted 34% of the board is in its first or second term. The motion by Supervisor Cavelli, seconded by Supervisor Crims, was carried but not unanimous.

### Election of at-large executive committee members
Three supervisors were nominated: Supervisor Hapa (nominated by Supervisor Mask), Supervisor Leur (nominated by Supervisor Rosenberg), and Supervisor Arstead (nominated by Vice Chair Boots). Supervisor Rosenberg spoke in support of Supervisor Leur, citing her advocacy for the county's smallest children and oldest elders. Paper ballots were distributed and counted. Supervisors Hapa and Arstead were elected to the executive committee.

### Consent agenda items C10-C14A
Motion by Supervisor Cavelli, seconded by Supervisor Drebeck. The motion was carried unanimously.

### Resolution 24-26: Out of home placement reserve increase
Motion by Supervisor Morash, seconded by Supervisor Robinson. The motion was carried but not unanimous.

### Resolution 25-26: Social services budget amendment
Motion by Supervisor P [inaudible], seconded by Supervisor Robinson. The motion was carried but not unanimous.

### Resolution 26-26: Landfill tipping fee modification
Administrator Leonard and Director Dave Hagen Busher presented on the severe weather disaster affecting Ringle and other communities. The state declared emergency allows waiving the $13.10 state fee, Ringle is waiving their $2.33 fee, and the county proposed reducing fees to $36.94 per ton (from $65 standard rate) to cover costs. Two residents, Jessica Toasty and Matt Haybeck, were granted special permission to speak, requesting the county waive fees entirely and extend the timeline beyond 60 days due to ongoing insurance delays. Supervisor Rosenberg, Supervisor Gao, Supervisor Leur, and others asked questions about policy implications and budgetary impacts. The board voted unanimously to suspend rules to consider the resolution, then approved Resolution 26-26 unanimously.

## Public Comment
Two residents were granted special dispensation to speak on the landfill tipping fee resolution. Jessica Toasty requested the county waive fees entirely for disaster victims, noting private businesses and the state have contributed but Marathon County has provided limited financial assistance. Matt Haybeck spoke on behalf of residents whose homes must be demolished, requesting extended fee waivers due to insurance adjusters not yet arriving at many properties.

## Action Items
- Committee appointments approved as presented by Chair Gibbs and Vice Chair Boots
- Supervisors Hapa and Arstead elected to executive committee at-large positions
- Resolution 26-26 approved reducing landfill tipping fees to $36.94 per ton for certified disaster debris through June 17, 2025
- Administrator Leonard to work with Community Foundation on potential reimbursement mechanism for unreimbursed insurance costs for landowners
- Solid waste management board directed to develop clear policy within six months for handling similar disaster situations in the future
- Supervisors encouraged to attend Wisconsin Counties Association officials workshops
