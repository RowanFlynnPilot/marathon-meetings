## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Board of Supervisors held an adjourned organizational meeting where they approved committee appointments, elected Supervisors Hapa and Arstead to the Executive Committee at-large positions, and unanimously passed a resolution reducing landfill tipping fees for disaster-related debris following severe weather that devastated parts of the county, particularly the town of Ringle.

## Key Discussions
### Committee Appointments
Chair Gibbs explained the goals guiding committee appointments, including increasing diversity of perspective, providing leadership development opportunities for newer supervisors (34% of the board is in first or second term), and honoring supervisor preferences. Vice Chair Boots assisted in developing the appointments. The motion was made by Supervisor Cavelli and seconded by Supervisor Crims. The motion carried but was not unanimous.

### Election of At-Large Executive Committee Members
Three supervisors were nominated for two at-large Executive Committee positions: Supervisor Hapa (nominated by Supervisor Mask), Supervisor Leur (nominated by Supervisor Rosenberg), and Supervisor Arstead (nominated by Supervisor Boots). Supervisor Rosenberg explained she nominated Supervisor Leur because she had seen her advocate for the county's youngest children and oldest elders. Paper ballots were distributed and Supervisors Hapa and Arstead were elected to the Executive Committee.

### Consent Agenda (Items C10-C14A)
The consent agenda was approved unanimously on a motion by Supervisor Cavelli and second by Supervisor Drebeck.

### Resolution 24-26: Out of Home Placement Reserve Increase
Motion by Supervisor Morash, seconded by Supervisor Robinson. The resolution was approved but was not unanimous.

### Resolution 25-26: Social Services Budget Amendment
Motion by Supervisor P, seconded by Supervisor Robinson. The resolution was approved but was not unanimous.

### Resolution 26-26: Landfill Tipping Fee Modification
Administrator Leonard and Solid Waste Director Hagen Busher presented a proposal from the Solid Waste Management Board to reduce tipping fees for debris from the recent severe weather disaster that impacted Ringle and other communities. The state waived $13.10 per ton, Ringle planned to waive $2.33, and the county proposed reducing fees to $36.94 per ton (covering operational costs). Public comment was allowed with Jessica Tolesie and Matt Haybeck requesting the county waive fees entirely and extend the timeline to six months. Multiple supervisors asked questions about fee structures, timeline extensions, and future policy. The board first voted unanimously to suspend rules to consider the resolution directly from the Solid Waste Management Board, then passed Resolution 26-26 unanimously on a motion by Supervisor Arstead and second by Supervisor Morash.

## Public Comment
Two residents were granted special dispensation to speak on the landfill tipping fee resolution. Jessica Tolesie asked the county to fully waive fees for six months, noting the state, DNR, and Ringle were waiving their portions, and that residents face insurance deductibles and uncovered expenses. Matt Haybeck spoke on behalf of residents whose homes must be demolished, requesting fee relief for underinsured homeowners and noting some insurance adjusters haven't yet arrived.

## Action Items
- Committee appointments for 2025-2027 term approved
- Supervisors Hapa and Arstead elected to Executive Committee at-large positions
- Resolution 26-26 approved reducing landfill tipping fees to approximately $36.94 per ton for disaster-related debris through June 17, 2025
- Administrator Leonard to continue discussions with the Community Foundation about reimbursing uninsured disaster cleanup costs
- Solid Waste Management Board directed to develop a formal policy on handling similar disaster situations within six months
- Board may revisit tipping fee relief at May meeting if extension beyond June 17 is needed
