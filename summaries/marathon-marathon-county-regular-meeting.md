## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Board of Supervisors held an adjourned organizational meeting where they approved committee appointments, elected Supervisors Hapa and Arstead to the at-large Executive Committee positions, and unanimously approved a resolution to reduce landfill tipping fees for disaster debris following the recent tornado and flooding that devastated the town of Ringle. The board also heard from affected residents requesting additional financial relief.

## Key Discussions
### Committee Appointments
Chair Gibbs delivered remarks thanking staff and Vice Chair Boots for developing committee appointments, emphasizing goals of increasing diversity of perspective, developing leadership among the 34% of supervisors in their first or second term, and honoring supervisor preferences. The appointments were approved in a single motion by Supervisor Cavelli, seconded by Supervisor Crims. The motion carried but was not unanimous.

### Election of At-Large Executive Committee Members
Three supervisors were nominated for two at-large positions: Supervisor Hapa (nominated by Supervisor Mask), Supervisor Leur (nominated by Supervisor Rosenberg), and Supervisor Arstead (nominated by Vice Chair Boots). Supervisor Rosenberg spoke in support of Supervisor Leur, citing her advocacy for the county's 'smallest children and oldest elders.' Paper ballots were distributed and counted. Supervisors Hapa and Arstead were elected to the Executive Committee.

### Resolution 24-26 - Out of Home Placement Reserve Increase
Motion by Supervisor Morash, seconded by Supervisor Robinson. No discussion was held. The motion carried but was not unanimous.

### Resolution 25-26 - Social Services Budget Amendment
Motion by Supervisor P, seconded by Supervisor Robinson. No discussion was held. The motion carried but was not unanimous.

### Resolution 26-26 - Landfill Tipping Fees Modification
Administrator Leonard and Solid Waste Director Hagen Busher presented the proposal following severe weather that devastated Ringle and other communities. The state declared an emergency, waiving the $13.10 state fee; the town of Ringle waived its $2.33 fee. The solid waste management board proposed reducing the county fee to $36.94 per ton (from $65 standard rate), essentially covering costs. Director Hagen Busher noted Brown County and Dane County did not offer similar relief. The board suspended rules (unanimous) to consider the resolution from the solid waste management board. Motion by Supervisor Arstead, second by Supervisor Morash. The resolution passed unanimously.

## Public Comment
Two residents were granted special dispensation to speak on the landfill tipping fee resolution. Jessica Toasty requested Marathon County wave all fees for six months, noting the state, DNR, and Ringle waived their fees, and that private businesses donated $40,000+ in cleanup assistance while residents face uninsured costs. Matt Haybeck spoke on behalf of residents with demolished homes, requesting fee waivers for underinsured homeowners and noting some insurance adjusters haven't arrived yet. He requested extending the waiver period beyond 60 days.

## Action Items
- Committee appointments approved as presented by Chair Gibbs and Vice Chair Boots
- Supervisors Hapa and Arstead elected to at-large Executive Committee positions
- Resolution 24-26 approved for out of home placement reserve increase
- Resolution 25-26 approved for social services budget amendment
- Resolution 26-26 approved reducing landfill tipping fees to $36.94 per ton for certified disaster debris through June 17
- Administrator Leonard to continue discussions with Community Foundation regarding reimbursement mechanism for unreimbursed insurance costs
- Solid Waste Management Board to develop long-term policy on handling disaster-related debris within six months
- Supervisors Rosenberg and Gao to be notified of future Solid Waste Board meetings
