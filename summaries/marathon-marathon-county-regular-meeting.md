## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Board held an adjourned organizational meeting where committee appointments were approved, two at-large executive committee members were elected (Supervisors Hapa and Arstead), and a resolution was passed to reduce landfill tipping fees for debris from the recent severe weather emergency. The board also heard from residents affected by the tornado in Ringle requesting additional fee relief.

## Key Discussions
### Committee appointments
Chair Gibbs presented committee appointments developed with Vice Chair Boots, emphasizing goals of increasing diversity of perspective, developing new supervisors as leaders, and respecting supervisor preferences. The appointments included both County Board Chair and County Administrator appointments. Motion by Supervisor Cavelli, seconded by Supervisor Crims. The motion carried but was not unanimous. Supervisor Nisa noted the system marked him absent but didn't allow him to vote, clarifying he was an 'I' vote.

### Election of at-large executive committee members
Three supervisors were nominated: Supervisor Hapa (nominated by Supervisor Mask), Supervisor Leur (nominated by Supervisor Rosenberg, who cited her advocacy for children and elderly), and Supervisor Arstead (nominated by Supervisor Boots). Paper ballots were distributed for board members to select two. Supervisors Hapa and Arstead were elected to the executive committee.

### Consent agenda
Items C10 through C14A were approved on consent. Motion by Supervisor Cavelli, seconded by Supervisor Drebeck. The motion carried unanimously.

### Resolution 24-26: Out of home placement reserve increase
Resolution from Health and Human Services and HR Finance and Property committees. Motion by Supervisor Morash, seconded by Supervisor Robinson. The motion carried but was not unanimous.

### Resolution 25-26: Social services budget amendment
Motion by Supervisor P, seconded by Supervisor Robinson. The motion carried but was not unanimous.

### Resolution 26-26: Landfill tipping fee modification for severe weather debris
Administrator Leonard and Director Hagen Busher presented the proposal from the Solid Waste Management Board to reduce tipping fees for disaster-related debris. The state is waiving $13.10, Ringle waiving $2.33, resulting in a proposed fee of $36.94 per ton covering landfill costs. Jessica Toasty and Matt Haybeck spoke requesting Marathon County wave fees entirely, noting 44 homes affected and insurance adjusters still pending. Multiple supervisors asked questions about coverage, timeline, and policy implications. Administrator Leonard mentioned potential partnership with Community Foundation for unreimbursed costs. The board suspended rules unanimously to consider the resolution, then passed Resolution 26-26 unanimously with motion by Supervisor Arstead and second by Supervisor Morash.

## Public Comment
Two residents were given special dispensation to speak on the tipping fee resolution. Jessica Toasty urged the county to wave fees entirely, noting the state, DNR, and Ringle are all waiving their portions, and requested relief for six months. She stated one business has spent $40,000 on cleanup and that the Community Foundation has received $180,000 in donations. Matt Haybeck spoke on behalf of affected homeowners, noting 44 homes affected with some insurance adjusters still not having arrived, requesting fee relief especially for underinsured residents.

## Action Items
- Committee appointments approved as presented by Chair Gibbs and Vice Chair Boots
- Supervisors Hapa and Arstead elected to executive committee at-large positions
- Reduced landfill tipping fees approved for disaster-related debris through June 17, 2025
- Administrator to work with Community Foundation on mechanism for reimbursing unreimbursed cleanup costs
- Solid Waste Management Board to develop clear policy on handling similar emergency situations within six months
- Staff to continue tracking disaster-related debris with certification forms
- Board to revisit tipping fee timeline at May meeting if extension needed
