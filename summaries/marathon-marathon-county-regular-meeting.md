## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Board of Supervisors held an adjourned organizational meeting where they approved committee appointments, elected Supervisors Hapa and Arstead to the Executive Committee, and unanimously approved a resolution reducing landfill tipping fees for disaster-related debris following severe weather that impacted Ringle and other communities. The board suspended its rules to consider the emergency tipping fee resolution, which reduces fees from $65/ton to approximately $36.94/ton for certified storm debris.

## Key Discussions
### Committee appointments
Chair Gibbs presented committee appointments developed with Vice Chair Boots, emphasizing goals of increasing diverse perspectives, developing supervisor leadership, and accommodating preferences. Over 34% of the board is in their first or second term. The motion by Supervisor Cavelli, seconded by Supervisor Crims, was carried but not unanimous. Supervisor Nisa noted the system marked them absent without allowing a vote.

### Election of at-large Executive Committee members
Three supervisors were nominated: Supervisor Hapa (nominated by Supervisor Mask), Supervisor Leur (nominated by Supervisor Rosenberg), and Supervisor Arstead (nominated by Vice Chair Boots). Supervisor Rosenberg explained nominating Supervisor Leur because she advocates for the county's youngest and oldest residents. Paper ballots were distributed and supervisors selected two names. Supervisors Hapa and Arstead were elected to the Executive Committee.

### Consent agenda
Items C10 through C14A were approved on a motion by Supervisor Cavelli, seconded by Supervisor Drebeck. The motion carried unanimously.

### Resolution 24-26: Out of home placement reserve increase
Motion by Supervisor Morash, seconded by Supervisor Robinson. The motion carried but was not unanimous.

### Resolution 25-26: Social services budget amendment
Motion by Supervisor P, seconded by Supervisor Robinson. The motion carried but was not unanimous.

### Resolution 26-26: Landfill tipping fee modification for disaster debris
Administrator Leonard and Solid Waste Director Hagen Busher presented the proposal following severe weather that caused flooding and tornado damage, particularly in Ringle. The state emergency declaration allows waiving the $13.10 state fee, and Ringle is waiving its $2.33 fee. The proposal reduces Marathon County's portion from the standard $65/ton gate rate to the commercial rate of $52.37, resulting in approximately $36.94/ton for certified disaster debris. Two residents, Jessica Toasty and Matt Haybeck, were granted special dispensation to speak, requesting the county waive fees entirely given that 44 homes need demolition and insurance adjusters haven't arrived for many. Administrator Leonard mentioned exploring partnership with the Community Foundation for unreimbursed costs. The board first voted unanimously to suspend rules since the resolution didn't go through a standing committee, then approved Resolution 26-26 unanimously on a motion by Supervisor Arstead, seconded by Supervisor Morash.

## Public Comment
No formal public comment period was held, but Supervisor Rosenberg requested special dispensation for two constituents to speak during the tipping fee discussion. Jessica Toasty spoke for three minutes, noting that the state, DNR, and Ringle are waiving fees and asking Marathon County to do the same, mentioning one business has spent $40,000 on cleanup efforts and requesting a six-month fee waiver. Matt Haybeck spoke briefly on behalf of residents whose homes must be demolished, noting insurance adjusters haven't arrived for many of the approximately 44 affected homes and requesting extended fee relief.

## Action Items
- Committee appointments approved as presented by Chair Gibbs and Vice Chair Boots
- Supervisors Hapa and Arstead elected to at-large Executive Committee positions
- Resolution 24-26 approved for out of home placement reserve increase
- Resolution 25-26 approved for social services budget amendment
- Resolution 26-26 approved reducing landfill tipping fees to approximately $36.94/ton for certified disaster debris through June 17th
- Administrator to work with Community Foundation on potential reimbursement mechanism for unreimbursed insurance costs
- Solid Waste Management Board to develop clear policy for handling similar situations within six months
- Staff to track certified disaster debris and maintain documentation for potential state disaster aid
