## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Board of Supervisors held an adjourned organizational meeting where they approved committee appointments, elected Supervisors Hapa and Arstead to at-large executive committee positions, and unanimously approved a resolution reducing landfill tipping fees from $65 to approximately $36.94 per ton for debris from the recent severe weather emergency affecting Ringle and other areas. The board suspended rules to consider the emergency tipping fee resolution after hearing from affected residents.

## Key Discussions
### Committee appointments
Chair Gibbs presented committee appointments developed with Vice Chair Boots, emphasizing goals of increasing diverse perspectives, developing new supervisors as leaders, and respecting supervisor preferences. Gibbs noted that over 34% of the board is in their first or second term. The motion by Supervisor Cavelli, seconded by Supervisor Crims, was carried but was not unanimous.

### Election of at-large executive committee members
Three supervisors were nominated for two at-large executive committee positions: Supervisor Hapa (nominated by Supervisor Mask), Supervisor Leur (nominated by Supervisor Rosenberg), and Supervisor Arstead (nominated by Vice Chair Boots). Supervisor Rosenberg spoke in support of Supervisor Leur, citing her advocacy for children and elders. After paper ballot voting, Supervisors Hapa and Arstead were elected to the executive committee.

### Resolution 26-26: Modification to landfill tipping fees
Administrator Leonard and Solid Waste Director Hagen Busher presented a proposal to reduce tipping fees for disaster-related debris following severe weather that devastated Ringle and other areas. The board suspended rules to consider the resolution that hadn't gone through a standing committee. The state waived $13.10, Ringle waived $2.33, and the county reduced its portion to bring fees from $65 to approximately $36.94 per ton. Residents Jessica Toasty and Matt Haybeck were granted special permission to speak, requesting the county waive fees entirely. Leonard mentioned exploring partnerships with the Community Foundation for additional relief. The resolution passed unanimously.

### Consent agenda
Items C10 through C14A were approved on consent agenda. Motion by Supervisor Cavelli, seconded by Supervisor Drebeck. The motion carried unanimously.

### Resolution 24-26: Out of home placement reserve increase
Motion by Supervisor Morash, seconded by Supervisor Robinson. The motion carried but was not unanimous.

### Resolution 25-26: Social services budget amendment
Motion by Supervisor P, seconded by Supervisor Robinson. The motion carried but was not unanimous.

## Public Comment
Two residents, Jessica Toasty and Matt Haybeck, were granted special permission by Supervisor Rosenberg to speak on the tipping fee resolution. Toasty requested the county waive fees entirely for six months, noting the state, DNR, and Ringle were waiving their portions and that businesses had donated $40,000+ in cleanup efforts. She questioned county presence during cleanup. Haybeck spoke on behalf of residents whose homes must be demolished, requesting fee relief for underinsured homeowners and extension beyond 60 days since many insurance adjusters haven't yet arrived.

## Action Items
- Committee appointments approved as presented by Chair Gibbs and Vice Chair Boots
- Supervisors Hapa and Arstead elected to at-large executive committee positions
- Resolution 26-26 approved: Landfill tipping fees reduced to approximately $36.94 per ton for disaster-related debris through June 17, 2025
- Administrator Leonard to explore partnership with Community Foundation for reimbursing unreimbursed insurance costs for affected landowners
- Solid waste management board directed to develop a clear policy within six months for handling similar disaster situations in the future
- Staff to track all certified disaster-related debris for potential state disaster aid documentation
