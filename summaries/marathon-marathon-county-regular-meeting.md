## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Board of Supervisors held an adjourned organizational meeting where they approved committee appointments, elected Supervisors Hapa and Arstead to the Executive Committee, and unanimously approved a resolution reducing landfill tipping fees for disaster-related debris following severe tornado and flood damage in the Ringle area. The board suspended its rules to consider the tipping fee resolution directly from the Solid Waste Management Board due to the emergency nature of the situation.

## Key Discussions
### Committee Appointments
Chair Gibbs presented committee appointments developed with Vice Chair Boots, emphasizing goals of increasing diversity of perspectives, developing leadership opportunities for newer supervisors (34% in first or second term), and honoring supervisor preferences. Motion by Supervisor Cavelli, seconded by Supervisor Crims. The motion carried but was not unanimous. Supervisor Kratzke noted the system marked him absent but he wanted to be recorded as voting aye.

### Election of At-Large Executive Committee Members
Three supervisors were nominated: Supervisor Hapa (nominated by Supervisor Mask), Supervisor Leur (nominated by Supervisor Rosenberg, who praised her advocacy for 'smallest children and oldest elders'), and Supervisor Arstead (nominated by Supervisor Boots). Paper ballots were distributed for supervisors to select two. Supervisors Hapa and Arstead were elected to the Executive Committee.

### Consent Agenda
Items C10 through C14A were approved on motion by Supervisor Cavelli, seconded by Supervisor Drebeck. The motion carried unanimously.

### Resolution 24-26: Out of Home Placement Reserve Increase
Motion by Supervisor Morash, seconded by Supervisor Robinson. The motion carried but was not unanimous.

### Resolution 25-26: Social Services Budget Amendment
Motion by Supervisor P, seconded by Supervisor Robinson. The motion carried but was not unanimous.

### Resolution 26-26: Modification to Landfill Tipping Fees
Administrator Leonard and Solid Waste Director Hagen Busher presented the proposal to reduce tipping fees for disaster-related debris following severe weather that damaged approximately 44 homes in Ringle. The standard tipping fee of $65/ton would be reduced to $36.94/ton after the state waives $13.10 and Ringle waives $2.33, with the county reducing approximately $13 to cover costs only. Leonard noted the county won't meet the $670,743 threshold for state disaster aid but expects Ringle will. Supervisor Rosenberg requested and received permission for public comment. Residents Jessica Toasty and Matt Haybeck spoke, asking the county to fully waive fees and extend the timeline beyond 60 days. Multiple supervisors asked clarifying questions about fee calculations and coverage. The board first voted unanimously to suspend rules to consider the resolution directly from the Solid Waste Management Board, then approved the resolution unanimously on motion by Supervisor Arstead, seconded by Supervisor Morash.

## Public Comment
Two residents were granted special permission to speak on the landfill tipping fee resolution. Jessica Toasty emphasized that the state, DNR, and Ringle are waiving fees and asked Marathon County to do the same, noting private businesses have donated up to $40,000 in cleanup assistance. She requested a six-month extension. Matt Haybeck spoke on behalf of homeowners whose houses need demolition, noting many are still waiting for insurance adjusters and need more time for fee relief.

## Action Items
- Committee appointments approved for the term
- Supervisors Hapa and Arstead elected to Executive Committee as at-large members
- Resolution 24-26 approved increasing out of home placement reserve
- Resolution 25-26 approved amending social services budget
- Resolution 26-26 approved reducing landfill tipping fees to $36.94/ton for disaster-related debris through June 17, 2025
- Administrator Leonard to continue discussions with Community Foundation about reimbursing uninsured debris disposal costs
- Solid Waste Management Board to develop long-term policy on handling disaster-related waste within six months
- Staff to notify Supervisor Gao of future Solid Waste Board meetings
