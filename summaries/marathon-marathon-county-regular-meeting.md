## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Board of Supervisors held an adjourned organizational meeting where they approved committee appointments, elected two at-large executive committee members (Supervisors Hapa and Arstead), and unanimously passed a resolution to reduce landfill tipping fees for debris related to the recent severe weather and tornado damage in Ringle and other affected areas. The board suspended its rules to consider the emergency tipping fee resolution, which reduces fees from the standard $65/ton to approximately $36.94/ton for certified storm-related debris.

## Key Discussions
### Committee Appointments
Chair Gibbs presented committee appointments developed with Vice Chair Boots, emphasizing goals of increasing diversity of perspectives, developing leadership opportunities for newer supervisors (34% of the board is in first or second term), and honoring supervisor preferences. The appointments were approved by motion from Supervisor Cavelli, seconded by Supervisor Crims. The motion carried but was not unanimous. Supervisor Hendricks noted he was marked absent but didn't get the option to vote and wanted to be recorded as voting aye.

### Election of At-Large Executive Committee Members
Three supervisors were nominated for two at-large executive committee positions: Supervisor Hapa (nominated by Supervisor Mask), Supervisor Leur (nominated by Supervisor Rosenberg), and Supervisor Arstead (nominated by Vice Chair Boots). Supervisor Rosenberg explained she nominated Supervisor Leur because she has seen her advocate for the county's smallest children and oldest elders, bringing a voice to underrepresented populations. Paper ballots were distributed and counted, resulting in Supervisors Hapa and Arstead being elected to the executive committee.

### Consent Agenda
Items C10 through C14A were approved by motion from Supervisor Cavelli, seconded by Supervisor Drebeck. The motion carried unanimously.

### Out-of-Home Placement Reserve Increase (Resolution 24-26)
Resolution from Health and Human Services and HR Finance and Property committees was moved by Supervisor Morash and seconded by Supervisor Robinson. The motion carried but was not unanimous.

### Social Services Budget Amendment (Resolution 25-26)
Motion by Supervisor P, seconded by Supervisor Robinson. The motion carried but was not unanimous.

### Landfill Tipping Fee Modification (Resolution 26-26)
Administrator Leonard and Director Hagen Busher presented the emergency resolution from the Solid Waste Management Board responding to severe tornado and flood damage in Ringle and other areas. The proposal reduces tipping fees from the standard $65/ton to approximately $36.94/ton for certified storm debris, with the state waiving $13.10 and Ringle waiving $2.33 of their fees. Two residents, Jessica Toasty and Matt Haybeck, were granted special permission to speak, requesting the county wave fees entirely for affected residents. Multiple supervisors asked questions about the fee structure, timeline, and policy implications. Administrator Leonard noted the county will not meet the $670,743.18 threshold for state disaster aid but expects Ringle township to meet their damage threshold. The board first voted unanimously to suspend rules to consider the resolution, then voted unanimously to approve the resolution.

## Public Comment
Special permission was granted for two residents to speak on the tipping fee resolution. Jessica Toasty spoke for three minutes, requesting Marathon County wave its fees entirely for disaster debris, noting the state, DNR, and Ringle are waiving their fees. She mentioned one business has spent $40,000 on cleanup efforts and multiple businesses have donated time and resources. She requested a six-month timeline for fee waivers. Matt Haybeck spoke on behalf of approximately 44 affected homeowners, noting many are still waiting for insurance adjusters and requesting extended relief beyond the proposed timeline due to uncertainty about insurance coverage.

## Action Items
- Committee appointments approved as presented by Chair Gibbs and Vice Chair Boots
- Supervisors Hapa and Arstead elected to at-large executive committee positions
- Resolution 24-26 approving out-of-home placement reserve increase approved
- Resolution 25-26 amending social services budget approved
- Resolution 26-26 modifying landfill tipping fees for storm debris approved through June 17, 2025
- Administrator Leonard to work with Community Foundation on potential reimbursement mechanism for unreimbursed insurance costs
- Solid Waste Management Board to develop long-term policy on handling disaster-related debris within six months
- Staff to track certified storm-related debris and apply reduced rates accordingly
