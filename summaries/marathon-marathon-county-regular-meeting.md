## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Board of Supervisors held an adjourned organizational meeting where they approved committee appointments, elected two at-large members to the executive committee (Supervisors Hapa and Arstead), and unanimously approved a resolution to reduce landfill tipping fees for disaster-related debris following severe tornado and flood damage in the Ringle area. The board also heard public comment from affected residents requesting additional fee relief.

## Key Discussions
### Committee appointments
Chair Gibbs presented committee appointments developed with Vice Chair Boots, emphasizing goals of increasing diversity of perspective, developing leadership among newer supervisors (noting 34% are in first or second terms), and honoring supervisor preferences. The appointments included both county board chair and administrator appointments. Motion by Supervisor Cavelli, seconded by Supervisor Crims. The motion carried but was not unanimous. Supervisor Mask noted the system marked him absent but didn't allow him to vote, clarifying he was an 'I' on the vote.

### Election of at-large executive committee members
Three supervisors were nominated: Supervisor Hapa (nominated by Supervisor Mask), Supervisor Leur (nominated by Supervisor Rosenberg, who cited her advocacy for 'smallest children and oldest elders'), and Supervisor Arstead (nominated by Supervisor Boots). Paper ballots were distributed for supervisors to select two names. Supervisors Hapa and Arstead were elected to the executive committee.

### Consent agenda
Items C10 through C14A were moved by Supervisor Cavelli and seconded by Supervisor Drebeck. The motion carried unanimously by voice vote.

### Resolution 24-26: Out of home placement reserve increase
Motion by Supervisor Morash, seconded by Supervisor Robinson. No discussion occurred. The motion carried but was not unanimous.

### Resolution 25-26: Amend social services budget
Motion by Supervisor P, seconded by Supervisor Robinson. No discussion occurred. The motion carried but was not unanimous.

### Resolution 26-26: Modification to landfill tipping fees
Administrator Leonard and Director Hagen Busher presented the proposal from the Solid Waste Management Board to reduce tipping fees for disaster-related debris following severe weather that particularly impacted Ringle. The state is waiving its $13.10 fee, Ringle is waiving its $2.33 fee, and the county proposed reducing to $36.94 per ton (commercial rate minus state and local fees) through June 17. About 400 tons of debris has been processed. Public comment was allowed from Jessica Toasty and Matt Haybeck, who requested the county fully waive fees for affected residents. Multiple supervisors asked clarifying questions about fee calculations, coverage areas, and future policy. The board first voted unanimously to suspend rules to consider the resolution without standing committee review, then voted unanimously to approve Resolution 26-26.

## Public Comment
Two residents were granted special dispensation to speak on the landfill tipping fee resolution. Jessica Toasty requested Marathon County wave its fees entirely for six months, noting the state, DNR, and Ringle are waiving their fees, and that local businesses have donated up to $40,000 in cleanup assistance. She emphasized residents are paying deductibles and uncovered expenses. Matt Haybeck spoke on behalf of homeowners facing demolition, noting some insurance adjusters haven't yet arrived and requesting more time and relief for the approximately 44 affected homes.

## Action Items
- Committee appointments approved as presented by Chair Gibbs and Vice Chair Boots
- Supervisors Hapa and Arstead elected to at-large executive committee positions
- Resolution 24-26 approved for out of home placement reserve increase
- Resolution 25-26 approved to amend social services budget
- Resolution 26-26 approved to reduce landfill tipping fees for disaster-related debris through June 17
- Administrator Leonard to continue discussions with Community Foundation regarding potential reimbursement mechanism for unreimbursed insurance costs
- Solid Waste Management Board to develop long-term policy on handling disaster-related tipping fees within six months
- Supervisor Gao requested notification of future solid waste board meetings to attend
