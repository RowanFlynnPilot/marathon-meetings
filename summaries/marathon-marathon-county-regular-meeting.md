## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Board approved committee appointments for the new term, elected Supervisors Hapa and Arstead to the Executive Committee at-large positions, and unanimously passed a resolution reducing landfill tipping fees for storm debris following severe weather that damaged the Ringle area. The board suspended its rules to consider the emergency tipping fee resolution directly from the Solid Waste Management Board.

## Key Discussions
### Committee Appointments
Chair Gibbs presented committee appointments developed with Vice Chair Boots, emphasizing goals of increasing diversity of perspective, developing leadership among newer supervisors (34% in first or second term), and respecting supervisor preferences. Motion by Supervisor Cavelli, seconded by Supervisor Crims. The motion carried but was not unanimous.

### Election of At-Large Executive Committee Members
Three supervisors were nominated: Supervisor Hapa (nominated by Supervisor Mask), Supervisor Leur (nominated by Supervisor Rosenberg, who cited her advocacy for children and elders), and Supervisor Arstead (nominated by Supervisor Boots). After paper ballot voting, Supervisors Hapa and Arstead were elected to the Executive Committee.

### Consent Agenda
Items C10 through C14A were approved on motion by Supervisor Cavelli, seconded by Supervisor Drebeck. The motion carried unanimously.

### Resolution 24-26: Out of Home Placement Reserve Increase
Motion by Supervisor Morash, seconded by Supervisor Robinson. The motion carried but was not unanimous.

### Resolution 25-26: Social Services Budget Amendment
Motion by Supervisor P, seconded by Supervisor Robinson. The motion carried but was not unanimous.

### Resolution 26-26: Modification to Landfill Tipping Fees
Administrator Leonard and Director Hagen Busher presented a proposal to reduce tipping fees for storm debris following severe weather that caused significant damage in Ringle, Kronenwetter, and other areas. The state waived $13.10/ton and Ringle waived $2.33/ton. The county proposed reducing fees to $36.94/ton (from $65 standard rate) to cover costs. Two residents, Jessica Toasty and Matt Haybeck, were granted special permission to speak and requested the county waive fees entirely, citing that approximately 44 homes need demolition and insurance adjusters haven't yet visited many properties. After extensive discussion about enterprise fund operations, precedent concerns, and potential community foundation partnership for unreimbursed costs, the board unanimously suspended rules to consider the resolution and then passed it unanimously.

## Public Comment
No formal public comment period was held, but Supervisor Rosenberg requested special dispensation for two residents to speak on the tipping fee resolution. Jessica Toasty spoke for three minutes, asking the county to waive fees entirely for six months, noting the state, DNR, and Ringle had waived their fees and that private businesses had donated up to $40,000 in cleanup efforts. Matt Haybeck spoke on behalf of residents whose homes need demolition, requesting fee waivers for underinsured homeowners and more time since many insurance adjusters haven't yet arrived.

## Action Items
- Committee appointments approved for the new term
- Supervisors Hapa and Arstead elected to Executive Committee at-large positions
- Resolution 24-26 approved increasing out of home placement reserve
- Resolution 25-26 approved amending social services budget
- Resolution 26-26 approved reducing landfill tipping fees to $36.94/ton for certified storm debris through June 17, 2025
- Administrator Leonard to work with community foundation on mechanism for reimbursing unreimbursed insurance costs for landowners
- Solid waste management board to develop clear policy within six months for handling future disaster debris situations
- Staff to continue tracking certified storm debris and monitor if extension beyond 60 days is needed
