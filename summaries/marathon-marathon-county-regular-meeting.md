## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Board approved committee appointments for the new term, elected Supervisors Hapa and Arstead to the executive committee, and unanimously approved a resolution to reduce landfill tipping fees for storm debris following the recent tornado and flooding disaster. The board heard from affected Ringle residents requesting additional fee relief beyond the proposed reduction.

## Key Discussions
### Committee appointments
Chair Gibbs presented committee appointments developed with Vice Chair Boots, emphasizing goals of increasing diverse perspectives, developing new supervisors as leaders, and honoring supervisor preferences. Over 34% of the board is in its first or second term. Supervisor Cavelli moved and Supervisor Crims seconded approval of both county board chair and administrator appointments. The motion carried but was not unanimous.

### Election of at-large executive committee members
Three supervisors were nominated for two at-large executive committee positions: Supervisor Hapa (nominated by Supervisor Mask), Supervisor Leur (nominated by Supervisor Rosenberg), and Supervisor Arstead (nominated by Vice Chair Boots). Supervisor Rosenberg spoke in support of Supervisor Leur, citing her advocacy for children and elders. Paper ballots were distributed and counted. Supervisors Hapa and Arstead were elected to the executive committee.

### Resolution 26-26: Modification to landfill tipping fees
Administrator Leonard and Solid Waste Director Hagen Busher presented a proposal to reduce tipping fees for disaster-related debris following the recent tornado and flooding. The standard $65/ton rate would be reduced to $36.94/ton for certified storm debris, with the state waiving $13.10, Ringle waiving $2.33, and the county reducing its portion. The board suspended rules unanimously to consider the resolution. Supervisor Rosenberg requested public comment, and two Ringle residents spoke: Jessica Tolesie asked the county to waive fees entirely for six months like other entities, and Matt Haybeck requested relief for underinsured homeowners facing demolition costs. Multiple supervisors asked questions about eligibility, timeline, and costs. Administrator Leonard noted ongoing discussions with the Community Foundation about covering unreimbursed out-of-pocket costs. The resolution passed unanimously.

## Public Comment
Two Ringle residents were granted special dispensation to speak on the landfill tipping fee resolution. Jessica Tolesie requested Marathon County waive fees entirely for six months, noting the state, DNR, and Ringle are waiving their portions, and that private businesses have donated $40,000+ in cleanup efforts. Matt Haybeck spoke on behalf of affected homeowners, requesting fee relief for underinsured residents whose insurance adjusters haven't yet arrived, noting approximately 44 homes were affected.

## Action Items
- Committee appointments approved for the new term
- Supervisors Hapa and Arstead elected to executive committee at-large positions
- Resolution 24-26 approving out of home placement reserve increase approved (not unanimous)
- Resolution 25-26 amending social services budget approved (not unanimous)
- Resolution 26-26 reducing landfill tipping fees for storm debris to $36.94/ton through June 17th approved unanimously
- Administrator to continue discussions with Community Foundation on reimbursing unreimbursed out-of-pocket disaster costs
- Solid waste management board to develop clear policy on handling similar disaster situations within six months
- Board may revisit tipping fee reduction timeline at May meeting if extension needed
