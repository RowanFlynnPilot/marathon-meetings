## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Board of Supervisors held an adjourned organizational meeting where they approved committee appointments, elected Supervisors Hapa and Arstead to the Executive Committee at-large positions, and unanimously passed a resolution to reduce landfill tipping fees for severe weather debris following the recent tornado and flooding that devastated the Ringle area. The board also approved consent agenda items and budget amendments for social services and out-of-home placement reserves.

## Key Discussions
### Committee appointments
Chair Gibbs presented committee appointments developed with Vice Chair Boots, emphasizing goals of increasing diverse perspectives, developing supervisor leadership, and honoring supervisor preferences. Gibbs noted that 34% of the board is in first or second terms and most supervisors received their first or second choice. Motion by Supervisor Cavelli, seconded by Supervisor Crims. The motion carried but was not unanimous.

### Election of at-large Executive Committee members
Three supervisors were nominated: Supervisor Hapa (nominated by Supervisor Mask), Supervisor Leur (nominated by Supervisor Rosenberg), and Supervisor Arstead (nominated by Vice Chair Boots). Supervisor Rosenberg spoke in support of Leur, citing her advocacy for children and elders. Paper ballots were distributed for the vote. Supervisors Hapa and Arstead were elected to the Executive Committee at-large positions.

### Resolution 26-26: Modification to landfill tipping fees
County Administrator Leonard and Solid Waste Director Hagen Busher presented a proposal from the Solid Waste Management Board to reduce tipping fees for severe weather debris. The state waived $13.10/ton, Ringle waived $2.33/ton, and the county proposed reducing fees to $36.94/ton (commercial rate minus state and local waivers) to cover costs. Leonard noted the county won't meet the $670,743 damage threshold for state disaster aid but expects Ringle will. Public comment was allowed by special dispensation for Jessica Toasty and Matt Haybeck, who requested Marathon County waive fees entirely like other entities. Multiple supervisors discussed concerns about timing, insurance delays, and future policy. The board suspended rules unanimously to bring the resolution forward, then approved Resolution 26-26 unanimously.

### Out-of-home placement reserve increase
Resolution 24-26 was presented from Health and Human Services and HR Finance and Property Committees. Motion by Supervisor Morash, seconded by Supervisor Robinson. The motion carried but was not unanimous.

### Social services budget amendment
Resolution 25-26 to amend the 2026 social services budget was presented. Motion by Supervisor P, seconded by Supervisor Robinson. The motion carried but was not unanimous.

## Public Comment
Two residents were granted special dispensation to speak on the landfill tipping fee resolution. Jessica Toasty urged the county to waive fees entirely, noting the state, DNR, and Ringle are waiving their fees, and that local businesses have donated up to $40,000 in cleanup efforts. She requested a six-month waiver period. Matt Haybeck spoke on behalf of residents with demolished homes, requesting fee waivers for underinsured homeowners and noting many insurance adjusters haven't yet arrived at the 44 affected homes.

## Action Items
- Committee appointments approved for the term
- Supervisors Hapa and Arstead elected to Executive Committee at-large positions
- Resolution 24-26 approved for out-of-home placement reserve increase
- Resolution 25-26 approved for social services budget amendment
- Resolution 26-26 approved reducing landfill tipping fees to $36.94/ton for certified severe weather debris through June 17th
- Administrator Leonard to work with Community Foundation on potential reimbursement mechanism for uninsured debris disposal costs
- Solid Waste Management Board directed to develop clear policy on handling similar disaster situations within six months
