## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Board approved committee appointments for the new term, elected Supervisors Hapa and Arstead to the executive committee, and unanimously approved a resolution reducing landfill tipping fees for storm debris following the recent tornado and flooding disaster. The fee reduction from $65 to approximately $36.94 per ton will help residents affected by the severe weather that impacted Ringle and other communities.

## Key Discussions
### Committee Appointments
Chair Gibbs presented committee appointments developed with Vice Chair Boots, emphasizing goals of increasing diverse perspectives, developing supervisor leadership, and respecting preferences. He noted 34% of the board is in first or second terms. Motion by Supervisor Cavelli, seconded by Supervisor Crims. The motion carried but was not unanimous.

### Election of At-Large Executive Committee Members
Three supervisors were nominated: Supervisor Hapa (nominated by Supervisor Mask), Supervisor Leur (nominated by Supervisor Rosenberg who praised her advocacy for children and elders), and Supervisor Arstead (nominated by Vice Chair Boots). After paper ballot voting, Supervisors Hapa and Arstead were elected to the executive committee.

### Consent Agenda
Items C10 through C14A were approved on consent. Motion by Supervisor Cavelli, seconded by Supervisor Drebeck. Motion carried unanimously.

### Resolution 24-26: Out of Home Placement Reserve Increase
Motion by Supervisor Morash, seconded by Supervisor Robinson. The motion carried but was not unanimous with no discussion.

### Resolution 26-26: Landfill Tipping Fee Modification
Administrator Leonard and Solid Waste Director Hagen Busher presented the proposal to reduce tipping fees for disaster debris from $65 to approximately $36.94 per ton. The state is waiving $13.10 and Ringle is waiving $2.33. Two residents, Jessica Toasty and Matt Haybeck, were granted special dispensation to speak, requesting the county waive fees entirely for affected residents. Extensive discussion followed regarding insurance reimbursement, timeline extensions, and future policy development. The board first voted unanimously to suspend rules to consider the resolution, then approved it unanimously.

## Public Comment
Two residents were granted special permission to address the board regarding the landfill fee resolution. Jessica Toasty spoke on behalf of affected residents, noting that the state, DNR, and Ringle are waiving fees and asking Marathon County to do the same. She mentioned one business has spent $40,000 on hauling expenses and requested fee waivers for six months. Matt Haybeck spoke for homeowners facing demolition, noting many are still waiting for insurance adjusters and requested more time for fee waivers.

## Action Items
- Committee appointments approved for the new term
- Supervisors Hapa and Arstead elected to executive committee at-large positions
- Landfill tipping fees reduced to $36.94 per ton for certified storm debris through June 17, 2025
- Administrator Leonard to work with Community Foundation on potential reimbursement mechanism for unreimbursed insurance costs
- Solid waste management board to develop long-term disaster debris fee policy within six months
- Staff to continue tracking certified storm debris for potential state disaster aid documentation
