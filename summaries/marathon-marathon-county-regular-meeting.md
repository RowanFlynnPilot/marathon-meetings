## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Board held its adjourned organizational meeting, approving committee appointments and electing Supervisors Hapa and Arstead to at-large executive committee positions. The board unanimously approved a resolution reducing landfill tipping fees for disaster-related debris following the April tornado and flooding that devastated parts of Ringle and other communities, with fees reduced from $65/ton to approximately $36.94/ton for affected residents.

## Key Discussions
### Committee Appointments
Chair Gibbs presented committee appointments developed with Vice Chair Boots, emphasizing goals of increasing perspective diversity, developing new supervisors as leaders, and honoring supervisor preferences. He noted 34% of the board is in its first or second term. The motion by Supervisor Cavelli, seconded by Supervisor Crims, was carried but not unanimously. Supervisor Nisa noted he was marked absent but did not receive the option to vote, indicating he was an 'I' on the motion.

### Election of At-Large Executive Committee Members
Three supervisors were nominated: Supervisor Hapa (nominated by Supervisor Mask), Supervisor Leur (nominated by Supervisor Rosenberg), and Supervisor Arstead (nominated by Vice Chair Boots). Supervisor Rosenberg spoke in support of Supervisor Leur, citing her advocacy for the county's youngest and oldest residents. Paper ballots were distributed and supervisors selected two candidates. Supervisors Hapa and Arstead were elected to the executive committee.

### Consent Agenda
Items C10 through C14A were approved on motion by Supervisor Cavelli, seconded by Supervisor Drebeck. The motion carried unanimously by voice vote.

### Resolution 24-26: Out of Home Placement Reserve Increase
Motion by Supervisor Morash, seconded by Supervisor Robinson. The resolution was carried but not unanimously, with no discussion recorded.

### Resolution 25-26: Social Services Budget Amendment
Motion by Supervisor P, seconded by Supervisor Robinson. The resolution was carried but not unanimously, with no discussion recorded.

### Resolution 26-26: Landfill Tipping Fee Modification for Disaster Relief
Administrator Leonard and Director Hagen Busher presented this resolution from the Solid Waste Management Board in response to the April tornado and flooding. The proposal reduces tipping fees from $65/ton to approximately $36.94/ton for certified disaster debris, with the state waiving $13.10 and the Town of Ringle waiving $2.33. The board suspended rules unanimously to consider the resolution since it bypassed standing committees. Public speakers Jessica Tolesie and Matt Haybeck requested the county waive fees entirely for affected residents, noting many are underinsured and waiting on insurance adjusters. Supervisor Gao questioned the fee calculations and Supervisor Rosenberg requested continued discussion by the Solid Waste Board. The resolution was approved unanimously on motion by Supervisor Arstead, seconded by Supervisor Morash.

## Public Comment
Two residents were granted special dispensation to speak on the landfill tipping fee resolution. Jessica Tolesie advocated for Marathon County to completely waive fees, noting the state, DNR, and Town of Ringle have all waived their portions, and that local businesses have donated up to $40,000 in cleanup efforts. She requested a six-month fee waiver. Matt Haybeck spoke on behalf of homeowners whose houses must be demolished, noting 44 homes are affected and some insurance adjusters haven't arrived yet, requesting more time for fee relief.

## Action Items
- Committee appointments approved as presented by Chair Gibbs and Vice Chair Boots
- Supervisors Hapa and Arstead elected to at-large executive committee positions
- Resolution 26-26 approved reducing landfill tipping fees to $36.94/ton for certified disaster debris through June 17, 2025
- Administrator Leonard to continue discussions with Community Foundation regarding reimbursement mechanism for uninsured debris disposal costs
- Solid Waste Management Board directed to develop long-term policy on disaster-related fee reductions within six months
- Vice chairs directed to shepherd strategic plan updates within their committees
