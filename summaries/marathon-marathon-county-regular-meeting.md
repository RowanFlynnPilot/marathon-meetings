## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Board of Supervisors held an adjourned organizational meeting where they approved committee appointments, elected Supervisors Hapa and Arstead to the Executive Committee, and unanimously passed a resolution reducing landfill tipping fees for disaster debris following severe tornado and flooding damage in the Ringle area. The board suspended rules to address the emergency tipping fee measure directly from the Solid Waste Management Board.

## Key Discussions
### Committee Appointments
Chair Gibbs delivered remarks thanking staff and Vice Chair Boots for assistance in developing committee appointments, noting 34% of the board is in its first or second term. He emphasized that committee appointments were guided by goals including increasing perspective representation and supervisor development. The motion by Supervisor Cavelli, seconded by Supervisor Crims, was carried but not unanimous. Supervisor Dobeck noted she was marked absent but was not given the option to vote.

### Election of At-Large Executive Committee Members
Three supervisors were nominated: Supervisor Hapa (nominated by Supervisor Mask), Supervisor Leur (nominated by Supervisor Rosenberg), and Supervisor Arstead (nominated by Supervisor Boots). Supervisor Rosenberg spoke in support of Supervisor Leur, citing her advocacy for the county's children and elders. Paper ballots were distributed and supervisors selected two names. Supervisors Hapa and Arstead were elected to the Executive Committee.

### Consent Agenda (Items C10-C14A)
Motion by Supervisor Cavelli, seconded by Supervisor Drebeck. Passed unanimously with no discussion.

### Resolution 24-26: Out of Home Placement Reserve Increase
Motion by Supervisor Morash, seconded by Supervisor Robinson. The resolution was carried but not unanimous with no discussion.

### Resolution 25-26: Social Services Budget Amendment
Motion by Supervisor P, seconded by Supervisor Robinson. The resolution was carried but not unanimous with no discussion.

### Resolution 26-26: Modification to Landfill Tipping Fees
Administrator Leonard and Solid Waste Director Hagen Busher presented the resolution addressing debris from severe tornado and flooding damage, particularly in Ringle. The proposal reduces tipping fees from $65/ton standard rate to approximately $36.94/ton by removing the state fee ($13.10), town of Ringle fee ($2.33), and county portion. Two residents, Jessica Toasty and Matt Haybeck, were granted special permission to speak and requested the county waive fees entirely for affected residents. Administrator Leonard mentioned potential partnership with Community Foundation for unreimbursed costs. The board first voted unanimously to suspend rules to bring the resolution directly from Solid Waste Management Board, then voted unanimously to approve the resolution with motion by Supervisor Arstead and second by Supervisor Morash.

## Public Comment
Two residents were granted special dispensation to speak on the landfill tipping fee resolution. Jessica Toasty requested Marathon County waive fees entirely for six months, noting the state, DNR, and Ringle are waiving their fees and that private businesses have donated significantly to cleanup efforts. Matt Haybeck spoke on behalf of residents whose homes need demolition, requesting fee relief for underinsured homeowners and noting some insurance adjusters have not yet arrived at affected properties.

## Action Items
- Committee appointments approved as presented by Chair Gibbs and Vice Chair Boots
- Supervisors Hapa and Arstead elected to Executive Committee as at-large members
- Resolution 26-26 approved reducing landfill tipping fees for disaster debris through June 17, 2025
- Administrator Leonard to work with Community Foundation on mechanism for reimbursing unreimbursed insurance costs for disaster victims
- Solid Waste Management Board directed to develop clear policy on handling similar disaster situations within six months
- Staff to track disaster-related waste through certification forms from haulers
