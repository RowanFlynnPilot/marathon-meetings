## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Board held an organizational meeting approving committee appointments, electing Supervisors Hapa and Arstead to the executive committee, and unanimously passing a resolution to reduce landfill tipping fees for storm debris cleanup following the April severe weather emergency. The board heard emotional testimony from Ringle residents requesting additional fee relief for tornado and flood damage cleanup.

## Key Discussions
### Committee appointments
Chair Gibbs outlined goals for committee appointments including increasing diversity of perspectives, developing leadership among newer supervisors (34% in first or second term), and honoring supervisor preferences. Vice Chair Boots assisted in developing the appointments. The motion by Supervisor Cavelli, seconded by Supervisor Crims, was carried but not unanimous.

### Election of at-large executive committee members
Three supervisors were nominated: Supervisor Hapa (nominated by Supervisor Mask), Supervisor Leur (nominated by Supervisor Rosenberg), and Supervisor Arstead (nominated by Supervisor Boots). Supervisor Rosenberg spoke in support of Supervisor Leur, citing her advocacy for children and elders. Paper ballots were distributed and supervisors selected two names. Supervisors Hapa and Arstead were elected to the executive committee.

### Consent agenda items C10-C14A
Motion by Supervisor Cavelli, seconded by Supervisor Drebeck. Passed unanimously.

### Resolution 24-26: Out of home placement reserve increase
Motion by Supervisor Morash, seconded by Supervisor Robinson. Motion carried but was not unanimous.

### Resolution 25-26: Social services budget amendment
Motion by Supervisor P, seconded by Supervisor Robinson. Motion carried but was not unanimous.

### Resolution 26-26: Modification to landfill tipping fees
County Administrator Leonard and Solid Waste Director Hagen Busher presented the proposal from the Solid Waste Management Board following severe weather damage in Ringle and surrounding areas. The resolution reduces tipping fees from $65/ton to approximately $36.94/ton for certified storm debris by waiving state fees ($13.10), town fees ($2.33), and reducing to the commercial contract rate. Supervisor Rosenberg requested public comment be allowed. Jessica Toasty and Matt Haybeck spoke, asking the county to fully waive fees given that the state, DNR, and Ringle are waiving their portions. Leonard noted the county will likely not meet the $670,743 damage threshold for state disaster aid but expects Ringle will qualify. The board suspended rules to consider the resolution (passed unanimously), then approved Resolution 26-26 unanimously. Administrator Leonard indicated discussions would continue with the Community Foundation regarding reimbursement mechanisms for landowners.

## Public Comment
Two residents were granted special permission to speak on the landfill tipping fee resolution. Jessica Toasty asked the county to fully waive fees for six months, noting the state, DNR, and Ringle have waived their portions, and describing how businesses have donated up to $40,000 in cleanup costs. Matt Haybeck spoke on behalf of residents whose homes must be demolished, requesting fee waivers for underinsured homeowners and noting some insurance adjusters haven't yet arrived at affected properties.

## Action Items
- Committee appointments approved as presented by Chair Gibbs and Vice Chair Boots
- Supervisors Hapa and Arstead elected to executive committee at-large positions
- Resolution 26-26 approved reducing landfill tipping fees for storm debris to approximately $36.94/ton through June 17, 2025
- Administrator Leonard to continue discussions with Community Foundation regarding reimbursement mechanism for landowner out-of-pocket costs
- Solid Waste Management Board directed to develop clear policy within six months for handling similar disaster situations in the future
- Staff to track certified storm debris separately for potential state disaster aid documentation
