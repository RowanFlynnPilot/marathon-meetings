## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Board held an adjourned organizational meeting where committee appointments were approved, two at-large executive committee members were elected (Supervisors Hapa and Arstead), and a resolution was passed unanimously to reduce landfill tipping fees for storm debris following severe weather that devastated parts of the county, particularly the town of Ringle. The board heard public comment from flood-affected residents requesting fee waivers.

## Key Discussions
### Committee appointments
Chair Gibbs presented committee appointments developed with Vice Chair Boots, emphasizing goals of increasing diverse perspectives, developing new supervisors as leaders, and honoring supervisor preferences. Over 34% of the board is in their first or second term. The motion by Supervisor Cavelli, seconded by Supervisor Crims, was carried but not unanimous. Both county board chair and administrator appointments were included in one motion.

### Election of at-large executive committee members
Three supervisors were nominated: Supervisor Hapa (nominated by Supervisor Mask), Supervisor Leur (nominated by Supervisor Rosenberg), and Supervisor Arstead (nominated by Vice Chair Boots). Supervisor Rosenberg explained she nominated Supervisor Leur for advocating for the county's youngest children and oldest elders. Paper ballots were distributed and supervisors selected two names. Supervisors Hapa and Arstead were elected to the executive committee.

### Consent agenda
Items C10 through C14A were approved on motion by Supervisor Cavelli, seconded by Supervisor Drebeck. The motion carried unanimously.

### Resolution 24-26: Out of home placement reserve increase
Motion by Supervisor Morash, seconded by Supervisor Robinson. The motion carried but was not unanimous.

### Resolution 25-26: Social services budget amendment
Motion by Supervisor P, seconded by Supervisor Robinson. The motion carried but was not unanimous.

### Resolution 26-26: Landfill tipping fees modification
Administrator Leonard and Solid Waste Director Hagen Busher presented a proposal to reduce tipping fees for storm debris following severe weather that particularly impacted Ringle. The state waived $13.10 per ton, Ringle waived $2.33, and the county proposed reducing fees to $36.94 per ton (essentially covering costs). About 400 tons of debris was already processed. Two residents, Jessica Toasty and Matt Haybeck, were granted special permission to speak, requesting the county waive fees entirely for affected residents. Multiple supervisors asked questions about fee structures, timelines, and policy development. The board first voted unanimously to suspend rules to consider the resolution without standing committee review, then approved Resolution 26-26 unanimously on motion by Supervisor Arstead, seconded by Supervisor Morash.

## Public Comment
Two residents were granted special dispensation to speak on the tipping fee resolution. Jessica Toasty requested the county waive fees entirely, noting the state, DNR, and Ringle were waiving their portions, and asked for a six-month extension rather than 60 days. She stated Marathon County provided limited financial assistance and that one business alone had $40,000 in hauling expenses helping the community. Matt Haybeck spoke on behalf of residents whose homes must be demolished, noting some are underinsured and insurance adjusters haven't arrived yet for some of the approximately 44 affected homes.

## Action Items
- Committee appointments approved as presented by Chair Gibbs
- Supervisors Hapa and Arstead elected to at-large executive committee positions
- Resolution 24-26 approved for out of home placement reserve increase
- Resolution 25-26 approved for social services budget amendment
- Resolution 26-26 approved reducing landfill tipping fees to approximately $36.94 per ton for certified storm debris through June 17th
- Administrator to work with Community Foundation on potential reimbursement mechanism for uninsured debris disposal costs
- Solid waste management board to develop clear policy within six months for handling similar situations in the future
- Supervisors to be notified of future solid waste board meetings on this topic
