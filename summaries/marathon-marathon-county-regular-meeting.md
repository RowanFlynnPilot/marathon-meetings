## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Board held its organizational meeting, approving committee appointments and electing Supervisors Hapa and Arstead to the executive committee. The board unanimously approved a resolution to reduce landfill tipping fees for disaster-related debris following severe weather that impacted Ringle and other communities, with the county waiving approximately $13 per ton to bring fees down to $36.94 for certified storm debris.

## Key Discussions
### Committee Appointments
Chair Gibbs outlined goals for committee appointments including increasing diversity of perspective, allowing supervisors to grow as leaders, and noting that over 34% of the board is in their first or second term. The appointments were developed with Vice Chair Boots. Motion by Supervisor Cavelli, seconded by Supervisor Crims. The motion carried but was not unanimous.

### Election of At-Large Executive Committee Members
Three supervisors were nominated: Supervisor Hapa (nominated by Supervisor Mask), Supervisor Leur (nominated by Supervisor Rosenberg, who praised her advocacy for children and elders), and Supervisor Arstead (nominated by Supervisor Boots). Following paper ballot voting, Supervisors Hapa and Arstead were elected to the executive committee.

### Consent Agenda
Items C10 through C14A were approved. Motion by Supervisor Cavelli, seconded by Supervisor Drebeck. The motion carried unanimously.

### Resolution 24-26 - Out of Home Placement Reserve Increase
Motion by Supervisor Morash, seconded by Supervisor Robinson. The motion carried but was not unanimous.

### Resolution 25-26 - Social Services Budget Amendment
Motion by Supervisor P [name unclear], seconded by Supervisor Robinson. The motion carried but was not unanimous.

### Resolution 26-26 - Landfill Tipping Fees Modification
Administrator Leonard and Director Hagen Busher presented this resolution from the Solid Waste Management Board responding to severe weather that impacted Ringle and surrounding areas. The proposal reduces tipping fees from $65/ton to approximately $36.94 for certified disaster debris by waiving the state fee ($13.10), town of Ringle fee ($2.33), and county's portion. Two members of the public, Jessica Toasty and Matt Haybeck, were granted special permission to speak, requesting the county wave fees entirely for affected residents. Supervisor Rosenberg requested continued discussion. The board first voted unanimously to suspend rules to consider the resolution directly, then approved the resolution unanimously. Motion by Supervisor Arstead, seconded by Supervisor Morash.

## Public Comment
Two members of the public were granted special dispensation to speak on the landfill tipping fee resolution. Jessica Toasty urged the county to wave fees entirely, noting the state, DNR, and Ringle are waving their fees, and that private businesses have donated significant resources. She stated Marathon County had not provided much financial assistance and requested a six-month fee waiver. Matt Haybeck spoke on behalf of homeowners facing demolition, noting 44 homes affected and many still waiting for insurance adjusters, requesting more time and relief from fees.

## Action Items
- Landfill tipping fees reduced to approximately $36.94 per ton for certified disaster-related debris through June 17, 2025
- Administrator Leonard to work with community foundation on potential reimbursement mechanism for unreimbursed insurance costs
- Solid waste management board to develop clear policy on handling similar disaster situations within six months
- Supervisors Hapa and Arstead elected to executive committee
- Committee appointments approved as presented by Chair Gibbs and Vice Chair Boots
