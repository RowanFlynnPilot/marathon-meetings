## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Board of Supervisors held an adjourned organizational meeting where they approved committee appointments, elected Supervisors Hapa and Arstead to at-large executive committee positions, and unanimously approved a resolution reducing landfill tipping fees for storm debris from the recent severe weather disaster affecting Ringle and other communities. The board also approved budget amendments for out-of-home placement reserves and social services.

## Key Discussions
### Committee Appointments
Chair Gibbs presented committee appointments developed with Vice Chair Boots, emphasizing goals of increasing diversity of perspectives, developing new supervisors as leaders, and honoring supervisor preferences. He noted that over 34% of the board is in their first or second term and most supervisors received their first or second choice assignments. The motion by Supervisor Cavelli, seconded by Supervisor Crims, was carried but not unanimously. Supervisor Siebert noted he was marked absent but was not given the option to vote, indicating he would have voted aye.

### Election of At-Large Executive Committee Members
Three supervisors were nominated for two at-large executive committee positions: Supervisor Hapa (nominated by Supervisor Mask), Supervisor Leur (nominated by Supervisor Rosenberg), and Supervisor Arstead (nominated by Vice Chair Boots). Supervisor Rosenberg spoke in favor of Supervisor Leur, stating she had seen Leur advocate for the county's smallest children and oldest elders. Paper ballots were distributed and counted. Supervisors Hapa and Arstead were elected to the executive committee.

### Consent Agenda
Items C10 through C14A were approved on the consent agenda. Motion by Supervisor Cavelli, seconded by Supervisor Drebeck. The motion carried unanimously.

### Resolution 24-26: Out-of-Home Placement Reserve Increase
Resolution from Health and Human Services Committee and Human Resources and Finance and Property Committee. Motion by Supervisor Morash, seconded by Supervisor Robinson. The motion carried but was not unanimous.

### Resolution 25-26: Social Services Budget Amendment
Motion by Supervisor P, seconded by Supervisor Robinson. The motion carried but was not unanimous.

### Resolution 26-26: Landfill Tipping Fee Modification
Administrator Leonard and Solid Waste Director Hagen Busher presented a proposal to reduce tipping fees for storm debris following severe weather that impacted Ringle and other communities. The standard tipping fee of $65/ton would be reduced to approximately $36.94/ton for certified storm debris, with the state waiving $13.10 and Ringle waiving $2.33. Two residents, Jessica Toasty and Matt Haybeck, were granted special permission to address the board, both asking the county to waive fees entirely for affected residents. Director Hagen Busher noted that Brown County and Dane County only dropped the state fee, and warned about FEMA reimbursement issues if fees are fully waived. The board voted unanimously to suspend rules to consider the resolution, then voted unanimously to approve Resolution 26-26. Administrator Leonard indicated ongoing discussions with the community foundation about additional relief options.

## Public Comment
Two residents were granted special dispensation to speak on the landfill tipping fee resolution at the request of Supervisor Rosenberg. Jessica Toasty urged the county to waive fees entirely, noting the state, DNR, and Ringle are waiving their fees and that Marathon County had not provided much financial assistance. She mentioned businesses have donated up to $40,000 in cleanup efforts and requested the waiver extend for six months. Matt Haybeck spoke on behalf of residents with homes requiring demolition, noting some insurance adjusters haven't arrived yet and requesting more time for the fee waiver.

## Action Items
- Committee appointments approved as presented by Chair Gibbs and Vice Chair Boots
- Supervisors Hapa and Arstead elected to at-large executive committee positions
- Resolution 24-26 approved: Out-of-home placement reserve increase
- Resolution 25-26 approved: Social services budget amendment
- Resolution 26-26 approved: Reduced landfill tipping fees for storm debris through June 17, 2025
- Administrator Leonard to continue discussions with community foundation regarding additional relief for uninsured costs
- Solid waste management board to develop policy on handling similar disaster situations within six months
- Supervisor Gao requested notification of future solid waste board meetings
