## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Board of Supervisors held an adjourned organizational meeting where they approved committee appointments, elected Supervisors Hapa and Arstead to the executive committee, and unanimously approved a resolution to reduce landfill tipping fees for disaster debris following severe weather that impacted the Ringle area. The board also heard from affected residents requesting additional fee relief.

## Key Discussions
### Committee Appointments
Chair Gibbs presented committee appointments developed with Vice Chair Boots, emphasizing goals of increasing perspective diversity, developing supervisor leadership, and accommodating preferences. The appointments were approved with a motion by Supervisor Cavelli and second by Supervisor Crims. The motion carried but was not unanimous. Supervisor Sybert noted the system marked him absent but he wished to be recorded as an 'aye' vote.

### Election of At-Large Executive Committee Members
Three supervisors were nominated for two at-large executive committee positions: Supervisor Hapa (nominated by Supervisor Mask), Supervisor Leur (nominated by Supervisor Rosenberg), and Supervisor Arstead (nominated by Vice Chair Boots). Supervisor Rosenberg spoke in support of Supervisor Leur, citing her advocacy for children and elders. Paper ballots were distributed and counted. Supervisors Hapa and Arstead were elected to the executive committee.

### Consent Agenda
Items C10 through C14A were approved on consent with a motion by Supervisor Cavelli and second by Supervisor Drebeck. The motion carried unanimously.

### Resolution D15 - Out of Home Placement Reserve Increase
Resolution 24-26 was moved by Supervisor Morash and seconded by Supervisor Robinson. The motion carried but was not unanimous.

### Resolution D16 - Social Services Budget Amendment
Resolution 25-26 was moved by Supervisor P and seconded by Supervisor Robinson. The motion carried but was not unanimous.

### Resolution E17 - Landfill Tipping Fees Modification for Disaster Debris
Administrator Leonard and Solid Waste Director Hagen Busher presented a proposal to reduce tipping fees for debris from the severe weather emergency. The state is waiving its $13.10 fee, the Town of Ringle is waiving its $2.33 fee, and the county proposed reducing to $36.94 per ton (commercial rate minus state and local fees) to cover costs. Two residents, Jessica Toasty and Matt Haybeck, were granted special permission to speak and requested the county waive fees entirely, citing the 44 affected homes and ongoing insurance delays. Multiple supervisors asked questions about fee calculations, timeline flexibility, and coverage scope. The board first voted unanimously to suspend rules to consider the resolution, then unanimously approved Resolution 26-26 to modify tipping fees through June 17th.

## Public Comment
Two residents were granted special dispensation to speak on the landfill tipping fee resolution. Jessica Toasty requested Marathon County wave fees entirely, noting the state, DNR, and Ringle are waiving their fees, and that private businesses have donated significant resources while the county has provided limited financial assistance. Matt Haybeck spoke on behalf of the approximately 44 homes affected, requesting fee relief for underinsured residents and an extended timeline since many insurance adjusters have not yet arrived.

## Action Items
- Committee appointments approved as presented by Chair Gibbs and Vice Chair Boots
- Supervisors Hapa and Arstead elected to executive committee at-large positions
- Resolution 26-26 approved reducing landfill tipping fees to approximately $36.94 per ton for certified disaster debris through June 17th
- Administrator Leonard to work with Community Foundation on potential reimbursement mechanism for unreimbursed insurance costs
- Solid waste management board directed to develop clear policy for handling similar disaster situations within six months
- Board to revisit fee structure at May meeting if extension beyond state declaration period is needed
