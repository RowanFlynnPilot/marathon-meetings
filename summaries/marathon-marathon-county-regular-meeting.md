## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Board held an organizational meeting where they approved committee appointments, elected Supervisors Hapa and Arstead to the at-large Executive Committee positions, and unanimously approved a resolution reducing landfill tipping fees for disaster-related debris following severe tornado and flood damage in Ringle and other communities. The fee reduction from $65 to approximately $36.94 per ton will help residents dispose of storm debris through June 17th.

## Key Discussions
### Committee Appointments
Chair Gibbs presented committee appointments developed with Vice Chair Boots, noting goals included increasing diversity of perspectives, allowing supervisors to grow as leaders, and accommodating supervisor preferences. He noted over 34% of the board is in its first or second term. The motion by Supervisor Cavelli, seconded by Supervisor Kremes, was carried but not unanimous. Supervisor Gao noted the system marked him absent but did not allow him to vote.

### Election of At-Large Executive Committee Members
Three supervisors were nominated: Supervisor Hapa (nominated by Supervisor Mask), Supervisor Leur (nominated by Supervisor Rosenberg), and Supervisor Arstead (nominated by Vice Chair Boots). Supervisor Rosenberg explained she nominated Supervisor Leur because she had seen her advocate for the county's youngest children and oldest elders. After paper ballot voting, Supervisors Hapa and Arstead were elected to the Executive Committee.

### Resolution 26-26: Modification to Landfill Tipping Fees
County Administrator Leonard and Solid Waste Director Hagen Busher presented a proposal to reduce tipping fees for disaster debris following severe tornado and flood damage. The standard $65/ton fee would be reduced to approximately $36.94/ton for certified disaster debris, with the state waiving $13.10 and the Town of Ringle waiving $2.33. Two residents, Jessica Toasty and Matt Haybeck, were granted special dispensation to speak, requesting the county waive all fees and extend the timeline beyond 60 days. Supervisor Robinson suggested tracking costs and potentially reimbursing the Community Foundation retroactively. The board voted unanimously to suspend rules to hear the resolution, then voted unanimously to approve Resolution 26-26.

### Consent Agenda
Items C10 through C14A were approved unanimously on a motion by Supervisor Cavelli, seconded by Supervisor Drebeck.

### Resolution 24-26: Out of Home Placement Reserve Increase
Motion by Supervisor Morash, seconded by Supervisor Robinson. The motion carried but was not unanimous.

### Resolution 25-26: Social Services Budget Amendment
Motion by Supervisor P and seconded by Supervisor Robinson. The motion carried but was not unanimous.

## Public Comment
Two residents were granted special dispensation to speak on the landfill tipping fee resolution. Jessica Toasty asked the county to waive all fees for six months, noting the state, DNR, and Ringle are waiving their fees, and that private businesses have donated significant resources. She stated one business has incurred $40,000 in cleanup expenses. Matt Haybeck spoke on behalf of residents with demolished homes, requesting fee waivers for underinsured residents and extended timelines since many insurance adjusters have not yet visited affected properties.

## Action Items
- Committee appointments approved as presented by Chair Gibbs and Vice Chair Boots
- Supervisors Hapa and Arstead elected to at-large Executive Committee positions
- Resolution 26-26 approved unanimously reducing disaster debris tipping fees to approximately $36.94/ton through June 17th
- Administrator Leonard to continue discussions with Community Foundation regarding unreimbursed disaster costs
- Solid waste management board to bring back a clear policy on handling similar disaster situations within six months
- Staff directed to track disaster debris costs for potential future review
