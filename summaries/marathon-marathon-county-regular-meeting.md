## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Board of Supervisors held its adjourned organizational meeting, approving committee appointments and electing Supervisors Hapa and Arstead to at-large executive committee positions. The board also unanimously approved a resolution to reduce landfill tipping fees for debris related to the recent tornado and flooding disaster that devastated the Ringle area, with fees reduced from $65/ton to approximately $36.94/ton through June 17th.

## Key Discussions
### Committee Appointments
Chair Gibbs delivered remarks thanking staff and Vice Chair Boots for developing committee appointments, emphasizing that 34% of the board is in its first or second term. He outlined goals including increasing diversity of perspective, providing leadership development opportunities, and honoring supervisor preferences. The motion to approve all committee appointments was made by Supervisor Cavelli, seconded by Supervisor Crims, and carried but was not unanimous.

### Election of At-Large Executive Committee Members
Three supervisors were nominated: Supervisor Hapa (nominated by Supervisor Mask), Supervisor Leur (nominated by Supervisor Rosenberg), and Supervisor Arstead (nominated by Supervisor Boots). Supervisor Rosenberg spoke in support of Supervisor Leur, citing her advocacy for 'our county's smallest children and our oldest elders.' Paper ballots were distributed and supervisors voted for two candidates. Supervisors Hapa and Arstead were elected to the executive committee.

### Consent Agenda
Items C10 through C14A were approved on the consent agenda. Motion by Supervisor Cavelli, seconded by Supervisor Drebeck. The motion carried unanimously.

### Resolution 24-26 Out of Home Placement Reserve Increase
Motion by Supervisor Morash, seconded by Supervisor Robinson. The motion carried but was not unanimous.

### Resolution 25-26 Social Services Budget Amendment
Motion by Supervisor P, seconded by Supervisor Robinson. The motion carried but was not unanimous.

### Resolution 26-26 Landfill Tipping Fee Modification
Administrator Leonard and Solid Waste Director Hagen Busher presented a proposal from the Solid Waste Management Board to reduce tipping fees for disaster-related debris following the April tornado and flooding. The standard $65/ton fee would be reduced to approximately $36.94/ton through June 17th, with the state waiving $13.10 and the Town of Ringle waiving $2.33. Two residents, Jessica Tolesie and Matt Haybeck, were granted special permission to speak, requesting the county waive fees entirely for affected homeowners, with Tolesie noting 'We didn't see anyone from Marathon County' during cleanup and asking for six months of fee waivers. Leonard noted the county would not meet the $670,743 damage threshold for state disaster aid reimbursement. The board first voted unanimously to suspend rules to hear the resolution without committee review, then approved Resolution 26-26 unanimously. Motion by Supervisor Arstead, seconded by Supervisor Morash.

## Public Comment
Two residents were granted special dispensation to speak on the landfill tipping fee resolution at Supervisor Rosenberg's request. Jessica Tolesie asked Marathon County to waive fees entirely for six months, noting that the state, DNR, and Town of Ringle had all waived their fees, and that private businesses had donated up to $40,000 in cleanup costs. Matt Haybeck spoke on behalf of affected homeowners, noting approximately 44 homes were damaged and many insurance adjusters had not yet arrived, requesting extended fee relief.

## Action Items
- Committee appointments approved as presented by Chair Gibbs and Vice Chair Boots
- Supervisors Hapa and Arstead elected to at-large executive committee positions
- Resolution 26-26 approved reducing landfill tipping fees for disaster debris to approximately $36.94/ton through June 17, 2025
- Administrator Leonard to continue discussions with Community Foundation about potential reimbursement mechanism for unreimbursed insurance costs
- Solid Waste Management Board directed to develop a clear policy within six months on handling similar disaster situations in the future
- Board to revisit tipping fee reduction timeline at May meeting if extension beyond June 17th is needed
