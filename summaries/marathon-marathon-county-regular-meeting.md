## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Board of Supervisors held an adjourned organizational meeting where they approved committee appointments, elected Supervisors Hapa and Arstead to the Executive Committee, and unanimously approved a resolution reducing landfill tipping fees for disaster debris following the recent tornado and flooding that devastated the Ringle area. The board also approved several budget amendments and heard from affected residents requesting additional fee relief.

## Key Discussions
### Committee Appointments
Chair Gibbs presented committee appointments developed with Vice Chair Boots, emphasizing goals of increasing diverse perspectives, developing supervisor leadership, and honoring supervisor preferences. Chair Gibbs noted that 34% of the board is in its first or second term. Motion by Supervisor Cavelli, seconded by Supervisor Crims. The motion carried but was not unanimous.

### Election of At-Large Executive Committee Members
Three supervisors were nominated: Supervisor Hapa (nominated by Supervisor Mask), Supervisor Leur (nominated by Supervisor Rosenberg), and Supervisor Arstead (nominated by Vice Chair Boots). Supervisor Rosenberg spoke in support of Supervisor Leur, citing her advocacy for the county's youngest children and oldest elders. Paper ballots were distributed and supervisors selected two names. Supervisors Hapa and Arstead were elected to the Executive Committee.

### Consent Agenda (Items C10-C14A)
Motion by Supervisor Cavelli, seconded by Supervisor Drebeck. The motion carried unanimously.

### Resolution Approving Out-of-Home Placement Reserve Increase (Resolution 24-26)
Motion by Supervisor Morash, seconded by Supervisor Robinson. The motion carried but was not unanimous.

### Resolution to Amend Social Services Budget (Resolution 25-26)
Motion by Supervisor P, seconded by Supervisor Robinson. The motion carried but was not unanimous.

### Modification to Landfill Tipping Fees (Resolution 26-26)
County Administrator Leonard and Solid Waste Director Dave Hagen Busher presented the proposal following the April tornado and flooding. Leonard explained that approximately 400 tons of debris have been processed, and the state emergency declaration allows waiving the $13.10 state fee. The town of Ringle is waiving its $2.33 fee. The Solid Waste Management Board proposed reducing fees to $36.94 per ton (covering costs) for certified disaster debris. Residents Jessica Toasty and Matt Haybeck were granted special permission to speak, requesting the county waive all fees and extend the timeline beyond 60 days. Supervisor Gao questioned fee structures, and Supervisor Rosenberg requested continued discussion. The board first voted unanimously to suspend rules to consider the resolution, then voted unanimously to approve Resolution 26-26.

## Public Comment
Two residents were granted special permission to address the board regarding the landfill tipping fee resolution. Jessica Toasty asked the county to completely waive fees for disaster debris, noting the state, DNR, and Ringle are waiving their fees, and requesting a six-month timeline. She stated one business has spent $40,000 on hauling and cleanup. Matt Haybeck spoke on behalf of homeowners whose houses must be demolished, noting that many of the approximately 44 affected homes are still waiting for insurance adjusters and requested extended relief.

## Action Items
- Committee appointments approved as presented by Chair Gibbs and Vice Chair Boots
- Supervisors Hapa and Arstead elected to Executive Committee at-large positions
- Resolution 24-26 approved increasing out-of-home placement reserve
- Resolution 25-26 approved amending social services budget
- Resolution 26-26 approved reducing landfill tipping fees to $36.94 per ton for certified disaster debris through June 17, 2025
- Solid Waste Management Board directed to develop long-term policy on disaster-related tipping fees within six months
- Administrator Leonard to continue discussions with Community Foundation regarding reimbursement mechanism for uninsured debris disposal costs
