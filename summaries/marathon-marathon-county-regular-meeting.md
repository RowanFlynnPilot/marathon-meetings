## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Board of Supervisors held an adjourned organizational meeting where they approved committee appointments, elected Supervisors Hapa and Arstead to the at-large Executive Committee positions, and unanimously approved a resolution to reduce landfill tipping fees for storm debris following severe weather that devastated the Ringle area. The board heard emotional public testimony from affected residents requesting fee waivers and discussed potential future relief options through the community foundation.

## Key Discussions
### Committee appointments
Chair Gibbs delivered remarks thanking Vice Chair Boots for assistance developing appointments, emphasizing that 34% of the board is in their first or second term. He outlined goals including increasing diversity of perspectives, providing leadership development opportunities, and honoring supervisor preferences. Motion by Supervisor Cavelli, seconded by Supervisor Crims. The motion carried but was not unanimous.

### Election of at-large Executive Committee members
Three supervisors were nominated: Supervisor Hapa (nominated by Supervisor Mask), Supervisor Leur (nominated by Supervisor Rosenberg), and Supervisor Arstead (nominated by Vice Chair Boots). Supervisor Rosenberg explained her nomination of Supervisor Leur, stating she has 'advocated for our county's smallest children and our oldest elders.' Paper ballots were distributed and supervisors selected two names. Supervisors Hapa and Arstead were elected to the Executive Committee.

### Resolution 26-26: Modification to landfill tipping fees
County Administrator Leonard and Solid Waste Director Hagen Busher presented a proposal to reduce tipping fees for storm debris following the April severe weather that devastated Ringle. The standard $65/ton fee would be reduced to approximately $36.94 by waiving state fees ($13.10), town of Ringle fees ($2.33), and reducing to commercial rate. Leonard noted the county will not meet the $670,743 threshold for state disaster aid but expects Ringle to qualify. He mentioned discussions with the community foundation about helping residents with unreimbursed insurance costs. The board suspended rules unanimously to consider the resolution from the Solid Waste Management Board, then approved Resolution 26-26 unanimously.

### Public testimony on tipping fees
At Supervisor Rosenberg's request, the board allowed two residents to speak. Jessica Toasty asked the county to waive fees entirely for six months, noting that businesses have donated over $40,000 in cleanup costs and the state, DNR, and Ringle are all waiving their portions. Matt Haybeck spoke on behalf of residents whose homes must be demolished, requesting fee waivers for underinsured residents and asking for more than 60 days since many insurance adjusters haven't yet arrived.

## Public Comment
Two residents were granted special permission to speak on the tipping fee resolution. Jessica Toasty asked the county to waive all fees for six months, citing that the state, DNR, town of Ringle, and private businesses are all contributing while Marathon County has provided limited financial assistance. Matt Haybeck requested fee waivers for underinsured residents facing home demolition and asked for timeline extensions since some insurance adjusters haven't arrived.

## Action Items
- Committee appointments approved as presented by Chair Gibbs and Vice Chair Boots
- Supervisors Hapa and Arstead elected to at-large Executive Committee positions
- Resolution 24-26 approving out of home placement reserve increase approved (not unanimous)
- Resolution 25-26 amending social services budget approved (not unanimous)
- Resolution 26-26 reducing landfill tipping fees for storm debris through June 17th approved unanimously
- Administrator Leonard to continue discussions with community foundation regarding reimbursement mechanism for unreimbursed insurance costs
- Solid Waste Management Board to develop long-term policy on disaster-related fee reductions within six months
- Staff to track certified storm debris and review town of Ringle resolution for fee waiver scope
