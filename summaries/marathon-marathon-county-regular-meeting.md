## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Board of Supervisors held an adjourned organizational meeting where they approved committee appointments, elected Supervisors Hapa and Arstead to the Executive Committee, and unanimously passed a resolution reducing landfill tipping fees for disaster-related debris following severe weather that impacted the Ringle area. The board suspended its rules to consider the emergency tipping fee resolution directly from the Solid Waste Management Board.

## Key Discussions
### Committee Appointments
Chair Gibbs outlined goals for committee appointments including increasing diversity of perspectives, ensuring supervisor development opportunities, and noting that over 34% of the board is in its first or second term. Vice Chair Boots assisted in developing appointments. The motion by Supervisor Cavelli, seconded by Supervisor Crims, was carried but not unanimous. Supervisor Arstead noted the system marked him as absent but he was present and an aye vote.

### Election of At-Large Executive Committee Members
Three supervisors were nominated: Supervisor Hapa (nominated by Supervisor Mask), Supervisor Leur (nominated by Supervisor Rosenberg), and Supervisor Arstead (nominated by Vice Chair Boots). Supervisor Rosenberg explained her nomination of Supervisor Leur, citing her advocacy for the county's youngest children and oldest elders. Paper ballots were distributed and supervisors selected two names. Supervisors Hapa and Arstead were elected to the Executive Committee.

### Consent Agenda
Items C10 through C14A were approved on motion by Supervisor Cavelli, seconded by Supervisor Drebeck. The motion carried unanimously.

### Resolution D15: Out of Home Placement Reserve Increase
Resolution 24-26 was moved by Supervisor Morash and seconded by Supervisor Robinson. The motion carried but was not unanimous.

### Resolution D16: Amend Social Services Budget
Resolution 25-26 was moved by Supervisor P and seconded by Supervisor Robinson. The motion carried but was not unanimous.

### Modification to Landfill Tipping Fees
Administrator Leonard and Director Hagen Busher presented a proposal to reduce tipping fees for disaster-related debris following severe weather that damaged approximately 44 homes in Ringle. The state waived its $13.10 fee, Ringle waived its $2.33 fee, and the proposal reduced the county's rate to $36.94 per ton (covering operational costs). Two residents, Jessica Toasty and Matt Haybeck, were granted special dispensation to speak, requesting the county waive fees entirely for six months citing financial hardships and delayed insurance adjusters. Multiple supervisors asked clarifying questions about fee structures, eligibility, and future policy development. The board first voted unanimously to suspend rules to consider the resolution directly from the Solid Waste Management Board, then passed Resolution 26-26 unanimously on motion by Supervisor Arstead, seconded by Supervisor Morash.

## Public Comment
Two residents were granted special dispensation to address the board during the tipping fee discussion. Jessica Toasty spoke about the community being self-reliant in cleanup, noting one business spent $40,000 in hauling and cleanup expenses, and requested the county waive fees for six months. Matt Haybeck spoke on behalf of approximately 44 affected homeowners, requesting fee waivers for underinsured residents and noting many insurance adjusters have not yet arrived.

## Action Items
- Committee appointments approved as presented by Chair Gibbs and Vice Chair Boots
- Supervisors Hapa and Arstead elected to Executive Committee at-large positions
- Resolution 26-26 approved reducing landfill tipping fees to $36.94 per ton for certified disaster-related debris through June 17, 2025
- Administrator Leonard to continue discussions with community foundation about reimbursement mechanism for unreimbursed out-of-pocket debris disposal costs
- Solid waste management board to develop clear policy within six months for handling similar disaster situations in the future
- Board to revisit tipping fee reduction at May meeting if extension beyond state declaration period is needed
