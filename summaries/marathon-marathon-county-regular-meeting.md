## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Board held an adjourned organizational meeting where they approved committee appointments, elected Supervisors Hapa and Arstead to at-large executive committee positions, and unanimously approved a resolution to reduce landfill tipping fees for storm debris following the recent tornado and flooding disaster. The tipping fee reduction allows affected residents to dispose of disaster-related debris at $36.94 per ton instead of the standard $65 rate.

## Key Discussions
### Committee Appointments
Chair Gibbs outlined the goals guiding committee appointments, including increasing diversity of perspective, ensuring leadership development opportunities for newer supervisors (34% in first or second term), and honoring supervisor preferences. He thanked Vice Chair Boots for assistance in developing appointments. The motion by Supervisor Cavelli, seconded by Supervisor Crims, was carried but not unanimous.

### Election of At-Large Executive Committee Members
Three supervisors were nominated: Supervisor Hapa (nominated by Supervisor Mask), Supervisor Leur (nominated by Supervisor Rosenberg, who cited her advocacy for young children and elderly residents), and Supervisor Arstead (nominated by Vice Chair Boots). Paper ballots were distributed for supervisors to select two candidates. Supervisors Hapa and Arstead were elected to the executive committee.

### Consent Agenda
Items C10 through C14A were approved on motion by Supervisor Cavelli, seconded by Supervisor Drebeck. The motion carried unanimously.

### Resolution 24-26: Out of Home Placement Reserve Increase
Motion by Supervisor Morash, seconded by Supervisor Robinson. The motion carried but was not unanimous.

### Resolution 25-26: Social Services Budget Amendment
Motion by Supervisor P, seconded by Supervisor Robinson. The motion carried but was not unanimous.

### Resolution 26-26: Modification to Landfill Tipping Fees
Administrator Leonard and Solid Waste Director Hagen Busher presented the proposal from the Solid Waste Management Board to reduce tipping fees for disaster-related debris from $65/ton to $36.94/ton (after state waives $13.10 and Ringle waives $2.33). The rules were suspended unanimously to bring the resolution directly from the Solid Waste Management Board. Public speakers Jessica Toasty and Matt Haybeck requested the county waive fees entirely, noting 44 homes affected and many awaiting insurance adjusters. Supervisor Robinson suggested tracking costs and potentially revisiting the policy. Administrator Leonard mentioned exploring partnership with the Community Foundation for reimbursing unreimbursed insurance costs. Motion by Supervisor Arstead, seconded by Supervisor Morash, passed unanimously.

## Public Comment
Special dispensation was granted for two speakers on the landfill tipping fee resolution. Jessica Toasty asked the county to fully waive fees, noting the state, DNR, and Ringle are waiving their portions, and that businesses have donated up to $40,000 in cleanup services. She requested a six-month waiver period. Matt Haybeck spoke on behalf of residents whose homes must be demolished, noting some of the 44 affected homes are still awaiting insurance adjusters and requested extended relief.

## Action Items
- Committee appointments approved as presented by Chair Gibbs and Vice Chair Boots
- Supervisors Hapa and Arstead elected to at-large executive committee positions
- Landfill tipping fees reduced to $36.94/ton for disaster-related debris through June 17, 2025
- Administrator Leonard to explore partnership with Community Foundation for reimbursing unreimbursed insurance costs for disaster victims
- Solid Waste Management Board to develop long-term policy for handling disaster situations within six months
- Staff to track disaster-related waste separately and certify debris through signed forms
