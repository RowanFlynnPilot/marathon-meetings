## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Board of Supervisors held an adjourned organizational meeting where they approved committee appointments, elected Supervisors Hapa and Arstead to the Executive Committee, and unanimously approved a resolution reducing landfill tipping fees for debris from the recent severe weather emergency affecting Ringle and other communities. The board heard public testimony from residents requesting expanded fee relief for tornado and flood victims.

## Key Discussions
### Committee Appointments
Chair Gibbs presented committee appointments developed with Vice Chair Boots, emphasizing goals of increasing diverse perspectives, developing supervisors as leaders, and accommodating supervisor preferences. Motion by Supervisor Cavelli, seconded by Supervisor Crims. The motion carried but was not unanimous, with Supervisor Mask noting the system marked him absent but he wished to be recorded as voting aye.

### Executive Committee At-Large Election
Three supervisors were nominated: Supervisor Hapa (nominated by Supervisor Mask), Supervisor Leur (nominated by Supervisor Rosenberg, who praised her advocacy for children and elders), and Supervisor Arstead (nominated by Vice Chair Boots). Paper ballots were distributed and supervisors selected two names. Supervisors Hapa and Arstead were elected to the Executive Committee.

### Consent Agenda
Items C10 through C14A were approved on motion by Supervisor Cavelli, seconded by Supervisor Drebeck. The motion carried unanimously.

### Resolution 24-26: Out of Home Placement Reserve Increase
Motion by Supervisor Morash, seconded by Supervisor Robinson. The motion carried but was not unanimous.

### Resolution 25-26: Social Services Budget Amendment
Motion by Supervisor P, seconded by Supervisor Robinson. The motion carried but was not unanimous.

### Resolution 26-26: Landfill Tipping Fee Modification
Administrator Leonard and Director Hagen Busher explained the fee reduction proposal in response to severe weather damage affecting Ringle, Kronenwetter, and other areas. The proposal reduces fees from $65/ton standard rate to approximately $36.94/ton by waiving the state fee ($13.10) and town of Ringle fee ($2.33) for certified disaster-related debris. About 400 tons of debris have already been processed. The board voted unanimously to suspend rules to hear this resolution directly from the Solid Waste Management Board, then approved the resolution unanimously on motion by Supervisor Arstead, seconded by Supervisor Morash.

## Public Comment
Two residents were granted special permission to speak on the tipping fee resolution. Jessica Tolesie urged the county to waive fees entirely for six months, noting that the state, DNR, and Ringle are waiving their fees and requesting Marathon County do the same. She stated private businesses have donated significant resources (one up to $40,000) and asked the county to contribute similarly. Matt Haybeck spoke on behalf of residents with demolished homes, requesting fee waivers for underinsured homeowners and noting some insurance adjusters haven't arrived yet, requesting more time beyond the proposed deadline.

## Action Items
- Committee appointments approved as presented by Chair Gibbs and Vice Chair Boots
- Supervisors Hapa and Arstead elected to Executive Committee at-large positions
- Resolution 26-26 approved reducing landfill tipping fees for disaster-related debris through June 17, 2025
- Administrator to work with Community Foundation on potential mechanism for reimbursing uninsured disaster cleanup costs
- Solid Waste Management Board directed to develop long-term policy on emergency fee reductions within six months
- Staff to continue tracking and certifying disaster-related debris for potential state disaster aid documentation
