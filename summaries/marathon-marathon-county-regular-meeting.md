## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Board of Supervisors held an adjourned organizational meeting where they approved committee appointments, elected two at-large Executive Committee members (Supervisors Hapa and Arstead), and unanimously approved a resolution reducing landfill tipping fees for storm debris following the recent tornado and flooding disaster. The tipping fee reduction to $36.94/ton will apply to certified storm-related debris through June 17th.

## Key Discussions
### Committee Appointments
Chair Gibbs presented committee appointments developed with Vice Chair Boots, emphasizing goals of increasing perspective diversity, developing new leaders (34% of board in first or second term), and honoring supervisor preferences. The motion by Supervisor Cavelli, seconded by Supervisor Crims, included both county board chair and administrator appointments. The motion carried but was not unanimous. Supervisor Drebeck noted the system marked him absent but he wanted to be recorded as voting aye.

### Election of At-Large Executive Committee Members
Three supervisors were nominated: Supervisor Hapa (nominated by Supervisor Mask), Supervisor Leur (nominated by Supervisor Rosenberg), and Supervisor Arstead (nominated by Vice Chair Boots). Supervisor Rosenberg explained her nomination of Supervisor Leur, citing her advocacy for the county's youngest and oldest residents. Paper ballots were distributed and counted. Supervisors Hapa and Arstead were elected to the Executive Committee.

### Consent Agenda
Items C10 through C14A were approved on motion by Supervisor Cavelli, seconded by Supervisor Drebeck. The motion carried unanimously by voice vote.

### Resolution 24-26: Out of Home Placement Reserve Increase
Motion by Supervisor Morash, seconded by Supervisor Robinson. The resolution from Health and Human Services Committee and Human Resources and Finance and Property Committee was approved but not unanimously.

### Resolution 25-26: Social Services Budget Amendment
Motion by Supervisor P, seconded by Supervisor Robinson. The resolution to amend the 2026 social services budget carried but was not unanimous.

### Resolution 26-26: Landfill Tipping Fees Modification
Administrator Leonard and Director Hagen Busher presented the proposal from the Solid Waste Management Board's special Friday meeting to reduce tipping fees for storm debris following the April tornado and flooding. The state is waiving $13.10/ton, Ringle is waiving $2.33/ton, and the county proposes reducing to commercial rate of $36.94/ton. Public speakers Jessica Toasty and Matt Haybeck requested the county waive fees entirely, citing that 44 homes were damaged and insurance adjusters haven't arrived for many. Multiple supervisors asked questions about costs, timelines, and coverage areas. The board first voted unanimously to suspend rules since the resolution hadn't gone through a standing committee, then unanimously approved Resolution 26-26.

## Public Comment
Two members of the public were granted special dispensation to speak on the tipping fee resolution. Jessica Toasty requested Marathon County waive fees entirely for six months, noting that the state, DNR, and Town of Ringle are all waiving their fees, and that private businesses have donated up to $40,000 in cleanup costs. Matt Haybeck spoke on behalf of the approximately 44 homeowners whose houses may need demolition, requesting fee waivers for underinsured residents and more time since many insurance adjusters haven't arrived yet.

## Action Items
- Committee appointments approved for 2025-2026 term
- Supervisors Hapa and Arstead elected to at-large Executive Committee positions
- Reduced landfill tipping fee of $36.94/ton approved for certified storm debris through June 17th
- Administrator Leonard to work with Community Foundation on mechanism for reimbursing unreimbursed insurance costs for individual landowners
- Solid Waste Management Board to develop long-term policy on handling disaster-related tipping fees within six months
- Situation to be revisited at May board meeting if extension beyond June 17th is needed
