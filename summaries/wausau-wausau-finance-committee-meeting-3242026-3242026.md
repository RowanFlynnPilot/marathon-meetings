## Meeting Overview
The Wausau Finance Committee approved several routine items including airport ground leases and budget carryover funds, while postponing decisions on an opioid class action settlement and a waterworks budget amendment pending more information. The committee denied a tax recovery claim related to ongoing Greenwood Hills litigation and approved transfers from GMT revenues to cover shortfalls in recycling, airport, and parking funds.

## Key Discussions
### Minutes from March 10, 2026
Alder Watson moved to approve the minutes, seconded by Alder Griner. Motion passed unanimously.

### Alleged claim for recovery of unlawful tax for Greenwood Hills LLC
This assessment is part of ongoing litigation with Greenwood Hills. The chair explained that a motion to approve followed by a 'no' vote would deny the claim. Alder Watson moved to approve, seconded by Griner. The motion failed with opposition votes, effectively denying the tax recovery claim.

### Airport ground lease transfers at 939 Woods Place
Three related items were approved to transfer a hangar from Win O. Jones to Owen Jones: consent to transfer title (Watson moved, Griner seconded), termination of lease with Win O. Jones (Tierney moved, Watson seconded), and new lease with Owen Jones (Watson moved, Tierney seconded). All passed unanimously.

### Airport ground lease with Cole Lundberg
Alder Griner moved to approve the ground lease, seconded by Watson. Passed unanimously.

### National opioid settlement agreement
Committee members expressed discomfort with proceeding without more information. Alder Malini asked where the item came from, and Assistant Attorney Vincent explained law firms seek potential plaintiffs for class actions. Alder Watson noted concerns about signing away future legal remedies. Alder Griner moved to postpone to the next meeting, seconded by Tierney. Motion to postpone passed unanimously. Deadline to participate is May 4th.

### Waterworks lead service line replacement budget amendment
Public Works Director Eric explained that $709,000 in non-construction costs were deemed ineligible for the DNR subsidized loan, contrary to earlier agreements. Finance Director Marian outlined options: borrowing via GO bonds, using general fund reserves, or potentially PFAS settlement money for the utility portion. Alder Tierney stated opposition to adding more debt given current debt levels. Committee discussed splitting the $283,868 homeowner portion and $425,803 utility portion differently. Alder Watson moved to postpone to the next meeting, seconded by Griner. Motion passed unanimously.

### Budget amendment for carryover funds from 2025 to 2026
Finance Director explained major items include 10 transit buses funded by VW mitigation grant and various airport projects with grant funding. Some projects like city hall chimney liner and public safety roof haven't started yet. Alder Watson moved to approve, seconded by Griner. Passed unanimously.

### 2025 motorpool fund financial results
Finance Director reported the motorpool would show approximately $150,000 net profit after GMT transfer. There's a potential cash flow shortfall of $177,000 due to outstanding capital purchases, but ARPA savings and sale proceeds should cover this. Solomon from MotorPool explained two dump trucks from 2023 are awaiting spreader upfits and should arrive shortly. This was an informational item with no action required.

### 2025 general fund financial results
Finance Director reported a surplus of approximately $1.2 million driven by strong building permits, GMT money, and investment income. Expenditures were over by $320,000, primarily due to motorpool charges in public works ($481,000 over), fire ($527,000 over), and police ($468,000 over). CCITC was over budget by $194,000 due to communication issues on subscription costs and an Office 365 upgrade. After proposed transfers, surplus would be $539,000. Alder Tierney moved to approve transfers to recycling, airport, and parking funds from GMT revenues, seconded by Watson. Passed unanimously.

### 2026 general obligation promissory note calendar
Finance Director presented the borrowing schedule for 2026 capital improvements with various amortization periods. Alder Watson noted debt utilization will decrease even with new issuance. Watson moved to approve the calendar, chair seconded. Finance Director noted Phil Cawson from Ehlers will present parameters resolution at next meeting. Motion passed unanimously. Most projects have already been bid out.

### Purchase of properties for DPW Streets Division
Item was scheduled for closed session discussion. Due to time constraints with council meeting starting at 6:30, Alder Watson moved to postpone to the next meeting, noting time is not of the essence. Seconded by Tierney. Motion passed unanimously.

## Public Comment
No public comment was offered.
