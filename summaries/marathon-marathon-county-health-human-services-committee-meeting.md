## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Health & Human Services Committee held an introductory meeting for new board members, providing educational presentations on the Health Department, Social Services, Veteran Service Office, and Aging Disability Resource Center. The only action taken was approval of the April 1st meeting minutes. The meeting served primarily to orient new supervisors to department missions, budgets, and organizational structures.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of April 1st meeting minutes
Supervisor Cavelli made a motion to approve the minutes, seconded by Vice Chair Rosenberg. The motion carried unanimously with all members voting in favor.

### Review of standing committee jurisdiction
Administrator Lance Leonard explained the committee's structure under ordinance 2.04 sub5, including oversight of Veterans Service Department, Social Services, and Health Department, plus affiliated entities North Central Health Care and Aging Disability Resource Center. He outlined subordinate boards including the Social Services Board, Veteran Service Commission, Transportation Coordinating Committee, and Board of Health that may bring recommendations to the committee.

### Health Department presentation
Health Officer Caitlyn Suginaka presented department operations including 37.6 FTE staff across four divisions: environmental health, nursing, community health improvement, and administration. She highlighted over 1,200 licenses managed, Nurse Family Partnership program graduating its first two-year-olds, public health vending machines stocked with free resources including tick kits, and a new homeless system response position. Budget discussion noted approximately $5 million total with about half from tax levy and significant intergovernmental grant revenue.

### Social Services Department overview
Administrator Leonard presented on behalf of Director Christa who was absent, covering the department's 140 FTE staff working in child welfare, youth justice, children's long-term support, economic support, and child support. He highlighted the successful Family Keys housing stability pilot program addressing housing instability for child welfare cases. The department's 2026 budget shows over $22 million total revenue with approximately $7 million from tax levy.

### Veteran Service Office presentation
Veteran Service Officer Aaron Galindo reported the office now has 3.0 FTE and completed 8,149 interactions in 2025, up from 5,900 the prior year. He reported $44 million annually in VA benefits flowing to Marathon County veterans, with $2.6 million in retroactive payments in 2025. Galindo discussed suicide prevention efforts noting Marathon County had 30 suicides in 2025, seven of which were veterans, and announced partnership with Center for Veterans Issues for housing stability. Supervisor Kremes asked about regional coordination with neighboring counties.

### Aging Disability Resource Center presentation
Executive Director Mike Ray presented on the four-county ADRC serving Marathon, Lincoln, Langlade, and Wood counties with about 60 FTE and 385-400 volunteers. He described services including information assistance, caregiver support, dementia care, falls prevention, and Meals on Wheels nutrition program. Ray noted significant financial challenges with nutrition costs up 40-45% over four years while federal Older American Act funding remains flat. Marathon County's 2026 contribution is $413,135.
