## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Health & Human Services Committee held an orientation meeting for new board members, featuring educational presentations from department heads on the Health Department, Social Services, Veterans Service Office, and Aging Disability Resource Center. No major policy decisions were made as this was primarily an informational session to familiarize new committee members with departmental operations, budgets, and services.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of April 1st committee meeting minutes
Supervisor Cavelli made a motion to approve the minutes, seconded by Vice Chair Rosenberg. The motion carried unanimously with no opposition.

### Review of standing committee ordinances and jurisdiction
Administrator Lance Leonard explained the committee's structure under section 2.04 sub5 of county ordinances. He outlined reporting relationships with direct county departments (Veteran Services, Social Services, Health Department) and multi-county entities (North Central Health Care, ADRC of Central Wisconsin). Chair Boots noted the strategic plan development will be a focus this year, with objectives to be presented at upcoming meetings.

### Health Department presentation
Health Officer Caitlyn Suginaka presented the department's mission of preventing disease, promoting health, and protecting from environmental hazards. She highlighted 1,200 licenses overseen, tick kit distribution through public health vending machines, the Nurse Family Partnership program graduating its first two-year-olds, and a new FTE position for homeless system response. Administrator Leonard noted the department budget is approximately $5 million with about half from tax levy and significant revenue from public charges for services and intergovernmental grants.

### Social Services Department overview
Administrator Leonard presented on behalf of Director Christa, noting the department has nearly 140 FTE with a 2026 budget over $22 million but only about $7 million from tax levy. He highlighted the successful Family Keys housing stability pilot program, explaining the board's feedback substantially changed and strengthened the program. He noted potential restructuring requests coming due to increased need in children's long-term support and child support units.

### Veterans Service Office presentation
VSO Aaron Galindo reported the office now has 3.0 FTE and had 8,149 client interactions in 2025, up from 5,900. He noted $44 million in annual VA compensation comes to Marathon County veterans, with $2.6 million in retroactive payments in 2025 alone. He highlighted housing stability work with Center for Veterans Issues and suicide prevention efforts, noting 7 of Marathon County's 30 suicides in 2025 were veterans. Supervisor Kremes asked about regional cooperation; Galindo explained state statute requires each county have a VSO but offices regularly collaborate.

### ADRC of Central Wisconsin presentation
Executive Director Mike Ray explained the ADRC serves four counties (Marathon, Lincoln, Langlade, Wood) with about 60 FTE and 385-400 volunteers. He described services including meals on wheels, Medicare assistance, dementia care, and falls prevention. Ray noted serious financial challenges with nutrition costs up 40-45% over four years while federal funding remains flat. Marathon County's 2026 contribution is $413,135. Vice Chair Rosenberg praised the culturally appropriate meals program at the Hmong American Center.
