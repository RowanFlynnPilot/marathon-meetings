## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Health & Human Services Committee held an orientation meeting for new board members, featuring educational presentations from the Health Department, Social Services, Veteran Services, and the Aging & Disability Resource Center. No action items were voted on beyond approving April meeting minutes. The committee learned about department structures, budgets, and current initiatives including homelessness response, veteran suicide prevention, and challenges facing nutrition programs.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of April 1st Meeting Minutes
Supervisor Cavelli made a motion to approve the minutes, seconded by Vice Chair Rosenberg. The motion carried unanimously with no opposition.

### Standing Committee Ordinance Review
Administrator Lance Leonard explained the committee's structure under ordinance section 2.04 subsection 5, covering jurisdiction over the Health Department, Social Services, Veteran Services, North Central Health Care, and the Aging & Disability Resource Center. He outlined affiliated boards including the Social Services Board, Veteran Service Commission, Transportation Coordinating Committee, and Board of Health. Chair Boots noted the committee will be working on a new strategic plan with a target completion of December.

### Health Department Presentation
Health Officer Caitlyn Suginaka presented the department's mission and structure, highlighting 37.6 FTE staff across environmental health, nursing, and community health improvement teams. She discussed public health vending machines offering free supplies like tick kits, the Nurse Family Partnership program with first graduates, and the new homeless system response position created through a task force last session. Administrator Leonard noted about half of the department's $5 million budget comes from tax levy, with significant grant and fee revenue.

### Social Services Department Overview
Administrator Leonard presented on behalf of Director Christa, covering the department's 140 FTE staff and $22 million budget with approximately $7 million in tax levy. He highlighted the successful Family Keys pilot program addressing housing instability for child welfare and youth justice families, noting many participants had never had a bank account or formal lease. He mentioned potential upcoming requests for restructuring including converting lead worker positions to supervisory roles.

### Veteran Service Department Presentation
Veteran Service Officer Aaron Galindo reported the department now has 3.0 FTE and had 8,149 interactions in 2025. He highlighted $44 million in annual VA compensation flowing to county veterans and $2.6 million in retroactive payments in 2025. Key initiatives include surviving spouse benefits outreach, housing stability partnerships with Center for Veterans Issues, and suicide prevention efforts noting 7 of Marathon County's 30 suicides in 2025 were veterans. Supervisor Kremes asked about regional coordination, and Galindo confirmed good working relationships with neighboring counties while noting state statute requires each county to have its own VSO.

### ADRC Presentation
Executive Director Mike Ray explained the four-county regional organization serving Marathon, Lincoln, Langlade, and Wood counties with about 60 FTE and 385-400 volunteers. He discussed financial challenges with nutrition program costs up 40-45% over four years while federal funding remained flat. The organization hadn't requested a levy increase from 2011 until 2024. Marathon County's contribution is $413,135. Vice Chair Rosenberg thanked the program for culturally appropriate meals served at the Hmong American Center.
