## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Health & Human Services Committee held an organizational meeting primarily focused on educating new board members about the committee's jurisdiction, departments, and responsibilities. Minutes from April 1st were approved unanimously. Department heads from Health, Veteran Services, and the Aging Disability Resource Center presented overviews of their operations, budgets, and key initiatives.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of Minutes
Supervisor Cavelli made a motion to approve the April 1st committee meeting minutes. Vice Chair Rosenberg seconded. Motion passed unanimously with no discussion.

### Committee Jurisdiction and Ordinance Review
Administrator Leonard explained the committee's structure under ordinance section 2.04 sub5, including oversight of Health Department, Social Services, Veteran Services, and affiliated entities like North Central Health Care and the Aging Disability Resource Center. He explained the committee receives input from the Social Services Board, Veteran Service Commission, Transportation Coordinating Committee, Board of Health, and others.

### Health Department Presentation
Health Officer Caitlyn Suginaka presented the department's mission of preventing disease, promoting health, and protecting from environmental hazards. The department has 37.6 FTE staff across environmental health, nursing, and community health improvement teams. She highlighted public health vending machines stocked with free resources including tick kits, the Nurse Family Partnership program with first graduates, and a new homeless system response position. Supervisor Conway asked about vending machine locations - one at Community Partners Campus and one at Health First.

### Social Services Department Overview
Administrator Leonard presented on behalf of Director Christa who could not attend. The department has nearly 140 FTE and a $22 million budget with about $7 million in tax levy. He highlighted the successful Family Keys housing stability pilot program conducted in partnership with the state, which addresses housing instability for child welfare and youth justice cases. Chair Boots noted this relates to a homelessness task force from the previous session that Supervisors Cavelli and Conway served on.

### Veteran Service Department Presentation
Veteran Service Officer Erin Galindo reported his small department (3.0 FTE) had 8,149 interactions in 2025, up from 5,900. Veterans received $2.6 million in retroactive payments and $44 million annually flows into the county. Key initiatives include assisting surviving spouses with dependency and indemnity compensation benefits, housing stability partnerships with Center for Veterans Issues, and suicide prevention efforts - noting 30 suicides in Marathon County in 2025, seven of which were veterans. Supervisor Kremes asked about relationships with neighboring counties and staffing adequacy; Galindo confirmed good collaboration exists and noted every VSO office statewide is undermanned.

### ADRC Presentation
Executive Director Mike Ray explained the four-county regional agency serves as the one-stop shop for aging and disability services, including Meals on Wheels nutrition program with 385-400 volunteers. He noted significant financial challenges - federal funding flat since 2024 while nutrition costs rose 40-45% in four years, forcing meal and staffing reductions. The ADRC hadn't requested a levy increase from 2011 until 2024. Vice Chair Rosenberg praised the culturally appropriate nutrition program at the Hmong American Center.

## Public Comment
