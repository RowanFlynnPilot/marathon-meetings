## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Health & Human Services Committee held an orientation meeting for new board members, featuring presentations from the Health Department, Veteran Services, and Aging Disability Resource Center. The committee approved April 1st meeting minutes unanimously and received educational briefings on department missions, budgets, and services with no other action items.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of April 1st meeting minutes
Supervisor Cavelli made a motion to approve the minutes, seconded by Vice Chair Rosenberg. The motion passed unanimously with all members voting in favor.

### Review of standing committee ordinance and structure
Administrator Lance Leonard explained the committee's jurisdiction over Veterans Service Department, Social Services Department, Health Department, North Central Health Care, and ADRC of Central Wisconsin. He outlined reporting relationships with various boards including the Social Services Board, Veterans Service Commission, Transportation Coordinating Committee, and Board of Health. Chair Boots noted the committee will be working on a new strategic plan expected by December.

### Health Department presentation
Health Officer Caitlyn Suginaka presented on the department's 37.6 FTE staff across four divisions: environmental health, nursing, community health improvement, and administration. She highlighted over 1,200 licenses overseen, a new homeless system response position, Nurse Family Partnership program with first graduates, public health vending machines with free resources including tick kits, and lead mitigation efforts. Administrator Leonard noted approximately half of the $5 million budget is tax levy with significant intergovernmental and fee revenues.

### Social Services Department overview
Administrator Leonard presented on behalf of Director Christa, noting the department has nearly 140 FTE and a $22 million budget with approximately $7 million in tax levy. He highlighted the Family Keys housing stability pilot program partnership with the state involving Marathon, LaCrosse, and Wood counties, which has been very successful. He indicated future restructuring requests may come as the department addresses increased needs in child support and other units.

### Veteran Service Department presentation
VSO Erin Galindo reported his department increased to 3.0 FTE in 2025 and had 8,149 client interactions, up from 5,900 the previous year. He noted $44 million in annual VA benefits flow to Marathon County veterans, with $2.6 million in retroactive payments in 2025. Galindo highlighted partnerships with Center for Veterans Issues for housing, suicide prevention initiatives (noting 7 of 30 county suicides in 2025 were veterans), and work on surviving spouse benefits. Supervisor Kremes asked about regional coordination; Galindo explained state statute requires each county to have a VSO but offices collaborate well across county lines.

### Aging Disability Resource Center presentation
Executive Director Mike Ray explained ADRC serves four counties (Marathon, Lincoln, Langlade, and Wood) with about 75 staff and 385-400 volunteers. He noted 11% of budget is county levy with the remainder being state and federal funding. Ray highlighted significant challenges with the nutrition program as food costs increased 40-45% over four years while federal funding remained flat, forcing meal and staffing reductions. Marathon County's 2026 contribution is $413,135. Vice Chair Rosenberg thanked the program for culturally appropriate meals at the Hmong American Center.
