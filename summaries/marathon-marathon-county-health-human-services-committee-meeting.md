## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Health & Human Services Committee held an organizational meeting for new board members, providing educational presentations on the Health Department, Social Services, Veteran Services, and Aging & Disability Resource Center. The committee approved April 1st meeting minutes unanimously and received detailed overviews of department missions, budgets, and current initiatives including homelessness response efforts and veteran suicide prevention programs.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of April 1st Meeting Minutes
Supervisor Cavelli made a motion to approve the minutes, seconded by Vice Chair Rosenberg. The motion carried unanimously with all members voting in favor.

### Standing Committee Ordinance Review
Administrator Lance Leonard explained the committee's structure under section 2.04 subsection 5, covering jurisdiction over Health Department, Social Services, Veteran Services, North Central Health Care, and the Aging & Disability Resource Center. He outlined relationships with subordinate bodies including the Social Services Board, Veteran Service Commission, Transportation Coordinating Committee, and Board of Health.

### Health Department Presentation
Health Officer Caitlyn Suginaka presented the department's mission of preventing disease, promoting health, and protecting against environmental hazards. The department has 37.6 FTE staff across four divisions. She highlighted public health vending machines offering free resources including tick kits, the Nurse Family Partnership program with first graduates, and a new homeless system response position created from a task force recommendation. The department's $5 million budget is approximately half tax levy with significant grant funding.

### Social Services Department Overview
Administrator Leonard presented on behalf of absent Director Christa, explaining the department's 140 FTE staff handling child welfare, youth justice, children's long-term support, economic support, and child support. He highlighted the successful Family Keys pilot program addressing housing instability, noting the program substantively changed based on board feedback. The department's $22 million budget includes about $7 million in tax levy.

### Veteran Service Department Presentation
Veteran Service Officer Aaron Galindo reported his department increased to 3.0 FTE in 2025. Customer interactions jumped from 5,900 to 8,149 in 2025. He highlighted $44 million annually in VA benefits flowing to county veterans and $2.6 million in retroactive payments in 2025. Key initiatives include surviving spouse benefits outreach, housing stability partnerships with Center for Veterans Issues, and suicide prevention efforts noting 7 of 30 county suicides in 2025 were veterans. He discussed the Staff Sergeant Gordon Fox suicide prevention grant opportunity.

### ADRC Presentation
Executive Director Mike Ray explained the four-county regional model serving Marathon, Lincoln, Langlade, and Wood counties with about 60 FTEs and 385-400 volunteers. He noted 11% of budget comes from county levy with Marathon contributing $413,135. The nutrition program faces 40-45% cost increases over four years while federal funding remains flat, forcing meal and service reductions. The organization hadn't requested a levy increase from 2011 until 2024.
