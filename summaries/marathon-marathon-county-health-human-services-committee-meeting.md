## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Health & Human Services Committee held an educational orientation meeting for new and returning board members. The committee approved April 1st meeting minutes unanimously and received presentations from department heads covering the Health Department, Social Services, Veteran Services, and the Aging Disability Resource Center. No major policy decisions were made as this was primarily an informational session to familiarize members with departmental operations and budgets.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of April 1st Meeting Minutes
Supervisor Cavelli made a motion to approve the minutes, seconded by Vice Chair Rosenberg. The motion carried unanimously with all members voting in favor.

### Health Department Overview
Health Officer Caitlyn Suginaka presented on the department's mission to prevent disease, promote health, and protect the public from environmental hazards. She highlighted the department's 37.6 FTE staff across four divisions: environmental health (managing 1,200+ licenses), nursing/communicable disease, family health, and community health improvement. She noted new initiatives including public health vending machines stocked with free resources like tick kits, the Nurse Family Partnership program now graduating its first cohort of two-year-olds, and a new homeless system response position approved by the county board last session.

### Social Services Department Overview
Administrator Leonard provided an overview in the absence of Director Christa. He explained the department's divisions including child welfare, child protective services, youth justice, children's long-term support, economic support, and child support. With nearly 140 FTE, it's the county's second-largest department. Leonard highlighted the successful Family Keys pilot program addressing housing instability for families involved with child welfare and youth justice, noting the program has helped families who had never had bank accounts, credit, or formal leases. He indicated potential restructuring requests may come during budget season.

### Veteran Service Department Overview
VSO Aaron Galindo presented on his department of 3.0 FTE that had 8,149 interactions in 2025, up significantly from 5,900 the prior year. He reported $44 million in annual VA benefits flowing to county veterans, plus $2.6 million in retroactive payments in 2025. Galindo highlighted initiatives including housing stability partnerships with Center for Veterans Issues, suicide prevention efforts (noting 7 of Marathon County's 30 suicides in 2025 were veterans), and education efforts with hospice and medical examiner's office on proper death certificate documentation for surviving spouse benefits.

### Aging Disability Resource Center Overview
Executive Director Mike Ray presented on the four-county ADRC serving Marathon, Lincoln, Langlade, and Wood counties with about 60 FTE and 385-400 volunteers. He explained their role as the one-stop shop for aging and disability services, including Meals on Wheels. Ray noted significant financial challenges with federal funding flat since 2024 while nutrition costs rose 40-45%, forcing reductions in meals served and staffing. The organization hadn't requested a levy increase from 2011 until 2024. Marathon County's contribution is $413,135.
