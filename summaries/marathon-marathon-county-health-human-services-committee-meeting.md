## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Health & Human Services Committee held an educational orientation meeting for new and returning board members, featuring presentations from the Health Department, Veteran Service Office, and Aging Disability Resource Center on their missions, programs, and budgets. The committee approved April 1st meeting minutes unanimously and received updates on homelessness response efforts and various county services.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of April 1st committee meeting minutes
Supervisor Cavelli made a motion to approve the minutes, seconded by Vice Chair Rosenberg. The motion passed unanimously with all members voting in favor.

### Health Department presentation
Health Officer Caitlyn Suginaka presented an overview of the department's mission to prevent disease, promote health, and protect against environmental hazards. She highlighted the department's 37.6 FTE staff working across environmental health, nursing, and community health improvement teams. Suginaka noted new public health vending machines stocked with free tick kits and resources, the Nurse Family Partnership program graduating its first two-year-olds, and a new homeless system response position created in partnership with the City of Wausau. Supervisor Kremes asked about pandemic preparedness, and Suginaka confirmed comprehensive emergency plans are in place with staff well-trained from COVID-19 experience.

### Social Services Department overview
Administrator Lance Leonard presented on behalf of Director Christa who was unavailable. He explained the department's 140 FTE staff working in child welfare, youth justice, children's long-term support, economic support, and child support. Leonard highlighted the successful Family Keys pilot program addressing housing instability, noting many participants had never had a bank account or been on a lease before. He indicated future restructuring requests may come forward due to increased community need.

### Veteran Service Office presentation
Veteran Service Officer Erin Galindo reported the office increased from 2.75 to 3.0 FTE in 2025 and conducted 8,149 veteran interactions, up significantly from previous years. He noted the financial impact of $44 million annually coming to county veterans, plus $2.6 million in retroactive payments in 2025. Galindo highlighted partnerships with the Center for Veterans Issues for housing stability and suicide prevention efforts, noting Marathon County had 30 suicides in 2025, seven of which were veterans. Supervisor Kremes asked about regional collaboration with neighboring counties, and Galindo explained strong working relationships exist but state statute requires each county to maintain its own veteran service officer.

### Aging Disability Resource Center presentation
Executive Director Mike Ray explained the ADRC serves four counties (Marathon, Lincoln, Langlade, and Wood) with about 60 FTE staff and 385-400 volunteers. He described services including information assistance, caregiver support, dementia care, falls prevention, and the Meals on Wheels nutrition program. Ray noted significant financial challenges with nutrition program costs rising 40-45% over four years while federal funding remained flat. The organization had not requested a levy increase from 2011 until 2024 due to the efficiency of the regional model. Vice Chair Rosenberg praised the culturally appropriate meals program at the Hmong American Center.
