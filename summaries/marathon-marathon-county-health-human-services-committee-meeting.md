## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Health & Human Services Committee held an educational orientation meeting for new and returning board members, providing overviews of the Health Department, Social Services, Veteran Services Office, and Aging Disability Resource Center. The only action taken was approval of the April 1st meeting minutes. No policy decisions or substantive votes occurred.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of April 1st Meeting Minutes
Supervisor Cavelli made a motion to approve the minutes, seconded by Vice Chair Rosenberg. The motion carried with unanimous voice vote.

### Health Department Overview
Health Officer Caitlyn Suginaka presented on the department's mission to prevent disease, promote health, and protect from environmental hazards. She highlighted 1,200+ licenses overseen, the Nurse Family Partnership program with first graduates at age 2, public health vending machines stocked with free tick kits and other resources, and a new FTE position for homeless system response. Administrator Leonard explained that about half of the $5 million budget is tax levy, with environmental health largely self-supporting through fees.

### Social Services Department Overview
Administrator Leonard presented on behalf of Director Christa who was unavailable. He explained the department has nearly 140 FTE staff with a $22 million budget but only about $7 million in tax levy. He highlighted the successful Family Keys housing stability pilot program that addressed housing instability for child welfare and youth justice cases, noting many participants had never had credit accounts, bank accounts, or formal leases before.

### Veteran Service Office Presentation
VSO Aaron Galindo reported the office now has 3.0 FTE and had 8,149 interactions in 2025, up from 5,900 previously. He highlighted $44 million annually coming into the county for veterans, plus $2.6 million in retroactive payments in 2025. Key initiatives include the PACT Act claims for presumptive conditions, surviving spouse benefits (up to $2,400/month tax-free), housing stability work with Center for Veterans Issues, and suicide prevention efforts noting 7 of 30 county suicides in 2025 were veterans.

### Aging Disability Resource Center Presentation
Executive Director Mike Ray explained the ADRC serves Marathon, Lincoln, Langlade, and Wood counties as a one-stop shop for aging and disability services. He noted 11% of their budget comes from county levy, with Marathon County contributing $413,135. He highlighted significant financial challenges with nutrition program costs up 40-45% over four years while federal funding remained flat, forcing reduced meals and staffing. The organization relies on approximately 385-400 volunteers alongside about 75 staff.
