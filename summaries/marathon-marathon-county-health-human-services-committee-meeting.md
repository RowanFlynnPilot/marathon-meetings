## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Health & Human Services Committee held an introductory meeting for new board members, providing educational presentations from department heads on the Health Department, Social Services, Veteran Service Office, and Aging Disability Resource Center. No action items were voted on beyond approving prior meeting minutes. The meeting focused on orienting supervisors to committee jurisdiction, department missions, and budget structures.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of April 1st Minutes
Supervisor Cavelli made a motion to approve the minutes, seconded by Vice Chair Rosenberg. The motion carried unanimously with no discussion.

### Review of Committee Jurisdiction and Ordinances
Administrator Lance Leonard explained the committee's structure under section 2.04 sub-5 of county ordinances. He outlined that the committee oversees three direct county departments (Health, Social Services, Veterans Service) plus affiliated entities North Central Health Care and Aging Disability Resource Center. He also explained relationships with statutory boards including the Social Services Board, Veterans Service Commission, Transportation Coordinating Committee, and Board of Health.

### Health Department Presentation
Health Officer Caitlyn Suginaka presented on the department's mission of preventing disease, promoting health, and protecting from environmental hazards. She highlighted over 1,200 licenses overseen, communicable disease prevention, Nurse Family Partnership program with first graduates, homeless system response position, and radon/lead mitigation efforts. The department has 37.6 FTE across environmental health, nursing, community health improvement, and administrative teams. Administrator Leonard noted about half of the $5 million budget is tax levy with significant intergovernmental grant revenue.

### Social Services Department Overview
Administrator Leonard presented on behalf of Director Christa who was unavailable. He explained the department's divisions: child welfare (child protective services and youth justice), children's long-term support, economic support, and child support. With nearly 140 FTE, it's the second largest department. The 2026 budget shows $22 million total with approximately $7 million in tax levy. Leonard highlighted the successful Family Keys housing stability pilot program that addresses root causes of housing instability.

### Veteran Service Office Presentation
VSO Erin Galindo reported the office increased from 2.75 to 3.0 FTE. Customer interactions jumped from 5,900 to 8,149 in 2025. The PACT Act has significantly increased claims for presumptive conditions. Veterans in Marathon County receive $44 million annually in benefits, with $2.6 million in retroactive payments in 2025. Galindo discussed initiatives including surviving spouse benefits outreach, housing stability partnerships with Center for Veterans Issues, and suicide prevention efforts noting 7 of 30 county suicides in 2025 were veterans.

### Aging Disability Resource Center Presentation
Executive Director Mike Ray explained ADRC serves four counties (Marathon, Lincoln, Langlade, Wood) as an integrated organization providing aging and disability resource services. About 11% of budget comes from county levy. Services include information and assistance, options counseling, caregiver support, dementia care specialists, falls prevention, benefit specialists, and Meals on Wheels nutrition program. Ray noted significant financial challenges as federal funding has been flat while nutrition costs increased 40-45% over four years. The organization has approximately 60 FTE staff plus 385-400 volunteers.
