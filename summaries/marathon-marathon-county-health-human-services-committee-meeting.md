## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Health & Human Services Committee held an orientation meeting for new board members, featuring educational presentations from department heads on the Health Department, Social Services, Veteran Services, and Aging & Disability Resource Center. No action items were voted on besides approval of April 1st meeting minutes. The committee learned about departmental missions, budgets, and ongoing initiatives including homelessness response efforts and veteran suicide prevention programs.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of April 1st Meeting Minutes
Supervisor Cavalli made a motion to approve the minutes, seconded by Vice Chair Rosenberg. Motion carried unanimously with voice vote.

### Standing Committee Ordinance Review
Administrator Lance Leonard explained the committee structure under section 2.04 subsection 5, covering jurisdiction over Health Department, Social Services, Veteran Services, and affiliated entities including North Central Health Care and ADRC. He outlined reporting relationships with statutory boards including Social Services Board, Veteran Service Commission, and Board of Health. Chair Boots noted the strategic plan process will be a focus for the committee over the next two years.

### Health Department Presentation
Health Officer Caitlyn Suginaka presented on the department's mission to prevent disease, promote health, and protect from environmental hazards. The department has 37.6 FTE staff across environmental health, nursing, community health improvement, and administration teams. She highlighted public health vending machines offering free resources including tick kits, the Nurse Family Partnership program with first graduates, and a new homelessness system response position. In response to Supervisor Kremes' question about pandemic preparedness, Suginaka confirmed staff are trained in emergency response with plans for measles and other infectious disease outbreaks.

### Social Services Overview
Administrator Leonard provided overview of the county's second largest department with nearly 140 FTE, covering child welfare, youth justice, children's long-term support, economic support, and child support. He highlighted the successful Family Keys pilot program addressing housing instability, which was modified based on board feedback. The department's 2026 budget shows $22 million total revenue with approximately $7 million in tax levy.

### Veteran Service Office Presentation
VSO Aaron Galindo reported his department increased to 3.0 FTE in 2025 and customer interactions jumped from 5,900 to 8,149. The office facilitated $44 million annually in benefits to county veterans, with $2.6 million in retroactive payments in 2025. Key initiatives include partnership with Center for Veterans Issues for housing stability, surviving spouse benefits outreach, and suicide prevention efforts (Marathon County had 30 suicides in 2025, 7 were veterans). In response to Supervisor Kremes' questions about staffing adequacy, Galindo acknowledged the office is busy but managing with the new FTE position.

### ADRC Presentation
Executive Director Mike Ray presented on the four-county Aging & Disability Resource Center serving Marathon, Lincoln, Langlade, and Wood counties. The agency provides information and assistance, meals on wheels, caregiver support, dementia care, and falls prevention. He noted significant financial challenges with nutrition program costs increasing 40-45% over four years while federal funding remained flat. The agency has approximately 60 FTE staff and relies on 385-400 volunteers. The levy remained flat from 2011 until requesting an increase in 2024.
