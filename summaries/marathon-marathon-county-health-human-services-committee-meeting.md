## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Health & Human Services Committee held an orientation meeting for new and returning members, featuring educational presentations from department heads on the Health Department, Social Services, Veteran Services, and Aging Disability Resource Center. The committee approved April 1st meeting minutes unanimously and received comprehensive overviews of each department's mission, budget structure, and current initiatives.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of April 1st Meeting Minutes
Supervisor Cavelli made a motion to approve the minutes, seconded by Vice Chair Rosenberg. The motion carried unanimously with all members voting aye.

### Standing Committee Ordinance Review
Administrator Lance Leonard explained the committee's jurisdiction under ordinance 2.04 sub 5, including oversight of Veteran Services, Social Services, Health Department, North Central Health Care, and ADRC. He detailed the committee's relationships with statutory bodies including the Social Services Board, Veteran Service Commission, Transportation Coordinating Committee, Board of Health, and North Central Healthcare Board.

### Health Department Presentation
Health Officer Caitlyn Suginaka presented the department's mission of preventing disease and protecting public health. She highlighted over 1,200 licenses overseen, infectious disease monitoring, Nurse Family Partnership program now graduating its first two-year-olds, and a new homeless system response position. The department has 37.6 FTE and approximately half of its $5 million budget comes from tax levy, with significant intergovernmental grant revenue.

### Social Services Overview
Administrator Leonard covered the department's divisions including child welfare, youth justice, children's long-term support, economic support, and child support. He highlighted the successful Family Keys housing stability pilot program addressing root causes of housing instability. The department has nearly 140 FTE with a $22 million budget, of which approximately $7 million is tax levy.

### Veteran Services Presentation
VSO Aaron Galindo reported 8,149 interactions in 2025, up from 5,900 the previous year. He noted $44 million in annual VA benefits flowing to Marathon County veterans, plus $2.6 million in retroactive payments in 2025. The department now has 3.0 FTE. Galindo discussed initiatives including surviving spouse benefits outreach, housing stability efforts with Center for Veterans Issues, and suicide prevention planning noting 7 of Marathon County's 30 suicides in 2025 were veterans.

### ADRC Presentation
Executive Director Mike Ray explained the four-county regional ADRC model serving Lincoln, Langlade, Marathon, and Wood counties. He described services including information assistance, Meals on Wheels, caregiver support, dementia care, and falls prevention. He noted significant financial challenges with nutrition program costs up 40-45% while federal funding remained flat since 2024. The organization has about 60 FTE and relies on 385-400 volunteers.
