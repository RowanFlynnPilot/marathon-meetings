## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Health & Human Services Committee held an educational meeting to orient new board members on the departments under their jurisdiction. Department heads from the Health Department, Veterans Services, and Aging & Disability Resource Center presented overviews of their programs, budgets, and current initiatives. The committee approved the April 1st meeting minutes unanimously.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of April 1st meeting minutes
Supervisor Cavalli made a motion to approve the minutes, seconded by Vice Chair Rosenberg. The motion passed unanimously with all in favor.

### Committee ordinance and jurisdiction overview
Administrator Lance Leonard explained the committee's structure under section 2.04 subsection 5, noting they oversee the Health Department, Social Services, Veterans Services, and receive reports from North Central Health Care and the Aging & Disability Resource Center. He explained reporting relationships with statutory boards including the Social Services Board, Veterans Service Commission, and Board of Health.

### Health Department presentation
Health Officer Caitlyn Suginaka presented on the department's 37.6 FTE staff and four divisions. She highlighted public health vending machines stocked with free tick kits and other supplies, the Nurse Family Partnership program graduating its first two-year-olds, and a new homeless system response position. The department has approximately $5 million budget with about half from tax levy. Supervisor Kremes asked about pandemic preparedness, and Suginaka confirmed the department has plans in place and staff trained in emergency response from COVID experience.

### Social Services Department overview
Administrator Leonard presented on behalf of Director Christa who was absent. The department has nearly 140 FTE with a $22 million budget, about $7 million from tax levy. He highlighted the successful Family Keys program addressing housing instability, noting it was substantially changed based on board feedback. He mentioned potential restructuring requests may come for children's long-term support and other units due to increased need.

### Veterans Services Department presentation
Veteran Service Officer Aaron Galindo reported his 3.0 FTE office had 8,149 veteran interactions in 2025, up from approximately 5,900 the prior year. He noted $44 million in annual VA compensation flows to Marathon County veterans, with $2.6 million in retroactive payments in 2025. He highlighted partnerships with Center for Veterans Issues for housing and suicide prevention efforts, noting 7 of 30 county suicides in 2025 were veterans. Supervisor Kremes asked about regional coordination, and Galindo explained state statute requires each county have a VSO but veterans can work with any office.

### Aging & Disability Resource Center presentation
Executive Director Mike Ray explained the ADRC serves four counties (Marathon, Lincoln, Langlade, Wood) with about 60 FTEs and 385-400 volunteers. He noted funding challenges with federal Older Americans Act not reauthorized in two years while nutrition costs increased 40-45% in four years. Marathon County's contribution is $413,135. The ADRC had not requested a levy increase from 2011 until 2024. Vice Chair Rosenberg thanked the program, mentioning volunteering at the Hmong American Center meal site.
