## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Health & Human Services Committee held an educational orientation meeting for new board members, featuring presentations from department heads on the Health Department, Social Services, Veteran Service Office, and Aging Disability Resource Center. No formal votes were taken beyond approval of minutes. The meeting provided an overview of services, budgets, and ongoing initiatives including homelessness response efforts and veteran services.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of April 1st Meeting Minutes
Supervisor Cavelli made a motion to approve the minutes, seconded by Vice Chair Rosenberg. The motion passed unanimously with all members voting in favor.

### Review of Standing Committee Ordinances
Administrator Lance Leonard explained the committee's jurisdiction under section 2.04 subsection 5, including oversight of Veteran Service, Social Services, and Health departments, plus affiliated entities North Central Health Care and the Aging Disability Resource Center. He described the committee's relationship with subordinate boards including the Social Services Board, Veteran Service Commission, Transportation Coordinating Committee, Board of Health, and North Central Community Action Program.

### Health Department Presentation
Health Officer Caitlyn Suginaka presented an overview of the department's mission to prevent disease, promote health, and protect from environmental hazards. She highlighted 1,200 licenses overseen, the Nurse Family Partnership program with its first graduates, a new homeless system response position, and public health vending machines offering free tick kits. The department has approximately 37.6 FTE across four divisions. Administrator Leonard noted about half of the department's $5 million budget comes from tax levy, with significant funding from public charges for services and intergovernmental grants.

### Emergency Preparedness Discussion
Supervisor Kremes asked about pandemic preparedness following news of an H5 virus outbreak on a cruise ship. Suginaka confirmed the department has plans in place, has conducted measles response training exercises, and staff are trained in emergency response from COVID experience. She explained the department would reallocate staff if needed and noted they monitor foodborne illness outbreaks, STIs in the jail population, and receive alerts about potential disease contacts.

### Social Services Department Overview
Administrator Leonard presented on behalf of Director Christa, describing the department's 140 FTE staff working in child welfare, youth justice, children's long-term support, economic support, and child support. He highlighted the successful Family Keys pilot program addressing housing instability, noting significant numbers of clients had never had credit accounts, bank accounts, or formal leases. The department's $22 million budget includes about $7 million in tax levy.

### Veteran Service Office Presentation
Veteran Service Officer Erin Galindo reported the office grew from 2.75 to 3.0 FTE in 2025 and had 8,149 client interactions, up from 5,900. He noted $44 million in annual VA compensation flows to Marathon County veterans, with $2.6 million in retroactive payments in 2025. Galindo discussed housing stability work with Center for Veterans Issues, suicide prevention initiatives (noting 7 of 30 county suicides in 2025 were veterans), and new legislation helping National Guard and Reserve veterans. Each staff member averages 20-30 contacts daily.

### Aging Disability Resource Center Presentation
Executive Director Mike Ray described the four-county ADRC serving Marathon, Lincoln, Langlade, and Wood counties with about 60 FTE and 385-400 volunteers. He explained services including the Meals on Wheels nutrition program, Medicare/Medicaid assistance, falls prevention, dementia care, and serving as the entry point for Family Care and IRIS programs. Ray noted significant financial challenges as federal funding has remained flat while nutrition costs increased 40-45% over four years, forcing reductions in meals served and staff. Marathon County's contribution is $413,135.
