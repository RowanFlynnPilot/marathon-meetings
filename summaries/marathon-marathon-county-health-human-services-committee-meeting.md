## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Health & Human Services Committee held an introductory meeting for new board members, providing educational presentations on the Health Department, Social Services, Veteran Service Office, and Aging Disability Resource Center. The only action taken was approval of April 1st meeting minutes. The meeting focused on orienting new supervisors to department missions, budgets, and services.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of April 1st meeting minutes
Supervisor Cavelli made a motion to approve the minutes, seconded by Vice Chair Rosenberg. The motion carried unanimously with all members voting in favor.

### Review of standing committee ordinance and structure
Administrator Lance Leonard explained the committee's jurisdiction over Health Department, Social Services, and Veteran Service Office, plus affiliated multi-county entities North Central Health Care and the Aging Disability Resource Center. He outlined statutory responsibilities and explained how the Social Services Board, Veteran Service Commission, Transportation Coordinating Committee, and Board of Health provide input but not direct oversight of departments.

### Health Department presentation
Health Officer Caitlyn Suginaka presented the department's mission to prevent disease, promote health, and protect from environmental hazards. The department has 37.6 FTE staff and oversees 1,200 licenses for restaurants, pools, campgrounds, and hotels. She highlighted the Nurse Family Partnership program graduating its first two-year-olds, a new homeless system response position funded jointly with City of Wausau, public health vending machines with free resources including tick kits, and lead mitigation efforts through a HUD grant. Administrator Leonard noted approximately half of the $5 million budget is tax levy with the rest from grants and fees.

### Social Services Department overview
Administrator Leonard presented on behalf of absent Director Christa, noting the department is the second largest with nearly 140 FTE. The department handles child welfare, youth justice, children's long-term support, economic support, and child support. He highlighted the successful Family Keys housing stability pilot program done in partnership with the state, which addresses root causes of housing instability for families. Of the $22 million budget, approximately $7 million is tax levy.

### Veteran Service Office presentation
Veteran Service Officer Aaron Galindo reported his office increased to 3.0 FTE in 2025 and had 8,149 veteran interactions, up significantly from 5,900 the prior year. He reported veterans in Marathon County receive $44 million annually in VA benefits, with $2.6 million in retroactive payments in 2025 alone. Galindo discussed initiatives including surviving spouse benefits outreach, housing stability partnerships with Center for Veterans Issues, and suicide prevention efforts noting Marathon County had 30 suicides in 2025, seven of whom were veterans. The office is exploring the Staff Sergeant Gordon Fox suicide prevention grant.

### Aging Disability Resource Center presentation
Executive Director Mike Ray explained the ADRC serves Marathon, Lincoln, Langlade, and Wood counties as a one-stop shop for aging and disability services. The organization has about 60 FTEs plus 385-400 volunteers. He discussed significant financial challenges with nutrition program costs up 40-45% over four years while federal funding remained flat, noting the Older Americans Act hasn't been reauthorized in over two years. Marathon County's contribution is $413,135 annually, with the first levy increase since 2011 granted in 2025.

### Emergency preparedness discussion
Supervisor Kremes asked about pandemic preparedness in light of norovirus outbreak news. Health Officer Suginaka confirmed the department has plans in place, conducts training exercises including recent measles response practice, and has a full-time public health emergency preparedness staff member. She noted staff would be reallocated from chronic disease work if needed for an outbreak response.
