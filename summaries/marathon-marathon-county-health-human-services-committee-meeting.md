## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Health & Human Services Committee held an educational orientation meeting for new and returning members, featuring presentations from department heads on the Health Department, Social Services, Veteran Services, and Aging Disability Resource Center. No action items were voted on; the meeting focused on introducing committee structure, departmental missions, and budget overviews.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of April 1st meeting minutes
Supervisor Cavelli made a motion to approve the minutes, seconded by Vice Chair Rosenberg. The motion passed unanimously with all members voting aye.

### Committee structure and ordinance review
Administrator Lance Leonard explained the committee's jurisdiction under ordinance section 2.04 sub-5, covering departments including Health, Social Services, and Veteran Services, plus affiliated entities North Central Health Care and the Aging Disability Resource Center. He outlined reporting relationships with the Social Services Board, Veterans Service Commission, Transportation Coordinating Committee, and Board of Health.

### Health Department presentation
Health Officer Caitlyn Suginaka presented the department's mission to prevent disease, promote health, and protect from environmental hazards. She highlighted 1,200 licenses overseen, public health vending machines stocked with free resources including tick kits, the Nurse Family Partnership program's first graduates, and a new homeless system response position. The department has 37.6 FTEs with about half of the $5 million budget from tax levy and significant grant funding.

### Social Services Department overview
Administrator Leonard provided an overview of the department's divisions including child welfare, youth justice, children's long-term support, economic support, and child support. He highlighted the successful Family Keys housing stability pilot program addressing root causes of housing instability. The department has nearly 140 FTEs with a $22 million budget, of which approximately $7 million is tax levy.

### Veteran Services Department presentation
VSO Erin Galindo reported the department increased to 3.0 FTEs and had 8,149 interactions in 2025. He emphasized PACT Act benefits, noting veterans received $2.6 million in retroactive payments in 2025 and $44 million annually flows into the county. Galindo highlighted suicide prevention efforts, noting Marathon County had 30 suicides in 2025 with 7 being veterans, and discussed partnership with Center for Veterans Issues for housing stability.

### Aging Disability Resource Center presentation
Executive Director Mike Ray explained the ADRC serves four counties (Marathon, Lincoln, Langlade, and Wood) as a one-stop shop for aging and disability resources. He noted significant financial challenges with nutrition program costs rising 40-45% over four years while federal funding remained flat. The organization has about 60 FTEs and relies on 385-400 volunteers, with approximately 11% of budget from county levy.
