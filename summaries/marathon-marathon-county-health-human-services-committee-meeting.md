## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Health & Human Services Committee held an orientation meeting for new board members, featuring presentations from department heads on the Health Department, Social Services, Veterans Service Office, and Aging & Disability Resource Center. No substantive votes were taken beyond approving April 1st meeting minutes. The meeting provided educational overviews of each department's mission, services, and budget structure.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of April 1st Minutes
Supervisor Cavelli moved to approve the minutes, seconded by Vice Chair Rosenberg. Motion carried unanimously with no discussion.

### Committee Structure and Jurisdiction
Administrator Lance Leonard explained how the committee oversees Health Department, Social Services, and Veterans Service Office as direct county departments, plus affiliated entities North Central Health Care and Aging & Disability Resource Center. He explained the committee's relationship to statutory boards including the Social Services Board, Veterans Service Commission, Board of Health, and Transportation Coordinating Committee.

### Health Department Presentation
Health Officer Caitlyn Suginaka presented on the department's four divisions: environmental health (1,200+ licenses for restaurants, pools, campgrounds), nursing/communicable disease, family health, and community health improvement. She highlighted the nurse family partnership program graduating its first two-year-olds, a new homelessness system response position, and public health vending machines providing free resources including tick kits. The department has 37.6 FTE with roughly half its $5 million budget from tax levy.

### Social Services Overview
Administrator Leonard presented on behalf of absent Director Christa, explaining the department's nearly 140 FTE covering child welfare, youth justice, children's long-term support, economic support, and child support. He highlighted the successful Family Keys housing stability pilot program partnering with the state, noting significant budget of $22 million with about $7 million from tax levy.

### Veterans Service Office Presentation
VSO Aaron Galindo reported the office increased from 2.75 to 3.0 FTE and had 8,149 interactions in 2025, up from 5,900. He noted $44 million annually flows to local veterans in benefits, with $2.6 million in retroactive payments in 2025 alone. Major initiatives include surviving spouse benefits outreach, housing stability partnerships with Center for Veterans Issues, and suicide prevention efforts after 7 of 30 county suicides in 2025 were veterans.

### ADRC Presentation
Executive Director Mike Ray explained the four-county regional model serving Marathon, Lincoln, Langlade, and Wood counties with about 60 FTE plus 400 volunteers. Services include Meals on Wheels, Medicare counseling, caregiver support, and dementia care. He noted severe financial pressure as nutrition costs rose 40-45% over four years while federal funding remained flat, forcing meal and staffing reductions. County levy contribution is $413,135.
