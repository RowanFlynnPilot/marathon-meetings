## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Health & Human Services Committee held an introductory meeting for new board members, providing educational presentations from department heads including the Health Department, Veteran Services, and Aging Disability Resource Center. The only formal action taken was approval of the April 1st meeting minutes, which passed unanimously. No policy items were brought forward for action.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of April 1st meeting minutes
Supervisor Cavelli made a motion to approve the minutes, seconded by Vice Chair Rosenberg. The motion passed unanimously with all members voting in favor.

### Health Department Overview
Health Officer Caitlyn Suginaka presented an overview of the department's mission to prevent disease, promote health, and protect from environmental hazards. The department has approximately 37.6 FTE staff across four teams: environmental health (overseeing 1,200+ licenses), nursing, community health improvement, and administration. Suginaka highlighted the new homeless system response position created through partnership with the City of Wausau, and discussed public health vending machines stocked with free resources including tick kits. Administrator Leonard noted about half of the department's $5 million budget is tax levy, with environmental health services designed to be self-supporting.

### Social Services Department Overview
Administrator Leonard presented on behalf of absent director Christa, explaining the department's nearly 140 FTE staff across divisions including child welfare, youth justice, children's long-term support, economic support, and child support. Of their $22 million revenue budget, approximately $7 million is tax levy. Leonard highlighted the successful Family Keys program addressing housing instability, which was piloted with state funding and substantially modified based on board feedback. The program addresses root causes of housing instability, noting many participants had never had credit accounts or formal leases.

### Veteran Service Department Overview
Veteran Service Officer Aaron Galindo reported his department increased from 2.75 to 3.0 FTE this year. In 2025, they had 8,149 interactions with veterans and families, up from approximately 5,900 the prior year. Galindo reported veterans in Marathon County received $2.6 million in retroactive VA payments in 2025, with total annual benefits of $44 million flowing into the county. The department is focusing on surviving spouse benefits, housing stability (partnering with Center for Veterans Issues), and suicide prevention (noting 7 of 30 county suicides in 2025 were veterans). Supervisor Kremes asked about staffing adequacy, and Galindo acknowledged they don't have enough time but do well with current resources.

### Aging Disability Resource Center Overview
Executive Director Mike Ray explained the ADRC serves four counties (Marathon, Lincoln, Langlade, and Wood) with about 60 FTE staff and 385-400 volunteers. The organization receives about 11% of its budget from county levy, with Marathon County contributing $413,135. Ray described significant financial challenges, noting federal Older Americans Act funding has remained flat while nutrition program costs increased 40-45% over four years. The organization has had to reduce meals served, service days, and staffing. Ray noted they hadn't requested a levy increase from 2011 until 2024. Vice Chair Rosenberg praised the culturally appropriate nutrition program at the Hmong American Center.

### Strategic Plan Discussion
Chair Boots explained that the county completed its comprehensive plan last session and will be working on a new strategic plan, targeting completion by December. He indicated he will visit all standing committee meetings to discuss how each committee will follow through on strategic plan objectives, noting that educational presentations should advance strategic plan goals.
