## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Health & Human Services Committee held an educational orientation meeting for new board members, featuring presentations from department heads on the Health Department, Social Services, Veteran Services, and Aging & Disability Resource Center. No action items were voted on beyond approving April 1st meeting minutes. The meeting focused on introducing committee jurisdiction, department missions, budgets, and key programs.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of April 1st Meeting Minutes
Supervisor Cavelli made a motion to approve the minutes, seconded by Vice Chair Rosenberg. The motion carried unanimously with no opposition.

### Standing Committee Jurisdiction Overview
Administrator Lance Leonard explained the committee's structure under ordinance section 2.04 subsection 5, including oversight of Veterans Service Department, Social Services Department, Health Department, North Central Health Care, and Aging Disability Resource Center. He explained reporting relationships with statutory boards including the Social Services Board, Veteran Service Commission, Transportation Coordinating Committee, and Board of Health.

### Health Department Presentation
Health Officer Caitlyn Suginaka presented the department's mission of preventing disease, promoting health, and protecting from environmental hazards. She highlighted 1,200 licenses overseen, communicable disease prevention, Nurse Family Partnership program (first graduates at age 2), and a new FTE position for homeless system response. The department has 37.6 FTE and approximately half of their $5 million budget comes from tax levy, with significant grant and fee revenue.

### Social Services Department Overview
Administrator Leonard presented on behalf of Director Christa (not present). The department has nearly 140 FTE handling child welfare, youth justice, children's long-term support, economic support, and child support. Their 2026 budget is over $22 million with about $7 million in tax levy. Leonard highlighted the successful Family Keys housing stability pilot program addressing housing instability among child welfare and youth justice populations.

### Veteran Services Department Presentation
Veteran Service Officer Aaron Galindo reported his department increased from 2.75 to 3.0 FTE in 2025. Customer interactions jumped from 5,900 to 8,149 in 2025. The department brings $44 million annually in VA compensation to local veterans, with $2.6 million in retroactive payments in 2025. Galindo discussed new suicide prevention initiatives (noting 30 county suicides in 2025, 7 being veterans), housing stability partnerships with Center for Veterans Issues, and efforts to help surviving spouses with dependency and indemnity compensation benefits.

### ADRC Presentation
Executive Director Mike Ray explained the four-county Aging & Disability Resource Center serves Marathon, Lincoln, Langlade, and Wood counties with about 60 FTE and 385-400 volunteers. Services include Meals on Wheels, Medicare counseling, caregiver support, dementia care, and falls prevention. Ray noted significant budget challenges with nutrition program costs up 40-45% over four years while federal funding remained flat. Marathon County's 2026 contribution is $413,135. The organization had no levy increase from 2011-2024.
