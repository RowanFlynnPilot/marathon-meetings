## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Health & Human Services Committee held an educational orientation meeting for new and returning members, featuring presentations from the Health Department, Veteran Service Office, and Aging Disability Resource Center. No substantive votes were taken beyond approving previous meeting minutes; the meeting focused on educating committee members about department functions, budgets, and current initiatives.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of April 1st meeting minutes
Supervisor Cavelli made a motion to approve the minutes, seconded by Vice Chair Rosenberg. The motion carried unanimously with all members voting aye.

### Committee structure and strategic plan overview
Administrator Lance Leonard explained the committee's jurisdiction over Veterans Service, Social Services, Health Department, North Central Health Care, and the Aging Disability Resource Center. Chair Boots noted the county completed its comprehensive plan last session and will develop a new strategic plan by December 2025. He indicated future meetings will track progress against strategic plan objectives.

### Health Department presentation
Health Officer Caitlyn Suginaka presented on the department's 37.6 FTE staff across four divisions: environmental health, nursing, community health improvement, and administration. She highlighted the department's work on food/water safety with over 1,200 licenses, a new homeless system response position, and the Nurse Family Partnership program that just graduated its first cohort of two-year-olds. Supervisor Kremes asked about pandemic preparedness; Suginaka confirmed the department has emergency plans, trained staff, and recently conducted measles response exercises.

### Social Services Department overview
Administrator Leonard presented on behalf of absent Director Christa, describing the department's nearly 140 FTE staff handling child welfare, youth justice, children's long-term support, economic support, and child support. He highlighted the successful Family Keys housing stability pilot program, noting many participants had never had bank accounts or formal leases before. The department's $22 million budget includes approximately $7 million in tax levy.

### Veteran Service Office presentation
Veteran Service Officer Aaron Galindo reported his 3.0 FTE office had 8,149 veteran interactions in 2025, up significantly from 5,900 the previous year. He noted $44 million in annual VA compensation flows to Marathon County veterans, with $2.6 million in retroactive payments in 2025 alone. Key initiatives include surviving spouse benefits outreach, housing stability partnerships with Center for Veterans Issues, and suicide prevention efforts following 7 veteran suicides among the county's 30 total suicides in 2025. Supervisor Kremes asked about regional coordination; Galindo explained veterans can use any county's VSO and that state statute requires each county to have its own office.

### Aging Disability Resource Center presentation
Executive Director Mike Ray presented on the four-county ADRC serving Marathon, Lincoln, Langlade, and Wood counties with approximately 60 FTEs and 400 volunteers. He noted significant financial challenges with federal nutrition program funding flat since 2024 while food costs rose 40-45%. Marathon County's contribution is $413,135. The ADRC went from 2011 to 2024 without requesting a levy increase. Vice Chair Rosenberg praised the culturally appropriate meals program at the Hmong American Center.

### Next meeting date correction
Vice Chair Rosenberg caught an error in the announced next meeting date. Administrator Leonard had stated May 29th, but that is not a Wednesday. The correct date was confirmed as Tuesday, June 3rd at 3:00 PM.
