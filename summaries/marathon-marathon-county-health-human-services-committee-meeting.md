## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Health & Human Services Committee held an orientation meeting for new board members, featuring educational presentations from department heads on the Health Department, Social Services, Veteran Services, and Aging & Disability Resource Center. No action items were voted on beyond approving April 1st meeting minutes. The meeting focused on familiarizing new supervisors with departmental missions, budgets, and organizational structures.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of April 1st Meeting Minutes
Supervisor Cavelli moved to approve the minutes, seconded by Vice Chair Rosenberg. Motion carried unanimously with voice vote.

### Standing Committee Ordinance Review
Administrator Leonard explained the committee structure under section 2.04 sub 5, including jurisdiction over Health Department, Social Services, Veteran Services, and affiliated entities like North Central Health Care and ADRC. He outlined reporting relationships with various statutory boards including Social Services Board, Veteran Service Commission, and Board of Health.

### Health Department Presentation
Health Officer Caitlyn Suginaka presented an overview of the department's 37.6 FTE staff serving four divisions: environmental health (1,200+ licenses for restaurants, pools, etc.), nursing/communicable disease, family health, and community health improvement. She highlighted the new homeless system response position, nurse family partnership program with first graduates, and public health vending machines offering free resources including tick kits. Administrator Leonard noted approximately half of the $5 million budget is tax levy with significant grant and fee revenue.

### Social Services Department Overview
Administrator Leonard presented on behalf of Director Christa, describing the nearly 140 FTE department covering child welfare, youth justice, children's long-term support, economic support, and child support. He highlighted the successful Family Keys housing stability pilot program in partnership with the state, noting significant outcomes addressing root causes of housing instability. The $22 million budget includes approximately $7 million in tax levy.

### Veteran Services Presentation
VSO Erin Galindo reported his 3.0 FTE office handled 8,149 interactions in 2025, up significantly from prior years. The office facilitated $44 million annually in VA benefits to local veterans plus $2.6 million in retroactive payments in 2025. He discussed priorities including surviving spouse benefits, housing stability through Center for Veterans Issues partnership, and suicide prevention (noting 7 of 30 county suicides in 2025 were veterans). Supervisor Kremes asked about regional coordination; Galindo explained state statute requires each county have a VSO but offices cooperate across boundaries.

### ADRC Presentation
Executive Director Mike Ray presented on the four-county regional organization serving Lincoln, Langlade, Marathon and Wood counties with about 60 FTEs and 385-400 volunteers. Services include information/assistance, Meals on Wheels nutrition program, Medicare/Medicaid counseling, falls prevention, and entry point for Family Care/IRIS programs. He noted significant financial challenges with federal funding flat since 2024 while nutrition costs increased 40-45% in four years, forcing service reductions. Marathon County contributes $413,135 in levy.
