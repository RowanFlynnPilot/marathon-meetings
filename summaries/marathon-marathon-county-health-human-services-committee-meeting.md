## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Health & Human Services Committee held an orientation meeting for new board members, featuring educational presentations from department heads including the Health Department, Social Services (presented by Administrator Leonard), Veterans Service Office, and Aging Disability Resource Center. The only formal action was approval of the April 1st meeting minutes. No policy decisions or substantive votes were taken.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of April 1st Meeting Minutes
Supervisor Cavelli made a motion to approve the minutes, seconded by Vice Chair Rosenberg. The motion passed unanimously with all members voting in favor.

### Standing Committee Ordinance Review
Administrator Leonard explained the committee's structure under ordinance section 2.04 sub5, covering jurisdiction over the Health Department, Social Services, Veterans Service Office, North Central Health Care, and Aging Disability Resource Center. He outlined statutory responsibilities and relationships with subordinate boards including the Social Services Board, Veterans Service Commission, Transportation Coordinating Committee, and Board of Health.

### Health Department Presentation
Health Officer Caitlyn Suginaka presented the department's mission of preventing disease, promoting health, and protecting from environmental hazards. She highlighted over 1,200 licenses overseen, the Nurse Family Partnership program with first graduates at age 2, a new homeless system response position funded through partnership with the City of Wausau, and public health vending machines stocked with free resources including tick kits. The department has 37.6 FTE across four teams. Supervisor Kremes asked about pandemic preparedness; Suginaka confirmed plans are in place with staff trained in emergency response.

### Social Services Department Overview
Administrator Leonard presented on behalf of Director Christa Kiernan. The department has nearly 140 FTE handling child welfare, youth justice, children's long-term support, economic support, and child support. He highlighted the successful Family Keys housing stability pilot program conducted with state partners, noting many clients served had never had credit accounts, bank accounts, or formal leases before entering the program.

### Veterans Service Office Presentation
VSO Aaron Galindo reported the office increased from 2.75 to 3.0 FTE in 2025 and saw customer interactions jump from 5,900 to 8,149. He noted $44 million in annual VA compensation flows to Marathon County veterans, plus $2.6 million in retroactive payments in 2025. Key initiatives include surviving spouse benefits outreach, housing stability partnerships with Center for Veterans Issues, and suicide prevention efforts (7 of 30 county suicides in 2025 were veterans). Supervisor Kremes asked about regional cooperation; Galindo explained good relationships exist with neighboring counties and veterans can work with any CVSO statewide.

### ADRC Presentation
Executive Director Mike Ray explained the four-county regional organization (Marathon, Lincoln, Langlade, Wood) serves as the one-stop shop for aging and disability resources. The ADRC handles functional eligibility for Family Care and Iris programs, caregiver support, dementia care, falls prevention, and meals on wheels. Ray noted significant financial challenges with federal funding flat since 2024 while nutrition program costs increased 40-45% over four years, forcing meal reductions. Marathon County's contribution is $413,135 annually. The organization relies on approximately 385-400 volunteers.
