## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Health & Human Services Committee held an organizational meeting for new board members, featuring educational presentations from the Health Department, Veteran Services, and Aging Disability Resource Center. The committee approved April 1st meeting minutes unanimously and received detailed overviews of department missions, budgets, and current initiatives including homelessness response efforts and veteran suicide prevention programs.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of April 1st Meeting Minutes
Supervisor Cavelli made a motion to approve the minutes, seconded by Vice Chair Rosenberg. The motion passed unanimously with all members voting aye.

### Standing Committee Ordinances Review
Administrator Lance Leonard explained the committee structure under ordinance section 2.04 subsection 5, including jurisdiction over Health Department, Social Services, Veteran Services, North Central Health Care, and ADRC. He outlined statutory requirements and relationships with boards including the Social Services Board, Veteran Service Commission, Transportation Coordinating Committee, and Board of Health. Chair Boots noted the upcoming strategic plan development expected to be completed by December.

### Health Department Overview
Health Officer Caitlyn Suginaka presented on the department's 37.6 FTE staff across four divisions: environmental health, nursing (communicable disease and family health), community health improvement, and administration. She highlighted 1,200+ licenses overseen, the Nurse Family Partnership program with first graduates at age 2, new homeless system response position, and public health vending machines offering free resources including tick kits. Administrator Leonard noted roughly half of the $5 million budget is tax levy with environmental health being largely self-supporting through fees.

### Social Services Department Overview
Administrator Leonard presented on behalf of absent Director Christa, describing the department's nearly 140 FTE staff handling child welfare, youth justice, children's long-term support, economic support, and child support. He highlighted the successful Family Keys pilot program addressing housing instability, noting the program was substantively improved based on county board feedback. The department's $22 million budget includes about $7 million in tax levy.

### Veteran Service Department Overview
VSO Erin Galindo reported on the small but mighty 3.0 FTE department that had 8,149 interactions in 2025, up from 5,900. He emphasized the PACT Act's impact on presumptive conditions, noting $44 million annually flows to Marathon County veterans with $2.6 million in retroactive payments in 2025. Key initiatives include surviving spouse benefits outreach, veteran housing stability partnership with Center for Veterans Issues, and suicide prevention efforts following 7 veteran suicides among 30 total county suicides in 2025. Supervisor Kremes asked about regional coordination; Galindo explained state statute requires each county to have a VSO and that regionalizing was previously rejected.

### ADRC Presentation
Executive Director Mike Ray described the four-county integrated organization serving Marathon, Lincoln, Langlade, and Wood counties with about 60 FTEs and 385-400 volunteers. He explained services including information assistance, caregiver support, dementia care, falls prevention, and Meals on Wheels. Ray noted significant financial challenges with nutrition program costs up 40-45% in four years while federal funding remained flat, forcing meal and staffing reductions. The organization received its first levy increase in 2024/2025 after remaining flat since formation in 2011. Marathon County's 2026 contribution is $413,135.
