## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Health & Human Services Committee held an educational orientation meeting for new board members, featuring presentations from the Health Department, Social Services, Veteran Service Office, and Aging Disability Resource Center. No action items were voted on besides approving the April 1st meeting minutes. The meeting focused on introducing department missions, budgets, and current initiatives including homelessness response, veteran services, and nutrition programs.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of April 1st committee meeting minutes
Supervisor Cavalli made a motion to approve the minutes, seconded by Vice Chair Rosenberg. The motion passed unanimously with all members voting in favor.

### Health Department presentation
Health Officer Caitlyn Suginaka presented the department's mission of preventing disease, promoting health, and protecting the public. She highlighted 1,200+ licenses overseen, a new homeless system response position funded through partnership with City of Wausau, public health vending machines stocked with free resources including tick kits, and the Nurse Family Partnership program now graduating its first two-year-olds. The department has approximately 37.6 FTE with about half the $5 million budget from tax levy. Supervisor Kremes asked about pandemic preparedness, and Suginaka confirmed plans are in place with staff trained in emergency response from COVID experience.

### Social Services Department overview
Administrator Leonard presented on behalf of Director Christa who was unavailable. He described the department's divisions including child welfare, youth justice, children's long-term support, and economic/child support services with about 140 FTE. He highlighted the successful Family Keys housing stability pilot program that addresses root causes of housing instability, noting many participants had never had a credit account, bank account, or formal lease. The department has a $22 million budget with approximately $7 million from tax levy.

### Veteran Service Office presentation
VSO Aaron Galindo reported the office now has 3.0 FTE and had 8,149 interactions in 2025, up from 5,900 projected. He noted $44 million in annual benefits flowing to local veterans and $2.6 million in retroactive payments in 2025. Key initiatives include working with Center for Veterans Issues on veteran housing, suicide prevention efforts (noting 7 of 30 county suicides in 2025 were veterans), and helping surviving spouses with dependency and indemnity compensation benefits of up to $2,400/month. Supervisor Kremes asked about regional cooperation, and Galindo confirmed good working relationships with neighboring counties while noting state statute requires each county to have its own VSO.

### Aging Disability Resource Center presentation
Executive Director Mike Ray explained the ADRC serves four counties (Marathon, Lincoln, Langlade, Wood) as an integrated organization providing aging and disability services. He noted significant financial challenges with nutrition costs up 40-45% over four years while federal funding remained flat due to Older Americans Act not being reauthorized since 2024. The organization has about 60 FTEs plus 385-400 volunteers. Vice Chair Rosenberg praised the culturally appropriate nutrition program at the Hmong American Center.
