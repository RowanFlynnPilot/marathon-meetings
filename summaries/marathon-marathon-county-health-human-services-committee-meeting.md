## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Health & Human Services Committee held an introductory meeting for new board members, providing educational presentations from department heads on the Health Department, Social Services, Veteran Services, and the Aging Disability Resource Center. The committee approved the April 1st meeting minutes unanimously and received detailed briefings on department missions, budgets, and current initiatives.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of April 1st meeting minutes
Supervisor Cavalli made a motion to approve the minutes, seconded by Vice Chair Rosenberg. The motion passed unanimously with all members voting in favor.

### Review of standing committee ordinance and jurisdiction
Administrator Lance Leonard provided an overview of the committee's structure under section 2.04 sub5, explaining reporting relationships with county departments (Health, Social Services, Veterans) and affiliated entities (North Central Health Care, Aging Disability Resource Center). He explained the committee's role in relation to the Social Services Board, Veteran Service Commission, Transportation Coordinating Committee, and Board of Health. Chair Boots noted the upcoming strategic plan development expected to be completed by December.

### Health Department presentation
Health Officer Caitlyn Suginaka presented an overview of the department's four divisions: environmental health, nursing (communicable disease and family health), community health improvement, and administration. The department has 37.6 FTE staff. She highlighted programs including over 1,200 licenses for restaurants and pools, public health vending machines with free tick kits, the Nurse Family Partnership program with first graduates, and a new homeless system response position. The department's 2026 budget is approximately $5 million with about half from tax levy.

### Social Services Department overview
Administrator Leonard presented on behalf of Director Christa who was absent. The department has nearly 140 FTE and a 2026 revenue budget of over $22 million with approximately $7 million in tax levy. He highlighted the successful Family Keys housing stability pilot program and noted potential restructuring requests for child support and other units due to increased need.

### Veteran Services Department presentation
Veteran Service Officer Erin Galindo reported the department increased from 2.75 to 3.0 FTE this year. Customer interactions jumped from 5,900 to 8,149 in 2025. Veterans in Marathon County receive $44 million annually in benefits, with $2.6 million in retroactive payments in 2025. Key initiatives include housing stability through HUD-VASH partnerships with Center for Veterans Issues, and suicide prevention efforts following 7 veteran suicides among 30 total county suicides in 2025. The department is exploring the Staff Sergeant Gordon Fox VA suicide prevention grant.

### Aging Disability Resource Center presentation
Executive Director Mike Ray presented on the four-county ADRC serving Marathon, Lincoln, Langlade, and Wood counties. The organization has about 60 FTEs plus 385-400 volunteers. Marathon County's 2026 contribution is $413,135. He noted significant financial challenges with nutrition program costs increasing 40-45% over four years while federal funding has remained flat due to the Older American Act not being reauthorized since 2024. The organization had not requested a levy increase from 2011 until 2024.
