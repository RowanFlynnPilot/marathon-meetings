## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Health & Human Services Committee held an orientation meeting for new members, featuring educational presentations from the Health Department, Veteran Services, and Aging Disability Resource Center. The committee approved the April 1st meeting minutes and received detailed overviews of department missions, budgets, and key programs including homelessness response, veteran services, and nutrition programs for seniors.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of April 1st meeting minutes
Supervisor Cavalli made a motion to approve the minutes, seconded by Vice Chair Rosenberg. The motion carried unanimously with all members voting in favor.

### Review of standing committee jurisdiction and ordinances
Administrator Lance Leonard explained the committee's structure under section 2.04 subsection 5, including oversight of the Health Department, Social Services, and Veteran Services. He noted the committee also receives reports from North Central Health Care, ADRC of Central Wisconsin, Social Services Board, Veteran Service Commission, Transportation Coordinating Committee, and Board of Health. Chair Boots noted the upcoming strategic plan development expected to be completed by December.

### Health Department presentation
Health Officer Caitlyn Suginaka presented the department's mission of preventing disease, promoting health, and protecting from environmental hazards. She highlighted over 1,200 licenses overseen, the Nurse Family Partnership program now graduating its first two-year-olds, a new homeless system response position funded through county board and city of Wausau partnership, and free public health vending machines stocked with tick kits and harm reduction supplies. The department has approximately 37.6 FTE with about half of its $5 million budget from tax levy and significant intergovernmental grant revenues.

### Social Services Department overview
Administrator Leonard presented on behalf of Director Christa who was unavailable. He described the department's divisions including child welfare, youth justice, children's long-term support, economic support, and child support. The department has nearly 140 FTE with a 2026 budget over $22 million but only about $7 million from tax levy. Leonard highlighted the successful Family Keys housing stability pilot program implemented with state funding that addresses root causes of housing instability among families.

### Veteran Services Department presentation
Veteran Service Officer Erin Galindo reported the department increased to 3.0 FTE as of 2025. Customer interactions increased from 5,900 to 8,149 in 2025. The department brought in $44 million annually in veteran benefits to the county, with $2.6 million in retroactive payments in 2025 alone. Galindo discussed priorities including surviving spouse benefits through the PACT Act, housing stability through HUDVASH partnerships, and suicide prevention noting 7 of 30 county suicides in 2025 were veterans. He mentioned pursuing the Staff Sergeant Gordon Fox suicide prevention grant.

### Aging Disability Resource Center presentation
Executive Director Mike Ray explained the ADRC serves four counties (Marathon, Lincoln, Langlade, and Wood) as a one-stop shop for aging and disability services. He noted significant financial challenges with nutrition program costs up 40-45% over four years while federal funding remained flat. The ADRC has about 60 FTE plus 385-400 volunteers. Marathon County's contribution is $413,135. Ray noted they hadn't requested a levy increase from 2011 until 2024 due to the efficiency of the regional model.
