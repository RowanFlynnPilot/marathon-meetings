## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Health & Human Services Committee held an educational orientation meeting for new and returning members. The committee approved April 1st meeting minutes unanimously and received comprehensive presentations from department heads about the Health Department, Social Services, Veteran Service Office, and Aging Disability Resource Center operations and budgets. No major policy decisions were made as this was primarily an informational session.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of April 1st Minutes
Supervisor Cavelli made a motion to approve the minutes. Vice Chair Rosenberg seconded. The motion carried unanimously with all members voting aye.

### Standing Committee Ordinance Review
Administrator Lance Leonard explained the committee's jurisdiction under section 2.04 subsection 5, covering Health Department, Social Services, and Veteran Services as direct county departments, plus North Central Health Care and ADRC as multi-county entities. He outlined statutory requirements including the Social Services Board, Veteran Service Commission, Transportation Coordinating Committee, and Board of Health. Chair Boots noted the committee will focus on developing a new strategic plan by December.

### Health Department Presentation
Health Officer Caitlyn Suginaka presented the department's mission of preventing disease, promoting health, and protecting from environmental hazards. The department has 37.6 FTE across four divisions: environmental health (1,200+ licenses), nursing/communicable disease, family health, and community health improvement. She highlighted the Nurse Family Partnership program graduating its first cohort of 2-year-olds, public health vending machines with free tick kits, and a new homeless system response position funded by the county and City of Wausau. Supervisor Cavelli asked about vending machine locations - one is at community partners campus, another believed to be at Health First.

### Social Services Department Overview
Administrator Leonard presented on behalf of Director Christa who could not attend. The department has nearly 140 FTE handling child welfare, youth justice, children's long-term support, economic support, and child support. Budget is over $22 million with approximately $7 million from tax levy. Leonard highlighted the successful Family Keys housing stability pilot program addressing housing instability among child welfare and youth justice families, noting significant numbers of participants had never had credit accounts, bank accounts, or formal leases.

### Veteran Service Office Presentation
VSO Aaron Galindo reported his small but mighty team of 3.0 FTE had 8,149 interactions in 2025, up from 5,900. Veterans in Marathon County receive $44 million annually in VA benefits, with $2.6 million in retroactive payments in 2025. Key initiatives include expanded surviving spouse benefits under the PACT Act, housing stability efforts with Center for Veterans Issues establishing local office space, and suicide prevention focus after 7 of 30 county suicides in 2025 were veterans. Galindo noted appointment wait times reduced from 6 weeks to under 3 weeks after reaching full staffing.

### ADRC Presentation
Executive Director Mike Ray presented on the four-county Aging Disability Resource Center serving Marathon, Lincoln, Langlade, and Wood counties with about 60 FTE and 385-400 volunteers. The ADRC provides one-stop aging and disability services, Meals on Wheels nutrition program, Medicare counseling, falls prevention, and entry point services for Family Care and IRIS programs. Ray noted severe financial challenges with federal Older Americans Act funding flat since 2024 while nutrition costs increased 40-45%, forcing meal and staffing reductions. Marathon County contribution is $413,135.
