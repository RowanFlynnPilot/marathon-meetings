## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Health & Human Services Committee held an orientation meeting for new and returning board members, featuring educational presentations from the Health Department, Veteran Service Office, and Aging Disability Resource Center. The only formal action taken was approval of the April 1st meeting minutes. Department heads outlined their missions, services, and budget structures.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of April 1st Meeting Minutes
Supervisor Cavelli moved to approve the minutes, seconded by Vice Chair Rosenberg. The motion carried unanimously with no discussion.

### Standing Committee Ordinance Review
Administrator Lance Leonard explained the committee's jurisdiction under ordinance section 2.04 subsection 5, covering the Health Department, Social Services Department, Veteran Service Office, North Central Health Care, and Aging Disability Resource Center. He explained the committee's relationship with subordinate bodies including the Social Services Board, Veteran Service Commission, Transportation Coordinating Committee, and Board of Health.

### Health Department Presentation
Health Officer Caitlyn Suginaka presented an overview of the department's mission to prevent disease, promote health, and protect from environmental hazards. She highlighted programs including 1,200+ licenses for food establishments and public facilities, nurse family partnership for pregnant mothers, public health vending machines offering free tick kits and harm reduction supplies, and a new homeless system response position created from the previous session's task force. The department has 37.6 FTE and is funded approximately half by tax levy with significant grant revenue.

### Social Services Department Overview
Administrator Leonard presented on behalf of Director Christa (not present), explaining the department's 140 FTE staff covering child welfare, youth justice, children's long-term support, economic support, and child support programs. He highlighted the successful Family Keys pilot program addressing housing instability, noting the program was substantively changed based on board feedback. The department's $22 million budget includes approximately $7 million in tax levy.

### Veteran Service Office Presentation
Veteran Service Officer Erin Galindo reported the office grew from 2.75 to 3.0 FTE in 2025. Customer interactions increased from 5,900 to 8,149 in 2025. Veterans in Marathon County received $44 million annually in benefits plus $2.6 million in retroactive payments in 2025. Galindo highlighted initiatives including surviving spouse benefits outreach, housing stability programs with Center for Veterans Issues, and suicide prevention efforts noting 7 of 30 county suicides in 2025 were veterans. In response to Supervisor Kremes' question about staffing adequacy, Galindo acknowledged the office is under-manned but performing well with current resources.

### ADRC Presentation
Executive Director Mike Ray explained the four-county (Marathon, Lincoln, Langlade, Wood) organization providing aging and disability services with approximately 60 FTE and 385-400 volunteers. He noted significant financial challenges with nutrition program costs up 40-45% over four years while federal funding remained flat. The organization received its first levy increase in over a decade in 2025, with Marathon County contributing $413,135 for 2026. Vice Chair Rosenberg praised the culturally appropriate meal program at the Hmong American Center.
