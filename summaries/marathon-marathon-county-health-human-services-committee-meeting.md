## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Health & Human Services Committee held an orientation meeting for new board members, approving April 1st meeting minutes and receiving educational presentations from department heads about the Health Department, Social Services, Veterans Service Office, and Aging & Disability Resource Center. No substantive policy actions were taken beyond the minutes approval.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of April 1st meeting minutes
Supervisor Cavalli made a motion to approve the minutes, seconded by Vice Chair Rosenberg. The motion carried with all in favor voting 'aye' with no opposition.

### Health Department overview
Health Officer Caitlyn Suginaka presented on the department's mission to prevent disease, promote health, and protect against environmental hazards. She highlighted over 1,200 licenses overseen, communicable disease prevention including recent tick awareness efforts, Nurse Family Partnership program graduating its first two-year-olds, and the new homeless system response position created by the previous board's task force. The department has approximately 37.6 FTE across four teams: environmental health, nursing, community health improvement, and administration.

### Social Services Department overview
Administrator Lance Leonard presented on behalf of Director Christa (not present). He explained the department's 140 FTE structure covering child welfare, youth justice, children's long-term support, economic support, and child support. He highlighted the successful Family Keys housing stability pilot program addressing root causes of housing instability, noting many participants had never had credit accounts, bank accounts, or formal leases. He mentioned potential restructuring requests may come for increased staffing needs.

### Veterans Service Office overview
Veteran Service Officer Aaron Galindo presented on the 3.0 FTE department serving veterans and their dependents. He reported 8,149 interactions in 2025 (up from 5,900 in 2024), $44 million annually coming to local veterans, and $2.6 million in retroactive payments in 2025. Key initiatives include surviving spouse benefits outreach, housing stability partnerships with Center for Veterans Issues, and suicide prevention efforts (noting 7 of 30 county suicides in 2025 were veterans). The Man of Honor Society provided $116,000 to local veterans in 2025.

### ADRC presentation
Executive Director Mike Ray presented on the four-county Aging & Disability Resource Center serving Marathon, Lincoln, Langlade, and Wood counties. He described services including information assistance, meals on wheels nutrition program, falls prevention, Medicare/Medicaid benefits counseling, and caregiver support. He noted significant financial challenges with nutrition costs up 40-45% over four years while federal funding remains flat, requiring meal and staffing reductions. The organization has about 60 FTEs and relies on 385-400 volunteers.
