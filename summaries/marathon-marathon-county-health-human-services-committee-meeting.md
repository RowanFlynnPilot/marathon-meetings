## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Health & Human Services Committee held an introductory meeting for new board members, featuring educational presentations from department heads covering the Health Department, Social Services, Veteran Services, and Aging & Disability Resource Center. The committee approved April 1st meeting minutes unanimously and received comprehensive overviews of each department's mission, services, staffing, and budget structures.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of April 1st Meeting Minutes
Supervisor Cavelli made a motion to approve the minutes, seconded by Vice Chair Rosenberg. The motion passed unanimously with all members voting in favor.

### Standing Committee Ordinances Review
Administrator Lance Leonard explained the committee's structure under section 2.04 sub5, covering jurisdiction over Health Department, Social Services, Veteran Services, North Central Health Care, and ADRC. He detailed reporting relationships with subordinate bodies including the Social Services Board, Veteran Service Commission, Transportation Coordinating Committee, and Board of Health. Chair Boots noted the upcoming strategic plan development expected to be completed by December.

### Health Department Presentation
Health Officer Caitlyn Suginaka presented the department's mission to prevent disease, promote health, and protect from environmental hazards. The department manages over 1,200 licenses for restaurants, pools, and hotels, and operates public health vending machines with free resources including tick kits. She highlighted the Nurse Family Partnership program with first graduates, a new homeless system response position, and emergency preparedness capabilities. The department has 37.6 FTE with approximately half of its $5 million budget from tax levy.

### Social Services Department Overview
Administrator Leonard presented on behalf of Director Christa who was unavailable. The department has nearly 140 FTE handling child welfare, youth justice, children's long-term support, economic support, and child support. He highlighted the successful Family Keys housing stability pilot program addressing housing instability among child welfare and youth justice populations. The department's 2026 budget exceeds $22 million with approximately $7 million from tax levy.

### Veteran Service Department Presentation
Veteran Service Officer Aaron Galindo reported his office increased to 3.0 FTE and had 8,149 interactions in 2025, up from 5,900 the previous year. He highlighted PACT Act benefits bringing $44 million annually to local veterans and $2.6 million in retroactive payments in 2025. The office is focusing on surviving spouse benefits, veteran housing stability through partnership with Center for Veterans Issues, and suicide prevention. He noted 30 suicides in Marathon County in 2025, seven of which were veterans.

### ADRC Presentation
Executive Director Mike Ray explained the four-county integrated organization serving aging and disability populations. Services include information assistance, options counseling, caregiver support, dementia care specialists, falls prevention, benefit specialists, and Meals on Wheels nutrition program. He noted significant financial challenges with nutrition costs up 40-45% over four years while federal funding remained flat. The organization has about 60 FTE and 385-400 volunteers, with Marathon County contributing $413,135 in levy.
