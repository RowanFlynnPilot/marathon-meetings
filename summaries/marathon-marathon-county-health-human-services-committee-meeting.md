## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Health & Human Services Committee held an educational orientation meeting for new board members, featuring presentations from department heads on the Health Department, Social Services, Veteran Service Office, and Aging Disability Resource Center. No policy votes were taken; the meeting focused on introducing committee structure, department missions, and budgets to new supervisors.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of April 1st committee meeting minutes
Supervisor Cavalli moved to approve the minutes, Vice Chair Rosenberg seconded. The motion passed unanimously with no discussion.

### Review of standing committee ordinance and structure
Administrator Lance Leonard explained the committee's jurisdiction, including oversight of the Health Department, Social Services, Veteran Services, North Central Health Care, and the Aging Disability Resource Center. He outlined statutory responsibilities, reporting relationships, and affiliated boards including the Social Services Board, Veteran Service Commission, Transportation Coordinating Committee, and Board of Health. Chair Boots noted the committee will focus on the strategic plan development over the next year.

### Health Department presentation
Health Officer Caitlyn Suginaka presented the department's mission of preventing disease, promoting health, and protecting against environmental hazards. She highlighted over 1,200 licenses overseen, communicable disease monitoring, the Nurse Family Partnership program with its first graduates, public health vending machines stocked with free resources including tick kits, and a new homeless system response position. The department has approximately 37.6 FTE staff organized into environmental health, nursing, community health improvement, and administrative teams.

### Social Services Department overview
Administrator Leonard provided an overview of the department's 140 FTE staff covering child welfare, youth justice, children's long-term support, economic support, and child support programs. He highlighted the successful Family Keys housing stability pilot program developed with board input, noting housing instability was a growing concern. He mentioned potential restructuring requests may come during budget season to address increased service needs.

### Veteran Service Office presentation
Veteran Service Officer Erin Galindo reported the office increased from 2.75 to 3.0 FTE in 2025. Customer interactions jumped from approximately 5,900 to 8,149 in 2025. The PACT Act has significantly increased claims. Annual VA compensation flowing to Marathon County veterans totals $44 million, with $2.6 million in retroactive payments in 2025. Key initiatives include surviving spouse benefits outreach, veteran housing stability partnerships with Center for Veterans Issues, and suicide prevention efforts (7 of 30 county suicides in 2025 were veterans). When asked by Supervisor Kremes about adequate staffing, Galindo acknowledged the office is very busy with staff averaging 20-30 daily contacts but stated they manage well with current resources.

### Aging Disability Resource Center presentation
Executive Director Mike Ray explained the ADRC serves four counties (Marathon, Lincoln, Langlade, Wood) as a one-stop shop for aging and disability services. Programs include information assistance, caregiver support, dementia care, falls prevention, Medicare counseling, and Meals on Wheels nutrition services. He noted significant financial challenges with nutrition program costs rising 40-45% over four years while federal funding remains flat. The organization relies on approximately 385-400 volunteers. Vice Chair Rosenberg praised the culturally appropriate meal program at the Hmong American Center.
