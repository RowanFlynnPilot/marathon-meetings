## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Health & Human Services Committee held an introductory meeting for new board members, featuring educational presentations from department heads covering the Health Department, Social Services, Veteran Services, and the Aging Disability Resource Center. The committee approved the April 1st meeting minutes unanimously and received comprehensive overviews of departmental missions, budgets, and current initiatives including homelessness response efforts and veteran suicide prevention programs.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of April 1st Meeting Minutes
Supervisor Cavalli made a motion to approve the minutes, seconded by Vice Chair Rosenberg. The motion carried unanimously with no opposition.

### Review of Standing Committee Ordinance Section 2.04
Administrator Lance Leonard explained the committee's jurisdiction, including oversight of the Veteran Service Department, Social Services Department, and Health Department, plus affiliated entities North Central Health Care and the Aging Disability Resource Center. He outlined the committee's relationship with subordinate bodies including the Social Services Board, Veteran Service Commission, Transportation Coordinating Committee, and Marathon County Board of Health. Chair Boots noted the committee will be working on the strategic plan, targeting completion by December.

### Health Department Presentation
Health Officer Caitlyn Suginaka presented an overview of the department's 37.6 FTE staff organized into environmental health, nursing, community health improvement, and administrative teams. She highlighted the department's 1,200+ licenses for restaurants, pools, and campgrounds, the Nurse Family Partnership program with its first graduates, and new public health vending machines stocked with free tick kits and other supplies. Administrator Leonard noted approximately half of the department's $5 million budget comes from tax levy, with significant funding from public charges for services and intergovernmental grants.

### Social Services Department Overview
Administrator Leonard presented on behalf of Director Christa (not present), describing the department's nearly 140 FTE staff working in child welfare, youth justice, children's long-term support, economic support, and child support. He highlighted the successful Family Keys pilot program addressing housing instability for child welfare and youth justice clients, noting the program substantively changed based on board feedback. The department's 2026 budget totals over $22 million with approximately $7 million in tax levy.

### Veteran Services Department Presentation
Veteran Service Officer Erin Galindo reported the department now has 3.0 FTE after receiving board approval to increase from 2.75. He noted customer interactions jumped from 5,900 in 2024 to 8,149 in 2025. The department facilitated $44 million annually in veteran benefits and $2.6 million in retroactive payments in 2025. Galindo discussed major initiatives including housing stability partnerships with the Center for Veterans Issues, suicide prevention efforts (noting 7 of 30 county suicides in 2025 were veterans), and potential application for the Staff Sergeant Gordon Fox federal suicide prevention grant.

### Aging Disability Resource Center Presentation
Executive Director Mike Ray explained the ADRC serves four counties (Marathon, Lincoln, Langlade, and Wood) with about 60 FTEs plus 385-400 volunteers. He described services including information and assistance, caregiver support, dementia care, falls prevention, and Meals on Wheels nutrition programs. Ray noted significant financial challenges with nutrition costs up 40-45% over four years while federal funding remained flat due to the Older Americans Act not being reauthorized for two years. Marathon County's contribution is $413,135. The organization hadn't requested a levy increase from 2011 until 2024.
