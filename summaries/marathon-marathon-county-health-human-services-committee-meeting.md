## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Health & Human Services Committee held an introductory meeting for new board members, providing educational presentations from department heads on the Health Department, Social Services, Veteran Services, and Aging & Disability Resource Center. The only action taken was approval of the April 1st meeting minutes. The committee learned about departmental missions, budgets, and current initiatives including homelessness response, veteran suicide prevention, and nutrition program funding challenges.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of April 1st meeting minutes
Supervisor Cavelli made a motion to approve the minutes, seconded by Vice Chair Rosenberg. The motion carried with all in favor by voice vote.

### Standing Committee Ordinance Review
Administrator Lance Leonard explained the committee's jurisdiction under section 2.04 sub-5, covering the Health Department, Social Services, Veteran Services, and affiliated entities including North Central Health Care and the Aging & Disability Resource Center. He noted the committee will receive reports from statutory bodies including the Social Services Board, Veteran Service Commission, Transportation Coordinating Committee, Board of Health, and others.

### Health Department Presentation
Health Officer Caitlyn Suginaka outlined the department's mission of preventing disease, promoting health, and protecting against environmental hazards. She highlighted over 1,200 licenses overseen, public health vending machines with free resources including tick kits, the Nurse Family Partnership program with first graduates, and a new homeless system response position. Administrator Leonard noted about half of the department's $5 million budget is tax levy, with significant funding from grants and fees for services.

### Social Services Department Overview
Administrator Leonard presented on behalf of Director Christa, noting the department has nearly 140 FTE staff with a $22 million budget and about $7 million in tax levy. He highlighted the successful Family Keys pilot program addressing housing instability for child welfare and youth justice cases, and mentioned anticipated restructuring requests due to increased need in areas like child support.

### Veteran Services Department Presentation
VSO Aaron Galindo reported the department grew from 2.75 to 3.0 FTE and increased interactions from 5,900 to 8,149 in 2025. He noted $44 million annually flows to local veterans with $2.6 million in retroactive payments in 2025. Key initiatives include surviving spouse benefits under the PACT Act, housing stability partnerships with Center for Veterans Issues, and suicide prevention efforts after 7 of 30 county suicides in 2025 were veterans. Supervisor Kremes asked about regional collaboration; Galindo explained state statute requires each county to have a VSO but veterans can work with any office.

### Aging & Disability Resource Center Presentation
Executive Director Mike Ray explained the ADRC serves four counties (Marathon, Lincoln, Langlade, Wood) with about 75 staff and 385-400 volunteers. He described significant financial challenges with the nutrition/Meals on Wheels program, noting federal funding has remained flat while food costs increased 40-45% over four years. Marathon County's contribution is $413,135, with the first levy increase request in over a decade coming in 2024 and approved for 2025. Vice Chair Rosenberg praised the culturally appropriate nutrition program at the Hmong American Center.
