## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Health & Human Services Committee held an orientation meeting for new and returning members, featuring educational presentations from four county departments: Health Department, Social Services, Veteran Services, and Aging & Disability Resource Center. The committee approved April 1st meeting minutes unanimously and scheduled North Central Healthcare for a separate future presentation.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of April 1st meeting minutes
Supervisor Cavelli made a motion to approve the minutes. Vice Chair Rosenberg seconded. The motion passed unanimously with all members voting aye.

### Health Department overview
Health Officer Caitlyn Suginaka presented on the department's mission to prevent disease, promote health, and protect against environmental hazards. She highlighted the department's 37.6 FTE staff across four divisions, their 1,200+ licenses for restaurants and public facilities, communicable disease prevention, nurse family partnership program, and new homeless system response position. The department has public health vending machines stocked with free resources including tick kits. Regarding pandemic preparedness, Suginaka stated the department has trained extensively since COVID and conducts regular measles response exercises.

### Social Services Department overview
Administrator Leonard presented on behalf of Director Christa who could not attend. The department has nearly 140 FTE staff with a 2026 budget of over $22 million (roughly $7 million tax levy). Leonard highlighted the successful Family Keys housing stability pilot program, developed in partnership with the board, which addresses housing instability as a root cause affecting child welfare and youth justice cases. He noted high-cost placements can reach $600,000 annually per child.

### Veteran Services Department overview
Veteran Service Officer Aaron Galindo reported his small team of 3.0 FTE had 8,149 interactions in 2025, up significantly from prior years. He noted $44 million in annual VA compensation flows to Marathon County veterans, with $2.6 million in retroactive payments in 2025 alone. Galindo highlighted suicide prevention efforts, noting 7 of the county's 30 suicides in 2025 were veterans. He described partnership with Center for Veterans Issues for housing vouchers serving 30-70 veterans. When asked about staffing adequacy, Galindo acknowledged the office is busy but performing well, having reduced appointment wait times from 6 weeks to under 3 weeks.

### Aging & Disability Resource Center overview
Executive Director Mike Ray presented on the four-county ADRC serving Marathon, Lincoln, Langlade, and Wood counties with approximately 60 FTE and 400 volunteers. He highlighted significant financial challenges with nutrition program costs up 40-45% over four years while federal funding remained flat. The Older Americans Act hasn't been reauthorized in two years. Ray noted Wisconsin leads the nation in falls and falls-related deaths, and described their Stepping On falls prevention program.

### North Central Healthcare presentation
Administrator Leonard noted that North Central Healthcare Executive Director Jason Hake will be scheduled for a separate dedicated presentation before the committee due to the organization's complexity and budget integration requirements.

## Public Comment
No public comment was offered.
