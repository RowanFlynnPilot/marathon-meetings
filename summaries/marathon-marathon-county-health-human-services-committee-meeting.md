## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Health & Human Services Committee held an orientation meeting for new members, featuring educational presentations from department heads about the Health Department, Social Services, Veteran Service Office, and Aging Disability Resource Center. No formal votes were taken beyond approving minutes, as the meeting focused on introducing committee members to departmental missions, services, and budget structures.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of Minutes
Supervisor Cavelli moved to approve the April 1st committee meeting minutes, seconded by Vice Chair Rosenberg. Motion carried unanimously with voice vote.

### Health Department Overview
Health Officer Caitlyn Suginaka presented on the department's mission to prevent disease, promote health, and protect against environmental hazards. She highlighted 1,200+ licenses overseen, communicable disease monitoring, nurse family partnership program with first graduates, public health vending machines stocked with free tick kits and harm reduction supplies, and a new homeless system response position funded by the county board and City of Wausau partnership.

### Social Services Department Overview
Administrator Lance Leonard presented on behalf of absent Director Christa, explaining the department's divisions including child welfare, youth justice, children's long-term support, economic support, and child support. He highlighted the successful Family Keys housing stability pilot program that addressed housing instability for child welfare and youth justice cases, noting the program substantively changed based on board feedback.

### Veteran Service Office Presentation
VSO Aaron Galindo reported his department grew from 2.75 to 3.0 FTE in 2025. Customer interactions jumped from 5,900 to 8,149 in 2025. Veterans in Marathon County received $44 million annually in VA benefits, plus $2.6 million in retroactive payments in 2025. He noted Marathon County had 30 suicides in 2025, seven of which were veterans, prompting focus on suicide prevention grants. The office is pursuing surviving spouse benefits outreach and partnering with Center for Veterans Issues on housing stability.

### ADRC Presentation
Executive Director Mike Ray explained the four-county regional organization (Marathon, Lincoln, Langlade, Wood) provides aging and disability resource services funded primarily by state/federal sources with 11% county levy. He noted significant financial challenges as nutrition program costs increased 40-45% over four years while federal Older Americans Act funding remained flat since 2024. The ADRC hadn't requested a levy increase from 2011 until 2024.

## Public Comment
