## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Board of Trustees approved increased park and recreation impact fees for new residential development, passed ordinances regulating mobile food vendors and e-bikes, and discussed ongoing challenges with the farmers market relocation to Kennedy Park. The board also adjourned the Board of Review to June 15, 2026 and approved various license renewals.

## Key Discussions
### Public Hearing on Park and Recreation Impact Fees
Staff presented a proposal to increase impact fees from $300 per single family unit to $761 for single family, $563 for duplex, and $495 for multifamily units, consistent with the 2022 public facilities needs assessment. Jim Pitel commented that the village lacks detailed data on actual park maintenance costs to justify the fees. Trustee Barb expressed support, noting that as population increases, new residents should help pay for park costs. Ordinance 26-010 was approved unanimously with motion by Lope Sorrell and second by Zagami. Resolution 2026-015 was also approved unanimously with motion by Hermling and second by Clark.

### Ordinance 26-008: Mobile Food Vendor License
Chief Finkey explained this creates a reciprocal inspection agreement with Marshfield, Wisconsin Rapids, Stevens Point, and other municipalities. Fire inspections from any participating community will be recognized by all, reducing redundant inspections for food truck operators. One department inspects and places a sticker on the truck valid for all participating communities. Approved unanimously with motion by Lope and second by Zagami.

### Remote Meeting Attendance Policy Amendments
The board debated amendments to the remote meeting attendance policy. There was confusion about whether the policy language matched what was voted on at the previous meeting. Trustees agreed to strike sentences requiring health, safety, or welfare justification for remote attendance, keeping the policy broad. The amendment passed with two no votes from Trustee Hermling and Trustee Barb. The original motion as amended also passed with the same two no votes. Key requirements remaining: notify 12 hours in advance, camera must be on, no remote participation in closed sessions.

### Appointments to Committees and Commissions
The president appointed Stephanie Daniels (currently on Finance and HR) and Golden Mua to the CLIPS committee. Both appointments were approved unanimously with motion by Hermling and second by Lopes. The board agreed that future committee/commission applications should be included in board packets for all trustees to review.

### Secondhand Article and Jewelry Dealer License
Scoffield Coin and Hobby LLC at 5703 Jamar Street applied for secondhand dealer licenses. Discussion clarified that state statutes require licensing for businesses buying and selling used personal property to protect against stolen merchandise. Chief noted this involves precious metals and gold with large dollar transactions. Approved unanimously with an amendment requiring completion of background check and receipt of occupancy permit, as the building is still under construction.

### Farmers Market Location Discussion
The board discussed challenges with the farmers market relocation to Kennedy Park after losing the Camp Phillips/Scoffield Avenue location when the property owner chose to develop the land. Trustee Lisa Beck raised safety concerns about cars parking on grass along Alderson Street. Renee explained the market grew from 25 stalls at the original location to 53 stalls now, with vendors able to keep their vehicles nearby. The board discussed that Mock Miller Park was considered but rejected due to lower visibility. Discussion was informational only with no action taken. The market operates at no financial loss to the village.

### Department Reports - Public Works
Michael Dolan reported on warranty work on Weston Avenue, Fuller, and Scoffield Avenue for settling pavement from previous projects. He addressed the engineering technician vacancy, noting it's a specialized skill set difficult to fill locally. Regarding Weston Avenue speed limits, a speed study showed the 85th percentile speed was 45 mph; the matter will return to the board for decision once construction is complete.

### Planning Department Update on Ringle Township Assistance
Jen reported that Ringle Township contacted the village for help with building inspections for tornado-damaged homes. Staff will prepare an intergovernmental service agreement for the Ringle board to allow village inspectors to assist. The comprehensive plan steering committee first meeting was scheduled for Monday, June 1st at 6:00 pm as a joint meeting with the plan commission.

## Public Comment
Jim Pitel of 2nd Street spoke during public comment and again during the impact fee public hearing. He criticized the village for spending $108,000 on outside engineering consultants rather than filling vacant engineering technician positions, questioned the administrator's accountability, complained that Lisa Beck was introduced as a Safer board member before official appointment, criticized lack of Kennedy Park information in the board packet, and argued the village lacks sufficient data to justify park impact fee increases.
