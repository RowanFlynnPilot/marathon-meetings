## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Board of Trustees approved increased park and recreation impact fees for new residential development, passed several ordinances including mobile food vendor licensing and e-bike regulations, and discussed ongoing concerns about the farmers market location at Kennedy Park. The board also adjourned the Board of Review to June 15, 2026.

## Key Discussions
### Public Hearing on Park and Recreation Impact Fees
Staff presented proposed increases to park and recreation impact fees from $300 per single family unit to $761, from $250 per duplex to $563, and from $250 per multifamily to $495, based on the 2022 public facilities needs assessment. Jim Pitel spoke during public comment questioning the justification for the numbers, stating there is no real data on park maintenance costs. Trustee Clark supported the fee increase, noting population growth requires more park equipment and space. Ordinance 26-010 and Resolution 2026-015 both passed unanimously.

### Remote Meeting Attendance Policy Amendments
The board debated amendments to the remote meeting attendance policy. Discussion centered on removing specific reasons for remote attendance (health, safety, welfare) to make the policy broader. Trustee Beck moved to amend the policy by striking sentences requiring justification for remote attendance. The amendment passed with two no votes from Trustees Herling and Lobsinger. The original motion as amended also passed with the same two dissenting votes.

### Mobile Food Vendor License Ordinance
Chief Finkey explained the ordinance creates a reciprocal inspection agreement between Safer, Marshfield, Wisconsin Rapids, Stevens Point, Clover, and Riverside. Under this system, a fire inspection by any participating department results in a sticker allowing the food truck to operate in all participating communities without additional inspections. Ordinance 26-008 passed unanimously.

### Appointments to Committees and Commissions
President appointed Stephanie Daniels (currently on Finance and HR) and Golden Mua to the CLIPS committee. Golden Mua may be considered for Finance committee after gaining experience on CLIPS. Motion by Herling, second by Lobsinger, passed unanimously. The board agreed that future committee applicant resumes should be included in board packets.

### Farmers Market Location Discussion
The board discussed ongoing concerns about the farmers market's new location at Kennedy Park after losing the Camp Phillips/Schofield Avenue site to development. Trustee Beck raised safety concerns about vehicles parking on grass along Alderson. Renee explained the market has grown from 25 to 53 vendor spots and vendors prefer having vehicles with them. Mont Miller Park was discussed but rejected due to poor visibility and distance from traffic. The board acknowledged this may require ongoing adjustments and could potentially be discontinued if no suitable location is found.

### Secondhand Article Dealer License
The board approved a secondhand article dealer and secondhand jewelry dealer license for Schofield Coin and Hobby LLC at 5703 Jamar Street. Chief Hunt explained these licenses protect against stolen merchandise sales. An amendment was added requiring satisfactory background check completion and receipt of occupancy permit since the building is still under construction. Passed unanimously as amended.

### Weston Avenue Speed Limit
Public Works Director Michael Dolan reported on the ongoing speed limit issue. An engineering study showed the road should be 35 mph, but traffic data showed the 85th percentile speed was 45 mph. A trustee noted the speed limit has been inconsistent with the ordinance for over two months. Dolan indicated this would need to come back to the board, possibly at the joint meeting.

## Public Comment
Jim Pitel of 52nd Street spoke during general public comment criticizing the hiring of outside engineering consultants for $108,000 when an engineering technician position remains unfilled. He criticized the administrator for not filling the role and questioned why Lisa Beck was introduced as a Safer board member before being officially appointed. He also complained about lack of Kennedy Park information in meeting packets. Pitel also spoke during the park impact fee public hearing questioning the justification for the fee amounts without detailed park maintenance cost data.
