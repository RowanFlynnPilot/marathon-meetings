## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Board of Trustees approved increased park and recreation impact fees, passed several ordinances including mobile food vendor licensing and e-bike regulations, and held extensive discussion about parking and logistics challenges at the relocated farmers market at Kennedy Park. The board also adjourned the Board of Review to June 15, 2026 and made committee appointments.

## Key Discussions
### Park and Recreation Impact Fees
Staff presented proposed fee increases from $300 to $761 for single family units, $250 to $563 for duplex units, and $250 to $495 for multifamily units, consistent with the 2022 public facilities needs assessment. Jim Pitel spoke in public comment expressing concern that there is no real data justifying the specific numbers. Trustee Barb supported the fees as justified given population growth. Ordinance 26-010 was approved unanimously and Resolution 2026-015 was approved unanimously.

### Mobile Food Vendor License Ordinance
Chief Finkey explained the ordinance creates a regional agreement with Marshfield, Wisconsin Rapids, Stevens Point, Clover, Riverside, and Safer fire departments for mutual recognition of food truck fire inspections. A truck inspected by any participating department receives a sticker recognized by all, eliminating redundant inspections. Ordinance 26-008 was approved unanimously.

### Remote Meeting Attendance Policy
The board debated amendments to the policy governing virtual attendance. Trustee Beck raised concerns that the draft language requiring health, safety, or welfare justifications did not match what was discussed at the previous meeting. After discussion, Trustee Beck moved to amend the policy to strike sentences requiring specific justifications, making it more broad. The amendment passed with two no votes from Trustees Hermeling and Lobsral. The original motion as amended also passed with the same two no votes.

### Committee Appointments
The president appointed Stephanie Daniels and Golden Mua to the CLIPS committee. Motion by Hermeling, second by Lobsral, approved unanimously. The board agreed to include committee applicant resumes in future board packets.

### Farmers Market Location Discussion
The board held extensive discussion-only on the farmers market relocation to Kennedy Park after losing the Camp Phillips/Schofield Avenue location to development. Trustee Beck expressed safety concerns about cars parking on grass along Alderson. Renee explained the market grew from 25 stalls at the old location to 53 spots at Kennedy Park, and vendors appreciate having vehicles with them. Multiple trustees acknowledged the difficulty finding an ideal location, with Tamach Miller Park rejected due to poor visibility. Renee confirmed the market is not running at a loss due to grants and vendor fees. No action was taken.

### Weston Avenue Speed Limit
Public Works Director Michael reported on the speed study for Weston Avenue. The DOT engineering study indicated 35 mph but the 85th percentile speed was 45 mph. He noted the road is currently under construction so additional data collection is delayed. The matter will return to a future board meeting.

### Secondhand Article Dealer License
The board approved a secondhand article dealer and secondhand jewelry dealer license for Schofield Coin and Hobby LLC at 5703 Jamar Street, with an amendment requiring satisfactory background check completion and receipt of occupancy permit. Chief explained these licenses are required under state statute for businesses buying and selling used personal property, with a 30-day hold period to check for stolen merchandise. Motion by Hermeling, second by Clark, approved unanimously as amended.

## Public Comment
Jim Pitel of 5102 Street spoke during public comment criticizing the board for hiring outside engineering consultants ($108,000 estimated) rather than filling the vacant engineering technician position. He also criticized the appointment process for committees, noting Lisa Beck was introduced as a Safer board member before official appointment. He expressed frustration that no detailed Kennedy Park information was included in the packet despite prior board requests. During the impact fee public hearing, Pitel again spoke expressing concern about lack of real data justifying the specific fee amounts.
