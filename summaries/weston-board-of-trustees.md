## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Board of Trustees approved increased park and recreation impact fees, passed ordinances on mobile food vendor licensing and e-bike regulations, and discussed ongoing challenges with relocating the farmers market to Kennedy Park. The board also adjourned the Board of Review to June 15, 2026 and approved multiple license renewals for the upcoming licensing term.

## Key Discussions
### Park and Recreation Impact Fee Amendment
Staff presented proposed increases to park impact fees from $300/$250 to $761 for single family, $563 for duplex, and $495 for multifamily units, based on the 2022 public facilities needs assessment. Jim Pitel spoke during public comment questioning whether the village has real data on park maintenance costs. Trustee Clark supported the fee as justified given population growth. Ordinance 26-010 was approved unanimously, and Resolution 2026-015 amending the fee schedule was also approved unanimously.

### Mobile Food Vendor License Ordinance
Chief Finkey explained this ordinance allows mutual recognition of fire safety inspections among Safer, Marshfield, Wisconsin Rapids, Stevens Point, Clover, and Riverside communities. A food truck inspected and stickered by one department would not need reinspection in other participating municipalities. Ordinance 26-008 passed unanimously.

### Remote Meeting Attendance Policy
Significant debate occurred over striking language requiring members to justify remote attendance based on health, safety, or welfare concerns. Trustees argued chairs should not have to determine if someone's excuse is 'good enough.' Trustee Beck moved to amend striking sentences requiring justification; Trustee Clark seconded. The amendment passed with Trustees Hermling and Clark voting no. The original motion as amended then passed with the same two dissenting votes.

### Farmers Market Location Discussion
Board discussed ongoing challenges with the farmers market relocated to Kennedy Park after losing their previous location to development. Concerns raised included parking on grass creating safety issues and potential field damage. Renee explained the market grew from 25 to 53 vendor stalls and vendors appreciate having vehicles nearby. Board acknowledged Mont Miller Park was considered but rejected as too far from traffic. Trustee Beck questioned costs but was assured the market operates at no loss. Discussion only - no action taken.

### Secondhand Article Dealer License
Board approved a secondhand article and jewelry dealer license for Scoffield Coin and Hobby LLC at 5703 Jamar Street. Chief Hunt explained the licensing provides safety checks for stolen merchandise through a police-accessible database. Motion was amended to require satisfactory background check completion and occupancy permit before license is issued. Passed unanimously as amended.

### Committee Appointments
President appointed Stephanie Daniels and Golden Mua to the CLIPS committee. Motion by Hermling, second by Lopes, passed unanimously. Parks, HR/Finance, and CLIPS still have openings. Board requested that committee applicant resumes be included in future packets.

## Public Comment
Jim Pitel of 52nd Street spoke during public comment criticizing the hiring of outside engineering consultants at $108,000 when an engineering technician position remains unfilled. He questioned why Lisa Beck was introduced as a Safer board member before being officially appointed and criticized lack of transparency around committee appointments. He also complained about missing information on Kennedy Park funding in the meeting packet and called for better administrator accountability.
