## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Board of Trustees approved increased park and recreation impact fees for new residential development, passed several ordinances including mobile food vendor licensing and e-bicycle regulations, and discussed ongoing challenges with the farmers market's new location at Kennedy Park. The board also adjourned the Board of Review to June 15, 2026 and made appointments to the CLIPS committee.

## Key Discussions
### Park and Recreation Impact Fees
Staff presented proposed fee increases from $300 per single family unit to $761, aligned with the 2022 public facilities needs assessment. Jim Pitel spoke during public comment criticizing the lack of detailed cost data for park maintenance, calling the decision 'irresponsible.' Trustee Barb supported the fee increase noting population growth requires more park space. Ordinance 26-010 was approved with motion by Lope Sorrell, second by Zagami, passing unanimously. Resolution 2026-015 also passed unanimously with motion by Herming, second by Clark.

### Mobile Food Vendor License Ordinance
Chief Finkey explained the ordinance creates a regional fire inspection reciprocity agreement with Marshfield, Wisconsin Rapids, Stevens Point, Clover Belt, and Riverside. Once a food truck is inspected by any participating municipality, a sticker indicates compliance for all jurisdictions, eliminating redundant inspections. Ordinance 26-008 passed unanimously with motion by Lope, second by Zagami.

### Remote Meeting Attendance Policy
The board debated amendments to the policy, with some trustees wanting to remove language requiring health, safety, or welfare reasons for remote attendance. Trustee Beck moved to amend the policy by striking sentences requiring justification, arguing chairs shouldn't determine whose excuse is 'good enough.' The amendment passed with two opposing votes from Trustees Herling and Lobsra. The original motion as amended also passed with the same two dissenting votes.

### Secondhand Article Dealer License
The board approved a secondhand article dealer and jewelry dealer license for Scoffield Coin and Hobby LLC at 5703 Jamar Street, with an amendment requiring satisfactory background check completion and receipt of occupancy permit since the building is still under construction. Motion by Herling, second by Clark, passed unanimously as amended.

### Farmers Market Location Discussion
The board discussed challenges with the farmers market's new Kennedy Park location after losing the Camp Phillips/Scoffield Avenue site to development. Trustee Lisa Beck raised safety concerns about vehicles parking on grass. Renee explained the market grew from 25 to 53 vendor stalls and vendors appreciate having their vehicles with them. Discussion included whether to potentially discontinue the market if a suitable location cannot be found. Renee clarified the market is not losing money and detailed grant funding for SNAP/EBT token programs. This was discussion only with no action taken.

### Committee Appointments
The president appointed Stephanie Daniels (currently on Finance and HR) and Golden Mua to the CLIPS committee. Motion by Herling, second by Lopes, passed unanimously. The board agreed to include committee applications in future board packets.

## Public Comment
Jim Pitel of 52 Street spoke during public comment criticizing the hiring of outside engineering consultants for $108,000 when an engineering technician position remains unfilled, questioning administrator accountability. He also criticized the appointment process for committees, noting Lisa Beck was introduced at a Safer meeting before being officially appointed. He called for more transparent public discussions and criticized lack of information on Kennedy Park in the meeting packet. During the park impact fee public hearing, Pitel again spoke questioning the justification for fee amounts without detailed park maintenance cost data.
