## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Board of Trustees approved increased park and recreation impact fees for new residential development, passed multiple ordinances including mobile food vendor licensing and e-bike regulations, and held extensive discussion about farmers market location challenges at Kennedy Park. The board also adjourned the Board of Review to June 15, 2026.

## Key Discussions
### Public Hearing - Park and Recreation Impact Fees
Staff presented a proposal to increase park and recreation impact fees from $300 per single family unit to $761, from $250 to $563 for duplexes, and from $250 to $495 for multifamily units, based on the 2022 public facilities needs assessment. Jim Pitel spoke in public comment questioning whether the village has real data on park maintenance costs. Trustee Motti supported the fees as justified given population growth. Ordinance 26-010 was approved with motion by Lopsorrel, second by Zagami, passed unanimously. Resolution 2026-015 was approved with motion by Hermling, second by Clark, passed unanimously.

### Mobile Food Vendor License Ordinance 26-008
Chief Finkey explained this ordinance establishes reciprocal fire inspection agreements between Safer and municipalities including Marshfield, Wisconsin Rapids, Stevens Point, and others. A food truck inspected by any participating department receives a sticker recognized by all, eliminating redundant inspections. Motion by Lopsorrel, second by Zagami, passed unanimously.

### Remote Meeting Attendance Policy Amendments
The board debated amendments to the remote meeting attendance policy. Original 2023 policy language requiring health, safety, or welfare justifications was contested. Trustee Beck moved to amend by striking sentences requiring specific justifications, keeping only the requirement to notify 12 hours in advance, be on camera, and not participate in closed session. Amendment passed with Trustees Hermling and Motti voting no. Final amended policy passed with Hermling and Motti again voting no.

### Committee Appointments
The president appointed Stephanie Daniels (currently on Finance and HR) and Golden Mua to the CLIPS committee. Motion by Hermling, second by Lopsorrel, passed unanimously. Parks, Finance/HR still have openings, and one more CLIPS opening remains. Board agreed to include committee applications in future packets.

### Secondhand Article Dealer License
Scoffield Coin and Hobby LLC at 5703 Jamar Street requested secondhand article and jewelry dealer licenses. Chief Hunt explained these dealers must report items to a police-accessible database to prevent fencing of stolen goods. The business location is still under construction, so the board amended the motion to approve contingent on satisfactory background check completion and receipt of occupancy permit. Passed unanimously.

### Farmers Market Location Discussion
Extensive discussion on challenges at the new Kennedy Park location after losing the Camp Phillips/Scoffield site to development. Concerns raised about parking on grass along Alderson, safety issues, and field damage. Renee explained the market grew from 25 stalls at the original location to 53 at Kennedy Park, and vendors now have their vehicles. Mock Miller Park was discussed but deemed too remote. The board acknowledged this may require ongoing adjustments. Renee confirmed the farmers market operates at a profit through vendor fees, grants, and reimbursements.

### Engineering Technician Position
Public Works Director Michael Dolan reported the engineering technician position has been advertised multiple times but the specialized skill set is difficult to fill locally. Consulting firms are also struggling to find qualified candidates in central Wisconsin. Two contracts for outside engineering inspection services were approved for projects starting in June.

## Public Comment
Jim Pitel of 52 Street spoke during public comments criticizing the hiring of outside engineering consultants at $108,000 when the engineering technician position remains unfilled, questioned why Lisa Beck was introduced at a Safer meeting before her official appointment, and complained about lack of Kennedy Park information in the packet. He also spoke during the park impact fee public hearing questioning whether the village has actual data on park maintenance costs.
