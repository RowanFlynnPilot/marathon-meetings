## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Board of Trustees approved increases to park and recreation impact fees for new residential development, passed several ordinances including mobile food vendor licensing and e-bike regulations, and discussed ongoing challenges with the farmers market's new location at Kennedy Park. The board also adjourned the Board of Review to June 15, 2026.

## Key Discussions
### Public Hearing on Park and Recreation Impact Fees
Staff presented proposed increases to park and recreation impact fees from $300 to $761 for single family units, $250 to $563 for duplex units, and $250 to $495 for multifamily units, consistent with the 2022 public facilities needs assessment. Jim Pitel spoke in public comment arguing there was no solid data justifying the specific amounts. Trustee Barb supported the fees as justified given population increases. Ordinance 26-010 passed unanimously, and Resolution 2026-015 amending the fee schedule also passed unanimously.

### Mobile Food Vendor License Ordinance
Chief Finkey explained the ordinance creates a reciprocal inspection agreement between Safer and surrounding municipalities including Marshfield, Wisconsin Rapids, Stevens Point, and others. Under this system, if a food truck is inspected by any participating department, a sticker is placed on it recognizing the inspection across all communities, eliminating the need for multiple inspections. The ordinance passed unanimously.

### Remote Meeting Attendance Policy Amendments
The board debated amendments to the remote attendance policy. Trustee Beck raised concerns that the policy language about health, safety, and welfare reasons didn't match what was discussed at the previous meeting. After discussion, Trustee Beck moved to amend the policy by striking sentences requiring specific justifications for remote attendance, keeping only requirements to notify 12 hours in advance, have camera on, and prohibiting remote participation in closed sessions. The amendment passed with two no votes from Trustees Hermling and Lopesell. The original motion as amended also passed with the same two dissenting votes.

### Committee Appointments
The president appointed Stephanie Daniels and Golda Mua to the CLIPS committee. Daniels is currently on Finance and HR. The motion to approve both appointments passed unanimously. The board also agreed to include committee applicant resumes in future board packets.

### Secondhand Article and Jewelry Dealer License
The board considered a license for Scoffield Coin and Hobby LLC at 5703 Jamar Street. Chief Hunt explained this involves buying and selling precious metals with large dollar amounts. The motion was amended to require satisfactory completion of background check and receipt of occupancy permit since the building is still under construction. The amended motion passed unanimously.

### Farmers Market Location Discussion
The board discussed challenges with the farmers market's new location at Kennedy Park after losing the Camp Phillips/Scoffield Avenue site to development. Trustee Beck raised safety concerns about cars parking on grass along Alderson and in the park. Renee explained the market grew from 25 to 53 vendor spots and vendors appreciate having their vehicles with them. Mock Miller Park was considered but deemed too far from traffic. The board acknowledged if no suitable location can be found, the market may need to be suspended. Renee clarified the market is not losing money and she obtains grants for programs like double-up food bucks.

### Engineering Contracts and Staffing
During public comment, Jim Pitel criticized spending $108,000 on outside engineering consultants and asked why the engineering technician position hasn't been filled. Public Works Director Michael Dolan later explained the position has been advertised multiple times but the specific skill set needed is scarce in central Wisconsin, with even consulting firms struggling to find local talent.

### Weston Avenue Speed Limit
Public Works Director Dolan reported that the DOT engineering study classified Weston Avenue as a 35 mph road, not the 45 mph currently posted. He conducted a speed study showing the 85th percentile speed was 45 mph, which would support keeping it at that speed. He needs to complete additional data collection once construction is finished before bringing a recommendation back to the board.

## Public Comment
Jim Pitel of 52nd Street spoke during public comment criticizing the board for spending $108,000 on outside engineering consultants instead of filling the vacant engineering technician position. He also criticized the appointment process for committees, noting that Lisa Beck was introduced as a Safer board member before being officially appointed by the board, calling this 'inappropriate government business taking action outside of this boardroom.' He further criticized the lack of detailed information on Kennedy Park in the meeting packet. During the park impact fee public hearing, Pitel argued the fee increases lacked justification based on actual operational data.
