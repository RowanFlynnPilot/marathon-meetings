## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Board of Trustees approved increased park and recreation impact fees, passed several ordinances including mobile food vendor licensing and e-bike regulations, and discussed ongoing challenges with the farmers market location at Kennedy Park. The board also adjourned the Board of Review to June 15, 2026 and made appointments to the CLIPS committee.

## Key Discussions
### Public hearing on park and recreation impact fees
Staff presented proposed increases to park and recreation impact fees from $300 to $761 for single family units, $250 to $563 for duplex units, and $250 to $495 for multifamily units, based on the 2022 public facilities needs assessment. Jim Pitel spoke in public comment questioning the justification for the numbers without detailed operational cost data. Trustee Motti supported the increase citing population growth and fairness to existing residents. Ordinance 26-010 was approved with motion by Lope Sorrell, second by Zagami, passing unanimously. Resolution 2026-015 was approved with motion by Hermling, second by Clark, passing unanimously.

### Mobile food vendor license ordinance (26-008)
Chief Finkey explained the ordinance creates a cooperative inspection system where Safer and neighboring municipalities (Marshfield, Wisconsin Rapids, Stevens Point, and others) will recognize each other's fire inspections for food trucks via a sticker system, eliminating the need for multiple inspections. Motion by Lope Sorrell, second by Zagami, passed unanimously.

### Remote meeting attendance policy amendments
The board debated amendments to the remote meeting attendance policy. Discussion focused on removing language requiring justification for health, safety, or welfare reasons, with trustees wanting a broader policy where chairs simply approve or deny requests without evaluating excuses. Trustee Beck moved to amend by striking sentences requiring specific justifications. The amendment passed with two no votes from Trustee Hermling and Trustee Motti. The original motion as amended also passed with the same two no votes.

### Committee appointments
President appointed Stephanie Daniels (currently on Finance and HR) and Goldsen Mua to the CLIPS committee. Motion by Hermling, second by Lopes, passed unanimously. The board also agreed to include committee applicant resumes in future meeting packets. Openings remain on Parks, Finance/HR, and CLIPS committees.

### Farmers market location at Kennedy Park
Discussion-only item regarding the farmers market's new location after losing the Camp Phillips/Schofield corner site to development. Trustee Beck raised safety concerns about cars parking on grass areas. Renee explained history: the market grew from 25 stalls at the old municipal building to 42 at Camp Phillips to 53 current spots at Kennedy Park where vendors can keep their vehicles. Mock Miller Park was discussed but rejected due to poor visibility and distance concerns. The board discussed that if no suitable location can be found, the market may need to pause. Renee clarified the market operates at no loss due to grants covering tokens and reimbursements from EBT/Aspirus programs.

### Engineering technician vacancy and outside contractors
In public comment, Jim Pitel criticized spending $108,000 on outside engineering consultants. Public Works Director Michael Dolan explained the engineering technician position has been advertised multiple times but the specialized skill set is scarce in central Wisconsin, with even consulting firms pulling staff from southeastern Wisconsin. The contracts on the agenda are for two projects starting in June.

### Weston Avenue speed limit
Public Works Director Dolan reported on the speed limit issue raised previously. The DOT engineering study showed the road as 35 mph, but the 85th percentile speed study showed 45 mph. A second speed study is needed but was delayed due to construction. The matter will return to the board, possibly at the joint meeting.

## Public Comment
Jim Pitel of Deon 52nd Street spoke criticizing the $108,000 expenditure for outside engineering contractors, questioning why the engineering technician position remains unfilled, criticizing the process for committee appointments noting Lisa Beck was introduced at a Safer meeting before official board appointment, and requesting better documentation on Kennedy Park project funding and timeline. He also questioned the park impact fee increases during the public hearing, arguing the village lacks detailed operational cost data to justify specific numbers.
