## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Board of Trustees approved increased park and recreation impact fees, passed several ordinance amendments including mobile food vendor licensing and e-bike regulations, and engaged in extensive discussion about the farmers market relocation to Kennedy Park following the loss of their previous location. The board also adjourned the Board of Review to June 15, 2026.

## Key Discussions
### Public Hearing - Park and Recreation Impact Fees
Staff presented proposed fee increases from $300 per single family unit to $761, consistent with the 2022 public facilities needs assessment. Jim Pitel spoke during public comment criticizing the lack of detailed operational data to justify the specific fee amounts. Trustee Motti supported the fees as justified given population growth. Both Ordinance 26-010 and Resolution 2026-015 were approved unanimously.

### Committee Chair Updates
Parks and Recreation reported playground mats installed at Yellow Banks and trees removed from the river. Public Works discussed upcoming recycling and inspection contracts. Tourism reported on grants awarded: $2,500 to GG's, $10,000 to Wausau Mum Festival, $5,000 to Mountain Bay Cup, and $5,000 offered to Wisconsin Valley Fair. Finance committee learned about clerk department costs for elections and newsletters. The MPD joint finance committee voted to disperse funds back to the three communities (Schofield, Town of Weston, Village of Weston).

### Remote Meeting Attendance Policy Amendments
Significant debate occurred over policy language. The board voted to strike sentences requiring specific justifications (health, safety, welfare) for remote attendance, making the policy more broad as discussed at the previous meeting. The amended policy requires 12-hour advance notice, camera must be on, and remote attendance is not permitted for closed sessions. Amendment passed with two no votes from Trustees Erling and Motti. Final amended policy passed with two no votes from the same trustees.

### Committee Appointments
President appointed Stephanie Daniels and Golden Mua to the CLIPS committee. Motion approved unanimously. The board agreed to include committee/commission applicant resumes in future meeting packets for transparency.

### Mobile Food Vendor License Ordinance
Chief Finkey explained the ordinance creates a regional inspection sticker system with Marshfield, Wisconsin Rapids, Stevens Point, and other municipalities. Once a food truck is inspected by any participating department, a sticker indicates compliance so it doesn't need re-inspection in each community. Ordinance 26-008 passed unanimously.

### Secondhand Article Dealer License - Scoffield Coin and Hobby LLC
The board discussed the purpose of secondhand dealer licensing as a safety measure to track potentially stolen merchandise. Chief Hunt explained this involves buying and selling precious metals with large dollar amounts. The motion was amended to require satisfactory background check completion and receipt of occupancy permit since the building is still under construction. Approved unanimously as amended.

### Farmers Market Location Discussion
Extensive discussion about the market's relocation to Kennedy Park after losing their previous location at Camp Phillips and Schofield Avenue when the property owner decided to develop. Trustee Beck raised safety concerns about cars parking on grass along Alderson. Renee provided history: original site had 25 stalls, Camp Phillips had 42 stalls without vehicle parking, Kennedy Park now has 53 spots with vendor vehicle parking. The board discussed that Mont Miller Park would have poor visibility. Renee confirmed the market operates at no loss to the village, with grant funding covering token programs and vendor fees covering operational costs.

### Weston Avenue Speed Limit
Public Works Director Michael reported that engineering study showed 35 mph, but traffic data showed 85th percentile speed at 45 mph. A second speed study is needed but was delayed due to construction. Trustee Pitel noted during discussion that the current speed is inconsistent with ordinance and needs resolution. Staff will bring back to the board.

### Engineering Technician Position
Michael addressed public comment about the unfilled engineering technician position, explaining it requires a very specific skill set that is scarce in central Wisconsin. Even consulting firms are pulling staff from southeast Wisconsin offices. Two outside engineering contracts totaling approximately $108,000 were discussed for current projects while position remains unfilled.

## Public Comment
Jim Pitel of 5902 Street spoke criticizing the board for hiring outside engineering consultants at $108,000 instead of filling the vacant engineering technician position, questioned why Lisa Beck was introduced at a SAFER meeting before official board appointment, complained about lack of Kennedy Park information in the meeting packet, and criticized the administrator's accountability. He also spoke during the park impact fee public hearing questioning the lack of operational data to justify specific fee amounts.
