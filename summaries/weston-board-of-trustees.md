## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Board of Trustees approved increased park and recreation impact fees, passed several ordinances including mobile food vendor licensing and e-bicycle regulations, and discussed ongoing challenges with the farmers market relocation to Kennedy Park. The board also adjourned the Board of Review to June 15, 2026 and made appointments to the CLIPS committee.

## Key Discussions
### Public hearing on park and recreation impact fees
Staff presented proposed increases to park and recreation impact fees from $300 to $761 for single family units, $250 to $563 for duplex units, and $250 to $495 for multifamily units, based on the 2022 public facilities needs assessment. Jim Pitel spoke during public comment questioning the lack of real operational data to justify the numbers. Trustee Barb supported the fee increase citing population growth and fairness. Ordinance 26-010 was approved with motion by Lope Sorrell, second by Zagami. Resolution 2026-015 was approved with motion by Hermling, second by Clark. Both passed unanimously.

### Mobile food vendor license ordinance
Chief Finkey explained that Safer and multiple municipalities including Marshfield, Wisconsin Rapids, Stevens Point, and Clover created a reciprocal inspection agreement for food trucks. One fire inspection by any participating department places a sticker on the truck valid for all communities, eliminating the need for multiple inspections. Ordinance 26-008 passed unanimously with motion by Lope, second by Zagami.

### Remote meeting attendance policy amendments
The board debated amendments to the remote meeting policy. Several trustees wanted to remove specific language about health, safety, and welfare justifications, preferring a broader policy. Trustee Beck moved to amend by striking sentences requiring specific justifications. The amendment passed with two no votes from Trustees Hermling and Lope Sorrell. The original motion as amended then passed with the same two dissenting votes.

### Committee appointments
The president appointed Stephanie Daniels and Golden Mua to the CLIPS committee. Motion by Hermling, second by Lope Sorrell, passed unanimously. Parks, Finance/HR, and CLIPS still have openings. The board agreed to include committee applicant resumes in future packets.

### Farmers market location discussion
The board discussed challenges with the farmers market's new location at Kennedy Park after being displaced from the Camp Phillips/Scoffield corner by development. Trustee Beck raised safety concerns about parking on grass along Alderson. Renee explained the market grew from 25 to 53 vendor spots and vendors appreciate having their vehicles with them. Mount Miller Park was discussed but rejected due to its remote location. The board acknowledged this is a discussion-only item with no action taken.

### Engineering consultant contracts
Public Works Director Michael explained that two engineering consultant contracts totaling approximately $108,000 are needed for project oversight because the engineering technician position remains unfilled. He noted the specific skill set required is in short supply in central Wisconsin, with even consulting firms pulling staff from southeastern Wisconsin offices.

### Secondhand dealer license approval
The board approved a secondhand article dealer and secondhand jewelry dealer license for Scoffield Coin and Hobby LLC at 5703 Jamar Street. Chief Hunt explained this involves buying and selling precious metals with large dollar amounts. The approval was amended to be contingent on satisfactory background check completion and receipt of occupancy permit. Passed unanimously.

## Public Comment
Jim Pitel of 5200 N 52nd Street spoke during public comment criticizing the $108,000 expenditure for outside engineering consultants, questioning why the engineering technician position remains unfilled. He also criticized the appointment process for committees, noting Lisa Beck was introduced at a Safer meeting before being officially appointed. He requested better documentation on Kennedy Park project status and called for more administrator accountability.
