## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Board of Trustees approved increases to park and recreation impact fees, passed several ordinances including mobile food vendor licensing and e-bike regulations, and discussed ongoing challenges with the farmers market relocation to Kennedy Park. The board also adjourned the Board of Review to June 15, 2026 and made appointments to the CLIPS committee.

## Key Discussions
### Public Hearing on Park and Recreation Impact Fees
Staff presented proposed increases to park impact fees from $300 to $761 for single family units, $250 to $563 for duplex units, and $250 to $495 for multifamily units, based on the 2022 public facilities needs assessment. Jim Pitel spoke during public comment questioning whether the village has actual data on park maintenance costs. Trustee Barb supported the fee increase stating it's fair for new homes to pay for increased park needs. Ordinance 26-010 was approved with motion by Lope Sorrell and second by Zagami. Resolution 2026-015 was approved with motion by Hermling and second by Clark. Both passed with no opposition noted.

### Mobile Food Vendor License Ordinance (26-008)
Chief Finkey explained this ordinance creates a regional mutual recognition system for food truck fire inspections. Participating municipalities including Marshfield, Wisconsin Rapids, Stevens Point, and others will accept each other's inspection stickers, eliminating the need for multiple inspections. Motion by Lope, second by Zagami, passed unanimously.

### E-Bike/E-Moto Ordinance (26-012)
The board discussed regulations for e-bikes and e-motos on sidewalks, with trustees noting e-motos can travel up to 52 mph and are different from e-bikes due to having engines rather than pedals. Motion by Zagami, second by Clark, passed unanimously.

### Remote Meeting Attendance Policy Amendments
Significant debate occurred over the policy language. Trustees agreed to strike sentences requiring specific health/safety/welfare justifications for remote attendance, leaving only requirements to notify 12 hours in advance, be on camera, and not participate in closed session. Trustees Hermling and Lope Sorrell voted against the amendment to strike the justification language. The amended policy passed with two opposed.

### Committee Appointments
The president appointed Stephanie Daniels (currently on Finance and HR) and Golden Mua to the CLIPS committee. Motion by Hermling, second by Lope, passed unanimously. The board also agreed to include committee applicant resumes in future board packets.

### Farmers Market Location Discussion
The board discussed challenges with the new Kennedy Park location after losing the Camp Phillips/Schofield Avenue site to development. Trustee Lisa Beck raised safety concerns about parking on grass areas. Renee explained the market grew from 25 to 53 vendor spots with the moves. Discussion included whether Mock Miller Park would work (concerns about being too far from traffic) and whether the market should pause if no suitable location exists. The market generates revenue and Renee confirmed grants cover token programs and she is not losing money. No action taken; discussion only.

### Weston Avenue Speed Limit
Director Michael Dolan reported on ongoing speed study efforts. The engineering study showed the 85th percentile speed was 45 mph, but construction has delayed completion of additional required data collection. A resident inquiry noted the speed is currently inconsistent with village ordinance. Staff will bring this back to the board.

## Public Comment
Jim Pitel of 52nd Street offered two public comments. During general public comment, he criticized hiring outside engineering consultants for $108,000 when an engineering technician position remains unfilled, questioned why Lisa Beck was introduced as a Safer board member before official appointment, and complained about lack of information on Kennedy Park in the packet. During the park impact fee hearing, he questioned whether the village has actual data on park maintenance costs to justify the fee increase.
