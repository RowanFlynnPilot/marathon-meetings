## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Board of Trustees approved increased park and recreation impact fees, passed ordinances on mobile food vendor licensing and e-bike regulations, and discussed ongoing challenges with the farmers market relocation to Kennedy Park. The board also adjourned the Board of Review to June 15, 2026 and made appointments to the CLIPS committee.

## Key Discussions
### Park and Recreation Impact Fee Amendment
Staff presented a proposal to increase impact fees from $300 per single family unit to $761, $250 per duplex to $563, and multifamily to $495, based on the 2022 public facilities needs assessment. Jim Pitel questioned the lack of detailed cost data justifying the fees. Trustee Motti supported the increase noting population growth requires more park infrastructure. Ordinance 26-010 was approved unanimously with motion by Lopes Sorrell and second by Zagami. Resolution 2026-015 was also approved unanimously with motion by Hermling and second by Clark.

### Mobile Food Vendor License Ordinance
Chief Finkey explained the ordinance creates a regional reciprocity agreement between Safer, Marshfield, Wisconsin Rapids, Stevens Point, Clover, and Riverside for fire inspections of food trucks. Once inspected by one participating municipality, a sticker indicates compliance for all, eliminating redundant inspections. Ordinance 26-008 was approved unanimously with motion by Lopes Sorrell and second by Zagami.

### Remote Meeting Attendance Policy
The board debated amendments to the policy. Trustees agreed to remove language requiring specific justifications like health, safety, or welfare for remote attendance, keeping the policy broad. Members must notify 12 hours in advance, be on camera, and cannot participate in closed sessions. Trustee Beck moved to amend with these strikethroughs, seconded by Clark. The amendment passed with two nos from Hermling and Motti. The original motion as amended passed with the same two dissenting votes.

### Committee Appointments
The president appointed Stephanie Daniels and Golden Mua to the CLIPS committee. Motion by Hermling, second by Lopes Sorrell, approved unanimously. The board also agreed to include applicant resumes in future board packets for transparency.

### Farmers Market Location Discussion
The board discussed challenges with the farmers market's relocation to Kennedy Park after losing the Camp Phillips/Scoffield Avenue location to development. Concerns included parking congestion with vehicles on grass areas, safety issues, and field damage. Renee explained the market grew from 25 to 53 vendor stalls. The board considered but rejected Mont Miller Park due to its remote location. No action was taken; this was discussion only. Renee clarified the market operates at a profit through vendor fees, grants, and reimbursements.

### Weston Avenue Speed Limit
Village Engineer Michael Dolan reported that DOT engineering studies showed the road should be 35 mph, not 45. A traffic study showed the 85th percentile speed was 45 mph. He needs to complete a second study after construction ends to potentially justify changing the posted limit. A resident noted the speed has been inconsistent with the ordinance for over two months.

### Engineering Technician Position
In response to Jim Pitel's public comment about unfilled positions, Michael Dolan explained the engineering technician position has been advertised multiple times but the specific skill set is scarce in central Wisconsin, with even consulting firms struggling to find local candidates.

## Public Comment
Jim Pitel of Deon 52 Street spoke during public comment criticizing the hiring of outside engineering consultants ($108,000 estimated) when an engineering technician position remains unfilled. He questioned why Lisa Beck was introduced at a Safer board meeting before being officially appointed and criticized the lack of information on Kennedy Park in the meeting packet. He also commented during the impact fee public hearing questioning the lack of detailed operational cost data to justify the fee amounts.
