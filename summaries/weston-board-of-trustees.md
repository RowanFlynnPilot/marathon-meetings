## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Board of Trustees approved increased park and recreation impact fees for new residential development, passed several ordinances including mobile food vendor licensing and e-bike regulations, and held extensive discussion about relocating the farmers market to Kennedy Park due to the previous location being developed. The board also adjourned the Board of Review to June 15, 2026.

## Key Discussions
### Park and Recreation Impact Fee Amendment
Staff presented proposed increases to park and recreation impact fees from $300/$250 to $761 for single family, $563 for duplex, and $495 for multifamily units, based on the 2022 public facilities needs assessment. Jim Pitel spoke during public comment questioning the lack of detailed cost data to justify the fees. Trustee Clark supported the fee increase citing population growth and the need for new residents to contribute to park costs. Ordinance 26-010 was approved with motion by Lope Sorrell, second by Zagami, passing unanimously. Resolution 2026-015 amending the fee schedule was also approved unanimously with motion by Hermling, second by Clark.

### Mobile Food Vendor License Ordinance
Chief Finkey explained that Safer and surrounding municipalities (Marshfield, Wisconsin Rapids, Stevens Point, and others) created a reciprocal inspection agreement where one fire inspection sticker is valid across all participating communities, eliminating redundant inspections for food trucks operating in multiple jurisdictions. Ordinance 26-008 was approved unanimously with motion by Lope, second by Zagami.

### Remote Meeting Attendance Policy Amendments
Significant debate occurred over the policy language. Trustees Beck and Olsen questioned whether the draft policy matched what was previously discussed, noting the policy still contained language about health, safety, and welfare justifications that the board had voted to remove to make the policy broader. The board voted to amend the policy to strike sentences requiring justification reasons, with Hermling and Clark voting no on the amendment. The final amended policy passed with Hermling and Clark voting no, requiring only 12-hour advance notice, camera on, and no participation in closed session.

### Farmers Market Location at Kennedy Park
Extensive discussion-only item about the farmers market's relocation from Camp Phillips/Schofield Avenue (where property owner is developing) to Kennedy Park. Trustee Beck raised safety concerns about parking on grass along Alderson Street. Renee explained history: the market grew from 25 to 53 vendor spots and vendors appreciate having vehicles with them for shelter and restocking. Concerns were raised about grass damage and traffic. Trustee Clark noted if no suitable location can be found, the market may need to pause. Renee confirmed the market operates at a profit through vendor fees and grants, not a loss.

### Engineering Consulting Contracts
Director Dolan explained that two engineering inspection contracts totaling approximately $108,000 are needed for summer projects because the engineering technician position remains unfilled. He noted the specialized skillset is difficult to recruit in central Wisconsin, with even consulting firms losing employees to relocation. The contracts are for projects starting in June.

### Secondhand Dealer License for Schofield Coin and Hobby LLC
Chief Hunt explained the license is required under state statute for businesses buying and selling precious metals and used items, with merchandise tracked through a police-accessible database for stolen property verification. The motion was amended to require satisfactory background check completion and receipt of occupancy permit since the building is still under construction. Approved unanimously with motion by Hermling, second by Clark.

### Committee Appointments
President appointed Stephanie Daniels and Goldin Mua to the CLIPS committee. Openings remain on Parks, Finance/HR (two), and CLIPS (one). Board agreed that future committee applications should be included in meeting packets for trustee review.

## Public Comment
Jim Pitel of 5702 Street spoke during public comment criticizing the $108,000 in outside engineering contracts, questioning why the engineering technician position remains unfilled, and stating the administrator needs to be held accountable. He also criticized the appointment process for committees, noting Lisa Beck was introduced at a Safer meeting before being officially appointed, and complained that the Kennedy Park funding information requested at a prior meeting was not included in the packet. He also questioned the justification for the park impact fee increase during that public hearing.
