## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Board of Trustees approved increased park and recreation impact fees for new residential development, passed several ordinances including mobile food vendor licensing and e-bike regulations, and discussed ongoing challenges with the farmers market relocation to Kennedy Park. The board also adjourned the Board of Review to June 15, 2026.

## Key Discussions
### Public Hearing - Park and Recreation Impact Fees
Staff presented proposed increases to park and recreation impact fees from $300 to $761 for single family units, $250 to $563 for duplex units, and $250 to $495 for multifamily units, consistent with the 2022 public facilities needs assessment. Jim Pitel spoke during public comment criticizing the lack of detailed operational data justifying the fees. Trustee Motti supported the increase, noting population growth requires more park equipment and space. Ordinance 26-010 was approved with motion by Lope Sorrell, second by Zagami, passed unanimously. Resolution 2026-015 was approved with motion by Hermling, second by Clark, passed unanimously.

### Mobile Food Vendor License Ordinance
Ordinance 26-008 creates a reciprocal fire inspection agreement with Marshfield, Wisconsin Rapids, Stevens Point, Clover, Riverside, and Safer. Chief Finkey explained that food trucks inspected by any participating department receive a sticker valid across all jurisdictions, eliminating redundant inspections. Motion by Lope, second by Zagami, passed unanimously.

### Remote Meeting Attendance Policy
The board debated amendments to section 1.08 of the elected officials handbook. Discussion centered on removing specific requirements about health, safety, and welfare justifications for remote attendance, keeping the policy broad. Trustee Beck moved to amend by striking sentences requiring specific reasons for remote attendance. The amendment passed with Hermling and Motti voting no. The original motion as amended then passed with the same two dissenting votes.

### Appointments to Committees and Commissions
President appointed Stephanie Daniels and Golden Mua to the CLIPS committee. Motion by Hermling, second by Lopes, passed unanimously. The board agreed to include future committee applicant resumes in board packets. Parks, Finance/HR, and CLIPS still have openings.

### Farmers Market Location Discussion
Board discussed challenges with the farmers market's relocation from the Camp Phillips/Scoffield corner to Kennedy Park after the property owner developed that site. Trustee Beck raised safety concerns about cars parking on grass along Alderson. Renee explained the market grew from 25 to 53 vendor spots and vendors appreciate having vehicles with them. Discussion included potential alternative locations like Mont Miller Park, but concerns about visibility and attendance were raised. No action was taken; this was discussion only.

### Engineering Technician Vacancy
Public Works Director Michael Dolan explained the difficulty filling the engineering technician position, noting the specific skill set required is scarce in central Wisconsin. Even consulting firms are pulling staff from southeast Wisconsin. Two contracts for outside engineering inspection services totaling approximately $108,000 were approved for projects starting in June.

### Weston Avenue Speed Limit
Michael Dolan reported that a speed study showed the 85th percentile speed on Weston Avenue was 45 mph. The original engineering study recommended 35 mph. A second study is needed but was delayed due to construction. The matter will return to the board, possibly at a joint meeting.

## Public Comment
Jim Pitel of 2052 Street offered public comment criticizing the hiring of outside engineering consultants for $108,000 when an engineering technician position remains unfilled. He questioned why Lisa Beck was introduced as a Safer board member at the April Fire Department meeting before her official appointment. He also criticized the lack of a detailed Kennedy Park report in the meeting packet and called for greater administrator accountability. Pitel also commented during the park impact fee hearing, stating there is no solid operational data justifying the fee amounts.

## Action Items
- Park and recreation impact fees increased effective immediately per Ordinance 26-010 and Resolution 2026-015
- Mobile food vendor reciprocal inspection ordinance approved
- E-bike and nuisance ordinances approved
- Remote meeting attendance policy amended to remove specific justification requirements
- Stephanie Daniels and Golden Mua appointed to CLIPS committee
- Secondhand dealer license for Scoffield Coin and Hobby LLC approved contingent on background check completion and receipt of occupancy permit
- Compliance assurance plan approved
- Comprehensive plan steering committee meeting scheduled for Monday, June 1 at 6:00 PM
- Staff to include committee applicant resumes in future board packets
- Weston Avenue speed limit study to return to board at future meeting
- Board of Review adjourned to June 15, 2026
