## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Board of Trustees approved increased park and recreation impact fees, passed several ordinances including mobile food vendor licensing and e-bicycle regulations, and discussed ongoing concerns about the farmers market relocation to Kennedy Park. The board also adjourned the Board of Review to June 15, 2026 and made appointments to the CLIPS committee.

## Key Discussions
### Public Hearing - Park and Recreation Impact Fees
Staff presented a proposed increase to park and recreation impact fees from $300 per single family unit to $761, $250 per duplex to $563, and multifamily to $495, based on the 2022 public facilities needs assessment. Jim Pitel spoke during public comment questioning the justification for the numbers without detailed park maintenance cost data. Trustee Motti supported the fee increase, noting population growth requires more park space. Ordinance 26-010 was approved with motion by Lope Sorrell, second by Zagami, passed unanimously. Resolution 2026-015 was approved with motion by Hermling, second by Clark, passed unanimously.

### Remote Meeting Attendance Policy Amendments
The board debated amendments to the remote meeting attendance policy. Discussion centered on removing specific justification requirements for remote attendance. Trustee Beck moved to amend the policy to strike sentences requiring health, safety, or welfare justifications, keeping only the requirements to notify officials 12 hours in advance, be on camera, and not participate in closed sessions. The amendment passed with two no votes from Trustees Hermling and Motti. The original motion as amended also passed with the same two dissenting votes.

### Appointments to Committees and Commissions
The president appointed Stephanie Daniels (currently on Finance and HR) and Golden Mua to the CLIPS committee. Motion by Hermling, second by Lopes, approved unanimously. The board discussed having committee applications shared in the packet for transparency. Openings remain on Parks, Finance/HR, and one more on CLIPS.

### Mobile Food Vendor License Ordinance
Chief Finkey explained the ordinance creates a regional reciprocity system where food trucks inspected by one participating municipality (including Marshfield, Wisconsin Rapids, Stevens Point, and others) receive a sticker valid across all participating communities, eliminating redundant inspections. Ordinance 26-008 was approved unanimously.

### Farmers Market Location Discussion
Board members discussed ongoing concerns about the farmers market's new location at Kennedy Park after losing their previous site to development. Trustee Beck raised safety concerns about cars parking on grass along Alderson and within the park. Renee explained the market has grown from 25 to 53 vendor spots and vendors now appreciate having vehicles with them. The board acknowledged if no suitable location can be found, they may need to suspend the market temporarily. No action was taken; this was discussion only.

### Secondhand Article Dealer License
The board approved a secondhand article and jewelry dealer license for Scoffield Coin and Hobby LLC at 5703 Jamar Street. Chief Hunt explained these licenses protect against stolen merchandise sales. The motion was amended to require satisfactory background check completion and receipt of occupancy permit, as the building is still under construction. Approved unanimously as amended.

### Engineering Consultant Contracts
Public Works Director Michael Dolan explained the need for outside engineering consultants ($108,000 estimated) for project oversight, noting difficulty filling an engineering technician position due to limited workforce in central Wisconsin. He mentioned even consulting firms are losing employees to relocation to southern Wisconsin.

## Public Comment
Jim Pitel of 52 Street spoke during public comment criticizing the board for hiring outside engineering consultants when an engineering technician position remains unfilled, questioning why barriers to filling the position haven't been shared with the board. He also criticized the appointment process for committees, noting Lisa Beck was introduced at a SAFER meeting before being officially appointed. Pitel complained about lack of detailed Kennedy Park information in the packet and called for better accountability from the administrator.
