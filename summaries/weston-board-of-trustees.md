## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Board of Trustees approved increased park and recreation impact fees, passed several ordinances including mobile food vendor licensing and e-bike regulations, and discussed ongoing challenges with the farmers market relocation to Kennedy Park. The board also adjourned the Board of Review to June 15, 2026 and made appointments to the CLIPS committee.

## Key Discussions
### Park and Recreation Impact Fees
Staff presented proposed fee increases from $300 to $761 for single family units, $250 to $563 for duplex units, and $250 to $495 for multifamily units, based on the 2022 public facilities needs assessment. Jim Pitel spoke during public comment criticizing the lack of real operational cost data for parks. Trustee Barb supported the increase citing population growth and fairness. Ordinance 26-010 was approved with motion by Lope Sorrell, second by Zagami, passing unanimously. Resolution 2026-015 was approved with motion by Hermling, second by Clark, passing unanimously.

### Mobile Food Vendor License Ordinance 26-008
Chief Finkey explained this creates a regional inspection sticker system where Safer, Marshfield, Wisconsin Rapids, Stevens Point, Cloverland, and Riverside will mutually recognize fire inspections for food trucks. One inspection sticker from any participating municipality will be valid across all communities. Motion by Lope Sorrell, passed unanimously.

### Remote Meeting Attendance Policy Amendments
The board debated amendments to Section 1.08 regarding virtual meeting attendance. Trustee Beck raised concerns that the written policy still contained language about health, safety, and welfare justifications that contradicted the previous meeting's vote to make attendance more broad. After discussion, the board voted to strike sentences requiring specific reasons for remote attendance, keeping only requirements to notify 12 hours in advance, be on camera, and prohibition from closed sessions. Amendment passed 5-2 with Hermling and Motti voting no. The original motion as amended passed 5-2 with the same dissenting votes.

### Appointments to Committees and Commissions
President appointed Stephanie Daniels (currently on Finance and HR) and Golden Mua to the CLIPS committee. Motion by Hermling, second by Lope Sorrell, passed unanimously. Parks, HR/Finance, and one CLIPS position remain open. The board agreed to include applicant resumes in future packets.

### Secondhand Article and Jewelry Dealer License
Scoffield Coin and Hobby LLC at 5703 Jamar Street requested licenses. Chief Hunt explained the license requirement protects against stolen merchandise sales and involves posting items on a police-accessible website. Since the building is still under construction, the board amended the motion to require satisfactory background check completion and receipt of occupancy permit. Amendment and original motion both passed unanimously.

### Farmers Market Location Discussion
The board discussed challenges with the farmers market's new Kennedy Park location after losing the Camp Phillips/Scoffield Avenue site to development. Concerns included parking on grass areas along Alderson Street and safety issues. Renee explained the market grew from 25 to 53 vendor spots. Trustee Beck questioned costs but was assured the market operates at no loss through vendor fees and grant funding for EBT/token programs. Mont Miller Park was considered but rejected as too remote. No action was taken; discussion only.

### Weston Avenue Speed Limit
Public Works Director Michael reported conducting speed studies showing the 85th percentile speed at 45 mph. The road was under construction preventing completion of the study. He indicated findings would be brought back to the board. A resident had complained the speed limit remains inconsistent with the ordinance over two months after the board directed a change.

## Public Comment
Jim Pitel of 52 Street spoke during public comment criticizing the board for hiring outside engineering consultants for $108,000 while an engineering technician position remains unfilled. He questioned why Lisa Beck was introduced at a Safer board meeting before being officially appointed, calling it inappropriate government action outside the boardroom. He also criticized the lack of detailed information on Kennedy Park in the meeting packet and called for greater administrator accountability.
