## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Board of Trustees approved increased park and recreation impact fees for new residential development, passed several ordinances amending mobile food vendor licensing, nuisances, and e-bike regulations, and engaged in extensive discussion about parking challenges at the Kennedy Park farmers market location. The board also made appointments to the CLIPS committee and approved various license renewals.

## Key Discussions
### Public hearing on park and recreation impact fees
Staff presented a proposal to increase park impact fees from $300 per single family unit to $761, from $250 to $563 for duplex units, and to $495 for multifamily units, based on the 2022 public facilities needs assessment. Jim Pitel spoke in public comment questioning whether the village has real data on park maintenance costs. Trustee Barb spoke in favor, stating the fee is justified as population growth increases park needs. Ordinance 26-010 was approved unanimously. Resolution 2026-015 was also approved unanimously.

### Remote meeting attendance policy amendments
The board debated amendments to section 1.08 of the elected officials handbook regarding remote meeting attendance. Trustees discussed removing language requiring specific health, safety, or welfare justifications, instead making the policy broader. An amendment by Trustee Beck to strike sentences requiring justification explanations passed with Trustees Herling and Lopesorrell voting no. The original motion as amended then passed with the same two dissenting votes.

### Mobile food vendor license ordinance
Chief Finkey explained this creates a regional inspection sticker system where fire inspections from one participating municipality (Marshfield, Wisconsin Rapids, Stevens Point, Clover, Riverside, and SAFER communities) are recognized by all others, eliminating redundant inspections for food trucks. Ordinance 26-008 passed unanimously.

### Farmers market location at Kennedy Park
Discussion-only item addressing concerns about the market's new location after losing the Camp Phillips/Schofield Avenue site to development. Trustee Beck raised safety concerns about customers parking on grass along Alderson Street and in the park. Renee explained the market grew from 25 stalls at the old municipal building to 42 at Camp Phillips to now 53 at Kennedy Park, with vendors now able to keep vehicles with them. Some trustees suggested discontinuing the market if suitable location cannot be found. Renee clarified the market is not losing money and operates using grants for food assistance programs.

### Secondhand article and jewelry dealer license
The board approved a secondhand article dealer and secondhand jewelry dealer license for Schofield Coin and Hobby LLC at 5703 Jamar Street. Chief explained this involves buying and selling precious metals with large dollar transactions. The motion was amended to require satisfactory completion of background check and receipt of occupancy permit, as the location is still under construction. Approved unanimously as amended.

### Committee appointments
The president appointed Stephanie Daniels (currently on Finance and HR) and Golden Mua to the CLIPS committee. Golden Mua may be considered for Finance committee after settling into the first committee. Motion approved unanimously. Openings remain for Parks, two for HR/Finance, and one more for CLIPS.

### Weston Avenue speed limit
During public works report, Michael Dolan reported on the ongoing speed limit issue from two board meetings ago. The DOT engineering study showed it should be a 35 mph road, but traffic data showed 85th percentile speed was 45 mph. Another speed study is needed after construction ends. A resident had inquired as the speed is currently inconsistent with the village ordinance.

## Public Comment
Jim Pitel of 52nd Street spoke during public comment criticizing the hiring of outside engineering consultants for $108,000 when an engineering technician position remains unfilled, questioned why Lisa Beck was introduced as a SAFER board member before official appointment, and complained about lack of detailed Kennedy Park information in the packet. He also spoke during the park impact fee public hearing questioning whether the village has actual data on park maintenance costs to justify the fee increase.
