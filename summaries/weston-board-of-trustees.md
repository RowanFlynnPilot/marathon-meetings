## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Board of Trustees approved increased park and recreation impact fees for new residential development, passed several ordinances including mobile food vendor licensing and e-bike regulations, and discussed ongoing concerns about the farmers market relocation to Kennedy Park. The board also adjourned the Board of Review to June 15, 2026 and approved license renewals for the upcoming licensing term.

## Key Discussions
### Park and Recreation Impact Fee Amendment
Staff presented proposed fee increases from $300 to $761 for single family units, $250 to $563 for duplex units, and $250 to $495 for multifamily units, consistent with the 2022 public facilities needs assessment. Jim Pitel commented during public hearing that the village lacks detailed data on park maintenance costs. Trustee Olsen asked about fuel costs and when fees were last adjusted (2022). Ordinance 26-010 was approved with motion by Lope Sorrell, second by Zagami, passed unanimously. Resolution 2026-015 amending the fee schedule was approved with motion by Hermling, second by Clark, passed unanimously.

### Meeting Minutes Approval
Minutes from April 13, 2026 special board meeting and April 21, 2026 board meeting were approved. Motion by Zagami, second by Beck, passed unanimously.

### Committee Chair Updates
Trustees provided brief updates from their committee assignments. Parks reported Yellow Banks mat installation and tree removal from the river. Public Works noted upcoming contracts for recycling and project inspections. Tourism awarded grants including $10,000 to Wausau Mum Festival, $5,000 to Mountain Bay Cup, and $2,500 to GG's. Finance discussed election costs and parking regulations. MPD Joint Finance Committee voted to disperse funds back to the three communities.

### Mobile Food Vendor License Ordinance 26-008
Chief Finkey explained the ordinance creates a regional fire inspection sticker system with Marshfield, Wisconsin Rapids, Stevens Point, Clover, Riverside, and Marathon County. Once a food truck is inspected by one participating department, others accept the sticker. Motion by Lope, second by Zagami, passed unanimously.

### E-Bike Ordinance 26-012
Board discussed differences between e-bikes and e-motos (which can go 52 mph). The ordinance amends chapter 82 traffic and vehicles article 3 bicycles. Motion by Zagami, second by Clark, passed unanimously.

### Remote Meeting Attendance Policy Amendment
Board debated striking language requiring health, safety, or welfare justification for remote attendance. Trustee Beck moved to amend by removing sentences requiring justification reasons, keeping only requirements to notify 12 hours in advance, be on camera, and not participate in closed session. Amendment motion by Beck, second by Clark, passed with two no votes (Hermling and Olsen). Original motion as amended by Zagami, second by Hermling, passed with same two no votes.

### Committee Appointments
President appointed Stephanie Daniels (currently on Finance and HR) and Golden Mua to the CLIPS committee. Motion by Hermling, second by Lope, passed unanimously. Board agreed to include committee/commission applications in future packets.

### Secondhand Dealer License - Scoffield Coin and Hobby LLC
Chief Hunt explained the license is required for businesses buying/selling precious metals and large dollar transactions, with items logged on a police-accessible database. Motion by Hermling, second by Clark, amended to require satisfactory background check and receipt of occupancy permit since building is under construction. Passed unanimously as amended.

### Farmers Market Location Discussion
Board discussed concerns about the farmers market relocation to Kennedy Park after the previous Camp Phillips/Scoffield site was sold for development. Trustee Beck raised safety concerns about cars parking on grass along Alderson. Trustee Olsen and others debated parking issues. Renee explained the market grew from 25 to 53 vendor stalls and vendors appreciate having vehicles with them. Mont Miller Park was discussed but rejected due to location being too remote. Trustee Beck questioned costs but was assured the market operates at a surplus through vendor fees and grants for food assistance programs. Discussion only - no action taken.

## Public Comment
Jim Pitel of 52 Street spoke during general public comment criticizing the hiring of outside engineering consultants for $108,000 when an engineering technician position remains unfilled. He questioned why Lisa Beck was introduced at the April Fire Department meeting as a Safer board member before being officially appointed by the board. He also criticized the lack of detailed Kennedy Park information in the packet. During the park impact fee public hearing, Pitel commented that the village lacks specific data on park maintenance costs to justify the fee amounts.
