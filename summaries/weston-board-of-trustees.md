## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Board of Trustees approved increased park and recreation impact fees, passed several ordinances including mobile food vendor licensing and e-bike regulations, and discussed ongoing challenges with the farmers market relocation to Kennedy Park. The board also adjourned the Board of Review to June 15, 2026 and made appointments to the CLIPS committee.

## Key Discussions
### Park and Recreation Impact Fees
Staff presented proposed fee increases from $300 to $761 for single family units, $250 to $563 for duplex units, and $250 to $495 for multifamily units, based on the 2022 public facilities needs assessment. Jim Pitel commented that the village lacks detailed data on actual park maintenance costs. Trustee Barb spoke in support, noting the fees help new development pay for park infrastructure. Ordinance 26-010 was approved with motion by Lope Sorrell, second by Zagami, passed unanimously. Resolution 2026-015 was approved with motion by Hermling, second by Clark, passed unanimously.

### Mobile Food Vendor License Ordinance
Chief Finkey explained the ordinance creates a regional agreement with Marshfield, Wisconsin Rapids, Stevens Point, Clover, Riverside and others where one fire inspection sticker from any participating municipality is valid across all communities, eliminating redundant inspections for food trucks. Ordinance 26-008 was approved with motion by Lope, second by Zagami, passed unanimously.

### Remote Meeting Attendance Policy
The board debated amendments to the policy regarding when trustees can attend meetings remotely. After discussion about not wanting chairs to judge whether reasons for remote attendance are acceptable, the board voted to strike sentences requiring health/safety/welfare justification. Amendment by Beck, second by Clark, passed 5-2 with Hermling and Lob Sra opposed. The original motion as amended also passed 5-2 with the same dissenting votes.

### Farmers Market Location at Kennedy Park
Discussion-only item addressed concerns about the farmers market's new location after losing the Camp Phillips/Schofield site when the property owner developed it. Trustee Lisa raised safety concerns about cars parking on grass in Kennedy Park. Renee explained the market grew from 25 to 53 vendor spots and vendors now have their vehicles with them. Multiple trustees discussed that Mont Miller Park would be too remote and traffic would decline. No action was taken; the board acknowledged this is an evolving situation.

### Committee Appointments
The president appointed Golden Mua and Stephanie Daniels to the CLIPS committee. Motion by Hermling, second by Lopes, passed unanimously. Openings remain on Parks, HR/Finance, and one more on CLIPS.

### Secondhand Article Dealer License
Schofield Coin and Hobby LLC at 5703 Jamar Street was approved for secondhand article and jewelry dealer licenses, contingent on satisfactory background check completion and receipt of occupancy permit since the building is still under construction. Motion by Hermling, second by Clark, with amendment for contingencies, passed unanimously.

### Engineering Technician Position
During public works report, Michael Dolan explained the village has advertised for an engineering tech position multiple times but the specific skill set is scarce in central Wisconsin. Even consulting firms are pulling staff from southeastern Wisconsin. This relates to the $108,000 being spent on outside engineering consultants that Jim Pitel criticized during public comment.

## Public Comment
Jim Pitel of 52nd Street spoke during public comment criticizing the $108,000 being spent on outside engineering consultants when the village should fill the vacant engineering technician position. He also criticized the appointment process for committees, noting Lisa Beck was introduced at a SAFER meeting before being formally appointed by the board. He called for more transparency and accountability from the administrator, and criticized the lack of a detailed Kennedy Park funding report in the meeting packet.
