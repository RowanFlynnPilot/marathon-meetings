## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Board of Trustees approved increases to park and recreation impact fees for new residential development, passed several ordinances including mobile food vendor licensing and e-bike regulations, and discussed ongoing challenges with the farmers market relocation to Kennedy Park. The board also adjourned the Board of Review to June 15, 2026.

## Key Discussions
### Public hearing on park and recreation impact fees
Staff presented proposed increases to park and recreation impact fees from $300 per single family unit to $761, from $250 to $563 for duplex units, and from $250 to $495 for multifamily units, consistent with the 2022 public facilities needs assessment. Jim Pitel commented that the village lacks detailed data on actual park maintenance costs and is 'flying by the seat of our pants.' Trustee Lob Sra questioned when fees were last adjusted (2022). Ordinance 26-010 was approved unanimously (motion by Lope Sorrell, second by Zagami). Resolution 2026-015 was also approved unanimously (motion by Hermlang, second by Clark).

### Mobile food vendor license ordinance
Chief Finkey explained that this ordinance establishes a reciprocal fire inspection agreement between Safer and multiple municipalities including Marshfield, Wisconsin Rapids, Stevens Point, and others. A food truck inspected by any participating department receives a sticker valid across all jurisdictions, eliminating redundant inspections. Ordinance 26-008 passed unanimously (motion by Lope, second by Zagami).

### Remote meeting attendance policy amendments
The board debated amendments to the remote attendance policy. Trustees discussed removing language requiring health, safety, or welfare justifications for remote attendance, preferring a broader policy where members simply notify the chair and administrator 12 hours in advance. The amendment to strike restrictive language passed with two no votes from Trustees Hermlang and Lob Sra. The original motion as amended also passed with the same two dissenting votes.

### Appointments to committees and commissions
The president appointed Stephanie Daniels and Golden Mua to the CLIPS committee. The motion was made by Hermlang, seconded by Lopes, and passed unanimously. Openings remain on Parks, Finance/HR (two openings), and CLIPS (one opening). The board agreed to include applicant resumes in future packets.

### Farmers market location at Kennedy Park
Discussion-only item addressing concerns about the farmers market relocation from the former Camp Phillips/Schofield Avenue location to Kennedy Park. Trustee Beck raised safety concerns about parking on grass along Alderson. Renee (staff) explained the market grew from 25 stalls to 53 stalls and vendors now can keep their vehicles with them. The previous location was donated by a local business owner who is now developing the property. Mock Miller Park was discussed but rejected due to its remote location. No action was taken; the board acknowledged this is an ongoing evaluation.

### Secondhand article dealer license
The board approved a secondhand article and jewelry dealer license for Schofield Coin and Hobby LLC at 5703 Jamar Street, contingent upon satisfactory background check completion and receipt of occupancy permit. The business will buy and sell precious metals. Motion by Hermlang, second by Clark, amendment for contingencies passed unanimously, final approval passed unanimously.

### Weston Avenue speed limit
Public Works Director Michael Dolan reported that an engineering study is underway regarding the speed limit on Weston Avenue. The 85th percentile speed was measured at 45 mph. The road is currently posted inconsistently with the village ordinance. The matter will return to the board, possibly at the joint meeting.

## Public Comment
Jim Pitel of 2052 Street spoke during public comment, criticizing the $108,000 expenditure for outside engineering consultants and questioning why the engineering technician position remains unfilled. He also criticized the process for appointing Lisa Beck to the Safer board, claiming she was introduced at a Fire Department meeting before official appointment. Pitel also complained about lack of detailed information on Kennedy Park in the meeting packet and called for greater administrator accountability.
