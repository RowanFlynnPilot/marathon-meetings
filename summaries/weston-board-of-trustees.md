## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Board of Trustees approved increased park and recreation impact fees, passed multiple ordinances including mobile food vendor licensing and e-bike regulations, and engaged in extensive discussion about parking challenges at the relocated farmers market at Kennedy Park. The board also adjourned the Board of Review to June 15, 2026.

## Key Discussions
### Public Hearing - Park and Recreation Impact Fees
Staff presented proposed fee increases from $300 to $761 for single family units, $250 to $563 for duplex units, and $250 to $495 for multifamily units, based on the 2022 public facilities needs assessment. Jim Pitel commented that the village lacks detailed data on actual park maintenance costs. Trustee Barb Lobsinger supported the fee increase, stating it's justified as new families require more park space. Ordinance 26-010 was approved unanimously, and Resolution 2026-015 amending the fee schedule was also approved unanimously.

### Mobile Food Vendor License Ordinance
Chief Finkey explained that this ordinance creates a regional inspection agreement with Marshfield, Wisconsin Rapids, Stevens Point, Clover, Riverside, and other municipalities. Under this system, when one department inspects a food truck and places a sticker on it, other participating communities accept that inspection rather than requiring separate inspections in each municipality. Ordinance 26-008 was approved unanimously.

### Remote Meeting Attendance Policy Amendments
The board debated amendments to section 1.08 of the elected officials handbook. Trustee Beck raised concerns that the policy language didn't match what was discussed at the previous meeting. After discussion, the board voted to strike sentences requiring health, safety, or welfare justifications, leaving a broader policy where members notify the administrator and chair 12 hours in advance, must be on camera, and cannot participate in closed session. The amendment passed with two no votes from Trustees Hermling and Lobsinger, and the amended policy passed with the same two opposing.

### Appointments to Committees and Commissions
President appointed Stephanie Daniels (currently on Finance and HR) and Golden Mua to the CLIPS committee. Motion approved unanimously. The board also agreed to include committee applicant resumes in future board packets.

### Farmers Market Location Discussion
The board discussed challenges with the farmers market's new location at Kennedy Park after being displaced from the Camp Phillips/Schofield Avenue location when the property owner decided to develop that land. Trustee Beck expressed safety concerns about cars parking on grass along Alderson Street. Renee explained the market grew from 25 to 53 vendor spots and that vendors appreciate having their vehicles with them. The board acknowledged that if no suitable location can be found, the market may need to be suspended. Discussion was informational only with no action taken.

### Weston Avenue Speed Limit
Village Engineer Michael Dolan reported that an engineering study showed the 85th percentile speed was 45 mph, which would support keeping the limit at 45 rather than the 35 mph the original designer recommended. He noted construction delayed collecting additional data but will bring the matter back to the board, possibly at the joint meeting.

### Secondhand Article Dealer License
The board approved a secondhand article and jewelry dealer license for Schofield Coin and Hobby LLC at 5703 Jamar Street. Chief Hunt explained this involves buying and selling precious metals with large dollar transactions requiring tracking for stolen merchandise. The motion was amended to require satisfactory background check completion and receipt of occupancy permit since the building is still under construction. Approved unanimously as amended.

## Public Comment
Jim Pitel of 52nd Street spoke during public comment, criticizing the $108,000 cost for outside engineering consultants when an engineering technician position remains unfilled. He questioned why Lisa Beck was introduced at a SAFER meeting as a board member before being officially appointed by the board. He also criticized the lack of detailed information on Kennedy Park funding in the board packet and called for greater administrator accountability.
