## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Board of Trustees approved increased park and recreation impact fees, passed several ordinances including mobile food vendor licensing and e-bike regulations, and discussed ongoing challenges with the farmers market relocation to Kennedy Park. The board also appointed two new members to the CLIPS committee and amended the remote meeting attendance policy.

## Key Discussions
### Public Hearing on Park and Recreation Impact Fees
Staff presented proposed increases to park impact fees from $300 per single family unit to $761, from $250 per duplex unit to $563, and from $250 to $495 for multifamily units, based on the 2022 public facilities needs assessment. Jim Pitel spoke in public comment questioning the justification for the numbers without detailed operational cost data. Trustee Barb supported the fees as justified given population growth. Ordinance 26-010 was approved with motion by Lope Sorrell, second by Zagami, passing unanimously. Resolution 2026-015 was approved with motion by Hermling, second by Clark, passing unanimously.

### Mobile Food Vendor License Ordinance
Chief Finkey explained the ordinance creates a regional inspection agreement between Safer and municipalities including Marshfield, Wisconsin Rapids, Stevens Point, and others. Food trucks inspected by any participating department receive a sticker recognized by all, eliminating duplicate inspections. Ordinance 26-008 passed unanimously with motion by Lope and second by Zagami.

### Remote Meeting Attendance Policy Amendment
The board debated amendments to the remote attendance policy. Discussion centered on removing language requiring specific health/safety/welfare justifications for remote attendance. Trustee Beck moved to amend the policy to strike sentences requiring detailed justifications, keeping only requirements to notify 12 hours in advance, be on camera, and not participate in closed sessions. The amendment passed with Trustees Hermling and Motti voting no. The original motion as amended then passed with the same two opposing votes.

### Committee Appointments
The president appointed Stephanie Daniels and Golden Mua to the CLIPS committee. Motion by Hermling, second by Lopes, passed unanimously. The board discussed having committee applications shared in packets going forward.

### Farmers Market Location Discussion
The board held discussion-only on the farmers market relocation to Kennedy Park after losing the Camp Phillips/Scoffield location. Trustee Beck raised safety concerns about parking on grass along Alderson. Renee explained the market grew from 25 stalls at the old location to about 53 at Kennedy Park, with vendors now able to keep vehicles with them. Mont Miller Park was discussed but rejected due to poor visibility and distance. The board acknowledged this is an ongoing situation with no immediate resolution, with some suggesting discontinuing the market if no suitable location is found.

### Engineering Services Contracts
Public Works Director Michael explained that contracts for outside engineering consultants (approximately $108,000 mentioned in public comment) are needed because the engineering technician position remains unfilled due to workforce shortages in central Wisconsin. Even local consulting firms are losing employees to relocations. The position has been advertised multiple times.

### Weston Avenue Speed Limit
Michael reported on the speed limit issue raised at prior meetings. The DOT engineering study showed a 35 mph road, but the 85th percentile speed was 45 mph. A second traffic study is needed but was delayed due to construction. A resident noted the speed is currently inconsistent with the village ordinance. Michael indicated this would need to come back to the board.

## Public Comment
Jim Pitel of 52nd Street spoke during public comment raising three concerns: criticism of spending $108,000 on outside engineering consultants instead of filling the vacant engineering technician position, objecting that Lisa Beck was introduced as a Safer board member before official appointment by the board, and expressing disappointment that no Kennedy Park funding information was included in the packet as previously requested. He called for more accountability from the village administrator.
