## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Board of Trustees approved increased park and recreation impact fees, passed several ordinances including mobile food vendor licensing and e-bike regulations, and discussed ongoing challenges with the farmers market relocation to Kennedy Park. The board also adjourned the Board of Review to June 15, 2026 and made appointments to the CLIPS committee.

## Key Discussions
### Park and Recreation Impact Fees
Staff presented proposed increases to park and recreation impact fees from $300 to $761 for single family units, $250 to $563 for duplex units, and $250 to $495 for multifamily units. Jim Pitel spoke during public comment questioning whether the village has real data on park maintenance costs. Trustee Motti supported the fees citing population growth requiring more park space. Ordinance 26-010 was approved with motion by Lope Sorrell, second by Zagami, passed unanimously. Resolution 2026-015 was approved with motion by Hermling, second by Clark, passed unanimously.

### Mobile Food Vendor License Ordinance
Chief Finkey explained that this ordinance allows reciprocal fire inspections between Safer and municipalities including Marshfield, Wisconsin Rapids, Stevens Point, and others. A food truck inspected by one participating department receives a sticker valid for all communities, eliminating redundant inspections. Ordinance 26-008 passed unanimously with motion by Lope, second by Zagami.

### E-Bike Ordinance
Ordinance 26-012 amending chapter 82 regarding bicycles and e-bikes was discussed briefly, with trustees noting the difference between e-bikes and e-motos (which can go 52 mph on sidewalks). The ordinance passed unanimously with motion by Zagami, second by Clark.

### Remote Meeting Attendance Policy
Significant debate occurred over the policy language. Trustees Beck and others argued the policy should match what was voted on at the previous meeting - simply requiring 12-hour notice, camera on, and no participation in closed session - rather than including language about health, safety, and welfare determinations. Trustee Motti opposed getting involved in judging whose excuse is valid. An amendment to strike sentences requiring chairs to evaluate reasons was approved 5-2, with Hermling and Motti opposed. The final amended policy passed 5-2 with the same dissents.

### Farmers Market Location Discussion
Discussion-only item addressed concerns about the farmers market's relocation from Camp Phillips/Scoffield to Kennedy Park after the previous landowner developed the property. Trustee Beck raised safety concerns about cars parking on grass along Alderson. Renee explained the market grew from 25 to 53 vendor spots and vendors now can keep vehicles with them. Trustee Beck questioned costs but staff confirmed the market operates at a profit through vendor fees and grants. No action was taken; this was discussion only to gather feedback.

### Committee Appointments
President appointed Stephanie Daniels and Golden Mua to the CLIPS committee. Motion by Hermling, second by Lopes, passed unanimously. Parks and recreation committee and finance/HR committee still have openings.

### Secondhand Dealer License
Scoffield Coin and Hobby LLC at 5703 Jamar Street was approved for secondhand article and jewelry dealer licenses. Chief Hunt explained the licensing requirements for precious metals dealers and tracking stolen merchandise. Motion was amended to require satisfactory background check completion and receipt of occupancy permit since building is still under construction. Passed unanimously as amended.

## Public Comment
Jim Pitel of Deon 52 Street spoke twice during public comments. First, he criticized hiring outside engineering consultants for $108,000 when an engineering technician position remains unfilled, questioned why Lisa Beck was introduced as a Safer board member before official appointment, and complained about lack of Kennedy Park project information in packets and administrator accountability. During the park fees public hearing, Pitel questioned whether the fee increase is justified without actual data on park maintenance costs.
