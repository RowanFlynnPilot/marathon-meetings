## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Board of Trustees approved increased park and recreation impact fees for new residential development, passed multiple ordinances including mobile food vendor licensing and e-bike regulations, and discussed ongoing challenges with the relocated farmers market at Kennedy Park. The board also adjourned the Board of Review to June 15, 2026.

## Key Discussions
### Park and Recreation Impact Fee Amendment
Staff presented proposed increases to impact fees from $300 to $761 for single family units, $250 to $563 for duplex units, and $250 to $495 for multifamily units, consistent with the 2022 public facilities needs assessment. Jim Pitel spoke during public comment questioning the lack of detailed cost data justifying the fees. Trustee Motti expressed support, noting the fees are justified as population increases demand more park space. Ordinance 26-010 was approved with motion by Lope Sorrell, second by Zagami, passing unanimously. Resolution 2026-015 was approved with motion by Hermling, second by Clark, passing unanimously.

### Remote Meeting Attendance Policy Amendment
The board debated amendments to section 1.08 regarding remote meeting attendance. Discussion centered on removing specific justification requirements (health, safety, welfare) and making the policy broader, requiring only 12-hour advance notice, camera on, and prohibiting remote participation in closed sessions. An amendment to strike sentences requiring chairs to evaluate reasons for remote attendance was approved 5-2, with Trustees Hermling and Motti voting no. The amended policy then passed with the same 5-2 vote.

### Mobile Food Vendor License Ordinance
Chief Finkey explained the new reciprocal inspection agreement between Safer, Marshfield, Wisconsin Rapids, Stevens Point, Cloverleaf, and Riverside fire departments. Under this agreement, a food truck inspected by any participating department receives a sticker recognized by all, eliminating redundant inspections. Ordinance 26-008 was approved unanimously with motion by Lope Sorrell, second by Zagami.

### Farmers Market Location at Kennedy Park
Discussion-only item addressing the farmers market relocation from Camp Phillips/Scoffield Avenue corner (lost due to property development) to Kennedy Park. Trustee Beck raised safety concerns about cars parking on grass near the parking lot. Trustee Motti explained the previous location was donated by Ray Lockery for $1/year but is now being developed. Renee provided background noting the market grew from 25 to 53 vendor spots. The board discussed whether parking on grass damages the field and whether alternative locations exist. No action was taken; this was discussion only.

### Committee Appointments
The president appointed Stephanie Daniels and Golden Mua to the CLIPS committee. Motion by Hermling, second by Lope Sorrell, approved unanimously. Parks, finance/HR, and CLIPS still have openings. The board agreed that future committee applications should be included in board packets.

### Secondhand Dealer License
Scoffield Coin and Hobby LLC at 5703 Jamar Street applied for secondhand article and jewelry dealer licenses. Chief Hunt explained this involves buying/selling precious metals with large dollar transactions requiring oversight similar to pawn brokers. The motion was amended to require satisfactory background check completion and receipt of occupancy permit, as the building is still under construction. Approved unanimously as amended.

### Weston Avenue Speed Limit
Director Michael Dolan reported on the speed limit issue from prior meetings. The DOT engineering study showed 35 mph, but the board had directed 45 mph. A speed study showed the 85th percentile speed was 45 mph. The road is currently under construction so additional data collection is delayed. Trustee Pitel noted in public comment the speed is currently inconsistent with ordinance. Staff will bring back to the board, possibly at a joint meeting.

## Public Comment
Jim Pitel of 5202 Street spoke during public comment criticizing the village for hiring outside engineering consultants at $108,000 when an engineering technician position remains unfilled. He questioned why Lisa Beck was introduced as a Safer board member before being officially appointed. He also criticized the lack of detailed information on Kennedy Park in the packet and called for greater accountability from the administrator.
