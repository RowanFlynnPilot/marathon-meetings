## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Board of Trustees approved increased park and recreation impact fees for new residential development, passed ordinances regulating mobile food vendors and e-bikes, and discussed ongoing challenges with the farmers market's new location at Kennedy Park. The board also adjourned the Board of Review to June 15, 2026 and approved various license renewals for the upcoming year.

## Key Discussions
### Public Hearing - Park and Recreation Impact Fees
The board held a public hearing on increasing park and recreation impact fees from $300/$250 per unit to $761 for single family, $563 for duplex, and $495 for multifamily units, consistent with the 2022 public facilities needs assessment. Jim Pitel spoke during public comment questioning the lack of detailed cost data to justify the fees. Trustee Barb supported the increase, noting population growth requires more park space. Ordinance 26-010 was approved with motion by Lopsorrell, second by Zagami, passing unanimously. Resolution 2026-015 amending the fee schedule was approved with motion by Hermling, second by Clark, passing unanimously.

### Mobile Food Vendor License Ordinance 26-008
Chief Finkey explained this ordinance creates a regional reciprocal inspection agreement between Safer and municipalities including Marshfield, Wisconsin Rapids, Stevens Point, and Clover Belt. Under the new system, a food truck inspected by any participating department receives a sticker recognized by all, eliminating the need for multiple inspections across communities. Motion by Lopsorrell, second by Zagami, passed unanimously.

### Remote Meeting Attendance Policy Amendment
The board debated amendments to the remote meeting attendance policy. After significant discussion, the board voted to remove language requiring specific health, safety, or welfare justifications for remote attendance and remove language prohibiting remote attendance for vacation or travel. The policy was amended to simply require 12-hour advance notice to the administrator and clerk, being on camera, and prohibition from closed sessions. Amendment motion by Beck, second by Clark passed with Hermling and Motti opposed. The amended policy then passed with the same two opposed.

### Farmers Market Location Discussion
The board held discussion-only on the farmers market's new location at Kennedy Park after losing their previous Camp Phillips/Scoffield Avenue location to development. Concerns were raised about parking on grass areas along Alderson creating safety issues and potential damage to fields. Renee explained the market has grown from 25 to 53 vendor stalls and generates positive revenue through vendor fees, grants, and token programs. Mount Miller Park was discussed but rejected due to poor visibility. No action was taken; the board agreed to continue monitoring the situation.

### Secondhand Article Dealer License - Scoffield Coin and Hobby
The board approved a secondhand article dealer and secondhand jewelry dealer license for Scoffield Coin and Hobby LLC at 5703 Jamar Street. Chief Hunt explained the business deals with precious metals and large dollar transactions requiring tracking for stolen merchandise protection. The motion was amended to require satisfactory completion of background check and receipt of occupancy permit since the building is still under construction. Passed unanimously with amendments.

### Committee Appointments
The president appointed Stephanie Daniels (currently on Finance and HR) and Golden Mua to the CLIPS committee. Motion by Hermling, second by Lopsorrell, passed unanimously. The board noted openings remain on Parks, Finance/HR (two), and CLIPS (one). The board agreed to include applicant resumes in future board packets.

## Public Comment
Jim Pitel of 52nd Street spoke during public comment criticizing the village for spending $108,000 on outside engineering consultants rather than filling the vacant engineering technician position. He questioned why Lisa Beck was introduced as a Safer board member at an April Fire Department meeting before being officially appointed by the board, calling it 'inappropriate government business taking action outside of this boardroom.' He also criticized the lack of detailed information on Kennedy Park funding in the board packet and called for the administrator to be held more accountable.
