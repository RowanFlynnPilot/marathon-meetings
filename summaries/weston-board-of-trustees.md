## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Board of Trustees approved increases to park and recreation impact fees, passed several ordinances including mobile food vendor licensing and e-bike regulations, and discussed ongoing challenges with the farmers market's new location at Kennedy Park. The board also adjourned the Board of Review to June 15, 2026 and made committee appointments.

## Key Discussions
### Public Hearing - Park and Recreation Impact Fees
Staff presented proposed fee increases from $300 per single family unit to $761, from $250 to $563 for duplex units, and to $495 for multifamily units, based on the 2022 public facilities needs assessment. Jim Pitel spoke during public comment questioning the justification for the numbers without detailed operational data. Trustee Clark supported the increase noting population growth requires more park infrastructure. Ordinance 26-010 passed unanimously with motion by Lope Sorrell and second by Zagami. Resolution 2026-015 also passed unanimously with motion by Hermling and second by Clark.

### Remote Meeting Attendance Policy Amendments
The board debated amendments to section 1.08 regarding virtual attendance. Trustees discussed removing specific requirements about health, safety, and welfare justifications, preferring broader language. Trustee Beck moved to amend striking sentences requiring justification reasons, seconded by Clark. The amendment passed with two opposed (Hermling and Clark). The original motion as amended also passed with Hermling and Clark voting no. The final policy requires 12-hour notice, camera on, and prohibits participation in closed session.

### Mobile Food Vendor License Ordinance (26-008)
Chief Finkey explained this creates reciprocal fire inspection recognition among Safer, Marshfield, Wisconsin Rapids, Stevens Point, Clover, and Riverside. Once one department inspects a food truck and applies a sticker, other communities accept that inspection for the season, reducing redundant inspections and costs for vendors. Passed unanimously with motion by Lopes Sorrell and second by Zagami.

### Farmers Market Location Discussion
Board discussed challenges with the new Kennedy Park location after losing the Camp Phillips/Schofield Avenue site to development. Concerns raised about parking on grass along Alderson Street and potential field damage. Trustee Beck raised cost concerns but staff confirmed the market operates at a surplus through vendor fees, grants, and token reimbursements. The market grew from 25 stalls at the original location to 53 currently. No action taken; discussion only item with possible future location changes if current site proves unworkable.

### Committee Appointments
President appointed Stephanie Daniels and Golden Mua to the CLIPS committee. Motion by Hermling, second by Lopes Sorrell, passed unanimously. Board agreed to include committee/commission applications in future packets for transparency.

### Secondhand Article Dealer License
Scoffield Coin and Hobby LLC at 5703 Jamar Street approved for secondhand article and jewelry dealer licenses. Motion amended by Beck, second by Clark, to require satisfactory background check completion and occupancy permit before license issuance. Passed unanimously as amended.

### Weston Avenue Speed Limit
Public Works Director Michael reported conducting speed studies showing 85th percentile at 45 mph. DOT engineering study classified it as 35 mph road, but village engineer may sign off on higher limit pending additional data collection. Will return to board for action. Trustee Zagami noted the speed has been inconsistent with ordinance for over two months.

## Public Comment
Jim Pitel of 52nd Street spoke criticizing the hiring of outside engineering consultants ($108,000) when an engineering technician position remains unfilled. He also criticized the appointment process for committees, noting Lisa Beck was introduced at a SAFER meeting before official board appointment. He requested a detailed breakdown of Kennedy Park project funding and timeline, and questioned justification for the proposed park impact fee increases without operational cost data.
