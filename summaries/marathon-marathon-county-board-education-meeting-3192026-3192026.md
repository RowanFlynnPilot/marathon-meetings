## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Board held an educational meeting featuring presentations on PFAS contamination litigation opportunities and county authority to regulate renewable energy projects. No votes were taken as this was an informational session, but the board received detailed explanations of legal options for joining multi-district PFAS litigation and strategies for addressing proposed wind energy projects in the county.

## Key Discussions
### Public Comment
Five residents spoke during public comment. Cindy Nelson from Stratford reported going to 200 houses regarding wind turbine projects, stating no one she spoke with wants wind turbines and residents feel uninformed. Wendy Rowski from Green Valley urged the board to vote no on advancing the comprehensive plan, arguing that 'wind farm' terminology is misleading for industrial-scale energy facilities. Barb Newton from Rib Mountain reiterated support for reducing speed limits on Double N, noting 75 residents signed a petition and describing near head-on collisions. Heidi Pesky from Town of McMillan argued against joint development agreements for wind projects, listing concerns about county liability and long-term obligations. Cindy Hogan from Rib Mountain supported the speed reduction petition.

### PFAS Multi-District Litigation Presentation
Attorney Andy Phillips from Atollis Law and Carrie McDougall from Baron and Bud Law Firm (appearing virtually) presented on opportunities for Marathon County to join nationwide PFAS litigation. McDougall explained the MDL has resulted in a $12-13 billion settlement from 3M and $3-5 billion from DuPont for water contamination claims. Soil-based claims including airports and landfills are expected to be addressed next. Supervisor Robinson asked whether settlements would restrict future claims; McDougall confirmed the water settlement specifically excluded airport, wastewater, and landfill claims. Vice Chair Dickinson noted the county airport has no known PFAS contamination currently. The proposed legal services agreement includes a 25% contingency fee with no upfront costs to the county.

### Renewable Energy Regulatory Authority Presentation
Rebecca Roker from Atollis Law, representing Wisconsin Counties Association, explained county authority to regulate wind and solar projects. She noted the Hub City Wind project from Alliant Energy has no PSC filing yet, and the Marathon Wind LLC project was purchased as part of Hub City. Roker emphasized that PSC has approved all 33 solar projects it has reviewed. She outlined county options: do nothing, negotiate a joint development agreement (JDA), intervene in PSC proceedings, or litigate. Roker strongly recommended JDAs as the best mechanism to protect county interests, noting they can address liability, environmental concerns, road damage, decommissioning, and emergency response training - protections not otherwise available under state law.

## Public Comment
