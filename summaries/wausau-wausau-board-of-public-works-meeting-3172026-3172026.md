## Meeting Overview
The Wausau Board of Public Works approved the lowest bid for the 26th Street construction project at $1,279,089.75, approved a change order for the Randolph Street/Cherry Street project totaling $14,436.50, and issued a concrete license to KSK Inc. All items passed unanimously in a brief meeting.

## Key Discussions
### March 10th Board of Public Works Minutes
The minutes from the March 10th regular meeting were approved. A motion was made and seconded, passing unanimously with all in favor.

### 26th Street Construction Project Bid Opening
Seven bids were opened and read aloud. Switlick submitted the lowest bid at $1,279,089.75, narrowly beating Hos at $1,280,877.96. Other bids ranged up to $1,686,708.75 from Earth. A board member noted the bids were 'tight.' A motion to approve Switlick as the winning bidder was made, seconded, and passed unanimously.

### North 8th Avenue Bid Opening
This item was postponed as the bid opening deadline was extended. Staff indicated it will come back to a future meeting.

### Randolph Street/Cherry Street Change Order 1
Staff explained four items in the change order totaling $14,436.50: an inside drop on a manhole ($4,856) due to an unrecorded large diameter sanitary service; water main tee installation ($2,317.50) after finding 6-inch pipe instead of expected 8-inch; miscellaneous storm sewer tie-ins ($5,016) at $66 per lineal foot; and geogrid installation ($2,247) for approximately 750 square yards near Thomas Jefferson Elementary due to poor soil conditions. Change Order 2 regarding liquidated damages was not considered pending further discussions. Motion to approve passed unanimously.

### Randolph Street/Cherry Street Pay Estimate Number 9
Staff recommended approval of pay estimate number nine for work completed through the end of the year in the amount of $535,114.20. Motion to approve was made, seconded, and passed unanimously.

### Portland Cement Concrete License - KSK Incorporated
Vinnie confirmed he reviewed the application and everything was in order. A motion to approve the Portland cement concrete license for KSK Inc. was made, seconded, and passed unanimously.

## Public Comment
No public comment was offered.
