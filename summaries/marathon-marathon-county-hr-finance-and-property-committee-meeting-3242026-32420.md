## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County HR, Finance, and Property Committee approved several routine items including a claim disallowance, revised property values for auction, carry forward funds, and a capital assets threshold policy change. The committee also received introductions from new healthcare consultants and detailed fiscal updates for 2025 and 2026.

## Key Discussions
### Claim disallowance - Mercedes Holmes
Corporation Counsel presented a claim received December 5th from Mercedes Holmes related to the death of her 3-year-old child Zalen Bernett, who was placed in a treatment foster care home licensed through another agency in Dunn County. The death was determined to be natural causes with no wrongdoing found through law enforcement and social services investigations. Outside counsel and the insurance carrier recommended disallowance. Chair Gibbs moved to disallow the claim; Supervisor Lemer seconded. Motion carried.

### Revised property values for public auction
Staff requested revised minimum sale prices for two parcels that failed to sell twice on Wisconsin Surplus because bids did not reach appraised values. The 529 Mullen Street parcel was set at $9,000 and 738 South 3rd Avenue at $7,500. Chair Gibbs moved, Supervisor Lemer seconded. Motion carried unanimously. Committee Chair Robinson asked about bidders who failed to pay, and staff confirmed they are marked as non-pay and banned from future auctions.

### Resolution to approve carry forward funds (R20-2026)
Finance Director Sam presented carry forward requests for program revenues and multi-year projects. Notable items included veterans relief fund replenishment (approximately 3 years of funding), register of deeds redaction funds, and $142,731 for administration special projects including $75,000 for homelessness contract. Vice Chair Marshall questioned redaction funds; staff agreed to bring more information from Register of Deeds Jenny Leur at a future meeting. Chair Gibbs moved, Supervisor Hart seconded. Motion carried.

### Capital assets threshold policy amendment
Finance Director Sam proposed increasing the capitalization threshold from $5,000 to $10,000, following GFOA guidance from 2006 that was never fully implemented. This change affects when items are expensed versus depreciated as capital assets. Supervisor Hart moved, Chair Gibbs seconded. Motion carried unanimously.

### Introduction of healthcare consultants - National Insurance Services
HR Director Candace introduced NIS representatives following their selection through RFP. Rick (28 years experience) and Jordan Stanley presented their team and approach focused on public sector clients. They discussed evaluating the near-site ATA clinic return on investment, assessing funding models (fully insured vs self-insured vs level funded), and increasing transparency with the committee. Vice Chair Marshall and Chair Gibbs asked detailed questions about comparative costs, emergency room utilization strategies, virtual care tools, and risk tolerance for self-insurance evaluation.

### 2025 year-end fiscal update
Finance Director Sam provided detailed department-by-department review of unaudited 2025 figures. Key items included: TID closure check of $257,238 from City of Wausau, unclaimed property of $222,752, sales tax approximately $2 million, opioid settlement fund at $2.2 million cash with $3.49 million in future payments expected. When asked about projected fund balance surplus, Sam indicated she would have that figure at the next meeting after capital assets reconciliation.

### Finance department quarterly update
Sam reported on department activities including welcoming a new payroll financial analyst, quarterly closeouts with departments, training on uniform practices, W-2 processing challenges related to the 'big beautiful bill' no-tax-on-overtime calculations, random cash audits (all successful), and upcoming policy work. County Administrator Lance publicly recognized Sam and her team for exceptional work during year-end close, particularly managing W-2 complications. Chair Gibbs and Vice Chair Marshall also praised the improved reporting and transparency.

### County Treasurer update
Treasurer Connie reported on tax collection activities including 1,582 delinquent tax notices sent (down from 1,786 last year), lottery credit processing issues with municipalities, and ongoing education efforts. Discussion covered municipal treasurer training needs, payment agreement policy changes (no longer offered due to high default rates), and the county's accelerated tax deed process. Supervisor Lemer asked about poverty trends; staff noted delinquencies are decreasing. County Administrator Lance clarified eviction processes are related to county tax deed efforts, not general evictions.

## Public Comment
No public comment was offered.

- Set revised minimum sale prices for 529 Mullen Street ($9,000) and 738 South 3rd Avenue ($7,500) for public auction

- Approved capital assets threshold policy amendment increasing threshold from $5,000 to $10,000 - moves to full county board
