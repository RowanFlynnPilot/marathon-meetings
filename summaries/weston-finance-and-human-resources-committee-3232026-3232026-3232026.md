## Meeting Overview
The Finance and Human Resources Committee debated a proposed increase to employee clothing allowances following the cancellation of a Cintas uniform contract. After multiple failed motions, the committee voted 4-1 to recommend $400 annually for clothing allowance plus a one-time purchase of a washer and dryer for staff use. The meeting also featured a detailed presentation on public works operations and budget.

## Key Discussions
### Public Works Operations and Budget Presentation
Public Works Director Michael presented a comprehensive overview of department operations, noting the 2026 budget decreased by $26,000 (1.1%) compared to 2025. He highlighted that the village maintains 119.5 centerline miles of road with 10 full-time staff (down from 11 in 2010). Michael emphasized the village spends approximately $9,700 less per mile than average central Wisconsin communities. He detailed the March 2026 storm response where some employees worked 17+ hours, with estimated costs around $50,000 for that single event. The department is pursuing disaster relief funding through the county.

### Clothing and Equipment Allowance Amendments
Following cancellation of the Cintas uniform contract, staff proposed increasing the clothing allowance from $300 to $600. Committee member Brad Daniels objected, arguing savings shouldn't automatically be spent given fiscal constraints and the upcoming fire department referendum. Director Michael advocated strongly for the increase, noting employees work in harsh conditions with chemicals and that the village already pays below average compared to other municipalities. Initial motion for $600 failed 2-3 (Daniels and Stephanie Olsson yes; Armain, Satai, and one other no). A $400 motion failed 2-2. A $500 motion with washer/dryer failed. Final motion for $400 annually with a one-time washer/dryer purchase passed 4-1 (Olsson voting no).

### Approval of Previous Meeting Minutes
Minutes from February 16, 2026 were approved. Motion by Steve, seconded by Stephanie. Passed unanimously.

### Financial Reports Acknowledgment
February 2026 financial reports for all funds were acknowledged unanimously. T1 and T2 detail reports and legal details for February were also acknowledged with unanimous votes.

## Public Comment
Lisa Beck of 1808 Cortez Lane offered public comment. She praised Mike for his work during the recent storm and questioned the proposed clothing allowance increase, suggesting the village consider a lesser amount rather than the highest proposed amount to save money. She noted every employee already receives safety apparel, shirts, hats, and $300 for boots or pants.
