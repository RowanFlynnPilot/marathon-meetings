## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Board of Supervisors adopted the 2026 Comprehensive Plan with multiple amendments addressing renewable energy terminology, data centers, AI technology, and energy policy. The board also approved salaries for elected officials, authorized phase 2 design for a new highway facility, engaged outside counsel for PFAS litigation, and ratified a local emergency declaration for the recent blizzard.

## Key Discussions
### Comprehensive Plan 2026 Adoption
Administrator Leonard presented 10 proposed amendments compiled from supervisor feedback. Amendment 1 (livability standards) passed unanimously. Amendments 2, 3, and 4 regarding alternative energy systems terminology were separated at Supervisor Crawl's request and each passed but not unanimously. Amendment 5 (data centers and battery storage) and Amendment 7 (regulate when allowed by law) both passed but not unanimously, with Supervisor Leur voting no on Amendment 5 citing it was 'too ideological.' Amendment 6 (radon and lead) passed unanimously. Amendment 8 (AI and automation language) proposed by Supervisor Leur passed unanimously. Amendment 9 by Supervisor Sundowski on coal, natural gas, and nuclear energy was amended by Supervisor Boots to read 'promote coal and natural gas until a long-term sustainable and reliable energy source can be found that does not adversely affect agricultural land' and passed but not unanimously. A late amendment by Supervisor Sundowski regarding utility-scale wind, solar, and battery storage as industrial uses was debated; Supervisor Jeang moved to refer it to committee but this was defeated, and the amendment itself was defeated. The final comprehensive plan as amended carried but was not unanimous.

### Establishing Salaries for Elected Officials
Resolution 12-26 establishing salaries for the clerk of courts, sheriff, and elected department heads for the upcoming term was approved. Motion by Supervisor Conway, second by Supervisor Rosenberg. The motion carried.

### Highway Facility Phase 2 Design Services
Resolution 13-26 authorizing staff to proceed with phase 2 design services for the new highway facility was approved unanimously. Supervisor Soyber requested future information on plans for the old facility. Chair Gibbs noted that HR Finance and Property Committee will address this but it may be years before the transition occurs.

### PFAS Litigation Outside Counsel
Resolution 14-26 authorizing engagement of outside counsel on a contingency basis for PFAS lawsuits passed unanimously with two amendments. Supervisor Robinson's amendment directing the county administrator to evaluate past and present practices that may have resulted in PFAS exposure passed unanimously. Vice Chair Dickinson's amendment modifying language regarding airport property also passed unanimously.

### Local State of Emergency Ratification
Resolution 22-26 ratifying the declaration of local state of emergency related to the recent blizzard passed unanimously. Administrator Leonard explained the ratification preserves potential reimbursement opportunities and praised county staff across multiple departments who worked 12-16 hour shifts, with some sleeping on cots to maintain operations. Supervisor Fifer echoed the praise as infrastructure committee chair.

### County Administrator Performance Evaluation
The board accepted the executive committee's recommendation regarding the administrator's performance evaluation and salary placement under the new pay structure. Motion by Supervisor Robinson, second by Supervisor Cavelli. Chair Gibbs noted no wording changes were made from the summary reviewed the previous Thursday. The motion carried unanimously.

## Public Comment
No public comment was offered.
