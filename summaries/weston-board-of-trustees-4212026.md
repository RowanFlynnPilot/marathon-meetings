## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Board of Trustees held its first meeting with three newly elected trustees, approving liquor license renewals, preliminary assessment resolutions for three street reconstruction projects, and a solid waste ordinance amendment. The board debated remote meeting attendance policy, ultimately voting 6-1 to allow remote attendance with camera requirements while prohibiting remote participation in closed sessions. Significant discussion occurred regarding Kennedy Park renovation funding transparency.

## Key Discussions
### Public Comments
Joe Jordan (4102 Camp Phillips Road) gave extended remarks welcoming new trustees and praising village staff, noting the village 'is not broke' and 'management isn't broken.' He reflected on his service, the fire department staffing needs, and defended the decision not to hold a referendum on the village hall building. Jim Pinsel criticized President Maloney's appointment of Louise Lopez as vice president despite Lopez not receiving any votes in the election, and objected to former trustees being reappointed to committees, calling it 'lack of transparency and disregard for the will of the people.'

### Approval of March Meeting Minutes
The board identified corrections needed to the March 16, 2026 minutes, including that the meeting actually occurred on March 23rd due to a snowstorm postponement, and that Trustee Cronin was marked excused but listed as calling the meeting to order. Motion by Lopez Saro, second by Clark to approve with corrections. Passed unanimously.

### Consent Agenda and Liquor License Renewals
Item 41 was pulled from consent agenda. Trustee Barb Oling made motion to approve liquor license renewals with a correction to Fairfield Inn and Suites' premise description to include only 'lobby, locked fridges, and locked storage room along with the south end hallway' rather than the entire hotel. Second by Olsen. Passed unanimously.

### Ordinance 26-008 Solid Waste Amendment
Motion to approve by unidentified trustee, second by Zagami. Passed unanimously with no discussion.

### Preliminary Assessment Resolutions for Street Reconstructions
Three resolutions passed for Gelick/Alderson, Bladel Avenue, and Concord Avenue/Bay's Street reconstructions. Director Michael explained assessments only cover driveway approaches (approximately $1,000-$1,500 per property) payable over 10 years. All three resolutions passed unanimously.

### Kennedy Park Renovation Quarterly Update
Extensive discussion occurred with trustees seeking clarity on funding sources. The Friends of Kennedy Park foundation account has $84,000, not including glow tournament proceeds. Trustee Beck and Trustee Zagami pressed for more transparency on fundraising efforts and grant applications. Director Stankevich explained the $851,000 in capital funds included unspent park project allocations over 20 years and budget surplus funds. Trustees expressed concern about public confusion regarding funding, with Zagami noting Kennedy Park was 'one of the biggest hot button issues' during the election. Staff agreed to provide more detailed information at the next meeting.

### Remote Meeting Attendance Policy
Trustee Lopez Saro moved to restrict virtual attendance to medical reasons only, requiring notification to clerk/administrator/chair, with camera requirements and prohibition on closed session remote attendance. Second by Zagami. Failed 3-4 (Lopez Saro, Zagami, Olsen in favor; Hushen, Beck, Maloney, Clark opposed). A second motion by Zagami with similar restrictions also failed. President Maloney then moved to allow remote attendance on camera with proper notification, prohibiting closed session remote attendance. Second by Olsen. Passed 6-1 with one opposed (Lopez Saro).

### President's Appointments to Committees
President Maloney presented committee appointments, noting Mark Kern remains on Community Development Authority, Joe Jordan was placed on Planning Commission and Public Works, and Steve Cronin was returned to Planning Commission replacing Travis Hoffman. New trustees were assigned: Katrina Clark to Mountain Bay Metro, Lisa Beck to SAFER, Barb Oling and Hushen Zagami to Everest Metro Joint Finance. Discussion was ongoing at transcript end.

## Public Comment
Two speakers offered public comment. Joe Jordan (4102 Camp Phillips Road) spoke at length welcoming new trustees, praising staff, and defending village decisions including the building construction and fire department funding approaches. Jim Pinsel criticized President Maloney's appointment of Louise Lopez as vice president and the reappointment of former trustees to committees, calling it favoritism and stating 'the residents overwhelmingly rejected the current policies and directions of the former board members.'
