## Meeting Overview
The Parks Committee approved minutes, selected Rettler Corporation for the Mock Mueller Park master plan project, and reviewed Yellow Banks kayak launch expenses showing significant grant funding success. Committee members also discussed increasing park impact fees to align with neighboring communities and received information about Kennedy Park ice rink operations.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of minutes from February 23rd, 2026
A motion to accept the minutes was made and seconded. The motion passed unanimously with all members voting in favor.

### Parks and Recreation Impact Fee Discussion
Jennifer presented information on park impact fees, noting the village currently charges $300 for single-family units while neighboring communities charge $600-$900. The 2020 study recommended fees of $761 for single-family. Plan Commission requested Parks Committee input before deciding. Committee members expressed support for a moderate increase to align with neighboring communities rather than the maximum allowed, with Katrina and others noting the need to fund amenities like trails. Jennifer noted Granite Ridge subdivision's 88 lots would generate $26,400 at current rates versus $52,800 at doubled rates. No formal action was taken; feedback will be provided to Plan Commission.

### Request for Proposals for Mock Mueller Park Master Plan
Staff received seven proposals for the park master plan, with four staff members reviewing and rating them based on firm experience, personnel, similar projects, scope understanding, and cost. The two lowest bids were JSD and Rettler Corporation. Roger made a motion to select Rettler Corporation, seconded by Katrina. The motion passed unanimously. Staff noted Rettler has done the village's most recent master plans including Kennedy Park.

### Yellow Banks Kayak Launch Expenses
Jessica presented a comprehensive breakdown of the kayak launch project expenses and grant funding. The project encountered unforeseen costs including poor subgrade and an unknown well casing. Multiple grants were secured by Jessica and Dan Higenbotham, with Marathon County Transportation covering the full expense for ADA accessibility components. Katrina praised the RFC documentation and suggested formal recognition for Dan Higenbotham's volunteer contributions. Lisa Beck also commended the RFC during public comment. No action was required; informational only.

### Ice Rink Operations at Kennedy Park
Staff presented information requested by Katrina on ice rink operations. The warming house has been unstaffed since 2020 due to COVID and subsequent recruitment difficulties. Staff noted Everest Youth Hockey remains interested in improvements including a roofed structure and possibly more than just an outdoor rink, but groups are hesitant to share details publicly until plans are finalized. Katrina expressed concern that hockey interests not be forgotten amid baseball focus at Kennedy Park. Committee requested additional historical attendance figures and user feedback for future meetings. No action taken.

## Public Comment
Jim Pinsel spoke expressing frustration about receiving no response to questions submitted at the previous meeting regarding playground equipment installation issues, Kennedy Park fundraising updates, and ice rink costs. He criticized the staff report for not including full labor costs for the ice rink, estimating true costs at $20,000-$30,000 versus the stated $1,320.98. Lisa Beck thanked Michael for snow plow work during the recent blizzard and praised Sean and Jessica for the well-written Yellow Banks RFC. A written response to Jim Pinsel's previous comments was submitted and will be included in the minutes.
