## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Parks Committee deferred discussion on allowing dogs at the aquatic center until next month, approved kayak launch signage for installation at village parks, and received updates on Kennedy Park fundraising and the Yellow Banks disc golf course. Public commenters expressed opposition to dogs at the pool and criticism of Kennedy Park financial transparency.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of minutes from April 27, 2026
Minutes were approved with a motion and second. No discussion. Motion carried unanimously.

### Kennedy Park update
Sean Oster-Brine presented a timeline document showing the Kennedy Park master plan process from 2023 to present, including the May 9th ribbon cutting for Ray Jack and Western Fields. Scott asked clarifying questions about village capital funds, learning there are currently no earmarked capital dollars for Kennedy Park construction as those items were pulled pending the master plan project. Discussion noted the Friends of Kennedy Park are fundraising for lights on the upper field, currently around $85,000 toward a $117,000 goal, and plan to make a public check presentation at their June tournament. Committee members emphasized that master plans are guidance documents subject to change and that the volunteer Friends group deserves grace while fundraising.

### Yellow Banks disc golf course update
Staff presented photos and maps showing fallen trees removed from the river and installation of wooden mats over gas pipelines to protect them during equipment access. Dan explained the mat installation process and showed pictures of the work. The mats are now village property with some extras available for other projects.

### Dog Day at the aquatic center
The committee deferred this item to next month because Committee Chair Katrina was absent due to an unforeseen issue. The chair stated she should have the opportunity to speak on this issue she had requested. One public commenter (Andy from 6504 [address]) had expressed strong opposition during public comment, stating 'There should not be animals allowed into a public pool. Period.'

### Graffiti on Oaklair River Trail
Staff explained challenges removing graffiti from concrete due to limited equipment access - pressure washers cannot reach the trail location. Best removal tool is a sandblaster but the trailer is too wide for the bridges. Discussion included possible anti-vandalism coatings and seal coats to make future cleaning easier. Committee members suggested volunteer work days, noting many people love the trail. No formal action was taken; staff will explore options and report back.

### Yellow Banks kayak launch signs
Dan from Marathon County (2625 Northwestern Avenue) presented version 7 of signage for kayak launches along the river. Signs will be installed at Highway J, Ross Avenue, and Yellow Banks within Village of Weston, plus locations in City of Schofield. The Highway J launch will likely be closed all season due to bridge replacement. The signs are funded by Marathon County Parks Foundation through state grants at no cost to the village. Motion to approve installation of the signs passed unanimously.

### Staff remarks
Sean reported that aquatic center pool repairs were completed, pool filling began and should be complete by end of day tomorrow, with opening day scheduled for June 6th. Dugout painting at Kennedy Park was completed. He noted his previous report incorrectly stated $10,000 from Marathon County Parks when it was actually $15,000, which was corrected in the minutes. The Mock Miller Park master plan process will begin with a kickoff meeting with Rettler next Tuesday, and a bike and pedestrian plan open house is scheduled for June 11th.

## Public Comment
Two public comments were offered. Andy (6504 [street]) spoke in person opposing dogs at the aquatic center, stating 'There should not be animals allowed into a public pool. Period. I like dogs, but it's not a place for them. We have rivers. We have lakes. That's where they belong.' Jim Pensan (5002 [street], Weston) spoke online under the name 'Vern M', criticizing the 4 p.m. meeting time as demonstrating 'zero desire for interaction and input from the general public' and questioning Kennedy Park financial reporting, calling the $8,700 figure 'too coincidental' and asking 'are the friends of Kennedy Park maybe frauds of Kennedy Park because they won't actually tell us where the money went?'
