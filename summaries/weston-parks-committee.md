## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Parks Committee approved kayak launch signs for the Plover River and deferred discussion on allowing dogs at the aquatic center until next month when the absent chair can participate. The committee also received updates on Kennedy Park fundraising progress and discussed graffiti removal challenges on the Oaklair River Trail.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of minutes
Minutes from April 27, 2026 meeting were approved unanimously with a motion and second. No discussion was held.

### Kennedy Park update
Director Sean Oster provided a timeline update on the Kennedy Park master plan project, noting the CORP was approved in November 2023 and the master plan the following month. Scott asked clarifying questions about the 10-year fundraising timeline and village capital funds. Oster explained the Friends of Kennedy Park are currently fundraising for $117,000 for upper field lights and have approximately $84-85,000 at the community foundation. He noted Youth Baseball contributed $8,700 for equipment and the friends group plans a public check presentation at their June tournament. Committee members emphasized this is a long-range volunteer effort and the master plan serves as a guide that can be adjusted over time.

### Yellow Banks disc golf course update
Staff provided photos and maps showing the approximate location of trees removed from the Plover River and the installation of wood mats over gas pipelines to protect them during equipment access. Dan explained the mats were installed by backing equipment onto them, with additional material added for approaches. TC Energy (formerly ATC/ANR Pipeline) was on site to monitor the work.

### Dog day at aquatic center
The item was deferred to next month because Committee Chair Katrina was absent due to an unforeseen issue. A member of the public, Andy (6504 [address inaudible]), spoke against allowing dogs in the public pool, stating 'There should not be animals allowed into a public pool. Period.' The chair noted Andy's objection would be recorded and he was welcome to return next month.

### Graffiti on Oaklair River Trail
Staff explained challenges removing graffiti from concrete and memorial benches on the trail, noting the pressure washer cannot access the area and benches weigh 2,000 pounds each. Options discussed included anti-vandalism coatings, luster seal, and volunteer work days. Committee member suggested community volunteers or groups like Boy Scouts could help with annual maintenance. No formal action was taken; staff will investigate cleaning options.

### Yellow Banks kayak launch signs
Dan Stroming from Marathon County Parks presented version seven of proposed signs for Plover River kayak launches. Signs will be installed at Highway J, Ross Avenue, and Yellow Banks in Weston, plus locations in Schofield. The signs include maps, river gauge QR codes, and location-specific historical information. Marathon County Parks Foundation funded the signs through state grants. Motion to approve the signs and forward to village board passed unanimously.

### Topics for future meetings
Staff requested discussion on food allergy awareness signage for playgrounds after receiving an email from an individual offering to provide signs encouraging people to keep food at picnic tables rather than play structures. Committee agreed to bring the item back for future discussion.

## Public Comment
Two members of the public commented. Andy (6504 [address inaudible]) spoke in person opposing dogs at the aquatic center, stating dogs belong in rivers and lakes, not public pools. Jim Pensan (5002 [Street] Weston), speaking online under the name 'Vern M,' criticized the 4 p.m. meeting time as discouraging public participation and questioned the Kennedy Park funding report, calling the $8,700 expenditure figure 'too coincidental' and asking where the $10,000 grant money, glow event proceeds, and lighting funds are, stating the 'numbers presented are extremely convoluted and misleading.'
