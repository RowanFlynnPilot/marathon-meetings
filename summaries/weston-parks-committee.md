## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Parks Committee deferred discussion on allowing dogs at the aquatic center to next month due to the chair's absence, approved kayak launch signage for three village locations, and received updates on Kennedy Park fundraising and trail maintenance issues. The meeting included public comment opposing the dog day concept and criticism of Kennedy Park financial transparency.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of minutes from April 27th, 2026
A motion was made and seconded to approve the previous meeting minutes. The motion carried with no opposition.

### Kennedy Park update
Director Sean Oster provided a timeline of the Kennedy Park project from the 2023 comprehensive plan approval through the May 9th ribbon cutting. Scott questioned fundraising timelines, and staff clarified the village's agreement allows approximately 10 years for project completion. The Friends of Kennedy Park are currently fundraising for field lights, having raised approximately $84-85,000 toward a $117,000 goal. Staff noted the friends group plans a public check presentation at their June tournament. Committee members emphasized the Friends group deserves grace as volunteers and that master plans are guidance documents subject to change.

### Yellow Banks disc golf course update
Dan provided photos and a PDF showing the approximate location of trees removed from the river, including images of wood mats installed to protect gas pipelines during equipment access. The mats, which are 8x8 timbers, are now village property with extras available for other trail projects. TC Energy (formerly ANR pipeline) representatives were on-site during the work.

### Dog Day at the aquatic center
The committee deferred this item to the June meeting because Chair Katrina was absent due to an unforeseen issue. One audience member who spoke during public comment expressed opposition to the concept, stating animals should not be allowed in public pools.

### Graffiti on Oaklair River Trail
Staff explained challenges removing graffiti from concrete and benches, noting pressure washing equipment cannot access the trail due to bridge width limitations and terrain. Discussion included applying anti-vandalism coatings or sealers and potentially organizing volunteer workdays. Staff will investigate cleaning options and report back. No formal action was taken.

### Yellow Banks kayak launch signs
Dan Verheyen from 2625 Northwestern Avenue presented version seven of signs for the Plover River water trail. Signs will be installed at three village locations: Highway J, Ross Avenue, and Yellow Banks. The signs include a map, QR code for river gauge, and location-specific history. Marathon County Parks Foundation is funding the signs through state waterway grants. Motion to approve installation passed unanimously.

### Food allergy awareness signage
Staff mentioned receiving a request to install food allergy awareness signs on playgrounds, which would be provided at no cost. Committee members agreed to bring this back for future discussion and education, acknowledging concerns about setting precedent for other signage requests.

## Public Comment
Two public comments were offered. Andy from 6504 [address unclear] spoke in person opposing dogs at the aquatic center, stating 'There should not be animals allowed into a public pool. Period.' Jim Pensan (appearing online under the name 'Vern M') from 5002 [street] Weston criticized the 4 p.m. meeting time as discouraging public participation, questioned Kennedy Park financial reporting calling the $8,700 expenditure figure 'too coincidental,' demanded to know where grant money and Glow event proceeds went, and accused the Friends of Kennedy Park of being 'frauds' for not sharing detailed finances.
