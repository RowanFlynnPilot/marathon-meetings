## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Parks Committee approved installation of new kayak launch signs along the Eau Claire River, deferred discussion on allowing dogs at the aquatic center until next month, and received updates on Kennedy Park fundraising progress and disc golf course tree removal. The committee also discussed ongoing graffiti issues on the Eau Claire River Trail.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of minutes from April 27, 2026
Minutes from the previous meeting were approved with a motion and second. The vote passed unanimously with no discussion.

### Kennedy Park update
Director Sean Oster presented a timeline document tracking the Kennedy Park master plan from approval in November 2023 through the May 9th ribbon cutting. He clarified that the Friends of Kennedy Park are fundraising for lights on the upper field, currently at approximately $84-85,000 toward their $117,000 goal. Staff explained that village infrastructure improvements are on hold pending fundraising progress. The $8,700 referenced in financials was clarified as a Youth Baseball contribution for equipment. Friends of Kennedy Park plan a public check presentation to the Community Foundation at their June tournament.

### Yellow Banks disc golf course update
Staff presented photographs showing tree removal locations along the river and mat installation over gas pipelines. Dan explained that wood mats were used to protect two gas pipelines during equipment access, and the village retained some extra mats for potential future trail projects. TC Energy (formerly ATC/ANR pipeline) conducted site visits and walkthrough after the work.

### Dog Day at the aquatic center
The item was deferred to next month because Committee Chair Katrina was absent due to an unforeseen issue. The presiding officer noted that resident Andy's objection to the program would be noted and that he was welcome to return for next month's discussion.

### Graffiti on Eau Claire River Trail
Staff explained challenges in removing graffiti from concrete and memorial benches, noting that pressure washers cannot access the trail due to bridge limitations and soft ground. Discussion included potentially using anti-vandalism coatings or sealers, and organizing volunteer work days for maintenance. Committee member suggested community volunteers might help with seal coating, comparing to Friends of Kennedy Park model. No formal action was taken; staff will investigate cleaning options.

### Yellow Banks kayak launch signs
Dan from Marathon County presented version 7 of proposed signage for Eau Claire River kayak launches. Signs will be installed at launches 3, 4, and 5 in Village of Weston (Highway J, Ross Avenue, and Yellow Banks). Signs include QR codes linking to river gauge data and Marathon County Parks Foundation donation URL. Funding comes from state grants through Marathon County Parks Foundation. The committee approved a motion to allow the village to install the signs, to be forwarded to the village board. Motion passed unanimously.

### Staff remarks
Director Oster reported aquatic center pool repairs were completed, filling began and should finish by end of next day. Dugout painting at Kennedy Park was completed. Pool training scheduled for the weekend with rentals starting next Wednesday and opening day on June 6th. Staff also mentioned upcoming Mock Miller master plan process kickoff with Rettler and bike/pedestrian plan open house on June 11th.
