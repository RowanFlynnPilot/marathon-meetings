## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Parks Committee approved installation of new kayak launch signs along the Plover River, deferred discussion on dog days at the aquatic center to next month, and received updates on Kennedy Park fundraising and Yellow Banks disc golf course improvements. Public commenters criticized the meeting time and questioned Kennedy Park financial transparency.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of minutes
Minutes from the April 27th, 2026 meeting were approved. A motion was made and seconded with no discussion. The motion carried unanimously with all present voting in favor.

### Kennedy Park update
Staff presented a timeline document showing the project history from the comprehensive outdoor recreation plan approval in November 2023 through the May 9th ribbon cutting. Committee member Scott asked clarifying questions about village capital dollars and fundraising timelines. Staff explained the Friends of Kennedy Park are a volunteer nonprofit working toward a 10-year plan, currently fundraising for $117,000 in field lights with approximately $84-85,000 already at the community foundation. Staff noted the Friends group plans to make a public check presentation at their first June tournament.

### Yellow Banks disc golf course update
Staff presented photos and a PDF showing approximate locations of trees removed from the river and the installation of wooden mats over gas pipelines to protect them during equipment access. Dan explained the mats are approximately 8x8 posts with notches and lifting rings, and the village has extra mats that may be used on trail projects.

### Dog Day at the aquatic center
The committee deferred this item to next month because Committee Chair Katrina was absent due to an unforeseen issue. A public commenter named Andy (6504 [street name inaudible]) spoke against allowing dogs in the public pool, stating 'There should not be animals allowed into a public pool. Period.' The chair noted Andy's objection would be recorded.

### Graffiti on the Oaklair River Trail
Staff explained challenges removing graffiti from concrete and benches, noting the pressure washer cannot access the trail and benches weigh 2,000 pounds each. They discussed possible solutions including anti-vandalism coatings and seal coats. Committee members suggested volunteer work days, noting community members love the trail. No formal action was taken; staff will investigate cleaning options.

### Yellow Banks kayak launch signs
Dan from Marathon County (2625 Northwestern Avenue) presented version seven of signs for kayak launches along the Plover River. Signs will be installed at Highway J, Ross Avenue, and Yellow Banks within Weston, plus locations in Schofield. The signs include a QR code to the river gauge site and donation URL to Marathon County Parks Foundation. The committee approved a motion to allow the village to install the signs, which will go to the village board. Motion carried unanimously.

### Topics for future meetings
Staff asked about interest in food allergy awareness signage at playgrounds, noting an individual offered to provide signs encouraging keeping food at picnic tables rather than play equipment. The committee agreed to add this as a future agenda item for further discussion.

## Public Comment
Two public comments were received. Andy (6504 [street name inaudible]) spoke in person opposing dogs at the aquatic center, stating dogs belong in rivers and lakes, not public pools. Jim Pensan (5002 [street name] Street, Weston), calling in under the name 'Vern M,' criticized the 4 p.m. meeting time as discouraging public participation and questioned Kennedy Park financial transparency, calling the $8,700 expense figure 'too coincidental' and asking about the $10,000 grant and glow event proceeds, suggesting the Friends of Kennedy Park may be 'frauds' for not sharing detailed finances.
