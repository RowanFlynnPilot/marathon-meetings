## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Parks Committee deferred discussion on allowing dogs at the aquatic center due to the chair's absence, approved installation of new kayak launch signs for the Plover River Water Trail, and received updates on Kennedy Park fundraising and the Yellow Banks disc golf course tree removal project. Public commenters voiced opposition to dogs at the pool and questioned Kennedy Park financial transparency.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of minutes
Minutes from the April 27, 2026 meeting were approved unanimously with a motion and second. No discussion occurred.

### Kennedy Park update
Director Sean Oster provided a timeline document showing the project history from the comprehensive plan update through the May 9th ribbon cutting. Committee member Scott asked about village capital funds, with Oster confirming no village dollars are currently earmarked pending fundraising progress. The Friends of Kennedy Park are targeting $117,000 for upper field lights, currently at approximately $84-85,000 in the community foundation. A committee member defended the Friends group, noting they are volunteers who plan to announce fundraising totals at their June tournament and deserve 'a little bit of grace.'

### Yellow Banks disc golf course update
Dan presented photos showing fallen trees removed from the river and wood mat installation over gas pipelines to protect them during equipment access. The mats are approximately 8x8 timber pieces owned by the village, with extras potentially available for other trail projects. TC Energy (formerly ATC/ANR pipeline) conducted site visits during the work.

### Dog Day at the aquatic center
Discussion was deferred to the June meeting because Katrina, who requested the agenda item, had an unforeseen issue preventing attendance. The chair noted he wanted her to have the opportunity to speak on the issue she raised.

### Graffiti on Oaklair River Trail
Staff explained challenges removing graffiti from concrete and benches, noting the best tool (sandblaster pressure washer) cannot access the trail due to steep ditches and narrow bridges. Suggestions included anti-vandalism coatings and organizing volunteer workdays. Memorial benches weighing 2,000 lbs each could potentially be removed one at a time for off-site cleaning. No formal action was taken; staff will investigate options.

### Yellow Banks kayak launch signs
Dan Lepper from Marathon County Parks (2625 Northwestern Avenue) presented version 7 of signs for the Plover River Water Trail. Signs will be installed at launches 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, and possibly 8, with three in the Village of Weston. The Highway J launch will likely be closed all season due to bridge replacement. Signs include QR codes for river gauge data and donation links to Marathon County Parks Foundation. Motion to approve installation passed unanimously.

### Topics for future meetings
Staff requested committee interest in food allergy awareness signage for playgrounds, noting one in 13 people have food allergies. Signs would be provided free and encourage keeping food away from play equipment. Committee agreed to bring it back for formal discussion. The Mock Miller Park master plan process will begin with a kickoff meeting with Rettler.

## Public Comment
Two speakers offered public comment. Andy (6504 [address unclear]) spoke against allowing dogs at the aquatic center, stating 'There should not be animals allowed into a public pool. Period.' Jim Pensan (5002 Street, Weston), calling in under the name 'Vern M' due to login issues, criticized the 4 p.m. meeting time as discouraging public participation and questioned Kennedy Park financial reporting, calling the $8,700 figure 'too coincidental' and asking where grant money and glow event proceeds are tracked, stating 'I do not understand why there is such a lack of cooperation and hesitation from the friends group to actually share the finances.'
