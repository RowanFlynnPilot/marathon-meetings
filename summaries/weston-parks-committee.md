## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Parks and Recreation Committee deferred discussion on allowing dogs at the aquatic center to next month, approved installation of new kayak launch signs for the Plover River Water Trail, and received updates on Kennedy Park fundraising and Yellow Banks disc golf course improvements. Public commenters expressed opposition to dogs at the pool and criticized meeting timing and Kennedy Park financial transparency.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of minutes
Minutes from the April 27, 2026 meeting were approved with a motion and second. The motion passed unanimously with no discussion.

### Kennedy Park update
Director Sean Oster provided a timeline of the Kennedy Park master plan development from 2023 to present, including the recent May 9th ribbon cutting for Ray Jack and Western Fields. Staff clarified that fundraising by Friends of Kennedy Park has no fixed timeline but the agreement references a 10-year hope for project completion. The Friends group plans to announce fundraising totals at their June tournament with a check presentation to the community foundation. Current focus is raising $117,000 for field lights, with approximately $84-85,000 already at the community foundation. Scott asked about village capital dollars for Kennedy Park; staff confirmed no village funds are currently earmarked pending fundraising progress. The committee chair emphasized giving the volunteer Friends group grace and noted they are not withholding information.

### Yellow Banks disc golf course update
Staff presented photos and maps showing tree removal locations along the river and installation of wooden mats over gas pipelines for equipment access. Dan provided documentation of the mat installation process and showed how fallen trees blocked river passage. The committee discussed ongoing coordination with TC Energy (formerly ATC/ANR Pipeline) regarding pipeline protection during construction work.

### Dog Day at the aquatic center
The item was deferred to next month because Committee Chair Katrina was absent due to an unforeseen issue. The chair noted the topic was originally brought forward by Katrina and she should have opportunity to speak on it. A public commenter named Andy from 6504 [address unclear] spoke against allowing dogs at the pool, stating 'There should not be animals allowed into a public pool. Period.'

### Graffiti on the Oaklair River Trail
Staff explained challenges with graffiti removal including limited equipment access (pressure washer cannot reach the area), heavy concrete benches (2,000 lbs each), and various paint types. Staff has tried multiple graffiti removers with limited success. Committee discussed anti-vandalism coatings and seal coatings as potential solutions. The chair suggested exploring volunteer work days similar to how benches were originally installed through a director team-building day. No formal action was taken; staff will investigate cleaning options.

### Yellow Banks kayak launch signs
Dan from Marathon County (2625 Northwestern Avenue) presented version 7 of proposed Plover River Water Trail signs for kayak launches. Signs will be installed at launches 3 (Highway J), 4 (Ross Avenue), and 5 (Yellow Banks) within Weston, with similar signs in Schofield. Signs include QR codes for river gauge levels and donation URL for Marathon County Parks Foundation which funded the signs through state grants. Dan noted Highway J launch may be closed all season due to bridge replacement. Motion to approve village installation of signs passed unanimously.

### Topics for future meetings
Staff requested permission to investigate food allergy awareness signage for playgrounds after receiving an email request from a community member. The signs would remind people to keep food at picnic tables rather than on playground equipment. Committee agreed to bring the topic back for further discussion. Staff also announced the Mock Miller Park master plan process will begin with a kickoff meeting with Rettler planned for the following Tuesday.

## Public Comment
Two public comments were offered. Andy (6504 [street inaudible]) spoke in person opposing dogs at the aquatic center, stating animals should not be allowed in public pools and belong in rivers and lakes. Jim Pensan (5002 [street] Street, Weston) spoke via Zoom expressing dissatisfaction with the 4 p.m. meeting time, stating no public meeting should be held before 5 p.m. He also questioned Kennedy Park funding transparency, calling the $8,700 matching receipts and expenditures 'too coincidental' and asking about the $10,000 grant and Glow event proceeds, characterizing the financial reporting as 'convoluted and misleading.'
