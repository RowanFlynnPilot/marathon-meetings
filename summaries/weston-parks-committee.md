## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Parks Committee deferred discussion on allowing dogs at the aquatic center due to the chair's absence, approved kayak launch signage for the Plover River Water Trail, and received updates on Kennedy Park fundraising and the Yellow Banks disc golf course. Public commenters expressed opposition to the dog day proposal and questioned Kennedy Park fundraising transparency.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of minutes
Minutes from the April 27, 2026 meeting were approved unanimously with a motion and second. No discussion was held.

### Kennedy Park update
Director Sean Oster Bring presented a timeline of the Kennedy Park master plan development, noting the comprehensive plan was approved in November 2023 and the master plan in December 2023. The total project cost is approximately $14.3 million with the village responsible for roughly half. The Friends of Kennedy Park are currently fundraising for field lights, having approximately $84-85,000 of the $117,000 goal at the community foundation. Scott asked clarifying questions about village capital dollars, with Oster Bring confirming no village funds are currently earmarked pending project development. A public check presentation is planned for the first tournament in June.

### Yellow Banks disc golf course update
Staff presented photos and documentation of tree removal from the Plover River and installation of protective wood mats over gas pipelines for equipment access. Dan showed the location of removed trees that were blocking the river and explained the mat installation process over the TC Energy (formerly ANR/ATC) pipelines. The pipeline company representative was present during the work and conducted a walkthrough afterward. The village has extra mats that may be used for future trail projects.

### Dog Day at the aquatic center
The committee deferred this item to the June meeting because Chair Katrina, who had requested the agenda item, was absent due to an unforeseen issue. Public commenter Andy from 6504 [street name unclear] stated he believes 'this is ridiculous' and 'there should not be animals allowed into a public pool.' His objection was noted for the record.

### Graffiti on Oaklair River Trail
Director Oster Bring explained challenges removing graffiti from concrete and benches on the trail, noting their best tool (a sandblaster on a pressure washer) cannot access the trail due to narrow bridges and steep ditches. Memorial benches weigh 2,000 pounds each. Committee members discussed anti-vandalism coatings and seal coats as potential solutions. Laura suggested volunteer work days, noting she would participate and believes trail users would help. Staff will explore cleaning options and potentially address items during off-season or one at a time.

### Yellow Banks kayak launch signs
Dan Nitz from Marathon County (2625 Northwestern Avenue) presented version seven of signage for the Plover River Water Trail. Signs will be installed at launches #3 (Highway J), #4 (Ross), and #5 (Yellow Banks) in Weston, plus locations in Schofield. Marathon County Parks Foundation is funding the signs through state waterway grants. The signs include QR codes linking to the Kelly Station river gauge and donation URL to the foundation. Dan noted the Highway J launch may be closed all season due to bridge replacement. Motion to approve the signs for installation passed unanimously.

### Topics for future meetings
Staff mentioned receiving a request about food allergy awareness signage for playgrounds, stating one in 13 people have food allergies. The signs would discourage eating on play equipment. Committee agreed to bring this back for future discussion. Director Oster Bring also announced the Mock Miller Park master plan process will begin, with a kickoff meeting with Rettler planned for the following Tuesday.

Jim Pensan (5002 [street] Street, Weston), speaking via Zoom under the name 'Vern M,' criticized the 4 p.m. meeting time as discouraging public participation, questioned the Kennedy Park funding report showing exactly $8,700 in receipts and expenditures as 'too coincidental,' asked about the location of a $10,000 grant and Glow Games proceeds, and called the numbers 'convoluted and misleading.'
