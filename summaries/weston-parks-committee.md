## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Parks Committee deferred discussion on allowing dogs at the aquatic center to next month, approved installation of new kayak launch signs along the Plover River, and received updates on Kennedy Park development and Yellow Banks disc golf course improvements. Public commenters expressed opposition to dogs at the pool and criticized the 4 p.m. meeting time and Kennedy Park fundraising transparency.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of minutes
Minutes from the April 27, 2026 meeting were approved. A motion was made and seconded with no discussion. The motion carried unanimously.

### Kennedy Park update
Director Sean Osterbrink presented a timeline of the Kennedy Park project from the comprehensive outdoor recreation plan approval in November 2023 through the recent ribbon cutting on May 9th. Scott asked about village capital dollars earmarked for Kennedy Park; Osterbrink confirmed there are currently no village funds earmarked as those items were pulled due to the Kennedy Park project. The Friends of Kennedy Park are fundraising for lights on the upper field, currently at approximately $84-85,000 toward a $117,000 goal. The committee discussed that the Friends group plans to make a public check presentation at their first tournament in June. Committee members emphasized giving the volunteer Friends group grace and noted master plans are guides that can change over time.

### Yellow Banks disc golf course update
Staff presented photos and a PDF showing the approximate location of trees removed from the river and the installation of wood mats over gas pipelines to protect them during equipment access. The mats are 8x8 posts with notches and lifting rings. TC Energy (formerly ATC/ANR pipeline) visited the site and conducted a walkthrough after the work was completed. The village has a couple extra mats that may be used on another trail project.

### Dog Day at the aquatic center
The committee deferred this item to next month because Chair Katrina, who had requested the discussion, was absent due to an unforeseen issue. A member of the public who came specifically to oppose dogs at the pool was told to return next month. No formal motion was required for the deferral.

### Graffiti on Oaklair River Trail
The committee discussed challenges removing graffiti from concrete and benches along the trail. Staff explained they cannot get pressure washing equipment to the trail due to steep ditches and narrow bridges. Options discussed included anti-vandalism coatings, seal coating, and potentially organizing volunteer work days. Committee member Laro suggested community volunteers might help, noting people love the trail. Staff mentioned the memorial benches are 2,000 lbs each. No formal action was taken; staff will investigate cleaning options.

### Yellow Banks kayak launch signs
Dan Gleason from Marathon County (2625 Northwestern Avenue) presented version 7 of signs for Plover River kayak launches. Three signs will be installed in Village of Weston parks (Highway J, Ross Avenue, and Yellow Banks). The signs include a map, QR code for river gauge site, and donation URL for Marathon County Parks Foundation. Gleason noted the Highway J launch will likely be closed all season due to bridge replacement. A motion to approve installation of the signs was made, seconded, and passed unanimously.

### Topics for future meetings
Staff raised the possibility of food allergy awareness signage on playgrounds, noting an organization offered to provide free signs. The committee agreed to bring it back for further discussion. The Mock Miller park master plan process will begin with a kickoff meeting with Rettler, and the last bike and pedestrian plan open house is June 11th from 5-7 p.m.

## Public Comment
Two public comments were received. Andy (6504 [address inaudible]) spoke in person opposing dogs at the aquatic center, stating 'There should not be animals allowed into a public pool. Period.' Jim Pensan (5002 [Street] Weston), speaking online under the name 'Vern M', criticized the 4 p.m. meeting time, questioned Kennedy Park funding transparency, and called the $8,700 expenditure figure 'too coincidental.' He asked where the $10,000 grant money, glow event funds, and lighting fundraising money are, calling the numbers 'convoluted and misleading.'
