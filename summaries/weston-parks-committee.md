## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Parks Committee deferred discussion on allowing dogs at the aquatic center until next month due to the chair's absence, approved installation of new kayak launch signs along the Plover River, and received updates on Kennedy Park fundraising and trail maintenance. Public comment included opposition to dogs at the pool and criticism of the Kennedy Park funding transparency.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of minutes
Minutes from the April 27, 2026 meeting were approved with a motion and second. The motion carried unanimously with no discussion.

### Kennedy Park update
Staff presented a timeline document showing the project history from the comprehensive plan approval in November 2023 through the May 9th ribbon cutting. Director Osterbrink explained the village had plans to contribute approximately $7 million of the $14 million total project for infrastructure like bathrooms and shelters built in the 1970s. Scott questioned the fundraising timeline, and Osterbrink clarified the agreement with Friends of Kennedy Park references a 10-year timeframe for project completion. The Friends group plans to announce fundraising totals at their June tournament. They currently have approximately $84-85,000 at the community foundation toward a $117,000 goal for field lights.

### Yellow Banks disc golf course update
Staff presented photos and maps showing tree removal locations and mat installation over gas pipelines. Dan explained the mats are 8x8 wood construction mats used to protect infrastructure during equipment access. The mats are now village property with extras available for other trail projects.

### Dog Day at the aquatic center
The item was deferred to next month because Katrina, who requested the discussion, was absent due to an unforeseen issue. The presiding officer noted resident Andy's objection would be documented but wanted to ensure Katrina had opportunity to speak on the matter.

### Graffiti on the Oaklair River Trail
Staff explained challenges removing graffiti from concrete and memorial benches, noting the pressure washer cannot access the trail due to bridge width limitations and steep ditches. Options discussed included anti-vandalism coatings, sealer applications, and potential volunteer work days. Committee member Melissa suggested community members would likely volunteer to help. No formal action was taken; staff will investigate cleaning options.

### Yellow Banks kayak launch signs
Dan Hager from Marathon County Parks (2625 Northwestern Avenue) presented version 7 of proposed signage for Plover River kayak launches. Three signs will be installed in Village of Weston at Highway J, Ross Avenue, and Yellow Banks locations. Signs include QR codes for river gauge information and donation links to Marathon County Parks Foundation. The Highway J launch will likely be closed this season due to bridge replacement. Motion to approve sign installation passed unanimously.

### Food allergy awareness signage
Staff mentioned receiving an email requesting participation in placing food allergy awareness signs on playgrounds. Signs would be provided free and encourage keeping food at picnic tables rather than play equipment. Committee agreed to add this as a future discussion topic.

## Public Comment
Two speakers provided public comment. Andy (6504 [street inaudible]) spoke against allowing dogs at the public pool, stating 'There should not be animals allowed into a public pool. Period.' Jim Pensan (5002 [street] Weston), speaking via Zoom under the name 'Vern M', criticized the 4 p.m. meeting time as discouraging public participation and questioned the Kennedy Park funding report, calling the $8,700 expenditure figure 'too coincidental' and asking where the $10,000 grant money and glow event proceeds are reflected. He characterized the Friends of Kennedy Park financial reporting as 'extremely convoluted and misleading.'
