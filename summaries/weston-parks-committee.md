## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Parks Committee deferred discussion on allowing dogs at the aquatic center due to the chair's absence, approved installation of kayak launch signs along the Plover River, and received updates on Kennedy Park fundraising efforts and the Yellow Banks disc golf course. Public commenters expressed opposition to dogs at the pool and questioned Kennedy Park fundraising transparency.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of minutes from April 27, 2026
Minutes from the previous meeting were approved with a motion and second. The motion carried unanimously with no discussion.

### Kennedy Park update
Director Sean Oster provided an update including a timeline document prepared with Michael showing the project's history from 2023 to present. Scott asked about village capital dollars for Kennedy Park; Sean confirmed no village funds are currently earmarked as those items were pulled from capital improvement plans pending the project. The Friends of Kennedy Park are currently fundraising for lights on the upper field, needing $117,000 with approximately $85,000 already at the community foundation. Sean noted the friends group plans a public check presentation at their June tournament. Committee members emphasized this is a long-term 10-year plan and defended the volunteer friends group against public criticism about transparency.

### Yellow Banks disc golf course update
Sean presented photos and a PDF showing the location of trees removed from the river and the installation of wood mats over gas pipelines to protect them during construction. Dan from the disc golf association showed photos of the mat installation process. The mats are owned by the village with some extras available for other trail projects.

### Dog Day at the aquatic center
The committee deferred this item to the next meeting because Katrina, who requested the item, was absent due to an unforeseen issue. An audience member named Andy (6504 [street name inaudible]) had attended specifically to oppose the idea, stating 'There should not be animals allowed into a public pool. Period.' His objection was noted and he indicated he may return next month.

### Graffiti on Oaklair River Trail
Sean explained challenges with graffiti removal, noting the pressure washer cannot access the trail due to bridge weight limits and steep ditches. Staff has tried various graffiti removers with limited success. Committee members suggested anti-vandalism coatings or sealers, and the possibility of volunteer workdays for maintenance. Sean said they would investigate cleaning options but noted access limitations require frozen conditions for heavy equipment. No formal action was taken.

### Yellow Banks kayak launch signs
Dan from the kayak association presented version 7 of informational signs for kayak launches along the Plover River. Three of eight launches are in the Village of Weston (Highway J, Ross Avenue, and Yellow Banks). Signs will feature maps, QR codes for river gauge information, and location-specific history. Marathon County Parks Foundation is funding the signs through state grants. The committee approved a motion for the village to install the signs, to be forwarded to the village board. Motion carried unanimously.

### Topics for future meetings
The committee will bring back the dog day at aquatic center discussion. Sean raised a request from a community member about placing food allergy awareness signage at playgrounds. Committee agreed to add this for future discussion, though members expressed mixed feelings about setting precedent for other groups making similar requests.

### Staff remarks
Sean reported the aquatic center pool repairs were completed, painting of dugouts is complete, and the pool is being filled (about 3 feet shy of full). Pool opening day is June 6th with training this weekend and rentals starting next Wednesday. Sean also announced the Mock Miller Park master plan process will begin with a kickoff meeting with Rettler next Tuesday, and noted the final bike and pedestrian plan open house is June 11th from 5-7pm.
