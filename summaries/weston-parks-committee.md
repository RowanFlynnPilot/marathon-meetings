## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Parks Committee approved signage for Yellow Banks kayak launches, deferred discussion on allowing dogs at the aquatic center, and received updates on Kennedy Park fundraising and the Oaklair River Trail graffiti issues. The meeting featured public comment criticizing both the dog day proposal and the transparency of Kennedy Park fundraising efforts.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of minutes from April 27, 2026
Minutes were approved with a motion and second. The motion carried with all in favor and no opposition.

### Kennedy Park update
Staff presented a timeline document showing the Kennedy Park master plan process from its approval in November 2023 through the May 9, 2026 ribbon cutting. Director Oster explained that the Friends of Kennedy Park are currently fundraising for lights on the upper field, with approximately $84-85,000 currently at the community foundation toward a $117,000 goal. Scott asked clarifying questions about village capital funds, and Oster confirmed no village capital dollars are currently earmarked for Kennedy Park projects. Staff noted the Friends group plans a public check presentation at their first tournament in June. Committee members discussed that master plans are guiding documents subject to change and that the Friends group deserves grace as volunteers.

### Yellow Banks disc golf course update
Staff presented photos and a PDF showing the location of trees removed from the river and the installation of wood mats over gas pipelines to protect them during logging equipment access. Dan explained the mat installation process and showed that some mats remain for possible future trail projects.

### Dog Day at the aquatic center
The committee deferred this item to the next meeting because Chair Katrina was absent due to an unforeseen issue. The chair noted that since Katrina had requested this agenda item, she should have the opportunity to speak on it. The resident who spoke against the dog day proposal during public comment said he may return next month.

### Graffiti on the Oaklair River Trail
Staff explained challenges removing graffiti from concrete and memorial benches on the trail, noting the pressure washer cannot access the area due to steep ditches and narrow bridges. Discussion included possible anti-vandalism coatings and organizing volunteer work days for trail maintenance. Committee member stated she would participate and suggested community volunteers might help. No formal action was taken; staff will investigate options.

### Yellow Banks kayak launch signs
Dan presented version seven of signage for kayak launches along the river, explaining that signs would be installed at Highway J, Ross Avenue, and Yellow Banks in the Village of Weston, plus locations in the City of Schofield. The signs are funded through Marathon County Parks Foundation grants. Dan noted the Highway J launch may be closed all season due to bridge replacement. Motion to approve the signs and forward to the village board was made and seconded. Motion carried unanimously.

### Food allergy awareness signage
Staff mentioned receiving an email requesting participation in placing food allergy awareness signs at playgrounds. The committee agreed to bring this back for future discussion to allow other members and the public to weigh in.

## Public Comment
Two public comments were offered. Andy from 6504 [street inaudible] spoke against allowing dogs at the aquatic center, stating animals should not be allowed in a public pool and belong in rivers and lakes. Jim Pensan (appearing online as 'Vern M') from 5002 [street] Weston criticized the 4 p.m. meeting time as discouraging public participation, and questioned the Kennedy Park funding report, calling the $8,700 figure for equipment 'too coincidental' and demanding to know where grant money and glow event proceeds are. He characterized the Friends of Kennedy Park financial reporting as 'convoluted and misleading.'

- Staff to bring food allergy awareness signage proposal back for future discussion
