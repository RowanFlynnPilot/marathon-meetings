## Meeting Overview
The Parks and Recreation Committee received updates on Kennedy Park fundraising and construction timelines, deferred discussion on dogs at the aquatic center to next month, and approved signage for the Eau Claire River kayak launches. Public comment included opposition to allowing dogs at the pool and criticism of Kennedy Park financial reporting transparency.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of minutes from April 27, 2026
Minutes were approved with a motion and second. No discussion occurred. Motion carried unanimously with all present voting in favor.

### Kennedy Park update
Director Sean Oster Bring presented a timeline of the Kennedy Park master plan process from its approval in November 2023 through the May 9th ribbon cutting. Committee member Scott asked about village capital dollars, and Oster Bring clarified that infrastructure funds were put on hold pending the master plan project. The Friends of Kennedy Park currently have approximately $84-85,000 at the community foundation toward the $117,000 needed for field lights, and plan to make a public funding announcement at their June tournament. Chair emphasized that Friends of Kennedy Park deserve grace as volunteers and encouraged community members to get involved rather than criticize.

### Yellow Banks disc golf course update
Staff presented photos showing the location of trees removed from the river and the installation of wood mats over gas pipelines to protect them during equipment access. Dan explained the mats were placed across two pipeline locations and the village retained extra mats that may be used on future trail projects.

### Dog Day at the aquatic center
Discussion was deferred to next month because Committee Chair Katrina was absent due to an unforeseen issue. The chair noted that Katrina had requested this item and should have the opportunity to speak on it. The resident who spoke during public comment against dogs at the pool was informed his objection was noted and he was welcome to return next month.

### Graffiti on Eau Claire River Trail
Staff explained challenges removing graffiti from concrete structures due to limited equipment access on the trail. Memorial benches weigh 2,000 pounds each and cannot easily be removed for cleaning. Committee discussed applying anti-vandalism coatings or sealers to make cleaning easier, and the possibility of volunteer work days similar to how original installations were done with director team building days. No formal action taken; staff will explore cleaning options.

### Yellow Banks kayak launch signs
Dan from Marathon County Parks presented version 7 of proposed signage for Eau Claire River kayak launches. Signs will be installed at Highway J, Ross Avenue, and Yellow Banks landings within the Village of Weston, plus locations in City of Schofield. Signs include maps, QR codes for river gauge information, and location-specific history sections. The Highway J launch may be closed for the season due to bridge replacement. Motion to approve village installation of the signs passed unanimously.

### Topics for future meetings
Staff requested committee interest in discussing food allergy awareness signage at playgrounds, noting an organization offered to provide signs free of charge. Committee agreed to bring the topic back for future discussion and education. Staff also announced the Mock Miller master plan process will begin with a kickoff meeting with Rettler, and the final bike and pedestrian plan open house is June 11th from 5-7pm.
