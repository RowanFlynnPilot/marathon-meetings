## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Parks Committee approved the installation of new kayak launch signs along the Eau Claire River and deferred discussion on dogs at the aquatic center until next month. The meeting featured extensive discussion about Kennedy Park fundraising progress and timeline, with public commenters expressing concerns about meeting times and financial transparency.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of minutes
Minutes from April 27, 2026 meeting were approved with a motion, second, and unanimous voice vote with no discussion.

### Kennedy Park update
Sean Osterbrink presented a timeline document tracking Kennedy Park's development from the comprehensive outdoor recreation plan approval in November 2023 through the recent ribbon cutting on May 9th. Committee member Scott asked clarifying questions about funding structure, confirming that village capital dollars work in conjunction with Friends of Kennedy Park fundraising. Osterbrink noted Friends of Kennedy Park currently has approximately $84-85,000 at the community foundation toward a $117,000 goal for field lights, and plans a public check presentation at their June tournament. The chair emphasized that Friends of Kennedy Park is a volunteer organization deserving grace and that master plans are guides subject to change.

### Yellow Banks disc golf course update
Staff presented photos showing fallen trees in the river that need removal and the installation of wood mats over gas pipelines to protect them during logging operations. The mats were placed with TC Energy oversight. Dan explained the trees were removed due to high water levels that would make the river impassable as water drops.

### Dog Day at the aquatic center
The committee deferred this item to next month because Committee Chair Katrina, who requested the agenda item, was absent due to an unforeseen issue. A resident named Andy who attended specifically to oppose the idea (stating 'There should not be animals allowed into a public pool. Period.') had his objection noted and was invited to return next month.

### Graffiti on Eau Claire River Trail
Discussion focused on challenges removing graffiti from concrete benches and garbage cans along the trail. Osterbrink explained their sandblaster pressure washer cannot access the trail due to bridge width limitations and soft ground conditions. Committee members suggested anti-vandalism coatings, seal coating, or organizing volunteer work days. No formal action was taken; staff will explore cleaning options.

### Yellow Banks kayak launch signs
Dan Rau from Marathon County (2625 Northwestern Avenue) presented version 7 of proposed kayak launch signs for installation at three Weston locations (Highway J, Ross Avenue, and Yellow Banks). Signs include maps, river gauge QR codes, and location-specific history panels. Funding comes from Marathon County Parks Foundation and state grants at no cost to the village. Committee approved installation of the signs unanimously by voice vote to be forwarded to the village board.

### Future meetings and topics
Staff raised potential discussion of food allergy awareness signage at playgrounds after receiving a request from a community member. Committee agreed to add this as a future agenda item along with the deferred dog day discussion. Osterbrink announced the Mock Miller master plan process will begin with a kickoff meeting with Rettler next Tuesday.

## Public Comment
Two public comments were received. In person, Andy (6504 [address inaudible]) spoke against allowing dogs at the aquatic center, stating animals don't belong in public pools. Online, Jim Pensan (5002 [street] Weston) criticized the 4 p.m. meeting time as discouraging public participation and questioned Kennedy Park financial transparency, specifically the $8,700 figure for baseball equipment and asking about the location of the $10,000 grant funds and glow event proceeds.
