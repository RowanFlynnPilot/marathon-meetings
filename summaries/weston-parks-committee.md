## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Parks Committee approved signage for the Plover River Water Trail kayak launches and deferred discussion on allowing dogs at the aquatic center until next month. The meeting featured contentious public comment questioning the transparency of Friends of Kennedy Park fundraising, which staff and committee members defended as a long-term volunteer effort.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of minutes
Minutes from April 27, 2026 meeting were approved unanimously with a motion and second. No discussion occurred.

### Kennedy Park update
Director Sean Oster Bring presented a timeline of the Kennedy Park project from its inception through the May 9th ribbon cutting. Committee member Scott asked clarifying questions about village capital dollars and fundraising timelines. Staff explained the Friends of Kennedy Park are targeting $117,000 for field lights and have approximately $84-85,000 at the community foundation. The acting chair defended the Friends group, stating they are volunteers who shouldn't be accused of withholding information and plan to announce fundraising totals at their June tournament.

### Yellow Banks disc golf course update
Staff presented photos and maps showing tree removal locations along the river and wood mat installation over gas pipelines. Dan explained the mats were used to protect TC Energy pipelines during equipment access. The committee reviewed images of the construction process with no action required.

### Dog Day at the aquatic center
The item was deferred to the next meeting because Committee Chair Katrina was absent due to an unforeseen issue. The acting chair noted her objection from a public commenter (Andy) would be recorded. No formal vote was taken to table.

### Graffiti on Oaklair River Trail
Staff explained challenges removing graffiti from concrete and memorial benches, noting equipment access is limited and pressure washers cannot reach the trail. Committee member suggested anti-vandalism coatings or seal coats. Staff mentioned volunteer work days as a possibility, referencing how directors previously carried benches to the trail. No formal action was taken; staff will investigate cleaning options.

### Yellow Banks kayak launch signs
Dan from Marathon County presented version 7 of signage for the Plover River Water Trail. Signs will be installed at launches 3, 4, and 5 in Village of Weston (Highway J, Ross Avenue, and Yellow Banks). The Highway J launch may be closed this season due to bridge replacement. The committee approved a motion to install the signs and forward to the village board for approval. Motion passed unanimously.

### Future meeting topics
Staff requested committee interest in food allergy awareness signage at playgrounds after receiving an email inquiry. Committee agreed to bring it back for future discussion. The Mock Miller master plan process will begin with a kickoff meeting with Rettler, and June 11th is the last open house for the bike and pedestrian plan.

## Public Comment
Two members of the public commented. Andy (6504 [street name inaudible]) spoke against allowing dogs at the public pool, stating animals don't belong there and should use rivers and lakes instead. Jim Pensan (5002 [street name] Weston), speaking remotely under the name 'Vern M', criticized the 4 p.m. meeting time as discouraging public participation and questioned the Kennedy Park funding report, calling the $8,700 figure 'too coincidental' and demanding transparency on funds raised from the glow event and lighting fundraiser. He accused the Friends of Kennedy Park of being 'frauds' for not sharing detailed financials.

## Action Items
- Kayak launch signs approved for installation at Highway J, Ross Avenue, and Yellow Banks launches; forwarded to village board for approval
- Dog Day at aquatic center discussion deferred to June meeting when Chair Katrina can attend
- Staff to investigate cleaning solutions and anti-vandalism coatings for Oaklair River Trail benches and garbage cans
- Food allergy awareness playground signage to be brought back for future committee discussion
- Mock Miller master plan kickoff meeting scheduled with Rettler for the following Tuesday
- Next meeting scheduled for June 22, 2026
