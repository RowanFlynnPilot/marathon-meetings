## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Parks Committee approved installation of new kayak launch signs for the Plover River, deferred discussion on allowing dogs at the aquatic center until next month when the committee chair can be present, and received updates on Kennedy Park fundraising and the Yellow Banks disc golf course logging project. The meeting also included public comment expressing opposition to dogs at the pool and criticism of Kennedy Park financial transparency.

## Key Discussions
### Kennedy Park Update
Director Sean Osterbrink presented a timeline of the Kennedy Park master plan project from its 2023 approval to the May 9th ribbon cutting. Committee member Scott asked about timelines for fundraising, and Osterbrink clarified that the project could take up to 10 years with no firm deadline. The Friends of Kennedy Park are currently fundraising for $117,000 for field lights and have approximately $84-85,000 at the community foundation. Osterbrink noted the Friends group plans a public check presentation at their June tournament. The acting chair defended the volunteer group, stating they deserve 'a little bit of grace' and aren't withholding information.

### Yellow Banks Disc Golf Course Update
Staff presented photos and a PDF showing the location of fallen trees removed from the river and the installation of wood mats to protect gas pipelines during the logging operation. Dan explained the mats were installed over two pipeline crossings to allow heavy equipment access. ATC (now TC Energy) representatives were on site to monitor the work. The village now has extra mats that may be used on another trail project.

### Dog Day at the Aquatic Center
The committee deferred this item to the June meeting because Committee Chair Katrina, who requested the item, was absent due to an unforeseen issue. One public commenter, Andy from 6504 [street name inaudible], stated strong opposition: 'There should not be animals allowed into a public pool. Period... We have rivers. We have lakes. That's where they belong.' The acting chair noted Andy's objection would be recorded.

### Graffiti on the Oaklair River Trail
Staff discussed challenges removing graffiti from concrete and memorial benches along the trail, noting their best tool (a sand blaster/pressure washer) cannot access the trail due to bridge weight limits and terrain. Options discussed included anti-vandalism coatings, sealer applications, and potentially organizing volunteer workdays. Committee member Laro suggested community members might volunteer to help with maintenance. No formal action was taken; staff will research options.

### Yellow Banks Kayak Launch Signs
Dan Wierzba from Marathon County (2625 Northwestern Avenue) presented version 7 of signs for Plover River kayak launches. Three of eight launches are in Weston (Highway J, Ross Avenue, and Yellow Banks). Signs will include maps, QR codes linking to river gauge data, and location-specific history sections. The Highway J launch may be closed all season due to bridge replacement. The committee approved installation of the signs unanimously to be forwarded to the Village Board.

### Future Meeting Topics
Staff asked about committee interest in food allergy awareness signage for playgrounds, noting an individual offered to provide signs encouraging keeping food away from play equipment. The committee agreed to bring this back for future discussion. Staff also announced the Mock Miller Park master plan process will begin with a kickoff meeting with Rettler, and the final bike and pedestrian plan open house is June 11th from 5-7pm.

## Public Comment
Two public comments were received. Andy (6504 [street inaudible]) spoke in person opposing dogs at the aquatic center, stating animals don't belong in public pools. Jim Pensan (5002 [street] Weston), speaking online under the name 'Vern M', criticized the 4 p.m. meeting time as discouraging public participation and questioned Kennedy Park financial reporting, calling the $8,700 figure 'too coincidental' and asking about the $10,000 grant and Glow event funds, suggesting the Friends of Kennedy Park are being secretive about finances.
