## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Parks Committee deferred discussion on allowing dogs at the aquatic center due to the chair's absence, approved installation of new kayak launch signs along the Plover River, and received updates on Kennedy Park development and trail maintenance. Public commenters expressed concerns about the dog day event and questioned transparency of Kennedy Park fundraising finances.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of minutes
Minutes from the April 27, 2026 meeting were approved. A motion was made and seconded, with no discussion. The motion carried unanimously by voice vote.

### Kennedy Park update
Staff presented a timeline of the Kennedy Park master plan development as requested by the board. Director Sean Osterbring explained the village is responsible for approximately $7 million of the $14.3 million project, primarily infrastructure items like bathrooms and shelter. Scott asked clarifying questions about funding timelines, learning there is no fixed deadline but a 10-year evaluation period. The Friends of Kennedy Park have approximately $84-85,000 at the community foundation toward a $117,000 goal for field lights, and plan to make a public announcement at their June tournament. Luis emphasized the Friends group deserves grace as volunteers and encouraged community members to get involved.

### Yellow Banks disc golf course update
Staff provided photos and maps showing tree removal locations along the river and installation of wooden construction mats over gas pipelines to protect them during equipment access. Dan explained the mats were provided at no cost and the village has extras for potential trail projects. The work addressed fallen trees blocking river passage.

### Dog Day at the aquatic center
The committee deferred this item to the June meeting because Katrina, who requested the agenda item, was absent due to an unforeseen issue. A member of the public named Andy (6504 [address inaudible]) spoke against allowing dogs in the pool, stating 'There should not be animals allowed into a public pool. Period.' Andy indicated she may return next month to speak again.

### Graffiti on the Oaklair River Trail
Luis raised concerns about graffiti on trail benches and amenities. Osterbring explained challenges removing graffiti from concrete surfaces, noting the pressure washer cannot access the trail due to bridge width limitations and soft ground conditions. Staff discussed potential solutions including anti-vandalism coatings and luster seal. Committee members suggested volunteer work days, noting community members likely would help. No formal action was taken; staff will investigate cleaning options.

### Yellow Banks kayak launch signs
Dan from Marathon County presented version 7 of informational signs for kayak launches along the Plover River. Three signs will be installed in Village of Weston locations (Highway J, Ross Avenue, and Yellow Banks). Signs include maps, QR codes for river gauge information, and location-specific history. The Highway J launch may be closed this season due to bridge replacement. The committee approved the signs unanimously by voice vote and will forward to the village board for approval.

### Food allergy awareness signage
Osterbring mentioned receiving a request to install food allergy awareness signs at playgrounds, noting one in 13 people have food allergies. The committee agreed to add this as a future agenda item for further discussion, acknowledging mixed feelings but wanting to be educated on the topic.

## Public Comment
Two public comments were offered. Andy (6504 [street inaudible]) spoke in person against the dog day at aquatic center proposal, stating dogs do not belong in public pools. Jim Pensan (5002 [street] Weston), speaking online under the name 'Vern M,' criticized the 4 p.m. meeting time as discouraging public participation and questioned the Kennedy Park funding transparency, calling the $8,700 equipment expenditure 'too coincidental' and asking where grant money and glow event proceeds are being tracked. He questioned whether the Friends of Kennedy Park are 'frauds' for not sharing detailed financials.
