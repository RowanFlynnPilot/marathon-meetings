## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Parks Committee deferred discussion on allowing dogs at the aquatic center due to the chair's absence, approved installation of new kayak launch signs along the Plover River, and received updates on Kennedy Park fundraising and trail maintenance. Public commenters expressed opposition to dogs at the pool and criticized the 4 p.m. meeting time and Kennedy Park financial transparency.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of minutes
Minutes from the April 27, 2026 meeting were approved with a motion and second. The motion passed unanimously with no discussion.

### Kennedy Park update
Director Sean Oster provided a timeline of the Kennedy Park project from the 2023 comprehensive plan approval through the May 9th ribbon cutting. Committee member Scott asked about village capital dollars, and Oster clarified no village funds are currently earmarked as they were pulled pending the master plan. The Friends of Kennedy Park currently have approximately $84-85,000 at the community foundation toward a $117,000 goal for field lights. Oster noted a public check presentation is planned at the June tournament. A committee member emphasized that Friends of Kennedy Park is a volunteer organization that deserves grace and that the 10-year timeline for the master plan allows for changes.

### Yellow Banks disc golf course update
Dan presented photos of fallen trees that were removed from the river and showed images of wood mat installation over gas pipelines to protect them during equipment access. The mats were provided by TC Energy (formerly ANR pipeline/ATC) and some extra mats may be used on other trail projects.

### Dog Day at the aquatic center
Discussion was deferred to the next meeting because Committee Chair Katrina was absent due to an unforeseen issue. A public commenter named Andy from 6504 [street not fully captured] expressed opposition, stating animals should not be allowed in public pools and belong in rivers and lakes instead.

### Graffiti on the Oaklair River Trail
Staff explained challenges removing graffiti from concrete and benches along the trail due to limited equipment access. The pressure washer on a tandem axle trailer cannot fit on the narrow bridges. Committee discussed anti-vandalism coatings and sealer options. Lisa suggested volunteer work days, noting community members love the trail. Staff will investigate cleaning options and potentially address benches one at a time during off-season.

### Yellow Banks kayak launch signs
Dan presented version seven of proposed signage for kayak launches along the Plover River. Signs will be installed at Highway J, Ross Avenue, and Yellow Banks in Weston, plus locations in Schofield. Funding comes from Marathon County Parks Foundation and state grants at no cost to the village. Dan noted the Highway J launch may be closed all season due to bridge replacement. Motion to approve sign installation passed unanimously.

### Future meeting topics
Staff asked about interest in food allergy awareness signage at playgrounds after receiving an email request. Signs would be provided free and encourage keeping food at picnic tables rather than on play equipment. Committee agreed to bring this back for further discussion. Staff also announced the Mock Miller Park master plan process is beginning with Rettler, with a kickoff meeting scheduled for next Tuesday.

## Public Comment
Two public comments were offered. Andy from 6504 [street] spoke in person opposing dogs at the aquatic center, stating animals don't belong in public pools. Jim Pensan (appearing as 'Vern M' online) from 5002 Street Weston criticized the 4 p.m. meeting time as discouraging public participation and questioned Kennedy Park financial reporting, calling the $8,700 figure 'too coincidental' and asking about a $10,000 grant and glow event funds. He suggested the Friends of Kennedy Park may be 'frauds' for not sharing detailed finances.
