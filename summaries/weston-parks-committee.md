## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Parks Committee deferred discussion on allowing dogs at the aquatic center to next month, approved kayak launch signage for installation at village parks, and received updates on Kennedy Park fundraising efforts and the Yellow Banks disc golf course tree removal project. Public commenters expressed opposition to dogs at the pool and concerns about Kennedy Park financial transparency.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of minutes
Minutes from the April 27, 2026 meeting were approved unanimously with a motion and second. No discussion was held on the minutes.

### Kennedy Park update
Director Sean Oster Bring presented a timeline document showing the Kennedy Park project history from the comprehensive plan approval in November 2023 through the May 9th ribbon cutting. Committee member Scott asked clarifying questions about village capital funds and the fundraising timeline. Oster Bring explained that Friends of Kennedy Park are fundraising for lights on the upper field, currently at approximately $84-85,000 toward a $117,000 goal, and plan to announce their total at an upcoming June tournament. The acting chair emphasized that the Friends group deserves grace as volunteers and noted master plans are meant to guide future development, not dictate exact outcomes.

### Yellow Banks disc golf course update
Staff presented photos and maps showing tree removal locations along the river and mat installation over gas pipelines to protect them during equipment operations. Dan explained that wood mats were used to cross the pipeline areas, with some extra mats available for possible future trail use. The mats now belong to the village.

### Dog day at the aquatic center
The committee deferred this item to the June meeting because Chair Katrina, who had requested the item, was absent due to an unforeseen issue. One public commenter, Andy (6504 [street inaudible]), had spoken against allowing dogs at the pool during public comment, stating 'There should not be animals allowed into a public pool. Period.' His objection was noted for the record.

### Graffiti on Oaklair River Trail
The acting chair raised concerns about graffiti on benches and garbage cans along the trail. Staff explained challenges with removal, noting the pressure washer cannot access the trail and memorial benches weigh approximately 2,000 pounds each. Options discussed included anti-vandalism coatings, seal coating, and organizing volunteer work days. Committee member suggested community volunteers might help, noting people love the trail. No formal action was taken; staff will explore cleaning options.

### Yellow Banks kayak launch signs
Dan Korvino from Marathon County Parks (2625 Northwestern Avenue) presented version 7 of proposed kayak launch signs for installation at Yellow Banks, Ross Avenue, and Highway J landings in Weston. The signs include maps, history, QR codes for river gauge information, and donation links to Marathon County Parks Foundation. Motion to approve installation of the signs passed unanimously. Signs will go to the village board for final approval.

### Topics for future meetings
Staff requested discussion on food allergy awareness signage for playgrounds after receiving an email request from a community member. The program would provide free signs encouraging keeping food away from play structures. Committee agreed to add this for future discussion along with the deferred dog day item.

## Public Comment
Two members of the public commented. Andy (6504 [street inaudible]) spoke in person opposing dogs at the aquatic center, stating it's 'ridiculous' and dogs belong in rivers and lakes, not public pools. Jim Pensan (5002 [street] Weston), speaking online under the name 'Vern M', criticized the 4 p.m. meeting time as discouraging public participation and questioned the Kennedy Park funding report, calling the $8,700 expense figure 'too coincidental' and demanding to know where the $10,000 grant money and glow event fundraising proceeds are, suggesting the numbers are 'convoluted and misleading.'
