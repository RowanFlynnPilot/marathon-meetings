## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Parks Committee approved kayak launch signs for installation along the Eau Claire River, deferred discussion on allowing dogs at the aquatic center until next month, and received updates on Kennedy Park fundraising and Yellow Banks disc golf course maintenance. Public commenters expressed opposition to dogs at the pool and questioned Kennedy Park financial transparency.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of minutes from April 27th, 2026
A motion was made and seconded to approve the previous meeting minutes. The motion passed unanimously with no discussion.

### Kennedy Park update
Director Sean Oster provided a timeline document showing Kennedy Park's master plan progress from 2023 to present, including the May 9th ribbon cutting for youth baseball fields. Staff explained the Friends of Kennedy Park are fundraising for field lights, currently at approximately $85,000 toward a $117,000 goal. Scott asked clarifying questions about village capital funds, with staff confirming no village dollars are currently earmarked pending fundraising progress. A trustee defended the Friends group as volunteers who shouldn't be pressured to release financials before their planned public announcement at their June tournament.

### Yellow Banks disc golf course update
Dan provided photos and maps showing tree removal locations along the river and wood mat installation over gas pipelines. The mats were placed to protect TC Energy pipelines during equipment access. Staff showed photos of the large trees that had fallen into the river, noting one would make passage impassible when water levels drop. The village retained some extra mats for future trail projects.

### Dog Day at the aquatic center
The committee deferred this item to next month because Committee Chair Katrina was absent due to an unforeseen issue. The chair noted her objection from the earlier public commenter would be noted but wanted Katrina present to speak on the item she had requested.

### Graffiti on Eau Claire River Trail
Staff explained challenges removing graffiti from concrete and memorial benches, noting limited equipment access to the trail. The pressure washer on a tandem axle trailer cannot fit on the 8,000-pound bridges. Committee members discussed potential solutions including anti-vandalism coatings, sealers, and volunteer workdays. No formal action was taken, but staff will explore cleaning options and potentially organizing volunteer assistance.

### Yellow Banks kayak launch signs
Dan from Marathon County presented version seven of proposed kayak launch signs for three Weston locations (Highway J, Ross Avenue, and Yellow Banks). Signs will include river maps, QR codes linking to river gauge data, and location-specific history panels. The signs are funded through Marathon County Parks Foundation grants with no village funding required. A motion to approve installation was made, seconded, and passed unanimously.

### Topics for future meetings
Staff requested committee interest in discussing food allergy awareness signage at playgrounds after receiving an email from an advocacy group. The committee agreed to add this topic for future discussion. Staff also announced the Mock Miller master plan process will begin with a kickoff meeting with Rettler the following Tuesday.
