# Wausau Finance Committee Meeting

## Meeting Overview
The Wausau Finance Committee met on March 24, 2026 and took action on multiple items including denying a tax recovery claim related to ongoing Greenwood Hills litigation, approving airport ground lease transfers, and postponing decisions on a national opioid settlement and lead service line replacement funding. The committee also approved budget carryover funds and transfers to support struggling funds while reviewing positive 2025 general fund results showing a $540,000 surplus.

## Key Discussions
### Green Acres at Greenwood Hills LLC tax recovery claim
This claim relates to ongoing litigation with Greenwood Hills. Alder Watson moved to approve the claim and Alder Griner seconded. The chair explained that a 'no' vote would deny the claim. The motion failed with opposition voting 'no', effectively denying the tax recovery claim.

### Airport ground lease transfers at 939 Woods Place
Three related items transferred a hangar from Win O. Jones to Owen Jones. The committee approved consent to transfer title (Watson motion, Griner second), terminated the lease with Win O. Jones (Tierney motion, Watson second), and approved a new lease with Owen Jones (Watson motion, Tierney second). All three passed unanimously. A separate ground lease with Cole Lundberg was also approved unanimously (Griner motion, Watson second).

### National opioid settlement agreement participation
The committee discussed whether to join a class action settlement involving six defendants with a potential $97 million payout. Alder Maloney questioned where this came from, and Assistant Attorney Vincent explained the city received a letter identifying them as potential class members. Alder Tierney and others expressed discomfort proceeding without more information. Alder Griner moved to postpone to the next meeting, seconded by Tierney. Motion passed unanimously. The deadline to participate is May 4th.

### Budget amendment for lead service line replacement
Eric from Public Works explained that the DNR provided less favorable funding terms than originally discussed, creating a $709,000 shortfall split between homeowner side ($283,868) and water utility side ($425,803). Finance Director Marian outlined options including using general fund surplus, PFAS settlement money, or borrowing. Alder Griner suggested using surplus for the homeowner portion and PFAS settlement for the utility portion. The committee agreed PFAS settlement spending requires broader discussion. Alder Watson moved to postpone, Griner seconded, passed unanimously.

### Budget amendment for 2025-2026 carryover funds
The carryover includes significant items like 10 transit buses funded by VW mitigation grants and various airport projects. Finance Director noted some projects like city hall chimney liner, public safety roof, and DPW fence replacement haven't started yet. Alder Watson moved to approve, Griner seconded. Passed unanimously.

### Review of 2025 motorpool fund financial results
Finance Director reported the motorpool fund continues to struggle with cash flow. After GMT transfer of $191,000, there's a $150,000 net profit, but a $177,000 cash flow shortfall remains after pending vehicle purchases. Solomon from MotorPool explained that dump trucks ordered in 2023 are nearly ready, and recent ambulance delays were due to quality issues. Staff indicated ARPA savings may cover the shortfall. This was informational only - no action required.

### Review of 2025 general fund financial results
The general fund showed a $1.2 million surplus driven by strong building permits, GMT money, and investment income. After motorpool charges, the surplus is $125,000. After proposed transfers to recycling, airport, and parking funds, $540,000 remains available. Public works, fire, and police were over budget primarily due to motorpool charges that couldn't be fully budgeted due to levy limits. CCITC was over by $194,000 due to communication issues and an Office 365 upgrade. Alder Tierney moved to approve the transfers, Watson seconded. Passed unanimously.

### 2026 general obligation promissory note for capital improvements
Finance Director presented the borrowing calendar showing approximately $10 million in new borrowing against $12 million in retiring debt, reducing debt utilization slightly. Projects include street improvements (10-year), motorpool (5-year), and various TID projects. Phil Cawson from Ehlers will present parameters at the next meeting. Alder Watson moved to approve the calendar, chair seconded. Passed unanimously. Most 2026 projects have already been bid out.

### Consideration of purchasing properties for DPW expansion
This item was scheduled for closed session discussion regarding properties at 108, 112, 112½ Adolf Street and 233 Myron Street. Due to time constraints before the 6:30 council meeting and no urgency, Alder Watson moved to postpone to the next meeting, Tierney seconded. Passed unanimously. Closed session was not held.

## Public Comment
No public comment was offered.
