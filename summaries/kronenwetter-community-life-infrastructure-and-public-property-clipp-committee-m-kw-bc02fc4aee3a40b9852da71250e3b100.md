## Meeting Overview
The Kronenwetter CLIPP Committee voted 5-0 to support a Quit Claim Deed transferring village-owned property to Marathon County for the County Highway Shop Facility project, clearing the way for a Village Board recommendation on the significant land transfer. The committee also voted 5-0 to support the Wausau Metropolitan Planning Organization's draft model e-bike ordinance, moving toward regional uniformity on e-bike regulations. Road rehabilitation bids for the Flanner/Jamroz project were tabled after Public Works Director Greg Ulman reported bids came in far higher than expected.

## Key Discussions
### Selection of a Chairperson and Vice-Chairperson
Trustee Aaron Myszka was nominated for chairperson by Patty Tikalsky, seconded by President Dan Joling, and elected by acclamation. Garrett Lysne was nominated for vice-chairperson by President Joling, seconded by Tikalsky, and approved by voice vote 4-0-1, with Lysne abstaining.

### Approval of April 6, 2026 CLIPP Committee Meeting Minutes
Motion by Joling, seconded by Mijal, to approve the April 6, 2026 meeting minutes. Motion carried by voice vote 5-0.

### Police Chief Report
Police Chief Terry McHugh presented his report and answered a committee question regarding the recent hiring of new police officers. No action was taken.

### Fire Chief Report
Fire Chief Theresa O'Brien reported that April 2026 was the busiest month she had experienced, with 24 fire calls and 55 medical calls. She also noted that live burn training was a success for her department. No action was taken.

### Public Works Director Report
Public Works Director Greg Ulman updated the committee on the effects of recent flooding and tornado events. He reported meeting with Lee Recreation to develop a design proposal for Buska Park and answered committee questions on the cost of Country Pumpers, sewer vacuum truck capability, and park donations. Finance Director John Jacobs and Administrator James Davel also answered questions regarding the budget and the Bulk Item Drop-off event. No action was taken.

### Community Development, Planning and Zoning Director Report
Administrator Davel presented the report in the absence of a director, informing the committee that Peter Wegner is no longer with the Village and that the Village is actively hiring a new Community Development Director and planning technician. No action was taken.

### Complaint Log
Administrator Davel presented the complaint log report, noting that complaint numbers have increased. No action was taken.

### Wausau Metropolitan Planning Organization Draft Model E-Bike Ordinance
Motion by Joling, seconded by Mijal, to support the language of the Wausau Metropolitan Planning Organization Draft Model E-Bike Ordinance. Administrator Davel summarized resident comments regarding e-bikes in the village, and Police Chief McHugh stated he had no concerns about the ordinance language. The committee agreed it represented an agreeable and regionally uniform set of standards. Motion carried by voice vote 5-0.

### Discussion of Possible Truck Route on Kowalski Road
Public Works Director Ulman reviewed the topic and advised the committee that there is currently no need for a designated truck route on village roads. He noted the issue could be revisited once the distribution center is completed. No action was taken.

### Quit Claim Deed - Proposed Marathon County Highway Shop Facility
Motion by Mijal, seconded by Lysne, to recommend the Village Board approve the Quit Claim Deed for Tax Parcel ID Number 145-2707-023-0994. Administrator Davel presented the item and explained the rationale for approval. Marathon County Administrator Lance Leonhard and other county staff attended to discuss the County Highway Garage Facility project, addressing the project timeline, the need for the quit claim deed, and potential benefits for Kronenwetter residents. Leonhard acknowledged the county would make a better effort to attend Village meetings on the subject. The committee voted to carry the motion; no vote count beyond implied 5-0 was recorded.

### Kronenwetter Flanner/Jamroz Rehabilitation Bids
Public Works Director Ulman requested the item be tabled, reporting that bids came in significantly higher than expected and that additional information was needed. The item was tabled until the next meeting. No vote count was recorded beyond the tabling action.

## Public Comment
David Baker of 690 Happy Hollow Road submitted written public comment, which was read into the record by the clerk and attached to the minutes. Baker addressed the Quit Claim Deed item (8L) and the broader planning process for the proposed Marathon County Highway Facility. He urged the committee to make any property transfer contingent on the county agreeing to take jurisdiction of and upgrade Highway Business 51 from Village Way to Kowalski Road to four lanes, arguing that without such improvements the safety and traffic burdens currently affecting county highway workers and Wausau residents would simply be transferred to Kronenwetter residents. Baker also asked that the county consider installing a well on the property to avoid straining the village water system, and noted that newly elected Supervisor/Trustee Coyle serves on the Marathon County Infrastructure Committee. Trustee Patty Tikalsky of 2153 Peach Road offered public comment thanking the Kronenwetter Police Department, Fire Department, and Public Works Department for their response and aid during recent severe weather events.
