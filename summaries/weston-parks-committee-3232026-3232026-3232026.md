## Meeting Overview
The Parks Committee reviewed park impact fees with plan commission input, selected Rettler Corporation for the Mock Mueller Park master plan, and discussed ice rink operations at Kennedy Park. The committee endorsed increasing park impact fees to align with neighboring communities and approved Rettler's $24,700 proposal unanimously.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of minutes from February 23rd, 2026
A motion to accept the minutes was made and seconded. The motion passed unanimously with no discussion.

### Review of parks and recreation impact fee discussion
Jennifer presented information on park impact fees, noting the village currently charges $300 for single family units while neighboring communities charge $600-$650. The 2020 study recommended fees of $761 for single family. Plan commission asked for parks committee input before deciding. Committee members Katrina and Roger expressed support for a moderate increase to align with neighboring communities. Jennifer noted that at current rates, the Granite Ridge subdivision (88 lots) would generate $26,400, while doubling fees would yield $52,800. No formal action was taken; feedback will be provided to plan commission.

### Requests for proposals for Mock Mueller Park master plan
Staff received seven proposals for the park master plan, which were reviewed by four staff members based on firm experience, personnel experience, similar projects, and cost. The two lowest bidders were JSD and Rettler Corporation, both with village experience. Roger made a motion to select Rettler Corporation, seconded by Katrina. The motion passed unanimously.

### Review of Yellow Banks kayak launch expenses
Jessica presented a detailed breakdown of kayak launch expenses and grant funding. The project had unforeseen costs including bad subgrade and an unknown well casing. Lisa Beck praised the RFC during public comment. Grants were secured by Jessica and Dan Higginbotham, with Marathon County Transportation covering full expense for ADA accessibility. Committee members praised staff work and discussed recognizing Dan Higginbotham's contributions. No formal action required; informational only.

### Discussion on ice rink operations at Kennedy Park
Staff presented information on Kennedy Park ice rink operations per committee request. The warming house has been unattended since 2020 due to COVID and subsequent staffing shortages. Everest Youth Hockey remains interested in improvements including a covered structure. Staff noted Marathon Park changes may increase demand for ice facilities. Katrina expressed concern about hockey being forgotten amid baseball focus at Kennedy Park. Committee requested additional information including historical attendance figures from 2018-19 and user feedback for a future meeting.

## Public Comment
Jim Pinsel expressed frustration about not receiving responses to questions submitted at the previous meeting regarding playground equipment installation issues and Kennedy Park fundraising updates. He criticized the ice rink cost analysis as incomplete, arguing true costs including labor hours are $20,000-$30,000 rather than the stated $1,320.98. Lisa Beck thanked Michael for snow management during the recent blizzard and praised Sean and Jessica for the well-written Yellow Banks RFC. A written response to Jim Pinsel's previous email was also submitted and will be included in the minutes.
