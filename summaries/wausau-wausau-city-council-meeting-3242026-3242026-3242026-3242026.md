## Meeting Overview
The Wausau City Council approved a $10 million development agreement for 11 Scott Street (Waterside Place) by a 6-3 vote, which will convert a vacant 100,000 square foot building into 52 mid-priced residential units. The council also recognized DPW crews for their response to a record 30.9-inch snowfall and presented a sustainability award to Kolbe and Kolbe Millwork for their solar energy initiatives.

## Key Discussions
### Development Agreement for 11 Scott Street/Waterside Place
The council debated the joint resolution from economic development and infrastructure committees to approve a development agreement with 11 Scott Street LLC. Developers Raleigh Lray and Mark Craig spoke during public comment, noting the $10+ million project would create 52 mid-priced residential units in a vacant 100,000 square foot building. Alder Rasmusson supported the project, citing the need for downtown residents and mid-priced housing. Alder Neil emphasized the project would generate $55,000 in annual parking revenue and help close TID 8 within 5 years. Alder Larson dissented, arguing against discounting city assets during budget cuts. Alder Tierney questioned the parking agreement provision requiring the city to provide alternative parking within 300 yards if the ramp closes, which Economic Development Director Randy Feifer explained was a reduction from an existing 480-space obligation to 150 paid spaces. The motion passed 6-3.

### DPW Recognition for Record Snowfall Response
Mayor Denny presented a mayoral citation to the Department of Public Works crews for their response to the historic March 14-16, 2026 snowstorm that delivered a record 30.9 inches. Kevin Kester, street supervisor Josh, Dustin, Mitch Harris (storeroom manager who operated a plow), and mechanic Jieven Matah were specifically recognized. Kester noted that four municipal fleet technicians maintained 12-hour shifts for continuous 24-hour breakdown support and would have worked 12 straight days by Friday. Kester praised the crews saying 'you kicked its ass' to applause from the council.

### Sustainability Award - Kolbe and Kolbe Millwork
Christine Daniels from the Sustainability, Energy and Environment Committee presented the 2026 City of Wausau Sustainability Award to Kolbe and Kolbe Millwork. Representatives Mike Thompson and Keith Kaning accepted, describing their solar panel installation of over 2,000 panels on their manufacturing facility that became operational in July 2025, generating enough power for about 120 homes. They also discussed LED lighting projects and recycling initiatives for wood, aluminum, glass, vinyl, cardboard, and other materials.

### Residential Solid Waste Agreement
The council approved the resolution from finance committee for a 7-year residential solid waste and recycling service agreement with Harter's Fox Valley Disposal. Mayor Denny noted there had been confusion about whether the term was 7 or 10 years, confirming it was corrected to the 7-year term that public health and safety originally approved. Motion passed 9-0.

### Settlement Resolution - David Holes vs City of Wausau
Assistant City Attorney Vincent Bonito explained a 2022 bus accident case where Transit Mutual insurance paid the initial claim, then the individual who hit the bus filed a personal injury claim. The city filed a counter claim and third-party complaint against the individual's insurer, who agreed to pay damages for the bus. The resolution releases the city's counter claim. Alder Neil clarified this does not affect the separate personal injury claim. No closed session was needed. Motion passed 8-1.

## Public Comment
Two speakers addressed the council. Raleigh Lray (11 Scott Street project) asked for support for the green sustainable project converting a vacant building to mid-priced apartments. Mark Craig (3246 North 8th Street) emphasized the $10+ million project's challenges, noting the residential component alone costs over $8.3 million for 52 units, and stated 'Without your help, it won't happen.'
