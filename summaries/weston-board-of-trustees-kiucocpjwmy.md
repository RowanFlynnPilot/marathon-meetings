## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Board of Trustees held its regular meeting covering a wide range of topics including farmers market relocation feedback, fire department staffing challenges following a failed April referendum, liquor license renewals with compliance conditions, and road speed limit clarifications. Key decisions included referring the farmers market survey to the Parks Committee with the survey extended through June 30, and conditionally approving two liquor license renewals contingent on payment of outstanding debts.

## Key Discussions
### Public Comments
Three residents spoke. Grace Middle Street raised concerns about SAFER's $1.2 million fund balance, questioning why those funds weren't being used for firefighter staffing instead of seeking grants or a new referendum. Pam Zimmerman of 2550 Newport raised safety and parking concerns near Kennedy Park related to the farmers market, citing utility line risks, blocked sightlines from her driveway, and increased traffic. Jim Pel criticized omission of public safety meeting minutes from the packet, cited survey data showing 39% rate the new farmers market location poor or very poor and 57% prefer the former location, and questioned why the board continues borrowing for non-essential projects like golf fields while the fire department remains understaffed.

### Committee Chair and Department Reports
Trustee Herm reported that Public Works identified 15 million gallons of clear water being discharged into the sewer system monthly at a cost of about $20,000, totaling roughly $240,000 annually, and suggested penalties should be enforced. Fire Chief's representative reported six part-time firefighter positions will be offered, hail storm damage to department vehicles will cost only $2,500 total deductible, and two firefighters were injured during a water rescue that saved two people from a sinking boat on Lake Wausau. The board recognized the fire chief for receiving the state fire chief association award of the year. Public Works Director Michael reported a speed study on West Nav Avenue returned an 85th percentile speed of 48.8 mph, confirming the road can remain posted at 45 mph from Von Kennel to County J, with signs expected to be corrected by mid-week following road repair completion.

### Consent Agenda Items 34-40
Consent items 34 through 39 were approved unanimously, covering vouchers, cigarette and tobacco license renewals, lodging license renewals, liquor license renewals, mobile home park licenses, and weight measures licenses. Item 40, appointment of a new agent for Reliance Fuel LLC doing business as Fuel on 60, was taken separately due to a name typo in the RFC; the board corrected it and approved the appointment of Sarah Gravine as agent, passed unanimously.

### Resolutions 2026-016, 2026-017, and 2026-018
Resolution 2026-016 approving the 2025 CMAR for the Western Sewer Utility passed unanimously. Resolution 2026-017 authorizing special assessments for the Plato Avenue reconstruction from Pine Park Street to Alderson Street passed unanimously. Resolution 2026-018 for the Alderson Street and Gellet Avenue intersection project prompted questions about why the assessment rate was significantly higher ($8.61 per square foot) compared to Plato Avenue ($5.40 per square foot); Public Works explained the difference was due to economies of scale, with only two properties assessed on the Alderson project versus 40-plus on Plato. The resolution passed unanimously.

### Farmers Market Feedback Survey and Parking Concerns
The board discussed survey results showing 39% of 322 respondents rate the Kennedy Park location poor or very poor, and 57% prefer the former location, with 75% citing parking as a problem. Trustees and staff debated parking management options including rope barriers, additional no-parking signage on East Newport, Joseph, and east side of Alderson Street, and extra police patrols on Saturday mornings. Parks staff noted chaotic parking during ball games is a separate and ongoing safety concern. The board agreed the Village Hall site is not a viable alternative due to insufficient square footage for vendors and parking. After withdrawing an initial motion to discuss, the board voted unanimously to refer the farmers market issue to the Parks Committee and extend the survey through June 30, with a possible reopening of the survey in August or September.

### Liquor License Renewal - Hutches Kelly Club LLC
The board moved to conditionally approve the renewal of Hutches Kelly Club LLC's Class B fermented malt beverage and intoxicating liquor license for 2026-2027, contingent on payment of $662.76 in past-due utility amounts. Clerk confirmed the amount had already been paid by the time of the meeting, making the condition effectively satisfied. Motion passed unanimously.

### Liquor License Renewal - RJT and Sons LLC
The board considered the renewal of RJT and Sons LLC's Class B licenses, which was complicated by $660 in past-due rehabilitation proof, a supplier bill of $91,825 past due for over 30 days, a history of fire code violations requiring repeated follow-up, and concerns about the owner's background. Trustee Beck raised concerns about approving a license for a business unable to pay its bills or license fee. Clerk noted that denying a license outright requires a formal process. The board amended the motion to include payment of the supplier bill as a condition; the amendment passed with one nay vote. The amended motion then passed 5-1, with the license conditioned on payment of past-due amounts, supplier bill, and proof of rehabilitation, all due before July 1.

### SAFER Discussion Following April Referendum Failure
Administrator Gabbert outlined that a new referendum resolution would need to be filed with the county clerk by August 25 to make the November ballot. Finance Director Jessica explained the SAFER fund balance of approximately $1.3 million cannot be used for operations and is earmarked for capital projects; the board had discussed reducing it incrementally to $500,000-$600,000. With the TIF district closing, an estimated additional $503,000 in levy capacity may become available, potentially funding three additional firefighters. Trustees expressed skepticism about a second referendum at $600,000 given the April failure, with some suggesting a smaller, more realistic dollar amount or funding three positions through TIF proceeds and pursuing six later. Chief's deputy noted Rib Mountain, which shares 32% of SAFER costs, appears less enthusiastic about hiring six after the referendum failure. The board had extensive discussion but took no formal action, with budget discussions expected to continue.

### Community Engagement Efforts
Trustee Beck proposed community engagement initiatives modeled on Rib Mountain's well-received public event, including an annual open house at Village Hall and early morning coffee hours to accommodate residents on non-traditional schedules. The board received the idea positively and discussion began before the transcript ended, with Maloney noting the village had done similar events about 10-12 years ago.

## Public Comment

Grace Middle Street raised questions about SAFER's $1.2 million fund balance and why those funds weren't being directed toward firefighter staffing before seeking new grants or a referendum.
