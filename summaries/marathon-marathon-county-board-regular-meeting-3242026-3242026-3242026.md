## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Board of Supervisors adopted the 2026 Comprehensive Plan with multiple amendments addressing renewable energy language, AI technology, data centers, and energy policy. The board also approved salaries for elected officials, authorized highway facility design services, and approved litigation related to PFAS contamination. Several supervisors were recognized for their final meeting as their terms conclude.

## Key Discussions
### Marathon County Comprehensive Plan 2026
The board considered 10 proposed amendments to the comprehensive plan. Amendment 1 (livability standards) passed unanimously. Amendments 2, 3, and 4 regarding alternative energy systems language changes (changing 'renewable energy' to 'energy projects' and 'large-scale wind and solar') were separated after a motion by Supervisor Crawl and each passed but not unanimously. Amendment 5 (data centers and battery storage background language) passed but not unanimously, with Supervisor Leur voting no citing it was 'too ideological.' Amendment 6 (radon and lead remediation) passed unanimously. Amendment 7 (regulate energy projects when allowed by law) passed but not unanimously. Amendment 8 (AI and automation language) from Supervisor Leur passed unanimously. Amendment 9 was heavily debated - Supervisor Sundowski's original language promoting clean coal, natural gas, and nuclear was amended by Supervisor Boots to remove nuclear and 'clean' from coal, reading 'promote coal and natural gas until a long-term sustainable and reliable energy source can be found that does not adversely affect agricultural land.' Supervisors Robinson and Rosenberg opposed, noting 'there is no such thing as clean coal.' A late amendment by Supervisor Sundowski regarding utility-scale wind/solar as industrial uses was defeated after extensive debate about whether to refer it to committee. The final comprehensive plan as amended passed but not unanimously.

### Establishing salaries for elected officials
Resolution 12-26 establishing salaries for the clerk of courts, sheriff, and elected department heads for the upcoming term of office was approved. Motion by Supervisor Conway, second by Supervisor Rosenberg. The motion carried with no discussion.

### Highway facility phase 2 design services
Resolution 13-26 authorizing staff to proceed with phase 2 design services for the new highway facility passed unanimously. Supervisor Soyber requested future information about plans for the old facility. Supervisor Sundowski asked about the $53 million cost estimate but Chair Gibbs clarified that cost approval was not part of this resolution.

### PFAS litigation authorization
Resolution 14-26 authorizing outside counsel on contingency basis for PFAS lawsuits was approved unanimously with two amendments. Supervisor Robinson's amendment directing the county administrator to evaluate past and present practices that may have resulted in PFAS exposure passed unanimously. Vice Chair Dickinson's amendment modifying airport-related language to reference county property passed unanimously.

### Ratification of local state of emergency
Resolution 22-26 ratifying the declaration of local emergency related to the recent blizzard passed unanimously. Administrator Leonard explained the ratification preserves the county's opportunity for reimbursement after the governor's declaration expired. He praised staff across facilities, parks, forestry, highway, sheriff's office, and airport, noting over 600 hours of additional call-in time over 24 hours, with highway workers doing 12-16 hour shifts and sleeping on cots. Supervisor Fifer echoed thanks to all staff.

### County Administrator performance evaluation
The board accepted the executive committee's recommendation on the administrator's performance evaluation and salary placement without going into closed session. Supervisor Robinson moved to accept the recommendation, seconded by Supervisor Cavalli. The motion carried unanimously.

## Public Comment
No public comment was offered.
