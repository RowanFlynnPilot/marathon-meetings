## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Public Works Committee approved several contracts and resolutions, including awarding a $126,900 recycled asphalt crushing contract to King Gravel, approving a state transportation funding resolution, and directing staff to create enforcement language for sanitary sewer discharge violations with fines up to $100/day. The committee also approved construction agreements for Military Road utilities and construction services for upcoming road projects.

## Key Discussions
### CIP Update
Michael reported the village received a Local Road Improvement Grant for Birch Street reconstruction between Scoffield Avenue and Community Center Drive, originally a 2029 project that will likely move to 2028. The grant covers over 50% of the estimated $1.5 million project. Michael noted the village has received over $9 million in grant funding over the past 4-5 years. Discussion also covered Weston Avenue repairs by Integrity Grading, Scoffield Avenue repairs by HOS Construction, and Fuller Street repairs by Melvin. A special assessment meeting for Bladel Avenue was tentatively scheduled for Thursday, May 28th at 6:00 PM.

### Street Operation Update
Staff reported road limits were removed April 29th along with Marathon County. Spring activities included mailbox repairs, sign repairs, curb line sweeping, and patching with hot mix asphalt. Storm damage from East Nick was addressed, primarily tree damage. Crews installed an additional 18-inch culvert on Jones Street after two culverts failed. Discussion addressed flooding issues near Weston Avenue requiring dewatering pumps for about a week; staff is exploring permanent pump solutions similar to a storm lift station. Salt supply is adequate with 1,500 tons ordered and 200 tons arriving this month.

### Utility Operating Update
Josh reported on water service repairs at Caribou Coffee, well maintenance, and private well sampling. Sanitary side experienced significant clear water infiltration - April showed 37 million gallons pumped into the system but 53-54 million gallons treated, indicating approximately 15 million gallons of clear water infiltration. Staff identified sump pump discharges into sanitary sewers as a major contributor. The committee discussed upcoming INI study by Inpipe Technologies to identify problem areas. Well two rehab started today, and water main flushing notices were mailed with flushing to begin next week.

### 2025 Water Utility PSC Annual Report
Michael presented the annual report showing net operating income of $482,911 for 2025, consistent with 2024's $490,000. Water loss was 6%. Utility value increased to nearly $34 million with $6.7 million in infrastructure placed in service. The utility has approximately $6.8 million in cash reserves. The committee moved to acknowledge the report, which passed unanimously.

### Resolution 2026-014 Transportation Funding
Michael explained the League of Wisconsin Municipalities drafted this resolution supporting long-term transportation funding solutions. The village has decreased public works budgets recently and needs state support for recurring costs like street maintenance and snow plowing. Wisconsin is noted as having relatively affordable automobile ownership compared to neighboring states. The committee voted unanimously to recommend the village board adopt Resolution 2026-014.

### Recycled Asphalt Base Course Production
Michael presented the annual recycled asphalt crushing project, noting King Gravel was low bidder at $4.23/ton for an estimated 30,000 tons totaling $126,900. Analysis showed this saves approximately $11/cubic yard compared to City of Wausau's costs, equating to about $130,000 savings across three 2026 projects. The committee unanimously approved awarding the contract to King Gravel.

### Sanitary Sewer Discharge Violation Discussion
Staff sought guidance on enforcement of clear water discharge violations. Current code allows fines of $100-$1,000 per day. Committee members Zagami and Jordan distinguished between unknowing and willful violations. After discussion about waiting for INI study results and sending notices with 30-60 day correction periods, the committee voted unanimously to direct staff to create enforcement language with fines not to exceed $100/day for violations.

### Water and Sewer Connection Agreement - Radkkey Avenue
Michael presented an agreement for a duplex at 2230 Radkkey Avenue to temporarily connect via long lateral until Radkkey Avenue reconstruction. Committee added language requiring assessment payment when Radkkey is reconstructed no later than 2029. Motion passed unanimously.

### Military Road Construction Agreement
The committee approved an intergovernmental agreement with Rothschild for Military Road construction. Weston's utility costs are $94,860 ($6,156 sanitary, $88,704 water main). Steam Construction will hold the contract through Rothschild. Water main relocation is needed due to storm sewer conflicts. Motion passed unanimously.

### Construction Services for Alderson/Jelnik and Bladel Projects
Michael presented proposals for construction inspection services. For Alderson/Jelnik roundabout, MSA proposed $37,315 versus Clark Dietz at $48,910; staff recommended MSA. For Bladel Avenue, MSA proposed $70,400 with summer intern staffing, Clark Dietz proposed $73,730 with full-time staff; staff recommended Clark Dietz for the additional experience. Committee unanimously approved MSA for roundabout and Clark Dietz for Bladel.
