## Meeting Overview
The Public Works Committee approved minutes, acknowledged permits, discussed CIP updates including a new $780,000 LRIP grant for Birch Street reconstruction, and addressed sanitary sewer discharge violations by directing staff to create enforcement language with fines of $100/day. The committee also approved construction agreements for Military Road ($94,860) and awarded contracts for recycled asphalt crushing and construction services.

## Key Discussions
### CIP Update
Michael reported the village received a Local Road Improvement Program (LRIP) grant of approximately $780,000 for Birch Street reconstruction between Schofield Avenue and Community Center Drive, covering over 50% of the estimated $1.5 million project. This 2029 project will likely move to 2028. Michael noted the village has received over $9 million in grant funding over the past 4-5 years. Discussion also covered Weston Avenue settlement repairs by Integrity Grading and Excavating, and Schofield Avenue punch list items with HOS Construction including manhole repairs and concrete work.

### Street Operations Update
Staff reported road limits were removed April 29th along with Marathon County. Crews completed mailbox repairs, sign repairs, spring sweeping, and hot mix patching. Significant dewatering was required near Weston Avenue tributary for approximately one week due to flooding. Storm damage from East Nick area required tree cleanup. Culverts failed on Jones Street requiring installation of a third 18-inch pipe. Vehicle maintenance included removing plow equipment and applying anti-rust treatment.

### Utility Operations Update
Josh reported on clear water infiltration issues, noting April treated 53-54 million gallons while only 37 million was pumped into the system - approximately 15 million gallons of clear water. Investigation found sump pumps illegally connected to sanitary sewers at smaller lift stations like Kathleen. Staff discussed the upcoming INI study with in-pipe technologies to identify infiltration sources. The Carrie well rehabilitation started today with plans for tower cleaning.

### 2025 Water Utility PSC Annual Report
Michael presented the annual Public Service Commission report showing net operating income of $482,911 for 2025, consistent with 2024's $490,000. Water loss was 6%. Utility value reached nearly $34 million with $6.7 million in infrastructure placed in service including wells 7 and 8 and PFAS-related improvements. The utility serves approximately 6,100 customers: 5,250 in Weston, 830 in Rothschild, and 5 in other areas. Motion to acknowledge passed unanimously.

### Resolution 2026-014 Transportation Funding
Michael presented a League of Wisconsin Municipalities sample resolution supporting long-term transportation funding solutions. He noted the village has decreased public works budgets recently and Wisconsin is affordable compared to neighboring states for automobile ownership. The committee voted unanimously to recommend the village board adopt Resolution 2026-014.

### Recycled Base Course Production
Michael reported King Gravel submitted the low bid at $4.23/ton for crushing approximately 30,000 tons of recycled material at the Ryan Street site, totaling $126,900. Analysis showed the village's cost of $17.11/cubic yard compares to Wausau's $28/cubic yard, saving approximately $130,000 across this year's three projects. Motion to award to King Gravel passed unanimously.

### Sanitary Sewer Discharge Violations
Committee discussed enforcement of illegal sump pump connections to sanitary sewers. Current code allows fines of $100-$1,000 per day per violation. Committee members distinguished between unknowing and willful violations. After discussion about waiting for the INI study results to identify problem areas, the committee voted unanimously to direct staff to create enforcement language with fines of $100/day for violations.

### Radkey Avenue Water/Sewer Connection
Michael explained a proposed duplex at 2230 Radkey Avenue needs temporary connection to existing water/sewer mains since mains don't currently extend to the lot. When Radkey Avenue is reconstructed, the owner will pay assessment for main extension. Committee requested adding specific timeline language. Motion passed unanimously to recommend the agreement with reconstruction no later than 2029.

### Military Road Construction Agreement
Michael presented an intergovernmental agreement with Village of Rothschild for Military Road construction from Wolfman Street to Hellin Street. Rothschild holds the contract with Steam Construction; Weston is responsible for water/sewer work totaling $94,860. Motion to recommend approval passed unanimously.

### Construction Services for Alderson/Jelinek and Bladel Projects
Michael presented construction inspection proposals received Friday. For Alderson/Jelinek roundabout: MSA at $37,315 vs Clark Dietz at $48,910 - MSA recommended. For Bladel Avenue: MSA at $70,400 vs Clark Dietz at $73,730 - Clark Dietz recommended despite slightly higher cost due to having full-time experienced staff versus MSA's summer intern. Motion passed unanimously for both recommendations.
