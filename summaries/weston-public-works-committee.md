## Meeting Overview
The Public Works Committee approved several items including a resolution supporting state transportation funding, awarded a recycled asphalt crushing contract to King Gravel for $126,900, and directed staff to create enforcement language for sanitary sewer discharge violations with fines of $100 per day. The committee also approved construction agreements for Military Road utilities and construction services for two road projects.

## Key Discussions
### CIP Update
Michael reported the village received a local road improvement grant for Birch Street reconstruction between Schofield Avenue and Community Center Drive, originally a 2029 project that will likely move to 2028. The grant covers over 50% of the approximately $1.5 million project. Michael noted the village has received over $9 million in grant funding over the past 4-5 years. Discussion also covered Weston Avenue repairs by Integrity Grading and Excavating for road depression issues over the sanitary sewer trench, and upcoming repairs on Schofield Avenue and Fuller Street. A Bladel special assessment meeting was tentatively scheduled for Thursday, May 28th at 6:00 p.m.

### Retention Pond Update
Michael reported receiving updated modeling from Strand showing the pond will remove more phosphorus for Weston but less for Rothschild. An amendment to the inter-municipal agreement may be needed based on revised effectiveness calculations. Staff is attempting to get DOT to pay for their portion (approximately one quarter) which previously impacted grant eligibility.

### Street Operations Update
Staff reported on spring activities including road limit removal on April 29th, mailbox and sign repairs, dewatering operations on Weston Avenue tributary for approximately one week, curb line sweeping, hot mix patching, and storm damage cleanup. Jones Street culvert repairs were completed, adding a third 18-inch culvert. Vehicle maintenance included removing plow equipment, applying fluid film rust protection, and servicing mowing equipment. A new MAC tandem axle chassis has been ordered with upfitter items.

### Utility Operating Update
Josh reported on a service repair at Caribou Coffee, well maintenance items, and continued meter card maintenance. Sanitary side experienced high pump activity due to clear water infiltration. Data showed January had 36 million gallons pumped into the system with 32 million treated, but April showed 37 million in and 53-54 million treated - approximately 15 million gallons of clear water. Staff discussed upcoming INI study with inpipe technologies to identify infiltration sources.

### 2025 Water Utility PSC Annual Report
Michael presented the annual report showing operating net income of approximately $482,911, consistent with the prior year's $490,000. Water loss was 6%. The utility value increased to nearly $34 million with $6.7 million in infrastructure placed in service. The utility serves approximately 6,100 customers total: 5,250 in Weston, 830 in Rothschild, and 5 in Schofield/Rib Mountain. Cash position is $6.8 million. Motion to acknowledge passed unanimously.

### Resolution 2026-014 Transportation Funding
Michael explained this League of Wisconsin Municipalities resolution supports sustainable state transportation funding. He noted the village has decreased public works budgets recently and Wisconsin is generally affordable compared to neighboring states for automobile ownership. Motion to adopt Resolution 2026-014 passed unanimously.

### Recycled Asphalt Crushing Contract
Michael explained the Ryan Street site stockpiles concrete and asphalt that gets crushed into base course. King Gravel submitted the low bid at $4.23/ton (versus $4.25 from another bidder) with estimated 30,000 tons totaling $126,900. Analysis showed village cost of $17.11/cubic yard compared to City of Wausau paying $28/cubic yard, resulting in approximately $130,000 savings across three projects this year. Motion to award to King Gravel passed unanimously.

### Sanitary Sewer Discharge Violations
Discussion centered on enforcing existing code against sump pump discharges into sanitary sewers. Josh described finding clear water in lift stations, including one Kathleen station with only 5-6 houses where a sump pump was detected. Committee discussed whether violations are knowing or unknowing. Staff reported March flows were 43 million gallons versus 35.5 million the prior year, costing approximately $30,000 extra in March and $42,000 more in May. Committee directed staff to create enforcement language with fines of $100 per day. Motion passed unanimously.

### Radkey Avenue Utility Agreement
Michael explained a vacant lot needs water and sewer service but mains don't currently extend there. Proposed temporary long lateral service until Radkey Avenue reconstruction, at which point the owner would pay assessment for main extension. Committee added language requiring reconstruction no later than 2029. Motion passed unanimously.

### Military Road Construction Agreement
Michael presented an intergovernmental agreement with Rothschild for Military Road construction. Weston's share is $94,860 for sanitary sewer ($6,156) and water main ($88,704) relocation due to storm sewer conflicts. Steam Construction is the contractor. Motion to approve agreement passed unanimously.

### Construction Services for Alderson/Jelinek and Bladel Projects
Michael sought construction inspection services due to vacant engineering tech position. For Alderson/Jelinek roundabout: MSA proposed $37,315 versus Clark Dietz at $48,910; staff recommended MSA. For Bladel Avenue: MSA proposed $70,400 versus Clark Dietz at $73,730; staff recommended Clark Dietz due to having experienced full-time staff rather than a summer intern. Motion to approve both recommendations passed unanimously.
