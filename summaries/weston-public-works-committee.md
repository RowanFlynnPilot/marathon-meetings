## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Public Works Committee met on May 11, 2026, approving minutes and permits, awarding a $126,900 recycled asphalt crushing contract to King Gravel, and adopting a resolution supporting state transportation funding. The committee also directed staff to develop $100/day fine language for illegal sewer discharges and approved construction service contracts for two road projects.

## Key Discussions
### CIP Update
Michael reported the village received a Local Road Improvement Grant for Birch Street reconstruction between Scoffield Avenue and Community Center Drive, covering over 50% of the estimated $1.5 million project. This 2029 project may be moved to 2028. Michael noted the village has received over $9 million in grant funding in the past 4-5 years. He also discussed Weston Avenue repairs by Integrity Grading for road settlement over sewer trenches, and Scoffield Avenue repair items with HOS Construction including manhole repairs and concrete pitting.

### Street Operations Update
Staff reported spring road limit signs were removed April 29th along with Marathon County. Crews performed dewatering at the Weston Avenue tributary for approximately one week due to flooding concerns near Crane Meadows. The committee discussed the ongoing drainage issues in that area, with suggestions for a permanent pump solution. Spring curb sweeping, hot mix patching, and storm damage cleanup from East Nick were completed. A culvert failure on Jones Street required installation of a third 18-inch culvert.

### Utility Operations Update
Josh reported significant clear water infiltration into the sanitary sewer system, with April showing 37 million gallons pumped into the water system but 53-54 million gallons treated at the wastewater plant - approximately 15 million gallons of clear water. The Kathleen lift station showed particularly high clear water discharge from suspected sump pump connections. The INI study with in-pipe technologies is planned to identify problem areas. Well 2 rehab at Carrie started today.

### 2025 Water Utility PSC Annual Report
Michael presented the annual Public Service Commission report showing net operating income of $482,911 for 2025, consistent with 2024's $490,000. Water loss is at 6%. The utility value reached nearly $34 million with $6.7 million in infrastructure placed in service. The utility has 6,100 customers total with 5,250 in Weston and 830 in Rothschild. Motion to acknowledge passed unanimously.

### Resolution 2026-014 State Transportation Funding
The League of Wisconsin Municipalities drafted this resolution seeking improved transportation funding for local operations. Michael noted the village has decreased public works budgets recently and transportation funding doesn't cover what the public expects. The resolution passed unanimously with recommendation to the village board.

### Recycled Base Course Production Contract
Michael presented quotes for crushing recycled concrete and asphalt at the Ryan Street site. King Gravel submitted the low bid at $4.23/ton for an estimated 30,000 tons ($126,900 total). Michael demonstrated savings of approximately $11/cubic yard compared to City of Wausau costs, totaling about $130,000 in savings across three 2026 projects. Contract awarded to King Gravel unanimously.

### Sanitary Sewer Discharge Violation Discussion
Staff sought guidance on penalty amounts for illegal sump pump and clear water discharges to sanitary sewers. Current code allows fines of $100-$1,000 per day per violation. Committee members distinguished between unknowing and willful violations, recommending starting at $100/day for first offenses. The committee voted unanimously to direct staff to create enforcement language with fines not to exceed $100/day.

### Water and Sewer Connection Agreement - Radkey Avenue
Michael explained a duplex builder at 2230 Radkey Avenue needs temporary water/sewer service via long laterals through Outlot 1 until Radkey Avenue reconstruction. Committee added language requiring the street be reconstructed no later than 2029, at which point the property owner pays assessment for main extension. Motion passed unanimously.

### Military Road Construction Agreement
The village will reimburse Rothschild for Weston's water and sewer utility work on Military Road (Worfman to Helin Street) at an estimated $94,860 ($6,156 sanitary, $88,704 water main). Steam Construction is the contractor. Water main relocation is needed due to storm sewer conflicts. Agreement approved unanimously.

### Construction Service Proposals
For the Alderson/Jelnik roundabout project, MSA was selected at $37,315 (lower than Clark Dietz at $48,910). For Bladel Avenue, Clark Dietz was recommended at $73,730 over MSA's $70,400 due to having full-time staff rather than a summer intern. Both approved unanimously.
