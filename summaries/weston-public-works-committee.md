## Meeting Overview
The Public Works Committee approved several contracts and resolutions, including awarding the 2026 recycled asphalt crushing project to King Gravel for $126,900, approving a transportation funding resolution to send to state legislators, and directing staff to create enforcement language for sanitary sewer discharge violations with fines of $100 per day. The committee also approved construction agreements for the Military Road project and construction services for upcoming road projects.

## Key Discussions
### CIP Update
Michael reported the village received a Local Road Improvement Grant for Birch Street reconstruction between Schofield Avenue and Community Center Drive, covering about 50% of the estimated $1.5 million project cost. This 2029 project may be moved up to 2028. Michael noted the village has received over $9 million in grant funding over the past 4-5 years. Updates were also provided on Weston Avenue repairs by Integrity Grading and Excavating, and Schofield Avenue punch list items with HOS Construction.

### Street Operations Update
Staff reported on spring activities including road limit removal, mailbox and sign repairs, curb line sweeping, and hot mix patching. Significant flooding issues required dewatering operations near Weston Avenue for approximately a week. Jones Street had culvert failures requiring additional repairs. Staff is considering permanent pump solutions for recurring flooding areas. The Kennedy Park Farmers Market received positive feedback regarding parking and traffic flow.

### Utility Operations Update
Josh reported significant clear water infiltration issues, with April showing 37 million gallons pumped into the system but 53-54 million gallons treated at the wastewater plant - approximately 15 million gallons of clear water. The Kathleen lift station showed evidence of sump pump discharge into sanitary sewers. An INI study with in-pipe technologies is upcoming to identify infiltration sources. Well 2 rehab at Carey started today, and water main flushing notices are going out with flushing starting next week.

### 2025 Water Utility PSC Annual Report
Michael presented the annual report showing operating net income of approximately $482,911 for 2025, consistent with 2024. Water loss was 6%. The utility value increased to nearly $34 million with $6.7 million in infrastructure placed in service. The utility has about $6.8 million in cash reserves. Motion to acknowledge passed unanimously.

### Resolution 2026-014 Transportation Funding
Michael explained this League of Wisconsin Municipalities resolution supports long-term funding solutions for state transportation. Grant funding helps one-time costs but recurring annual payments for maintenance and snow plowing remain underfunded, requiring general tax dollar subsidies. The committee voted unanimously to recommend the village board adopt the resolution.

### Recycled Base Course Production
Michael reported King Gravel submitted the low bid at $4.23 per ton for crushing approximately 30,000 tons of recycled material, totaling $126,900. Analysis showed this saves the village approximately $11 per cubic yard compared to City of Wausau costs, resulting in about $130,000 savings across three 2026 projects. Motion to award to King Gravel passed unanimously.

### Sanitary Sewer Discharge Violation Discussion
Staff sought guidance on enforcement of sewer discharge violations, particularly sump pump connections to sanitary sewers. Current code allows fines between $100-$1,000 per day. Committee discussed distinguishing between unknowing and willful violations. The committee directed staff to create enforcement language with fines of $100 per day, with enforcement to begin after the infiltration study identifies problem areas. Motion passed unanimously.

### Water and Sewer Connection Agreement - Radkey Avenue
Michael explained a property owner at 2230 Radkey Avenue needs temporary water and sewer connection for a new duplex until Radkey Avenue reconstruction. The committee added language requiring reconstruction no later than 2029, at which time the owner would pay assessment for main extension. Motion passed unanimously.

### Military Road Construction Agreement
The committee approved an intergovernmental agreement with Rothschild for Military Road construction. Weston's share covers utility costs of approximately $94,860 for sanitary sewer ($6,156) and water main ($88,704) relocation. Steam Construction is the contractor. Motion passed unanimously.

### Construction Service Proposals
Michael presented proposals for construction inspection services. For the Alderson/Jelinek roundabout project, MSA was selected at $37,315 (lower than Clark Dietz at $48,910). For Bladel Avenue, Clark Dietz was recommended at $73,730 despite MSA's lower bid of $70,400, due to Clark Dietz having full-time experienced staff versus MSA's summer intern. Motion passed unanimously.
