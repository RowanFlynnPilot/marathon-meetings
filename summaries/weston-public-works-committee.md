## Meeting Overview
The Public Works Committee approved several routine items and made key decisions including awarding a recycled asphalt crushing contract to King Gravel for $126,900, adopting a resolution supporting state transportation funding, directing staff to create enforcement language for sanitary sewer discharge violations at $100/day fines, and approving construction service proposals for upcoming road projects. The committee also discussed significant clear water infiltration issues costing the village approximately $30,000-$42,000 monthly in excess treatment costs.

## Key Discussions
### CIP Update
Michael reported the village received a Local Road Improvement Grant for Birch Street reconstruction between Scofield Avenue and Community Center Drive, covering over 50% of the estimated $1.5 million project cost. This 2029 project will likely be moved to 2028. He noted the village has received over $9 million in grant funding over the past 4-5 years. Discussion also covered Weston Avenue repairs by Integrity Grading and Excavating for road settling over sanitary sewer trenches, with agreement the contractor must return if issues recur. Scofield Avenue manhole repairs with HOS Construction and Fuller Street repairs with Melvin were also discussed.

### Street Operations Update
Staff reported on spring activities including road limit removal, mailbox and sign repairs, curb line sweeping, and hot mix patching. Significant flooding mitigation occurred near Weston Avenue tributary requiring a week of dewatering pumps. Jones Street culvert failures required installation of a third 18-inch culvert. Shop repairs included transitioning plow trucks for summer use with fluid film anti-rust treatment. A new Mac tandem axle chassis has been ordered with upfitter items being coordinated through Scott's Repair.

### Utility Operating Update
Josh reported on service repairs at Caribou Coffee and ongoing lift station maintenance due to power and phase failures. The committee discussed significant clear water infiltration issues - April saw 37 million gallons pumped into the system but 54 million gallons treated at the wastewater plant, indicating approximately 15 million gallons of clear water from infiltration or illegal sump pump discharges. In-pipe Technologies will conduct an INI study to identify problem areas. The Kathleen lift station was cited as an example where one house's sump pump caused the station to run nearly continuously.

### 2025 Water Utility PSC Annual Report
Michael presented the annual report showing operating revenues of approximately $3 million with net operating income of $482,911 for 2025, consistent with 2024's $490,000. Water loss was 6%. The utility's total value reached nearly $34 million, an increase of 6.7% from infrastructure placed in service including Wells 7 and 8 and water mains from Weston Avenue project. The utility serves 6,100 customers total with 5,250 in Weston and 830 in Rothschild. Approved unanimously on motion to acknowledge.

### Resolution 2026-014 Transportation Funding
Michael explained this League of Wisconsin Municipalities sample resolution supports long-term funding solutions for state transportation. The resolution addresses the gap between grant funding for one-time costs versus recurring annual needs for street maintenance and snow plowing that are currently subsidized by general tax dollars. Wisconsin remains affordable compared to neighboring states for automobile ownership costs. Approved unanimously to recommend village board adoption.

### Recycled Asphalt Base Course Production
Michael presented quotes for crushing recycled concrete and asphalt at the Ryan Street site. King Gravel submitted the low bid at $4.23/ton for an estimated 30,000 tons totaling $126,900. He demonstrated cost savings by comparing the village's $17.11/cubic yard cost versus City of Wausau's $28/cubic yard, resulting in approximately $130,000 savings across three 2026 projects. Approved unanimously to award contract to King Gravel.

### Sanitary Sewer Discharge Violation Discussion
Staff sought guidance on enforcement of sewer discharge violations, particularly for residents illegally connecting sump pumps to sanitary sewers. Current code allows fines of $100-$1,000 per day. Committee members distinguished between unknowing versus willful violations. Discussion included waiting for In-Pipe Technologies study results before issuing violation notices. The committee directed staff to create enforcement language with fines not to exceed $100 per day, approved unanimously.

### Water and Sewer Connection Agreement - Radkkey Avenue
Michael explained a builder wants to construct a duplex on Lot 1 at 2230 Radkkey Avenue, which lacks frontage on a water main. Staff proposed allowing temporary long lateral connection until Radkkey Avenue reconstruction extends mains, at which point the owner would pay assessment. Committee directed adding language specifying reconstruction "no later than 2029." Approved unanimously with the date modification.

### Military Road Construction Agreement
The committee reviewed an intergovernmental agreement with Village of Rothschild for Military Road construction from Wolfman Street to Helin Street. Weston's share covers water main relocation ($88,704) and sanitary sewer ($6,156) for total utility costs of $94,860. Steam Construction is the contractor. Water main requires relocation due to conflict with storm sewer improvements. Approved unanimously.

### Construction Service Proposals - Alderson/Jelnik and Bladel Projects
Michael presented proposals for construction inspection services. For Alderson/Jelnik roundabout project, MSA proposed $37,315 versus Clark Dietz at $48,910; MSA recommended due to lower cost and roundabout design involvement. For Bladel Avenue, MSA proposed $70,400 versus Clark Dietz at $73,730; Clark Dietz recommended despite higher cost because they would provide experienced full-time staff versus MSA's summer intern. Approved unanimously to use MSA for roundabout and Clark Dietz for Bladel.
