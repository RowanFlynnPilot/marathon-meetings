## Meeting Overview
The Public Works Committee approved several infrastructure items including a $126,900 recycled asphalt crushing contract with King Gravel, a resolution supporting state transportation funding, and construction service agreements for upcoming road projects. The committee also discussed sanitary sewer discharge violations and directed staff to create enforcement language with fines starting at $100 per day.

## Key Discussions
### CIP Update
Michael reported the village received a local road improvement grant for Birch Street reconstruction between Schofield Avenue and Community Center Drive, originally a 2029 project that will likely move to 2028. The grant covers over 50% of the estimated $1.5 million project. Michael noted the village has received over $9 million in grant funding over the past 4-5 years. Discussion also covered Weston Avenue repairs by Integrity Grading and Excavating, Schofield Avenue repairs by HOS Construction, and Fuller Street work by Melvin. A special assessment meeting for Bladel Avenue was scheduled for Thursday, May 28th at 6:00 PM.

### Street Operations Update
Staff reported road limits were removed on April 29th along with Marathon County. Activities included mailbox and sign repairs, dewatering operations near Weston Avenue tributary for approximately one week, curb line sweeping, hot mix patching, storm damage cleanup from East Nick area, inlet repairs before Birch Street project, and installation of overhead signs on Schofield Avenue. Culvert failures on Jones Street required additional 18-inch culvert installation. Equipment maintenance included removing plow equipment, servicing mowers and tractors, and preparing units for summer use.

### Utility Operations Update
Josh reported on water service repairs including one at Caribou Coffee, well maintenance items, and meter card maintenance. Sanitary operations saw significant increased flows due to clear water infiltration - April showed 37 million gallons pumped into the system but 53-54 million gallons treated, indicating approximately 15 million gallons of infiltration/inflow. Staff identified sump pump discharges into sanitary lines as a contributor. The INI study with in-pipe technologies will help identify problem areas. Well 2 rehab with Carrie Well started today.

### 2025 Water Utility PSC Annual Report
Michael presented the annual report showing operating revenues of approximately $3 million, operating expenses resulting in net income of $482,911 (compared to $490,000 in 2024). Water loss was 6%. The utility value at year-end 2025 was nearly $34 million, an increase of 6.7% from infrastructure placed in service. The utility serves about 6,100 customers total - 5,250 in Weston, 830 in Rothschild, and 5 in Schofield/Rib Mountain Metro. PAS settlement funds of $1.44 million were received. Motion to acknowledge passed unanimously.

### Resolution 2026-014 Transportation Funding
Michael explained this League of Wisconsin Municipalities sample resolution supports long-term funding solutions for the state transportation fund, noting that while grant funding helps one-time costs, recurring annual payments for street maintenance and snow plowing remain underfunded and require general tax dollar subsidies. Motion to recommend village board adopt the resolution passed unanimously.

### Recycled Base Course Production
Michael presented the 2026 recycled asphalt crushing contract, with King Gravel as low bidder at $4.23/ton (versus $4.25 from competitor). Estimated tonnage is 30,000 tons for a total cost of $126,900. Analysis showed the village saves approximately $11/cubic yard compared to City of Wausau costs ($17.11 vs $28/cubic yard), saving about $130,000 across three 2026 projects. Motion to award to King Gravel passed unanimously.

### Sanitary Sewer Discharge Violation Discussion
Staff sought committee guidance on enforcement of ordinance violations for residents discharging sump pumps into sanitary sewers. The code allows fines of $100-$1,000 per day per violation. Committee discussed distinguishing between unknowing and willful violations. After discussion about the approximately $30,000-$42,000 in additional treatment costs from infiltration, committee directed staff to create enforcement language with fines starting at $100 per day for first offenses. Motion passed unanimously.

### Water and Sewer Connection Agreement - Radkey Avenue
Michael presented an agreement for a new duplex at 2230 Radkey Avenue to temporarily connect to water and sewer mains until Radkey Avenue reconstruction. Committee added requirement that reconstruction occur no later than 2029, at which time property owner will pay assessment for main extension. Motion to recommend approval passed unanimously.

### Military Road Construction Agreement
Committee approved intergovernmental agreement with Village of Rothschild for Military Road construction from Wolfman Street to Helen Street. Weston's share for utility work (sanitary sewer and water main relocation) is estimated at $94,860. Steam Construction is the contractor. Motion to recommend approval passed unanimously.

### Construction Services Proposals
Michael presented proposals for construction inspection services. For Alderson/Jelinek intersection (roundabout), MSA was selected at $37,315 (vs Clark Dietz at $48,910). For Bladel Avenue, Clark Dietz was recommended at $73,730 (vs MSA at $70,400) due to having full-time experienced staff versus MSA's summer intern. Motion to approve both selections passed unanimously.
