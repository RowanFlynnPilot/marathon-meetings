## Meeting Overview
The Public Works Committee approved several infrastructure-related items including a state transportation funding resolution, a recycled asphalt crushing contract with King Gravel for $126,900, and construction service agreements for upcoming road projects. The committee also directed staff to develop $100/day penalty language for illegal sewer discharges after discussion of significant clear water infiltration adding millions of gallons to wastewater treatment costs.

## Key Discussions
### CIP Update
Michael reported the village received a Local Road Improvement Grant for Birch Street reconstruction between Schofield Avenue and Community Center Drive, covering over 50% of the estimated $1.5 million project cost. This 2029 project may be moved to 2028. Michael noted the village has received over $9 million in grant funding over the past 4-5 years. Discussion also covered upcoming Weston Avenue repairs by Integrity Grading, Schofield Avenue repairs by HOS Construction, and Fuller Street restoration work.

### Street Operations Update
Staff reported on road limits removal, spring sweeping, hot mix patching, storm damage cleanup off East Nick, inlet repairs on Birch Street, and overhead sign installation on Schofield Avenue. Discussion of flooding issues on Weston Avenue area included use of sandbags and dewatering pumps for about a week. Staff is considering permanent pump solutions. Culvert failures on Jones Street required installation of a third 18-inch pipe.

### Utility Operations Update
Josh reported on water service repairs, well maintenance, and significant clear water infiltration into sanitary sewers. April data showed 37 million gallons pumped into the water system but 53-54 million gallons treated at the wastewater plant - approximately 15 million gallons of clear water infiltration. Staff identified sump pump discharges into sanitary sewers as a major contributor. INI study with in-pipe technologies is planned to identify problem areas.

### 2025 Water Utility PSC Annual Report
Michael presented the annual report showing operating revenue of approximately $3 million and net operating income of $482,911 for 2025, consistent with 2024's $490,000. Water loss is at 6%. The utility value increased to nearly $34 million with 6.7% infrastructure placed in service. The utility serves 6,100 customers total including 5,250 in Weston, 830 in Rothschild, and 5 in Schofield/Rib Mountain Metro. Motion to acknowledge passed unanimously.

### Resolution 2026-014 Transportation Funding
Michael explained this League of Wisconsin Municipalities sample resolution requests long-term sustainable funding solutions for the state transportation fund, noting that current grant funding helps one-time costs but recurring operational funding remains inadequate. The resolution will be sent to the governor and state legislators. Motion to adopt passed unanimously.

### Recycled Asphalt Base Course Production
Michael presented quotes for crushing recycled concrete and asphalt at the Ryan Street site. King Gravel submitted the low bid at $4.23/ton for an estimated 30,000 tons totaling $126,900. This compares favorably to City of Wausau paying $28/cubic yard versus Weston's $17.11/cubic yard, saving approximately $130,000 across three 2026 projects. Motion to award to King Gravel passed unanimously.

### Sanitary Sewer Discharge Violation Discussion
Staff sought committee guidance on enforcement of illegal sump pump discharges into sanitary sewers. Current ordinance allows fines of $100-$1,000 per day. Committee discussed distinguishing between unknowing versus willful violations. After discussion, committee directed staff to develop penalty language starting at $100/day for violations, with motion passing unanimously. Staff will wait for INI study results to identify problem areas before sending violation notices.

### Water/Sewer Connection Agreement - Radkkey Avenue
Michael presented an agreement allowing a duplex builder on lot one at 2230 Radkkey Avenue to temporarily connect to existing water/sewer mains via long lateral service until Radkkey Avenue reconstruction. Committee added language requiring reconstruction "no later than 2029" when the owner would pay assessment for permanent main extension. Motion passed unanimously.

### Military Road Construction Agreement
Michael presented an intergovernmental agreement with Village of Rothschild for construction on Military Road from Wolfman Street to Helin Street. Weston's share for utility relocation (sanitary sewer $6,156 and water main $88,704) totals $94,860. Steam Construction is the contractor. Water main requires relocation due to storm sewer conflicts. Motion to approve passed unanimously.

### Construction Service Proposals - Alderson/Jelnik and Bladel Projects
Michael presented construction inspection service proposals. For Alderson/Jelnik roundabout project, MSA proposed $37,315 versus Clark Dietz at $48,910; staff recommended MSA. For Bladel Avenue, MSA proposed $70,400 versus Clark Dietz at $73,730; staff recommended Clark Dietz for the extra $3,000 due to having full-time experienced staff rather than summer intern. Motion to approve both recommendations passed unanimously.
