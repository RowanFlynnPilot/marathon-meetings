## Meeting Overview
The Public Works Committee approved several infrastructure contracts and discussed ongoing road and utility projects. Key actions included awarding a recycled asphalt crushing contract to King Gravel for $126,900, approving a resolution supporting state transportation funding, and directing staff to develop enforcement language for sanitary sewer discharge violations at $100 per day.

## Key Discussions
### CIP Update
Michael reported the village received a Local Road Improvement Grant for Birch Street reconstruction between Schofield Avenue and Community Center Drive, originally a 2029 project that will likely move to 2028. The grant covers about 50% of the estimated $1.5 million cost. Michael noted the village has received over $9 million in grant funding over the past 4-5 years. Discussion also covered Weston Avenue repairs by Integrity Grading, Schofield Avenue repairs by HOS Construction, and a special assessment meeting scheduled for May 28th for the Bladel Avenue project.

### Street Operations Update
Staff reported road limits were removed April 29th. Work included mailbox and sign repairs, dewatering near Weston Avenue tributary for about a week due to flooding, spring curb sweeping, hot mix patching, storm damage cleanup on East Nick, inlet repairs on Birch Street, and overhead signing on Schofield Avenue. Discussion addressed ongoing flooding issues in the Zenzer area, with staff looking at permanent pump solutions.

### Utility Operations Update
Josh reported on water service repairs at Caribou Coffee, well maintenance, and private well sampling. Sanitary side saw significant pump activity due to clear water infiltration. April data showed 37 million gallons pumped into the system but 53-54 million gallons treated, indicating approximately 15 million gallons of clear water infiltration. Staff discussed the upcoming INI study with In-Pipe Technologies to identify infiltration sources.

### 2025 Water Utility PSC Annual Report
Michael reviewed the annual report showing net operating income of approximately $482,911 for 2025, consistent with 2024's $490,000. Water loss was 6%. Utility value increased to nearly $34 million with $6.7 million in infrastructure added. The utility serves about 6,100 customers total. Motion to acknowledge passed unanimously.

### Resolution 2026-014 Transportation Funding
Michael explained this League of Wisconsin Municipalities resolution supports long-term transportation funding solutions. The village has decreased public works budgets recently and seeks state assistance for recurring transportation costs rather than just one-time grants. Motion to recommend adoption passed unanimously.

### Recycled Base Course Production
King Gravel submitted the low bid at $4.23 per ton for crushing recycled material, with estimated 30,000 tons totaling $126,900. Michael showed this saves approximately $11 per cubic yard compared to City of Wausau costs, representing about $130,000 in savings across three 2026 projects. Motion to award to King Gravel passed unanimously.

### Sanitary Sewer Discharge Violations
Staff sought guidance on fining residents who discharge clear water into sanitary sewers. Current code allows fines of $100-$1,000 per day. Committee discussed distinguishing between unknowing and willful violations. After discussion, committee directed staff to create enforcement language with fines not exceeding $100 per day for violations. Motion passed unanimously.

### Water and Sewer Connection Agreement - Radkey Avenue
Michael explained a duplex builder on Radkey Avenue needs temporary connection to utilities until the street is reconstructed. Committee added language requiring reconstruction no later than 2029. Motion to recommend approval with the 2029 deadline passed unanimously.

### Military Road Construction Agreement
The village will reimburse Rothschild for water and sewer work on Military Road, estimated at $94,860. Steam Construction is the contractor. Motion to recommend approval passed unanimously.

### Construction Services Proposals
Michael recommended MSA for the Alderson/Jelinek roundabout project at $37,315 and Clark Dietz for the Bladel Avenue project at $73,730, citing preference for experienced full-time staff over interns. Motion to approve both passed unanimously.
