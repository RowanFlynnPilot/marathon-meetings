## Meeting Overview
The Public Works Committee approved several routine items and made key decisions including awarding a $126,900 recycled asphalt crushing contract to King Gravel, adopting a resolution supporting state transportation funding, and establishing a $100/day fine policy for illegal sanitary sewer discharges. The committee also approved construction agreements for multiple road projects totaling over $200,000 in consulting and utility costs.

## Key Discussions
### CIP Update
Michael reported the village received a Local Road Improvement Grant for Birch Street reconstruction between Scoffield Avenue and Community Center Drive, covering over 50% of the estimated $1.5 million project. This 2029 project may be moved up to 2028. Staff noted the village has received over $9 million in grant funding over the past 4-5 years. Discussion included updates on Weston Avenue repairs by Integrity Grading, Scoffield Avenue concrete repairs by HOS Construction, and Fuller Street restoration by Melvin. A special assessment meeting for Bladel Avenue was tentatively scheduled for Thursday, May 28 at 6:00 PM.

### Street Operations Update
Staff reported road limits were removed April 29 along with Marathon County. Crews completed mailbox repairs, sign repairs, and spring curb sweeping. Dewatering operations were conducted near Weston Avenue tributary for approximately one week due to flooding. Staff addressed storm damage from an event near East Nick affecting trees. Culvert failures on Jones Street required installation of a third 18-inch culvert. Vehicle maintenance included removing plow equipment and preparing summer equipment. A new MAC tandem axle chassis has been ordered.

### Utility Operations Update
Josh reported a service repair at Caribou Coffee and continued well maintenance. Sanitary lift stations experienced significant activity due to clear water infiltration. Data showed April treated 53-54 million gallons while only 37 million gallons were pumped into the distribution system, indicating approximately 15 million gallons of clear water infiltration. Staff identified sump pump discharges into sanitary sewers as a contributing factor. In-pipe technologies will conduct an INI study to identify infiltration sources. Well 2 rehab at Carrie started the day of the meeting.

### 2025 Water Utility PSC Annual Report
Michael presented the annual report showing operating revenues resulting in net income of approximately $482,911 for 2025, consistent with 2024's $490,000. Water loss was reported at 6%. Utility value reached nearly $34 million, an increase of 6.7% from infrastructure placed in service including Wells 7 and 8. The utility serves approximately 6,100 customers total: 5,250 in Weston, 830 in Rothschild, and 5 in other areas. Cash reserves total $6.8 million. Motion to acknowledge passed unanimously.

### Resolution 2026-014 Transportation Funding
Michael explained this League of Wisconsin Municipalities resolution supports long-term transportation funding solutions at the state level. He noted while grants help with one-time costs, recurring annual funding for street maintenance and snow plowing remains inadequate, requiring subsidies from general tax dollars. Wisconsin remains affordable compared to neighboring states for automobile ownership costs. Motion to adopt passed unanimously.

### Recycled Base Course Production
Michael reported King Gravel submitted the low bid at $4.23/ton for crushing recycled concrete and asphalt at the Ryan Street site, with an estimated 30,000 tons totaling $126,900. Analysis showed the village saves approximately $11/cubic yard compared to City of Wausau costs, resulting in approximately $130,000 savings across 2026 projects. Motion to award contract to King Gravel passed unanimously.

### Sanitary Sewer Discharge Violation Discussion
Staff sought guidance on fining residents illegally discharging sump pump water into sanitary sewers. Current code allows fines of $100-$1,000 per day. Committee discussed distinguishing between unknowing and willful violations. After discussion about waiting for the in-pipe technology study results to identify violators, the committee directed staff to create enforcement language with fines not to exceed $100/day for first offenses. Motion passed unanimously.

### Water and Sewer Connection Agreement - Radkkey Avenue
Michael explained a builder wants to construct a duplex on lot 1 at 2230 Radkkey Avenue where water/sewer mains currently dead end. Staff proposed allowing a temporary long lateral connection until Radkkey Avenue is reconstructed. Committee requested adding a deadline, resulting in language requiring reconstruction no later than end of 2029 construction season. Motion passed unanimously.

### Military Road Construction Agreement
The committee approved an intergovernmental agreement with Village of Rothschild for Military Road construction from Wolfman Street to Helin Street. Weston's share covers utility costs of $94,860 ($6,156 sanitary sewer, $88,704 water main). Steam Construction is the contractor. The water main requires relocation due to storm sewer conflicts. Motion passed unanimously.

### Construction Services Proposals
Michael presented proposals for construction inspection services. For Alderson/Jelnik roundabout project, MSA proposed $37,315 versus Clark Dietz at $48,910; staff recommended MSA. For Bladel Avenue, MSA proposed $70,400 versus Clark Dietz at $73,730; staff recommended Clark Dietz due to using full-time experienced staff versus MSA's summer intern. Committee approved MSA for roundabout and Clark Dietz for Bladel. Motion passed unanimously.
