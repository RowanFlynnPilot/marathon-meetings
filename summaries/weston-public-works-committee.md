## Meeting Overview
The Public Works Committee approved several contracts and resolutions, including awarding a $126,900 recycled asphalt crushing contract to King Gravel, recommending approval of a state transportation funding resolution, and establishing a $100/day fine for sanitary sewer discharge violations. The committee also learned the village secured a local road improvement grant for Birch Street reconstruction.

## Key Discussions
### CIP Update
Michael reported the village received a local road improvement grant for Birch Street reconstruction between Schofield Avenue and Community Center Drive, a project estimated at $1.5 million with over 50% grant funding. The project will likely be moved from 2029 to 2028. Michael noted the village has secured over $9 million in grant funding over the past 4-5 years. Discussion also covered Weston Avenue road repairs by Integrity Grading and Excavating for settling issues over sewer trenches, with the committee emphasizing the contractor must return if problems recur. A special assessment meeting for Bladel Avenue was scheduled for Thursday, May 28 at 6:00 PM.

### Street Operations Update
Staff reported spring activities including road limit removal, mailbox and sign repairs, curb line sweeping, and hot mix patching. Significant dewatering was required near Weston Avenue due to flooding, with discussion about potential permanent pump solutions. Two culverts failed on Jones Street requiring replacement. Equipment maintenance and changeover from winter to summer operations was detailed. The committee discussed the need for better water management solutions in the Zenzer/Shore Avenue area.

### Utility Operations Update
Josh reported on water service repairs, well maintenance, and significant clear water infiltration issues in the sanitary sewer system. In April, 37 million gallons of water entered the distribution system but 53-54 million gallons were treated at the wastewater plant - approximately 15 million gallons of clear water infiltration. The Kathleen lift station showed clear evidence of sump pump discharge into sanitary sewers. An INI study with in-pipe technologies will help identify problem areas.

### 2025 Water Utility PSC Annual Report
Michael presented the annual report showing net operating income of $482,911 for 2025, consistent with prior year. Water loss was 6%. Utility value increased to nearly $34 million with $6.7 million in infrastructure placed in service. The utility has approximately $6.8 million in cash reserves and serves about 6,100 customers total. Motion to acknowledge passed unanimously.

### Resolution 2026-014 Transportation Funding
Michael explained this League of Wisconsin Municipalities resolution supports long-term transportation funding solutions at the state level. He noted grant funding helps one-time costs but recurring operational funding remains inadequate, requiring general tax subsidies. The committee voted unanimously to recommend village board adoption of Resolution 2026-014.

### Recycled Asphalt Base Course Production
Michael presented quotes for crushing recycled concrete and asphalt at the Ryan Street site. King Gravel submitted the low bid at $4.23/ton for an estimated 30,000 tons totaling $126,900. Analysis showed this saves approximately $11 per cubic yard compared to purchasing commercially, resulting in about $130,000 savings across 2026 projects. Motion to award to King Gravel passed unanimously.

### Sanitary Sewer Discharge Violation Discussion
Staff sought guidance on fines for illegal sump pump and clear water discharges to the sanitary system. Current code allows fines of $100-$1,000 per day. The committee discussed distinguishing between unknowing and willful violations. After discussion, the committee directed staff to create enforcement language with fines not exceeding $100 per day for first offenses. Motion passed unanimously.

### Radkey Avenue Water/Sewer Connection
Michael explained a builder wants to construct a duplex on a lot without direct main frontage. Staff proposed allowing a temporary long lateral connection until Radkey Avenue reconstruction extends mains. The committee added language requiring reconstruction no later than 2029. Motion to recommend approval with the 2029 deadline passed unanimously.

### Military Road Construction Agreement
The committee reviewed an intergovernmental agreement with Rothschild for Military Road construction. Weston's share for water main relocation is estimated at $94,860. The water main must be relocated due to storm sewer conflicts. Motion to recommend approval passed unanimously.

### Construction Services Proposals
Michael presented proposals for construction inspection services. For the Alderson/Jelinek roundabout project, MSA bid $37,315 versus Clark Dietz at $48,910. For Bladel Avenue, MSA bid $70,400 versus Clark Dietz at $73,730. Staff recommended MSA for the roundabout and Clark Dietz for Bladel due to having full-time experienced staff versus an intern. Motion to approve both recommendations passed unanimously.
