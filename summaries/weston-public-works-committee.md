**Organization:** Village of Weston

**Summarized:** 2026-05-29 18:37 UTC

## Meeting Overview
The Public Works Committee approved several infrastructure projects including awarding a $126,900 recycled asphalt crushing contract to King Gravel, authorizing a water/sewer connection agreement for a Radkkey Avenue duplex, approving a $94,860 construction agreement with Rothschild for Military Road improvements, and selecting construction service consultants for upcoming road projects. The committee also discussed strategies to address sanitary sewer violations from residents illegally discharging sump pump water into the sewer system.

## Key Discussions
### CIP Update
Michael reported the village received a Local Road Improvement Grant for Birch Street reconstruction between Schofield Avenue and Community Center Drive, covering over 50% of the estimated $1.5 million project. This 2029 project will likely move to 2028. He noted the village has received over $9 million in grant funding over the past 4-5 years. Discussion included updates on Weston Avenue repairs by Integrity Grading, Schofield Avenue punch list items with HOS Construction, and the retention pond phosphorus modeling showing increased benefit for Weston but decreased benefit for Rothschild.

### Street Operations Update
Staff reported on spring activities including removing road limits with Marathon County, mailbox and sign repairs, dewatering operations near Weston Avenue tributary for approximately one week, curb line sweeping, hot mix patching, and storm damage cleanup from East Nick. Culvert failures on Jones Street required installation of a third 18-inch culvert. Vehicle maintenance included removing plow equipment and servicing mowers. The committee discussed ongoing flooding issues near Zenzer Road and potential permanent pump station solutions.

### Utility Operating Update
Josh reported on water service repairs at Caribou Coffee, well maintenance, and meter card maintenance. Sanitary lift stations experienced high activity due to clear water infiltration. Data showed April treated 53.7 million gallons versus 37 million gallons pumped into the system - indicating roughly 15 million gallons of clear water infiltration. Staff identified sump pump discharges as a major contributor, with one Kathleen lift station house causing continuous pumping. The in-pipe technologies I&I study will help identify problem areas.

### 2025 Water Utility PSC Annual Report
Michael presented the annual report showing net operating income of $482,911 for 2025, similar to 2024's $490,000. Water loss was 6%. Utility value reached nearly $34 million, an increase of 6.7% due to infrastructure including Wells 7 and 8 and water main from the Weston Avenue project. The utility serves 6,100 customers total with 5,250 in Weston, 830 in Rothschild, and 5 in other areas. PFAS settlement funds of $1.44 million were received. Motion to acknowledge passed unanimously.

### Resolution 2026-014 Transportation Funding
Michael presented a League of Wisconsin Municipalities sample resolution supporting long-term transportation funding solutions. He noted the village has decreased public works budgets recently and that Wisconsin remains affordable compared to neighboring states for automobile ownership. Motion to adopt resolution 2026-014 passed unanimously.

### Recycled Asphalt Base Course Production
Michael explained the Ryan Street recycling operation where concrete and asphalt are crushed into base course. King Gravel submitted the low bid at $4.23/ton versus $4.25/ton from competitor. Estimated 30,000 tons equals $126,900. Analysis showed savings of nearly $11/cubic yard compared to City of Wausau's costs, totaling approximately $130,000 savings across three 2026 projects. Motion to award to King Gravel passed unanimously.

### Sanitary Sewer Discharge Violation Discussion
Staff sought guidance on fining residents who illegally discharge sump pumps into sanitary sewers. The municipal code allows fines of $100-$1,000 per day per violation. Committee members distinguished between unknowing violations (which should receive notice first) versus willful violations (higher fines). Discussion included requiring proof of correction after violations. The committee directed staff to create enforcement language starting at $100/day for violations. Motion passed unanimously.

### Water and Sewer Connection Agreement - Radkkey Avenue
Michael presented an agreement for a duplex at 2230 Radkkey Avenue to temporarily connect via long lateral service until Radkkey Avenue reconstruction. Committee member Zagami requested adding a date requirement, settling on 'no later than 2029' for reconstruction. The property owner will pay assessment when mains are extended. Motion to recommend approval with the 2029 deadline passed unanimously.

### Military Road Construction Agreement
The agreement with Village of Rothschild covers Military Road construction from Wolfman Street to Helin Street. Steam Construction is the contractor. Weston's share for utility work (sanitary sewer $6,156 and water main $88,704) totals $94,860. Water main must be relocated due to storm sewer improvements. Motion to recommend approval passed unanimously.

### Construction Service Proposals
Michael presented proposals for construction inspection services. For Alderson/Jelnik intersection roundabout: MSA at $37,315 (recommended) versus Clark Dietz at $48,910. For Bladel Avenue: MSA at $70,400 versus Clark Dietz at $73,730 (recommended due to full-time experienced staff versus MSA's summer intern). Motion to select MSA for roundabout and Clark Dietz for Bladel passed unanimously.
