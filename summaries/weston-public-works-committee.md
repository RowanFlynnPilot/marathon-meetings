## Meeting Overview
The Public Works Committee approved several significant items including a resolution supporting state transportation funding, awarded a $126,900 recycled asphalt crushing contract to King Gravel, and directed staff to create enforcement language for sewer discharge violations with fines up to $100/day. The committee also approved construction agreements for Military Road utilities and construction services for the Alderson/Jelinek roundabout and Bladel Avenue projects.

## Key Discussions
### CIP Update
Michael reported the village received a local road improvement grant for Birch Street reconstruction (Schofield Avenue to Community Center Drive), covering over 50% of the estimated $1.5 million project. This 2029 project may be moved to 2028. Michael noted the village has secured over $9 million in grant funding over the past 4-5 years. Discussion also covered Weston Avenue repairs by Integrity Grading, Schofield Avenue repairs by HOS Construction, and Fuller Street repairs. A special assessment meeting for Bladel Avenue was tentatively scheduled for Thursday, May 28 at 6:00 PM.

### Street Operations Update
Staff reported spring road maintenance including mailbox repairs, sign repairs, and curb line sweeping. Significant flooding issues required dewatering operations on Weston Avenue tributary for approximately a week. Discussion of potential permanent pump solutions for recurring flooding near Crane Meadows area. Culverts failed on Jones Street requiring replacement. Equipment winterization changeover and maintenance was detailed.

### Utility Operations Update
Josh reported on water service repairs at Caribou Coffee and ongoing well maintenance. Significant infiltration/inflow issues were identified - April showed 37 million gallons pumped into the system but 53-54 million gallons treated at the wastewater plant, indicating approximately 15 million gallons of clear water infiltration. The Kathleen lift station showed evidence of sump pump discharges into sanitary sewers. In-pipe technologies contractor will begin INI study to identify problem areas.

### 2025 Water Utility PSC Annual Report
Michael presented the annual report showing net operating income of $482,911 for 2025, consistent with prior year's $490,000. Water loss was 6%. Utility value increased to nearly $34 million due to infrastructure additions including Wells 7 and 8. PAS settlement funds of $1.44 million were received. The utility serves 6,100 customers including 5,250 in Weston and 830 in Rothschild. Motion to acknowledge passed unanimously.

### Resolution 2026-014 Transportation Funding
Michael explained this League of Wisconsin Municipalities sample resolution requests state legislature support for long-term transportation funding. Current state funding doesn't adequately cover local road maintenance, snow plowing, and recurring costs, requiring subsidy from general tax dollars. Wisconsin remains relatively affordable compared to neighboring states for automobile ownership costs. Motion to adopt passed unanimously.

### Recycled Asphalt Base Course Production
Michael presented the recycling program at the Ryan Street site, noting King Gravel submitted the low bid at $4.23/ton (vs. $4.25 from competitor) for crushing 30,000 tons, totaling $126,900. Analysis showed this saves approximately $11/cubic yard compared to City of Wausau costs, representing about $130,000 in savings across this year's projects. Motion to award to King Gravel passed unanimously.

### Sanitary Sewer Discharge Violation Discussion
Following discussion of clear water infiltration issues, staff sought guidance on enforcement fines. Current code allows fines of $100-$1,000 per day per violation. Committee discussed distinguishing between unknowing and willful violations. Committee member Zagami suggested waiting for the INI study results, then sending violation letters with 30-60 day correction periods. Motion passed directing staff to create enforcement language with fines not to exceed $100/day.

### Water and Sewer Connection Agreement - Radkey Avenue
Michael explained a duplex developer at 2230 Radkey Avenue needs temporary water/sewer connection since mains don't extend to the lot. Agreement allows long lateral service until Radkey Avenue reconstruction, when the owner will pay assessment for main extension. Committee directed that language specify reconstruction no later than 2029. Motion passed unanimously with the 2029 deadline amendment.

### Military Road Construction Agreement
Agreement with Village of Rothschild for utility work on Military Road reconstruction from Wolfman Street to Helin Street. Weston's share is $94,860 for sanitary sewer ($6,156) and water main ($88,704) relocation needed due to storm sewer conflicts. Rothschild holds the contract with Steam Construction. Motion to approve passed unanimously.

### Construction Services Proposals
Michael presented proposals for construction inspection services. For Alderson/Jelinek roundabout, MSA proposed $37,315 vs. Clark Dietz at $48,910; staff recommended MSA. For Bladel Avenue, MSA proposed $70,400 vs. Clark Dietz at $73,730; staff recommended Clark Dietz for the additional $3,000 due to having full-time experienced staff versus MSA's summer intern. Motion to approve MSA for roundabout and Clark Dietz for Bladel passed unanimously.
