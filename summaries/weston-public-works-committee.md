## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Public Works Committee met on May 11, 2026 and approved several items including a resolution supporting state transportation funding, awarded a $126,900 recycled asphalt crushing contract to King Gravel, approved construction agreements for Military Road utilities and Radkkey Avenue water/sewer connections, and directed staff to create enforcement language for sanitary sewer discharge violations with fines starting at $100/day.

## Key Discussions
### CIP Update
Michael reported the village received a Local Road Improvement Program grant for Birch Street reconstruction between Schofield Avenue and Community Center Drive, originally a 2029 project that will likely move to 2028. The grant covers over 50% of the approximately $1.5 million project. Michael noted the village has received over $9 million in grant funding over the past 4-5 years. Updates were also provided on Weston Avenue repairs by Integrity Grading, Schofield Avenue punch list items with HOS Construction, and Fuller Street restoration with Melvin. A special assessment meeting for Bladel Avenue was scheduled for Thursday, May 28th at 6:00 PM.

### Street Operations Update
Staff reported on spring activities including removing road limits on April 29th, mailbox and sign repairs, dewatering operations along Weston Avenue tributary for approximately one week, spring curb line sweeping, and hot mix patching. Storm damage from East Nick required tree cleanup. Culverts failed on Jones Street requiring installation of a third 18-inch pipe. Shop repairs included winterizing plow trucks, tractor and mower maintenance, and various equipment servicing. A new tandem axle chassis has been ordered with upfitter items for the first truck.

### Utility Operations Update
Josh reported on water service repairs at Caribou Coffee, well maintenance, and private well sampling. Sanitary side experienced significant clear water infiltration issues with lift stations running overtime. Data showed 37 million gallons pumped into the water system in April but 53-54 million gallons treated at sanitary facilities - approximately 15 million gallons of clear water infiltration. Staff identified homes illegally discharging sump pumps into sanitary sewers. The INI study with in-pipe technologies will help identify problem areas. Water main flushing notices going out with work starting next week. Well 2 rehab at Carey started today.

### 2025 Water Utility PSC Annual Report
Michael presented the annual Public Service Commission report showing operating revenues of approximately $3 million, net operating income of $482,911, and water loss at 6%. The utility value increased to nearly $34 million with $6.7 million in infrastructure placed in service. The utility serves 6,100 customers total with 5,250 in Weston and 830 in Rothschild. PAS settlement funds of $1.44 million were received. The committee moved to acknowledge the report and it passed unanimously.

### Resolution 2026-014 State Transportation Funding
Michael explained this League of Wisconsin Municipalities resolution supports long-term sustainable funding for state transportation, noting that current grant funding helps one-time costs but recurring annual payments for street maintenance and snow plowing remain underfunded. The committee moved to recommend village board adoption of Resolution 2026-014, which passed unanimously.

### Recycled Asphalt Base Course Production
Michael presented quotes for crushing recycled material at the Ryan Street site. King Gravel submitted the low bid at $4.23/ton for an estimated 30,000 tons totaling $126,900. Analysis showed village cost of $17.11/cubic yard versus City of Wausau paying $28/cubic yard - saving approximately $130,000 across three 2026 projects. The committee unanimously approved awarding the contract to King Gravel.

### Sanitary Sewer Discharge Violation Discussion
Staff sought guidance on enforcing violations for illegal clear water discharge into sanitary sewers. Current ordinance allows fines of $100-$1,000 per day. Committee members discussed distinguishing between unknowing versus willful violations. Trustee Zagami suggested waiting for the INI study results, then sending violation letters with 30-60 day correction periods. The committee unanimously directed staff to create enforcement language with fines not to exceed $100 per day for first offenses.

### Radkkey Avenue Water/Sewer Connection Agreement
Michael explained a builder wants to construct a duplex on lot 1 which currently lacks water/sewer main frontage. Staff proposed allowing a temporary long lateral connection until Radkkey Avenue is reconstructed, at which time the owner would pay assessment for main extension. Committee members requested adding a specific date, agreeing on 'no later than 2029.' Motion passed unanimously to recommend board approval with the added timeline.

### Military Road Construction Agreement
Michael presented an intergovernmental agreement with Village of Rothschild for Military Road reconstruction. Weston's share covers utility relocation at estimated $94,860 ($6,156 sanitary sewer, $88,704 water main). Steam Construction is the contractor. The water main requires relocation due to storm sewer conflicts. Motion passed unanimously to recommend board approval.

### Construction Service Proposals
Michael presented proposals for construction inspection services. For the Alderson/Jelnik roundabout project, MSA proposed $37,315 versus Clark Dietz at $48,910 - MSA recommended. For Bladel Avenue, MSA proposed $70,400 versus Clark Dietz at $73,730, but Michael recommended Clark Dietz for the extra $3,000 due to having experienced full-time staff rather than summer interns. Committee unanimously approved MSA for the roundabout and Clark Dietz for Bladel.
