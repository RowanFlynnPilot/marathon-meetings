## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Public Works Committee met on May 11, 2026, approving minutes and reports, awarding a $126,900 recycled asphalt crushing contract to King Gravel, and recommending board approval for construction agreements related to Military Road and water/sewer connections. The committee also directed staff to develop enforcement language for sanitary sewer discharge violations at $100/day fines.

## Key Discussions
### CIP Update
Michael reported the village received a Local Road Improvement Grant for Birch Street reconstruction between Schofield Avenue and Community Center Drive, originally a 2029 project that will likely move to 2028. The grant covers over 50% of the estimated $1.5 million project. Michael noted the village has secured over $9 million in grant funding in the past 4-5 years. Discussion also covered Weston Avenue repairs by Integrity Grading and Excavating for road settlement issues, and upcoming Schofield Avenue manhole repairs by HOS Construction.

### Approval of Minutes and Permits
Motion to approve April 13, 2026 meeting minutes passed unanimously. Motion to acknowledge April 2026 water and sewer permits passed unanimously.

### Street Operations Update
Staff reported road limits were removed April 29th along with Marathon County. Activities included mailbox/sign repairs, dewatering operations near Weston Avenue tributary for about a week, spring curb sweeping, hot mix patching, storm damage cleanup from East Nick area, inlet repairs on Birch, and signing installation on Schofield Avenue. Culvert failures on Jones Street required installing a third 18-inch culvert. Shop repairs included seasonal equipment changeover from winter to summer operations.

### Utility Operations Update
Josh reported on a service repair at Caribou Coffee, well maintenance, and significant clear water infiltration issues. April data showed 37 million gallons pumped into the system but 53-54 million gallons treated at wastewater facility, indicating approximately 15 million gallons of clear water infiltration. Staff identified sump pump discharges into sanitary sewers as a major contributor. The in-pipe technologies INI study will help identify problem areas. Water main flushing notices going out with work starting next week.

### 2025 Water Utility PSC Annual Report
Michael presented the annual report showing net operating income of $482,911 for 2025, consistent with 2024's $490,000. Water loss was 6%. Utility value reached nearly $34 million, up 6.7% due to infrastructure additions including Wells 7 and 8, and water main from Weston Avenue project. The utility serves 6,100 customers total: 5,250 in Weston, 830 in Rothschild, and 5 in other areas. Motion to acknowledge passed unanimously.

### Resolution 2026-014 Transportation Funding
Michael explained this League of Wisconsin Municipalities sample resolution supports long-term transportation funding solutions, noting the village has decreased public works budgets recently and needs state support beyond one-time grants. Wisconsin remains affordable compared to neighboring states for automobile ownership. Motion to recommend board adoption passed unanimously.

### Recycled Base Course Production
King Gravel was low bidder at $4.23/ton for crushing recycled asphalt at the Ryan Street site, with estimated 30,000 tons totaling $126,900. Michael noted savings of approximately $11/cubic yard compared to City of Wausau costs, translating to about $130,000 savings across three 2026 projects. Motion to award contract to King Gravel passed unanimously.

### Sanitary Sewer Discharge Violations
Committee discussed enforcement of ordinance violations for clear water discharge into sanitary sewers. Current code allows fines of $100-$1,000 per day. Committee members distinguished between unknowing violations (first offense with correction period) versus willful violations. After discussion, committee directed staff to create enforcement language starting at $100/day for violations. Motion passed unanimously.

### Radkkey Avenue Water/Sewer Connection
Michael requested approval for temporary water/sewer connection for a new duplex at 2230 Radkkey Avenue via long lateral until Radkkey Avenue reconstruction. Committee added language requiring reconstruction no later than 2029 when permanent mains would be extended and assessed. Motion to recommend board approval with 2029 deadline passed unanimously.

### Military Road Construction Agreement
Agreement with Village of Rothschild for Military Road reconstruction from Wolfman Street to Helin Street, with Steam Construction as contractor. Weston's share for utility relocation (water main and sanitary sewer) estimated at $94,860. Motion to recommend board approval passed unanimously.

### Construction Services Proposals
Michael presented proposals for construction inspection services. For Alderson/Jelnik intersection project, MSA proposed $37,315 versus Clark Dietz at $48,910; MSA recommended due to lower cost and roundabout design involvement. For Bladel Avenue, MSA proposed $70,400 versus Clark Dietz at $73,730; Clark Dietz recommended despite slightly higher cost due to full-time experienced staff versus MSA's summer intern. Motion to approve MSA for roundabout and Clark Dietz for Bladel passed unanimously.
