## Meeting Overview
The Public Works Committee approved several infrastructure projects and discussed utility operations. Key actions included awarding a recycled asphalt crushing contract to King Gravel for $126,900, approving a resolution supporting state transportation funding, and directing staff to develop enforcement language for sanitary sewer discharge violations with fines of $100 per day.

## Key Discussions
### CIP Update
Michael reported the village received a Local Road Improvement Grant for Birch Street reconstruction between Schofield Avenue and Community Center Drive, covering about 50% of the estimated $1.5 million project. This 2029 project will likely move to 2028. Michael noted the village has received over $9 million in grant funding over the past 4-5 years. Discussion also covered Weston Avenue repairs by Integrity Grading, Schofield Avenue repairs by HOS Construction, and Fuller Street repairs. A Bladel Avenue special assessment meeting was scheduled for May 28, 2026 at 6:00 PM.

### Street Operations Update
Staff reported on road limit removal, mailbox and sign repairs, spring curb line sweeping, and hot mix patching. Dewatering operations were conducted near Weston Avenue tributary for approximately one week due to flooding. Jones Street culvert failures required installation of a third 18-inch culvert. Equipment maintenance included removing plow systems and servicing mowers. Kennedy Park Farmers Market parking received positive feedback with no reported issues.

### Utility Operations Update
Josh reported on water service repair at Caribou Coffee, well maintenance, and private well sampling. Significant clear water infiltration was identified, with April showing 37 million gallons pumped into the system but 53-54 million gallons treated at the wastewater plant - approximately 15 million gallons of clear water infiltration. Staff identified sump pumps illegally connected to sanitary sewers as a major contributor. Water main flushing notices were being mailed with work starting the following week.

### 2025 Water Utility PSC Annual Report
Michael presented the annual Public Service Commission report showing net operating income of $482,911 for 2025, consistent with 2024's $490,000. Water loss was 6%. Utility value increased to nearly $34 million with $6.7 million in infrastructure placed in service. The utility serves approximately 6,100 customers total: 5,250 in Weston, 830 in Rothschild, and 5 in Schofield/Rib Mountain Metro area. Motion to acknowledge passed unanimously.

### Resolution 2026-014 Transportation Funding
Michael explained this League of Wisconsin Municipalities resolution supports long-term transportation funding solutions. He noted the village has decreased public works budgets recently and grant funding helps one-time costs but not recurring expenses like street maintenance and snow plowing. Wisconsin is relatively affordable for automobile ownership compared to neighboring states. Motion to recommend village board adoption passed unanimously.

### Recycled Asphalt Crushing Contract
Michael presented quotes for crushing recycled material at the Ryan Street site. King Gravel submitted the low bid at $4.23 per ton with estimated 30,000 tons totaling $126,900. Michael noted this saves approximately $11 per cubic yard compared to purchasing material, estimating $130,000 in savings across three 2026 projects. Motion to award to King Gravel passed unanimously.

### Sanitary Sewer Discharge Violations
Staff sought guidance on enforcing violations for illegal sump pump connections to sanitary sewers. Current ordinance allows fines of $100 to $1,000 per day. Committee discussed distinguishing between unknowing and willful violations. Staff was directed to create enforcement language starting at $100 per day for first offenses. An INI study by In-Pipe Technologies will help identify problem areas. Motion passed unanimously.

### Water and Sewer Extension Agreement - Radkey Avenue
Michael presented agreement for a new duplex at 2230 Radkey Avenue to temporarily connect to water and sewer mains via long lateral service until Radkey Avenue reconstruction. Committee added requirement that reconstruction occur no later than 2029, at which time the owner will pay assessment for main extension. Motion passed unanimously.

### Military Road Construction Agreement
The committee approved an intergovernmental agreement with Village of Rothschild for construction on Military Road from Wolfman Street to Helin Street. Weston's share covers water main relocation ($88,704) and sanitary sewer ($6,156) totaling $94,860. Steam Construction is the contractor. Motion passed unanimously.

### Construction Services Proposals
Michael presented proposals for construction inspection services. For Alderson/Jelinek intersection project, MSA was selected at $37,315 over Clark Dietz ($48,910). For Bladel Avenue project, Clark Dietz was selected at $73,730 over MSA ($70,400) due to having full-time staff rather than summer interns. Motion passed unanimously.
