## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Public Works Committee met on May 11, 2026, approving minutes and reports, awarding a $126,900 recycled asphalt crushing contract to King Gravel, adopting a resolution supporting state transportation funding, and directing staff to establish $100/day fines for illegal sanitary sewer discharges. The committee also approved construction agreements for Military Road utilities and construction services for upcoming road projects.

## Key Discussions
### CIP Update
Michael reported the village received a Local Road Improvement Grant for Birch Street reconstruction between Schofield Avenue and Community Center Drive, originally a 2029 project that will likely move to 2028. The grant covers over 50% of the estimated $1.5 million project cost. He noted the village has received over $9 million in grant funding over the past 4-5 years. Discussion included Weston Avenue road repairs by Integrity Grading and Excavating for settling over sewer trenches, and upcoming repairs on Schofield Avenue and Fuller Street for manhole issues and concrete pitting.

### Street Operations Update
Staff reported road limits were lifted April 29th. Crews performed dewatering operations near Weston Avenue tributary for approximately one week due to flooding. A culvert failure on Jones Street required installation of a third 18-inch culvert. Equipment maintenance included removing plow equipment from trucks and servicing mowers. Two Mac tandem axle chassis have been ordered with upfitter items. Salt supply discussed - 1,500 tons ordered for November delivery with current stock adequate.

### Utility Operations Update
Josh reported significant clear water infiltration issues, with April showing 37 million gallons pumped into the system but 53-54 million gallons treated at the wastewater plant - approximately 15 million gallons of clear water. Kathleen lift station showed clear water from sump pump discharge. In-pipe Technologies will conduct INI study when dry weather permits. Well 2 rehab started that day with Carry, and tower chemical cleaning planned.

### 2025 Water Utility PSC Annual Report
The water utility reported net operating income of $482,911 for 2025, consistent with 2024's $490,000. Water loss was 6%. Utility value increased to nearly $34 million with $6.7 million in infrastructure placed in service. PAS settlement funds of $1.44 million received. Utility serves approximately 6,100 customers - 5,250 in Weston, 830 in Rothschild. Motion to acknowledge passed unanimously.

### Resolution 2026-014 Transportation Funding
Michael presented the League of Wisconsin Municipalities sample resolution supporting long-term transportation funding solutions. He noted the village has decreased public works budgets recently and Wisconsin remains affordable compared to neighboring states for vehicle ownership. Motion to adopt Resolution 2026-014 passed unanimously.

### Recycled Asphalt Crushing Contract
King Gravel submitted the low bid at $4.23/ton for crushing approximately 30,000 tons of recycled material at Ryan Street site, totaling $126,900. PJ and Michael's Materials also bid at $4.25/ton. Michael noted this saves approximately $11/cubic yard compared to City of Wausau costs, representing about $130,000 in savings on 2026 projects. Motion to award to King Gravel passed unanimously.

### Sanitary Sewer Discharge Violation Discussion
Committee discussed enforcement of illegal sump pump discharges to sanitary sewers contributing to 15 million gallons of clear water treatment costs. Josh noted one Kathleen station resident admitted to illegal discharge. Committee debated fine amounts between $100-$1,000/day. Consensus reached on $100/day for first offenses, with escalation for willful violations. Motion directing staff to create enforcement language with fines not exceeding $100/day passed unanimously.

### Water and Sewer Connection Agreement - Radkey Avenue
Michael presented agreement allowing temporary water/sewer connection for a duplex at 2230 Radkey Avenue via long lateral until Radkey Avenue reconstruction. Committee added language requiring reconstruction no later than 2029. Motion to recommend approval with 2029 deadline passed unanimously.

### Military Road Construction Agreement
Agreement with Village of Rothschild for utility work on Military Road (Wolfman to Helin Street). Weston's share is $94,860 for sanitary sewer ($6,156) and water main ($88,704) relocation due to storm sewer conflicts. Steam Construction is the contractor. Motion to recommend approval passed unanimously.

### Construction Services Proposals
For Alderson/Jelinek intersection roundabout, MSA proposed $37,315 versus Clark Dietz at $48,910 - MSA recommended due to lower cost and roundabout design involvement. For Bladel Avenue, MSA proposed $70,400 versus Clark Dietz at $73,730 - Clark Dietz recommended due to full-time experienced inspector versus MSA's summer intern. Motion to approve both recommendations passed unanimously.
