## Meeting Overview
The Public Works Committee approved several routine items and made key decisions including awarding a $126,900 recycled asphalt crushing contract to King Gravel, adopting a resolution supporting state transportation funding, establishing $100/day fines for sanitary sewer discharge violations, and approving construction agreements for multiple road projects. The committee also received updates on CIP projects including a $800,000+ grant award for Birch Street reconstruction.

## Key Discussions
### CIP Update
Michael reported the village received a Local Road Improvement Grant for Birch Street reconstruction between Scofield Avenue and Community Center Drive, covering over 50% of the estimated $1.5 million project cost. This 2029 project will likely be moved up to 2028. Michael noted the village has received over $9 million in grant funding over the past 4-5 years. Updates were provided on Weston Avenue repairs by Integrity Grading, Scofield Avenue repairs by HOS Construction, and Fuller Street repairs by Melvin. A special assessment meeting for Bladel Avenue was tentatively scheduled for May 28th at 6:00 PM.

### Street Operations Update
Staff reported on spring activities including road limit removal on April 29th, mailbox and sign repairs, dewatering operations near Weston Avenue tributary for approximately one week, curb line sweeping, hot mix patching, storm damage cleanup from East Nick area, inlet repairs on Birch Street, and signing installation on Scofield Avenue. Culvert failures on Jones Street required installation of a third 18-inch culvert. Equipment maintenance included removing plow equipment and applying anti-rust treatment for summer operations.

### Utility Operations Update
Josh reported on water service repairs at Caribou Coffee, well maintenance, and meter card maintenance. Significant clear water infiltration was identified - April showed 37 million gallons pumped into the system but 53-54 million gallons treated, indicating approximately 15 million gallons of clear water entering the sanitary system. Staff identified sump pump discharges as a major contributor, particularly at the Kathleen lift station. The INI study with In-Pipe Technologies will help identify problem areas.

### 2025 Water Utility PSC Annual Report
The committee acknowledged the annual report showing net operating income of approximately $482,911 for 2025, consistent with 2024's $490,000. Water loss was 6%. The utility's value increased to nearly $34 million with $6.7 million in infrastructure placed in service. Cash reserves total $6.8 million. The utility serves 6,100 customers including 5,250 in Weston and 830 in Rothschild. Motion to acknowledge passed unanimously.

### Resolution 2026-014 Transportation Funding
Michael presented a League of Wisconsin Municipalities sample resolution supporting long-term transportation funding solutions. He noted recurring annual costs for street maintenance and snow plowing aren't adequately funded by the state, requiring general tax dollar subsidies. Wisconsin remains affordable compared to neighboring states for automobile ownership costs. The committee unanimously recommended the village board adopt the resolution.

### Recycled Base Course Production
Michael explained the Ryan Street site stockpiles recycled concrete and asphalt that gets crushed into base course. King Gravel submitted the low bid at $4.23/ton for an estimated 30,000 tons totaling $126,900. Cost analysis showed the village saves approximately $11 per cubic yard compared to City of Wausau costs, resulting in about $130,000 savings across three 2026 projects. Motion to award to King Gravel passed unanimously.

### Sanitary Sewer Discharge Violation Discussion
The committee discussed enforcement of ordinance violations for illegal sump pump discharges to the sanitary sewer. After debate about willful versus unknowing violations, the committee directed staff to create enforcement language with fines starting at $100/day. Trustee Zagami suggested waiting for the In-Pipe Technologies study to identify violators, then sending notices with 30-60 day correction periods. Motion passed unanimously to direct staff to create enforcement language not to exceed $100/day.

### Water and Sewer Connection Agreement - Radkkey Avenue
Michael presented an agreement allowing a duplex developer at 2230 Radkkey Avenue to temporarily connect via long lateral service until Radkkey Avenue reconstruction, when they would pay assessment for main extension. Committee amended the agreement to specify reconstruction no later than 2029 due to existing asbestos water main and poor road condition. Motion passed unanimously with the amendment.

### Military Road Construction Agreement
The committee approved an intergovernmental agreement with Village of Rothschild for construction on Military Road from Wolfman Street to Helin Street. Weston's share for water and sewer relocation is estimated at $94,860. Steam Construction is the contractor. Motion passed unanimously.

### Construction Service Proposals
Michael presented proposals for construction inspection services. For the Alderson/Jelnik roundabout project, MSA bid $37,315 versus Clark Dietz at $48,910. For Bladel Avenue, MSA bid $70,400 versus Clark Dietz at $73,730. Staff recommended MSA for the roundabout and Clark Dietz for Bladel due to using full-time experienced staff rather than summer interns. Motion passed unanimously to approve both recommendations.
