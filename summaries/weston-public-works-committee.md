## Meeting Overview
The Public Works Committee approved several infrastructure projects and discussed ongoing road maintenance, utility work, and enforcement of sewer discharge violations. Key actions included awarding a recycled asphalt crushing contract to King Gravel for $126,900, approving a resolution supporting state transportation funding, and directing staff to create violation language for illegal sewer discharges at $100/day fines.

## Key Discussions
### CIP Update
Michael reported the village received a local road improvement grant for Birch Street reconstruction between Schofield Avenue and Community Center Drive, covering over 50% of the estimated $1.5 million project cost. The project will likely move from 2029 to 2028. Michael noted the village has received over $9 million in grant funding over the past 4-5 years. Discussion also covered upcoming repairs on Weston Avenue by Integrity Grading and Excavating, and pavement/manhole repairs on Schofield Avenue by HOS Construction.

### Street Operations Update
Staff reported on spring activities including road limit removal, mailbox and sign repairs, curb line sweeping, and hot mix patching. Significant flooding required dewatering operations near Weston Avenue for approximately one week. Culvert failures on Jones Street required installation of a third 18-inch culvert. Equipment maintenance included removing plow equipment and servicing mowers and tractors.

### Utility Operations Update
Josh reported on water service repairs, well maintenance, and sanitary sewer issues. Significant clear water infiltration was identified, with April showing 37 million gallons pumped into the system but 53-54 million gallons treated at the treatment plant - indicating approximately 15 million gallons of clear water infiltration. The committee discussed the upcoming INI study by In-Pipe Technologies to identify infiltration sources. Kennedy Park Farmers Market received positive feedback regarding parking and traffic flow.

### 2025 Water Utility PSC Annual Report
Michael presented the annual report showing net operating income of approximately $482,911 for 2025, consistent with 2024's $490,000. Water loss was 6%. The utility's total value reached nearly $34 million, an increase of 6.7% due to infrastructure additions including wells 7 and 8. The utility serves approximately 6,100 customers total, with 5,250 in Weston and 830 in Rothschild. Motion to acknowledge passed unanimously.

### Resolution 2026-014 State Transportation Funding
Michael explained this League of Wisconsin Municipalities resolution seeks improved transportation funding for day-to-day operations, noting that current grant funding helps one-time costs but recurring annual payments for street maintenance and snow plowing remain underfunded. Wisconsin was noted as relatively affordable compared to neighboring states for automobile ownership costs. Motion to adopt passed unanimously.

### Recycled Base Course Production Contract
Staff sought quotes for crushing recycled concrete and asphalt at the Ryan Street site. King Gravel submitted the low bid at $4.23/ton for an estimated 30,000 tons totaling $126,900. Michael explained this saves approximately $11/cubic yard compared to City of Wausau costs, resulting in approximately $130,000 savings across three 2026 projects. Motion to award to King Gravel passed unanimously.

### Sanitary Sewer Discharge Violation Discussion
Committee discussed enforcement of violations where residents discharge sump pumps into sanitary sewers. Josh reported significant clear water infiltration is increasing treatment costs. The committee debated fine amounts, with the code allowing $100-$1,000/day. Members distinguished between unknowing versus willful violations. Motion passed to direct staff to create enforcement language with fines not exceeding $100/day for violations.

### Water and Sewer Connection Agreement - Radkey Avenue
Michael presented an agreement allowing a duplex at 2230 Radkey Avenue to temporarily connect via long lateral until Radkey Avenue reconstruction. Committee added language requiring reconstruction no later than 2029, at which time the owner will pay assessment for main extension. Motion passed unanimously.

### Military Road Construction Agreement
The committee approved an intergovernmental agreement with Village of Rothschild for Military Road construction from Wolfman Street to Helin Street. Weston's utility costs (sanitary sewer and water main) total $94,860. Steam Construction will perform the work. Motion passed unanimously.

### Construction Services Proposals
Michael presented proposals for construction inspection services. For Alderson/Jelinek roundabout project, MSA was selected at $37,315 (lower than Clark Dietz's $48,910). For Bladel Avenue, Clark Dietz was recommended at $73,730 despite being slightly higher than MSA's $70,400, due to having full-time experienced staff rather than summer interns. Motion passed unanimously.
