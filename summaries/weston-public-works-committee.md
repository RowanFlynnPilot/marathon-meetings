## Meeting Overview
The Public Works Committee approved several key items including a resolution supporting state transportation funding, awarded a $126,900 recycled asphalt crushing contract to King Gravel, and directed staff to develop enforcement language for sanitary sewer discharge violations at $100/day. The committee also approved construction agreements for multiple road projects and utility connections.

## Key Discussions
### CIP Update
Michael reported that the village received a local road improvement grant for Birch Street reconstruction between Schofield Avenue and Community Center Drive, originally a 2029 project now likely moving to 2028. The grant covers over 50% of the approximately $1.5 million project. Michael noted the village has received over $9 million in grant funding over the past 4-5 years. Discussion included Weston Avenue repairs by Integrity Grading and Excavating for road depression issues over the sanitary sewer trench, with understanding repairs may need to continue if settlement recurs.

### Street Operations Update
Staff reported on spring operations including road limit removal on April 29th, mailbox and sign repairs, and dewatering operations near Weston Avenue tributary for approximately one week. Discussion addressed flooding issues in the Crane Meadows area with potential permanent pump solutions being explored. Crews completed curb line sweeping, hot mix patching, storm damage cleanup from East Nick area, and inlet repairs before Birch Street reconstruction.

### Utility Operations Update
Josh reported on water service repair at Caribou Coffee, well maintenance items, and sanitary sewer issues including pump maintenance and power failures at lift stations. Significant discussion focused on clear water infiltration - in April, 37 million gallons entered the system but 53-54 million gallons were treated, indicating approximately 15 million gallons of clear water from infiltration or illegal sump pump connections. The committee discussed the upcoming INI study by In-Pipe Technologies to identify problem areas.

### 2025 Water Utility PSC Annual Report
Michael presented the annual Public Service Commission report showing net operating income of $482,911 for 2025, consistent with 2024's $490,000. Water loss was 6%. The utility value increased to nearly $34 million with $6.7 million in infrastructure placed in service. The utility has approximately 6,100 total customers with 5,250 in Weston and 830 in Rothschild. Motion to acknowledge passed unanimously.

### Resolution 2026-014 Transportation Funding
Michael explained this League of Wisconsin Municipalities sample resolution supports long-term funding solutions for state transportation. The resolution addresses the need for recurring annual funding for street maintenance and snow plowing rather than just one-time grant funding. The village has decreased its public works budget recently. Motion to recommend adoption passed unanimously.

### Recycled Base Course Production
Michael presented the recycling program at the Ryan Street site where concrete and asphalt are crushed into base course. King Gravel submitted the low bid at $4.23/ton for an estimated 30,000 tons totaling $126,900 (two cents lower than the next bidder at $4.25). Analysis showed this saves approximately $11 per cubic yard compared to City of Wausau costs, representing about $130,000 savings across three 2026 projects. Motion to award to King Gravel passed unanimously.

### Sanitary Sewer Discharge Violation Discussion
Staff sought guidance on enforcing violations for illegal sump pump connections to sanitary sewers. Current code allows fines of $100-$1,000 per day. Committee discussed distinguishing between unknowing violations (corrected with notice) versus willful violations. Trustee Zagami recommended $100/day starting point, noting it adds up quickly. Staff will bring cost analysis of treatment expenses to future meeting. Motion to direct staff to create enforcement language at $100/day maximum passed unanimously.

### Water and Sewer Connection Agreement - Radkey Avenue
Michael presented an agreement for a duplex at 2230 Radkey Avenue to temporarily connect via long lateral until Radkey Avenue reconstruction when mains will be extended. Committee directed adding specific date language that reconstruction occur no later than 2029. Motion passed unanimously with amendment.

### Military Road Construction Agreement
The committee approved an intergovernmental agreement with Village of Rothschild for Military Road construction from Wolfman Street to Helin Street. Weston's share for sanitary sewer ($6,156) and water main ($88,704) totals $94,860. Steam Construction is the contractor. Motion passed unanimously.

### Construction Services Proposals
Michael presented proposals for construction inspection services. For Alderson/Jelinek intersection project, MSA bid $37,315 versus Clark Dietz at $48,910 - MSA recommended. For Bladel Avenue, MSA bid $70,400 versus Clark Dietz at $73,730 - Clark Dietz recommended due to having full-time experienced staff rather than summer intern. Motion to approve both recommendations passed unanimously.
