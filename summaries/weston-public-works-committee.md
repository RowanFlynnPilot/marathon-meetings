## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Public Works Committee approved several contracts and agreements including recycled asphalt crushing to King Gravel for $126,900, a transportation funding resolution, and construction services for upcoming road projects. The committee also discussed enforcement of sanitary sewer discharge violations, directing staff to create language for $100/day fines for violations.

## Key Discussions
### CIP Update
Michael reported the village received a Local Road Improvement Grant for Birch Street reconstruction between Schofield Avenue and Community Center Drive, originally planned for 2029 but likely moving to 2028. The grant covers over 50% of the estimated $1.5 million project. Michael noted the village has received over $9 million in grant funding over the past 4-5 years. Discussion also covered Weston Avenue repairs by Integrity Grading and Excavating for settlement issues, and upcoming manhole repairs on Schofield Avenue by HOS Construction.

### Street Operation Update
Staff reported road limits were lifted on April 29th. Spring activities included mailbox repairs, sign repairs, curb line sweeping, and hot mix patching. Storm damage cleanup was done on East Nick. A culvert failure on Jones Street required installation of a third 18-inch culvert. Equipment maintenance was detailed including changeover from winter to summer operations. Staff reported an additional 1,500 tons of salt ordered for next season.

### Utility Operating Update
Josh reported significant clear water infiltration into the sanitary system, with April showing 37 million gallons pumped into distribution but 53-54 million gallons treated at the wastewater facility - approximately 15 million gallons of clear water. Discussion focused on sump pumps illegally connected to sanitary sewers. An INI study using In-Pipe Technologies will help identify problem areas. The committee discussed enforcement strategies for violations.

### 2025 Water Utility PSC Annual Report
Michael presented the annual report showing operating revenues of approximately $3 million with net operating income of $482,911, consistent with 2024's $490,000. Water loss was 6%. The utility value increased to nearly $34 million with $6.7 million in infrastructure placed in service. The utility has approximately 6,100 customers. Approved unanimously to acknowledge.

### Resolution 2026-014 Transportation Funding
Michael explained this League of Wisconsin Municipalities sample resolution supports improved state transportation funding for local operations. The resolution notes Wisconsin has lower automobile ownership costs than neighboring states. The committee voted to recommend village board adoption. Approved unanimously.

### Recycled Base Course Production
Michael reported King Gravel submitted the low bid at $4.23/ton for crushing approximately 30,000 tons of recycled material, totaling $126,900. Analysis showed the village saves approximately $11/cubic yard compared to City of Wausau costs, representing about $130,000 savings on 2026 projects. Motion to award contract to King Gravel passed unanimously.

### Sanitary Sewer Discharge Violation Discussion
The committee discussed enforcement of sewer discharge violations, particularly sump pumps illegally connected to sanitary sewers. Current code allows fines of $100-$1,000 per day. The committee directed staff to create enforcement language with fines at $100/day for violations, distinguishing between unknowing and willful violations. Approved unanimously.

### Water and Sewer Connection Agreement for Radkey Avenue
Michael presented an agreement allowing a duplex at 2230 Radkey Avenue to temporarily connect via long lateral service until Radkey Avenue reconstruction, at which point they would pay assessment for main extension. The committee added language specifying reconstruction no later than 2029. Motion passed unanimously.

### Military Road Construction Agreement
Michael presented an intergovernmental agreement with Village of Rothschild for Military Road construction. Weston's share for utility work is $94,860 (sanitary sewer $6,156; water main $88,704). Steam Construction is the contractor. Motion to recommend board approval passed unanimously.

### Construction Services for Alderson/Jelinek and Bladel Projects
Michael recommended MSA for Alderson/Jelinek roundabout project construction services at $37,315, and Clark Dietz for Bladel Avenue project at $73,730 due to having experienced full-time staff rather than interns. Both recommendations approved unanimously.

No public comment was offered.
