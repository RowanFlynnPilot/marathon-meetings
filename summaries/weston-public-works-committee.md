## Meeting Overview
The Public Works Committee approved several infrastructure projects and contracts, including a $126,900 recycled asphalt crushing contract, a resolution supporting state transportation funding, and construction service agreements for upcoming road projects. The committee also directed staff to develop enforcement language for sewer discharge violations at $100/day penalties.

## Key Discussions
### CIP Update
Michael reported the village received a Local Road Improvement Grant for Birch Street reconstruction between Scofield Avenue and Community Center Drive, originally a 2029 project now likely moving to 2028. The grant covers over 50% of the estimated $1.5 million project cost. Michael noted the village has secured over $9 million in grant funding over the past 4-5 years. Discussion also covered Weston Avenue repairs by Integrity Grading and Excavating for settlement issues, and upcoming Scofield Avenue repairs by HOS Construction for pavement markings and manhole issues.

### Street Operations Update
Staff reported road limits were removed April 29th along with Marathon County. Crews handled dewatering near Weston Avenue for about a week due to flooding, with discussion about potential permanent pump solutions. Spring curb line sweeping, hot mix patching, storm damage cleanup, inlet repairs, and culvert replacements on Jones Street were completed. Vehicle maintenance included removing plow equipment and servicing mowers. A new MAC tandem axle chassis has been ordered.

### Utility Operations Update
Josh reported a service repair at Caribou Coffee, well maintenance items, and continued private well sampling. Sanitary side experienced significant clear water infiltration, with April showing 37 million gallons pumped into the system but 53-54 million gallons treated - indicating approximately 15 million gallons of clear water infiltration. Staff identified sump pump discharges into sanitary sewers as a significant contributor. The in-pipe technologies INI study will help identify problem areas.

### 2025 Water Utility PSC Annual Report
Michael presented the annual report showing operating revenues of approximately $3 million, net operating income of $482,911 (slightly down from $490,000 in 2024), and 6% water loss. The utility value increased to nearly $34 million with $6.7 million in infrastructure placed in service. Total cash reserves are $6.8 million. Motion to acknowledge passed unanimously.

### Resolution 2026-014 Transportation Funding
Michael explained this League of Wisconsin Municipalities resolution supports long-term funding solutions for state transportation. The village has decreased public works budgets recently while grant funding helps with capital projects but not recurring operational costs. Motion to recommend village board adoption passed unanimously.

### Recycled Base Course Production
Michael presented the recycled asphalt crushing contract, with King Gravel as low bidder at $4.23/ton for an estimated 30,000 tons ($126,900 total). Analysis showed this saves approximately $11 per cubic yard compared to purchasing material, resulting in about $130,000 savings on 2026 projects. Motion to award contract to King Gravel passed unanimously.

### Sanitary Sewer Discharge Violations
Committee discussed enforcement of sewer discharge violations, particularly sump pump connections to sanitary sewers. Staff recommended establishing fines, with code allowing $100-$1,000 per day per violation. Committee member Jordan distinguished between unknowing versus willful violations. Committee directed staff to create enforcement language with $100/day penalty, starting after the in-pipe technology study identifies problem areas. Motion passed unanimously.

### Radkkey Avenue Water/Sewer Connection
Michael explained a duplex builder on lot 1 needs temporary water/sewer connection via long lateral until Radkkey Avenue reconstruction extends mains through outlot 1. Committee added language requiring reconstruction no later than 2029, with owner paying assessment at that time. Motion passed unanimously.

### Military Road Construction Agreement
Committee approved intergovernmental agreement with Village of Rothschild for Military Road construction from Wolfman Street to Helin Street. Weston's share for water main relocation and sanitary sewer is estimated at $94,860. Steam Construction is the contractor. Motion passed unanimously.

### Construction Services Proposals
Michael presented construction inspection proposals. For Alderson/Jelinek roundabout project, MSA was lower at $37,315 versus Clark Dietz at $48,910. For Bladel Avenue, MSA was $70,400 and Clark Dietz was $73,730, but Michael recommended Clark Dietz due to having full-time experienced staff versus an intern. Committee approved MSA for the roundabout and Clark Dietz for Bladel Avenue unanimously.

## Public Comment
No public comment was offered.
