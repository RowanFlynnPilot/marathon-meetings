## Meeting Overview
The Public Works Committee approved several infrastructure contracts and discussed ongoing road projects, utility operations, and water/sewer issues. Key actions included awarding a recycled asphalt crushing contract to King Gravel, approving a transportation funding resolution, and authorizing construction service agreements for upcoming road projects.

## Key Discussions
### CIP Update
Michael reported the village received a Local Road Improvement Grant for Birch Street reconstruction between Schofield Avenue and Community Center Drive, covering over 50% of the estimated $1.5 million project cost. This 2029 project will likely move to 2028. He noted the village has received over $9 million in grant funding over the past 4-5 years. Discussion also covered Weston Avenue repairs by Integrity Grading and Excavating for road depression issues, and upcoming repairs on Schofield Avenue and Fuller Street for manhole and pavement issues.

### Street Operations Update
Staff reported on spring activities including road limit removal, mailbox and sign repairs, curb line sweeping, and hot mix patching. Dewatering operations were conducted near Weston Avenue tributary for about a week due to flooding. Culverts failed on Jones Street requiring replacement. Discussion ensued about potential permanent pump solutions for recurring flooding issues near Crane Meadows area.

### Utility Operating Update
Josh reported on water service repairs, well maintenance, and sanitary system issues. Significant clear water infiltration was detected - April showed 37 million gallons pumped into the system but 53-54 million gallons treated at the wastewater plant, indicating approximately 15 million gallons of clear water infiltration. The INI study with In-Pipe Technologies will help identify sources. Kennedy Park Farmers Market received positive feedback with no traffic or parking complaints.

### 2025 Water Utility PSC Annual Report
Michael presented the annual report showing operating revenues of approximately $3 million and net income of $482,911 for 2025, consistent with 2024's $490,000. Water loss was 6%. The utility value increased to nearly $34 million with $6.7 million in infrastructure placed in service. The utility serves approximately 6,100 customers. Motion to acknowledge passed unanimously.

### Resolution 2026-014 Transportation Funding
Michael explained this League of Wisconsin Municipalities sample resolution supports long-term transportation funding solutions at the state level. The village has decreased its public works budget recently and seeks state assistance for recurring operational costs beyond one-time grants. Motion to adopt passed unanimously.

### Recycled Base Course Production
Michael presented the recycled asphalt crushing contract, noting King Gravel submitted the low bid at $4.23 per ton for an estimated 30,000 tons ($126,900 total). He demonstrated this practice saves approximately $11 per cubic yard compared to City of Wausau costs, totaling about $130,000 in savings across this year's three projects. Motion to award to King Gravel passed unanimously.

### Sanitary Sewer Discharge Violation Discussion
Staff sought guidance on enforcement of violations for residents discharging clear water (primarily sump pumps) into sanitary sewers. Current code allows fines of $100-$1,000 per day. Committee discussed distinguishing between unknowing and willful violations. Motion passed to direct staff to create enforcement language with fines not to exceed $100 per day, with enforcement to begin after the INI study identifies problem areas.

### Water and Sewer Connection Agreement - Radkey Avenue
Michael explained a vacant lot at 2230 Radkey Avenue needs temporary water/sewer connection via long lateral until Radkey Avenue reconstruction. Committee added requirement that reconstruction occur no later than 2029 when the property owner would pay assessment for main extension. Motion passed unanimously with the 2029 deadline addition.

### Military Road Construction Agreement
The committee approved an intergovernmental agreement with Village of Rothschild for Military Road construction. Weston's share for water and sanitary sewer work is estimated at $94,860. Steam Construction is the contractor. Motion passed unanimously.

### Construction Services Proposals
Michael presented proposals for construction inspection services. For Alderson/Jelinek roundabout project, MSA was selected at $37,315 (lower than Clark Dietz at $48,910). For Bladel Avenue project, Clark Dietz was recommended at $73,730 despite MSA's lower $70,400 bid because Clark Dietz proposed full-time experienced staff rather than a summer intern. Motion passed unanimously.

## Public Comment
No public comment was offered.
