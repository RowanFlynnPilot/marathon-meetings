## Meeting Overview
The Public Works Committee approved several routine items and made key decisions including awarding a $126,900 recycled asphalt crushing contract to King Gravel, adopting a resolution supporting state transportation funding, directing staff to create enforcement language for sewer discharge violations at $100/day fines, and recommending construction service agreements for upcoming road projects.

## Key Discussions
### CIP Update
Michael reported the village received a Local Road Improvement Grant for Birch Street reconstruction between Scoffield Avenue and Community Center Drive, covering over 50% of the estimated $1.5 million project cost. This 2029 project will likely be moved to 2028. Michael noted the village has secured over $9 million in grant funding over the past 4-5 years. Discussion also covered Weston Avenue repairs by Integrity Grading and Excavating for road settlement issues, and Scoffield Avenue repairs by HOS Construction for pavement marking and manhole issues.

### Street Operation Update
Staff reported on spring road activities including removing road limits on April 29th, mailbox and sign repairs, dewatering operations along a tributary near Weston Avenue for approximately one week due to flooding, curb line sweeping, hot mix patching, and storm damage cleanup from East Nick. Two culverts failed on Jones Street requiring additional repairs. Equipment maintenance and seasonal changeovers were detailed including servicing plow trucks and mowing equipment.

### Utility Operating Update
Josh reported on water service repairs at Caribou Coffee, ongoing well maintenance, and significant clear water infiltration into the sanitary sewer system. In April, the village put 37 million gallons into the water distribution system but treated nearly 54 million gallons of wastewater - approximately 15 million gallons of clear water infiltration. Staff identified homes discharging sump pumps into sanitary sewers, particularly affecting the Kathleen lift station. The INI study with In-Pipe Technologies will help identify problem areas.

### 2025 Weston Water Utility PSC Annual Report
Michael presented the annual report showing operating revenues of approximately $3 million with net operating income of $482,911, consistent with 2024's $490,000. Water loss was 6%. The utility value increased to nearly $34 million with $6.7 million in infrastructure placed in service. The utility has approximately 6,100 customers including 5,250 in Weston and 830 in Rothschild. Motion to acknowledge passed unanimously.

### Resolution 2026-014 Transportation Funding
Michael explained this League of Wisconsin Municipalities resolution supports long-term transportation funding solutions at the state level. He noted grant funding helps one-time costs but recurring annual payments for street maintenance and snow plowing remain underfunded, requiring subsidies from general tax dollars. Wisconsin remains affordable compared to neighboring states for automobile ownership costs. Motion to recommend village board adoption passed unanimously.

### Recycled Asphalt Crushing Contract
Michael presented quotes for crushing recycled concrete and asphalt at the Ryan Street site. King Gravel submitted the low bid at $4.23/ton for an estimated 30,000 tons totaling $126,900. This compares favorably to City of Wausau paying $28/cubic yard versus Weston's $17.11/cubic yard, saving approximately $130,000 across three projects this year. Motion to award contract to King Gravel passed unanimously.

### Sanitary Sewer Discharge Violation Discussion
Staff sought guidance on enforcement of sewer discharge violations, particularly sump pumps connected to sanitary sewers. Current code allows fines between $100-$1,000 per day. Committee discussed distinguishing between unknowing and willful violations. After discussion about the approximately $70,000+ in extra treatment costs from clear water infiltration, the committee directed staff to create enforcement language with fines not exceeding $100 per day. Motion passed unanimously.

### Water and Sewer Connection Agreement - Radkey Avenue
Michael explained a builder wants to construct a duplex on lot one of Radkey Avenue where water/sewer mains currently dead-end. Staff proposed allowing a temporary long lateral service until Radkey Avenue reconstruction, when the owner would pay assessment for main extension. Committee added language specifying reconstruction would occur no later than 2029. Motion to recommend approval with this addition passed unanimously.

### Military Road Construction Agreement
The committee reviewed an intergovernmental agreement with Village of Rothschild for Military Road construction from Wolfman Street to Helin Street. Steam Construction is the contractor. Weston's share for water and sanitary sewer work is estimated at $94,860. Water main relocation is needed due to storm sewer improvements. Motion to recommend approval passed unanimously.

### Construction Services Proposals
Michael presented proposals for construction inspection services. For the Alderson/Jelnik intersection roundabout project, MSA proposed $37,315 versus Clark Dietz at $48,910; staff recommended MSA. For Bladel Avenue, MSA proposed $70,400 versus Clark Dietz at $73,730; staff recommended Clark Dietz due to having full-time staff rather than a summer intern for the extra $3,000. Motion to approve both recommendations passed unanimously.
