## Meeting Overview
The Public Works Committee approved multiple infrastructure projects including awarding a $126,900 recycled asphalt crushing contract to King Gravel, adopted a resolution supporting state transportation funding, and directed staff to create enforcement language for sewer discharge violations with fines up to $100/day. The committee also approved construction agreements for the Military Road project and consultant services for upcoming roadway projects.

## Key Discussions
### CIP Update
Michael reported the village received a local road improvement grant for Birch Street reconstruction between Schofield Avenue and Community Center Drive, covering over 50% of the estimated $1.5 million project cost. This 2029 project will likely move to 2028. Michael noted the village has received over $9 million in grant funding over the past 4-5 years. Discussion also covered Weston Avenue repairs by Integrity Grading and Excavating for pavement settlement over sewer trenches, and upcoming repairs on Schofield Avenue and Fuller Street for manhole and concrete issues.

### Street Operations Update
Staff reported on spring activities including road limit removal, mailbox repairs, curb line sweeping, and storm damage cleanup from a tornado near East Nick. A culvert failure on Jones Street required installation of a third 18-inch pipe. Equipment maintenance and changeover from winter to summer operations was detailed. The committee discussed salt supply, confirming 1,500 tons ordered with delivery scheduled before November.

### Utility Operations Update
Josh reported significant clear water infiltration issues, with April showing 37 million gallons pumped into the system but 53-54 million gallons treated - approximately 15 million gallons of clear water. The Kathleen lift station showed evidence of sump pump discharge into sanitary sewers. An INI study using In-Pipe Technologies will help identify infiltration sources. Water main flushing notices were being mailed with work starting the following week.

### 2025 Water Utility PSC Annual Report
Michael presented the annual report showing net operating income of $482,911 for 2025, consistent with 2024's $490,000. Water loss was 6%. The utility value increased to nearly $34 million with $6.7 million in infrastructure placed in service. The utility serves 6,100 customers across Weston, Rothschild, Schofield, and Rib Mountain Metro. Motion to acknowledge passed unanimously.

### Resolution 2026-014 Transportation Funding
Michael explained this League of Wisconsin Municipalities resolution supports long-term transportation funding solutions. He noted the village has decreased public works budgets recently and Wisconsin remains affordable for automobile ownership compared to neighboring states. Motion to recommend village board adoption passed unanimously.

### Recycled Asphalt Crushing Contract
Michael presented quotes for crushing recycled material at the Ryan Street site. King Gravel was low bidder at $4.23/ton for an estimated 30,000 tons ($126,900 total). Analysis showed this saves approximately $11/cubic yard compared to Wausau's costs, totaling about $130,000 savings on 2026 projects. Motion to award to King Gravel passed unanimously.

### Sanitary Sewer Discharge Violation Discussion
Staff sought guidance on enforcement penalties for illegal sump pump connections to sanitary sewers. Current code allows fines of $100-$1,000 per day. Committee members distinguished between unknowing violations (first offense notification) and willful violations. After discussion, the committee directed staff to create enforcement language with fines not exceeding $100/day, with understanding that repeat offenders would face escalating consequences.

### Water and Sewer Connection Agreement - Radkey Avenue
Michael explained a duplex builder needs temporary water/sewer connections via long lateral services until Radkey Avenue reconstruction extends mains through Outlot 1. Committee added language requiring reconstruction 'no later than 2029.' Motion to recommend approval passed unanimously.

### Military Road Construction Agreement
Michael presented an intergovernmental agreement with Rothschild for utility work on Military Road between Wolfman and Helian Streets. Weston's share is $94,860 for water main relocation and sanitary sewer work. Steam Construction is the contractor. Motion to recommend approval passed unanimously.

### Construction Services Proposals
Michael presented consultant proposals for construction inspection. For Alderson/Jelinek roundabout: MSA at $37,315 (lower than Clark Dietz at $48,910). For Bladel Avenue: Clark Dietz at $73,730 preferred over MSA at $70,400 due to full-time experienced staff versus intern. Committee approved MSA for the roundabout and Clark Dietz for Bladel.
