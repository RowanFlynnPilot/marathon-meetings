## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Public Works Committee approved several contracts and agreements, including a $126,900 recycled asphalt crushing contract with King Gravel, a construction agreement with Village of Rothschild for Military Road improvements costing approximately $94,860, and construction service proposals for two road projects. The committee also directed staff to develop enforcement language for sanitary sewer discharge violations with fines starting at $100 per day.

## Key Discussions
### CIP Update
Michael reported the village received a Local Road Improvement Grant for Birch Street reconstruction between Schofield Avenue and Community Center Drive, covering over 50% of the estimated $1.5 million project. This 2029 project may be moved to 2028. He noted the village has received over $9 million in grant funding over the past 4-5 years. Updates were given on Weston Avenue repairs by Integrity Grading and Excavating, and upcoming repairs on Schofield Avenue and Fuller Street addressing manhole settlements.

### Street Operations Update
Staff reported road limits were removed April 29th. Significant dewatering operations occurred near Weston Avenue tributary for approximately one week due to heavy rain. Spring sweeping, patching with hot mix, storm damage cleanup, and culvert repairs on Jones Street were completed. Discussion ensued about potential permanent pump solutions for recurring flooding issues near Crane Meadows area.

### Utility Operations Update
Josh reported on water service repairs, well maintenance, and significant clear water infiltration issues. April sanitary flows showed 53-54 million gallons treated versus only 37 million gallons of water pumped into the system, indicating approximately 15 million gallons of clear water infiltration. Staff caught residents with sump pumps illegally connected to sanitary sewers. InPipe Technologies will be conducting an infiltration study.

### 2025 Water Utility PSC Annual Reports
Michael reviewed the annual report showing net operating income of $482,911 for 2025, consistent with the prior year. Water loss was 6%. The utility value reached nearly $34 million with $6.7 million in infrastructure placed in service. Additional non-operating revenue of $1.44 million came from PFAS settlement funds. Motion to acknowledge passed unanimously.

### Resolution 2026-014 Transportation Funding
The committee discussed a League of Wisconsin Municipalities resolution supporting long-term state transportation funding solutions. Michael noted local communities subsidize transportation costs with general tax dollars due to insufficient state funding. The committee voted to recommend village board adoption of Resolution 2026-014. Passed unanimously.

### Recycled Base Course Production
Michael presented the annual asphalt crushing contract, noting King Gravel was low bidder at $4.23/ton for an estimated 30,000 tons totaling $126,900. He demonstrated the program saves approximately $11 per cubic yard compared to City of Wausau costs, equating to roughly $130,000 savings on this year's projects. Motion to award to King Gravel passed unanimously.

### Sanitary Sewer Discharge Violations
Committee discussed enforcement of sewer discharge violations, particularly illegal sump pump connections. After debate about willful versus unknowing violations, the committee directed staff to develop enforcement language with fines of $100 per day for violations. Trustee Zagami suggested waiting for InPipe Technologies' study results before sending violation notices. Motion passed unanimously.

### Radkey Avenue Water/Sewer Connection Agreement
Michael presented an agreement allowing a duplex developer to temporarily connect to water and sewer mains via long lateral services until Radkey Avenue reconstruction. Committee amended the agreement to specify reconstruction would occur no later than 2029, at which time the property owner would pay assessments for main extensions. Motion passed unanimously.

### Military Road Construction Agreement
The committee reviewed an intergovernmental agreement with Village of Rothschild for Military Road construction. Weston's share for water main relocation and sanitary sewer work is estimated at $94,860. Steam Construction is the contractor. Motion to recommend board approval passed unanimously.

### Construction Services for Alderson/Jelnik and Bladel Projects
Michael presented proposals for construction inspection services. For Alderson/Jelnik intersection, MSA proposed $37,315 versus Clark Dietz at $48,910. For Bladel Avenue, MSA proposed $70,400 versus Clark Dietz at $73,730. Staff recommended MSA for the roundabout project and Clark Dietz for Bladel due to having full-time experienced staff versus an intern. Motion passed unanimously.

## Public Comment
No public comment was offered.
