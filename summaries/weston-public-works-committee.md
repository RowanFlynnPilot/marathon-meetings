## Meeting Overview
The Public Works Committee approved several infrastructure items including a resolution supporting state transportation funding, awarded a recycled asphalt crushing contract to King Gravel for $126,900, and directed staff to develop $100/day fines for illegal sewer discharge violations. The committee also approved construction agreements for Military Road utilities and inspection services for upcoming road projects.

## Key Discussions
### CIP Update
Michael reported the village received a local road improvement grant for Birch Street reconstruction between Scoffield Avenue and Community Center Drive, originally a 2029 project now likely moving to 2028. The grant covers about 50% of the estimated $1.5 million project cost. Michael noted the village has secured over $9 million in grant funding over the past 4-5 years. Discussion also covered ongoing repairs on Weston Avenue by Integrity Grading and Excavating to address pavement settling over sewer trenches, and upcoming repairs on Scoffield Avenue and Fuller Street for manhole and concrete issues.

### Utility Operations Update
Josh reported significant clear water infiltration into the sanitary sewer system, with April showing 37 million gallons pumped into the water system but 53-54 million gallons treated at the wastewater plant - approximately 15 million gallons of clear water. Staff identified sump pumps illegally connected to sanitary sewers at locations including the Kathleen lift station. An INI study with in-pipe technologies is planned to identify problem areas.

### 2025 Water Utility PSC Annual Report
Michael presented the annual report showing net operating income of $482,911, consistent with 2024's $490,000. Water loss is at 6%. The utility value increased to nearly $34 million due to well 7 and 8 additions and new water main installations. The utility serves 6,100 customers with 5,250 in Weston, 830 in Rothschild, and 5 in other areas. Motion to acknowledge passed unanimously.

### Resolution 2026-014 Transportation Funding
Michael explained this League of Wisconsin Municipalities resolution seeks improved state transportation funding for local road maintenance and operations beyond one-time grants. He noted Wisconsin remains affordable compared to neighboring states for automobile ownership. Motion to adopt passed unanimously.

### Recycled Base Course Production
Michael presented the recycling program at Ryan Street site, with King Gravel submitting the low bid at $4.23/ton versus $4.25 from competitor. Estimated 30,000 tons equals $126,900. Michael demonstrated savings of approximately $11/cubic yard compared to City of Wausau costs, totaling about $130,000 savings across three 2026 projects. Motion to award to King Gravel passed unanimously.

### Sanitary Sewer Discharge Violation Discussion
Committee discussed enforcement of illegal clear water discharge into sanitary sewers. Current code allows fines of $100-$1,000 per day per violation. Committee members Zagami and Jordan distinguished between unknowing versus willful violations. Committee directed staff to develop enforcement language starting at $100/day for first offenses. Motion passed unanimously.

### Water and Sewer Extension Agreement - Radkey Avenue
Michael proposed allowing a duplex builder at 2230 Radkey Avenue to temporarily connect via long lateral until Radkey Avenue reconstruction when mains would be extended. Committee directed adding language requiring reconstruction no later than 2029. Motion passed unanimously with the date amendment.

### Military Road Construction Agreement
Committee approved agreement with Village of Rothschild for Military Road reconstruction, with Weston responsible for water and sewer costs estimated at $94,860. Rothschild holds the contract with Steam Construction. Motion passed unanimously.

### Construction Services Proposals
Michael presented proposals for construction inspection services. For Alderson/Jelnik roundabout project: MSA at $37,315, Clark Dietz at $48,910 - MSA recommended. For Bladel Avenue: MSA at $70,400, Clark Dietz at $73,730 - Clark Dietz recommended due to having full-time experienced staff versus MSA's summer intern. Motion to approve both recommendations passed unanimously.
