## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Public Works Committee met on May 11, 2026 and approved several items including awarding a recycled asphalt crushing contract to King Gravel for $126,900, adopting a resolution supporting state transportation funding, and directing staff to develop $100/day fines for illegal sewer discharges. The committee also approved construction agreements for the Military Road project and construction services for upcoming road projects.

## Key Discussions
### CIP Update
Michael reported the village received a Local Road Improvement Grant for Birch Street reconstruction between Schofield Avenue and Community Center Drive, covering over 50% of the estimated $1.5 million project. This 2029 project will likely move to 2028. Michael noted the village has received over $9 million in grant funding over the past 4-5 years. Discussion also covered Weston Avenue settlement repairs by Integrity Grading and Excavating, and upcoming repairs on Schofield Avenue and Fuller Street for manhole and pavement issues.

### Street Operations Update
Staff reported road limits were removed April 29th along with Marathon County. Spring activities included mailbox repairs, sign repairs, curb line sweeping, and hot mix patching. Flooding issues required dewatering operations near Weston Avenue for about a week. Two culverts failed on Jones Street requiring replacement. Discussion focused on developing a permanent pump solution for recurring flooding issues in the Crane Meadows area. Kennedy Park Farmers Market received positive feedback with no parking or traffic issues reported.

### Utility Operations Update
Josh reported on water service repairs, well maintenance, and significant clear water infiltration issues. April data showed 37 million gallons pumped into the system but 53-54 million gallons treated at the wastewater plant - approximately 15 million gallons of clear water infiltration. Staff identified residents illegally connecting sump pumps to sanitary sewers. An INI study with in-pipe technologies will help identify infiltration sources.

### 2025 Water Utility PSC Annual Report
Michael reviewed the annual report showing net operating income of $482,911 for 2025, consistent with 2024's $490,000. Water loss is at 6%. Utility value increased to nearly $34 million with $6.7 million in infrastructure placed in service. The utility serves about 6,100 customers total - 5,250 in Weston, 830 in Rothschild. Cash reserves stand at $6.8 million. Motion to acknowledge passed unanimously.

### Resolution 2026-014 Transportation Funding
Michael explained this League of Wisconsin Municipalities sample resolution supports long-term transportation funding solutions at the state level. The resolution addresses the gap between grant funding for one-time projects versus ongoing operational needs like street maintenance and snow plowing. Wisconsin remains relatively affordable for automobile ownership compared to neighboring states. Motion to recommend adoption passed unanimously.

### Recycled Base Course Production
Michael presented the recycled asphalt crushing contract, with King Gravel as the low bidder at $4.23 per ton for an estimated 30,000 tons ($126,900 total). Analysis showed the village's recycling program saves approximately $11 per cubic yard compared to City of Wausau costs, resulting in about $130,000 savings across three projects this year. Motion to award to King Gravel passed unanimously.

### Sanitary Sewer Discharge Violation Discussion
Committee discussed enforcement of illegal sewer discharges, particularly sump pump connections to sanitary sewers contributing to the 15 million gallon clear water issue. Current code allows fines of $100-$1,000 per day per violation. Committee directed staff to develop enforcement language starting at $100 per day, distinguishing between unknowing first offenses (warning with correction period) and willful violations. Motion passed unanimously.

### Radkey Avenue Water/Sewer Connection
Michael requested approval for a temporary long lateral service connection for a new duplex at 2230 Radkey Avenue until Radkey Avenue is reconstructed. The agreement requires the property owner to pay assessment when mains are extended. Committee added language requiring reconstruction no later than 2029. Motion to recommend approval with the date condition passed unanimously.

### Military Road Construction Agreement
Michael presented the intergovernmental agreement with Village of Rothschild for water main relocation on Military Road. Steam Construction is the contractor. Village of Weston's share is $94,860 for sanitary sewer ($6,156) and water main ($88,704) work. The water main requires relocation due to storm sewer conflicts. Motion to recommend approval passed unanimously.

### Construction Services Proposals
Michael presented proposals for construction inspection services. For the Alderson/Jelinek roundabout project, MSA was recommended at $37,315 (lower than Clark Dietz at $48,910). For Bladel Avenue, Clark Dietz was recommended at $73,730 despite being slightly higher than MSA ($70,400) because MSA proposed using a summer intern while Clark Dietz would use full-time experienced staff. Motion to approve both recommendations passed unanimously.
