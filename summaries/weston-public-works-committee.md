## Meeting Overview
The Public Works Committee approved several key items including a resolution supporting state transportation funding, awarded a recycled asphalt crushing contract to King Gravel for $126,900, and directed staff to develop enforcement language for sanitary sewer discharge violations with fines starting at $100/day. The committee also approved construction agreements for Military Road utilities and consultant services for upcoming road projects.

## Key Discussions
### CIP Update
Michael reported the village received a Local Road Improvement Grant for Birch Street reconstruction between Schofield Avenue and Community Center Drive, covering over 50% of the estimated $1.5 million project. This 2029 project will likely move to 2028. The village has secured over $9 million in grant funding over the past 4-5 years. Discussion included Weston Avenue repairs by Integrity Grading and Excavating for road settlement issues, and Schofield Avenue punch list items with HOS Construction including manhole repairs and concrete work.

### Street Operation Update
Staff reported road limits were removed April 29th along with Marathon County. Spring activities included curb line sweeping, hot mix patching, storm damage cleanup from East Nick, inlet repairs on Birch, and signing installation on Schofield Avenue. Jones Street culvert failure required installing a third 18-inch culvert. Equipment changeover from winter to summer operations was detailed including removing plow equipment and servicing mowers and tractors.

### Utility Operating Update
Josh reported on service repairs at Caribou Coffee, well maintenance, and significant clear water infiltration issues. April data showed 37 million gallons pumped into the system but 53-54 million gallons treated at the wastewater plant - approximately 15 million gallons of clear water infiltration. The Kathleen lift station was identified as having significant clear water discharge, likely from sump pumps illegally connected to sanitary sewers. In-pipe Technologies will conduct an INI study to identify problem areas.

### 2025 Water Utility PSC Annual Report
Michael presented the annual Public Service Commission report showing net operating income of $482,911 for 2025, consistent with 2024's $490,000. Water loss was 6%. Utility value increased to nearly $34 million with $6.7 million in infrastructure placed in service. The utility has approximately $6.8 million in cash reserves. Approved unanimously on motion to acknowledge.

### Resolution 2026-014 Transportation Funding
Michael explained this League of Wisconsin Municipalities sample resolution supports long-term state transportation funding solutions. Current state funding doesn't cover recurring costs like street maintenance and snow plowing, requiring general tax subsidies. Wisconsin remains affordable compared to neighboring states for automobile ownership. Committee member thanked Michael for grant work. Motion to recommend village board adopt resolution passed unanimously.

### Recycled Asphalt Base Course Crushing
King Gravel submitted low bid of $4.23/ton for crushing approximately 30,000 tons of recycled material at the Ryan Street site, totaling $126,900. Michael demonstrated savings of approximately $11/cubic yard compared to City of Wausau costs, saving about $130,000 across three 2026 projects. Motion to award contract to King Gravel passed unanimously.

### Sanitary Sewer Discharge Violation Discussion
Staff sought guidance on fine amounts for illegal sump pump discharges to sanitary sewers. Current code allows fines of $100-$1,000 per day per violation. Committee members discussed distinguishing between unknowing and willful violations. Trustee Zagami suggested waiting for In-pipe Technologies study results, then sending violation letters with 30-60 day correction period. Committee agreed to start fines at $100/day. Motion to direct staff to create enforcement language not exceeding $100/day passed unanimously.

### Radkkey Avenue Water/Sewer Connection
A duplex builder at 2230 Radkkey Avenue needs temporary water/sewer connection via long laterals until Radkkey Avenue reconstruction extends mains through Outlot 1. Committee directed adding language that reconstruction occur no later than 2029. Motion to recommend agreement with assessment upon reconstruction passed unanimously.

### Military Road Construction Agreement
Agreement with Village of Rothschild for Military Road reconstruction from Wolfman Street to Helin Street. Rothschild holds contract with Steam Construction; Weston responsible for water ($88,704) and sanitary sewer ($6,156) totaling $94,860. Water main relocation needed due to storm sewer conflicts. Motion to recommend approval passed unanimously.

### Construction Service Proposals
Due to vacant engineering tech position, consultant proposals were sought for construction inspection. For Alderson/Jelinek roundabout project, MSA proposed $37,315 versus Clark Dietz at $48,910; MSA recommended. For Bladel Avenue, MSA proposed $70,400 versus Clark Dietz at $73,730; Clark Dietz recommended due to full-time experienced staff versus MSA's summer intern. Motion to approve MSA for roundabout and Clark Dietz for Bladel passed unanimously.
