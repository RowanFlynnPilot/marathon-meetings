## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Public Works Committee meeting covered CIP updates including a $785,000 DOT grant for Birch Street reconstruction, approved a $126,900 recycled asphalt crushing contract with King Gravel, and directed staff to create enforcement language for sanitary sewer discharge violations with fines of $100 per day. The committee also approved construction agreements for Military Road and consultant services for upcoming road projects.

## Key Discussions
### CIP Update
Michael reported the village received a Local Road Improvement Grant for Birch Street reconstruction between Schofield Avenue and Community Center Drive, covering about 50% of the estimated $1.5 million project cost. This 2029 project will likely be moved up to 2028. Michael noted the village has received over $9 million in grant funding over the past 4-5 years. Discussion also covered Weston Avenue repairs by Integrity Grading and Excavating for pavement settlement issues, and upcoming restoration work on Schofield Avenue and Fuller Street projects.

### Street Operation Update
Staff reported on spring activities including road limit removal, mailbox and sign repairs, spring sweeping, and hot mix patching. Significant flooding required dewatering operations along the Weston Avenue tributary for approximately one week. Discussion focused on developing a permanent pump solution for recurring flooding issues in the Zenzer/Shorey Avenue area. Culverts failed on Jones Street requiring additional repairs. Equipment maintenance and changeover from winter to summer operations was detailed.

### Utility Operating Update
Josh reported on service repairs, well maintenance, and lift station issues including power and phase failures. A major concern was identified: April showed 37 million gallons pumped into the distribution system but 53-54 million gallons treated at the wastewater plant, indicating approximately 15 million gallons of clear water infiltration/inflow. Staff identified residents illegally connecting sump pumps to sanitary sewers. The In-pipe Technologies INI study will help identify problem areas.

### 2025 Water Utility PSC Annual Report
Michael presented the annual Public Service Commission report showing net operating income of approximately $482,911 for 2025, consistent with 2024's $490,000. Water loss is at 6%. The utility value increased to nearly $34 million with $6.7 million in infrastructure placed in service. The utility serves approximately 6,100 customers total. The committee acknowledged the report unanimously.

### Resolution 2026-014 Transportation Funding
Michael explained this League of Wisconsin Municipalities sample resolution supports long-term funding solutions for state transportation. The resolution highlights that recurring annual transportation funding remains inadequate, requiring communities to subsidize with general tax dollars or implement wheel taxes. The committee recommended approval to the village board unanimously.

### Recycled Asphalt Base Course Production
Michael presented quotes for crushing recycled material at the Ryan Street site. King Gravel submitted the low bid at $4.23 per ton for an estimated 30,000 tons, totaling $126,900. Michael demonstrated the program saves approximately $11 per cubic yard compared to City of Wausau costs, resulting in about $130,000 savings across three 2026 projects. The committee unanimously approved awarding the contract to King Gravel.

### Sanitary Sewer Discharge Violation Discussion
Staff sought guidance on enforcing sewer ordinance violations for illegal sump pump discharges. Current code allows fines between $100 and $1,000 per day. Trustee Jordan distinguished between unknowing and willful violations. The committee directed staff to create enforcement language with fines not to exceed $100 per day, allowing time for the In-pipe Technologies study to identify problem areas before issuing violation notices. Motion passed unanimously.

### Water and Sewer Connection Agreement for Radkkey Avenue
Michael presented an agreement for a new duplex at 2230 Radkkey Avenue to temporarily connect via long lateral service until Radkkey Avenue is reconstructed. Committee members requested adding specific language that reconstruction occur no later than the 2029 construction season. The agreement requires the property owner to pay assessments when mains are extended. Motion passed unanimously with the added date requirement.

### Military Road Construction Agreement
Michael presented an intergovernmental agreement with Rothschild for Military Road construction. Weston's share for utility relocation is estimated at $94,860 ($61,560 for sanitary sewer, $88,740 for water main). Steam Construction is the contractor. Water main relocation is required due to conflicts with storm sewer improvements. The committee unanimously recommended approval to the village board.

### Construction Service Proposals
Michael presented consultant proposals for construction inspection services. For the Alderson/Jelnik roundabout project, MSA was selected at $37,315 (lower than Clark Dietz's $48,910). For Bladel Avenue, Clark Dietz was recommended at $73,730 despite being slightly higher than MSA's $70,400 because Clark Dietz proposed full-time staff rather than a summer intern. Both recommendations passed unanimously.
