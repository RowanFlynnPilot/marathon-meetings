## Meeting Overview
The Public Works Committee approved several items including a resolution supporting state transportation funding, awarded a $126,900 recycled asphalt crushing contract to King Gravel, and directed staff to develop $100/day fines for illegal sanitary sewer discharges. The committee also approved construction agreements for multiple road projects and acknowledged positive feedback on the Kennedy Park Farmers Market setup.

## Key Discussions
### CIP Update
Michael reported the village received a Local Road Improvement Grant for Birch Street reconstruction between Scoffield Avenue and Community Center Drive, covering over 50% of the estimated $1.5 million project cost. This 2029 project will likely move to 2028. Michael noted the village has received over $9 million in grant funding over the past 4-5 years. Discussion also covered Weston Avenue settlement repairs by Integrity Grading, Scoffield Avenue concrete and manhole repairs by HOS Construction, and Fuller Street restoration work.

### Street Operations Update
Staff reported on spring road maintenance including removing road limits April 29th, mailbox repairs, sign repairs, and shoulder work. Significant dewatering efforts were required along the Weston Avenue tributary due to high water levels threatening Crane Meadows area. Staff discussed potential permanent pump solutions. Spring curb sweeping, hot mix patching, and storm damage cleanup from East Nick area were completed. Culvert failures on Jones Street required installing a third 18-inch culvert.

### Utility Operations Update
Josh reported on water service repairs at Caribou Coffee, well maintenance, and ongoing private well sampling. Sanitary side experienced heavy pump station activity due to clear water infiltration. April showed 37 million gallons pumped into the system but 53-54 million gallons treated at the wastewater plant, indicating approximately 15 million gallons of clear water infiltration from sump pump discharges and groundwater intrusion. Staff identified specific violations on Kathleen Street lift station.

### 2025 Water Utility PSC Annual Report
Michael presented the annual Public Service Commission report showing net operating income of $482,911 for 2025, consistent with 2024's $490,000. Water loss was 6%. The utility value increased to nearly $34 million with $6.7 million in infrastructure placed in service. The utility serves approximately 6,100 customers total, with 5,250 in Weston and 830 in Rothschild. Motion to acknowledge passed unanimously.

### Resolution 2026-014 Transportation Funding
Michael explained this League of Wisconsin Municipalities resolution supports long-term sustainable funding for state transportation. He noted while grant funding helps one-time costs, recurring annual payments for street maintenance and snow plowing remain underfunded, requiring general tax dollar subsidies. Motion to recommend village board adoption passed unanimously.

### Recycled Base Course Production
Michael reported King Gravel submitted the low bid at $4.23/ton for crushing approximately 30,000 tons of recycled material at the Ryan Street site, totaling $126,900. He demonstrated cost savings showing the village's crushed material costs $17.11/cubic yard versus $28/cubic yard paid by City of Wausau, saving approximately $130,000 across three 2026 projects. Motion to award contract to King Gravel passed unanimously.

### Sanitary Sewer Discharge Violation Discussion
Josh and Michael discussed significant clear water infiltration into the sanitary system, largely from illegal sump pump connections. The committee debated fine amounts, with code allowing $100-$1,000 per day. Committee members distinguished between unknowing versus willful violations. Trustee Zagami suggested starting at $100/day, which adds up quickly. The committee directed staff to draft enforcement language with fines not to exceed $100/day. Motion passed unanimously.

### Water and Sewer Connection Agreement - Radkkey Avenue
Michael presented an agreement allowing a duplex at 2230 Radkkey Avenue to temporarily connect via long lateral until Radkkey Avenue reconstruction, when they would pay assessment for main extension. Committee added language specifying reconstruction no later than 2029. Motion to recommend approval passed unanimously.

### Military Road Construction Agreement
Michael presented an intergovernmental agreement with Village of Rothschild for Military Road reconstruction. Weston's share covers utility relocation at an estimated $94,860 ($6,156 sanitary sewer, $88,704 water main). Steam Construction is the contractor. Motion to recommend approval passed unanimously.

### Construction Services Proposals
Michael presented consultant proposals for construction inspection services. For Alderson/Jelnik intersection project, MSA proposed $37,315 versus Clark Dietz at $48,910; MSA recommended due to their roundabout design involvement. For Bladel Avenue, MSA proposed $70,400 and Clark Dietz $73,730; Clark Dietz recommended due to having full-time experienced staff versus MSA's summer intern. Motion to recommend MSA for roundabout and Clark Dietz for Bladel passed unanimously.
