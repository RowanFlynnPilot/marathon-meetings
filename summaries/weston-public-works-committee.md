## Meeting Overview
The Public Works Committee approved several infrastructure-related items including a resolution supporting state transportation funding, awarded a recycled asphalt crushing contract to King Gravel for $126,900, and directed staff to create enforcement language for sewer discharge violations with fines up to $100/day. The committee also approved construction agreements for Military Road and inspection services for upcoming road projects.

## Key Discussions
### CIP Update
Michael reported the village received a local road improvement grant for Birch Street reconstruction between Scofield Avenue and Community Center Drive, covering over 50% of the approximately $1.5 million project. This 2029 project will likely be moved up to 2028. Michael noted the village has received over $9 million in grant funding over the past 4-5 years. Discussion also covered Weston Avenue repairs by Integrity Grading and Excavating for settlement issues over sewer trenches, and upcoming repairs on Scofield Avenue and Fuller Street for manhole adjustments and concrete repairs.

### Street Operations Update
Staff reported on spring activities including removal of road limits on April 29th, mailbox and sign repairs, dewatering operations near Weston Avenue tributary for about a week due to flooding, and curb line sweeping. Hot mix patching has resumed. Storm damage from East Nick area required tree cleanup. Jones Street culvert failures required installation of a third 18-inch culvert. Equipment maintenance was detailed including winterizing plow trucks with fluid film anti-rust treatment and various vehicle servicing.

### Utility Operating Update
Josh reported on water service repairs at Caribou Coffee, well maintenance, and continued private well sampling. Sanitary system experienced significant clear water infiltration - April showed 37 million gallons pumped into system but 53-54 million gallons treated, indicating approximately 15 million gallons of clear water intrusion. Staff identified sump pump discharge violations at Kathleen lift station. Water main flushing notices going out with flushing starting next week. Well 2 rehab at Carey started today.

### 2025 Water Utility PSC Annual Reports
Michael presented the annual report showing net operating income of $482,911 for 2025, consistent with 2024's $490,000. Water loss is at 6%. Utility value increased to nearly $34 million with $6.7 million in infrastructure placed in service. The utility serves 6,100 customers total - 5,250 in Weston, 830 in Rothschild, and 5 in Scofield/Rib Mountain Metro. Cash on hand is $6.8 million. Motion to acknowledge passed unanimously.

### Resolution 2026-014 Transportation Funding
Michael explained this League of Wisconsin Municipalities resolution seeks long-term funding solutions for state transportation. While grant funding helps one-time costs, recurring funding for street maintenance and snow plowing is inadequate, requiring general tax subsidies. Wisconsin remains affordable for automobile ownership compared to neighboring states. Motion to recommend village board adopt resolution passed unanimously.

### Recycled Base Course Production
Michael presented the bid for crushing recycled concrete and asphalt at the Ryan Street site. King Gravel was low bidder at $4.23/ton for estimated 30,000 tons totaling $126,900. Analysis showed village saves approximately $11/cubic yard compared to City of Wausau's costs, resulting in about $130,000 savings across three 2026 projects. Motion to award to King Gravel passed unanimously.

### Sanitary Sewer Discharge Violation Discussion
Staff sought guidance on enforcement of sewer discharge violations, particularly sump pump discharges into sanitary sewers. Current code allows fines of $100-$1,000 per day. Committee discussed distinguishing between unknowing and willful violations. An upcoming I&I study by Inpipe Technologies will help identify problem areas. Committee directed staff to create enforcement language with fines not to exceed $100/day for first offenses. Motion passed unanimously.

### Radkey Avenue Water/Sewer Connection
Michael explained a builder wants to construct a duplex on a lot without direct main frontage. Staff proposed allowing a temporary long lateral connection until Radkey Avenue reconstruction, at which time the owner would pay assessment for main extension. Committee added language requiring reconstruction no later than 2029. Motion to recommend approval with the 2029 deadline passed unanimously.

### Military Road Construction Agreement
Michael presented intergovernmental agreement with Rothschild for Military Road construction from Wolfman Street to Helin Street. Weston's share for utility relocation is $94,860 ($6,156 sanitary, $88,704 water main). Steam Construction is the contractor. Water main relocation needed due to storm sewer conflicts. Motion to recommend approval passed unanimously.

### Construction Services for Alderson/Jelinek and Bladel Projects
Michael presented proposals for construction inspection services. For Alderson/Jelinek roundabout project, MSA proposed $37,315 versus Clark Dietz at $48,910 - staff recommended MSA. For Bladel Avenue, MSA proposed $70,400 and Clark Dietz $73,730 - staff recommended Clark Dietz for the extra $3,000 due to having full-time experienced staff versus MSA's summer intern. Motion to recommend MSA for roundabout and Clark Dietz for Bladel passed unanimously.
