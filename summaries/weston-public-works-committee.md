## Meeting Overview
The Public Works Committee approved several key items including a transportation funding resolution, awarded a $126,900 recycled asphalt crushing contract to King Gravel, and directed staff to create enforcement language for sewer discharge violations with fines of $100/day. The committee also approved construction service proposals and intergovernmental agreements for multiple road projects.

## Key Discussions
### CIP Update
Michael reported the village received a Local Road Improvement Grant for Birch Street reconstruction between Scoffield Avenue and Community Center Drive, originally a 2029 project that will likely move to 2028. The grant covers over 50% of the estimated $1.5 million project. Michael noted the village has received over $9 million in grant funding over the past 4-5 years. Discussion also covered Weston Avenue repairs by Integrity Grading and Excavating for pavement depression issues, and upcoming Scoffield Avenue repairs by HOS Construction for manhole and concrete issues.

### Street Operations Update
Staff reported road limits were removed April 29th with Marathon County. Work included mailbox and sign repairs, dewatering operations near Weston Avenue for about a week, spring curb sweeping, hot mix patching, storm damage cleanup from East Nick, inlet repairs before Birch road work, and signing installation on Scoffield Avenue. Culvert failures on Jones Street required installation of a third 18-inch culvert. Shop work included seasonal changeover of plow equipment and various vehicle maintenance.

### Utility Operations Update
Josh reported on water service repairs, well maintenance, and significant clear water infiltration into the sanitary sewer system. In April, 37 million gallons were pumped into the water system but 53-54 million gallons were treated at the wastewater plant - approximately 15 million gallons of clear water infiltration. Staff identified sump pumps illegally connected to sanitary sewers as a contributing factor. The committee discussed the upcoming INI study by In-Pipe Technologies to identify infiltration sources.

### 2025 Water Utility PSC Annual Report
Michael presented the annual report showing net operating income of $482,911 for 2025, similar to 2024's $490,000. Water loss is at 6%. The utility value increased to nearly $34 million with $6.7 million in infrastructure additions. The utility has approximately $6.8 million in cash reserves and serves about 6,100 customers total - 5,250 in Weston, 830 in Rothschild. Motion to acknowledge passed unanimously.

### Resolution 2026-014 Transportation Funding
Michael explained this League of Wisconsin Municipalities sample resolution supports long-term transportation funding solutions at the state level. The village has decreased public works budgets recently and grant funding helps one-time costs but not recurring maintenance expenses. Wisconsin is relatively affordable for automobile ownership compared to neighboring states. Motion to recommend village board adoption passed unanimously.

### Recycled Base Course Production
Michael reported King Gravel submitted the low bid at $4.23/ton for crushing approximately 30,000 tons of recycled material at the Ryan Street site, totaling $126,900. The village saves approximately $11/cubic yard compared to market prices, resulting in about $130,000 savings on the three 2026 projects. Motion to award to King Gravel passed unanimously.

### Sanitary Sewer Discharge Violation Discussion
Staff sought guidance on fining residents who discharge clear water (primarily sump pumps) into the sanitary sewer. Current code allows fines of $100-$1,000 per day. The committee discussed distinguishing between unknowing and willful violations. They agreed to start at $100/day after first warning. Staff will wait for INI study results, then send violation letters giving 30-60 days to correct before fines begin. Motion to direct staff to create enforcement language with $100/day fines passed unanimously.

### Water and Sewer Connection - Radkkey Avenue
A duplex developer at 2230 Radkkey Avenue needs temporary water/sewer connection via long lateral until Radkkey Avenue is reconstructed. The committee added language requiring reconstruction no later than 2029, at which point the property owner will pay assessment for main extension. Motion passed unanimously with the 2029 deadline added.

### Military Road Construction Agreement
The village will reimburse Rothschild for $94,860 in water and sewer work as part of Rothschild's Military Road project from Wolfman Street to Helin Street. Steam Construction is the contractor. The water main must be relocated due to storm sewer conflicts. Motion to approve the intergovernmental agreement passed unanimously.

### Construction Service Proposals
Staff sought construction inspection services for two projects. For Alderson/Jelnik intersection (roundabout), MSA proposed $37,315 vs Clark Deetz at $48,910 - staff recommended MSA. For Bladel Avenue, MSA proposed $70,400 vs Clark Deetz at $73,730 - staff recommended Clark Deetz due to having full-time experienced staff rather than summer interns. Motion to approve MSA for roundabout and Clark Deetz for Bladel passed unanimously.
