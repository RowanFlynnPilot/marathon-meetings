## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Public Works Committee approved minutes, acknowledged permits, discussed infrastructure updates including a $780,000+ grant for Birch Street reconstruction, and made several recommendations to the village board including a resolution supporting state transportation funding, contracts for asphalt crushing and road construction, and establishing $100/day fines for illegal sewer discharges.

## Key Discussions
### CIP Update
Michael reported the village received a Local Road Improvement Grant for Birch Street reconstruction between Schofield Avenue and Community Center Drive, covering over 50% of the estimated $1.5 million project cost. This 2029 project will likely move to 2028. Michael noted the village has received over $9 million in grant funding over the past 4-5 years. Discussion also covered Weston Avenue repairs by Integrity Grading and Excavating for road depression issues, and upcoming pavement/manhole repairs on Schofield Avenue and Fuller Street.

### Street Operations Update
Staff reported road limits were lifted April 29th. Activities included mailbox and sign repairs, dewatering operations near Weston Avenue tributaries to prevent flooding at Crane Meadows, spring curb sweeping, hot mix patching, storm damage cleanup from an East Nick area storm, culvert repairs on Jones Street, and extensive equipment maintenance. Discussion addressed need for permanent pump solutions for recurring flooding issues near Zenzer Drive and Shorey Avenue.

### Utility Operations Update
Josh reported on water service repairs, well maintenance, private well sampling, and lift station issues including pump maintenance and power failures. Significant clear water infiltration was identified - April showed 37 million gallons pumped into the system but 53-54 million gallons treated, indicating approximately 15 million gallons of clear water entering the sanitary system. Staff identified homeowners with sump pumps illegally connected to sanitary sewers. The INI study with In-Pipe Technologies will help identify problem areas.

### 2025 Water Utility PSC Annual Report
Michael presented the annual report showing operating revenues of approximately $3 million, net operating income of $482,911 (consistent with 2024's $490,000), and 6% water loss. The utility value increased to nearly $34 million with $6.7 million in infrastructure added. The utility serves approximately 6,100 customers total - 5,250 in Weston, 830 in Rothschild. Motion to acknowledge passed unanimously.

### Resolution 2026-014 Transportation Funding
Michael explained this League of Wisconsin Municipalities resolution supports long-term transportation funding solutions, noting grants help with one-time costs but recurring annual payments for maintenance and snow plowing are underfunded. Wisconsin remains affordable compared to neighboring states for automobile ownership costs. Motion to recommend village board adoption passed unanimously.

### Recycled Base Course Production
Michael reported King Gravel submitted the low bid of $4.23/ton for crushing recycled material at the Ryan Street site, with an estimated project total of $126,900 for 30,000 tons. Analysis showed this saves approximately $11/cubic yard compared to City of Wausau costs, totaling about $130,000 in savings for 2026 projects. Motion to award contract to King Gravel passed unanimously.

### Sanitary Sewer Discharge Violation Discussion
Committee discussed establishing enforcement for illegal sump pump connections to sanitary sewers. Josh explained the significant costs from clear water infiltration. Committee directed staff to create enforcement language with fines of $100/day, distinguishing between unknowing violations (warning first) versus willful violations. Motion passed unanimously.

### Water and Sewer Connection Agreement - Radkey Avenue
Michael explained a duplex builder needs temporary long lateral service to lot one on Radkey Avenue until the road is reconstructed. Committee added requirement that Radkey Avenue reconstruction occur no later than 2029, at which point the property owner will pay assessment for main extension. Motion to recommend approval passed unanimously.

### Military Road Construction Agreement
Committee approved intergovernmental agreement with Village of Rothschild for Military Road construction. Weston's share for utility work (sanitary sewer and water main) is $94,860. Steam Construction is the contractor. Motion to recommend approval passed unanimously.

### Construction Services Proposals
Michael presented proposals for construction inspection services. For Alderson/Jelinek roundabout project, MSA bid $37,315 versus Clark Dietz at $48,910 - MSA recommended. For Bladel Avenue, MSA bid $70,400 versus Clark Dietz at $73,730 - Clark Dietz recommended due to having full-time experienced staff rather than summer intern. Motion to recommend both awards passed unanimously.
