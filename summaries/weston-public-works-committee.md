## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Public Works Committee approved several items including awarding a recycled asphalt crushing contract to King Gravel for $126,900, directing staff to create enforcement language for sewer discharge violations with fines up to $100/day, and recommending approval of construction agreements for Military Road and two local street projects. The committee also acknowledged grant funding success with over $9 million received in the past 4-5 years.

## Key Discussions
### CIP Update
Michael reported the village received a Local Road Improvement Grant for Birch Street reconstruction between Scofield Avenue and Community Center Drive, originally a 2029 project that will likely move to 2028. The grant covers over 50% of the estimated $1.5 million cost. Michael noted the village has received over $9 million in grant funding over the past 4-5 years. Discussion also covered Weston Avenue repairs by Integrity Grading and Excavating for pavement settling over sanitary sewer trenches, and upcoming repairs on Scofield Avenue and Fuller Street for manhole adjustments and concrete work.

### Street Operation Update
Staff reported on spring activities including removal of road limits, mailbox and sign repairs, curb line sweeping, and hot mix patching. Flooding issues required dewatering pumps near Weston Avenue for about a week to protect Crane Meadows area. Committee discussed need for permanent pump solutions. Culverts failed on Jones Street requiring installation of a third 18-inch pipe. Shop repairs included transitioning plow trucks to summer use and servicing mowing equipment. A new tandem axle chassis has been ordered with upfitter items being coordinated through Scott's Repair.

### Utility Operating Update
Josh reported on water service repairs, well maintenance, and ongoing lift station issues. Significant clear water infiltration was identified - in April, 37 million gallons of water entered the system but 53-54 million gallons were treated, indicating approximately 15 million gallons of clear water infiltration. Staff identified homeowners with sump pumps illegally connected to sanitary sewers on smaller lift stations like Kathleen. The INI study by In-Pipe Technologies will help identify problem areas once dry weather arrives.

### 2025 Water Utility PSC Annual Report
Michael presented the annual report showing net operating income of $482,911 for 2025, consistent with 2024's $490,000. Water loss was 6%. Utility value increased to nearly $34 million with $6.7 million in infrastructure placed in service. The utility has approximately 6,100 customers total. PAS settlement funds of $1.44 million were received for future treatment costs. Motion to acknowledge passed unanimously.

### Resolution 2026-014 Transportation Funding
Michael explained this League of Wisconsin Municipalities sample resolution supports long-term transportation funding solutions. He noted grant funding helps one-time costs but recurring operational funding is inadequate, requiring general tax subsidies. Wisconsin remains affordable compared to neighboring states for automobile ownership. Motion to recommend village board adoption passed unanimously.

### Recycled Base Course Production
Michael presented the contract for crushing recycled concrete and asphalt at the Ryan Street site. King Gravel was low bidder at $4.23/ton for an estimated 30,000 tons totaling $126,900. Analysis showed the village saves approximately $11/cubic yard compared to City of Wausau costs, representing about $130,000 savings across this year's three projects. Motion to award to King Gravel passed unanimously.

### Sanitary Sewer Discharge Violation Discussion
Committee discussed enforcement of sewer ordinance violations for illegal sump pump connections. Current code allows fines of $100-$1,000 per day. Committee member Mumper distinguished between unknowing and willful violations. Committee directed staff to create enforcement language starting at $100/day for first offenses, with the understanding that repeat offenders would face escalating penalties. Staff will also include notices in monthly bills and newsletters. Motion passed unanimously.

### Radkey Avenue Water/Sewer Connection Agreement
Michael explained a duplex builder needs temporary water and sewer connection via long lateral through Outlot 1 until Radkey Avenue reconstruction. Committee added language requiring reconstruction and assessment payment no later than 2029. Motion to recommend approval with the timeline addition passed unanimously.

### Military Road Construction Agreement
Agreement with Village of Rothschild for Military Road reconstruction from Wolfman Street to Helin Street. Weston's utility costs are $94,860 ($6,156 sanitary sewer, $88,704 water main). Water main must be relocated due to storm sewer conflicts. Steam Construction is the contractor. Motion to recommend approval passed unanimously.

### Construction Services Proposals
Due to vacant engineering tech position, Michael sought proposals for construction inspection services. For Alderson/Jelnik roundabout project, MSA was selected at $37,315 over Clark Dietz's $48,910. For Bladel Avenue, Clark Dietz was recommended at $73,730 over MSA's $70,400 because MSA proposed using a summer intern while Clark Dietz offered full-time experienced staff. Motion to recommend both selections passed unanimously.
