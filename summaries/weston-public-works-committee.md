## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Public Works Committee met on May 11, 2026, approving several key items including a recycled asphalt crushing contract with King Gravel for $126,900, a resolution supporting state transportation funding, and construction service proposals for two road projects. The committee also discussed ongoing infrastructure repairs, grant funding successes totaling over $9 million in recent years, and directed staff to create enforcement language for sanitary sewer discharge violations with fines starting at $100 per day.

## Key Discussions
### CIP Update
Michael reported that the village received a Local Road Improvement Grant for Birch Street reconstruction between Schofield Avenue and Community Center Drive, originally a 2029 project, which will likely move to 2028. The grant covers over 50% of the estimated $1.5 million project cost. Michael noted the village has secured over $9 million in grant funding over the past 4-5 years. Discussion also covered Weston Avenue repairs by Integrity Grading and Excavating for pavement depression over a sanitary sewer trench, and ongoing repairs needed on Schofield Avenue and Fuller Street including manhole adjustments.

### Street Operation Update
Staff reported on seasonal activities including removal of road limits, mailbox and sign repairs, spring curb line sweeping, and hot mix patching. Dewatering operations were conducted near Weston Avenue tributary for approximately one week due to high water levels. Storm damage cleanup was performed after an event off East Nick. Culvert failures on Jones Street required installation of a third 18-inch culvert. Equipment maintenance included removing plow equipment, servicing mowers, and ordering a new Mac tandem axle chassis.

### Utility Operating Update
Josh reported on water service repairs at Caribou Coffee, well maintenance, and ongoing private well sampling. On the sanitary side, lift stations experienced significant activity due to high groundwater, with clear water infiltration being a major issue. April data showed 37 million gallons pumped into the system but 53-54 million gallons treated, indicating approximately 15 million gallons of clear water infiltration. The committee discussed that residents illegally connecting sump pumps to sanitary sewers contributes to this problem.

### 2025 Water Utility PSC Annual Reports
Michael presented the annual Public Service Commission report showing operating revenue of approximately $3 million and net operating income of $482,911 for 2025, consistent with 2024's $490,000. Water loss was 6%. The utility value increased to nearly $34 million with $6.7 million in infrastructure placed in service. Cash reserves stand at $6.8 million. Motion to acknowledge passed unanimously.

### Resolution 2026-014 Transportation Funding
Michael explained this League of Wisconsin Municipalities resolution supports long-term funding solutions for the state transportation fund. He noted that while the village receives grant funding for one-time costs, recurring annual funding for street maintenance and snow plowing remains inadequate, requiring subsidization from general tax dollars. The committee voted unanimously to recommend the village board adopt the resolution.

### Recycled Asphalt Base Course Production
Michael presented quotes for crushing recycled material at the Ryan Street site. King Gravel submitted the low bid at $4.23 per ton for an estimated 30,000 tons, totaling $126,900. Michael demonstrated this saves approximately $11 per cubic yard compared to purchasing material, saving about $130,000 on the three 2026 projects. Motion to award to King Gravel passed unanimously.

### Sanitary Sewer Discharge Violation Discussion
Staff sought guidance on fines for residents illegally discharging sump pumps into sanitary sewers. Current code allows fines of $100-$1,000 per day per violation. After discussion distinguishing between unknowing and willful violations, the committee directed staff to create enforcement language with fines starting at $100 per day. Staff will wait for the INI study results to identify problem areas before sending violation notices. Motion passed unanimously.

### Water and Sewer Connection Agreement - Radkkey Avenue
Michael explained that a builder wants to construct a duplex on lot one, which lacks frontage on a water main. Staff proposed allowing a temporary long lateral connection until Radkkey Avenue reconstruction, at which time the owner would pay assessment for main extension. The committee amended the agreement to specify reconstruction no later than 2029. Motion passed unanimously.

### Military Road Construction Agreement
The committee reviewed an intergovernmental agreement with Village of Rothschild for construction of Military Road from Wolfman Street to Helin Street. Weston's share for water and sanitary sewer work is $94,860. Steam Construction is the contractor. Motion to recommend approval passed unanimously.

### Construction Service Proposals
Michael presented proposals for construction inspection services. For the Alderson/Jelnik roundabout project, MSA proposed $37,315 versus Clark Dietz at $48,910; staff recommended MSA. For Bladel Avenue, MSA proposed $70,400 versus Clark Dietz at $73,730; staff recommended Clark Dietz for the additional $3,000 due to having full-time experienced staff rather than a summer intern. Both recommendations were approved unanimously.
