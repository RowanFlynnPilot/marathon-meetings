## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Public Works Committee approved several contracts and discussed ongoing infrastructure projects. Key actions included awarding a $126,900 recycled asphalt crushing contract to King Gravel, approving a $94,860 intergovernmental agreement with Rothschild for Military Road construction, and directing staff to develop $100/day fine language for illegal sewer discharges. The committee also learned the village received a local road improvement grant covering over 50% of a $1.5 million Birch Street reconstruction project.

## Key Discussions
### CIP Update
Michael reported the village received a local road improvement grant for Birch Street reconstruction between Schofield Avenue and Community Center Drive, covering over 50% of the estimated $1.5 million project. This 2029 project will likely move to 2028. Michael noted the village has received over $9 million in grant funding over the past 4-5 years. Discussion also covered Weston Avenue settlement repairs by Integrity Grading, Schofield Avenue repairs by HOS Construction, and Fuller Street repairs by Melvin. A Bladel Avenue special assessment meeting was tentatively scheduled for May 28, 2026 at 6:00 PM.

### Water Utility PSC Annual Report
Michael presented the 2025 annual report showing net operating income of $482,911, consistent with 2024's $490,000. Water loss was 6%. The utility's total value reached nearly $34 million, a 6.7% increase from infrastructure placed in service including Wells 7 and 8 and water main from the Weston Avenue project. The utility serves approximately 6,100 customers total: 5,250 in Weston, 830 in Rothschild, and 5 in other areas. The committee voted to acknowledge the report, passed unanimously.

### Resolution 2026-014 Transportation Funding
Michael explained this League of Wisconsin Municipalities resolution supports long-term state transportation funding solutions. The village has decreased its public works budget recently and relies on grant funding for major projects while needing recurring annual funding for maintenance and snow plowing. Wisconsin is noted as affordable compared to neighboring states for automobile ownership. Motion to adopt passed unanimously.

### Recycled Base Course Production
Michael reported King Gravel submitted the low bid at $4.23/ton for crushing approximately 30,000 tons of recycled material at the Ryan Street site, totaling $126,900. The competing bid was $4.25/ton. Michael demonstrated this program saves approximately $11 per cubic yard compared to City of Wausau costs, equating to roughly $130,000 savings across this year's three projects. Motion to award to King Gravel passed unanimously.

### Sanitary Sewer Discharge Violations
Josh reported significant clear water infiltration, with April showing 37 million gallons pumped into the system but 53-54 million gallons treated - approximately 15 million gallons of clear water. Staff identified residents illegally connecting sump pumps to sanitary sewers. The committee discussed enforcement options under existing code allowing fines of $100-$1,000 per day. Trustee Zogami suggested waiting for the INI study results to identify problem areas, then sending violation letters with 30-60 day correction periods. Motion to direct staff to create enforcement language with fines not to exceed $100/day passed unanimously.

### Radkkey Avenue Water/Sewer Connection
Michael explained a builder wants to construct a duplex on lot 1, which currently lacks water/sewer main frontage. Staff proposed allowing a temporary long lateral connection until Radkkey Avenue is reconstructed, at which time the owner would pay assessment costs. Committee requested adding a specific date; Michael agreed to add 'no later than 2029.' Motion to recommend approval with the 2029 deadline passed unanimously.

### Military Road Construction Agreement
Michael presented an intergovernmental agreement with Rothschild for Military Road reconstruction. Weston's share for water main relocation and sanitary sewer work totals an estimated $94,860. Steam Construction is the contractor. Motion to recommend approval passed unanimously.

### Construction Services for Alderson/Jelinek and Bladel Projects
Michael received proposals for construction inspection services. For Alderson/Jelinek intersection (roundabout), MSA proposed $37,315 versus Clark Dietz at $48,910; staff recommended MSA. For Bladel Avenue, MSA proposed $70,400 and Clark Dietz $73,730; staff recommended Clark Dietz due to having experienced full-time staff rather than summer interns. Motion to approve MSA for roundabout and Clark Dietz for Bladel passed unanimously.
