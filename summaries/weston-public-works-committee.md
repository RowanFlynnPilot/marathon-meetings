## Meeting Overview
The Public Works Committee approved several infrastructure-related items including a resolution supporting state transportation funding, awarded a $126,900 recycled asphalt crushing contract to King Gravel, and directed staff to create enforcement language for sanitary sewer discharge violations with fines of $100 per day. The committee also approved construction agreements for Military Road and water/sewer connections on Radkey Avenue.

## Key Discussions
### CIP Update
Michael reported the village received a Local Road Improvement grant for Birch Street reconstruction between Scofield Avenue and Community Center Drive, covering over 50% of the estimated $1.5 million project cost. The project will likely move from 2029 to 2028. Staff noted the village has received over $9 million in grant funding over the past four to five years. Discussion also covered Weston Avenue road repairs by Integrity Grading and Excavating for settlement issues over sanitary sewer trenches, with understanding that the contractor will return if problems recur.

### Street Operation Update
Staff reported road limits were lifted April 29th. Crews performed significant dewatering near Weston Avenue tributary for about a week due to heavy rain and runoff. Discussion addressed ongoing flooding issues in the Crane Meadows area, with staff looking at permanent pump solutions. The Kennedy Park Farmers Market received positive feedback with no parking or traffic complaints reported.

### Utility Operation Update
Josh reported significant clear water infiltration into the sanitary system, with April showing 37 million gallons pumped into the water system but 53-54 million gallons treated at the wastewater plant - approximately 15 million gallons of clear water. Staff identified sump pump discharges as a major contributor and noted an upcoming INI study with In-Pipe Technologies to identify problem areas. Well 2 rehab at Carrie started the day of the meeting.

### 2025 Weston Water Utility PSC Annual Report
Michael presented the annual report showing operating revenues of approximately $3 million and net operating income of $482,911 for 2025, consistent with 2024's $490,000. The utility value increased to nearly $34 million with $6.7 million in infrastructure placed in service. Water loss was reported at 6%. The committee moved to acknowledge the report, which passed unanimously.

### Resolution 2026-014 State Transportation Funding
The resolution drafted by the League of Wisconsin Municipalities supports long-term sustainable funding solutions for state transportation. Michael noted the village has decreased its public works budget in recent years and emphasized that state funding doesn't cover what the public thinks it does, requiring subsidies from general tax dollars. The committee recommended village board adoption, passed unanimously.

### Recycled Base Course Production
King Gravel submitted the low quote at $4.23 per ton for crushing approximately 30,000 tons of recycled material at the Ryan Street site, totaling $126,900. Michael calculated the village saves approximately $11 per cubic yard compared to the City of Wausau's costs, representing about $130,000 in savings across the three 2026 projects. The committee awarded the contract to King Gravel unanimously.

### Sanitary Sewer Discharge Violation Discussion
Staff sought guidance on enforcement fines for residents illegally discharging sump pumps and other clear water into the sanitary system. The committee discussed distinguishing between unknowing and willful violations. After discussion, the committee directed staff to create enforcement language with fines of $100 per day, passed unanimously. Staff will also calculate the dollar impact of the excess clear water treatment.

### Water and Sewer Connection Agreement - Radkey Avenue
A duplex builder requested temporary water and sewer connection for lot 1 on Radkey Avenue via long lateral service until the street is reconstructed. The committee added language specifying Radkey Avenue reconstruction would occur no later than 2029, at which time the property owner would pay assessment for main extension. Motion passed unanimously.

### Military Road Construction Agreement
The committee approved an intergovernmental agreement with Village of Rothschild for Military Road construction from Wolfman Street to Helen Street. Weston's share for water and sanitary sewer utilities is estimated at $94,860. Steam Construction will hold the contract through Rothschild. Motion passed unanimously.

### Construction Service Proposals - Alderson/Jelnik and Bladel Projects
Michael presented proposals for construction inspection services. For the Alderson/Jelnik roundabout project, MSA was selected at $37,315 (lower than Clark Dietz at $48,910). For Bladel Avenue, Clark Dietz was recommended at $73,730 despite being slightly higher than MSA's $70,400, due to having full-time experienced staff rather than a summer intern. Motion passed unanimously.
