## Meeting Overview
The Wausau City Council approved several key items including a development agreement for the 11 Scott Street/Waterside Place project (6-3 vote), a 7-year residential solid waste agreement with Harter's Fox Valley Disposal, and recognized city employees for their response to a record 30.9-inch snowfall. The council also presented the 2026 Sustainability Award to Colby and Colby Millwork and issued a proclamation declaring March 31st as Sarah Rafi Day.

## Key Discussions
### Sarah Rafi Day Proclamation
Mayor Doug Denny summarized a proclamation previously read at a meeting with about 50 attendees, honoring attorney Sarah Rafi who was diagnosed with stage 4 brain cancer (glioblastoma) in July. The mayor noted that Elk Lodge member Brad Gessel also recently passed from the same disease. March 31st (Rafi's birthday) was proclaimed as Sarah Rafi Day in Wausau.

### Department of Public Works Recognition
Mayor Denny presented a citation to DPW plow crews and municipal fleet staff for their response to the record 30.9-inch snowfall from March 14-16, 2026. Kevin Kester, street supervisor, accepted on behalf of the team, noting the mechanics kept vehicles operational throughout 12-hour shifts. Four municipal fleet technicians worked 12 straight days without time off. Kester emotionally praised the crew saying 'you kicked its ass.'

### Sustainability Award to Colby and Colby Millwork
Sustainability Committee members Christine Daniels and Gina Brew presented the 2026 City of Wausau Sustainability Award to Colby and Colby Millwork. Keith Kaning and Mike Thompson accepted, describing their 2,000+ solar panel installation that became operational in July and generates enough power for approximately 120 homes. They also highlighted LED lighting upgrades, high-efficiency air compressors, and comprehensive recycling programs for manufacturing materials.

### 11 Scott Street LLC Development Agreement
The council approved 6-3 a joint resolution for the Waterside Place development at 11 Scott Street. Developers Mark Craig and Raleigh Lray spoke during public comment, noting the $10+ million project to convert a vacant 100,000 sq ft building into 52 mid-priced residential units. Alder Rasmusson supported the project for returning the building to taxable status and reclaiming parking spaces. Alder Neil cited benefits including $55,000 annual parking revenue and helping TID 8 reach valuation goals. Alder Larson dissented, objecting to discounting city assets. Alder Tierney questioned the city's ability to provide alternative parking within 300 yards if the ramp closes. Director Randy Feifer explained the agreement reduces parking obligations from 480 to 150 spaces from an existing 2062 agreement.

### Harter's Fox Valley Disposal Agreement
The council unanimously approved (9-0) the 7-year residential solid waste and recycling service agreement with Harter's Fox Valley Disposal, correcting a previous discrepancy between 7 and 10 year terms.

### Thompson Submachine Gun Proceeds for Police Equipment
The council unanimously approved (9-0) a budget modification allowing the Wausau Police Department to use proceeds from the sale of a Thompson submachine gun to purchase Red Dot Optics. Mayor Denny noted the money had been 'sitting in the safe a long time.'

### Settlement of David Holes vs City of Wausau
Assistant City Attorney Vincent Bonito explained a 2022 bus accident where Transit Mutual paid the initial claim, but the individual later filed a personal injury claim. The city's counter claim and third-party complaint will be released as the insurer agreed to pay damages. The resolution passed 8-1. Alder Neil confirmed this settlement is separate from the individual's ongoing injury claim.

## Public Comment
Two speakers addressed the council regarding the 11 Scott Street project. Raleigh Lray from 11 Scott Street LLC requested support for their green sustainable project converting a vacant building to mid-priced apartments. Mark Craig of 3246 North 8th Street noted the project exceeds $10 million and stated 'Without your help, it won't happen,' referencing the previous 7-4 term sheet vote.
