## Meeting Overview
The Wausau Board of Public Works approved all items on the agenda, including recommendations for real estate services qualifications for two state highway projects, a pay estimate for lead service line replacement, and contractor licenses. The board also approved a $2,338.87 subrogated insurance claim for American Family Insurance on behalf of Kara Blank following closed session deliberation.

## Key Discussions
### April 14, 2026 Regular Board of Public Works Minutes
Approved. The board passed the minutes from the previous meeting.

### Statement of Qualifications for Real Estate Services, STH 52 (East Wausau Avenue) Project ID 6999-00-23
Approved. The board recommended approval of the statement of qualifications for real estate services for this state highway project. Qualifications were opened on April 14, 2026.

### Statement of Qualifications for Real Estate Services, Bus. 51 (Grand Avenue) Project ID 6999-02-20
Approved. The board recommended approval of the statement of qualifications for real estate services for the Business 51 Grand Avenue project. Qualifications were opened on April 14, 2026.

### Pay Estimate #26 with Community Infrastructure Partners for replacement of lead service lines
Approved. The board approved the pay estimate for ongoing lead service line replacement work with Community Infrastructure Partners.

### Portland Cement Concrete License: Potrykus Construction, LLC and Miron Construction Co., Inc.
Approved. The board granted Portland Cement Concrete licenses to both Potrykus Construction, LLC and Miron Construction Co., Inc.

### Bituminous Concrete Paving License: Miron Construction Co., Inc.
Approved. The board granted a Bituminous Concrete Paving license to Miron Construction Co., Inc.

### Closed Session for deliberating on claims
Approved. The board entered closed session pursuant to Wisconsin State Statute §19.85(1)(g) to deliberate on claims.

### Reconvene into Open Session and action on Closed Session items
Approved 3-0. The board reconvened into open session and approved the American Family Insurance subrogated claim on behalf of Kara Blank in the amount of $2,338.87. Motion moved by Vincent Bonino, seconded by MaryAnne Groat. Eric Lindman, MaryAnne Groat, and Vincent Bonino all voted yes.
