## Meeting Overview
The Wausau Board of Public Works approved contracts for the 26th Street construction project, approved a change order for the Randolph/Cherry Street project addressing unexpected underground conditions, and approved a pay estimate and concrete license. All items passed unanimously with no opposition.

## Key Discussions
### March 10th Board of Public Works Minutes
Minutes were approved with a motion and second. Passed unanimously with voice vote.

### 26th Street Construction Project Bids
Seven bids were opened and read aloud. Switlick submitted the lowest bid at $1,279,089.75, narrowly beating Hos at $1,280,877.96. Other bidders included A1 ($1,374,600), Francis Melvin ($1,385,383), Steen ($1,489,126), James Peterson ($1,570,698.56), and Earth ($1,686,780.75). A board member noted the 'tight bids' between the top two. Motion to approve Switlick as the contractor passed unanimously.

### North 8th Avenue Bids
Staff announced this item would be postponed as the bid opening was extended. Will return at a future meeting.

### 2025 Street Construction Project A - Change Order 1
Staff presented four items totaling $14,436.50: an inside drop on a manhole ($4,856) due to an unrecorded large diameter sanitary service; water main work ($2,317.50) because an existing 6-inch main was found instead of the expected 8-inch; miscellaneous storm sewer tie-ins ($5,016) negotiated down to $66 per linear foot; and geogrid installation ($2,247) for approximately 750 square yards near Thomas Jefferson Elementary due to poor soil conditions. Change Order 2 regarding liquidated damages was postponed pending further discussions. Motion to approve passed unanimously.

### 2025 Street Construction Project A - Pay Estimate Number 9
Staff recommended approval of pay estimate for work completed through the end of the year in the amount of $535,114.20 from Haw Suns Incorporated. Motion to approve passed unanimously.

### Portland Cement Concrete License - KSK Incorporated
A board member named Vinnie confirmed he reviewed the application and everything was in order. Motion to approve the license passed unanimously.

## Public Comment
No public comment was offered.

## Action Items
- Switlick awarded 26th Street construction project contract at $1,279,089.75
