## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Board of Supervisors adopted the 2026 Comprehensive Plan with multiple amendments addressing renewable energy terminology, data centers, AI technology, and energy policy. The board also approved salaries for elected officials, authorized phase 2 design for a new highway facility, approved outside legal counsel for PFAS litigation, and ratified a local emergency declaration for the recent blizzard event.

## Key Discussions
### Comprehensive Plan 2026 Adoption (Ordinance 0-13-26)
Administrator Leonard presented 10 proposed amendments compiled from supervisor feedback. Amendment 1 on livability standards passed unanimously. Amendments 2, 3, and 4 on alternative energy systems terminology (changing 'renewable energy' to 'energy projects' and 'large-scale wind and solar') were separated at Supervisor Crawl's request and each passed but not unanimously. Amendment 5 on data centers and battery energy storage passed not unanimously, with Supervisor Leur voting no citing it was 'too ideological.' Amendment 6 on radon and lead remediation passed unanimously. Amendment 7 on regulating data centers 'when allowed by state law' passed not unanimously. Amendment 8 on AI and automation language, offered by Supervisor Leur, passed unanimously. Amendment 9 by Supervisor Sindellski on promoting coal, natural gas, and nuclear was amended by Supervisor Boots to read 'promote coal and natural gas until a long-term sustainable and reliable energy source can be found that does not adversely affect agricultural land' - this passed not unanimously after debate where Supervisor Rosenberg stated 'there is no such thing as clean coal' and noted coal is not economically viable. A late amendment by Supervisor Sindellski regarding utility-scale wind and solar as industrial uses was debated; Supervisor Jeang moved to refer it to committee, but after discussion about process requirements including needing new public hearings, the referral motion was defeated and the amendment itself was defeated. The full comprehensive plan as amended was then approved but not unanimously.

### Salaries for Elected Officials (Resolution 12-26)
Resolution establishing salaries for clerk of courts, sheriff, and elected department heads for the upcoming term of office. Motion by Supervisor Conway, second by Supervisor Rosenberg. Passed with no discussion.

### Highway Facility Phase 2 Design (Resolution 13-26)
Authorization to proceed with phase 2 design services for new highway facility. Supervisor Soyber requested future information on plans for the old facility. Chair Gibbs noted that decision is years away and will be addressed by HR Finance and Property Committee. Supervisor Sindellski asked about the $53 million cost estimate but was informed that cost approval was not part of this resolution. Passed unanimously.

### PFAS Litigation Authorization (Resolution 14-26)
Resolution authorizing outside counsel on contingency basis for PFAS contamination lawsuits. Two amendments were adopted: Supervisor Robinson's amendment directing the county administrator to evaluate past and present practices that may have resulted in PFAS exposure in county operations (passed unanimously), and Vice Chair Dickinson's amendment modifying language regarding airport property references (passed unanimously). The resolution as amended passed unanimously.

### Local Emergency Declaration Ratification (Resolution 22-26)
Administrator Leonard explained the ratification preserves the county's opportunity for state reimbursement after the governor's emergency declaration expired on the 16th during the blizzard. Leonard praised staff across facilities, parks, highway, sheriff's office, and airport, noting over 600 hours of call-in time over 24 hours and staff working 12-16 hour shifts sleeping on cots. Supervisor Fifer echoed thanks to staff. Passed unanimously.

### County Administrator Performance Evaluation
Chair Gibbs explained the executive committee completed the administrator's evaluation using board input summarized at the previous Thursday meeting, with no wording changes. Supervisor Robinson moved to accept the executive committee's recommendation on salary and performance evaluation without going into closed session. Passed unanimously.

## Public Comment
No public comment was offered.

## Action Items
- Comprehensive Plan 2026 adopted with 9 amendments including new language on energy systems, data centers, AI technology, and promoting coal and natural gas
- Salaries established for clerk of courts, sheriff, and elected department heads for upcoming term
- Phase 2 design services for new highway facility authorized
- Outside legal counsel authorized on contingency basis for PFAS litigation; administrator directed to evaluate county PFAS exposure risks
- Budget carry forwards and capital asset thresholds approved
- Law enforcement drug trafficking response grant accepted
- Local state of emergency declaration ratified for blizzard response
- County Administrator salary and performance evaluation approved per executive committee recommendation
- Departing supervisors recognized: Crawl, Fifick, Marshall, Rosenberg, Hardinger, V, and Reynolds
