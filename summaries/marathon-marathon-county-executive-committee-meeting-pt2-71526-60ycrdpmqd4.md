## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Executive Committee reconvened in open session to receive an update on FEMA disaster declaration status and community assistance programs following the April 2026 storms, and briefly reviewed the County Board Strategic Plan Survey. The committee learned Marathon County was approved for individual assistance but denied for public assistance due to insufficient documented damages, and that 24 Marathon County residents have already applied for FEMA individual assistance.

## Key Discussions
### FEMA Declaration and Community Assistance Update
Sarah, the county's Public Information Officer, presented a detailed update on FEMA and SBA disaster assistance following the April 13–23, 2026 severe storms, tornadoes, and flooding. She reported that as of July 15, 24 Marathon County residents had filed FEMA individual assistance applications, with four filed the previous day. A FEMA Disaster Recovery Center opened July 14 at the Town of Ringle Municipal Center (Monday–Friday, 1–7 p.m.), and an SBA Business Recovery Center opened July 13 at the Marathon County Public Library Wausau headquarters (Monday–Friday, 9 a.m.–4:30 p.m.). A new recovery resource page was also launched at marathoncounty.gov/ia.

### Public Assistance Denial
Sarah explained that while Marathon County was approved for individual assistance, it was denied for FEMA public assistance — which covers governmental costs such as debris removal, road repairs, and public infrastructure — along with Bayfield, Manitowoc, and Racine counties. The per-capita damage threshold for Marathon County was $670,743.18, but documented damages from the April storms totaled only approximately $271,000. County staff reviewed whether any additional damages had been missed and concluded they had not; therefore, the county informed Wisconsin Emergency Management it would not pursue an appeal. Gibbs emphasized to committee members that the public assistance denial does not affect individuals' ability to apply for individual assistance.

### Insurance Settlements and Town of Ringle Property Questions
Supervisor Robinson asked about the status of insurance company settlements for homes in the Town of Ringle that may need to be demolished, raising concerns about deadlines and potential need for the county to extend deadlines or forgive fees. Gibbs responded that he had not received a follow-up from Ringle representatives but pledged to reach out, noting that Sarah had attended a recent Ringle board meeting on these topics. He mentioned that free legal consultation through the State Bar of Wisconsin and resources from the State Commission on Insurance had been made available to affected residents, and that the Town of Ringle was also exploring whether to issue raze orders for disputed properties.

### County Board Strategic Plan Survey
Gibbs presented the item on behalf of Vice Chair Boots, who had to leave to catch a flight. He asked committee members whether anything was missing from the strategic plan survey included in their packets. Supervisor Arseth asked whether the survey would be available online; Gibbs confirmed it would be sent to county board members as a link-based online survey, similar to the work plan ranking process. No content gaps were identified by members present. Gibbs noted a separate public input process would be used.
