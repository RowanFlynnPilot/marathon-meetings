## Meeting Overview
The Village of Kronenwetter's CLIPP Committee met on July 6, 2026, conducting a public hearing on revisions to Chapter 382 (Nuisances) of the village code and unanimously voting to forward the updated ordinance language to the Village Board for approval. The committee also discussed a traffic study recommendation for the County Road XX and Terabonding Drive intersection and heard a public comment requesting a bike and pedestrian path along Highway X.

## Key Discussions
### Public Hearing - Chapter 382 Nuisances Revision
Village President Dan Joling provided an overview of the proposed changes, explaining the ordinance removes the word 'public' from 'public nuisance' to make it more legally enforceable, requires complaints to be verified by law enforcement or supported by quantifiable evidence to prevent neighbor disputes, and extends the abatement period from 24 to 48 hours. Police Chief Terry McHugh confirmed the ordinance is largely similar to existing language with wordsmithing changes. No members of the public offered comment during the hearing.

### Public Comment - Highway X Bike and Pedestrian Path
Kim Mayer, a resident near the intersection of Highway X and Pleasant Drive, presented a seven-page petition with signatures collected door-to-door and at the YMCA, advocating for a dedicated bike and pedestrian path along Highway X from Holland Avenue at the YMCA south to the intersection of Highway X and Highway XX. She cited safety concerns for children on scooters, cyclists, and pedestrians sharing the road with fast-moving traffic, and noted that both Police Chief McHugh and County Highway Commissioner Kevin Lane expressed support, with Lane indicating grant money is available. Committee members asked clarifying questions about the route and indicated staff would research costs and grant options for a future agenda.

### Approval of June 1 CLIPP Meeting Minutes
A motion was made by Patty [Tikowski] to accept the June 1 CLIPP meeting minutes, seconded by Paul [Mayjol]. The committee approved the minutes by voice vote with no opposition.

### Police Chief Report
Chief McHugh reported that calls for service are on pace to reach 8,000 in 2026, up from 7,100 the prior year, with an uptick in disturbances, fights, and drug-related incidents. He noted that following Officer Don's resignation, the department accelerated hiring: a new officer with prior law enforcement experience completed field training early and will soon begin solo patrol, a second hire's background process has been expedited with a target start of next month, and a third position has been posted on Wilinget with interviews planned for August and a fall hire anticipated.

### Public Works Director Report
Public Works Director Greg reported that the new maintenance shed is expected to have concrete poured in August with completion by January. He noted that state legislation raised the public bidding threshold, which is beneficial given rising costs. Sanitary sewer flow remains elevated at approximately 400,000 gallons per day due to a wet spring and early summer, down from higher levels but still above the target of 300,000. He also noted road salt prices increased about $17 per ton from the state contract.

### Chapter 382 Nuisances - Vote to Forward to Village Board
Following the public hearing with no public opposition, Garrett Lizney moved to pass the revised nuisance ordinance language to the Village Board for approval. The motion was seconded and passed unanimously on a 5-0 roll call vote with all members voting yes.

### Traffic Control - County Road XX and Terabonding Drive
Public Works Director Greg explained that County Highway Commissioner Kevin Lane requested elected officials' input on whether to make the current temporary four-way stop at County Road XX and Terabonding Drive permanent. Staff recommended commissioning a traffic study before making a decision rather than either immediately supporting or opposing a permanent four-way stop. The committee reached consensus to recommend the county conduct a traffic study while keeping the four-way stop in place during the study period, and that recommendation will be forwarded to the county.

## Public Comment
One speaker offered public comment. Kim Mayer, a resident near Highway X and Pleasant Drive, advocated for construction of a dedicated bike and pedestrian path along Highway X from Holland Avenue at the YMCA south to the intersection of Highway X and Highway XX. She presented a seven-page petition with signatures gathered door-to-door and at the YMCA, cited safety concerns she personally witnessed involving children, cyclists, and pedestrians, and reported that both Police Chief McHugh and County Highway Commissioner Kevin Lane expressed support for the idea, with Lane noting grant funding is available. No public comment was offered at the nuisance ordinance public hearing.

## Action Items
