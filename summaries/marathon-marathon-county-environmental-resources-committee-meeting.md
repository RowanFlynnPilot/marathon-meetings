## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Environmental Resources Committee approved two rezoning petitions and voted to recommend a limited fee waiver for tornado-affected properties to the county board. The committee also received educational presentations from the Solid Waste, Parks Recreation and Forestry, and Conservation Planning and Zoning departments.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of March 31st, 2026 committee minutes
Supervisor Sefeld made a motion to approve the minutes, seconded by Supervisor Drebeck. The motion carried unanimously with no discussion.

### Tim Veland rezoning petition (Town of Green Valley)
Land Use Specialist Garrett Pogle presented a petition to rezone land from Urban Residential and General Agriculture to Rural Residential for a proposed land division. The Town of Green Valley recommended approval on March 10, 2026. No public testimony was offered. Supervisor Drebeck moved to approve based on findings of fact and the town's approval, seconded by Supervisor Kurt. Motion carried unanimously.

### Jeffrey and Cynthia Schmidtz rezoning petition (Town of McMillan)
Garrett Pogle presented a petition to rezone from Conservancy Recreation to General Agriculture. Supervisor Leur asked about the conservancy designation and potential loss of natural areas. Pogle explained the property contains a home and horse riding stable, with no natural areas being preserved - the rezone would make existing structures conforming uses. The Town of McMillan recommended approval on April 20, 2026. No public testimony was offered. Supervisor Sefeld moved to approve, seconded by Supervisor Drew. Motion carried unanimously.

### Fee waivers for April 17, 2026 severe weather impacts
Director Lori Mskimmons presented a proposal to waive county zoning and septic fees for properties impacted by the April 17 tornado affecting towns of Ringle, Easton, and villages of Kronenwetter and Weston. Up to 149 parcels were potentially affected. Supervisor Drew questioned where to draw the line for future events. Supervisor Drebeck raised concerns about fairness to property owners not in declared disaster areas. Eric Vogel asked about homeowners insurance coverage. After debate, Supervisor Sefeld moved to approve the full resolution. Supervisor Drebeck then offered an amendment to limit fee waivers to only reconnect permits ($160 each) for existing septic systems, excluding other zoning or new system fees. The amendment passed and the amended resolution was approved to forward to the county board, though not unanimously.

### Solid Waste Department presentation
Director Dave Hagen Busher provided an overview of the landfill operations, explaining the facility has operated since 1980 and accepts about 1,000 tons daily. He highlighted the 2023 partnership with Vidi Energy to convert landfill gas to renewable natural gas, significant capital expenses for expansions and closures ($4-6 million per project), and upcoming state-mandated leachate treatment requirements by 2032. Marilyn Bend praised the department for keeping private landfill costs in line.

### Parks, Recreation, and Forestry Department presentation
County Forest Administrator Tom Lovelin presented on behalf of Director Jamie, covering the department's 44 full-time and 120 seasonal staff serving both county and city facilities. He noted Nine Mile County Forest generates $3.25 million for the local economy from out-of-town visitors. Supervisor Sefeld announced Lovelin's retirement effective June 1st after nearly 30 years of service. The committee thanked him for his service.

## Public Comment
No public comment was offered.

## Action Items
- Rezoning from Urban Residential/General Agriculture to Rural Residential for James Offer and David Tract in Town of Green Valley approved and forwarded to County Board
- Rezoning from Conservancy Recreation to General Agriculture for Jeffrey and Cynthia Schmidtz in Town of McMillan approved and forwarded to County Board
- Limited fee waiver for septic reconnect permits only ($160) for properties impacted by April 17, 2026 severe weather forwarded to County Board for consideration
- Staff to track costs and requests related to severe weather fee waivers for future policy development
