## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Environmental Resources Committee approved two rezoning petitions and voted to forward a modified fee waiver resolution to the county board. The committee amended the severe weather fee waiver proposal to cover only septic system reconnect fees ($160) rather than all zoning and pouts fees for properties damaged in the April 17, 2026 tornado.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of March 31, 2026 committee minutes
Supervisor Sefeld made a motion to approve the minutes, seconded by Supervisor Drebeck. The motion carried unanimously with no discussion.

### Tim Veland rezoning petition (Town of Green Valley)
Land Use Specialist Garrett Pogle presented the staff report for rezoning from Urban Residential and General Agriculture to Rural Residential. The Town of Green Valley recommended approval on March 10, 2026. No public testimony was offered for or against. Supervisor Drebeck moved to approve based on findings of fact, conclusions of law, and town approval, seconded by Supervisor Kurt. Motion carried unanimously.

### Jeffrey and Cynthia Schmidtz rezoning petition (Town of McMillan)
Garrett Pogle presented the petition to rezone from Conservancy Recreation to General Agriculture. Supervisor Leur asked about the conservancy nature, and Pogle explained the property contains a home and horse riding stable, with the rezoning intended to make the home a conforming use rather than non-conforming. The Town of McMillan recommended approval on April 20, 2026. No public testimony was offered. Supervisor Sefeld moved to approve, seconded by Supervisor Drew. Motion carried unanimously.

### Fee waiver for April 17, 2026 severe weather damage
Director Lori Mskimmons presented a proposal to waive county zoning and pouts fees for tornado-damaged properties. Significant debate ensued. Supervisor Drew questioned where to draw the line for future events. Supervisor Drebeck expressed concerns about fairness to those not in declared disaster areas. Supervisor Sefeld moved to approve the full resolution, seconded by Supervisor Leur. Supervisor Drebeck then proposed an amendment to limit the waiver to only reconnect fees ($160) for pouts systems, excluding all other fees. The amendment was seconded by Supervisor Kurt and passed. The amended resolution then passed on a non-unanimous vote to forward to the county board.

## Public Comment
No public comment was offered.
