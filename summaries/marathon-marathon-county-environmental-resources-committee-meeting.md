## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Environmental Resources Committee met on May 5, 2026, approving two rezoning petitions unanimously and voting to forward a modified fee waiver resolution to the county board that will only waive reconnect fees for septic systems damaged by the April 17, 2026 tornado, rather than the broader fee waiver originally proposed by staff.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of March 31, 2026 committee minutes
Supervisor Sefeld made a motion to approve the minutes with Supervisor Drebeck seconding. The motion carried unanimously with no discussion.

### Tim Veland rezoning petition - Town of Green Valley
Land Use Specialist Garrett Pogle presented the staff report for rezoning from urban residential and general agriculture to rural residential for a proposed land division. The Town of Green Valley recommended approval on March 10, 2026. No public testimony was offered. Supervisor Drebeck moved to approve based on findings of fact, conclusions of law, and town approval. Supervisor Kurt seconded. Motion carried unanimously.

### Jeffrey and Cynthia Schmidtz rezoning petition - Town of McMillan
Garrett Pogle presented the rezoning from conservancy recreation to general agriculture. Supervisor Leur asked about the conservancy nature of the property. Pogle explained the property has a home and horse riding stable, and the rezoning would make the home a conforming use rather than non-conforming. He confirmed no natural areas would be lost. Town of McMillan recommended approval April 20, 2026. Supervisor Sefeld moved to approve; Supervisor Drew seconded. Motion carried unanimously.

### Fee waiver for tornado-impacted properties
Director Lori Mskimmons presented a proposal to waive county zoning and septic fees for properties damaged by the April 17, 2026 tornado. Supervisor Drew questioned the precedent this sets for future disasters. Supervisor Leur asked about potential levy impacts. Supervisor Drebeck expressed concern about fairness to property owners affected by smaller disasters. After debate, Supervisor Sefeld moved to forward the resolution. Supervisor Leur seconded. Supervisor Drebeck then amended the motion to limit the waiver to only reconnect fees ($160) for septic systems, excluding other zoning and septic fees. Supervisor Kurt seconded the amendment. The amendment passed with some opposition. The amended motion to forward to county board also passed with some opposition.

### Department presentations
Dave Hagen Busher presented on Solid Waste operations, covering the landfill's history, tipping fees, renewable natural gas partnership with Vidi Energy, and upcoming leachate treatment requirements by 2032. Tom Lovelin presented on Parks, Recreation and Forestry, noting the department serves both county and city of Wausau, operates 17 county parks and 30,700 acres of county forest. A Nine Mile study showed out-of-town visitors generate $3.25 million for the local economy. Supervisor Sefeld announced Tom Lovelin is retiring June 1st after nearly 30 years of service.

## Public Comment
No public comment was offered.

## Action Items
- Forward amended fee waiver resolution to county board - waiving only reconnect fees ($160) for septic systems on tornado-damaged properties for one year
- Two rezoning petitions forwarded to Marathon County Board of Supervisors for final approval
- Conservation services director Kirsty Heiden to present at next meeting
- Special summer educational meeting on conservation topics to be scheduled
