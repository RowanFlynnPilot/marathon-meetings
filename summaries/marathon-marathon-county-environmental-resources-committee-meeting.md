## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Environmental Resources Committee approved two rezoning petitions and voted to forward a modified fee waiver resolution to the county board that would only cover reconnect fees for properties impacted by the April 17, 2026 tornado. The committee also received department overviews from Solid Waste, Parks Recreation and Forestry, and Conservation Planning and Zoning.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of March 31st, 2026 committee minutes
Supervisor Sefeld made a motion to approve the minutes with Supervisor Drebeck seconding. The motion carried with no opposition on a voice vote.

### Rezoning petition for James Offer and David Tract
Land Use Specialist Garrett Pogle presented the staff report for rezoning from Urban Residential and General Agriculture to Rural Residential in the Town of Green Valley. The Town Board recommended approval on March 10, 2026. No public testimony was offered. Supervisor Drebeck moved to approve based on findings of fact and the town's approval, seconded by Supervisor Kurt. Motion carried unanimously.

### Rezoning petition for Jeffrey and Cynthia Schmidtz
Garrett Pogle presented the petition to rezone from Conservancy Recreation to General Agriculture in the Town of McMillan. Supervisor Leur asked about the conservancy designation, and Pogle explained the property has a home and horse riding stable, and the rezone would make the structures conforming uses. The Town Board recommended approval on April 20, 2026. Supervisor Sefeld moved to approve, seconded by Supervisor Drew. Motion carried unanimously.

### Fee waivers for April 17, 2026 severe weather victims
Director Lori Mskimmons presented a proposal to waive county zoning and septic fees for properties damaged by the tornado affecting Ringle, Kronenwetter, Easton, and Weston. Supervisor Drew questioned where to draw the line for future events. Supervisor Drebeck expressed concerns about fairness to others who face disasters without emergency declarations. After discussion, Supervisor Drebeck moved to amend the resolution to only waive reconnect fees ($160) for existing septic systems, not other zoning or new system fees. Supervisor Kurt seconded the amendment. The amendment passed on a voice vote. The amended resolution then passed to forward to the county board, though not unanimously.

### Solid Waste Department overview
Director Dave Hagen Busher provided an educational presentation covering landfill operations, the new renewable natural gas partnership with Vidi Energy generating royalties for the county, significant capital expenses including $6 million for cell construction, and upcoming leachate treatment requirements by 2032. He noted lithium-ion battery fires occur about once a week at the facility.

### Parks, Recreation and Forestry overview
County Forest Administrator Tom Lovelin presented on behalf of Director Jamie, covering the department's 44 full-time and 120 seasonal employees serving both county and city of Wausau parks. He noted Nine Mile County Forest generates $3.25 million for the local economy from out-of-town visitors. Supervisor Sefeld announced Tom Lovelin is retiring June 1st after nearly 30 years of service, with Joe Tucker selected as his replacement.

## Public Comment
No public comment was offered.

## Action Items
- Rezoning for James Offer and David Tract approved and forwarded to County Board
- Rezoning for Jeffrey and Cynthia Schmidtz approved and forwarded to County Board
- Modified fee waiver resolution covering only septic reconnect fees forwarded to County Board for consideration
- Staff to track costs and requests related to tornado fee waivers for future policy development
- New county forest administrator Joe Tucker to replace retiring Tom Lovelin effective June 1st
