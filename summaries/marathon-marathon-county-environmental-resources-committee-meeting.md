## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Environmental Resources Committee approved two rezoning petitions and voted to forward a modified fee waiver resolution to the county board that will waive only septic system reconnect fees (not other zoning or pouts fees) for properties damaged by the April 17, 2026 tornado. The committee also received educational presentations from Solid Waste, Parks Recreation and Forestry, and Conservation Planning and Zoning departments.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of March 31st, 2026 committee minutes
Supervisor Sefeld made a motion to approve the minutes, seconded by Supervisor Drebeck. The motion carried with no opposition on a voice vote.

### Rezoning petition - Tim Veland on behalf of James Offer and David Tract
Land Use Specialist Garrett Pogle presented the staff report for rezoning from urban residential and general agriculture to rural residential in the Town of Green Valley. The Town Board recommended approval on March 10, 2026. No public testimony was offered for or against. Supervisor Drebeck moved to approve based on findings of fact and conclusions of law, seconded by Supervisor Kurt. Motion carried unanimously.

### Rezoning petition - Jeffrey and Cynthia Schmidtz
Garrett Pogle presented the petition to rezone from conservancy recreation to general agriculture in the Town of McMillan. Supervisor Leur asked about the conservancy nature of the property; Pogle explained it currently has a home and horse riding stable, and the rezoning would make the home a conforming use rather than non-conforming. He confirmed no natural area would be lost. The Town Board recommended approval on April 20, 2026. No public testimony was offered. Supervisor Sefeld moved to approve, seconded by Supervisor Drew. Motion carried unanimously.

### Fee waiver for April 17, 2026 severe weather damaged properties
Director Lori Mskimmons presented a proposal to waive county zoning and pouts fees for properties damaged by the tornado affecting the towns of Ringle, Easton, and villages of Kronenwetter and Weston. Supervisor Drew questioned where to draw the line for future events. Supervisor Leur asked about tracking costs and potential levy impacts. County Administrator Lance clarified this wouldn't require a budget amendment. Supervisor Drebeck questioned fairness to individual tornado victims who wouldn't qualify. After discussion, Supervisor Sefeld moved to forward the resolution to the county board. Supervisor Drebeck then offered an amendment to limit the waiver to only reconnect fees for pouts systems ($160 each), excluding all other zoning and pouts fees. The amendment was seconded by Supervisor Kurt and passed. The amended resolution then passed on a non-unanimous voice vote to forward to the county board.

### Solid Waste Department presentation
Director David Hagenbusher provided an overview of the department's operations, including the landfill facility operating since 1980, the renewable natural gas partnership with Vidi Energy that began injecting gas in September 2025, and upcoming challenges including a $6 million 11-acre expansion and required leachate treatment facility by 2032. He noted the department applied for clean water fund financing but was asked to reapply for 2027 funding. Marilyn Bend praised the department's work and competitive influence on private landfills.

### Parks, Recreation and Forestry Department presentation
County Forest Administrator Tom Lovelin presented on behalf of Director Jamie, covering the department's 44 full-time and 120 seasonal employees serving both city and county park systems. He highlighted Nine Mile County Forest generates $3.25 million annually for the local economy from out-of-town visitors. Supervisor Sefeld announced Tom Lovelin is retiring June 1st after nearly 30 years of service, with Joe Tucker selected as his replacement.

## Public Comment
No public comment was offered.

## Action Items
- Rezoning from urban residential/general agriculture to rural residential for James Offer and David Tract property in Town of Green Valley approved - forwarded to County Board
- Rezoning from conservancy recreation to general agriculture for Jeffrey and Cynthia Schmidtz property in Town of McMillan approved - forwarded to County Board
- Modified fee waiver resolution (reconnect fees only) for April 17, 2026 tornado-damaged properties forwarded to County Board for consideration
- CPZ staff to track costs and requests related to tornado fee waivers
- Solid Waste to reapply for clean water fund financing by October 2025 for 2027 funding cycle
