## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Environmental Resources Committee approved two rezoning petitions and voted to forward a modified fee waiver resolution to the county board that will only waive reconnect fees for septic systems on properties damaged by the April 17, 2026 tornado. The committee also received educational presentations from Solid Waste, Parks Recreation and Forestry, and Conservation Planning and Zoning departments.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of March 31st, 2026 committee minutes
Supervisor Sefeld made a motion to approve the minutes, seconded by Supervisor Drebeck. The motion carried with no opposition.

### Rezoning petition - Tim Veland on behalf of James Offer and David Tract
Garrett Pogle presented the staff report for rezoning from urban residential and general agriculture to rural residential in the Town of Green Valley. The Town Board recommended approval on March 10, 2026. No public testimony was offered. Supervisor Drebeck moved to approve based on findings of fact and conclusions of law, seconded by Supervisor Kurt. Motion carried unanimously.

### Rezoning petition - Jeffrey and Cynthia Schmidtz
Garrett Pogle presented the petition to rezone from conservancy recreation to general agriculture in the Town of McMillan. Supervisor Leur asked about the conservancy designation, and Pogle explained the property has a home and horse riding stable, and the rezone would make these conforming uses. The Town Board recommended approval on April 20, 2026. No public testimony was offered. Supervisor Sefeld moved to approve, seconded by Supervisor Drew. Motion carried unanimously.

### Fee waivers for April 17, 2026 severe weather victims
Director Lori Miskimmons presented staff recommendations to waive county zoning and septic fees for tornado-impacted properties. Supervisor Drew questioned where to draw the line for future events. Supervisor Leur asked about tracking costs. Supervisor Drebeck expressed concerns about fairness to individuals not in declared disaster areas and referenced the recent $6 fee increase for septic systems. Eric Vogel asked if homeowners insurance would cover fees. After significant debate, Supervisor Drebeck moved to amend the resolution to only waive reconnect fees ($160) for septic systems, not other zoning or septic fees. The amendment was seconded by Supervisor Kurt and passed. The amended resolution was then approved to forward to the county board, though not unanimously.

### Solid Waste Department overview
Director Dave Hagenbusher presented an overview of the department's operations, explaining the landfill accepts about 1,000 tons daily with 11 staff members. He highlighted the renewable natural gas partnership with Vidi Energy that began injecting gas into pipelines in September 2025, significant capital expenses of $4-6 million for expansions and closures, and the requirement by 2032 to have on-site leachate treatment due to PFAS concerns. Marilyn Bend praised the department for keeping private landfills competitive on pricing.

### Parks, Recreation and Forestry Department overview
Tom Lovelin, County Forest Administrator, presented on behalf of Director Jamie who had an emergency. He explained the department operates both county and City of Wausau park systems since 1924, manages 17 county parks and 30,700 acres of county forest. A Nine Mile County Forest study showed out-of-town visitors generate $3.25 million annually for the county economy. Supervisor Sefeld thanked Tom and announced his retirement effective June 1st after 35 years of service. Joe Tucker was selected as the new county forest administrator.

## Public Comment
No public comment was offered.

## Action Items
- Rezoning for James Offer and David Tract property approved - forwarded to Marathon County Board of Supervisors
- Rezoning for Jeffrey and Cynthia Schmidtz property approved - forwarded to Marathon County Board of Supervisors
- Modified fee waiver resolution (reconnect fees only) forwarded to county board for consideration
- Staff directed to track costs and requests related to fee waivers for future policy development
- Town of Green Valley and Town of McMillan should update comprehensive plans to reflect zoning changes if applicable
