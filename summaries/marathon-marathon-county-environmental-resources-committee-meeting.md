## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Environmental Resources Committee approved two rezoning petitions and amended a resolution to waive only reconnect fees for septic systems for properties impacted by the April 17, 2026 tornado. The committee also received educational presentations from Solid Waste, Parks Recreation and Forestry, and Conservation Planning and Zoning departments.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of March 31st, 2026 committee minutes
Supervisor Sefeld made a motion to approve the minutes, seconded by Supervisor Drebeck. The motion carried unanimously with no discussion.

### Rezoning petition 5A1: James Offer and David Tract
Land Use Specialist Garrett Pogle presented the staff report for rezoning from Urban Residential and General Agriculture to Rural Residential in the Town of Green Valley. The Town of Green Valley Town Board recommended approval on March 10, 2026. No public testimony was offered for or against. Supervisor Drebeck made a motion to approve based on findings of fact, conclusions of law, and town approval, seconded by Supervisor Kurt. Motion carried unanimously.

### Rezoning petition 5A2: Jeffrey and Cynthia Schmidtz
Garrett Pogle presented the petition to rezone from Conservancy Recreation to General Agriculture in the Town of McMillan. Supervisor Leur asked about the conservancy nature of the property; Pogle explained the property contains a home and horse riding stable, and the rezone would make these conforming uses. Pogle confirmed no natural areas would be lost. The Town of McMillan recommended approval on April 20, 2026. No public testimony was offered. Supervisor Sefeld moved to approve, seconded by Supervisor Drew. Motion carried unanimously.

### Fee waivers for April 17, 2026 severe weather impacted properties
Director Lori Mskimmons presented a proposal to waive county zoning and septic system fees for tornado-impacted properties in Ringle, Kronenwetter, Easton, and Weston. Supervisor Drew questioned the precedent this could set. Supervisor Leur asked about tracking costs. Supervisor Drebeck proposed an amendment to limit the waiver to only reconnect fees ($160) for septic systems, excluding other zoning and new system fees. The amendment passed and the amended resolution was approved to forward to the county board, though not unanimously.

### Solid Waste Department presentation
Director David Hagen Busher provided an overview of solid waste operations, including the landfill facility, renewable natural gas partnership with Vidi Energy, leachate treatment challenges, and upcoming capital projects. He noted the department accepts about 1,000 tons daily and operates with 11 FTEs. Marilyn Bend praised the department's work helping Ringle tornado victims.

### Parks, Recreation and Forestry Department presentation
County Forest Administrator Tom Lovelin presented on behalf of Director Jamie, covering the department's 44 full-time and 120 seasonal employees, 17 county parks, 30,700 acres of county forest, and services provided to the City of Wausau. He noted Nine Mile County Forest generates $3.25 million for the local economy from out-of-town visitors. Supervisor Sefeld announced Tom Lovelin's retirement effective June 1st after nearly 30 years of service.

## Public Comment
No public comment was offered.

## Action Items
- Rezoning for James Offer and David Tract (Urban Residential/General Agriculture to Rural Residential in Town of Green Valley) forwarded to County Board with approval recommendation
- Rezoning for Jeffrey and Cynthia Schmidtz (Conservancy Recreation to General Agriculture in Town of McMillan) forwarded to County Board with approval recommendation
- Amended resolution to waive only septic system reconnect fees for April 17, 2026 tornado-impacted properties forwarded to County Board
- Staff to track costs and requests related to tornado fee waivers for future policy development
- Conservation department to schedule special educational meeting on conservation topics for summer
