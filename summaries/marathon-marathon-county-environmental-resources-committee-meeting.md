## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Environmental Resources Committee approved two rezoning petitions unanimously and voted to forward a modified fee waiver resolution to the county board that will only waive reconnect fees for septic systems (not other zoning or POWTS fees) for properties damaged by the April 17, 2026 tornado. The committee also received educational presentations from Solid Waste, Parks Recreation and Forestry, and Conservation Planning and Zoning departments.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of March 31, 2026 minutes
Supervisor Sefeld made a motion to approve the minutes, seconded by Supervisor Drebeck. The motion carried unanimously with no discussion.

### Tim Veland rezoning petition - Town of Green Valley
Garrett Pogle, Land Use Specialist, presented the staff report for rezoning from urban residential and general agriculture to rural residential for property owned by James Offer and David Tract. The Town of Green Valley recommended approval on March 10, 2026, and CPZ staff recommended approval. No public comment was offered. Supervisor Drebeck moved to approve based on findings of fact and town approval, seconded by Supervisor Kurt. Motion carried unanimously.

### Jeffrey and Cynthia Schmidtz rezoning petition - Town of McMillan
Garrett Pogle presented the petition to rezone from Conservancy Recreation to General Agriculture. Supervisor Leur asked about the conservancy nature and potential loss of natural areas. Pogle explained the property contains a home and horse riding stable, and rezoning would make the home a conforming use rather than non-conforming. No natural areas would be lost. Town of McMillan recommended approval April 20, 2026. Supervisor Sefeld moved to approve, seconded by Supervisor Drew. Motion carried unanimously.

### Fee waiver for April 17, 2026 severe weather victims
Director Lori Mskimmons presented a proposal to waive county zoning and POWTS fees for properties damaged by the tornado affecting towns of Ringle, Eastston, villages of Kronenwetter and Weston. Supervisor Drew questioned where to draw the line for future events. Supervisor Sefelt asked about potential levy impacts. Marilyn Bend asked about septic reconnection costs. After discussion, Supervisor Sefeld moved to forward the resolution. Supervisor Drebeck amended the motion to only waive reconnect fees ($160) for POWTS systems, not other zoning or POWTS fees. The amendment was seconded by Supervisor Kurt and passed. The amended resolution to forward to county board passed, though not unanimously.

### Solid Waste Department presentation
Director Dave Hagen Busher presented an overview of the department's operations, including the landfill history since 1980, the renewable natural gas partnership with Vidi Energy generating royalties for the county, and the upcoming $6 million expansion project. He highlighted challenges with leachate treatment due to PFAS regulations requiring on-site treatment by 2032, and lithium-ion battery fires occurring about once weekly.

### Parks, Recreation and Forestry presentation
Tom Lovelin, County Forest Administrator, presented the department overview covering 17 county parks, 30,700 acres of county forest, and services provided to the City of Wausau. He noted Nine Mile County Forest generates $3.25 million for the local economy from out-of-town visitors. Supervisor Sefeld announced Lovelin is retiring June 1st after nearly 30 years of service.

## Public Comment
No public comment was offered.

## Action Items
- Forward amended resolution to county board waiving only POWTS reconnect fees ($160) for properties damaged by April 17, 2026 severe weather
- Rezoning for James Offer and David Tract property forwarded to county board for approval
- Rezoning for Jeffrey and Cynthia Schmidtz property forwarded to county board for approval
- CPZ to track costs and requests related to tornado fee waivers for future policy development
- County to reapply for Clean Water Fund grant for leachate treatment facility by October 2025 for 2027 funding
