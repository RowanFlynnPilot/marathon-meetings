## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Environmental Resources Committee approved two rezoning petitions and voted to forward a modified fee waiver resolution to the county board that will only cover septic system reconnect fees for properties impacted by the April 17, 2026 tornado. The committee also received educational presentations from Solid Waste, Parks Recreation and Forestry, and Conservation Planning and Zoning departments.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of March 31, 2026 committee minutes
Supervisor Sefeld made a motion to approve the minutes, seconded by Supervisor Drebeck. The motion carried unanimously with no discussion.

### Tim Veland rezoning petition (Green Valley)
Land Use Specialist Garrett Pogle presented the petition to rezone land from Urban Residential and General Agriculture to Rural Residential for James Offer and David Tract in the Town of Green Valley. The Town Board recommended approval on March 10, 2026, and staff recommended approval as the rezoning meets all criteria and is consistent with the comprehensive plan. No public testimony was offered. Supervisor Drebeck moved to approve based on findings of fact and town approval, seconded by Supervisor Kurt. Motion carried unanimously.

### Jeffrey and Cynthia Schmidtz rezoning petition (McMillan)
Garrett Pogle presented the petition to rezone from Conservancy Recreation to General Agriculture in the Town of McMillan. Supervisor Leur asked about the conservancy nature of the property; Pogle explained the property contains a home and horse riding stable, with the rezoning intended to make these conforming uses rather than non-conforming. No natural areas are being lost. The Town Board recommended approval on April 20, 2026. No public testimony was offered. Supervisor Sefeld moved to approve, seconded by Supervisor Drew. Motion carried unanimously.

### Fee waiver for April 17, 2026 severe weather impacted properties
Director Lori Mskimmons presented a proposal to waive county zoning and septic system fees for properties impacted by the April 17 tornado, noting the governor declared a state of emergency. Significant debate ensued. Supervisor Drew questioned where to draw the line for future incidents. Supervisor Drebeck expressed concern about fairness to individual tornado victims not in declared disaster areas. Marilyn Bend asked about hookup costs for septic systems. Eric Vogel questioned whether homeowners insurance would cover these fees. Supervisor Sefeld moved to forward the resolution; Supervisor Leur seconded. Supervisor Drebeck then proposed an amendment to limit the waiver to only septic system reconnect fees ($160), excluding other zoning and pouts fees. The amendment was seconded by Supervisor Kurt and passed. The amended resolution then passed on a non-unanimous vote to forward to the county board.

### Solid Waste Department presentation
Director Dave Hagen Busher provided an educational overview of the Solid Waste Department, covering the landfill's history since 1980, the renewable natural gas partnership with Vidi Energy launched in 2023, current challenges with leachate treatment and PFAS regulations requiring pre-treatment by 2032, and upcoming capital projects totaling millions of dollars. He noted the department accepts about 1,000 tons daily with 11 FTE staff.

### Parks, Recreation and Forestry Department presentation
County Forest Administrator Tom Lovelin presented on behalf of Director Jamie, who had an emergency. He outlined the department's management of 17 county parks, 30,700 acres of county forest across 10 units, and services provided to the City of Wausau. He highlighted the Nine Mile County Forest economic impact study showing $3.25 million generated for Marathon County from out-of-town visitors. Supervisor Sefeld announced that Tom Lovelin is retiring June 1st after nearly 30 years of service.

## Public Comment
No public comment was offered.

## Action Items
- Rezoning from Urban Residential and General Agriculture to Rural Residential approved for James Offer and David Tract in Town of Green Valley - forwarded to County Board
- Rezoning from Conservancy Recreation to General Agriculture approved for Jeffrey and Cynthia Schmidtz in Town of McMillan - forwarded to County Board
- Modified fee waiver resolution covering only septic system reconnect fees for April 17, 2026 tornado-impacted properties forwarded to County Board for consideration
- Town of Green Valley recommended to update comprehensive plan to reflect zoning changes if applicable
- Town of McMillan recommended to update comprehensive plan to reflect zoning changes if applicable
