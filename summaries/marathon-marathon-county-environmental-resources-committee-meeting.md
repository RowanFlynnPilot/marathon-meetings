## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Environmental Resources Committee approved two rezoning petitions and voted to forward a modified fee waiver resolution to the county board that will only waive reconnect fees (not other zoning or septic fees) for properties damaged by the April 17, 2026 tornado. The committee also received educational presentations from the Solid Waste, Parks Recreation and Forestry, and Conservation Planning and Zoning departments.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of March 31, 2026 committee minutes
Supervisor Sefeld made a motion to approve the minutes with Supervisor Drebeck seconding. The motion carried with no opposition.

### Tim Veland rezoning petition - Town of Green Valley
Garrett Pogle presented the staff report for rezoning from Urban Residential and General Agriculture to Rural Residential for a land division. The Town of Green Valley recommended approval on March 10, 2026. No public testimony was offered. Supervisor Drebeck moved to approve based on findings of fact and conclusions of law, seconded by Supervisor Kurt. Motion carried unanimously.

### Jeffrey and Cynthia Schmidtz rezoning petition - Town of McMillan
The petition sought to rezone from Conservancy Recreation to General Agriculture. Supervisor Leur asked about the conservancy nature of the property; Garrett Pogle explained it currently has a home and horse riding stable, and the rezone would make the non-conforming home use conforming. No natural areas would be lost. Town of McMillan recommended approval on April 20, 2026. Supervisor Sefeld moved to approve, seconded by Supervisor Drew. Motion carried unanimously.

### Fee waiver for April 17, 2026 severe weather properties
Director Lori Mskimmons presented a proposal to waive county zoning and septic fees for tornado-damaged properties. Supervisor Drew questioned fairness to property owners not in declared disaster areas. Supervisor Drebeck expressed concern about precedent and costs. Eric Vogel asked if insurance would cover fees. After discussion, Supervisor Drebeck moved to amend the resolution to only waive reconnect fees ($160) for existing septic systems, not other permits. The amendment passed. The amended resolution was then approved to forward to the county board, though the vote was not unanimous.

## Public Comment
No public comment was offered.
