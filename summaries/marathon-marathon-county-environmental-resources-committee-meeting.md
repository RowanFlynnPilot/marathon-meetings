## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Environmental Resources Committee approved two rezoning petitions unanimously and voted to forward a modified fee waiver resolution to the county board that will only waive reconnect fees for septic systems (not other zoning or pouts fees) for properties damaged by the April 17, 2026 tornado. The committee also received educational presentations from Solid Waste, Parks Recreation and Forestry, and Conservation Planning and Zoning departments.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of March 31, 2026 committee minutes
Supervisor Sefeld made a motion to approve the minutes, seconded by Supervisor Drebeck. The motion carried with no opposition.

### Rezoning petition 5A1: James Offer and David Trait property
Garrett Pogle presented the staff report for rezoning from urban residential and general agriculture to rural residential in the Town of Green Valley. The Town Board recommended approval on March 10, 2026. No public testimony was offered. Supervisor Drebeck moved to approve based on findings of fact and town approval, seconded by Supervisor Kurt. Motion carried unanimously.

### Rezoning petition 5A2: Jeffrey and Cynthia Schmidtz property
Garrett Pogle presented the petition to rezone from Conservancy Recreation to General Agriculture in the Town of McMillan. Supervisor Lever asked about the conservancy nature; Pogle explained it currently has a home and horse riding stable, and the rezone would make the non-conforming home a conforming use. The Town Board recommended approval on April 20, 2026. No public testimony was offered. Supervisor Sefeld moved to approve, seconded by Supervisor Drew. Motion carried unanimously.

### Fee waivers for April 17, 2026 severe weather damage
Director Lori Mskimmons presented a proposal to waive county zoning and septic fees for properties damaged by the tornado in Ringle, Kronenwetter, Easton, and Weston areas. Supervisor Drew questioned where to draw the line for future events. Supervisor Drebeck proposed an amendment to only waive reconnect fees for septic systems ($160), not other fees. The amendment was seconded by Supervisor K and passed. The amended resolution to forward to county board also passed, though not unanimously.

### Solid Waste Department presentation
Director Dave Hagen Busher provided an overview of the department's operations, explaining the landfill accepts about 1,000 tons daily, partnerships with Portage and Shawano counties, the renewable natural gas project with Vidi Energy that came online in September 2025, and upcoming challenges including a $6 million cell construction project and required leachate treatment by 2032 due to PFAS concerns.

### Parks, Recreation and Forestry Department presentation
Tom Lovelin, County Forest Administrator, presented on behalf of Director Jamie. He noted the department operates both county and city park systems since 1924, manages 17 county parks and approximately 30,700 acres of county forest. Nine Mile County Forest generates $3.25 million annually for the local economy from out-of-town visitors. Lovelin announced his retirement effective June 1st after nearly 30 years of service.

## Public Comment
No public comment was offered.

## Action Items
- Forward amended resolution to county board waiving only septic system reconnect fees ($160) for properties damaged by April 17, 2026 tornado
- Rezoning for James Offer/David Trait property forwarded to county board for final approval
- Rezoning for Jeffrey and Cynthia Schmidtz property forwarded to county board for final approval
- Solid Waste to reapply for clean water fund loan for leachate treatment facility by October 2025 for 2027 funding cycle
- Joe Tucker selected as new County Forest Administrator following Tom Lovelin's retirement
