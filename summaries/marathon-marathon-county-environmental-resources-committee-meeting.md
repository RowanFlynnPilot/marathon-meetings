## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Environmental Resources Committee approved two rezoning petitions unanimously and voted to forward a modified tornado relief fee waiver proposal to the county board. The committee amended the fee waiver to cover only septic system reconnect fees ($160) rather than the broader waiver originally proposed by staff for properties impacted by the April 17, 2026 tornado.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of March 31, 2026 minutes
Supervisor Sefeld moved to approve the minutes, seconded by Supervisor Drebeck. The motion carried unanimously with no discussion.

### Tim Veland rezoning petition (Town of Green Valley)
Garrett Pogle presented the staff report for rezoning from urban residential and general agriculture to rural residential for a proposed land division. The Town of Green Valley recommended approval on March 10, 2026. No public testimony was offered. Supervisor Drebeck moved to approve based on findings of fact and town approval, seconded by Supervisor Kurt. Motion carried unanimously.

### Jeffrey and Cynthia Schmidtz rezoning petition (Town of McMillan)
The petition sought to rezone from conservancy recreation to general agriculture. Supervisor Leur asked about the conservancy designation, and Garrett Pogle explained the property contains a home and horse riding stable, with the rezoning needed to make the home a conforming use. The Town of McMillan recommended approval on April 20, 2026. Supervisor Sefelt moved to approve, seconded by Supervisor Drew. Motion carried unanimously.

### Tornado relief fee waiver consideration
Director Lori Mskimmons presented a proposal to waive county zoning and septic system fees for properties damaged by the April 17, 2026 tornado. Significant debate ensued. Supervisor Drew questioned where to draw the line for future disasters. Supervisor Drebeck expressed concern about fairness to individuals hit by disasters not declared emergencies. Supervisor Sefelt questioned staff time costs and potential levy impacts. Administrator Lance Leonhard clarified this wouldn't require a budget amendment. Supervisor Sefelt moved to approve the full resolution; Supervisor Leur seconded. Supervisor Drebeck then amended the motion to limit fee waivers to only reconnect permits ($160) for existing septic systems, not new system installations or zoning fees. Supervisor Kurt seconded the amendment. The amendment carried. The amended motion then passed, though not unanimously.

## Public Comment
No public comment was offered.

## Action Items
- Forward amended resolution to county board waiving only septic system reconnect fees ($160) for properties damaged by April 17, 2026 tornado
- Two rezoning petitions forwarded to Marathon County Board of Supervisors for final approval
- Staff to track costs and requests related to tornado fee waivers for future policy development
