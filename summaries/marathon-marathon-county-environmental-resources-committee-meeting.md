## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Environmental Resources Committee approved two rezoning petitions and voted to forward a modified fee waiver resolution to the county board to help property owners impacted by the April 17, 2026 tornado. The committee amended the fee waiver to only cover septic system reconnect fees ($160), rejecting broader fee waivers for other zoning and septic permits.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of March 31, 2026 committee minutes
Supervisor Sefeld made a motion to approve the minutes with Supervisor Drebeck seconding. The motion carried unanimously with no discussion.

### Tim Veland rezoning petition (Green Valley)
Garrett Pogle presented the staff report for rezoning from urban residential and general agriculture to rural residential in the Town of Green Valley. The Town Board recommended approval on March 10, 2026. No public testimony was offered for or against. Supervisor Drebeck moved to approve based on findings of fact and town approval, seconded by Supervisor Kurt. Motion carried unanimously.

### Jeffrey and Cynthia Schmidtz rezoning petition (McMillan)
The petition sought to rezone from Conservancy Recreation to General Agriculture in the Town of McMillan. Supervisor Leur asked about the conservancy designation, and staff explained the property contains a home and horse riding stable, with the rezoning needed to make the home a conforming use. Staff confirmed no natural areas would be lost. The Town Board recommended approval April 20, 2026. Supervisor Sefeld moved to approve, seconded by Supervisor Drew. Motion carried unanimously.

### Fee waiver consideration for April 17, 2026 severe weather impacts
Director Lori Mskimmons presented a proposal to waive county zoning and septic fees for properties impacted by the tornado. Significant debate ensued. Supervisor Drew questioned the precedent and fairness to others not in declared disaster areas. Supervisor Drebeck expressed concern about costs and questioned whether the county was truly financially constrained given recent fee increases. Marilyn Bend asked about mound system costs. Eric Vogel questioned whether homeowners insurance would cover fees. Supervisor Drebeck moved to amend the resolution to only waive reconnect fees ($160), not other zoning or septic permit fees. The amendment was seconded by Supervisor K and passed. The amended resolution then passed on a non-unanimous vote to forward to the county board.

### Solid Waste Department overview
Director David Hagenbusher presented an educational overview covering the department's 16-year history, landfill operations, the renewable natural gas partnership with Vidi Energy launched in 2023, and upcoming capital projects including an $6 million cell expansion and leachate treatment facility required by 2032 under new state law. He noted lithium-ion battery fires occur about once weekly at the facility.

### Parks, Recreation, and Forestry overview
Tom Lovelin, County Forest Administrator, presented on behalf of Director Jamie who had an emergency. He outlined the department's 44 full-time and 120 seasonal staff serving both county and city systems. He noted Nine Mile County Forest generates $3.25 million for the local economy from out-of-town visitors. Supervisor Sefeld announced Tom Lovelin is retiring June 1st after nearly 30 years of service, with Joe Tucker selected as his replacement.

## Public Comment
No public comment was offered.

## Action Items
- Rezoning for James Offer and David Tract (Green Valley) approved and forwarded to county board
- Rezoning for Jeffrey and Cynthia Schmidtz (McMillan) approved and forwarded to county board
- Modified fee waiver resolution (reconnect fees only) forwarded to county board for April 17, 2026 tornado-impacted properties
- CPZ staff to track costs and requests related to fee waivers for future policy consideration
- Conservation services educational presentation planned for summer meeting
