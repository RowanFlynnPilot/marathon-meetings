## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Environmental Resources Committee approved two rezoning petitions unanimously and voted to forward a modified fee waiver proposal to the full county board that would only waive reconnect fees for septic systems damaged by the April 17, 2026 tornado, rather than the broader fee waiver originally proposed by staff. The committee also received educational presentations from Solid Waste, Parks Recreation and Forestry, and Conservation Planning and Zoning departments.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of March 31, 2026 minutes
Supervisor Sefeld made a motion to approve the minutes, seconded by Supervisor Drebeck. The motion carried unanimously with no discussion.

### Rezoning - James Offer and David Tract
Garrett Pogle presented the staff report for rezoning from urban residential and general agriculture to rural residential in the town of Green Valley. The town board recommended approval on March 10, 2026. No public testimony was offered. Supervisor Drebeck moved to approve based on findings of fact and the town's approval, seconded by Supervisor Kurt. Motion carried unanimously.

### Rezoning - Jeffrey and Cynthia Schmidtz
The petition sought to rezone from conservancy recreation to general agriculture in McMillan township. Supervisor Leur asked about the conservancy nature of the property; staff explained it currently has a home and horse riding stable, and rezoning would make the structures conforming uses. No natural areas would be lost. Supervisor Sefelt moved to approve, seconded by Supervisor Drew. Motion carried unanimously.

### Severe weather fee waiver consideration
Staff proposed waiving county zoning and septic fees for properties damaged by the April 17 tornado, affecting up to 149 parcels. Significant committee debate ensued. Supervisor Drew questioned where to draw the line for future events. Supervisor Sefelt expressed concern about unanticipated expenses and potential levy impacts. Supervisor Drebeck raised fairness concerns about individual tornado victims not receiving similar treatment. After discussion, Supervisor Drebeck amended the motion to limit fee waivers only to reconnect fees ($160) for existing septic systems, excluding new system permits and other fees. The amendment was seconded by Supervisor Kurt and passed. The amended resolution was then approved to forward to the county board, though not unanimously.

### Solid Waste Department presentation
Director Dave Hagen Busher provided an overview covering landfill operations, the renewable natural gas partnership with Vidi Energy, lithium-ion battery fire challenges, leachate treatment requirements by 2032, and upcoming capital projects including $6 million for cell construction.

### Parks, Recreation and Forestry presentation
Tom Lovelin, county forest administrator, presented on behalf of Director Jamie. He noted this is his last committee meeting before retiring June 1 after 30 years. The department operates 17 county parks, 30,700 acres of county forest, and provides services to the City of Wausau. Nine Mile County Forest generates $3.25 million annually for the local economy from out-of-town visitors.

## Public Comment
No public comment was offered.

- Forward Jeffrey and Cynthia Schmidtz rezoning to county board for approval
