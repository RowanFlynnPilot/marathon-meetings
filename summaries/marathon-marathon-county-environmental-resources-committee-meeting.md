## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Environmental Resources Committee approved two rezoning petitions unanimously and voted to forward a modified fee waiver resolution to the county board that will waive only septic system reconnect fees for properties damaged by the April 17, 2026 tornado. The committee also received educational presentations from Solid Waste, Parks Recreation and Forestry, and Conservation Planning and Zoning departments.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of March 31st, 2026 committee minutes
Supervisor Sefeld made a motion to approve the minutes with Supervisor Drebeck seconding. The motion carried unanimously with no discussion.

### Rezoning petition - James Offer and David Trait (Urban Residential and General Agriculture to Rural Residential)
Land Use Specialist Garrett Pogle presented the staff report for property in the Town of Green Valley. The Town Board recommended approval on March 10, 2026. Staff found the rezoning consistent with the comprehensive plan and met all criteria. Supervisor Drebeck moved to approve based on findings of fact and conclusions of law, seconded by Supervisor Kurt. The motion carried unanimously.

### Rezoning petition - Jeffrey and Cynthia Schmidtz (Conservancy Recreation to General Agriculture)
Garrett Pogle presented the petition for property in the Town of McMillan. Supervisor Leur asked about the conservancy nature of the property; Pogle explained there is a home and horse riding stable, and the rezoning would make the home a conforming use rather than non-conforming. No natural areas would be lost. The Town Board recommended approval on April 20, 2026. Supervisor Sefelt moved to approve, seconded by Supervisor Drew. The motion carried unanimously.

### Fee waiver for April 17, 2026 severe weather impacted properties
Director Lori Mskimmons presented a proposal to waive county zoning and septic system fees for tornado-damaged properties. Supervisor Drew questioned where to draw the line for future events. Supervisor Drebeck expressed concern about fairness to those affected by smaller disasters not declared emergencies. Eric Vogel asked if homeowners insurance would cover fees. Supervisor Sefelt moved to forward the resolution, seconded by Supervisor Leur. Supervisor Drebeck then proposed an amendment to limit the waiver to only reconnect fees ($160) for existing septic systems, excluding other pouts and zoning fees. The amendment was seconded by Supervisor Kurt and carried unanimously. The amended resolution then passed with one opposing vote to forward to the county board.

### Solid Waste Department presentation
Director David Hagenbusher provided an overview of the department's operations including the landfill facility, renewable natural gas partnership with Vidi Energy, and upcoming leachate treatment requirements mandated by state law by 2032. He noted significant capital expenses including $6 million for 11-acre construction and equipment costs of $1.5 million per compactor. Marilyn Bend commended the department for keeping costs competitive.

### Parks, Recreation and Forestry Department presentation
County Forest Administrator Tom Lovelin presented on behalf of Director Jamie who had an emergency. He explained the department operates both county and City of Wausau park systems with 44 full-time and 120 seasonal employees. A Nine Mile County Forest study showed out-of-town visitors generate $3.25 million for the county economy. Lovelin announced his retirement effective June 1st after nearly 30 years. Supervisor Sefelt praised the department's work.

## Public Comment
No public comment was offered.

## Action Items
- Forward rezoning approval for James Offer and David Trait property to Marathon County Board of Supervisors
- Forward rezoning approval for Jeffrey and Cynthia Schmidtz property to Marathon County Board of Supervisors
- Forward amended resolution to county board waiving only septic system reconnect fees ($160) for properties damaged by April 17, 2026 severe weather
- Town of Green Valley and Town of McMillan should update comprehensive plans to reflect zoning changes if applicable
- Solid Waste to resubmit clean water fund application for leachate treatment facility by October 2026 for 2027 funding
