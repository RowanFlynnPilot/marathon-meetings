## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Environmental Resources Committee approved two rezoning petitions unanimously and voted to forward a modified fee waiver resolution for tornado-affected properties to the county board. The committee also received educational presentations from the Solid Waste, Parks Recreation and Forestry, and Conservation Planning and Zoning departments.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of March 31, 2026 committee minutes
Supervisor Sefeld made a motion to approve the minutes, seconded by Supervisor Drebeck. The motion carried unanimously with no discussion.

### Tim Veland rezoning petition (Town of Green Valley)
Land Use Specialist Garrett Pogle presented the staff report for rezoning from Urban Residential and General Agriculture to Rural Residential for a proposed land division. The Town of Green Valley recommended approval on March 10, 2026. No public testimony was offered. Supervisor Drebeck moved to approve based on findings of fact, conclusions of law, and the town's approval. Supervisor Kurt seconded. The motion carried unanimously.

### Jeffrey and Cynthia Schmidz rezoning petition (Town of McMillan)
Garrett Pogle presented the petition to rezone from Conservancy Recreation to General Agriculture. Supervisor Leur asked about the conservancy nature of the property; Pogle explained the property contains a home and horse riding stable, and the rezoning makes the existing structures conforming uses rather than preserving any natural area. The Town of McMillan recommended approval on April 20, 2026. No public testimony was offered. Supervisor Sefeld moved to approve, Supervisor Drew seconded. The motion carried unanimously.

### Consideration of fee waiver for April 17, 2026 severe weather
Director Lori Mskimmons presented a proposal to waive county zoning and septic (POWTS) fees for properties damaged by the April 17 tornado, noting the governor's emergency declaration. Significant debate ensued. Supervisor Drew questioned where to draw the line for future events. Supervisor Drebeck expressed concern about fairness to individuals hit by disasters that aren't declared emergencies. Marilyn Bend asked about septic hookup costs. Eric Vogel asked if homeowners insurance would cover fees. Supervisor Sefeld moved to forward the resolution to the county board. Supervisor Leur seconded. Supervisor Drebeck then offered an amendment to limit the waiver to only POWTS reconnect fees ($160), excluding all other zoning and septic fees. Supervisor Kurt seconded the amendment. The amendment carried. The amended motion to forward to the county board then passed, though not unanimously.

### Solid Waste Department presentation
Director Dave Hagen Busher provided an overview of the department's operations, including the landfill's 45-year history, the renewable natural gas partnership with Vidi Energy that began injecting gas in September 2025, and upcoming capital projects including an $6 million cell construction and leachate treatment facility required by state law by 2032. He noted lithium-ion battery fires occur about once a week at the facility.

### Parks, Recreation, and Forestry Department presentation
Tom Lovelin, County Forest Administrator, presented on behalf of Director Jamie who had an emergency. He outlined the department's 44 full-time and 120 seasonal staff, management of 17 county parks and 30,700 acres of county forest. He noted Nine Mile County Forest generates $3.25 million annually for the local economy from out-of-town visitors. Supervisor Sefeld announced that Lovelin is retiring June 1st after nearly 30 years of service.

## Public Comment
No public comment was offered.

## Action Items

- Rezoning for Jeffrey and Cynthia Schmidz property (Town of McMillan) approved and forwarded to Marathon County Board of Supervisors
- Modified fee waiver resolution (limited to POWTS reconnect fees only) forwarded to Marathon County Board of Supervisors for consideration
- Staff to track costs and requests related to any fee waivers granted
- Conservation Planning and Zoning to present at next meeting with Conservation Director Kirsty Heiden
