## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Environmental Resources Committee approved two rezoning petitions unanimously and voted to forward a modified fee waiver resolution for tornado-affected properties to the county board. The fee waiver was amended to only cover septic system reconnect fees ($160) rather than all zoning and POWTS fees as originally proposed. New committee members also received department overviews from Solid Waste, Parks/Recreation/Forestry, and Conservation Planning and Zoning.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of March 31st, 2026 minutes
Supervisor Sefeld made a motion to approve the minutes, seconded by Supervisor Drebeck. The motion carried unanimously with no discussion.

### Rezoning petition - James Offer and David Tract
Land Use Specialist Garrett Pogle presented the staff report for rezoning from Urban Residential and General Agriculture to Rural Residential in the Town of Green Valley. The Town Board recommended approval on March 10, 2026. No public comment was offered. Supervisor Drebeck moved to approve based on findings of fact and conclusions of law, seconded by Supervisor Kurt. Motion carried unanimously.

### Rezoning petition - Jeffrey and Cynthia Schmidtz
Garrett Pogle presented the rezoning request from Conservancy Recreation to General Agriculture in Town of McMillan. Supervisor Leur asked about the conservancy nature of the property; Pogle explained it currently has a home and horse riding stable, and the rezoning makes these conforming uses. He confirmed no natural areas would be lost - the property is home, barns, stables, and pasture. Town Board recommended approval April 20, 2026. Supervisor Sefeld moved to approve, seconded by Supervisor Drew. Motion carried unanimously.

### Fee waiver for April 17, 2026 severe weather victims
Director Lori Miskimmons presented a proposal to waive county zoning and POWTS fees for properties damaged by the April 17th tornado affecting towns of Ringle, Easton, villages of Kronenwetter and Weston. The proposal included a one-year duration and affected up to 149 parcels. Supervisor Drew questioned where to draw the line for future incidents. Supervisor Drebeck expressed concerns about fairness to others not in declared disaster areas. Marilyn Bend asked about mound system costs. After discussion, Supervisor Drebeck offered an amendment to limit the waiver to only reconnect fees ($160 for septic reconnections) rather than all fees. Supervisor Kurt seconded the amendment, which passed. The amended resolution was then approved to forward to the county board, though not unanimously.

### Solid Waste Department overview
Director David Hagenbusher presented on the department's operations, explaining the landfill accepts about 1,000 tons daily with 150-200 transactions. He discussed the renewable natural gas partnership with Vidi Energy generating revenue through gas royalties, upcoming leachate treatment requirements by 2032 due to PFAS concerns, and significant capital expenses including $6 million for 11-acre expansion. He noted hauling costs reached $90,000 in April due to heavy rainfall.

### Parks, Recreation and Forestry overview
Tom Lovelin, County Forest Administrator, presented on behalf of Director Jamie. He explained the department operates both city and county park systems since 1924, managing 17 county parks and 30,700 acres of county forest across 10 units. A Nine Mile County Forest study showed out-of-town visitors generate $3.25 million for the local economy. Lovelin announced his retirement effective June 1st after nearly 30 years, with Joe Tucker selected as his replacement.

## Public Comment
No public comment was offered.
