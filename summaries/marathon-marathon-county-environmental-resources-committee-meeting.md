## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Environmental Resources Committee approved two rezoning petitions and voted to forward a modified fee waiver resolution to the county board that will only waive reconnect fees for septic systems damaged by the April 17, 2026 tornado. The committee also received educational presentations from Solid Waste, Parks Recreation and Forestry, and Conservation Planning and Zoning departments.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of March 31st, 2026 committee minutes
Supervisor Sefeld made a motion to approve the minutes with Supervisor Drebeck seconding. The motion carried with no opposition on a voice vote.

### Tim Veland rezoning petition - Town of Green Valley
Land Use Specialist Garrett Pogle presented the staff report for rezoning from urban residential and general agriculture to rural residential for a proposed land division. The Town of Green Valley recommended approval on March 10, 2026. No public testimony was offered. Supervisor Drebeck moved to approve based on findings of fact, conclusions of law, and town approval. Supervisor Kurt seconded. The motion carried unanimously.

### Jeffrey and Cynthia Schmidz rezoning petition - Town of McMillan
Garrett Pogle presented the petition to rezone from conservancy recreation to general agriculture. Supervisor Leur asked about the conservancy nature and potential loss of natural areas. Pogle explained the property contains a home and horse riding stable, and rezoning would make these conforming uses rather than non-conforming. The Town of McMillan recommended approval on April 20, 2026. No public testimony was offered. Supervisor Sefeld moved to approve, Supervisor Drew seconded. The motion carried unanimously.

### Fee waiver for April 17, 2026 severe weather impacted properties
Director Lori Mskimmons presented a proposal to waive county zoning and septic system fees for tornado-impacted properties. Significant debate ensued about the precedent this would set. Supervisor Drew questioned where to draw the line for future events. Supervisor Drebeck expressed concern about fairness to individual tornado victims not in declared disaster areas. Supervisor Leur asked about budget impacts. Supervisor Drebeck moved to amend the resolution to only waive reconnect fees ($160) for existing septic systems, not fees for new systems or other permits. Supervisor K seconded the amendment, which passed on a voice vote. The amended resolution was then approved on a non-unanimous voice vote to forward to the county board.

### Solid Waste Department presentation
Director David Hagenbusher presented an overview of solid waste operations, explaining the landfill accepts about 1,000 tons daily with 11 FTE staff. He discussed the renewable natural gas partnership with Vidi Energy operational since September 2025, significant capital projects including $6 million expansion construction, and the critical need for on-site leachate treatment by 2032 due to PFAS regulations. He noted hauling costs reached $90,000 in April due to heavy rainfall.

### Parks, Recreation and Forestry Department presentation
County Forest Administrator Tom Lovelin presented on behalf of Director Jamie, who had an emergency. He explained the department operates both city and county park systems with 44 full-time and 120 seasonal employees. A Nine Mile County Forest study showed out-of-town visitors generate $3.25 million for the local economy. Lovelin announced his retirement effective June 1st after nearly 30 years, with Joe Tucker selected as the new county forest administrator. Supervisor Sefeld praised the department's work.

## Public Comment
No public comment was offered. The chair noted no one signed up for public comment.

## Action Items
- Rezoning for James Offer and David Tract property in Town of Green Valley approved and forwarded to county board
- Rezoning for Jeffrey and Cynthia Schmidz property in Town of McMillan approved and forwarded to county board
- Modified fee waiver resolution (reconnect fees only) for tornado-impacted properties forwarded to county board for consideration
- CPZ staff to track costs and requests related to tornado fee waivers for future policy development
- Joe Tucker to assume county forest administrator position following Tom Lovelin's June 1st retirement
