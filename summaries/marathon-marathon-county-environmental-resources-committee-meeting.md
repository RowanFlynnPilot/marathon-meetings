## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Environmental Resources Committee approved two rezoning petitions unanimously and voted to recommend a limited fee waiver for tornado-affected properties, though the committee narrowed the waiver to only cover septic system reconnect fees. The meeting also featured department overview presentations from Solid Waste, Parks Recreation and Forestry, and Conservation Planning and Zoning.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of March 31st, 2026 committee minutes
Supervisor Sefeld made a motion to approve the minutes, seconded by Supervisor Drebeck. The motion carried unanimously with no discussion.

### Tim Veland rezoning petition - Town of Green Valley
Land Use Specialist Garrett Pogle presented the staff report for rezoning from urban residential and general agriculture to rural residential for a proposed land division. The Town of Green Valley recommended approval on March 10, 2026. No public testimony was offered. Supervisor Drebeck moved to approve based on findings of fact, conclusions of law, and town approval, seconded by Supervisor Kurt. The motion carried unanimously.

### Jeffrey and Cynthia Schmidtz rezoning petition - Town of McMillan
Garrett Pogle presented the petition to rezone from Conservancy Recreation to General Agriculture. Supervisor Leur asked about the conservancy designation; Pogle explained the property has a home and horse riding stable, and the rezoning makes these conforming uses rather than non-conforming. He confirmed no natural areas would be lost. The Town of McMillan recommended approval on April 20, 2026. Supervisor Sefelt moved to approve and forward to county board, seconded by Supervisor Drew. The motion carried unanimously.

### Fee waiver for April 17, 2026 severe weather impacted properties
Director Lori Mskimmons presented a proposal to waive county zoning and septic fees for properties damaged by the April 17 tornado affecting Ringle, Kronenwetter, Easton, and Weston. Supervisor Drew questioned where to draw the line for future events. Supervisor Leur asked about tracking costs. Supervisor Drebeck expressed concern about fairness to those affected by non-declared disasters. Eric Vogel asked whether homeowner's insurance would cover fees. Supervisor Drebeck moved to amend the resolution to only waive reconnect fees for septic systems (not other zoning or new system fees), seconded by Supervisor Kurt. The amendment passed. The amended motion to forward to county board passed, but was noted as not unanimous.

### Solid Waste Department overview
Director Dave Hagen Busher presented on solid waste operations including the landfill's 45-year history, the renewable natural gas partnership with Vidi Energy, leachate treatment challenges due to PFAS regulations requiring on-site treatment by 2032, and upcoming capital projects totaling millions of dollars. Marilyn Bend praised the department's work. Supervisor Brook asked about the clean water fund grant application; Hagen Busher said they must reapply for 2027 funding.

### Parks, Recreation, and Forestry Department overview
County Forest Administrator Tom Lovelin presented on behalf of Director Jamie, covering the department's 44 full-time and 120 seasonal employees serving both county and city of Wausau. He highlighted Nine Mile County Forest generating $3.25 million for the local economy from out-of-town visitors. Supervisor Sefelt noted Tom's retirement effective June 1st after nearly 30 years of service. The committee thanked him for his service.

## Public Comment
No public comment was offered.
