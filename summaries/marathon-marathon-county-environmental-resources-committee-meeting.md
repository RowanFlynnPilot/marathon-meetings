## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Environmental Resources Committee approved two rezoning petitions and voted to forward a modified fee waiver resolution to the county board for tornado victims. The fee waiver was amended to only cover septic system reconnect fees ($160) rather than all zoning and septic-related fees originally proposed.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of March 31, 2026 committee minutes
Supervisor Sefeld moved and Supervisor Drebeck seconded approval of the minutes. The motion passed unanimously on voice vote.

### Tim Veland rezoning petition (Town of Green Valley)
Land Use Specialist Garrett Pogle presented the staff report for rezoning from Urban Residential and General Agriculture to Rural Residential. The Town of Green Valley recommended approval on March 10, 2026. No public testimony was offered for, against, or as interested. Supervisor Drebeck moved to approve based on findings of fact and town approval, Supervisor Kurt seconded. Motion carried unanimously.

### Jeffrey and Cynthia Schmidtz rezoning petition (Town of McMillan)
Garrett Pogle presented the request to rezone from Conservancy Recreation to General Agriculture. Supervisor Leur asked about conservancy implications; Pogle explained the property contains a home and horse riding stable, and rezoning would make the home a conforming use rather than non-conforming. No natural areas would be lost. Town of McMillan recommended approval April 20, 2026. No public testimony was offered. Supervisor Sefeld moved to approve, Supervisor Drew seconded. Motion carried unanimously.

### Fee waivers for April 17, 2026 severe weather victims
Director Lori Mskimmons presented a proposal to waive county zoning and septic fees for tornado-impacted properties in towns of Ringle, Easton, and villages of Kronenwetter and Weston. Supervisor Drew questioned where to draw the line for future events. Supervisor Drebeck expressed concerns about fairness to individual disaster victims not in declared areas. After discussion, Supervisor Sefeld moved to forward the resolution; Supervisor Leur seconded. Supervisor Drebeck then amended the motion to only waive reconnect fees ($160) for septic systems, not other zoning or septic fees. Supervisor Kurt seconded the amendment. The amendment passed unanimously. The amended motion to forward to county board passed but was not unanimous.

### Solid Waste Department overview
Director David Hagen Busher presented on department operations, explaining the landfill accepts about 1,000 tons daily with 11 FTEs. He highlighted the renewable natural gas partnership with Vidi Energy generating royalties for the county, discussed leachate treatment challenges requiring a $6 million on-site treatment facility by 2032 due to PFAS regulations, and noted lithium-ion battery fires occur about weekly.

### Parks, Recreation, and Forestry overview
Tom Lovelin, County Forest Administrator, presented for Director Jamie who had an emergency. He noted the department has 44 full-time and 120 seasonal employees serving both city and county. A Nine Mile County Forest study showed out-of-town visitors generate $3.25 million annually. Supervisor Sefeld announced Lovelin is retiring June 1st after nearly 30 years, with Joe Tucker selected as successor.

## Public Comment
No public comment was offered.
