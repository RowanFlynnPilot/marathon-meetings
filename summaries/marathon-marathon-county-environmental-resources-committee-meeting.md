## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Environmental Resources Committee approved two rezoning petitions unanimously and voted to forward a modified fee waiver resolution to the county board that will only waive reconnect fees for septic systems for properties damaged by the April 17, 2026 tornado. The committee also received educational presentations from Solid Waste, Parks Recreation and Forestry, and Conservation Planning and Zoning departments.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of March 31, 2026 committee minutes
Supervisor Sefeld made a motion to approve the minutes with Supervisor Drebeck seconding. The motion carried unanimously with no discussion.

### Tim Veland rezoning petition - Town of Green Valley
Garrett Pogle presented the staff report for rezoning from urban residential and general agriculture to rural residential for a land division. The Town of Green Valley recommended approval on March 10, 2026. No public testimony was offered. Supervisor Drebeck moved to approve based on findings of fact, conclusions of law, and town approval. Supervisor Kurt seconded. Motion carried unanimously.

### Jeffrey and Cynthia Schmidtz rezoning petition - Town of McMillan
Staff presented the petition to rezone from conservancy recreation to general agriculture. Supervisor Leur asked about the conservancy nature and potential loss of natural areas. Garrett Pogle explained the property contains a home and horse riding stable, and rezoning would make these conforming uses. The Town of McMillan recommended approval on April 20, 2026. Supervisor Sefeld moved to approve, Supervisor Drew seconded. Motion carried unanimously.

### Fee waiver for April 17, 2026 severe weather victims
Director Lori Mskimmons presented a proposal to waive county zoning and septic fees for properties damaged by the tornado. Administrator Lance explained this was triggered by the governor's emergency declaration. Supervisor Drew questioned where to draw the line for future events. Supervisor Drebeck raised concerns about fairness to those affected by non-declared disasters. After discussion, Supervisor Drebeck moved to amend the resolution to only waive reconnect fees for septic systems ($160), not other fees. Supervisor Kurt seconded the amendment. The amendment carried. The amended resolution was then approved to forward to the county board, though the vote was not unanimous.

### Department educational presentations
Dave Hagenbusher presented on Solid Waste operations including the renewable natural gas partnership with Vidi Energy, upcoming leachate treatment requirements by 2032, and capital projects. Tom Lovelin presented on Parks, Recreation and Forestry, noting his upcoming retirement June 1st after 30 years. Supervisor Sefeld praised the department and acknowledged Tom's service. Lori Mskimmons began the CPZ presentation before the transcript ended.

## Public Comment
No public comment was offered.
