## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Environmental Resources Committee approved two rezoning petitions unanimously and voted to forward a modified fee waiver resolution to the county board that will only waive reconnect fees for septic systems for properties damaged by the April 17, 2026 tornado. The committee also received educational presentations from the Solid Waste, Parks Recreation and Forestry, and Conservation Planning and Zoning departments.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of March 31st, 2026 committee minutes
Supervisor Sefeld made a motion to approve the minutes, seconded by Supervisor Drebeck. The motion carried unanimously with no discussion.

### Tim Veland rezoning petition - Town of Green Valley
Garrett Pogle presented the staff report for rezoning from urban residential and general agriculture to rural residential for a proposed land division. The Town of Green Valley recommended approval on March 10, 2026. No public testimony was offered in favor, against, or as interested parties. Supervisor Drebeck moved to approve based on findings of fact, conclusions of law, and town approval, seconded by Supervisor Kurt. Motion carried unanimously.

### Jeffrey and Cynthia Schmidtz rezoning petition - Town of McMillan
Garrett Pogle presented the petition to rezone from Conservancy Recreation to General Agriculture. Supervisor Leur asked about the conservancy nature of the property; Pogle explained it currently has a home and riding stable, and the rezoning would make these conforming uses rather than non-conforming. He confirmed no natural area would be lost. The Town of McMillan recommended approval on April 20, 2026. No public testimony was offered. Supervisor Sefelt moved to approve, seconded by Supervisor Drew. Motion carried unanimously.

### Consideration of fee waiver for April 17, 2026 severe weather damage
Director Lori Mskimmons presented a proposal to waive county zoning and septic fees for properties damaged by the April 17 tornado in Ringle, Kronenwetter, Easton, and Weston. Supervisor Drew questioned where to draw the line for future events. Supervisor Drebeck expressed concerns about fairness to property owners not in declared disaster areas. Supervisor Leur asked about budget implications. After debate, Supervisor Drebeck moved to amend the resolution to only waive reconnect fees ($160) for septic systems, not other zoning or septic fees. Supervisor Kurt seconded the amendment. The amendment passed. The amended resolution then passed to forward to the county board, though not unanimously.

### Solid Waste Department presentation
Director Dave Hagen Busher provided an overview of landfill operations, explaining the facility accepts about 1,000 tons daily with 11 staff. He discussed the renewable natural gas partnership with Vidi Energy operational since September 2025, significant capital expenses including $6 million for 11-acre expansion, and leachate treatment requirements by 2032 due to PFAS concerns. The department applied for clean water fund financing but was asked to reapply for 2027 funding.

### Parks, Recreation, and Forestry Department presentation
Tom Lovelin, County Forest Administrator, presented on behalf of Director Jamie. He noted the department has 44 full-time and 120 seasonal employees serving both city and county. Nine Mile County Forest generates $3.25 million annually for Marathon County economy from out-of-town visitors. Supervisor Sefelt announced this was Lovelin's last committee meeting before his June 1st retirement after nearly 30 years, with Joe Tucker selected as his replacement.

## Public Comment
No public comment was offered.

## Action Items
- Rezoning petition for James Offer and David Tract (urban residential/general agriculture to rural residential in Town of Green Valley) approved and forwarded to Marathon County Board of Supervisors
- Rezoning petition for Jeffrey and Cynthia Schmidtz (Conservancy Recreation to General Agriculture in Town of McMillan) approved and forwarded to Marathon County Board of Supervisors
- Modified fee waiver resolution (reconnect fees only for septic systems) for April 17, 2026 tornado-damaged properties forwarded to Marathon County Board of Supervisors
- CPZ staff to track costs and requests related to fee waiver program
- Towns recommended to update comprehensive plans to reflect approved zoning changes
