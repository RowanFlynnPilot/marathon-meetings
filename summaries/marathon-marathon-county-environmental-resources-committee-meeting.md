## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Environmental Resources Committee approved two rezoning petitions and voted to forward a modified resolution waiving only reconnect fees (not other zoning or septic fees) for properties impacted by the April 17, 2026 tornado. The committee also received educational presentations from Solid Waste, Parks Recreation and Forestry, and Conservation Planning and Zoning departments.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of March 31st, 2026 committee minutes
Supervisor Sefeld made a motion to approve the minutes, seconded by Supervisor Drebeck. The motion carried unanimously with no discussion.

### Tim Veland rezoning petition (Green Valley)
Garrett Pogle presented the staff report for rezoning from urban residential and general agriculture to rural residential in the town of Green Valley. The town board recommended approval on March 10, 2026. No public testimony was offered. Supervisor Drebeck moved to approve based on findings of fact and conclusions of law, seconded by Supervisor Kurt. Motion carried unanimously.

### Jeffrey and Cynthia Schmidtz rezoning petition (McMillan)
The petition sought to rezone from Conservancy Recreation to General Agriculture in McMillan. Supervisor Leur asked about the conservancy designation; Garrett Pogle explained it contained a home and horse riding stable, and rezoning would make these conforming uses. No natural areas would be lost. The town board recommended approval on April 20, 2026. Supervisor Sefelt moved to approve, seconded by Supervisor Drew. Motion carried unanimously.

### Fee waivers for severe weather impacted properties
Director Lori Miskimmons presented a proposal to waive county zoning and septic fees for properties damaged by the April 17, 2026 tornado. Significant debate occurred about setting precedent and fairness to future disaster victims. Supervisor Drew questioned where to draw the line for future events. Supervisor Drebeck expressed concern about waving fees while the county recently increased POWTS fees. Supervisor Sefelt moved to forward the resolution; Supervisor Leur seconded. Supervisor Drebeck then proposed an amendment to limit the waiver to only reconnect fees ($160) rather than all fees. The amendment was seconded by Supervisor Kurt and passed. The amended resolution was then approved to forward to the county board, though not unanimously.

### Solid Waste Department overview
Director Dave Hagen Busher presented on solid waste operations, noting the facility accepts about 1,000 tons daily with 11 FTEs. He highlighted the renewable natural gas partnership with Vidi Energy generating royalties for the county, upcoming leachate treatment requirements by 2032 due to PFAS concerns, and significant capital expenses including $6 million for 11-acre expansion. He noted lithium-ion battery fires occur about once weekly.

### Parks, Recreation and Forestry overview
Tom Lovelin, County Forest Administrator, presented on behalf of Director Jamie. The department operates 17 county parks, 30,700 acres of county forest across 10 units, and manages city parks under contract. Nine Mile County Forest generates $3.25 million annually for the local economy from out-of-town visitors. Lovelin announced his retirement effective June 1st after nearly 30 years, with Joe Tucker selected as his replacement.

## Public Comment
No public comment was offered.

## Action Items
- Rezoning from urban residential and general agriculture to rural residential for James Offer and David Tract property in Green Valley forwarded to county board for approval
- Rezoning from Conservancy Recreation to General Agriculture for Jeffrey and Cynthia Schmidtz property in McMillan forwarded to county board for approval
- Modified resolution waiving only POWTS reconnect fees ($160) for tornado-damaged properties forwarded to county board for consideration
- CPZ staff to track costs and requests related to severe weather fee waivers for future policy development
