## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Environmental Resources Committee approved two rezoning petitions and voted to forward a modified fee waiver resolution to the county board that will only waive reconnect fees for septic systems for properties damaged by the April 17, 2026 tornado. The committee also received educational presentations from the Solid Waste, Parks Recreation and Forestry, and Conservation Planning and Zoning departments.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of March 31, 2026 committee minutes
Supervisor Sefeld made a motion to approve the minutes, seconded by Supervisor Drebeck. The motion carried unanimously with no discussion.

### Tim Veland rezoning petition - Town of Green Valley
Garrett Pogle presented the staff report for rezoning from urban residential and general agriculture to rural residential for a proposed land division. The Town of Green Valley recommended approval on March 10, 2026. No public testimony was offered for, against, or as interested. Supervisor Drebeck moved to approve based on findings of fact and conclusions of law, seconded by Supervisor Kurt. Motion carried unanimously.

### Jeffrey and Cynthia Schmidtz rezoning petition - Town of McMillan
The petition sought to rezone from conservancy recreation to general agriculture. Supervisor Leur asked about the conservancy nature of the property; Garrett Pogle explained the property contains a home and horse riding stable, and the rezoning would make these conforming uses rather than non-conforming. He confirmed no natural areas would be lost. The Town of McMillan recommended approval on April 20, 2026. No public testimony was offered. Supervisor Sefeld moved to approve, seconded by Supervisor Drew. Motion carried unanimously.

### Fee waiver for April 17, 2026 severe weather victims
Director Lori Mskimmons presented a proposal to waive county zoning and septic system fees for properties damaged by the tornado affecting Ringle, Kronenwetter, Easton, and Weston. Supervisor Drew questioned whether this creates precedent for future weather events. Supervisor Leur asked about tracking costs and potential levy impacts. Administrator Lance Pliml noted this was triggered by the governor's emergency declaration and staff costs are already budgeted. Supervisor Drebeck expressed concern about fairness to individual tornado victims not in declared disaster areas. After debate, Supervisor Sefeld moved to forward the resolution; Supervisor Leur seconded. Supervisor Drebeck then amended the motion to only waive reconnect fees for septic systems (not other zoning or pouts fees). Supervisor K seconded the amendment. The amendment carried, and the amended motion to forward to county board passed but was not unanimous.

### Solid Waste Department presentation
Director Dave Hagenbusher provided an overview of the landfill operations, explaining the facility accepts about 1,000 tons daily from Marathon, Portage, and Shawano counties. He highlighted the renewable natural gas partnership with Vidi Energy that began injecting gas into the pipeline in September 2025, the significant capital expenses including $6 million for 11-acre expansion, and the requirement to install leachate treatment by 2032 due to PFAS concerns. Marilyn Bend praised the department for keeping private landfill costs competitive.

### Parks, Recreation and Forestry Department presentation
Tom Lovelin, County Forest Administrator, presented on behalf of Director Jamie. He noted the department operates both city and county park systems with 44 full-time and 120 seasonal employees. He highlighted that Nine Mile County Forest generates $3.25 million annually for the county economy from out-of-town visitors. Supervisor Sefeld announced Tom's retirement effective June 1st after nearly 30 years of service. Chair Ritterder thanked him for his years of service.

## Public Comment
No public comment was offered.

## Action Items
- Rezoning from urban residential/general agriculture to rural residential for James Offer and David Tract property in Town of Green Valley approved - forwarded to county board
- Rezoning from conservancy recreation to general agriculture for Jeffrey and Cynthia Schmidtz property in Town of McMillan approved - forwarded to county board
- Modified fee waiver resolution (reconnect fees only) for April 17, 2026 tornado victims forwarded to county board for consideration
- Town of Green Valley and Town of McMillan recommended to update comprehensive plans to reflect zoning changes if applicable
