## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Environmental Resources Committee approved two rezoning petitions unanimously and voted to forward a modified fee waiver resolution to the county board that will only cover septic system reconnect fees (not other zoning or pouts fees) for properties impacted by the April 17, 2026 tornado. The committee also received educational presentations from three county departments.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of March 31, 2026 committee minutes
Supervisor Sefeld made a motion to approve the minutes with Supervisor Drebeck seconding. The motion carried with no opposition.

### Tim Veland rezoning petition - Town of Green Valley
Land Use Specialist Garrett Pogle presented the staff report for rezoning from urban residential and general agriculture to rural residential on behalf of property owners James Offer and David Tract. The Town of Green Valley recommended approval on March 10, 2026. No public testimony was offered. Supervisor Drebeck moved to approve based on findings of fact and town approval, seconded by Supervisor Kurt. Motion carried unanimously.

### Jeffrey and Cynthia Schmidtz rezoning petition - Town of McMillan
Garrett Pogle presented the petition to rezone from conservancy recreation to general agriculture. Supervisor Leur asked about the conservancy nature of the property; Pogle explained there is a home and horse riding stable that are non-conforming uses in conservancy, and rezoning would make them conforming uses. The property is home, barns, stables, and pasture - no natural area being preserved. Town of McMillan recommended approval April 20, 2026. No public testimony. Supervisor Sefeld moved to approve, Supervisor Drew seconded. Motion carried unanimously.

### Fee waiver for April 17, 2026 severe weather impacted properties
Director Lori Mskimmons presented staff recommendation to waive county zoning and pouts fees for tornado-impacted properties. County Administrator Lance clarified this follows the governor's emergency declaration. Supervisor Drew questioned where to draw the line for future events. Supervisor Drebeck questioned fairness to individuals affected by disasters not declared emergencies. Eric Vogel asked if homeowners insurance would cover fees. Supervisor Drebeck moved to amend the resolution to only waive reconnect fees (not other zoning or pouts fees), seconded by Supervisor Kurt. Amendment passed. Supervisor Sefeld moved to forward the amended resolution to county board, seconded by Supervisor Lam. Motion carried but not unanimously.

### Solid Waste Department overview
Director David Hagenbusher presented department overview including the landfill history, tipping fee breakdown, renewable natural gas partnership with Vidi Energy, leachate treatment challenges, and upcoming capital projects including $6 million cell construction and on-site leachate treatment facility. He noted lithium-ion batteries cause weekly fires at the facility. Marilyn Bend commented the county is fortunate to have the landfill to keep private facilities' prices in line.

### Parks, Recreation and Forestry Department overview
Tom Lovelin, County Forest Administrator, presented on behalf of Director Jamie. The department operates Wausau and Marathon County park systems since 1924, manages 30,700 acres of county forest, and has 44 full-time and 120 seasonal employees. Nine Mile County Forest generates $3.25 million for the local economy from out-of-town visitors. Supervisor Sefeld announced Tom Lovelin is retiring June 1st after nearly 30 years of service, with Joe Tucker selected as the new county forest administrator.

## Public Comment
No public comment was offered.
