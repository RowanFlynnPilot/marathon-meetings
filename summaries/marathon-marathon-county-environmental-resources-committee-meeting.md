## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Environmental Resources Committee approved two rezoning petitions unanimously and voted to forward a modified fee waiver resolution to the county board that will only waive reconnect fees for septic systems damaged by the April 17, 2026 tornado. The committee also received educational presentations from Solid Waste, Parks Recreation and Forestry, and Conservation Planning and Zoning departments.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of March 31, 2026 committee minutes
Supervisor Sefeld made the motion to approve and Supervisor Drebeck seconded. The motion carried unanimously with no discussion.

### Tim Veland rezoning petition (Town of Green Valley)
Garrett Pogle presented the staff report for rezoning from urban residential and general agriculture to rural residential for a land division. The Town of Green Valley recommended approval on March 10, 2026. No public testimony was offered. Supervisor Drebeck moved to approve based on findings of fact and conclusions of law, Supervisor Kurt seconded. Motion carried unanimously.

### Jeffrey and Cynthia Schmidtz rezoning petition (Town of McMillan)
Garrett Pogle presented the rezoning from Conservancy Recreation to General Agriculture. Supervisor Leur asked about the conservancy designation, and Pogle explained the property has a home and horse riding stable, with the rezoning needed to make the home a conforming use. The Town of McMillan recommended approval on April 20, 2026. No public testimony was offered. Supervisor Sefeld moved to approve, Supervisor Drew seconded. Motion carried unanimously.

### Fee waiver for severe weather impacted properties
Director Lori Mskimmons presented a proposal to waive county zoning and septic fees for properties damaged by the April 17, 2026 tornado. Significant debate ensued. Supervisor Drew questioned where to draw the line for future events. Supervisor Drebeck expressed concern about fairness to individual tornado victims not in declared disaster areas. Supervisor Sefeld questioned if this could lead to future levy increases. Eric Vogel asked about insurance coverage. Supervisor Sefeld moved to approve, Supervisor Leur seconded. Supervisor Drebeck then amended to only waive reconnect fees ($160) for septic systems, not other permits. Supervisor Kurt seconded the amendment. The amendment carried unanimously. The amended motion to forward to county board passed, though not unanimously.

### Department educational presentations
Dave Hagenbusher presented on Solid Waste operations, covering the landfill's 45-year history, tipping fee structure, renewable natural gas partnership with Vidi Energy, and upcoming $6 million leachate treatment project required by 2032. Tom Lovelin presented on Parks, Recreation and Forestry, noting the department serves both county and city of Wausau with 44 full-time and 120 seasonal employees. Nine Mile County Forest generates $3.25 million annually for the local economy from out-of-town visitors. Lovelin announced his retirement effective June 1st after 30 years of service.

## Public Comment
No public comment was offered.
