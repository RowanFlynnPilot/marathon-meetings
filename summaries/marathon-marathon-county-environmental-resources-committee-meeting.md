## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Environmental Resources Committee approved two rezoning petitions unanimously and voted to forward a modified fee waiver proposal for tornado-damaged properties to the county board. The committee also received educational presentations from Solid Waste, Parks Recreation and Forestry, and Conservation Planning and Zoning departments.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of March 31, 2026 committee minutes
Supervisor Sefeld made a motion to approve the minutes with Supervisor Drebeck providing the second. The motion carried unanimously with no discussion.

### Tim Veland rezoning petition - Town of Green Valley
Land Use Specialist Garrett Pogle presented the staff report for rezoning from urban residential and general agriculture to rural residential for a proposed land division. The Town of Green Valley recommended approval on March 10, 2026. No public comment was offered. Supervisor Drebeck moved to approve based on findings of fact, conclusions of law, and town approval. Supervisor Kurt seconded. Motion carried unanimously.

### Jeffrey and Cynthia Schmidtz rezoning petition - Town of McMillan
Garrett Pogle presented the rezoning request from Conservancy Recreation to General Agriculture. Supervisor Leur asked about the conservancy nature of the property; Pogle explained the property contains a home and horse riding stable, and the rezoning would make the existing structures conforming uses. No natural areas would be lost. The Town of McMillan recommended approval on April 20, 2026. No public comment was offered. Supervisor Sefeld moved to approve; Supervisor Drew seconded. Motion carried unanimously.

### Fee waivers for April 17, 2026 severe weather victims
Director Lori Mskimmons presented a proposal to waive county zoning and septic fees for properties damaged by the April 17 tornado affecting towns of Ringle, Easton, and villages of Kronenwetter and Weston. Supervisor Drew questioned where to draw the line for future events. Supervisor Leur asked about tracking costs. Supervisor Drebeck raised concerns about fairness to other disaster victims not in declared emergency areas. Eric Vogel asked about insurance coverage. After debate, Supervisor Drebeck moved to amend the proposal to only waive reconnect fees ($160) for septic systems, not other zoning or septic fees. Supervisor Kurt seconded the amendment. The amendment carried. The amended resolution then passed on a non-unanimous vote to forward to the county board.

### Solid Waste Department presentation
Director David Hagenbusher provided an overview of solid waste operations including the landfill history since 1980, the renewable natural gas partnership with Vidi Energy generating revenue since September 2025, and upcoming challenges including leachate treatment requirements by 2032. He noted they accept about 1,000 tons daily and work with Portage and Shawano counties. Marilyn Bend praised the department's work during the Ringle tornado response.

### Parks, Recreation and Forestry Department presentation
County Forest Administrator Tom Lovelin presented on behalf of Director Jamie, covering the department's 44 full-time and 120 seasonal employees serving both county and city of Wausau. He highlighted Nine Mile County Forest generating $3.25 million annually for the local economy from out-of-town visitors. Supervisor Sefeld announced Tom Lovelin is retiring June 1st after nearly 30 years of service, with Joe Tucker selected as his replacement.

## Public Comment
No public comment was offered. No one signed up for public comment at the beginning of the meeting, and no members of the public spoke during the two rezoning public hearings.

## Action Items
- Rezoning approved for James Offer and David Tract property from urban residential/general agriculture to rural residential in Town of Green Valley - forwarded to county board
- Rezoning approved for Jeffrey and Cynthia Schmidtz property from Conservancy Recreation to General Agriculture in Town of McMillan - forwarded to county board
- Modified fee waiver resolution (reconnect fees only) for April 17, 2026 tornado-impacted properties forwarded to county board for consideration
- Staff directed to track costs and requests related to fee waivers for potential future policy development
