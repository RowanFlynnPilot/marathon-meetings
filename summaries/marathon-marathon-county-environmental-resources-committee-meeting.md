## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Environmental Resources Committee approved two rezoning petitions and voted to forward a modified fee waiver resolution to the county board that will only waive reconnect fees for properties impacted by the April 17, 2026 tornado. The committee also received educational presentations from the Solid Waste, Parks Recreation and Forestry, and Conservation Planning and Zoning departments.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of March 31, 2026 committee minutes
Supervisor Sefeld made a motion to approve the minutes, seconded by Supervisor Drebeck. The motion carried with no opposition.

### Tim Veland rezoning petition - Town of Green Valley
Garrett Pogle presented the staff report for rezoning from Urban Residential and General Agriculture to Rural Residential for a proposed land division. The Town of Green Valley recommended approval on March 10, 2026. No public testimony was offered. Supervisor Drebeck moved to approve based on findings of fact and conclusions of law, seconded by Supervisor Kurt. The motion passed unanimously.

### Jeffrey and Cynthia Schmidtz rezoning petition - Town of McMillan
The petition sought to rezone from Conservancy Recreation to General Agriculture. Supervisor Leur asked about the conservancy designation, and Garrett Pogle explained the property contains a home and horse riding stable, with the home being a non-conforming use under current zoning. The rezone would make all structures conforming. The Town of McMillan recommended approval on April 20, 2026. Supervisor Sefelt moved to approve, seconded by Supervisor Drew. The motion passed unanimously.

### Fee waiver for tornado-impacted properties
Director Lori Mskimmons presented a proposal to waive county zoning and pouts fees for properties damaged by the April 17, 2026 tornado. Significant committee debate ensued. Supervisor Drew questioned fairness to individual tornado victims not in declared disaster areas. Supervisor Drebeck expressed concerns about costs and precedent. Eric Vogel asked about insurance coverage. Supervisor Sefelt moved to forward the resolution, seconded by Supervisor Leur. Supervisor Drebeck then amended the motion to only waive reconnect fees (approximately $160 per permit), not other zoning or pouts fees. Supervisor K seconded the amendment. The amendment passed, and the amended resolution passed with one dissenting vote.

### Solid Waste Department presentation
Director Dave Hagenbusher provided an overview of landfill operations, including the renewable natural gas partnership with Vidi Energy that began injecting gas in September 2025. He discussed major challenges including leachate treatment requirements mandated by state law by 2032, lithium-ion battery fires occurring weekly, and significant capital expenses including $6 million for cell construction and $3-4 million for closures.

### Parks, Recreation, and Forestry Department presentation
Tom Lovelin, County Forest Administrator, presented on behalf of Director Jamie. He noted the department operates both Marathon County and City of Wausau park systems with 44 full-time and 120 seasonal employees. A Nine Mile County Forest study found out-of-town visitors generate $3.25 million for the local economy. Supervisor Sefelt announced Tom Lovelin is retiring June 1st after nearly 30 years of service.

## Public Comment
No public comment was offered.

## Action Items

- Both rezoning petitions forwarded to Marathon County Board of Supervisors for final approval

- Conservation Planning and Zoning to schedule summer educational meeting on conservation topics
