## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Environmental Resources Committee approved two rezoning petitions and voted to forward a modified fee waiver resolution to the county board that will only waive reconnect fees (not other zoning or septic fees) for properties damaged by the April 17, 2026 tornado. The committee also received educational presentations from the Solid Waste, Parks Recreation and Forestry, and Conservation Planning and Zoning departments.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of March 31, 2026 committee minutes
Supervisor Sefeld made a motion to approve the minutes with Supervisor Drebeck seconding. The motion carried unanimously with no discussion.

### Tim Veland rezoning petition (Town of Green Valley)
Land Use Specialist Garrett Pogle presented the staff report for rezoning from Urban Residential and General Agriculture to Rural Residential for a proposed land division. The Town of Green Valley recommended approval on March 10, 2026. No public testimony was offered for, against, or as interested. Supervisor Drebeck moved to approve based on findings of fact, conclusions of law, and town approval. Supervisor Kurt seconded. The motion carried unanimously.

### Jeffrey and Cynthia Schmidtz rezoning petition (Town of McMillan)
Garrett Pogle presented the petition to rezone from Conservancy Recreation to General Agriculture. Supervisor Leur asked about the conservancy nature of the property; Pogle explained the property contains a home and horse riding stable, and the rezone would make the home a conforming use rather than non-conforming. The Town of McMillan recommended approval on April 20, 2026. No public testimony was offered. Supervisor Sefeld moved to approve and forward to county board. Supervisor Drew seconded. The motion carried unanimously.

### Fee waivers for April 17, 2026 severe weather damage
Director Lori Mskimmons presented a proposal to waive county zoning and septic fees for properties damaged by the April 17 tornado affecting the towns of Ringle, Easton, and villages of Kronenwetter and Weston. Significant debate ensued. Supervisor Drew questioned where to draw the line for future incidents. Supervisor Drebeck expressed concern about fairness to individuals who experience damage outside declared disaster areas. Supervisor Sefeld questioned potential levy impacts. Eric Vogel asked if homeowners insurance would cover fees. Supervisor Drebeck moved to amend the resolution to only waive reconnect fees ($160) for septic systems, not other zoning or septic fees. Supervisor Kurt seconded the amendment. The amendment carried unanimously. The amended resolution then passed on a non-unanimous vote to forward to the county board.

### Solid Waste Department overview
Director David Hagenbusher presented an educational overview of the Solid Waste Department, covering the landfill history since 1980, the renewable natural gas partnership with Vidi Energy, leachate treatment challenges due to PFAS regulations requiring treatment by 2032, and significant capital expenses including a $6 million 11-acre cell construction project. Marilyn Bend praised the department's work during the Ringle tornado response.

### Parks, Recreation, and Forestry Department overview
Tom Lovelin, County Forest Administrator, presented on behalf of Director Jamie who had an emergency. He covered the department's 44 full-time and 120 seasonal employees, the joint city-county park operation since 1924, and the 30,700 acres of county forest. He noted Nine Mile County Forest generates $3.25 million for the local economy from out-of-town visitors. Supervisor Sefeld announced that Tom Lovelin is retiring June 1st after nearly 30 years of service. Joe Tucker was selected as the new county forest administrator.

## Public Comment
No public comment was offered.
