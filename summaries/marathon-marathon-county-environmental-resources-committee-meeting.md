## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Environmental Resources Committee approved two rezoning petitions unanimously and voted to forward a modified fee waiver resolution to the county board that will only cover septic reconnect fees (not other zoning or septic fees) for properties damaged by the April 17, 2026 tornado. The committee also received educational presentations from Solid Waste, Parks Recreation and Forestry, and Conservation Planning and Zoning departments.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of March 31, 2026 committee minutes
Supervisor Sefeld made a motion to approve the minutes, seconded by Supervisor Drebeck. The motion passed unanimously with no discussion.

### Tim Veland rezoning petition - Town of Green Valley
Garrett Pogle presented the staff report for rezoning from urban residential and general agriculture to rural residential for a land division. The Town of Green Valley recommended approval on March 10, 2026. No public testimony was offered for or against. Supervisor Drebeck moved to approve based on findings of fact, conclusions of law, and town approval. Supervisor Kurt seconded. Motion passed unanimously.

### Jeffrey and Cynthia Schmidtz rezoning petition - Town of McMillan
The petition sought to rezone from Conservancy Recreation to General Agriculture. Supervisor Lever asked about the conservancy nature, and Garrett Pogle explained the property has a home and horse riding stable, with the rezoning needed to make the home a conforming use rather than non-conforming. No natural areas would be lost. Town of McMillan recommended approval on April 20, 2026. No public testimony was offered. Supervisor Sefeld moved to approve, Supervisor Drew seconded. Motion passed unanimously.

### Fee waiver for April 17, 2026 severe weather properties
Director Lori Mskimmons presented a proposal to waive county zoning and septic fees for tornado-damaged properties in Ringle, Kronenwetter, Easton, and Weston. Supervisor Drew questioned fairness to individual tornado victims who don't receive fee waivers. Supervisor Drebeck expressed concerns about precedent. Supervisor Sefeld questioned tracking costs and potential levy impacts. Administrator Lance Pliml clarified this wouldn't require additional staff costs. Supervisor Sefeld moved to forward the full resolution. After debate, Supervisor Drebeck proposed an amendment to limit the waiver to only reconnect fees ($160) for septic systems, not other zoning or septic fees. Supervisor Kurt seconded the amendment. The amendment passed (not unanimous). The amended resolution then passed to forward to county board (not unanimous).

### Solid Waste Department presentation
Director David Hagenbusher provided an overview of the landfill operations, explaining the tipping fee breakdown, the renewable natural gas partnership with Vidi Energy, and upcoming challenges including leachate treatment requirements by 2032 due to PFAS concerns. He noted a $6 million cell construction project starting this year. Marilyn Bend praised the department's work during the Ringo tornado response.

### Parks, Recreation, and Forestry Department presentation
Tom Lovelin, County Forest Administrator, presented on behalf of Director Jamie who had an emergency. He covered the joint city-county park system, the 30,700-acre county forest, and Nine Mile County Forest's $3.25 million annual economic impact from out-of-town visitors. Supervisor Sefeld announced Tom's retirement effective June 1st after nearly 30 years of service. Multiple committee members thanked him for his service.

## Public Comment
No public comment was offered. No one signed up for public comment.
