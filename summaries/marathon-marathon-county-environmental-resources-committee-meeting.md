## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Environmental Resources Committee approved two rezoning petitions and voted to forward a modified fee waiver resolution for tornado-affected properties to the full county board. The committee also received educational presentations from Solid Waste, Parks Recreation and Forestry, and Conservation Planning and Zoning departments.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of March 31st, 2026 committee minutes
Supervisor Sefeld made a motion to approve the minutes, seconded by Supervisor Drebeck. The motion carried with all in favor, no opposition.

### Tim Veland rezoning petition (Town of Green Valley)
Land Use Specialist Garrett Pogle presented the staff report for rezoning from Urban Residential and General Agriculture to Rural Residential. The Town of Green Valley recommended approval on March 10, 2026. No public testimony was offered for or against. Supervisor Drebeck moved to approve based on findings of fact and conclusions of law, seconded by Supervisor Kurt. Motion carried unanimously.

### Jeffrey and Cynthia Schmidtz rezoning petition (Town of McMillan)
Garrett Pogle presented the petition to rezone from Conservancy Recreation to General Agriculture. Supervisor Leur asked about the conservancy nature of the property. Pogle explained there is a home and horse riding stable on the property, and the rezoning would make the structures conforming uses. The Town of McMillan recommended approval on April 20, 2026. No public testimony was offered. Supervisor Sefeld moved to approve, seconded by Supervisor Drew. Motion carried unanimously.

### Fee waiver for April 17, 2026 severe weather impacted properties
Director Lori Mskimmons presented a proposal to waive county zoning and pouts fees for tornado-affected properties. Significant committee debate followed regarding fairness to other disaster victims, precedent-setting concerns, and financial implications. Supervisor Drew questioned where to draw the line for future events. Supervisor Drebeck proposed an amendment to limit the waiver to only reconnect fees ($160) for pouts systems, excluding other zoning and pouts fees. The amendment was seconded by Supervisor Kurt and passed. The amended resolution was then approved to forward to the county board, though the vote was not unanimous.

### Solid Waste Department presentation
Director Dave Hagen Busher provided an overview of landfill operations, explaining the $13.10 state fee portion of tipping fees, renewable natural gas partnership with Vidi Energy, leachate treatment challenges due to PFAS regulations, and upcoming capital projects including $6 million cell construction and on-site leachate treatment facility.

### Parks Recreation and Forestry presentation
County Forest Administrator Tom Lovelin presented on behalf of Director Jamie, covering the department's 44 full-time and 120 seasonal employees serving both county and city facilities. He noted Nine Mile County Forest generates $3.25 million annually from out-of-town visitors. Supervisor Sefeld acknowledged Tom's retirement effective June 1st after nearly 30 years of service.

## Public Comment
No public comment was offered.
