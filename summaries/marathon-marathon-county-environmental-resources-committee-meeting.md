## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Environmental Resources Committee approved two rezoning petitions and voted to forward a modified fee waiver resolution to the county board that only covers septic system reconnect fees for properties damaged by the April 17, 2026 tornado. The committee also received educational presentations from Solid Waste, Parks Recreation and Forestry, and Conservation Planning and Zoning departments.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of March 31, 2026 minutes
Supervisor Sefeld made a motion to approve the minutes with Supervisor Drebeck seconding. The motion carried unanimously with no discussion.

### Tim Veland rezoning petition - Green Valley
Land Use Specialist Garrett Pogle presented the staff report for rezoning from urban residential and general agriculture to rural residential in the town of Green Valley. The town board recommended approval on March 10, 2026. No public testimony was offered. Supervisor Drebeck moved to approve based on findings of fact and town approval, Supervisor Kurt seconded. Motion carried unanimously.

### Jeffrey and Cynthia Schmidtz rezoning petition - McMillan
Garrett Pogle presented the petition to rezone from Conservancy Recreation to General Agriculture. Supervisor Leur asked about the conservancy designation, and Pogle explained the property has a home and horse riding stable, with the rezoning needed to make the home a conforming use. The town of McMillan recommended approval on April 20, 2026. Supervisor Sefelt moved to approve, Supervisor Drew seconded. Motion carried unanimously.

### Fee waiver for tornado-impacted properties
Director Lori Mskimmons presented a proposal to waive county zoning and septic fees for properties damaged by the April 17, 2026 tornado. Supervisor Drew questioned fairness to future disaster victims not in declared emergency areas. Supervisor Drebeck expressed concerns about cost implications and consistency. After debate, Supervisor Sefelt moved to forward the resolution, Supervisor Leur seconded. Supervisor Drebeck then amended the motion to only waive reconnect fees ($160) for septic systems, not other zoning or POWTS fees. Supervisor Kurt seconded the amendment. The amendment passed, and the amended resolution was approved to forward to the county board, though not unanimously.

### Solid Waste Department presentation
Director Dave Hagenbusher provided an overview of landfill operations, explaining the $13.10 per ton state fee, renewable natural gas partnership with Vidi Energy generating royalties, and upcoming leachate treatment requirements by 2032. He noted lithium-ion battery fires occur about weekly and discussed major capital expenses including $6 million cell construction.

### Parks, Recreation and Forestry presentation
County Forest Administrator Tom Lovelin presented on behalf of Director Jamie, covering the joint city-county department structure with 44 full-time and 120 seasonal employees. He highlighted Nine Mile County Forest generating $3.25 million in economic impact from out-of-town visitors. Supervisor Sefelt announced Lovelin is retiring June 1 after nearly 30 years of service.

## Public Comment
No public comment was offered.

## Action Items
- Forward amended fee waiver resolution to county board - covers only POWTS reconnect fees ($160) for tornado-damaged properties
- Two rezoning petitions forwarded to county board for approval
- CPZ staff to track costs and requests related to fee waivers for future policy development
- Town of Green Valley and Town of McMillan should update comprehensive plans to reflect zoning changes if applicable
