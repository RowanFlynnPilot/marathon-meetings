## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Environmental Resources Committee approved two rezoning petitions unanimously and voted to forward a modified fee waiver resolution to the county board that will only waive reconnect fees for septic systems (not other zoning or pouts fees) for properties impacted by the April 17, 2026 tornado. The committee also received educational presentations from the Solid Waste, Parks Recreation and Forestry, and Conservation Planning and Zoning departments.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of March 31st, 2026 committee minutes
Supervisor Sefeld made a motion to approve the minutes, seconded by Supervisor Drebeck. The motion carried unanimously with no discussion.

### Tim Veland rezoning petition (Green Valley)
Garrett Pogle presented the staff report for rezoning from Urban Residential and General Agriculture to Rural Residential in the Town of Green Valley. The Town Board recommended approval on March 10, 2026. No public testimony was offered. Supervisor Drebeck moved to approve based on findings of fact, conclusions of law, and town approval. Supervisor Kurt seconded. Motion carried unanimously.

### Jeffrey and Cynthia Schmidtz rezoning petition (McMillan)
Garrett Pogle presented the petition to rezone from Conservancy Recreation to General Agriculture in the Town of McMillan. Supervisor Leur asked about the conservancy nature of the property; staff explained it currently has a home and horse riding stable, and rezoning would make these conforming uses. The Town Board recommended approval on April 20, 2026. No public testimony was offered. Supervisor Sefeld moved to approve, Supervisor Drew seconded. Motion carried unanimously.

### Fee waiver for April 17, 2026 severe weather impacted properties
Director Lori Mskimmons presented a proposal to waive county zoning and pouts fees for properties damaged by the April 17th tornado in the towns of Ringle, Easton, and villages of Kronenwetter and Weston. Supervisor Drew questioned where to draw the line for future events. Supervisor Drebeck expressed concerns about fairness to property owners impacted by disasters not declared emergencies. After discussion, Supervisor Sefeld moved to forward the resolution to the county board. Supervisor Leur seconded. Supervisor Drebeck then moved to amend the resolution to only waive reconnect fees for pouts systems, not other zoning or pouts fees. Supervisor Kurt seconded the amendment. The amendment carried. The amended motion to forward to county board passed, though not unanimously.

### Solid Waste Department overview
Director Dave Hagen Busher presented on the department's operations, explaining they accept about 1,000 tons of waste daily with 11 staff members. He highlighted the renewable natural gas partnership with Vidi Energy, significant capital expenses for landfill expansion and closure, and upcoming requirements by 2032 for leachate treatment due to PFAS concerns. Marilyn Bend commended the department for keeping independent landfills competitive.

### Parks, Recreation, and Forestry Department overview
Tom Lovelin, County Forest Administrator, presented on behalf of Director Jamie who had an emergency. He noted the department serves both Marathon County and City of Wausau with 44 full-time and 120 seasonal staff. He highlighted Nine Mile County Forest generates $3.25 million for the local economy from out-of-town visitors. Supervisor Sefeld announced Tom is retiring June 1st after nearly 30 years with the county, with Joe Tucker selected as his replacement.

## Public Comment
No public comment was offered.

## Action Items

- Rezoning petition for Jeffrey and Cynthia Schmidtz (McMillan) approved and forwarded to county board
- Modified fee waiver resolution (reconnect fees only) for April 17, 2026 tornado-impacted properties forwarded to county board
- Staff to track costs and requests related to fee waivers for potential future policy development
- Conservation services educational presentation scheduled for next meeting with Kirsty Heiden
