## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Environmental Resources Committee approved two rezoning petitions unanimously and passed an amended resolution to waive only reconnect fees for septic systems for properties impacted by the April 17, 2026 tornado. The committee also received educational presentations from Solid Waste, Parks Recreation and Forestry, and Conservation Planning and Zoning departments.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of March 31st, 2026 committee minutes
Supervisor Sefeld made a motion to approve the minutes, seconded by Supervisor Drebeck. The motion carried unanimously with no discussion.

### Tim Veland rezoning petition (Urban Residential and General Agriculture to Rural Residential)
Garrett Pogle presented the staff report for a rezoning in the Town of Green Valley. The Town Board recommended approval on March 10, 2026. No public comment was offered in favor, against, or as interested. Supervisor Drebeck moved to approve based on findings of fact, conclusions of law, and the town's approval, seconded by Supervisor Kurt. The motion carried unanimously.

### Jeffrey and Cynthia Schmidtz rezoning petition (Conservancy Recreation to General Agriculture)
Garrett Pogle presented the staff report for rezoning in the Town of McMillan. Supervisor Leur asked about the conservancy nature of the property; Pogle explained the property contains a home and horse riding stable, and the rezoning makes all structures conforming uses. The Town Board recommended approval on April 20, 2026. No public comment was offered. Supervisor Sefelt moved to approve, seconded by Supervisor Drew. The motion carried unanimously.

### Fee waivers for properties impacted by April 17, 2026 severe weather
Director Lori Mskimmons presented a proposal to waive county zoning and septic fees for tornado-impacted properties. Supervisor Drew questioned where to draw the line for future incidents. Supervisor Leur asked about potential levy impacts. Supervisor Drebeck raised concerns about fairness compared to non-disaster victims and proposed an amendment to limit the waiver to only septic reconnect fees ($160). The amendment was seconded by Supervisor Kurt and passed. The amended resolution then passed with the committee voting to forward it to the county board, though the vote was not unanimous.

### Solid Waste Department overview
Director David Hagenbusher presented on department operations including the landfill, renewable natural gas partnership with Vidi Energy, leachate treatment challenges, and upcoming capital projects. Marilyn Bend praised the department's work helping Ringle tornado victims. Supervisor Brook asked about the environmental loan application; Hagenbusher said the 2026 application was deemed incomplete and they are reapplying for 2027 funding.

### Parks, Recreation, and Forestry Department overview
Tom Lovelin presented on behalf of Director Jamie, covering the department's dual role serving both Marathon County and City of Wausau, the 17 county parks, 30,700-acre county forest system, and Nine Mile County Forest's $3.25 million economic impact. Supervisor Sefelt noted this was Lovelin's last committee meeting before his June 1st retirement after 35 years, and thanked him for his service.

## Public Comment
No public comment was offered.

## Action Items
- Rezoning from Urban Residential and General Agriculture to Rural Residential for James Offer and David Trait property forwarded to County Board with approval recommendation
- Rezoning from Conservancy Recreation to General Agriculture for Jeffrey and Cynthia Schmidtz property forwarded to County Board with approval recommendation
- Amended resolution to waive only septic reconnect fees (not other zoning or pouts fees) for tornado-impacted properties forwarded to County Board
- CPZ to track costs and requests related to tornado fee waivers for future policy development
- Solid Waste to resubmit environmental loan application for leachate treatment facility by October 2025 for 2027 funding
