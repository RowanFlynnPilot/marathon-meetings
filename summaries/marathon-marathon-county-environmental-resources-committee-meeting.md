## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Environmental Resources Committee approved two rezoning petitions unanimously and voted to recommend a limited fee waiver for tornado-damaged properties to the full county board. The committee also received educational presentations from Solid Waste, Parks Recreation and Forestry, and Conservation Planning and Zoning departments.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of March 31st, 2026 committee minutes
Supervisor Sefeld made a motion to approve the minutes, seconded by Supervisor Drebeck. The motion carried unanimously with no discussion.

### Tim Veland rezoning petition (Town of Green Valley)
Land Use Specialist Garrett Pogle presented the staff report for rezoning from Urban Residential and General Agriculture to Rural Residential. The Town of Green Valley recommended approval on March 10, 2026. CPZ staff recommended approval finding the rezoning consistent with the town's comprehensive plan. No public testimony was offered. Supervisor Drebeck moved to approve based on findings of fact and conclusions of law, seconded by Supervisor Kurt. Motion carried unanimously.

### Jeffrey and Cynthia Schmidtz rezoning petition (Town of McMillan)
Garrett Pogle presented the petition to rezone from Conservancy Recreation to General Agriculture. Supervisor Lever asked about the conservancy nature of the property; Pogle explained there is currently a home and horse riding stable there, and the rezoning would make all structures conforming uses rather than non-conforming. The Town of McMillan recommended approval on April 20, 2026. No public testimony was offered. Supervisor Sefeld moved to approve, seconded by Supervisor Drew. Motion carried unanimously.

### Fee waiver for April 17, 2026 severe weather impacted properties
Director Lori Miskimmons presented a proposal to waive county zoning and septic fees for properties damaged by the April 17th tornado affecting towns of Ringle, Easton, and villages of Kronenwetter and Weston. Supervisor Drew questioned where to draw the line for future incidents. Supervisor Drebeck proposed an amendment to only waive reconnect fees ($160) for pouts systems, excluding other zoning and septic fees. The amendment was seconded by Supervisor Kurt and passed. The amended resolution to forward only reconnect fee waivers to the county board was then approved, though not unanimously.

### Solid Waste Department presentation
Director David Hagenbusher provided an overview of department operations including the landfill facility, renewable natural gas partnership with Vidi Energy, leachate treatment requirements by 2032 due to PFAS concerns, and upcoming capital projects totaling millions of dollars. Marilyn Bend commended the department's work helping Ringle tornado victims.

### Parks, Recreation, and Forestry Department presentation
Tom Lovelin, County Forest Administrator, presented on behalf of Director Jamie. He noted the department serves both county and city of Wausau with 44 full-time and 120 seasonal employees. He highlighted Nine Mile County Forest generates $3.25 million annually from out-of-town visitors. Supervisor Sefeld announced Tom Lovelin is retiring June 1st after 35 years of service, with Joe Tucker selected as his replacement.

## Public Comment
No public comment was offered.

## Action Items
