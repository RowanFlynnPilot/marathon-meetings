## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Environmental Resources Committee met on May 5, 2026, approving two rezoning petitions unanimously and voting to forward a modified fee waiver resolution for tornado-damaged properties to the full county board. The committee also received educational presentations from the Solid Waste, Parks Recreation and Forestry, and Conservation Planning and Zoning departments.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of March 31, 2026 committee minutes
Supervisor Sefeld made a motion to approve the minutes, seconded by Supervisor Drebeck. The motion carried unanimously with no discussion.

### Rezoning petition - Tim Veland on behalf of James Offer and David Tract
Garrett Pogle presented the staff report for rezoning from urban residential and general agriculture to rural residential in the Town of Green Valley. The Town Board recommended approval on March 10, 2026. No public testimony was offered. Supervisor Drebeck moved to approve based on findings of fact and town approval, seconded by Supervisor Kurt. Motion carried unanimously.

### Rezoning petition - Jeffrey and Cynthia Schmidtz
Staff presented the petition to rezone from conservancy recreation to general agriculture in the Town of McMillan. Supervisor Leur asked about the conservancy nature; Garrett Pogle explained the property contains a home and horse riding stable, and rezoning would make the home a conforming use rather than non-conforming. The Town Board recommended approval on April 20, 2026. No public testimony was offered. Supervisor Sefeld moved to approve, seconded by Supervisor Drew. Motion carried unanimously.

### Fee waivers for April 17, 2026 severe weather victims
Director Lori Mskimmons presented a proposal to waive county zoning and POWTS fees for properties damaged by the April 17 tornado affecting the Town of Ringle, Village of Kronenwetter, Town of Easton, and Village of Weston. Significant committee debate ensued. Supervisor Drew questioned where to draw the line for future events. Supervisor Leur asked about budget impacts. Supervisor Drebeck expressed concern about fairness to tornado victims in non-declared disasters. Marilyn Bend asked about mound system costs. Supervisor Drebeck moved to amend the resolution to only waive reconnect fees ($160) and not other zoning or POWTS fees. Supervisor K seconded. The amendment carried. The amended resolution was then approved to forward to the full county board, though the vote was not unanimous.

### Solid Waste Department presentation
Director Dave Hagen Busher presented an overview of the department's operations, including the landfill's 45-year history, renewable natural gas partnership with Vidi Energy generating royalties, and significant challenges with leachate treatment due to PFAS regulations requiring on-site treatment by 2032. He noted the department operates with 11 FTEs processing up to 2,000 tons daily.

### Parks, Recreation and Forestry presentation
Tom Lovelin, County Forest Administrator, presented on behalf of Director Jamie. The department operates both county and city park systems with 44 full-time and 120 seasonal employees. Nine Mile County Forest generates $3.25 million annually for the local economy from out-of-town visitors. Lovelin announced his retirement effective June 1 after 35 years, with Joe Tucker selected as his successor.

## Public Comment
No public comment was offered.

- Solid Waste to reapply for Clean Water Fund grant for leachate treatment facility by October 2026
