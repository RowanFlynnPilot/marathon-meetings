## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Environmental Resources Committee approved two rezoning petitions and forwarded an amended fee waiver resolution to the county board. The committee voted to waive only reconnect fees (not other zoning or pouts fees) for properties impacted by the April 17, 2026 tornado, rather than the broader fee waiver originally proposed by staff.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of March 31, 2026 committee minutes
Supervisor Sefeld made a motion to approve the minutes, seconded by Supervisor Drebeck. The motion carried unanimously with no discussion.

### Tim Veland rezoning petition - Town of Green Valley
Land Use Specialist Garrett Pago presented a staff report for rezoning from urban residential and general agriculture to rural residential. The Town of Green Valley recommended approval on March 10, 2026. No public testimony was offered for or against. Supervisor Drebeck moved to approve based on findings of fact and the town's approval, seconded by Supervisor Kurt. Motion carried unanimously.

### Jeffrey and Cynthia Schmidtz rezoning petition - Town of McMillan
Garrett Pago presented a petition to rezone from Conservancy Recreation to General Agriculture. Supervisor Leur asked about the conservancy designation; Pago explained the property contains a home and horse riding stable, and rezoning would make the existing structures conforming uses. The Town of McMillan recommended approval on April 20, 2026. No public testimony was offered. Supervisor Sefeld moved to approve, seconded by Supervisor Drew. Motion carried unanimously.

### Fee waiver for April 17, 2026 severe weather
Director Lori Mskimmons presented a proposal to waive county zoning and pouts fees for properties damaged by the tornado. Supervisor Drew questioned fairness of helping only declared disaster areas. Supervisor Leur asked about revenue impact and staff costs. Supervisor Drebeck raised concerns about consistency and noted the recent fee increase for pouts systems. After debate, Supervisor Sefeld moved to forward the resolution; Supervisor Leur seconded. Supervisor Drebeck then proposed an amendment to limit the waiver to only reconnect fees ($160), excluding all other zoning and pouts fees. Supervisor Kurt seconded the amendment. The amendment passed. The amended resolution then passed but was noted as not unanimous.

### Solid Waste Department overview
Director Dave Hagenbusher provided an educational presentation covering the department's 16-year history, operations of the landfill serving Marathon, Portage and Shawano counties, the renewable natural gas partnership with Vidi Energy launched in September 2025, and upcoming challenges including leachate treatment requirements by 2032. The department processes about 1,000 tons daily with 11 FTEs.

### Parks, Recreation and Forestry Department overview
Tom Lovelin, County Forest Administrator, presented on behalf of Director Jamie (who had an emergency). He covered the department's 44 full-time and 120 seasonal positions, operation of both county and city park systems since 1924, and the 30,700-acre county forest. A Nine Mile County Forest study showed out-of-town visitors generate $3.25 million for the local economy. Lovelin announced his retirement effective June 1st after nearly 30 years of service.

## Public Comment
No public comment was offered.
