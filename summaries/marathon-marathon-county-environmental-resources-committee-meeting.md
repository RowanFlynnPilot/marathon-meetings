## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Environmental Resources Committee approved two rezoning petitions unanimously and passed an amended resolution to waive only septic system reconnect fees (not other zoning or pouts fees) for properties damaged by the April 17, 2026 tornado. The committee also received educational presentations from Solid Waste, Parks Recreation and Forestry, and Conservation Planning and Zoning departments.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of March 31, 2026 committee minutes
Supervisor Sefeld made a motion to approve the minutes, seconded by Supervisor Drebeck. The motion passed unanimously with no discussion.

### Tim Veland rezoning petition - Town of Green Valley
Land Use Specialist Garrett Pogle presented the staff report for rezoning from urban residential and general agriculture to rural residential for a proposed land division. The Town of Green Valley recommended approval on March 10, 2026. No public testimony was offered for or against. Supervisor Drebeck moved to approve based on findings of fact and town approval, seconded by Supervisor Kurt. Motion carried unanimously.

### Jeffrey and Cynthia Schmidtz rezoning petition - Town of McMillan
Garrett Pogle presented the rezoning from conservancy recreation to general agriculture. Supervisor Leur asked about the conservancy nature, and Pogle explained the property contains a home and horse riding stable, with the rezoning needed to make the home a conforming use rather than non-conforming. He confirmed no natural areas would be lost. Town of McMillan recommended approval on April 20, 2026. Supervisor Sefelt moved to approve, seconded by Supervisor Drew. Motion carried unanimously.

### Fee waivers for April 17, 2026 severe weather damage
Director Lori Mskimmons presented a proposal to waive county zoning and septic fees for tornado-damaged properties in Ringle, Kronenwetter, Easton, and Weston. Supervisor Drew questioned where to draw the line for future events. Supervisor Leur asked about tracking costs. Supervisor Drebeck raised concerns about fairness to individuals hit by isolated storms without disaster declarations. Eric Vogel asked if insurance would cover fees. After debate, Supervisor Drebeck moved to amend the resolution to only waive reconnect fees ($160) for septic systems, not other zoning or pouts fees. The amendment passed. The amended resolution to forward to the county board passed, though not unanimously.

### Solid Waste Department presentation
Director David Hagenbusher presented an overview of solid waste operations, explaining the landfill's 45-year history, current tipping fee structure, the renewable natural gas partnership with Vidi Energy generating royalties since 2023, and upcoming challenges including a $6 million cell construction project and required leachate treatment system by 2032 due to PFAS concerns. He noted April hauling costs were nearly $90,000 due to significant rainfall.

### Parks, Recreation, and Forestry Department presentation
Tom Lovelin, County Forest Administrator, presented on behalf of Director Jamie who had an emergency. He explained the department serves both city and county with 44 full-time and 120 seasonal employees, manages 17 county parks, 30,700 acres of county forest, and 37 city parks. He noted Nine Mile County Forest generates $3.25 million annually for the local economy from out-of-town visitors. Supervisor Sefelt announced that Tom is retiring June 1st after nearly 30 years of service, with Joe Tucker selected as his replacement.

## Public Comment
No public comment was offered.

## Action Items
- Rezoning for James Offer and David Tract (urban residential/general agriculture to rural residential in Town of Green Valley) approved and forwarded to County Board
- Rezoning for Jeffrey and Cynthia Schmidtz (conservancy recreation to general agriculture in Town of McMillan) approved and forwarded to County Board
- Amended resolution to waive only septic system reconnect fees ($160) for tornado-damaged properties forwarded to County Board for approval
- Staff to track costs and requests related to tornado fee waivers for future policy development
