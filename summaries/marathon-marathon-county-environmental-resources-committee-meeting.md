## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Environmental Resources Committee approved two rezoning petitions and voted to forward a modified fee waiver resolution to the county board that will only waive reconnect fees for septic systems for properties damaged in the April 17, 2026 tornado. The committee also received department overviews from Solid Waste, Parks Recreation and Forestry, and Conservation Planning and Zoning.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of March 31st, 2026 committee minutes
Supervisor Sefeld made a motion to approve the minutes, seconded by Supervisor Drebeck. The motion carried unanimously with no discussion.

### Tim Veland rezoning petition - Town of Green Valley
Land Use Specialist Garrett Pogle presented the staff report for rezoning from urban residential and general agriculture to rural residential for a proposed land division. The Town of Green Valley recommended approval on March 10, 2026. No public comment was offered. Supervisor Drebeck moved to approve based on findings of fact and conclusions of law, seconded by Supervisor Kurt. Motion carried unanimously.

### Jeffrey and Cynthia Schmidtz rezoning petition - Town of McMillan
Garrett Pogle presented the petition to rezone from conservancy recreation to general agriculture. Supervisor Leur asked about the conservancy nature, and Pogle explained the property contains a home and horse riding stable, and rezoning would make the home a conforming use rather than non-conforming. He confirmed no natural area would be lost. The Town of McMillan recommended approval on April 20, 2026. No public comment was offered. Supervisor Sefeld moved to approve, seconded by Supervisor Drew. Motion carried unanimously.

### Fee waivers for April 17, 2026 severe weather damage
Director Lori Mskimmons presented staff recommendations to waive county zoning and septic fees for properties damaged by the tornado affecting the towns of Ringle, Eastston, and villages of Cronenwetter and Weston. Supervisor Drew questioned where to draw the line for future events. Supervisor Leur asked about budget impacts. Supervisor Drebeck raised concerns about fairness to those not in declared disaster areas and questioned consistency with recent fee increases. After debate, Supervisor Drebeck proposed an amendment to limit fee waivers to only reconnect fees for septic systems ($160), excluding other pouts and zoning fees. The amendment was seconded by Supervisor Kurt and passed. The amended resolution was then approved to forward to the county board, though not unanimously.

### Department overview: Solid Waste
Director David Hagenbusher presented an overview of the Solid Waste Department, highlighting the renewable natural gas partnership with Vidi Energy launched in September 2025, significant capital expenses for landfill expansion and closure, and challenges with leachate treatment due to PFAS regulations requiring on-site treatment by 2032. He noted the department operates as an enterprise fund with 11 FTEs processing about 1,000 tons daily.

### Department overview: Parks, Recreation, and Forestry
County Forest Administrator Tom Lovelin presented on behalf of Director Jamie, covering the joint city-county department structure with 44 full-time and 120 seasonal employees. He highlighted Nine Mile County Forest generating $3.25 million in tourism spending and noted committee structure changes creating a five-member county forestry parks and recreation committee. Supervisor Sefeld acknowledged Lovelin's retirement effective June 1st after nearly 30 years of service.

## Public Comment
No public comment was offered. No one signed up for public comment at the beginning of the meeting, and no members of the public spoke during the public hearing portions for either rezoning petition.
