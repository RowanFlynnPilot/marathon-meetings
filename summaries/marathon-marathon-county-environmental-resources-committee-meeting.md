## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Environmental Resources Committee approved two rezoning petitions and voted to forward a modified fee waiver proposal to the county board that would waive only reconnect fees for septic systems for properties damaged by the April 17, 2026 tornado. The committee also received department overviews from Solid Waste, Parks Recreation and Forestry, and Conservation Planning and Zoning.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of March 31, 2026 committee minutes
Supervisor Sefeld made a motion to approve the minutes with Supervisor Drebeck seconding. The motion carried with no opposition.

### Tim Veland rezoning petition - Town of Green Valley
Land Use Specialist Garrett Pogle presented the staff report for rezoning from urban residential and general agriculture to rural residential for a proposed land division. The Town of Green Valley recommended approval on March 10, 2026. No public testimony was offered. Supervisor Drebeck moved to approve based on findings of fact, conclusions of law, and town approval. Supervisor Kurt seconded. Motion carried unanimously.

### Jeffrey and Cynthia Schmidtz rezoning petition - Town of McMillan
Garrett Pogle presented the rezoning request from conservancy/recreation to general agriculture. Supervisor Leur asked about the conservancy nature of the property; Pogle explained it currently has a home and horse riding stable, and the rezoning would make the home a conforming use rather than non-conforming. No natural areas are being lost. The Town of McMillan recommended approval on April 20, 2026. No public testimony was offered. Supervisor Sefeld moved to approve, Supervisor Drew seconded. Motion carried unanimously.

### Fee waivers for April 17, 2026 severe weather damage
Director Lori Mskimmons presented a proposal to waive county zoning and septic system fees for properties damaged by the April 17 tornado affecting the towns of Ringle, Easton, and villages of Kronenwetter and Weston. Supervisor Drew questioned where to draw the line for future events. Supervisor Leur asked about tracking costs. Supervisor Drebeck raised fairness concerns about properties not in declared disaster areas. After debate, Supervisor Drebeck proposed an amendment to limit the waiver to only reconnect fees ($160) for septic systems, excluding other zoning and septic fees. Supervisor Kurt seconded the amendment. The amendment carried. The amended motion to forward to the county board then passed, though not unanimously.

### Solid Waste Department overview
Director Dave Hagen Busher presented an overview of the solid waste operation, explaining the landfill serves Marathon, Portage, and Shawano counties, accepting about 1,000 tons daily. He highlighted the renewable natural gas partnership with Vidi Energy that began injecting gas into pipelines in September 2025, upcoming leachate treatment requirements by 2032 due to PFAS regulations, and significant capital expenses including a $6 million 11-acre expansion project.

### Parks, Recreation and Forestry Department overview
County Forest Administrator Tom Lovelin presented on behalf of Director Jamie, covering the joint city-county operation with 44 full-time and 120 seasonal employees. He noted Nine Mile County Forest generates $3.25 million annually for the local economy from out-of-town visitors. Supervisor Sefeld announced that Tom Lovelin is retiring June 1st after nearly 30 years of service, with Joe Tucker selected as the new county forest administrator.

## Public Comment
No public comment was offered.

## Action Items
- Rezoning petition for James Offer and David Tract approved and forwarded to Marathon County Board of Supervisors
- Rezoning petition for Jeffrey and Cynthia Schmidtz approved and forwarded to Marathon County Board of Supervisors
- Modified fee waiver resolution (reconnect fees only for septic systems) forwarded to county board for properties damaged by April 17, 2026 severe weather
- CPZ staff to track costs and requests related to the fee waiver program
- Town of Green Valley and Town of McMillan recommended to update comprehensive plans to reflect zoning changes if applicable
