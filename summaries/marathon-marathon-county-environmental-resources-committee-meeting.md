## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Environmental Resources Committee approved two rezoning petitions unanimously and voted to forward a modified fee waiver resolution to the county board that will only waive reconnect fees for septic systems on properties damaged by the April 17, 2026 tornado. The committee also received educational presentations from Solid Waste, Parks Recreation and Forestry, and Conservation Planning and Zoning departments.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of March 31st, 2026 committee minutes
Supervisor Sefeld made a motion to approve the minutes, seconded by Supervisor Drebeck. The motion carried with no opposition.

### Tim Veland rezoning petition (Town of Green Valley)
Land Use Specialist Garrett Pogle presented the staff report for rezoning from Urban Residential and General Agriculture to Rural Residential for a proposed land division. The Town of Green Valley recommended approval on March 10, 2026, and staff recommended approval based on consistency with the comprehensive plan. No public comment was offered. Supervisor Drebeck moved to approve based on findings of fact and the town's approval, seconded by Supervisor Kurt. Motion carried unanimously.

### Jeffrey and Cynthia Schmidz rezoning petition (Town of McMillan)
Garrett Pogle presented the petition to rezone from Conservancy Recreation to General Agriculture. Supervisor Lever asked about the conservancy designation; Pogle explained the property contains a home and horse riding stable, and the rezone will make these conforming uses. He confirmed no natural areas would be lost. The Town of McMillan recommended approval on April 20, 2026. Supervisor Sefeld moved to approve, seconded by Supervisor Drew. Motion carried unanimously.

### Fee waivers for April 17, 2026 severe weather damage
Director Lori Mskimmons presented a proposal to waive county zoning and septic fees for properties damaged by the tornado affecting the towns of Ringle, Eastston, and villages of Cronin Weter and Weston. Supervisor Drew questioned where to draw the line for future events. Supervisor Drebeck expressed concern about fairness to individual disaster victims not in declared areas. Marilyn Bend asked about septic hookup costs. After discussion, Supervisor Drebeck moved to amend the resolution to only waive reconnect fees ($160) for septic systems, not other zoning or septic fees. Supervisor Kurt seconded the amendment. The amendment carried. The amended resolution was then approved to forward to county board, though not unanimously.

### Solid Waste Department overview
Director David Hagenbusher presented an overview of the department's operations, including the landfill facility operating since 1980, the renewable natural gas partnership with Vidi Energy generating royalties for the county, and upcoming capital projects including $6 million in cell construction. He highlighted the leachate treatment challenge requiring on-site treatment by 2032 due to PFAS regulations, with current hauling costs of $90,000 in April alone. An environmental loan application for treatment facilities will be resubmitted for 2027 funding.

### Parks, Recreation, and Forestry Department overview
Tom Lovelin, County Forest Administrator, presented on behalf of Director Jamie who had an emergency. He explained the department serves both city and county with 44 full-time and 120 seasonal staff, managing 17 county parks, 30,700 acres of county forest, and 37 city parks. A Nine Mile County Forest study showed out-of-town visitors generate $3.25 million for the local economy. Supervisor Sefeld announced Tom is retiring June 1st after nearly 30 years, with Joe Tucker selected as the new county forest administrator.

## Public Comment
No public comment was offered.

## Action Items
- Rezoning from Urban Residential/General Agriculture to Rural Residential in Town of Green Valley approved and forwarded to county board
- Rezoning from Conservancy Recreation to General Agriculture in Town of McMillan approved and forwarded to county board
- Modified fee waiver resolution (reconnect fees only) for tornado-damaged properties forwarded to county board for consideration
- CPZ staff to track costs and requests related to tornado fee waivers for future policy development
- Solid Waste to resubmit environmental loan application for leachate treatment facility by October 2026 for 2027 funding
