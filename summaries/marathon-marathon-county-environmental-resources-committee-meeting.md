## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Environmental Resources Committee approved two rezoning petitions and voted to forward a modified fee waiver resolution to the county board that will only waive reconnect fees for septic systems damaged by the April 17, 2026 tornado. The committee also received educational presentations from the Solid Waste, Parks Recreation and Forestry, and Conservation Planning and Zoning departments.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of March 31, 2026 committee minutes
Supervisor Sefeld made a motion to approve the minutes, seconded by Supervisor Drebeck. The motion carried with no opposition.

### Tim Veland rezoning petition - Green Valley
Garrett Pogle presented the staff report for rezoning from urban residential and general agriculture to rural residential in the town of Green Valley. The town of Green Valley recommended approval on March 10, 2026. No public testimony was offered for or against. Supervisor Drebeck moved to approve based on findings of fact, conclusions of law, and town approval. Supervisor Kurt seconded. Motion carried unanimously.

### Jeffrey and Cynthia Schmidtz rezoning petition - McMillan
The petition sought to rezone from Conservancy Recreation to General Agriculture in the town of McMillan. Supervisor Leur asked about the conservancy nature of the property. Staff explained the property has a home and horse riding stable, and the rezone would make these conforming uses. The town of McMillan recommended approval on April 20, 2026. Supervisor Sefelt moved to approve, seconded by Supervisor Drew. Motion carried unanimously.

### Fee waiver for April 17, 2026 severe weather impacted properties
Director Lori Mskimmons presented a proposal to waive county zoning and septic system fees for properties damaged by the April 17 tornado affecting Ringle, Kronenwetter, Easton, and Weston. Supervisor Drew questioned where to draw the line for future events. Supervisor Leur asked about budget impacts. Supervisor Drebeck expressed concerns about fairness to property owners not in declared disaster areas. After discussion, Supervisor Sefelt moved to forward the resolution to the county board. Supervisor Drebeck offered an amendment to limit the waiver to only reconnect fees ($160) for septic systems, excluding all other fees. The amendment was seconded by Supervisor Kurt and passed. The amended motion to forward to the county board passed but was noted as not unanimous.

### Solid Waste Department presentation
Director Dave Hagen Busher presented an overview of solid waste operations, explaining the landfill's 45-year history, the renewable natural gas partnership with Vidi Energy generating royalties for the county, and upcoming capital projects including an $6 million expansion and leachate treatment facility required by 2032 under new state law. He noted lithium-ion battery fires occur about once weekly.

### Parks, Recreation and Forestry Department presentation
Tom Lovelin, County Forest Administrator, presented on behalf of Director Jamie. He explained the department operates both county and city park systems under contract, manages 30,700 acres of county forest, and noted Nine Mile County Forest generates $3.25 million in economic impact from out-of-town visitors. Supervisor Sefelt acknowledged Tom's retirement effective June 1st after nearly 30 years of service.

## Public Comment
No public comment was offered.

## Action Items
- Forward amended resolution to county board waiving only reconnect fees for septic systems on properties impacted by April 17, 2026 severe weather
- Green Valley rezoning petition forwarded to county board for approval
- McMillan rezoning petition forwarded to county board for approval
- Staff to track costs and requests related to any fee waivers implemented
- Joe Tucker selected as new County Forest Administrator following Tom Lovelin's retirement
