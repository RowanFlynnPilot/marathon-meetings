## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Environmental Resources Committee approved two rezoning petitions and voted to forward a modified fee waiver resolution to the county board that would waive only reconnect fees for properties damaged by the April 17, 2026 tornado. The committee also received educational presentations from Solid Waste, Parks Recreation and Forestry, and Conservation Planning and Zoning departments.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of March 31, 2026 committee minutes
Supervisor Sefeld made a motion to approve the minutes, seconded by Supervisor Drebeck. The motion carried unanimously with no discussion.

### Tim Veland rezoning petition - Town of Green Valley
Land use specialist Garrett Pogle presented the staff report for rezoning from urban residential and general agriculture to rural residential for a land division. The Town of Green Valley recommended approval on March 10, 2026. No public testimony was offered for or against. Supervisor Drebeck moved to approve based on findings of fact, conclusions of law, and town approval. Supervisor Kurt seconded. Motion carried unanimously.

### Jeffrey and Cynthia Schmidtz rezoning petition - Town of McMillan
Garrett Pogle presented the petition to rezone from conservancy recreation to general agriculture. Supervisor Leur asked about the conservancy nature; staff explained the property has a home and horse riding stable, and rezoning would make the non-conforming home use conforming. No natural areas would be lost. Town of McMillan recommended approval on April 20, 2026. No public testimony was offered. Supervisor Sefeld moved to approve, Supervisor Drew seconded. Motion carried unanimously.

### Fee waiver for properties impacted by April 17, 2026 severe weather
Director Lori Mskimmons presented staff recommendation to waive county zoning and pouts fees for tornado-damaged properties. Significant debate ensued. Supervisor Drew questioned where to draw the line for future events. Supervisor Drebeck expressed concern about fairness to those not in declared disaster areas. Supervisor Leur asked about tracking costs. Eric Vogel questioned if homeowners insurance would cover fees. Supervisor Sefeld moved to forward the resolution; Supervisor Lam seconded. Supervisor Drebeck then moved to amend the resolution to only waive reconnect fees ($160) for pouts systems, not other fees. Supervisor K seconded the amendment. The amendment passed, and the amended resolution was approved to forward to county board, though not unanimously.

### Solid Waste Department presentation
Director David Hagenbusher provided overview of operations including the landfill serving Marathon, Portage, and Shawano counties, accepting about 1,000 tons daily. He discussed the renewable natural gas partnership with Vidi Energy generating royalties, significant capital expenses of $4-6 million for expansions and closures, and the requirement by 2032 to install on-site leachate treatment for PFAS removal. Marilyn Bend praised the department for keeping private landfill costs in check.

### Parks, Recreation, and Forestry Department presentation
Tom Lovelin, county forest administrator, presented on behalf of Director Jamie who had an emergency. He explained the joint city-county operation with 44 full-time and 120 seasonal staff managing 17 county parks, 30,700 acres of county forest, and city of Wausau parks and street trees. A Nine Mile study showed out-of-town visitors generate $3.25 million annually. Supervisor Sefeld announced Tom's retirement effective June 1st after nearly 30 years, with Joe Tucker selected as successor.

## Public Comment
No public comment was offered.

## Action Items
- Rezoning for James Offer and David Tract property approved - forwarded to Marathon County Board of Supervisors
- Rezoning for Jeffrey and Cynthia Schmidtz property approved - forwarded to Marathon County Board of Supervisors
- Modified fee waiver resolution (reconnect fees only) forwarded to Marathon County Board of Supervisors for tornado-damaged properties
- Staff to track costs and requests related to tornado fee waivers for future policy development
- Solid Waste to reapply for clean water fund loan by October 2025 for on-site leachate treatment facility
