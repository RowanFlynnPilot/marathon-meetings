## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Environmental Resources Committee approved two rezoning petitions unanimously and voted to forward a modified fee waiver proposal for tornado-damaged properties to the full county board. The committee amended the fee waiver resolution to only cover septic system reconnect fees rather than the broader waiver originally proposed by staff.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of March 31, 2026 committee minutes
Supervisor Sefeld made a motion to approve the minutes, seconded by Supervisor Drebeck. The motion carried unanimously with no discussion.

### Tim Veland rezoning petition - Town of Green Valley
Land Use Specialist Garrett Pogle presented the staff report for rezoning from urban residential and general agriculture to rural residential for a land division. The Town of Green Valley recommended approval on March 10, 2026. No public comment was offered. Supervisor Drebeck moved to approve based on findings of fact, conclusions of law, and town approval, seconded by Supervisor Kurt. Motion carried unanimously.

### Jeffrey and Cynthia Schmidtz rezoning petition - Town of McMillan
The petition sought to rezone from Conservancy Recreation to General Agriculture. Supervisor Lever asked about the conservancy nature of the property. Garrett Pogle explained the property contains a home and horse riding stable, and the rezone would make currently non-conforming uses conforming. He confirmed no natural areas would be lost. The Town of McMillan recommended approval on April 20, 2026. Supervisor Sefeld moved to approve, seconded by Supervisor Drew. Motion carried unanimously.

### Fee waiver for April 17, 2026 severe weather impacted properties
Director Lori Mskimmons presented a proposal to waive county zoning and septic fees for properties damaged by the tornado affecting Towns of Ringle, Eastston, and villages of Kronenwetter and Weston. Supervisor Drew questioned where to draw the line on future disasters. Supervisor Drebeck expressed concern about fairness to those not in declared disaster areas. Marilyn Bend asked about mound system costs. After discussion, Supervisor Sefeld moved to forward the resolution. Supervisor Drebeck then amended the motion to only waive reconnect fees ($160) for pouts systems, not other fees. The amendment passed, and the amended resolution was approved to forward to the county board, though the vote was not unanimous.

### Solid Waste Department overview
Director David Hagenbusher presented a comprehensive overview of solid waste operations, including the landfill's 45-year history, the new renewable natural gas partnership with Vidi Energy that began injecting gas in September 2025, and upcoming leachate treatment requirements by 2032. He noted April hauling costs were nearly $90,000 due to significant rainfall and discussed the $6 million expansion project starting this year.

### Parks, Recreation, and Forestry Department overview
Tom Lovelin, County Forest Administrator, presented on behalf of Director Jamie who had an emergency. He discussed the department serving both city and county with 44 full-time and 120 seasonal employees, the 17 county parks, and 30,700-acre county forest system. A Nine Mile County Forest study showed out-of-town visitors generate $3.25 million for the local economy. Supervisor Sefeld announced that Tom Lovelin is retiring June 1st after nearly 30 years, with Joe Tucker selected as successor.

## Public Comment
No public comment was offered. The chair noted no one had signed up for public comment.

- Rezoning petition for Jeffrey and Cynthia Schmidtz approved - forwarded to Marathon County Board of Supervisors
- Modified fee waiver resolution (covering only pouts reconnect fees for tornado-damaged properties) forwarded to county board for consideration
- Town of Green Valley recommended to update comprehensive plan to reflect zoning change if applicable
- Town of McMillan recommended to update comprehensive plan to reflect zoning change if applicable
