## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Environmental Resources Committee approved two rezoning petitions and voted to forward a modified tornado relief fee waiver proposal to the county board. The committee amended the fee waiver to only cover septic system reconnect fees ($160) for properties damaged by the April 17, 2026 tornado, rather than the broader waiver originally proposed by staff.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of March 31st, 2026 committee minutes
Supervisor Sefeld made the motion to approve and Supervisor Drebeck seconded. The motion carried with all in favor.

### Rezoning: Tim Veland on behalf of James Offer and David Tract
Land Use Specialist Garrett Pogle presented the staff report for rezoning from urban residential and general agriculture to rural residential in the Town of Green Valley. The Town Board recommended approval on March 10, 2026. No public testimony was offered. Supervisor Drebeck moved to approve based on findings of fact, conclusions of law, and town approval. Supervisor Kurt seconded. Motion carried unanimously.

### Rezoning: Jeffrey and Cynthia Schmidtz
Garrett Pogle presented the petition to rezone from Conservancy Recreation to General Agriculture in the Town of McMillan. Supervisor Leur asked about the conservancy nature and potential environmental impact. Pogle explained the property currently has a home and horse riding stable, and rezoning would make the existing uses conforming rather than non-conforming. He confirmed no natural areas would be lost. The Town Board recommended approval April 20, 2026. Supervisor Sefeld moved to approve; Supervisor Drew seconded. Motion carried unanimously.

### Fee waivers for April 17, 2026 severe weather damage
Director Lori Miskimmons presented staff recommendations for waiving county zoning and septic fees for tornado-damaged properties. Administrator Lance Leonhard noted this follows the governor's emergency declaration. Supervisor Drew questioned where to draw the line for future events. Supervisor Drebeck expressed concern about fairness compared to isolated incidents. Supervisor Leur asked about tracking costs. After discussion, Supervisor Sefeld moved to approve the full proposal; Supervisor Leur seconded. Supervisor Drebeck then offered an amendment to limit the waiver to only pouts reconnect fees ($160). Supervisor Kurt seconded the amendment. The amendment passed with opposition. The amended motion to forward to county board passed but was not unanimous.

## Public Comment
No public comment was offered.

- Forward rezoning petition for Jeffrey and Cynthia Schmidtz (McMillan) to County Board for approval
