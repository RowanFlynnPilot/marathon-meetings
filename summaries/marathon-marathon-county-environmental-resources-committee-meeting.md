## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Environmental Resources Committee approved two rezoning petitions and voted to forward a modified fee waiver resolution to the county board that would only cover septic system reconnect fees for properties impacted by the April 17, 2026 tornado. The committee also received educational presentations from the Solid Waste, Parks Recreation and Forestry, and Conservation Planning and Zoning departments.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of March 31st, 2026 committee minutes
Supervisor Sefeld made a motion to approve the minutes with Supervisor Drebeck providing the second. The motion carried unanimously with no discussion.

### Tim Veland rezoning petition - Town of Green Valley
Land Use Specialist Garrett Pogle presented the staff report for rezoning from urban residential and general agriculture to rural residential for a proposed land division. The Town of Green Valley Town Board recommended approval on March 10, 2026. No public testimony was offered for or against. Supervisor Drebeck moved to approve based on findings of fact, conclusions of law, and town approval. Supervisor Kurt seconded. Motion carried unanimously.

### Jeffrey and Cynthia Schmidtz rezoning petition - Town of McMillan
Staff presented the petition to rezone from conservancy recreation to general agriculture. Supervisor Leur asked about the conservancy nature of the property; Garrett Pogle explained the property contains a home and horse riding stable, and the rezoning would make the structures conforming uses rather than non-conforming. No natural areas would be lost. The Town of McMillan recommended approval on April 20, 2026. No public testimony was offered. Supervisor Sefeld moved to approve and forward to county board; Supervisor Drew seconded. Motion carried unanimously.

### Fee waivers for April 17, 2026 severe weather impacted properties
Director Lori Mskimmons presented a proposal to waive county zoning and septic system fees for properties damaged by the April 17 tornado affecting towns of Ringle, Easton, and villages of Kronenwetter and Weston. Supervisor Drew questioned where to draw the line for future events. Supervisor Drebeck questioned fairness compared to individual tornado victims not in declared disaster areas. Eric Vogel asked whether insurance would cover fees. After significant debate, Supervisor Sefeld moved to forward the resolution; Supervisor Leur seconded. Supervisor Drebeck then amended the motion to only waive reconnect fees ($160) for septic systems, not other zoning or septic fees. Supervisor Kurt seconded the amendment. The amendment passed. The amended motion to forward to county board passed but was noted as not unanimous.

### Solid Waste Department overview
Director David Hagenbusher presented on department operations including the landfill facility active since 1980, the renewable natural gas partnership with Vidi Energy launched in 2023, leachate treatment challenges due to PFAS regulations requiring treatment by 2032, and upcoming $6 million expansion projects. Marilyn Bend praised the department for keeping private landfill costs competitive. Supervisor Brew asked about grant funding; Hagenbusher reported their Clean Water Fund application was deemed incomplete and they will reapply for 2027 funding.

### Parks, Recreation and Forestry Department overview
County Forest Administrator Tom Lovelin presented on behalf of Director Jamie, covering the joint city-county park system operational since 1924, 17 county parks, 30,700 acres of county forest, and Nine Mile County Forest which generates $3.25 million annually for the local economy from out-of-town visitors. Supervisor Sefeld announced that Tom Lovelin is retiring June 1st after nearly 30 years of service. Chair Ritterder thanked him for his years of service.

## Public Comment
No public comment was offered.

## Action Items
- Rezoning from urban residential and general agriculture to rural residential for James Offer and David Tract property in Town of Green Valley approved and forwarded to county board
- Rezoning from conservancy recreation to general agriculture for Jeffrey and Cynthia Schmidtz property in Town of McMillan approved and forwarded to county board
- Modified resolution to waive only septic system reconnect fees ($160) for tornado-impacted properties forwarded to county board for consideration
- Town of Green Valley should update comprehensive plan to reflect zoning change if applicable
- Town of McMillan should update comprehensive plan to reflect zoning change if applicable
