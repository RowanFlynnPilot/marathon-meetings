## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Environmental Resources Committee approved two rezoning petitions and voted to forward a modified fee waiver resolution to the county board that will only waive reconnect fees for septic systems damaged by the April 17, 2026 tornado. The committee also received educational presentations from Solid Waste, Parks Recreation and Forestry, and Conservation Planning and Zoning departments.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of March 31, 2026 committee minutes
Supervisor Sefeld made a motion to approve the minutes, seconded by Supervisor Drebeck. The motion carried unanimously with no discussion.

### Tim Veland rezoning petition - Town of Green Valley
Land Use Specialist Garrett Pogle presented the staff report for rezoning from urban residential and general agriculture to rural residential for a land division. The Town of Green Valley recommended approval on March 10, 2026. No public comment was offered. Supervisor Drebeck moved to approve based on findings of fact, conclusions of law, and town approval. Supervisor Kurt seconded. Motion carried unanimously.

### Jeffrey and Cynthia Schmidtz rezoning petition - Town of McMillan
Garrett Pogle presented the petition to rezone from conservancy recreation to general agriculture. Supervisor Leur asked about the conservancy nature of the property; Pogle explained the property contains a home and horse riding stable, and rezoning would make the home a conforming use rather than non-conforming. No natural areas would be lost. Town of McMillan recommended approval on April 20, 2026. No public comment was offered. Supervisor Sefeld moved to approve, Supervisor Drew seconded. Motion carried unanimously.

### Fee waiver consideration for April 17, 2026 severe weather damage
Director Lori Mskimmons presented staff recommendations for temporarily waiving county zoning and septic fees for properties damaged by the tornado affecting towns of Ringle, Eastston, villages of Cronenwetter and Weston. Supervisor Drew questioned where to draw the line for future events. Supervisor Leur asked about potential levy impacts. Supervisor Drebeck questioned fairness compared to individual disasters not declared emergencies. Eric Vogel asked about insurance coverage. After extensive debate, Supervisor Sefeld moved to forward the resolution to the county board. Supervisor Leur seconded. Supervisor Drebeck then amended the motion to only waive reconnect fees for septic systems (not other zoning or septic fees). Supervisor Kurt seconded the amendment. The amendment passed. The amended motion to forward to county board passed, though not unanimously.

### Solid Waste Department presentation
Director Dave Hagen Busher provided an educational overview of solid waste operations, including the landfill history since 1980, the renewable natural gas partnership with Vidi Energy, leachate treatment challenges due to PFAS regulations requiring treatment by 2032, and upcoming capital projects totaling millions of dollars. Marilyn Bend praised the department for keeping private landfill costs in check.

### Parks, Recreation, and Forestry Department presentation
Tom Lovelin, County Forest Administrator, presented on behalf of Director Jamie who had an emergency. He covered the department's 44 full-time and 120 seasonal employees, 17 county parks, 30,700 acres of county forest, and services provided to City of Wausau. He noted Nine Mile County Forest generates $3.25 million for the local economy from out-of-town visitors. Supervisor Sefeld announced Tom Lovelin is retiring June 1st after nearly 30 years of service, with Joe Tucker selected as his replacement.

## Public Comment
No public comment was offered. No one signed up for public comment at the beginning of the meeting, and no members of the public spoke during the rezoning public hearings.

## Action Items
- Both rezoning petitions forwarded to Marathon County Board of Supervisors for final approval
- Modified fee waiver resolution (reconnect fees only) forwarded to county board for consideration
- Towns should update comprehensive plans to reflect zoning changes if applicable
- Staff to track costs and requests related to tornado fee waivers for future policy development
- Conservation services presentation to be scheduled for next meeting with Kirsty Heiden
