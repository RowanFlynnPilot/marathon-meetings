## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Environmental Resources Committee approved two rezoning petitions unanimously and voted to forward a modified fee waiver resolution to the full county board that will only cover septic system reconnect fees for properties damaged by the April 17, 2026 tornado. The committee also received educational presentations from the Solid Waste, Parks Recreation and Forestry, and Conservation Planning and Zoning departments.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of March 31, 2026 committee minutes
Supervisor Sefeld made a motion to approve the minutes, seconded by Supervisor Drebeck. The motion carried unanimously with no discussion.

### Tim Veland rezoning petition (Town of Green Valley)
Land Use Specialist Garrett Pogle presented the staff report for rezoning from Urban Residential and General Agriculture to Rural Residential for a land division. The Town of Green Valley recommended approval on March 10, 2026. No public comment was offered. Supervisor Drebeck moved to approve based on findings of fact and conclusions of law, seconded by Supervisor Kurt. Motion carried unanimously.

### Jeffrey and Cynthia Schmidtz rezoning petition (Town of McMillan)
Garrett Pogle presented the petition to rezone from Conservancy Recreation to General Agriculture. Supervisor Leur asked about the conservancy nature, and Pogle explained the property contains a home and horse riding stable, and rezoning would make the non-conforming home use conforming. He confirmed no natural areas would be lost. The Town of McMillan recommended approval on April 20, 2026. Supervisor Sefelt moved to approve, seconded by Supervisor Drew. Motion carried unanimously.

### Fee waiver for April 17, 2026 severe weather victims
Director Lori Mskimmons presented a proposal to waive county zoning and septic fees for properties damaged by the April 17 tornado affecting the towns of Ringle, Easton, and villages of Kronenwetter and Weston. Supervisor Drew questioned where to draw the line for future events. Supervisor Leur asked about potential levy impacts. Supervisor Drebeck expressed concern about fairness to victims of non-declared disasters and proposed an amendment to only waive reconnect fees ($160) for septic systems rather than all fees. The amendment was seconded by Supervisor Kurt and passed. The amended resolution to forward to the county board also passed, though not unanimously.

### Solid Waste Department presentation
Director Dave Hagen Busher presented an overview of solid waste operations including the landfill's history since 1980, the renewable natural gas partnership with Vidi Energy launched in 2023, and challenges with leachate treatment due to PFAS regulations requiring on-site treatment by 2032. He noted significant capital expenses including $6 million for 11-acre construction and $3-4 million closures. Marilyn Bend praised the department's work helping Ringle tornado victims.

### Parks, Recreation, and Forestry Department presentation
Tom Lovelin, County Forest Administrator, presented on behalf of Director Jamie who had an emergency. He covered the joint city-county department structure with 44 full-time and 120 seasonal employees, 17 county parks, 30,700 acres of county forest, and Nine Mile County Forest recreation. A study showed out-of-town visitors generate $3.25 million annually for Marathon County. Supervisor Sefelt announced Lovelin's retirement effective June 1st after nearly 30 years of service.

## Public Comment
No public comment was offered. No one signed up for public comment and no one spoke during the public hearing portions for either rezoning petition.
