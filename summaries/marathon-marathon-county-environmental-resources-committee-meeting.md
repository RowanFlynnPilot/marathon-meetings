## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Environmental Resources Committee approved two rezoning petitions unanimously and voted to forward a modified fee waiver resolution to the county board that will only waive reconnect fees for properties impacted by the April 17, 2026 tornado. The committee also received educational presentations from Solid Waste, Parks Recreation and Forestry, and Conservation Planning and Zoning departments.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of March 31st, 2026 minutes
Supervisor Sefeld made a motion to approve the minutes, seconded by Supervisor Drebeck. The motion carried unanimously with no discussion.

### Tim Veland rezoning petition - Town of Green Valley
Garrett Pogle presented the staff report for rezoning from urban residential and general agriculture to rural residential for a land division. The Town of Green Valley recommended approval on March 10, 2026. No public testimony was offered. Supervisor Drebeck moved to approve based on findings of fact and town approval, seconded by Supervisor Kurt. Motion carried unanimously.

### Jeffrey and Cynthia Schmidtz rezoning petition - Town of McMillan
Staff presented the petition to rezone from Conservancy Recreation to General Agriculture. Supervisor Leur asked about the conservancy nature, and Garrett Pogle explained the property has a home and horse riding stable that are non-conforming uses in conservancy, and rezoning would make them conforming. He confirmed no natural area would be lost. Town of McMillan recommended approval on April 20, 2026. Supervisor Sefeld moved to approve, seconded by Supervisor Drew. Motion carried unanimously.

### Fee waiver for tornado-impacted properties
Director Lori Mskimmons presented a proposal to waive county zoning and private on-site wastewater treatment system fees for properties impacted by the April 17, 2026 severe weather. Supervisor Drew questioned where to draw the line for future events. Supervisor Leur asked about tracking costs. Supervisor Drebeck expressed concerns about fairness to others not in declared disaster areas and questioned consistency with recent fee increases. Supervisor Sefeld moved to forward the resolution, seconded by Supervisor Leur. Supervisor Drebeck then moved to amend the resolution to only waive reconnect fees ($160) and no other pouts or zoning fees, seconded by Supervisor Kurt. The amendment passed. The amended motion to forward to county board passed but was not unanimous.

## Public Comment
No public comment was offered.
