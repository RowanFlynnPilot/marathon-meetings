## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Environmental Resources Committee approved two rezoning petitions unanimously and voted to forward a modified fee waiver resolution to the county board that will only cover septic system reconnect fees (not other zoning or pouts fees) for properties damaged by the April 17, 2026 tornado. The committee also received educational presentations from Solid Waste, Parks Recreation and Forestry, and Conservation Planning and Zoning departments.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of March 31, 2026 committee minutes
Supervisor Sefeld made a motion to approve the minutes, seconded by Supervisor Drebeck. The motion carried unanimously with no discussion.

### Tim Veland rezoning petition - Town of Green Valley
Garrett Pogle presented the staff report for rezoning from urban residential and general agriculture to rural residential for a land division. The Town of Green Valley recommended approval on March 10, 2026. No public comment was offered. Supervisor Drebeck moved to approve based on findings of fact, conclusions of law, and town approval, seconded by Supervisor Kurt. Motion carried unanimously.

### Jeffrey and Cynthia Schmidtz rezoning petition - Town of McMillan
Garrett Pogle presented the petition to rezone from Conservancy Recreation to General Agriculture. Supervisor Leur asked about the conservancy nature of the property; Pogle explained the property contains a home and horse riding stable, and rezoning would make the existing structures conforming uses rather than non-conforming. The Town of McMillan recommended approval on April 20, 2026. No public comment was offered. Supervisor Sefeld moved to approve, seconded by Supervisor Drew. Motion carried unanimously.

### Fee waiver for severe weather impacted properties
Director Lori Mskimmons presented a proposal to waive county zoning and pouts fees for properties damaged by the April 17, 2026 tornado affecting the Town of Ringle and surrounding areas. Significant debate ensued. Supervisor Drew questioned where to draw the line for future events. Supervisor Drebeck expressed concerns about fairness to individuals hit by isolated severe weather who wouldn't qualify. Supervisor Sefeld questioned potential levy impacts. After discussion, Supervisor Sefeld moved to approve the full resolution; Supervisor Leur seconded. Supervisor Drebeck then offered an amendment to limit the waiver to only reconnect fees for pouts systems ($160 each), not other zoning or pouts fees. Supervisor Kurt seconded the amendment. The amendment passed. The amended motion to forward the limited fee waiver to the county board then passed, though not unanimously.

## Public Comment
No public comment was offered.

- Forward rezoning petition for Jeffrey and Cynthia Schmidtz (Town of McMillan) to Marathon County Board of Supervisors for approval
