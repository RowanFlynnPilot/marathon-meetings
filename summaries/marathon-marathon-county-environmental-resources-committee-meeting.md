## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Environmental Resources Committee approved two rezoning petitions unanimously and voted to forward a modified fee waiver resolution for tornado-affected properties to the county board. The committee also received educational presentations from Solid Waste, Parks Recreation and Forestry, and Conservation Planning and Zoning departments.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of March 31, 2026 committee minutes
Supervisor Sefeld made a motion to approve the minutes, seconded by Supervisor Drebeck. The motion passed unanimously with no discussion.

### Tim Veland rezoning petition (Town of Green Valley)
Land Use Specialist Garrett Pago presented the staff report for rezoning from Urban Residential and General Agriculture to Rural Residential for a proposed land division. The Town of Green Valley Town Board recommended approval on March 10, 2026. No public comment was offered. Supervisor Drebeck moved to approve based on findings of fact and the town's approval, seconded by Supervisor Kurt. The motion passed unanimously.

### Jeffrey and Cynthia Schmidtz rezoning petition (Town of McMillan)
Garrett Pago presented the petition to rezone from Conservancy Recreation to General Agriculture. Supervisor Leur asked about the conservancy nature of the property; Pago explained it currently has a home and horse riding stable, and the rezone would make the home a conforming use rather than non-conforming. The Town of McMillan recommended approval on April 20, 2026. No natural area is being lost. Supervisor Sefelt moved to approve, seconded by Supervisor Drew. The motion passed unanimously.

### Fee waiver for properties impacted by April 17, 2026 severe weather
Director Lori Mskimmons presented a proposal to waive county zoning and septic system fees for tornado-affected properties. Administrator Lance Leonhard noted this was in response to the governor's emergency declaration. Supervisor Drew questioned where to draw the line for future events. Supervisor Drebeck expressed concern about fairness to those affected by non-declared disasters. Marilyn Bend asked about septic hookup fees. After discussion, Supervisor Drebeck moved to amend the resolution to only waive reconnect fees ($160) for septic systems, not other zoning or septic fees. The amendment was seconded by Supervisor Kurt and passed. The amended resolution was then approved to forward to the county board, though the vote was not unanimous.

### Solid Waste Department presentation
Director David Hagen Busher provided an overview of the department's operations, including the landfill history, tipping fees, renewable natural gas partnership with Vidi Energy, and upcoming leachate treatment requirements by 2032. He noted hauling costs for April were close to $90,000 due to significant rainfall. The department has 11 FTE staff handling approximately 2,000 tons daily.

### Parks, Recreation and Forestry Department presentation
County Forest Administrator Tom Lovelin presented on behalf of Director Jamie who had an emergency. He explained the department operates both county and city park systems since 1924, manages 30,700 acres of county forest, and employs 44 full-time and 120 seasonal workers. A Nine Mile County Forest study showed out-of-town visitors generate $3.25 million for the county economy. Supervisor Sefelt announced Tom Lovelin is retiring June 1st after nearly 30 years of service.

## Public Comment
No public comment was offered.

## Action Items
- Rezoning from Urban Residential and General Agriculture to Rural Residential approved for James Offer and David Tract property in Town of Green Valley - forwarded to county board
- Rezoning from Conservancy Recreation to General Agriculture approved for Jeffrey and Cynthia Schmidtz property in Town of McMillan - forwarded to county board
- Modified fee waiver resolution (reconnect fees only) for April 17, 2026 tornado-affected properties forwarded to county board for consideration
- Staff to track costs and requests related to fee waiver program for future policy development
- Joe Tucker selected as new County Forest Administrator to replace retiring Tom Lovelin
