## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Environmental Resources Committee approved two rezoning petitions and voted to forward a modified fee waiver resolution to the full county board. The fee waiver, originally proposed to help properties impacted by the April 17, 2026 tornado, was amended to cover only septic system reconnect fees rather than all zoning and pouts fees.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of March 31st, 2026 committee minutes
Supervisor Sefeld made the motion to approve, with Supervisor Drebeck providing the second. The motion passed unanimously with no further discussion.

### Tim Veland rezoning petition - Town of Green Valley
Land Use Specialist Garrett Pogle presented the staff report for rezoning from urban residential and general agriculture to rural residential for a proposed land division. The Town of Green Valley Town Board recommended approval on March 10, 2026. No public comment was offered. Supervisor Drebeck moved to approve based on findings of fact, conclusions of law, and town approval. Supervisor Kurt seconded. The motion carried unanimously.

### Jeffrey and Cynthia Schmidtz rezoning petition - Town of McMillan
Garrett Pogle presented the petition to rezone from conservancy/recreation to general agriculture. Supervisor Lever asked about the conservancy designation, and Pogle explained the property contains a home and horse riding stable, and the rezoning would make these conforming uses. He confirmed no natural areas would be lost. The Town of McMillan recommended approval on April 20, 2026. Supervisor Sefelt moved to approve, Supervisor Drew seconded. The motion carried unanimously.

### Fee waiver for severe weather impacted properties
Director Lori Mskimmons presented a proposal to waive county zoning and pouts fees for properties impacted by the April 17, 2026 tornado, affecting towns of Ringle, Easton, villages of Kronenwetter and Weston. Supervisor Drew questioned where to draw the line for future events. Supervisor Sefelt asked about potential levy impacts. Supervisor Drebeck proposed an amendment to limit the waiver to only reconnect fees ($160) for septic systems, excluding other pouts and zoning fees. The amendment was seconded by Supervisor Kurt and passed. The amended resolution was then voted on and passed, though not unanimously, to be forwarded to the county board.

### Solid Waste Department presentation
Director David Hagenbusher provided an overview of landfill operations, explaining they accept about 1,000 tons daily, have partnerships with Portage and Shawano counties, and began injecting renewable natural gas into pipelines in September 2025. He discussed upcoming capital expenses including an $6 million 11-acre construction project and a required leachate treatment facility by 2032 due to new PFAS regulations. He noted hauling costs for April approached $90,000 due to significant rainfall.

### Parks, Recreation, and Forestry Department presentation
County Forest Administrator Tom Lovelin presented on behalf of Director Jamie, covering the department's 44 full-time and 120 seasonal employees who serve both county and city of Wausau facilities. He highlighted that Nine Mile County Forest generates $3.25 million annually for the local economy from out-of-town visitors. Supervisor Sefelt announced Tom's retirement effective June 1st, with Joe Tucker selected as his replacement. Committee members thanked Lovelin for his nearly 30 years of service.

## Public Comment
No public comment was offered.
