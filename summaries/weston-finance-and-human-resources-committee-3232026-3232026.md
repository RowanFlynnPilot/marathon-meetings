## Meeting Overview
The Finance and Human Resources Committee received a comprehensive presentation on public works operations and budget, then debated employee clothing allowance policy changes following cancellation of the Cintas uniform contract. After multiple failed votes, the committee ultimately recommended a $400 annual clothing allowance (reduced from the proposed $600) plus purchase of a washer and dryer for staff use.

## Key Discussions
### February 2026 Financial Reports and Minutes
The committee approved the minutes from February 16, 2026 meeting unanimously with motion by Steve and second by Stephanie. The committee also acknowledged the February 2026 financial reports for all funds, T1 and T2 detail reports, and legal details for February, all passing unanimously.

### Public Works Operations and Budget Presentation
Public Works Director Michael delivered an extensive presentation on department operations covering 119.5 centerline miles of roads, 50+ pieces of equipment, and utility infrastructure. He noted the 2026 public works budget decreased by $26,000 (1.1%) compared to 2025. Michael highlighted that Weston spends approximately $9,700 less per mile than the average central Wisconsin community on streets. He discussed the March 15th snowstorm that cost approximately $50,000 with some employees working 17+ hour shifts, and noted potential disaster relief funding through Marathon County. The department currently has 9 of 10 positions filled with a new employee starting Wednesday.

### Employee Clothing and Equipment Allowance Policy Amendment
Significant debate occurred over increasing the employee clothing allowance from $300 to $600 following cancellation of the Cintas uniform contract. Committee member Daniels argued fiscal responsibility given the upcoming fire department referendum, stating she was comfortable with $300-400 rather than $600. Michael defended the increase, noting employees work in harsh conditions with chemicals and that the village already spends less than comparable communities. The initial motion for $600 failed 2-3 (Daniels yes, Armain yes, Love no, My no, Satai no). A motion for $400 annually also failed 2-3. A motion for $500 annually with washer/dryer failed on tie vote. The final motion for $400 for remainder of 2026 and $500 annually starting 2027 with washer/dryer purchase passed with one opposed.

## Public Comment
Lisa Beck of 1808 Cortez Lane offered public comment. She praised Public Works Director Mike for his department's work during the recent storm. She also questioned the proposed employee clothing allowance increase, suggesting the village should save money rather than increase the stipend to the highest proposed amount, noting she doesn't know other companies offering such benefits.

## Action Items
- Recommend to Village Board: Employee clothing allowance of $400 for remainder of 2026, $500 annually starting 2027, plus one-time purchase of washer and dryer for staff use
- New public works employee starting Wednesday to fill 10th position
- Salt order of 1,500 tons placed with state cooperative purchasing program
- Marathon County disaster relief application pending for March snowstorm costs (approximately $50,000 for Village of Weston)
- Next meeting scheduled for Tuesday, April 21st at 5:00 PM due to new board member swearing-in
