## Meeting Overview
The Marathon County Board held an educational meeting featuring presentations on PFAS contamination litigation opportunities and renewable energy regulation. No votes were taken as this was an informational session, but the board heard from legal counsel about potentially joining multi-district litigation to recover costs from PFAS contamination at county facilities, and received detailed guidance on the county's limited authority to regulate large-scale wind and solar projects.

## Key Discussions
### PFAS Multi-District Litigation Presentation
Attorney Carrie McDougall from Baron and Bud Law Firm presented via WebEx on the ongoing PFAS litigation, explaining that the water system settlement was the largest toxic tort settlement in U.S. history at approximately $12-13 billion from 3M and $3-5 billion from DuPont. He explained that soil-based claims including airports, landfills, and wastewater facilities are likely next in the litigation queue. The firm works on a 25% contingency fee basis with no upfront costs to the county. Supervisor Robinson asked about land spreading impacts, and Vice Chair Dickinson clarified that the airport has no known PFAS contamination currently.

### Renewable Energy Regulation Presentation
Attorney Rebecca Roker from Atollis Law presented on county authority over wind and solar projects. She explained that projects over 100 megawatts fall under PSC jurisdiction, noting the PSC has approved 33 solar projects with zero denials. She discussed the Hub City Wind project from Alliant Energy and the Stormark Wind Energy Center being proposed for the county. Roker emphasized that Joint Development Agreements (JDAs) are the most effective tool for protecting county interests since litigation has proven ineffective, and that the Town of Brighton case, while successful at circuit court level, was dismissed on appeal when EDP Renewables purchased the Marathon Wind project.

### Public Comment on Wind Turbines
Three speakers addressed wind turbine concerns. Cindy Nielson from Stratford stated she visited 200 houses and found no support for wind turbines, expressing concern about setbacks of 1,250 feet from her home. Wendy Rowski from Green Valley urged the board to vote no on the comprehensive plan, objecting to the term 'wind farm' and requesting it be called 'industrial wind energy development.' Heidi Pesky from McMillan argued against Joint Development Agreements, citing concerns about 30-50 year commitments and inadequate decommissioning assurances.

### Public Comment on Double NN Speed Limit
Barb Newton and Cindy Hogan from the Village of Rib Mountain spoke in support of reducing the speed limit on Double NN and establishing a no-passing zone. Newton reported 75 residents signed a petition supporting the change, citing near head-on collisions and safety concerns near the ski hill and local businesses. Hogan thanked the infrastructure committee for forwarding the recommendation to the county board.
