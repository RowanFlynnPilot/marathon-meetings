## Meeting Overview
The Wausau Finance Committee approved several routine items including airport ground leases and budget carryovers, but postponed action on joining a national opioid settlement and a budget amendment for lead service line replacement costs. The committee also approved transfers to cover shortfalls in recycling, airport, and parking funds, and advanced the 2026 borrowing calendar showing the city's debt utilization will decrease slightly despite new issuances.

## Key Discussions
### Minutes approval
Alder Watson moved to approve the March 10, 2026 minutes, seconded by Alder Griner. Motion passed unanimously with voice vote.

### Green Acres at Greenwood Hills LLC tax claim
This claim relates to ongoing litigation with Greenwood Hills. Alder Watson moved to approve the claim for recovery of unlawful tax, seconded by Griner. The chair explained a 'no' vote would deny the claim. The motion failed when members voted 'no' to deny the claim.

### Airport ground leases - 939 Woods Place
Three related items handled the transfer of a hangar from Win O. Jones to Owen Jones. All three motions (consent to transfer, termination of old lease, approval of new lease) passed unanimously with voice votes. Motions made by Watson and Tierney, seconded by Griner and Watson.

### Airport ground lease with Cole Lundberg
Alder Griner moved to approve, seconded by Watson. Passed unanimously.

### National opioid settlement participation
Committee members expressed discomfort proceeding without more information. Alder Malini asked where this came from, and Assistant Attorney Vincent explained a law firm sent notice that the city may qualify for the class action. Alder Tierney and Watson expressed concerns about signing away future legal action without knowing details. Alder Griner moved to postpone to the next meeting, seconded by Tierney. Motion to postpone passed unanimously. Deadline to opt in is May 4th.

### Lead service line replacement budget amendment
Eric from Public Works explained the DNR changed funding terms, leaving $709,000 in costs not covered by the subsidized loan - $284,000 for homeowner side and $426,000 for water utility side. Finance Director Marian outlined options including using general fund reserves, PFAS settlement money, or adding to borrowing. Alder Tierney opposed adding more debt. Alder Watson moved to postpone to allow time to determine funding sources including potential PFAS settlement use. Motion to postpone passed unanimously.

### 2025-2026 carryover funds
Finance Director explained the large carryover amount includes grant-funded items like 10 transit buses from VW mitigation program. Several projects including city hall chimney liner and DPW fence replacement haven't started yet. Alder Watson moved to approve, seconded by Griner. Passed unanimously.

### 2025 motorpool fund results
Finance Director reported the motorpool fund shows a $41,373 loss before GMT transfer, but with the $191,000 GMT transfer would show $150,000 profit. There's a $177,000 cash flow shortfall expected, but ARPA savings and equipment sale proceeds should cover it. Solomon from MotorPool explained delays on dump trucks and ambulances ordered in 2023, noting supply chain issues but equipment should arrive soon. This was informational only; no action taken.

### 2025 general fund results
Finance Director reported $1.2 million surplus driven by strong building permits, GMT money, and investment income. Public works, fire, and police are over budget primarily due to motorpool charges. CCITC overage of $194,000 was attributed to communication problems including double-counting a 95% personnel budget adjustment and unbudgeted Office 365 upgrade ($65,000). After proposed transfers to recycling, airport, and parking funds, surplus would be $540,000. Alder Tierney moved to approve the transfers, seconded by Watson. Passed unanimously.

### 2026 general obligation promissory note
Finance Director presented borrowing calendar showing $10 million in new borrowing against $12 million in retired debt, reducing debt utilization slightly. Projects include street improvements (10-year), motorpool (5-year), and various TID district projects. Alder Watson moved to approve the calendar, the chair seconded. Passed unanimously. Phil Cawson from Ehlers will present parameters resolution at next meeting.

### Potential property purchases for DPW expansion
Properties at 108, 112, 112½ Adolf Street and 233 Myron Street were scheduled for closed session discussion. Due to time constraints with council meeting starting at 6:30, Alder Watson moved to postpone to next meeting, seconded by Tierney. Passed unanimously.

## Public Comment
No public comment was offered.
