## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Finance and Human Resources Committee held its June 15th meeting, approving monthly financial reports and receiving an educational presentation from the finance department on its operations. The most significant action was reversing two prior motions from March regarding employee uniform policy and approving a new hybrid approach: employees may either keep their Cintas uniform rental service or opt out and receive $500 annually to purchase their own clothing, with staff to explore a shorter-term Cintas contract.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of May 18th minutes
A motion to approve was made by Daniels and seconded by Mynull (phonetic). No discussion occurred and the motion carried unanimously.

### Items 5-9: May budget status report, TIF reports, legal details, PSC annual report, State Municipal Financial Report
Finance staff Jessica presented all five items together. Discussion arose around item 7 (legal details) about whether the committee still needed to review it monthly, with committee members suggesting it be brought to the full board to decide. Jessica noted the legal detail report was originally requested by members who wanted to monitor legal spending without searching the full check register. For item 8, Jessica and utility staff explained the PSC annual report in detail, noting 1,029 fire hydrants, 6,091 customers, a 2% rate of return, and approximately 10 miles of asbestos cement water main remaining, being replaced at about one mile per year. The committee acknowledged all five items via a motion by Mynull, seconded by Daniels, which passed unanimously.

### Item 10: Educational presentation - Finance Department
Finance Director Jessica and staff members Song and Renee gave a detailed presentation on the finance department's operations, covering payroll (ranging from 38 to 64 employees per pay period, averaging $111,410 bi-weekly), accounts payable ($50,266,621 in total disbursements in 2025, with 2,420 checks and 47 ACH payments), accounts receivable ($1.2 million billed in 2024), the multi-step invoice approval process, cash management, and required state reporting. Jessica emphasized the importance of asking questions about financial reports, illustrating with a cautionary example from a South Area Fair Emergency District that had hidden a $574,000 intergovernmental payable. Committee members asked about Excel-related human error risks, and Jessica clarified the department uses a software-integrated 'My Excel' tool that pulls directly from the Casselle financial system.

### Item 11: Old business - Clothing allowance amendments
Public Works Director Michael presented a revised recommendation on the employee uniform policy, walking back the committee's March decision to cancel the Cintas contract and offer $400 plus purchase a washer and dryer. Michael said employee morale concerns and the complexity of a one-size-fits-all policy led him to recommend a hybrid approach: employees may keep Cintas service or opt out and receive $500 annually to purchase their own clothing, with the election made each January. The committee also discussed pursuing a shorter-term or month-to-month Cintas arrangement rather than a new three-year contract given past billing disputes. Committee member Barb moved to first rescind the two prior motions from March (canceling Cintas and approving $400 plus washer/dryer), which passed unanimously, then moved the new hybrid policy, which also passed unanimously.

## Public Comment
No public comment was offered. The chair confirmed no one was present in the audience or online at the start of the meeting.

## Action Items

- Question of whether finance committee should continue reviewing monthly legal detail report to be referred to the full village board for a decision
