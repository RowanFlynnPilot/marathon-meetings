## Meeting Overview
The Wausau Plan Commission approved two major items: a conditional use permit for a 70-unit, 7-story apartment building at 731 North First Street for Beacon Resources LLC, and a transportation project plat for Grand Avenue signal replacements. A public hearing was held regarding a proposed personal storage facility at 218 South Fourth Street, with the applicants citing growing downtown housing demand as justification.

## Key Discussions
### Minutes for February 18th
Motion to approve made by Bugamman, seconded by Balkan. Minutes were approved unanimously with all members voting aye.

### Public hearing on conditional use permit for 218 South Fourth Street storage facility
Jason Dunwoody and Melinda Dunwoody spoke in favor of the storage facility proposal. They argued that with over 400 new apartment units recently approved downtown, including the 153-unit Foundry on Third and 102-unit Evergreen Landing projects, residents need convenient storage options rather than traveling to surrounding areas like Kronenwetter. The public hearing was closed but no vote was taken on this item during this meeting.

### Conditional use permit for 731 North First Street apartment building
Motion to approve made by Bornman, seconded by Bugamin. The permit authorizes Beacon Resources LLC to construct a 70-unit, 7-story apartment building. No discussion or questions were raised. The motion passed unanimously.

### Transportation project plat for Grand Avenue signal replacements
Motion to approve made by Bugamin, seconded by Balkan. The project covers signal replacements at Sturgeon and Townline Road (Project 370-40-40). No discussion was held. The motion passed unanimously.

## Public Comment
One written public comment was submitted by Linda Lawrence on March 12th supporting the Beacon Resources apartment development, stating 'Housing of this capacity will be good for downtown small businesses' and expressing personal willingness to live there. Jason Dunwoody and Melinda Dunwoody provided in-person public comment during the storage facility hearing, speaking in favor of the proposal.
