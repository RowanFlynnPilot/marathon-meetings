## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Board of Trustees approved multiple ordinances including rezoning requests and a modified speed limit ordinance, approved a 10-year baseball/softball field maintenance agreement, and deferred action on the remote meeting attendance policy until the new board is seated. A resident criticized the board's approach to funding the fire department through referendum rather than budget prioritization.

## Key Discussions
### Public Comments
Jim Pensel of 5002 Aerrol Street spoke critically of the board's approach to fire department funding. He praised SAFER personnel after attending their citizen academy but criticized the referendum approach, stating 'We don't have a revenue problem here in Weston. We have a priority problem.' He argued the board should prioritize fire/EMS funding over amenities like artificial turf and the aquatic center.

### Finance Director Response to Public Comment
Finance Director Jessica responded that the village cannot borrow for additional firefighters, only for capital projects like the Kennedy Park turf. She noted the village is 'the cheapest' and 'most efficient' but stated 'we need more money' and expressed frustration with criticism, suggesting her position might be open in a couple months.

### Speed Limit Ordinance 26-006
The original ordinance to set Weston Avenue speed limits at 35 mph from Von Kennel to Ryan failed 4-3, with Maloney, Jordan, and the presiding officer voting no. Trustee Maloney argued the 35 mph limit from Von Kennel to Ryan was unnecessary given the road conditions compared to Scoffield Avenue. An amended motion was approved setting Camp Phillips to Von Kennel at 35 mph while keeping Von Kennel to Highway J at 45 mph.

### Rezoning Ordinances
Two rezoning ordinances were approved unanimously as recommended by the Planning Commission: Ordinance 26-00004 rezoning a portion of 8905 Bert Street from RR5 to SFS, and Ordinance 26-00005 rezoning a portion of 7105 Christensen Avenue from SL to SFS.

### Intersection Signage at Community Center Drive
The board approved changing the stop sign on Community Center Drive at Birch Street to a yield sign, with an amendment adding a stop sign for bicyclists coming off the pedestrian bridge. Trustee Hooang raised concerns about cyclists traveling 15-20 mph off the bridge without any signage. Motion passed unanimously with the friendly amendment.

### Baseball/Softball Field Maintenance Agreement
A 10-year maintenance and user agreement with youth baseball and softball organizations was approved unanimously. The committee recommended the long term to protect the village's investment at Kennedy Park and provide stability despite leadership changes in organizations.

### Remote Meeting Attendance Policy
Trustee Lewis moved to defer this item to the next meeting so the newly seated board could make the decision. Motion to defer passed unanimously.

### Microsoft Teams for Communication
The board approved using Microsoft Teams for trustee communication starting with the next term. Training will be provided when the new board is seated. Motion passed unanimously.

### Trustee Remarks - Joe's Final Meeting
The board acknowledged that Trustee Joe Zagami's term is ending. Trustees Mark Maloney and others thanked him for his service, and Zagami indicated he intends to continue serving on committees including the planning commission.

## Public Comment
Jim Pensel of 5002 Aerrol Street, Weston, spoke for approximately 4 minutes criticizing the board's approach to fire department funding. He praised SAFER staff after attending their citizen academy, but argued the referendum approach was 'short-sighted' and that the board should reprioritize existing budget funds away from amenities like artificial turf and the aquatic center toward fully funding fire and EMS.
