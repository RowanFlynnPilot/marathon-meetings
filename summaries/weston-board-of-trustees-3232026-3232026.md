## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Board of Trustees approved multiple ordinances including rezonings and a modified speed limit ordinance for Weston Avenue, rejected the original speed limit proposal 4-3 before passing an amended version keeping Von Kennel to Highway J at 45 mph. The board heard passionate public comment criticizing the approach to funding the fire department through referendum rather than budget prioritization, and acknowledged significant staff efforts during a recent historic blizzard.

## Key Discussions
### Public Comment - Fire Department Funding
Jim Pensel of 5002 Aerrol Street spoke critically about the village's approach to funding SAFER. He attended the SAFER Citizen Academy and witnessed understaffing firsthand. He criticized the referendum approach as 'kicking the can down the road' since the $600,000 levy has no sunset date and costs will continue to increase. He called for budget reprioritization, stating 'We have the money. You just need to have the courage to spend it where it actually matters.' Finance Director Jessica responded that the village cannot borrow for additional firefighters, only for capital projects like the Kennedy Park turf, and defended staff efforts noting they are 'the cheapest' and 'most efficient.'

### Speed Limit Ordinance 26-006
The original ordinance to change speed limits including setting Weston Avenue from Von Kennel to Ryan at 35 mph failed 3-4, with Maloney, Jordan, and the presiding officer voting no. Trustee Maloney argued the road section was too sparse for 35 mph compared to other village roads. An amended motion by Maloney, seconded by Jordan, was then passed to keep Von Kennel to Highway J at 45 mph while setting Camp Phillips to Von Kennel at 35 mph, with all other ordinance changes intact.

### Rezoning Ordinances
Two rezoning ordinances were approved unanimously as recommended by planning commission: Ordinance 26-00004 rezoning portion of 8905 Bert Street from RR5 to SFS, and Ordinance 26-00005 rezoning portion of 7105 Christensen Avenue from SL to SFS single family residential small lot.

### Intersection Signage at Community Center Drive and Birch Street
The board approved changing the stop sign to a yield sign with a friendly amendment adding a stop sign for bicyclists coming off the pedestrian bridge. Trustee Hooang raised safety concerns about cyclists coming off the bridge at 15-20 mph with no signal to stop. The amended motion passed unanimously.

### Baseball/Softball Field Maintenance Agreement
A 10-year user agreement was approved unanimously. The committee recommended the longer term because youth organizations are investing money at Kennedy Park and shouldn't risk losing access after one year. The agreement formalizes expectations that have existed informally for over 10 years.

### Remote Meeting Attendance Policy
Trustee Lewis recommended postponing discussion until the next meeting so the newly seated board could make this decision. Motion to defer passed unanimously.

### Microsoft Teams for Communication
Approved unanimously. Starting next term, trustees will use Microsoft Teams instead of text messaging for inter-trustee communication. A training session will be held when the new board convenes.

## Public Comment
Jim Pensel of 5002 Aerrol Street spoke for approximately 4 minutes criticizing the village's approach to funding the fire department. He praised SAFER staff after attending their citizen academy but criticized the referendum approach as inadequate long-term, arguing the board should reprioritize existing budget funds rather than asking taxpayers for additional levies. He specifically mentioned the Kennedy Park turf project and aquatic center as 'wants' versus fire and police as 'needs.'
