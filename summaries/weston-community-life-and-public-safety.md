## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Community Life and Public Safety Committee met on May 4th to address several ordinance changes and enforcement matters. Key actions included recommending approval of nuisance ordinance amendments, an e-bike/e-moto ordinance with restrictions on emoto roadway use, mobile food vendor fire inspection requirements, address corrections, and a raze order for a condemned property at 4104 Shore Avenue.

## Key Discussions
### Department Reports
Code Enforcement Officer Audrey reported that 3020 Mountain View Avenue will come off the enforcement roll after property sale and new owner submitted repair permits. Police Chief Hunt reported 2,394 calls in March (77 per day), 410 traffic stops, and 281 citations. The department has hired its 40th officer, with the 38th starting last week, 39th in May, and 40th around January. Two SROs work with zoning during summer for all three municipalities. Motion to acknowledge reports 5, 6, and 7 passed unanimously.

### Chapter 50 Nuisance Amendments
Committee reviewed final copy of nuisance ordinance amendments previously discussed, including tree limb height requirements of 14 and 10 feet over streets and walkways. Motion by Olsen, seconded by Campkey, to recommend approval to the Board of Trustees passed unanimously.

### E-bicycle Ordinance Chapter 82
Extensive discussion on regulating e-bikes and emotos (electric motorcycles). Chief Hunt strongly advocated prohibiting emotos from roadways and sidewalks, comparing them to unlicensed dirt bikes. Committee discussed concerns about electric scooters used by children but decided to rely on officer discretion rather than specific exemptions. Motion by Olsen, seconded by Chwanka, to recommend approval with emoto prohibition on roadways, sidewalks, trails, and public property passed unanimously.

### Overnight Parking Regulations
Trustee Tolson presented research showing Weston's 12-hour overnight parking restriction (4am-4pm) is significantly longer than surrounding municipalities (typically 4-5 hours). Public Works Director Michael suggested eliminating the overnight parking restriction entirely and instead signing specific problem areas. Chief Hunt opposed removal, preferring uniform overnight restrictions to clear cars before plowing. Committee decided to table the item until July for Michael to identify specific areas needing restrictions.

### Crosswalk Request on Alderson Street
Mount Olive Church requested a crosswalk between the church and DC Everest High School parking lot. Committee discussed costs ($2,000-3,000 for basic crosswalk, $25,000-30,000 for lighted system). Chief Hunt noted no incidents in the area. Motion by Olsen, seconded by Chwanka, to recommend board approval contingent on Mount Olive covering installation costs passed unanimously.

### Mobile Food Vendor Fire Inspections
Fire department presented a regional inspection program with Wisconsin Rapids, Marshfield, Stevens Point, Plover, Rib Mountain, and SAFER. One inspection sticker is valid across all participating municipalities. Fire Marshal Marty already conducted an inspection that failed due to improper LP piping and unsecured propane containers. Motion by Chwanka, seconded by Campkey, to approve Ordinance 26-8 passed unanimously.

### Address Corrections for E911 Compliance
Staff presented three addressing errors: 5915 Delicowski Street (duplex needing split to 5915/5917), 3917 Ross Avenue (out of sequence with nearby apartment building), and 2211 Radkey Avenue (new duplex on private drive). Chief Hunt added Weston Villa Apartments on Delicowski has eight separate addresses for one structure. Motion to recommend approval passed unanimously.

### Raze Order for 4104 Shore Avenue
Property has been abandoned since 2010-2011 and posted since 2021. Previous attempt to use structure for fire training was rejected as unsafe. Owners are current on taxes and have indicated preference for village to proceed with demolition. Costs will be added to tax roll. Motion by Olsen, seconded by Campkey, to recommend board authorization for raze order passed unanimously.

## Public Comment
Jim Senalt spoke regarding multiple agenda items: criticized the new farmers market location at the park predicting grass destruction from vehicle parking; noted potential code violation with parking on grass; questioned ADA compliance of handicap spaces on grass versus firm surfaces; asked about the origin of food truck fire inspections; raised concerns about mobile food vendor regulations on public property versus private property; and criticized the age of some code enforcement cases requesting better accountability reporting on long-standing violations.
