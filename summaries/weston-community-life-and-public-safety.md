## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Community Life and Public Safety Committee advanced several ordinances and addressed code enforcement matters at this May 4th meeting. Key actions included recommending approval of nuisance code amendments, an e-bike ordinance with restrictions on emotos, mobile food vendor fire inspection requirements, and a raze order for a condemned property on Shore Avenue. The committee also discussed but deferred action on overnight parking regulations.

## Key Discussions
### Department Reports
Code Enforcement Officer Roman reported that 3020 Mountain View Avenue will be removed from the code enforcement roll after the property sale was completed and new owner submitted repair permits. Police Chief Hunt reported 2,394 calls in March (77 per day), 410 traffic stops, and 281 citations issued. The department has hired its 40th officer, with the 38th starting last week, 39th in May, and 40th around January. Two SROs work with zoning violations during summer for Weston, Rothschild, and Schofield. Reports were acknowledged unanimously on motion by Sherwanka, seconded by Olsen.

### Chapter 50 Nuisance Amendments
The committee reviewed final amendments to Chapter 50, including previously discussed changes to tree limb height requirements (14 and 10 feet over streets and walkways) with language that properties must comply with jurisdiction having authority if different. Motion to recommend approval to the board of trustees made by Olsen, seconded by Campkey. Passed unanimously.

### E-Bicycle Ordinance
Extensive discussion occurred regarding the model ordinance for e-bikes. Chief Hunt strongly recommended prohibiting emotos (electric dirt bike-type vehicles) on roadways, sidewalks, and trails, citing safety concerns about unlicensed vehicles without lights or turn signals. Committee member Mike raised concerns about electric scooters used by middle school children. The committee agreed to add emoto restrictions to roadways while leaving officer discretion for children's play scooters. Rothschild has not yet approved the ordinance; Schofield declined as unnecessary. Motion by Olsen to recommend approval with emoto prohibition on roadways, sidewalks, trails, and public property, seconded by Sherwanka. Passed unanimously.

### Overnight Parking Regulations
Trustee Tolson presented research showing Weston's 12-hour parking restriction (4am-4pm) is significantly longer than surrounding municipalities (most have 5-hour windows from approximately 2am-7am). Michael from Public Works noted plowing rarely occurs before 4-5am and suggested eliminating overnight parking restrictions while signing 6-8 specific problem areas. Chief Hunt preferred keeping overnight restrictions for officer enforcement time. Committee deferred action, directing Michael to identify specific exception areas and return in July with recommendations.

### Crosswalk Request on Alderson Street
Mount Olive Church requested a crosswalk between the church and DC Everest High School parking lot where parishioners park during services. Michael estimated a basic crosswalk would cost approximately $2,000, while a lighted system would cost $25,000-30,000. Committee agreed they would not pay for it but would approve installation if Mount Olive covers costs. Motion by Olsen that if Mount Olive agrees to cover costs, the committee recommends board approval. Seconded by Sherwanka. Passed unanimously. Michael will contact Mount Olive.

### Mobile Food Vendor Fire Inspections
Fire Chief explained a new regional program where Wisconsin Rapids, Marshfield, Stevens Point, Plover, Rib Mountain, and Weston fire departments can inspect food trucks with a sticker valid across all participating municipalities. Wausau opted out to do their own inspections. Fire Marshal Marty already inspected one truck that failed due to improper LP piping (galvanized steel) and unsecured propane cylinders. Motion by Sherwanka to approve Ordinance 26-8, seconded by Campkey. Passed unanimously.

### Address Corrections for E911 Compliance
Staff presented three addressing corrections: 5915 Delicoski Street (duplex needing separate addresses), Ross Avenue properties out of sequence near an apartment building, and 2211 Radkey Avenue duplex on a private drive. Chief Hunt noted an additional issue with Weston Villa Apartments having eight separate addresses for one structure. Motion made and seconded by Campkey. Passed unanimously.

### Raze Order for 4104 Shore Avenue
Staff requested authorization for a raze order on a structure abandoned since 2010-2011, posted since 2021. A fire department training burn was previously attempted but the structure was deemed too unsafe. Current owners are up to date on taxes and prefer the village proceed with demolition. Costs will be added to the tax roll. Motion by Olsen, seconded by Campkey. Passed unanimously.

## Public Comment
Jim Senalt spoke regarding several agenda items. He criticized the new farmers market location at a park, predicting the grass will be destroyed by June and noting that parking on grass appears to violate village code. He recommended relocating ADA parking spots from grass to adjacent asphalt for wheelchair accessibility. He questioned the new fire inspection requirement for food trucks, asking where it originated since no trucks have burned down. He raised concerns about mobile food vendor regulations on public property and interpretation of farmers market days. Finally, he criticized lack of accountability on long-standing code violations, noting some cases are very old with no visible progress.
