## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Community Life and Public Safety Committee met on May 4th, taking action on several ordinance amendments and property matters. Key decisions included recommending approval of nuisance code amendments, an e-bike ordinance with added e-moto restrictions, mobile food vendor fire inspection requirements, address corrections, and a raze order for an abandoned structure on Shore Avenue.

## Key Discussions
### Public Comments
Jim Senalt spoke critically about the new farmers market location at a park, predicting the grass will be destroyed by vehicles parking on it. He questioned whether current village code allows parking on grass and raised concerns about ADA-compliant handicap parking being placed on grass rather than asphalt. He also questioned the new fire inspection requirement for food trucks, asking where it came from, and criticized the age of some code violation cases on the enforcement list.

### Department Reports
Staff reported that 3020 Mountain View Avenue will be removed from the code enforcement list after the property sold at auction and the new owner submitted repair permits. Chief Hunt reported the police department had 2,394 calls in March (77 per day), 410 traffic stops, and 281 citations. The department has hired its 40th officer, with the 38th starting last week, 39th in May, and 40th around January. Two SROs work with zoning violations during summer. Motion to acknowledge reports 5, 6, and 7 passed unanimously.

### Chapter 50 Nuisance Amendments
The committee reviewed final amendments including switching tree limb clearance requirements to 14 and 10 feet over streets and walkways. Motion by Olsen, seconded by Campkey, to recommend approval to the Board of Trustees passed unanimously.

### E-Bicycle Ordinance
Committee discussed the model e-bike ordinance for Chapter 82. Chief Hunt strongly advocated for prohibiting e-motos (electric dirt bikes) from roadways and sidewalks, comparing them to unlicensed motorcycles that pose safety hazards. Discussion ensued about electric scooters used by middle schoolers, with the committee deciding to leave officer discretion for kids' play scooters. Motion by Olsen, seconded by Chwanka, to recommend approval with the addition of e-motos being prohibited on roadways, sidewalks, trails, and public property passed unanimously.

### Overnight Parking Regulations
Trustee Tolson presented research showing Weston's 12-hour parking restriction (4am-4pm) is far outside norms of surrounding municipalities, most having 5-hour windows. Michael (DPW) suggested eliminating overnight parking restrictions and instead signing the 6-8 problem areas, mostly near apartment complexes. Chief Hunt preferred keeping overnight restrictions to give officers time to clear vehicles before plowing. The committee decided to table the item until July for Michael to identify specific exception areas.

### Alderson Street Crosswalk Request
Mount Olive Church requested a crosswalk between the church and DC Everest parking area. Committee members noted no accidents have occurred and questioned costs. Michael estimated $2,000-3,000 for basic crosswalk, $25,000-30,000 for lighted system. Motion by Olsen, seconded by Chwanka, to recommend board approval contingent on Mount Olive covering installation costs passed unanimously.

### Mobile Food Vendors Fire Inspection
Chief Hunt explained a regional partnership between fire departments (Wisconsin Rapids, Marshfield, Stevens Point, Plover, Rib Mountain, and Safer) for food truck inspections. One inspection with a sticker covers all participating municipalities. The city of Wausau opted out to do their own. Fire Marshal Marty recently failed one truck with galvanized steel LP piping and unsecured propane tanks. Motion by Chwanka, seconded by Campkey, to approve Ordinance 26-8 passed unanimously.

### Address Corrections
Staff presented three addressing errors: 5915 Delicoski Street needs to become 5915/5917 for a duplex; Ross Avenue addresses need resequencing due to a discovered apartment unit; and 2211 Radkey Avenue needs correction for a new duplex on a private drive. Motion to recommend approval passed unanimously.

### Raze Order for 4104 Shore Avenue
Staff requested authorization for a raze order on an abandoned structure. The property has been abandoned since 2010-2011, with the structure previously used as a chicken coop. A fire department training burn was deemed unsafe due to building condition. Current owners prefer the village proceed with demolition; costs will be placed on tax roll. Motion by Olsen, seconded by Campkey, passed unanimously.

## Public Comment
Jim Senalt spoke during public comment, criticizing the new farmers market location execution, questioning ADA compliance of handicap parking on grass, asking about the origin of new food truck fire inspections, and expressing concern about the age of pending code violation cases.
