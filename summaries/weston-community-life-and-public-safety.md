## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Community Life and Public Safety Committee approved amendments to the nuisance code, recommended an e-bike ordinance prohibiting emotos on roadways/sidewalks, authorized fire inspections for mobile food vendors, and approved a raze order for a deteriorated structure. The committee also discussed overnight parking regulations and a crosswalk request, deferring both items for further review.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of minutes
Motion to approve minutes was made by Olsen, seconded by Chwanka. Motion passed unanimously.

### Department reports acknowledgment
Staff reported that 3020 Mountain View Avenue will be removed from the code enforcement roll after property sale and permit submission. Police Chief Hunt reported 2,394 calls in March (77 per day), 410 traffic stops, and 281 citations. The department has hired its 40th officer, with the 38th starting last week, 39th in May, and 40th around January. Two SROs work with zoning during summer for violations and pet licensing. Motion to acknowledge reports 5, 6, and 7 made by Chwanka, seconded by Olsen, passed unanimously.

### Proposed amendments to Chapter 50 nuisances
Committee reviewed final version of nuisance code amendments, including tree limb heights over streets and walkways (14 and 10 feet). Motion to recommend approval to the Board of Trustees made by Olsen, seconded by Campkey. Motion passed unanimously.

### E-bicycle ordinance amendments
Extensive discussion on emotos (electric dirt bike-style vehicles). Chief Hunt strongly recommended prohibiting emotos on roadways and sidewalks due to safety concerns, comparing them to unlicensed dirt bikes. Committee debated whether to restrict electric scooters used by children. Chief Hunt noted emotos are not yet covered by state statute. Motion to recommend approval with addition prohibiting emotos on roadways, sidewalks, trails, and public property made by Olsen, seconded by Chwanka. Motion passed unanimously.

### Overnight parking regulations
Trustee Tolson presented research showing Weston's 12-hour parking restriction (4 AM to 4 PM) is far outside other municipalities' 5-hour windows. DPW Director Michael Wodalski suggested eliminating village-wide overnight parking restrictions and instead signing 6-8 specific problem areas. Chief Hunt opposed eliminating overnight parking, noting it gives officers time to clear cars before plowing. Committee deferred action, asking Wodalski to identify specific exception areas for the July meeting.

### Crosswalk request on Alderson Street
Mount Olive Church requested a crosswalk between the church and DC Everest High School parking lot. DPW estimated $2,000-$3,000 for basic crosswalk, $25,000-$30,000 for lighted system. Committee members expressed reluctance to pay for private benefit. Motion made by Olsen to recommend approval only if Mount Olive covers the cost, seconded by Chwanka. Motion passed unanimously. Staff will contact Mount Olive about cost-sharing.

### Mobile food vendors fire inspection ordinance
Fire Chief explained new regional fire inspection program with Wisconsin Rapids, Marshfield, Stevens Point, Plover, Rib Mountain, and Weston (Wausau opted out). Food trucks inspected once get sticker valid for all participating municipalities. Fire Marshal Marty already conducted one inspection that failed due to improper LP piping and unsecured propane cylinders. Motion to approve Ordinance 26-8 made by Chwanka, seconded by Campkey. Motion passed unanimously.

### Address corrections for E911 compliance
Staff presented three addressing errors: 5915 Delicoski Street (duplex needing second address), 3917 Ross Avenue (out of sequence with apartment), and 2211 Radkey Avenue (new duplex on private drive). Chief Hunt noted additional addressing issue at Weston Villa Apartments on Delicoski. Motion to recommend approval made, seconded by Campkey. Motion passed unanimously.

### Raze request for 4104 Shore Avenue
Structure abandoned since 2010-2011, on enforcement list since 2021. Fire department previously declined training burn due to unsafe conditions. Owners discovered housing chickens inside in previous years. Property currently for sale. Demolition costs will be added to tax roll. Motion to recommend Board authorize raze order made by Olsen, seconded by Campkey. Motion passed unanimously.

## Public Comment
Jim Senalt spoke regarding multiple agenda items. He criticized the new farmers market location at the park, predicting grass destruction from vehicle parking and questioning ADA compliance of handicap spaces on grass rather than asphalt. He questioned the origin of new fire inspection requirements for food trucks. He raised concerns about food truck regulations on public property and unclear farmers market day definitions. He also criticized the age of some code enforcement cases, stating some have been on the list for years with no visible progress on at least three he personally knows about.
