## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Community Life and Public Safety Committee met on May 4th and recommended approval of amendments to nuisance ordinances, an e-bike ordinance prohibiting e-motos on roadways, mobile food vendor fire inspections, address corrections, and a raze order for an abandoned structure. The committee also discussed overnight parking regulations and a crosswalk request, deferring both for further information.

## Key Discussions
### Public Comments
Jim Senalt spoke regarding concerns about the new farmers market location at a park, stating vehicles parking on grass violates village code and ADA-compliant handicap spaces should be on asphalt rather than grass. He questioned the need for new fire inspections on food trucks and expressed concern that some code enforcement cases have been pending for years without visible progress.

### Department Reports
Audrey reported that 3020 Mountain View Avenue will be removed from the code enforcement roll after property sale and permit submission for repairs. Chief Hunt noted the police department had 2,394 calls in March (77 per day) and 410 traffic stops with 281 citations. The department has hired its 40th officer, with new officers starting in May and January. Two SROs are assigned to zoning violations during summer. Motion to acknowledge reports 5, 6, and 7 made by Chwanka, seconded by Olsen, passed unanimously.

### Chapter 50 Nuisances Amendments
The committee reviewed final amendments including tree limb height requirements over streets and walkways (14 and 10 feet). Motion to recommend approval to the Board of Trustees made by Olsen, seconded by Campkey, passed unanimously.

### E-Bicycle Ordinance
Extensive discussion occurred about e-motos, which Chief Hunt recommended prohibiting on roadways and sidewalks due to safety concerns, comparing them to unlicensed dirt bikes. Committee debated whether to include electric scooters in restrictions. Chief Hunt suggested officers could use discretion for children's play scooters. Motion to recommend approval with amendment prohibiting e-motos on roadways, sidewalks, trails, and public property made by Olsen, seconded by Chwanka, passed unanimously.

### Overnight Parking Regulations
Trustee Brent Tolson presented research showing Weston's 12-hour parking restriction (4 AM to 4 PM) is significantly longer than surrounding municipalities (most have 5-hour windows). Michael from BPW explained daytime restrictions help plowing operations but acknowledged only 6-8 problem areas exist. Chief Hunt preferred overnight restrictions to give officers time to clear vehicles before morning plowing. Committee decided to defer the item until July to allow Michael to identify specific problem areas for targeted signage.

### Alderson Street Crosswalk Request
Mount Olive Church requested a crosswalk to connect to DC Everest parking lot they use on Sundays. Committee discussed costs ($2,000-$3,000 for basic crosswalk, $25,000-$30,000 for lighted system). Chief Hunt had no safety concerns as no incidents have occurred. Motion to recommend board approval contingent on Mount Olive covering installation costs made by Olsen, seconded by Chwanka, passed unanimously.

### Mobile Food Vendor Fire Inspections
Chief Hunt explained a regional partnership with Wisconsin Rapids, Marshfield, Stevens Point, Plover, Rib Mountain, and Safer for mutual food truck fire inspections. A sticker system allows vendors inspected by any participating department to operate in all jurisdictions. A recent inspection failed a vendor due to improper LP piping. Motion to approve Ordinance 26-8 made by Chwanka, seconded by Campkey, passed unanimously.

### Address Corrections
Planning staff presented three address corrections for E911 compliance: 5915 Delicoski Street (duplex needing two addresses), Ross Avenue properties out of sequence, and 2211 Radkey Avenue. Chief Hunt mentioned an additional issue at Weston Villa Apartments with eight separate addresses for one building. Motion to recommend approval made and seconded by Campkey, passed unanimously.

### Raze Order for 4104 Shore Avenue
Staff requested authorization for raze order on an abandoned structure (abandoned since 2010-2011). A previous fire department training burn was cancelled due to unsafe conditions. The owners are current on taxes and prefer demolition. Costs will be placed on the tax roll. Motion to recommend board authorization made by Olsen, seconded by Campkey, passed unanimously.

## Public Comment
Jim Senalt spoke regarding concerns about the new farmers market location, questioning parking on grass as a code violation, ADA compliance for handicap spaces, the need for food truck fire inspections, and lack of progress on long-standing code enforcement cases.
