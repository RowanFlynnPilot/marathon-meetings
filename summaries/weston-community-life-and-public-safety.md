## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Community Life and Public Safety Committee met on May 4th, recommending approval of nuisance code amendments, an e-bike ordinance with restrictions on e-motos, mobile food vendor fire inspection requirements, address corrections, and a raze order for an abandoned property. The committee also discussed parking regulations and a crosswalk request, deferring both items for further staff research.

## Key Discussions
### Public Comments
Jim Senalt spoke regarding several agenda items. He criticized the new farmers market location, predicting the park's grassy areas will be destroyed by vehicles parking on grass. He noted potential ADA compliance issues with handicap parking on grass rather than asphalt, questioned the need for new fire inspections on food trucks, raised concerns about mobile food vendor regulations on public property, and criticized the age of some code violation cases on the report, suggesting better accountability and progress updates are needed.

### Approval of Minutes
Motion to approve minutes was made by Olsen and seconded by Chwanka. Motion passed unanimously.

### Department Reports
Audrey reported that 3020 Mountain View Avenue will be removed from the code enforcement roll after the property sale was completed and the new owner submitted repair permits. Chief Hunt reported the police department had 2,394 calls in March (77 per day), 410 traffic stops, and 281 citations issued. The department has hired their 40th officer. Two SROs work with code enforcement during summer for zoning violations and pet licensing. Motion to acknowledge reports 5, 6, and 7 was made by Chwanka and passed unanimously.

### Proposed Amendments to Chapter 50 Nuisances
The committee reviewed final amendments including changes to tree limb height requirements over streets and walkways (14 and 10 feet) and language requiring compliance with jurisdictions that have authority over specific areas. Motion to recommend approval to the board of trustees was made by Olsen, seconded by Campkey, and passed unanimously.

### E-Bicycle Ordinance Chapter 82
Extensive discussion focused on e-motos, which Chief Hunt recommended prohibiting from roadways, sidewalks, and trails due to safety concerns, comparing them to unlicensed dirt bikes. The committee debated whether to also restrict electric scooters used by children but decided to leave officer discretion for those situations. Motion to recommend approval with the addition that e-motos are prohibited from roadways, sidewalks, trails, and any public property in the Village of Weston was made by Olsen, seconded by Chwanka, and passed unanimously.

### Overnight Parking Regulations
Trustee Tolson presented research showing Weston's 12-hour parking restriction window (4 AM to 4 PM) is significantly longer than surrounding municipalities, which typically have 4-5 hour windows. Public Works Director Mike Bartel suggested either eliminating the regulation entirely and signing only the 6-8 problem areas, or matching Rothschild's approach. Chief Hunt advocated keeping overnight restrictions to allow officers time to clear vehicles before plows arrive. The committee deferred action, directing Bartel to identify specific problem areas and return with a proposal in July or August.

### Crosswalk Request on Alderson Street
Mount Olive Church requested a crosswalk between the church and DC Everest High School administrative parking lot. Staff estimated a basic crosswalk would cost approximately $2,000-$3,000, while a lighted system would cost $25,000-$30,000. Chief Hunt reported no known safety incidents in the area. The committee agreed to approve the crosswalk only if Mount Olive covers the installation costs. Motion to recommend board approval contingent on Mount Olive paying for the crosswalk was made by Olsen, seconded by Chwanka, and passed unanimously.

### Mobile Food Vendor Fire Inspection Ordinance
Fire Chief explained that this ordinance establishes a regional fire inspection program with Marshfield, Stevens Point, Wisconsin Rapids, Plover, and Rib Mountain. A food truck inspected by any participating department receives a sticker valid for all municipalities, eliminating redundant inspections. Wausau declined to participate. Fire Marshal Marty already conducted one inspection that failed due to improper LP piping and unsecured propane containers. Motion to approve Ordinance 26-8 was made by Chwanka, seconded by Campkey, and passed unanimously.

### Address Corrections for E911 Compliance
Staff presented three addressing errors: 5915 Delicoski Street (duplex needing separate addresses), Ross Avenue properties out of sequence, and 2211 Radkey Avenue needing correction for a new duplex on a private drive. Chief Hunt added another issue at Weston Villa Apartments on Delicoski with eight apartments having separate addresses plus a utility room address. Motion to recommend approval to the board was made and seconded by Campkey, passing unanimously.

### Raze Order for 4104 Shore Avenue
Staff requested authorization to proceed with a raze order for an abandoned structure that has been posted since 2021 and abandoned since approximately 2010. A planned fire training burn was cancelled due to unsafe structural conditions. The property owners are current on taxes and have agreed to the demolition. Demolition costs will be added to the tax roll. Motion to recommend board authorization was made by Olsen, seconded by Campkey, and passed unanimously.

## Public Comment
Jim Senalt spoke during public comment, criticizing the new farmers market location and predicting damage to park grass from vehicle parking. He raised concerns about ADA compliance for handicap parking on grass, questioned the necessity of new fire inspections for food trucks, noted ambiguity in mobile food vendor regulations on public property, and criticized the lack of visible progress on older code enforcement cases.
