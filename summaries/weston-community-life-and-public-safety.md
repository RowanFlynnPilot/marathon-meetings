## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Community Life and Public Safety Committee approved amendments to nuisance ordinances, recommended an e-bike ordinance with restrictions on emotos, and authorized the razing of an abandoned structure at 4104 Shore Avenue. The committee also discussed overnight parking regulations and a crosswalk request, deferring both items for further staff work.

## Key Discussions
### Public comments
Jim Senalt addressed the committee with concerns about the new farmers market location, stating it was a 'terrible execution' and predicting the park's grassy areas would be destroyed. He questioned ADA-compliant handicap parking placement on grass rather than asphalt, asked about the origin of new fire inspection requirements for food trucks, and criticized the age of some code violations that have been pending for years without visible progress.

### Minutes and department reports
Minutes from the previous meeting were approved unanimously on a motion by Olsen, seconded by Shawanka. The committee acknowledged reports from Code Enforcement, Fire/EMS, and Police. Staff noted 3020 Mountain View Avenue will come off the code enforcement roll after sale and permits filed. Chief Hunt reported 2,394 police calls in March and announced the department has hired its 40th officer. Motion to acknowledge reports passed unanimously.

### Chapter 50 nuisance amendments
The committee reviewed final amendments to Chapter 50, including previously discussed changes to tree limb height requirements over streets and walkways (14 and 10 feet specifications were switched). Tolson confirmed no additional changes were needed. Motion by Olsen, seconded by Campkey, to recommend approval to the Board of Trustees passed unanimously.

### E-bike ordinance amendments
Extensive discussion occurred regarding emotos (electric mini dirt bikes). Chief Hunt strongly advocated prohibiting emotos from roadways and sidewalks, comparing them to unlicensed dirt bikes and citing safety concerns including wheelies past pedestrians. The committee debated whether to also restrict electric scooters, ultimately deciding to leave officer discretion for low-speed children's scooters. Motion by Olsen, seconded by Shawanka, to recommend approval with emotos prohibited from roadways, sidewalks, trails, and public property passed unanimously.

### Overnight parking regulations
Tolson initiated discussion noting Weston's 12-hour parking restriction (4am-4pm) is significantly longer than surrounding municipalities (typically 5-hour windows). Public Works Director Michael stated plowing rarely occurs before 4-5am and suggested eliminating the overnight restriction except for signing 6-8 problem areas. Chief Hunt opposed eliminating overnight parking, preferring uniformity with other municipalities to give officers time to clear vehicles before plows arrive. The committee decided to defer the item until July, with Michael to prepare a list of specific problem areas requiring signed restrictions.

### Crosswalk request on Alderson Street
Mount Olive Church requested a crosswalk between the church and DC Everest school parking lot. Committee members questioned necessity given no reported accidents and existing nearby crossings. Staff estimated a basic crosswalk would cost approximately $2,000, while a lighted system would be $25,000-30,000. Motion by Olsen, seconded by Shawanka, to recommend board approval contingent on Mount Olive covering installation costs passed unanimously. Michael will contact the church to determine willingness to pay.

### Mobile food vendors fire inspection
Chief Hunt presented a regional fire inspection program involving Stevens Point, Marshfield, Wisconsin Rapids, Plover, Rib Mountain, and SAFER. One inspection and sticker would be valid across all participating municipalities. Fire Inspector Marty already failed one vendor due to improper galvanized steel LP piping and unsecured propane tanks. Wausau opted out to maintain their own inspection program. Motion by Shawanka, seconded by Campkey, to approve Ordinance 26-8 passed unanimously.

### Address corrections for E911 compliance
Staff presented three address corrections needed for E911 compliance: 5915 Delicowski Street (duplex needing separate addresses), Ross Avenue properties out of sequence, and 2211 Radkey Avenue for a new duplex on a private drive. Chief Hunt also identified Weston Villa Apartments on Delicowski as needing review (eight apartments with eight separate addresses plus a utility room address). Motion passed unanimously to recommend board approval.

### Raze request for 4104 Shore Avenue
Staff requested authorization to begin the raze order process for an abandoned structure. The property has been abandoned since 2010-2011 and was previously considered for a fire department training burn but deemed too unsafe. Owners had housed chickens inside the structure. Property taxes are currently paid; demolition costs will be added to the tax roll if unpaid. Motion by Olsen, seconded by Campkey, to recommend board authorization passed unanimously.

## Public Comment
Jim Senalt spoke during public comment, criticizing the new farmers market location as poorly executed and predicting damage to park grass areas. He questioned handicap parking placement on grass rather than asphalt for ADA compliance, asked about the rationale for new fire inspections on food trucks, raised concerns about food truck regulations on public vs private property, and criticized the age of some pending code violations.
