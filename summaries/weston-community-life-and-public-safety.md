## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Community Life and Public Safety Committee approved amendments to nuisance ordinances, recommended an e-bike ordinance with restrictions on emotos, and authorized demolition proceedings for an abandoned property. The committee also discussed overnight parking regulations and a crosswalk request, deferring both items for further research.

## Key Discussions
### Department Reports
Code Enforcement Officer Audrey reported that 3020 Mountain View Avenue will be removed from the code enforcement list after the property sold at auction and the new owner submitted repair permits. Chief Hunt noted the police department had 2,394 calls in March (77 per day), issued 281 citations, and made 410 traffic stops. The department has hired its 40th officer. Two SROs work summer zoning violations for Weston, Rothschild, and Schofield. Reports were acknowledged unanimously.

### Chapter 50 Nuisances Amendments
The committee reviewed final amendments including tree limb height requirements of 14 feet over streets and 10 feet over walkways. Motion by Olsen, seconded by Campkey, to recommend approval to the Board of Trustees. Passed unanimously.

### E-Bicycle Ordinance
Committee discussed the model e-bike ordinance with significant debate about emotos (electric dirt bikes). Chief Hunt strongly advocated prohibiting emotos on roadways and sidewalks, comparing them to unlicensed dirt bikes. Discussion included whether electric scooters should be exempt for children. Motion by Olsen, seconded by Chwanka, to recommend approval with amendment prohibiting emotos on roadways, sidewalks, trails, and public property. Passed unanimously. Rothschild and other municipalities are also considering adoption; Schofield declined; Wausau is making adjustments to their existing ordinance.

### Overnight Parking Regulations
Trustee Tolson presented research showing Weston's 12-hour no-parking window (4am-4pm) is significantly longer than surrounding municipalities (most have 4-5 hour windows). Public Works Director Michael Wodalski suggested eliminating overnight parking restrictions entirely and signing only 6-8 problem areas. Chief Hunt opposed elimination, preferring overnight restrictions to clear roads before plows arrive. Committee deferred action until July, directing staff to identify specific problem areas for targeted signage.

### Crosswalk Request on Alderson Street
Mount Olive Church requested a crosswalk connecting their parking to DC Everest High School administrative lot across Alderson Street. Committee discussed whether village should bear cost ($2,000-3,000 for basic crosswalk, $25,000-30,000 for lighted system). Motion by Olsen, seconded by Chwanka, to recommend approval contingent on Mount Olive covering installation costs. Passed unanimously. Staff will contact the church about cost-sharing.

### Mobile Food Vendors Fire Inspection
Fire Chief explained new regional fire inspection program with Wisconsin Rapids, Marshfield, Stevens Point, Plover, Rib Mountain, and Weston. Food trucks inspected by any participating department receive stickers valid in all jurisdictions. Fire Marshal Marty already conducted one inspection that failed due to improper LP piping. Wausau opted out to do their own inspections. Motion by Chwanka, seconded by Campkey, to approve Ordinance 26-8. Passed unanimously.

### Address Corrections for E911 Compliance
Staff presented three addressing errors: 5915 Delicoski Street (duplex needing second address), properties on Ross Avenue out of sequence, and 2211 Radkey Avenue near new duplex development. Chief Hunt noted additional issue at Weston Villa Apartments where each unit has separate address. Motion passed unanimously to recommend address corrections to the Board.

### Raze Order for 4104 Shore Avenue
Staff requested authorization to proceed with demolition of abandoned structure vacant since 2010. Fire department previously declined training burn due to unsafe conditions. Current owners previously housed chickens in the structure. Owners prefer demolition with costs added to tax roll. Motion by Olsen, seconded by Campkey, to authorize raze order. Passed unanimously.

## Public Comment
Jim Senalt spoke regarding multiple agenda items: criticized the new farmers market location at Kennedy Park, predicting grass damage from vehicle parking; questioned ADA compliance for handicap parking on grass rather than asphalt; asked why fire inspections were being added for mobile food vendors; noted the new public property location changes food truck permitting dynamics; and expressed concern about old code violations on the enforcement list that appear to have no visible progress after years.
