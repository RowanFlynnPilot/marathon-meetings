## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Community Life and Public Safety Committee recommended approval of nuisance code amendments and an e-bike ordinance (with emoto restrictions added), discussed but tabled overnight parking regulation changes until July, and authorized demolition proceedings for a condemned property. The committee also approved fire inspection requirements for mobile food vendors and addressed several property addressing corrections.

## Key Discussions
### Public Comments
Jim Senalt spoke extensively about concerns with the new farmers market location, stating it was 'a terrible execution' and predicting the park grass would be destroyed by summer. He raised concerns about vehicles parking on grass potentially violating village code, ADA-compliant handicap parking being on grass instead of asphalt, questioned the origin of new fire inspections for food trucks, and criticized the age of some code enforcement cases, noting some have been pending for years with no visible progress.

### Minutes and Department Reports
Minutes were approved unanimously with motion by Olsen and second by Shawanka. Code Enforcement reported that 3020 Mountain View Avenue will be removed from the enforcement roll after the property sold at auction and the new owner submitted repair permits. Police Chief Hunt reported 2,394 calls for March (77 per day), 410 traffic stops, and 281 citations. The department has hired its 40th officer, with the 38th starting last week, 39th in May, and 40th around January. Two SROs work with zoning during summer handling violations and pet licensing. Reports were acknowledged unanimously.

### Chapter 50 Nuisance Amendments
The committee reviewed final amendments including the tree limb height requirements (14 and 10 feet) that were switched in text per previous meeting direction, plus language about compliance with jurisdiction authority. Motion to recommend approval to the board of trustees was made by Olsen, seconded by Campey, and passed unanimously.

### E-bike Ordinance
Significant discussion focused on emotos (electric dirt bike-style vehicles). Chief Hunt strongly advocated prohibiting emotos on roadways and sidewalks, comparing them to unlicensed dirt bikes. Committee member Mike Compi raised concerns about electric scooters being included in the emoto definition, noting many middle schoolers use them. The committee decided to leave officer discretion for kid scooters. Motion by Olsen to recommend approval with the addition prohibiting emotos on roadways, sidewalks, trails, and public property was seconded by Shawanka and passed unanimously.

### Overnight Parking Regulations
Trustee Tolson presented research showing Weston's 12-hour no-parking window (4am-4pm) is far outside the norm compared to surrounding municipalities which typically use 4-5 hour overnight windows. Public Works Director Michael noted they rarely plow before 4-5am and residential streets aren't reached until 7-8am. Chief Hunt opposed eliminating overnight parking, preferring uniformity with other municipalities. The committee decided to table the item until July, directing Michael to identify 6-8 problem areas that could be specifically signed for alternating-side parking.

### Crosswalk Request on Alderson Street
Mount Olive Church requested a crosswalk between their property and DC Everest school parking lot. Staff estimated $2,000-3,000 for a basic crosswalk, $25,000-30,000 for a lighted system. Committee members expressed reluctance to set precedent of paying for every crosswalk request. Motion by Olsen, seconded by Shawanka, passed unanimously: if Mount Olive is willing to cover the installation cost, the board should approve it. Michael will contact Mount Olive to determine their willingness.

### Mobile Food Vendors Fire Inspection
Fire department presented a regional fire inspection program with Wisconsin Rapids, Marshfield, Stevens Point, Plover, Rib Mountain, and SAFER. One inspection would be valid across all participating municipalities. Fire Marshal Marty already inspected one truck that failed due to improper LP piping and unsecured propane cylinders. Wausau opted out to do their own inspections. Motion by Shawanka, seconded by Campey, to approve ordinance 26-8 passed unanimously.

### Address Corrections
Staff presented three addressing corrections: 5915 Delicowski Street (duplex needing split address, owners agreed), Ross Avenue properties out of sequence due to discovered apartment unit, and 2211 Radkey Avenue private drive with out-of-sequence apartment complex. Chief Hunt added another issue at Weston Villa Apartments on Delicowski where an 8-plex has separate addresses for each unit plus a utility room. Motion passed unanimously to recommend approval to the board.

### Raze Request for 4104 Shore Avenue
Property has been abandoned since 2010-2011 and posted for demolition since 2021. A planned fire training burn was cancelled because the structure was too deteriorated for safe training. Staff discovered chickens being housed inside the structure. Current owners are up to date on taxes and preferred the village proceed with demolition, with costs added to their tax roll. Motion by Olsen, seconded by Campey, passed unanimously to authorize demolition proceedings.

## Public Comment
Jim Senalt spoke during public comment, criticizing the new farmers market location execution and predicting damage to park grass. He raised concerns about grass parking violating village code, ADA compliance for handicap spaces on grass rather than asphalt, questioned the need for new food truck fire inspections, and criticized the lack of progress on long-standing code enforcement cases.
