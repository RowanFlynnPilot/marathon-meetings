## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Community Life and Public Safety Committee met on May 4th to address several regulatory matters including amendments to nuisance ordinances, e-bike regulations, overnight parking rules, and building demolition orders. Key actions included recommending approval of Chapter 50 nuisance amendments, an e-bike ordinance with emoto restrictions, mobile food vendor fire inspections, address corrections, and a raze order for a condemned property on Shore Avenue.

## Key Discussions
### Public Comment
Jim Senalt spoke critically about the new farmers market location, expressing concerns that parking vehicles on grass violates village code and will destroy the park. He questioned ADA compliance for handicap parking on grass rather than asphalt, asked about the rationale for adding fire inspections for mobile food vendors, and criticized the lack of clarity in food truck regulations. He also noted that some code violation cases have been pending for years without visible progress.

### Department Reports
Roman/Audrey reported that 3020 Mountain View Avenue will be removed from the code enforcement roll after the property sale was completed and the new owner submitted repair permits. Chief Hunt reported 2,394 calls for March (77 per day), 410 traffic stops, and 281 citations issued. The police department has hired its 40th officer, with the 38th starting last week, 39th starting in May, and 40th around January. Two SROs work with zoning during summer handling violations and pet licensing. Motion to acknowledge reports 5, 6, and 7 passed unanimously.

### Chapter 50 Nuisance Amendments
The committee reviewed final amendments including previously discussed changes to tree limb height requirements (14 and 10 feet switched) and language requiring compliance with jurisdiction authority. Motion by Olsen, seconded by Campkey, to recommend approval to the Board of Trustees passed unanimously.

### E-bike Ordinance
Committee discussed the proposed e-bike ordinance coordinated with other municipalities. Chief Hunt strongly recommended prohibiting emotos (electric dirt bike-style vehicles) from roadways, sidewalks, and trails, comparing them to unlicensed dirt bikes. Discussion addressed whether electric scooters used by middle schoolers should be exempted, with Chief Hunt suggesting officers use discretion. The committee decided to leave scooter language as-is and rely on officer judgment. Motion by Olsen, seconded by Chwanka, to recommend approval with emotos prohibited on roadways, sidewalks, trails, and public property passed unanimously.

### Overnight Parking Regulations
Brent Tolson presented research showing Weston's 12-hour parking restriction window (4am-4pm) is significantly longer than surrounding municipalities (most use 5 hours or less, typically 2am-7am). Michael from Public Works noted the even/odd system was adopted because snow plowing rarely occurs before 4-5am. Chief Hunt opposed eliminating overnight parking entirely, preferring uniformity with other municipalities. Committee agreed to table the item until July while Michael identifies specific problem areas that need signage.

### Crosswalk Request on Alderson Street
Mount Olive Church requested a crosswalk between their property and DC Everest parking lot where parishioners park. Staff estimated a basic crosswalk would cost approximately $2,000, while a lighted system would cost $25,000-30,000. Committee expressed reluctance to set precedent of paying for crosswalks on request. Motion by Olsen, seconded by Chwanka, to recommend board approval if Mount Olive agrees to cover installation costs passed unanimously. Michael will contact Mount Olive about cost sharing.

### Mobile Food Vendor Fire Inspections
Chief Hunt explained a new regional fire inspection program involving Wisconsin Rapids, Marshfield, Stevens Point, Plover, Rib Mountain, and Weston. Food trucks inspected by any participating department receive a sticker valid across all jurisdictions, eliminating duplicate inspections. Fire Marshal Marty already conducted one inspection this week that failed due to improper LP piping and unsecured propane containers. Motion by Chwanka, seconded by Campkey, to approve Ordinance 26-8 passed unanimously.

### Address Corrections
Staff presented three address corrections needed for E911 compliance: 5915 Delicoski Street (duplex needing separate addresses), Ross Avenue properties out of sequence near an apartment building, and 2211 Radkey Avenue for a new duplex on a private drive. Chief Hunt mentioned an additional issue at Weston Villa Apartments on Delicoski where eight apartments have eight separate addresses plus a utility room address. Motion passed unanimously to recommend approval.

### Raze Order for 4104 Shore Avenue
Staff requested authorization to begin raze proceedings on a structure abandoned since 2010-2011 that has been posted since 2021. The fire department previously declined to do a training burn due to unsafe conditions. The property was discovered to have chickens housed inside when someone reported lights on in the structure. Current owners are up to date on taxes and prefer the village handle demolition with costs added to their tax roll. Motion by Olsen, seconded by Campkey, passed unanimously.

## Public Comment
Jim Senalt spoke during public comment, criticizing the new farmers market location as 'terrible execution' that will destroy park grass, questioning ADA compliance for handicap parking placement on grass, asking about the origin of food truck fire inspections, noting ambiguity in food vendor regulations, and calling for better accountability on long-pending code enforcement cases.
