## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Community Life and Public Safety Committee approved amendments to nuisance ordinances, recommended an e-bike ordinance with emoto restrictions, and authorized demolition of an abandoned structure. The committee also discussed changes to overnight parking regulations and a crosswalk request from Mount Olive Church, deferring both items for further information.

## Key Discussions
### Department Reports
Code enforcement officer reported that 3020 Mountain View Avenue will be removed from the enforcement list after the property sale was completed and the new owner submitted repair permits. Police Chief Hunt reported 2,394 calls in March (77 per day), 410 traffic stops, and 281 citations. The department has hired its 40th officer, with the 38th starting last week and the 39th starting in May. Motion to acknowledge reports 5, 6, and 7 was made by Sherwanka, seconded by Olsen, and passed unanimously.

### Chapter 50 Nuisance Amendments
The committee reviewed final amendments including the tree limb height requirements (14 and 10 feet) that were switched in the text as discussed in previous meetings. Motion to recommend approval to the board of trustees was made by Olsen, seconded by Campkey, and passed unanimously.

### E-Bicycle Ordinance
Extensive discussion occurred regarding emotos (electric dirt bikes). Chief Hunt strongly advocated for prohibiting emotos on roadways, sidewalks, and trails, comparing them to unlicensed dirt bikes. Committee members discussed whether to also restrict electric scooters but decided to leave officer discretion for kids' play scooters. Rothschild has not yet approved the model ordinance. Motion by Olsen to recommend approval with the addition that emotos are prohibited from operating on roadways, sidewalks, trails, or any public property was seconded by Sherwanka and passed unanimously.

### Overnight Parking Regulations
Trustee Tolson presented research showing Weston's 12-hour parking restriction (4 AM-4 PM) is significantly longer than surrounding municipalities which typically have 5-hour windows. Chief Hunt preferred keeping overnight restrictions to give officers time to clear vehicles before plows arrive. Public Works Director Michael suggested identifying 6-8 problem areas for specific signage instead of village-wide restrictions. The committee deferred action, directing Michael to identify specific exception areas to bring back in July.

### Crosswalk Request on Alderson Street
Mount Olive Church requested a crosswalk between their church and DC Everest High School parking lot where parishioners park on Sundays. Committee members expressed reluctance to pay for the crosswalk, estimating $2,000-3,000 for a basic crosswalk or $25,000-30,000 for a lighted system. Motion by Olsen that if Mount Olive is willing to cover the installation cost, the committee recommends board approval was seconded by Sherwanka and passed unanimously. Michael will contact Mount Olive regarding cost-sharing.

### Mobile Food Vendors Fire Inspection
Fire Chief explained a new regional inspection program where multiple municipalities (Wisconsin Rapids, Marshfield, Stevens Point, Plover, Rib Mountain, and Weston) will recognize each other's food truck fire inspections. Wausau opted out to do their own inspections. Fire Marshal Marty recently inspected a truck that failed due to improper LP piping and unsecured propane cylinders. Motion by Sherwanka to approve Ordinance 26-8 was seconded by Campkey and passed unanimously.

### Address Corrections
Staff presented three addressing errors: 5915 Delicoski Street (duplex needing second address), Ross Avenue properties out of sequence, and 2211 Radkkey Avenue near a new duplex development. Chief Hunt noted an additional issue with Weston Villa Apartments having nine separate addresses for one eight-unit structure. Motion to recommend board approval was made and seconded by Campkey, passing unanimously.

### Raze Request for 4104 Shore Avenue
Staff requested authorization to begin demolition proceedings for an abandoned structure that has been vacant since 2010-2011. A previous fire department training burn was cancelled due to unsafe conditions. The current owners, who are up to date on taxes, previously housed chickens in the structure. Demolition costs will be placed on the tax roll for recovery. Motion by Olsen, seconded by Campkey, passed unanimously.

## Public Comment
Jim Senalt spoke regarding multiple agenda items. He criticized the new farmers market location at the park, predicting the grass will be destroyed by summer's end. He noted that parking vehicles on grass appears to violate village code and questioned the placement of ADA parking spaces on grass rather than asphalt. He questioned the origin of the new food truck fire inspection requirement. He also expressed concern about long-standing code violations on the enforcement list, noting some cases are very old with no visible progress.
