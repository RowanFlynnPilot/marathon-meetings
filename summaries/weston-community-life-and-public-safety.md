## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Community Life and Public Safety Committee met on May 4th and took action on several items including recommending approval of nuisance code amendments, an e-bike ordinance with emoto restrictions, mobile food vendor fire inspections, address corrections, and a raze order for a deteriorated structure. The committee also discussed but deferred action on overnight parking regulations.

## Key Discussions
### Public Comments
Jim Senalt spoke critically about the new farmers market location at Kennedy Park, expressing concern that parking vehicles on grass violates village code and will destroy grassy areas. He questioned ADA compliance for handicap parking on grass rather than asphalt, questioned the need for fire inspections on food trucks, and noted that some code violations have been pending for years without visible progress.

### Department Reports
Roman/Audrey reported 3020 Mountain View Avenue will be removed from code enforcement as the property sale was completed and new owner submitted repair permits. Chief Hunt reported the police department had 2,394 calls in March (77 per day), 410 traffic stops, and 281 citations. The department has hired its 40th officer, with new hires starting in May and January. Two SROs work with zoning violations during summer. Motion to acknowledge reports 5, 6, and 7 passed unanimously.

### Chapter 50 Nuisances Amendments
The committee reviewed final amendments including tree limb height requirements (14 and 10 feet switched in text) over streets and walkways, with language added requiring compliance with jurisdiction authority. Motion by Olsen, seconded by Kimi to recommend approval to Board of Trustees passed unanimously.

### E-Bicycle Ordinance
Extensive discussion occurred about emotos (electric dirt bikes). Chief Hunt strongly recommended prohibiting emotos from roadways and sidewalks, comparing them to unlicensed dirt bikes. Committee discussed whether to exempt kids' play scooters but decided to leave officer discretion. Motion by Olsen, seconded by Sherwanka to recommend approval with amendment prohibiting emotos on roadways, sidewalks, trails, and public property passed unanimously.

### Overnight Parking Regulations
Trustee Tolson presented research showing Weston's 12-hour parking restriction (4am-4pm) is significantly longer than surrounding municipalities (most have 5-hour windows). Michael from Public Works noted plowing typically doesn't reach residential streets until 7-8am. Chief Hunt opposed eliminating overnight parking entirely, preferring uniform hours with other municipalities. Committee discussed various options but deferred action, asking Michael to identify specific problem areas to potentially sign individually rather than village-wide restrictions.

### Crosswalk Request - Alderson Street
Mount Olive Church requested a crosswalk between the church and DC Everest parking lot. Staff estimated basic crosswalk cost at $2,000, lighted system at $25,000-$30,000. Committee expressed reluctance to pay for crosswalks on request. Motion by Olsen, seconded by Sherwanka that if Mount Olive is willing to cover the cost, recommend board approval. Passed unanimously. Michael will contact Mount Olive.

### Mobile Food Vendor Fire Inspections
Chief Hunt explained a regional fire inspection program with Wisconsin Rapids, Marshfield, Stevens Point, Plover, Rib Mountain, and Safer where any department's inspection is valid for all participating municipalities. One recent inspection failed due to improper LP piping (galvanized steel) and unsecured propane containers. Wausau opted not to participate. Motion by Sherwanka, seconded by Campkey to approve Ordinance 26-8 passed unanimously.

### Address Corrections
Staff presented three address corrections for E911 compliance: 5915 Delicoski Street (duplex needing separate addresses), Ross Avenue properties out of sequence, and 2211 Radkey Avenue (new duplex on private drive). Chief Hunt mentioned an additional issue at Weston Villa Apartments where each of 8 units has separate addresses. Motion passed unanimously to recommend approval.

### Raze Order - 4104 Shore Avenue
Staff requested authorization to proceed with raze order for abandoned structure. Property has been abandoned since 2010-2011, was previously considered for fire department training burn but deemed too unsafe. Owners previously housed chickens inside the structure. Current owners prefer the village proceed with demolition; costs will be placed on tax roll. Motion by Olsen, seconded by Campkey passed unanimously.

## Public Comment
Jim Senalt spoke regarding several agenda items, criticizing the new farmers market location at Kennedy Park, questioning ADA compliance for handicap parking on grass, questioning the need for food truck fire inspections, and expressing concern about the age of pending code violations.
