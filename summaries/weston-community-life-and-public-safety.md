## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Community Life and Public Safety Committee met on May 4th, approving amendments to the nuisance ordinance for recommendation to the board, advancing an e-bike ordinance with added emoto restrictions, and authorizing a raze order for a condemned property at 4104 Shore Avenue. The committee also approved a mobile food vendor fire inspection program and conditionally approved a crosswalk on Alderson Street if Mount Olive Church covers the cost.

## Key Discussions
### Public Comments
Jim Senalt spoke critically about the new farmers market location, stating his opinion that 'it's a terrible execution' and predicting the park's grassy areas will be destroyed. He raised concerns about parking on grass potentially violating village code, ADA handicap parking placement on grass versus asphalt, the addition of fire inspections for mobile food vendors, and code enforcement cases that have been pending for extended periods without visible progress.

### Department Reports
Code enforcement officer Audrey noted that 3020 Mountain View Avenue will be removed from the code enforcement roll after the property sale was completed and new owner submitted repair permits. Chief Hunt reported the police department had 2,394 calls in March (77 per day), 410 traffic stops, and 281 citations. The department has hired its 40th officer, with the 38th starting last week, 39th starting in May, and 40th around January. Two SROs work summer zoning violations for Weston, Rothschild, and Schofield. Motion to acknowledge reports 5, 6, and 7 passed unanimously.

### Chapter 50 Nuisance Amendments
The committee reviewed the final copy of proposed nuisance ordinance amendments, including previously discussed changes to tree limb height requirements over streets and walkways (14 and 10 feet). Motion by Olsen, seconded by Kimpie, to recommend approval to the board of trustees passed unanimously.

### E-Bicycle Ordinance
Extensive discussion occurred about the proposed e-bike ordinance for Chapter 82. Chief Hunt strongly advocated prohibiting emotos (electric dirt bikes) on roadways and sidewalks, comparing them to unlicensed dirt bikes. Discussion included whether electric scooters used by middle schoolers would be affected and officer discretion in enforcement. The committee decided to add language prohibiting emotos from operating on roadways, sidewalks, trails, and public property. Motion by Olsen, seconded by Sherwanka, to recommend approval with the emoto restriction passed unanimously.

### Overnight Parking Regulations
Trustee Tolson initiated discussion noting Weston's 12-hour parking restriction window (4 AM to 4 PM) is significantly longer than surrounding municipalities (most have 5-hour windows). Public Works Director Michael explained the even/odd system helps ensure plows can clear at least half the roadway. Chief Hunt opposed eliminating overnight restrictions, noting it gives officers time to clear vehicles before morning plowing. The committee decided to table the item until July to allow Michael to identify specific problem areas that might need signing rather than village-wide restrictions.

### Alderson Street Crosswalk Request
Discussion on Mount Olive Church's request for a crosswalk between the church and DC Everest parking lot. Michael estimated a basic crosswalk would cost about $2,000, while a lighted system would be $25,000-30,000. Committee members expressed reluctance to pay for it, citing no accident history. Motion by Olsen, seconded by Sherwanka, to recommend board approval if Mount Olive agrees to cover the installation cost passed unanimously.

### Mobile Food Vendor Fire Inspection Ordinance
Chief Hunt explained this creates a regional inspection program with Wisconsin Rapids, Marshfield, Stevens Point, Plover, Rib Mountain, and Weston, where one inspection with a sticker is valid across all municipalities. He noted Wausau opted out to do their own inspections. Fire Marshal Marty already inspected one truck that failed due to improper LP piping and unsecured propane containers. Motion by Sherwanka, seconded by Kimpie, to approve Ordinance 26-8 passed unanimously.

### Address Corrections
Planning staff presented three address correction cases for E911 compliance: 5915 Delicowski Street (duplex needing separate addresses), Ross Avenue properties out of sequence, and 2211 Radkey Avenue needing even number assignment. Chief Hunt mentioned another issue at Weston Villa Apartments on Delicowski where an 8-plex has separate addresses for each unit. Motion passed unanimously to recommend board approval.

### Raze Order for 4104 Shore Avenue
Staff requested authorization to begin raze proceedings for a condemned structure abandoned since 2010-2011. The fire department had previously declined to do a training burn due to unsafe conditions. Staff noted the current owners had housed chickens inside the structure. Demolition costs will be added to the property tax roll. Motion by Olsen, seconded by Kimpie, passed unanimously.

## Public Comment
Jim Senalt provided public comment criticizing the new farmers market location execution, raising concerns about parking on grass violating village code, ADA accessibility for handicap parking on grass, the origin of new fire inspection requirements for food trucks, and lack of progress on long-standing code enforcement cases.
