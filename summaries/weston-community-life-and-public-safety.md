## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Community Life and Public Safety Committee approved amendments to the nuisance ordinance, recommended an e-bike ordinance with emoto restrictions, and authorized razing a condemned structure at 4104 Shore Avenue. The committee also discussed overnight parking regulations and a crosswalk request, deferring both items for further research.

## Key Discussions
### Public Comments
Jim Senalt spoke regarding several agenda items. He criticized the new farmers market location, stating it would destroy grassy areas and that parking vehicles on grass may violate village code. He questioned ADA compliance for handicap parking on grass rather than asphalt. He also asked about the origin of fire inspections for food trucks and raised concerns about code enforcement cases being old without visible progress.

### Department Reports
Code Enforcement reported that 3020 Mountain View Avenue will be removed from the enforcement roll after the property sale completed and new owner submitted repair permits. Police Chief Hunt reported 2,394 calls in March (77 per day), 410 traffic stops, and 281 citations. The department has hired its 40th officer, with the 38th starting last week, 39th in May, and 40th around January. Two SROs work on zoning violations during summer for all three municipalities. Motion to acknowledge reports 5, 6, and 7 passed unanimously.

### Chapter 50 Nuisance Amendments
The committee reviewed final amendments including tree limb height requirements of 14 feet over streets and 10 feet over walkways. Motion by Olsen to recommend approval to the Board of Trustees, seconded by Campkey. Motion passed unanimously.

### E-Bicycle Ordinance
The committee discussed the proposed e-bike ordinance for Chapter 82. Chief Hunt expressed concern about emotos (electric dirt bike-style vehicles), stating they should not be allowed on roadways or sidewalks as they are essentially unlicensed dirt bikes. Discussion included whether electric scooters would be affected. The committee agreed to prohibit emotos from roadways, sidewalks, trails, and public property. Motion by Olsen to recommend approval with emoto restrictions, seconded by Sherwanka. Motion passed unanimously.

### Overnight Parking Regulations
Trustee Tolson presented research showing Weston's 12-hour overnight parking window (4 AM to 4 PM) is significantly longer than surrounding municipalities, which typically have 4-5 hour windows. Public Works Director Michael noted plowing rarely occurs before 4-5 AM and suggested signing specific problem areas instead of village-wide restrictions. Chief Hunt preferred keeping overnight restrictions to give officers time to clear vehicles before plowing. The committee deferred the item to July for Michael to identify specific problem areas requiring signage.

### Crosswalk Request on Alderson Street
Mount Olive Church requested a crosswalk between the church and DC Everest high school administrative parking lot. Staff estimated $2,000-$3,000 for a basic crosswalk, or $25,000-$30,000 for a lighted system. Chief Hunt reported no known incidents at the location. The committee agreed to approve a crosswalk only if Mount Olive covers the installation cost. Motion by Olsen, seconded by Sherwanka. Motion passed unanimously.

### Mobile Food Vendors Fire Inspection
Fire Chief explained a multi-municipality fire inspection program with Stevens Point, Marshfield, Wisconsin Rapids, Clover, and Rib Mountain. One inspection with a sticker would be valid across all participating municipalities. Fire Marshal Marty already inspected one truck that failed due to galvanized steel LP piping and improperly secured propane containers. Motion by Sherwanka to approve Ordinance 26-8, seconded by Campkey. Motion passed unanimously.

### Address Corrections
Staff presented three address corrections for E911 compliance: 5915 Delicoski Street (duplex needing separate addresses), Ross Avenue properties out of sequence, and 2211 Radkey Avenue for a new duplex. Chief Hunt noted an additional addressing issue at Weston Villa Apartments on Delicoski with eight separate addresses for one structure. Motion passed unanimously to recommend corrections to the Board.

### Raze Request for 4104 Shore Avenue
Staff requested authorization to begin raze proceedings on a structure abandoned since 2010-2011. The fire department previously declined a training burn due to unsafe conditions. Staff noted chickens had been housed in the structure. Current owners are up to date on taxes and prefer the village proceed with demolition, with costs to be placed on the tax roll. Motion by Olsen, seconded by Campkey. Motion passed unanimously.

## Public Comment
Jim Senalt spoke regarding concerns about the new farmers market location potentially destroying grass, ADA compliance issues with handicap parking on grass, questions about why fire inspections are being added for food trucks, and criticism that some code enforcement cases have been pending for years without visible progress.
