## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Community Life and Public Safety Committee met on May 4th and took action on several items including recommending approval of nuisance ordinance amendments, an e-bike ordinance with restrictions on e-motos, mobile food vendor fire inspection requirements, address corrections, and a raze order for a deteriorated structure. The committee also discussed overnight parking regulations but deferred action pending staff research.

## Key Discussions
### Public Comments
Jim Senalt spoke critically about the new farmers market location, expressing concern that parking vehicles on grass violates village code and will destroy the park. He questioned ADA compliance for handicap parking on grass rather than asphalt, asked why fire inspections were being added for mobile food vendors, and criticized the age of some code enforcement cases that have been pending for over a year without visible progress.

### Department Reports
Code Enforcement reported that 3020 Mountain View Avenue will be removed from the enforcement roll after the property sale was completed and new owner submitted repair permits. Police Chief Hunt reported 2,394 calls in March (77 per day), 410 traffic stops, and 281 citations issued. The department has hired its 40th officer, with the 38th starting last week, 39th in May, and 40th around January. Two SROs work on zoning violations during summer for all three municipalities. Reports were acknowledged by motion from Chwanka, seconded by Olsen, passed unanimously.

### Chapter 50 Nuisances Amendments
The committee reviewed final amendments to Chapter 50, including previously discussed changes to tree limb height requirements (14 and 10 feet switched) with language added for compliance with other jurisdictions' authority. Motion by Olsen, seconded by Campkey to recommend approval to the Board of Trustees. Passed unanimously.

### E-Bicycle Ordinance
Extensive discussion occurred about regulating e-bikes, e-motos, and electric scooters. Chief Hunt strongly advocated prohibiting e-motos from roadways and sidewalks, comparing them to unlicensed dirt bikes. Committee discussed whether to exempt children's play scooters but decided to rely on officer discretion. Motion by Olsen, seconded by Chwanka to recommend approval with amendment prohibiting e-motos on roadways, sidewalks, trails, and public property. Passed unanimously.

### Overnight Parking Regulations
Trustee Tolson presented research showing Weston's 12-hour parking restriction (4 AM to 4 PM) is far outside the norm compared to surrounding municipalities which typically have 4-5 hour overnight windows. DPW Director Michael Wodalski suggested either eliminating the restriction or signing specific problem areas (6-8 locations). Chief Hunt preferred maintaining overnight restrictions to give officers time to clear vehicles before plowing. Committee deferred action to July, directing staff to identify specific exception areas.

### Crosswalk Request for Alderson Street
Mount Olive Church requested a crosswalk between the church and DC Everest High School parking lot where parishioners park on Sundays. Staff estimated cost at $2,000-3,000 for basic crosswalk or $25,000-30,000 for lighted system. Committee was reluctant to set precedent of paying for requested crosswalks. Motion by Olsen, seconded by Chwanka to recommend approval contingent on Mount Olive covering installation costs. Passed unanimously.

### Mobile Food Vendor Fire Inspections
Fire Chief presented a regional mutual inspection program with Wisconsin Rapids, Marshfield, Stevens Point, Plover, Rib Mountain, and SAFER. Any participating department can inspect food trucks, which receive a sticker valid across all jurisdictions. Chief noted one recent inspection failed due to improper LP piping and unsecured propane containers. Wausau opted out to maintain their own program. Motion by Chwanka, seconded by Campkey to approve Ordinance 26-8. Passed unanimously.

### Address Corrections
Staff presented three address corrections needed for E911 compliance: 5915 Delicoski Street (duplex needing separate addresses), Ross Avenue properties out of sequence, and 2211 Radkey Avenue for a new duplex. Chief Hunt also identified Weston Villa Apartments on Delicoski as having addressing issues. Motion approved unanimously.

### Raze Order for 4104 Shore Avenue
Staff requested authorization to proceed with raze order for a structure abandoned since 2010-2011 that has been posted since 2021. The building was too deteriorated for fire department training burn. Current owners, who are current on taxes and running a small farm operation, had housed chickens in the structure. Owners prefer the village proceed with demolition, with costs added to tax roll. Motion by Olsen, seconded by Campkey. Passed unanimously.

## Public Comment
Jim Senalt spoke during public comment criticizing the new farmers market location at the park, expressing concerns about vehicles parking on grass violating village code, ADA compliance issues with handicap parking on grass, questioning the need for fire inspections on food trucks, and criticizing the lack of progress on long-standing code enforcement cases.
