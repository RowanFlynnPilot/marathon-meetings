## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Community Life and Public Safety Committee approved amendments to Chapter 50 nuisance ordinances, recommended an e-bike ordinance with restrictions on emotos, and authorized demolition of an abandoned structure at 4104 Shore Avenue. The committee also approved fire inspection requirements for mobile food vendors and discussed overnight parking regulations without taking action.

## Key Discussions
### Proposed amendments to Chapter 50 nuisances
The committee reviewed final amendments to Chapter 50 regarding nuisances, including previously discussed changes to tree limb height requirements over streets and walkways (switched to 14 and 10 feet) and added language requiring compliance with jurisdiction authority. Trustee Olsen made a motion to recommend approval to the Board of Trustees, seconded by Campkey. Motion passed unanimously.

### Proposed e-bicycle ordinance in Chapter 82
Committee discussed the model e-bike ordinance with significant focus on emotos (electric motorcycles/dirt bikes). Chief Hunt strongly advocated for prohibiting emotos on roadways, sidewalks, and trails, comparing them to unlicensed dirt bikes. Discussion addressed whether electric scooters should be exempt, with Chief Hunt suggesting adding 'with exemption to kids play scooters' for officer discretion. Olsen made a motion to recommend approval with the addition prohibiting emotos on roadways, sidewalks, trails, and any public property. Seconded by Chwanka. Motion passed unanimously.

### Overnight parking regulations discussion
Trustee Tolson presented research showing Weston's 12-hour no-parking window (4 AM to 4 PM, October 31-May 1) is significantly longer than comparable municipalities, which typically have 4-5 hour windows. Public Works Director Michael suggested eliminating the even/odd requirement and instead signing specific problem areas. Chief Hunt preferred keeping overnight restrictions to give officers time to clear vehicles before plowing. No action was taken; the item was tabled for Michael to identify specific exception areas for the July meeting.

### Crosswalk request on Alderson Street between Mount Olive Church and DC Everest
Staff presented a request for a crosswalk between Mount Olive Church and DC Everest High School. Committee members expressed reluctance to pay for crosswalks requested by private entities. Chief Hunt noted no incidents in that area. Olsen made a motion that if Mount Olive is willing to cover the cost of installing the crosswalk, the committee recommends board approval. Seconded by Chwanka. Motion passed unanimously. Michael will contact Mount Olive regarding cost sharing.

### Mobile food vendors fire inspection amendment
Fire Chief explained a regional fire inspection program involving Wisconsin Rapids, Marshfield, Stevens Point, Clover, Riverside, and Safer departments, where one inspection with a sticker is valid across all participating municipalities. Chief noted a recent inspection where a food truck failed due to galvanized steel LP piping and improperly secured propane containers. Wausau opted not to participate, preferring their own system. Chwanka made a motion to approve ordinance 26-8 as written, seconded by Campkey. Motion passed unanimously.

### Address corrections for E911 compliance
Staff presented three addressing corrections: 5915 Delicoski Street (duplex needing separate addresses 5915/5917), Ross Avenue properties requiring resequencing due to out-of-order numbering, and 2211 Radkey Avenue needing even-numbered address. Chief Hunt mentioned an additional issue at Weston Villa Apartments on Delicoski where an 8-plex has nine separate addresses including a utility room. Motion to recommend to the Board of Trustees passed unanimously.

### Raze request for 4104 Shore Avenue
Staff requested authorization to begin raze order on an abandoned structure at 4104 Shore Avenue, abandoned since 2010-2011. A planned fire department training burn was cancelled because the structure was too deteriorated to be safe. The current owners had previously housed chickens inside the structure. Staff noted demolition costs will be placed on the property's tax roll. Olsen made the motion, seconded by Campkey. Motion passed unanimously.

## Public Comment
Jim Senalt spoke during public comment, criticizing the new farmers market location at a park, predicting grass destruction by summer's end. He raised concerns about parking vehicles on grass potentially violating village code, ADA-compliant handicap spaces being placed on grass rather than asphalt, questioned the origin of new fire inspection requirements for mobile food vendors, and noted ambiguity in food truck regulations regarding non-farmers market days. He also criticized the lack of accountability on long-standing code violations, stating some cases have been pending without visible progress.
