## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Community Life and Public Safety Committee approved amendments to nuisance regulations, recommended an e-bike ordinance with emoto restrictions, and authorized razing a condemned structure at 4104 Shore Avenue. The committee also discussed overnight parking regulations and a crosswalk request from Mount Olive Church, deferring decisions on both items pending additional information.

## Key Discussions
### Chapter 50 Nuisance Amendments
The committee reviewed final amendments to Chapter 50 regarding nuisances, including previously discussed changes to tree limb height requirements over streets and walkways (14 and 10 feet specifications were switched as requested). Trustee Olsen made a motion to recommend approval to the Board of Trustees, seconded by Campkey. The motion passed unanimously.

### E-bike Ordinance in Chapter 82
The committee extensively discussed the proposed e-bike ordinance, with particular focus on emotos (electric dirt bike-style vehicles). Chief Hunt strongly advocated prohibiting emotos on roadways, sidewalks, and trails, comparing them to unlicensed dirt bikes and citing safety concerns. Committee members debated whether to exempt children's electric scooters, with Chief Hunt suggesting language like 'with exemption to kids play scooters' to allow officer discretion. Trustee Olsen made a motion to recommend approval with the addition of prohibiting emotos on roadways, sidewalks, trails, and any public property in the Village of Weston. Seconded by Sherwanka, the motion passed unanimously.

### Overnight Parking Regulations
Trustee Tolson presented research showing Weston's 12-hour no-parking window (4 AM to 4 PM, October 31 to May 1) is significantly longer than surrounding municipalities, which typically have 4-5 hour windows. Public Works Director Michael Wodalski noted the village could identify 6-8 problem areas and sign those specifically rather than village-wide restrictions. Chief Hunt advocated keeping overnight parking restrictions to give officers time to clear vehicles before plowing begins. The committee decided to defer the item until July, with Wodalski tasked to identify specific problem areas requiring signage.

### Crosswalk Request on Alderson Street
Mount Olive Church requested a crosswalk between their facility and DC Everest High School parking lot where parishioners park on Sundays. Committee members noted there have been no reported incidents and existing crosswalks nearby. Staff estimated a basic crosswalk would cost approximately $2,000-3,000, while a lighted system would cost $25,000-30,000. The committee agreed that if Mount Olive is willing to cover installation costs, they would recommend approval. Trustee Olsen made a motion to recommend board approval contingent on Mount Olive covering costs, seconded by Sherwanka. The motion passed unanimously. Public Works Director Wodalski will contact Mount Olive regarding cost-sharing.

### Mobile Food Vendors Fire Inspection Ordinance
Fire Chief explained a new regional fire inspection program involving Wisconsin Rapids, Marshfield, Stevens Point, Plover, Rib Mountain, and Weston. Food trucks inspected by any participating department receive a sticker valid for all jurisdictions, eliminating redundant inspections. The city of Wausau opted out to maintain their own inspection program. Fire Marshal Marty recently inspected a food truck that failed due to galvanized steel LP piping and improperly secured propane containers. Trustee Sherwanka made a motion to approve Ordinance 26-8, seconded by Campkey. The motion passed unanimously.

### Address Corrections for E911 Compliance
Staff presented three addressing errors requiring correction: 5915 Delicoski Street (duplex needing separate addresses), Ross Avenue properties out of sequence near an existing apartment building, and 2211 Radkey Avenue (new duplex on private drive). Chief Hunt also identified an issue with Weston Villa Apartments on Delicoski, an eight-unit building where each apartment has a separate street address. A motion to recommend approval to the Board of Trustees passed unanimously.

### Raze Order for 4104 Shore Avenue
Staff requested authorization to begin raze proceedings on a structure abandoned since 2010-2011. The fire department previously declined to use it for training burns due to unsafe conditions. Staff discovered chickens being housed inside the structure in recent years. The current owners prefer the village handle demolition with costs added to their tax roll. Trustee Olsen made a motion to authorize staff to begin the raze order, seconded by Campkey. The motion passed unanimously.

### Department Reports
Code Enforcement Officer Audrey reported 3020 Mountain View Avenue will be removed from the enforcement list after sale and new owner submitted repair permits. Police Chief Hunt reported 2,394 calls in March (77 per day), 410 traffic stops, and 281 citations issued. The department has hired its 40th officer, with the 38th starting last week, 39th starting in May, and 40th around January. Two SROs work with code enforcement during summer on zoning violations and pet licensing. Reports were acknowledged unanimously.

## Public Comment
Jim Senalt spoke regarding multiple agenda items. He criticized the new farmers market location at the park, predicting the grass will be destroyed by parking vehicles on it and questioning whether this violates village code prohibiting grass parking. He noted ADA parking spaces are marked on grass rather than adjacent asphalt, making wheelchair access difficult. He questioned the origin of the new fire inspection requirement for food trucks, asked about enforcement on farmers market days, and expressed concern about the age of several code enforcement cases, stating some have been on the list for years with no visible progress on at least three cases he's personally aware of.
