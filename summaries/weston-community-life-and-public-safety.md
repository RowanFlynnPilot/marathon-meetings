## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Community Life and Public Safety Committee met on May 4th, taking action on several ordinance amendments and addressing concerns about ebikes, parking regulations, and property issues. Key decisions included recommending approval of nuisance ordinance amendments, an ebike ordinance with emoto restrictions, mobile food vendor fire inspections, address corrections, and a raze order for an abandoned structure.

## Key Discussions
### Public Comments
Jim Senalt addressed the committee with concerns about the new farmers market location, predicting grass damage from vehicle parking and questioning ADA compliance for handicapped parking on grass. He also questioned the need for new fire inspections on food trucks and criticized the age of some code enforcement cases, suggesting the committee should receive regular updates on long-standing violations.

### Department Reports
Code Enforcement reported that 3020 Mountain View Avenue will be removed from the code enforcement roll after the property sale was completed and new owner submitted repair permits. Some enforcement-suspended cases may be reactivated for construction season. Police Chief Hunt reported 2,394 calls in March (77 per day), 410 traffic stops, and 281 citations. The department has hired its 40th officer, with new officers starting in various months. The committee acknowledged reports 5, 6, and 7 on motion by Chwanka, seconded by Olsen, passed unanimously.

### Amendments to Chapter 50 Nuisances
The committee discussed final amendments including tree limb height requirements (14 and 10 feet) over streets and walkways that were switched from the previous meeting. Motion by Olsen, seconded by Campkey to recommend approval to the Board of Trustees. Passed unanimously.

### E-bicycle Ordinance
Extensive discussion on the model ebike ordinance for Chapter 82. Chief Hunt expressed strong opposition to emotos (electric dirt bikes) on roadways and sidewalks, comparing them to unlicensed dirt bikes. Discussion included whether electric scooters used by middle schoolers should be affected. The committee decided to add language prohibiting emotos from roadways, sidewalks, trails, and public property. Motion by Olsen, seconded by Chwanka to recommend approval with the emoto prohibition added. Passed unanimously.

### Overnight Parking Regulations
Trustee Tolson presented research showing Weston's 12-hour parking restriction window (4am-4pm) is far outside what neighboring municipalities use (typically 4-5 hours overnight). Discussion included whether to eliminate overnight parking restrictions entirely or align with other communities. DPW Director Michael noted most snow plowing doesn't reach residential streets until 7-8am. Chief Hunt preferred keeping overnight restrictions for enforcement purposes. No action taken; staff will develop specific exception areas for discussion at the July meeting.

### Crosswalk Request on Alderson Street
Mount Olive Church requested a crosswalk between their facility and DC Everest parking lot where parishioners park. Committee discussed costs ($2,000-3,000 for basic crosswalk, $25,000-30,000 for lighted system). Chief Hunt reported no incidents at the location. Motion by Olsen, seconded by Chwanka that if Mount Olive is willing to cover the cost, the committee recommends board approval. Passed unanimously. DPW Director will contact the church.

### Mobile Food Vendor Fire Inspections
Fire Chief explained a regional consortium (Wisconsin Rapids, Marshfield, Stevens Point, Plover, Rib Mountain, and Weston) will share food truck fire inspections under NFPA 54. One inspection with sticker is valid for all participating municipalities. Wausau opted out to do their own. Chief noted a recent inspection failed due to improper LP piping (galvanized steel) and improperly secured propane containers. Motion by Chwanka, seconded by Campkey to approve Ordinance 26-8. Passed unanimously.

### Address Corrections
Staff presented three addressing errors: 5915 Delicoski Street (unknown duplex needing second address), Ross Avenue properties out of sequence, and 2211 Radkey Avenue for a new duplex on private drive. Chief Hunt mentioned an additional issue at Weston Villa Apartments where each of 8 units has separate addresses. Motion made and seconded to recommend approval. Passed unanimously.

### Raze Order for 4104 Shore Avenue
An abandoned structure since 2010-2011 that was too deteriorated for fire department training burn. Owners have been housing chickens inside. Current owners are up to date on taxes and prefer the village proceed with demolition, with costs added to tax roll. Motion by Olsen, seconded by Campkey to authorize staff to begin the raze order. Passed unanimously.

## Public Comment
Jim Senalt spoke during public comment, criticizing the new farmers market location as 'terrible execution' that will destroy grassy areas, questioning ADA compliance for handicapped parking on grass, questioning the origin of new fire inspection requirements for food trucks, and expressing concern that some code enforcement cases have been pending for years without visible progress.
