## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Community Life and Public Safety Committee met to address several regulatory matters, recommending approval of nuisance ordinance amendments, an e-bike ordinance with restrictions on e-motos, mobile food vendor fire inspections, address corrections, and a raze order for an abandoned property. The committee also discussed overnight parking regulations but deferred action pending staff research on problem areas.

## Key Discussions
### Department Reports
Code Enforcement reported that 3020 Mountain View Avenue will be removed from the enforcement roll after property sale and new owner submitted repair permits. Police Chief Hunt reported 2,394 calls in March (77 per day), 410 traffic stops, and 281 citations. The department has hired its 40th officer, with recent hires starting in phases through January. Two SROs work on zoning violations during summer for Weston, Rothschild, and Schofield. Motion to acknowledge reports 5, 6, and 7 passed unanimously.

### Chapter 50 Nuisance Amendments
The committee reviewed final amendments including tree limb height requirements (14 and 10 feet) that were switched as discussed in previous meetings, plus language requiring compliance with jurisdictional authority. Olsen made motion to recommend approval to board of trustees, seconded by Campkey. Motion passed unanimously.

### E-Bicycle Ordinance
Extensive discussion focused on e-motos, which Chief Hunt characterized as essentially electric dirt bikes that pose safety hazards. The committee debated whether to allow e-motos on roadways, sidewalks, or trails. Chief Hunt recommended prohibiting e-motos entirely on public property, comparing them to unlicensed dirt bikes. Discussion also covered electric scooters and whether exemptions for children's play scooters were needed. Olsen moved to recommend approval with amendment prohibiting e-motos on roadways, sidewalks, trails, and any public property in the village. Seconded by Chwanka. Motion passed unanimously.

### Overnight Parking Regulations
Trustee Tolson presented research showing Weston's 12-hour parking window (4am-4pm) is significantly different from surrounding municipalities, which typically have 5-hour windows overnight. Public Works Director Michael Wodalski explained the daytime restriction helps plow operations but acknowledged most plowing doesn't reach residential streets until 7-8am. Chief Hunt favored keeping overnight restrictions to clear cars before plowing begins. Committee agreed to defer action until July, directing Wodalski to identify 6-8 problem areas that could be specifically signed rather than village-wide restrictions.

### Crosswalk on Alderson Street
Mount Olive Church requested a crosswalk between their facility and DC Everest parking across the street. Committee discussed costs ($2,000-3,000 for basic crosswalk, $25,000-30,000 for lighted system) and whether the village should pay. Chief Hunt reported no incidents in the area. Motion passed directing staff to contact Mount Olive about covering installation costs; if they agree, the crosswalk would be recommended for board approval. Motion by Olsen, seconded by Chwanka, passed unanimously.

### Mobile Food Vendor Fire Inspections
Fire Chief presented new regional fire inspection program involving Wisconsin Rapids, Marshfield, Stevens Point, Plover, Rib Mountain, and Weston. One inspection by any participating department counts for all municipalities. Fire Marshal Marty already conducted an inspection that failed due to improper LP piping and unsecured propane cylinders. Wausau opted out to do their own inspections. Motion by Chwanka to approve Ordinance 26-8, seconded by Campkey. Passed unanimously.

### Address Corrections for E911 Compliance
Staff presented three address corrections: 5915 Delicoski Street (duplex needing separate addresses), Ross Avenue properties out of sequence with existing apartment building, and 2211 Radkey Avenue for new duplex on private drive. Chief Hunt mentioned additional issue with Weston Villa Apartments having nine addresses for one structure. Motion to recommend board approval passed unanimously.

### Raze Order for 4104 Shore Avenue
Property has been abandoned since 2010-2011 and posted since 2021. Previous fire department training burn was canceled due to unsafe structural conditions. Current owners housed chickens in the structure during winter. Owners are current on taxes and preferred the raze order process. Demolition costs will be added to tax roll. Motion by Olsen, seconded by Campkey, passed unanimously.

## Public Comment
Jim Senalt spoke during public comment criticizing the new farmers market location, predicting the park's grassy areas will be destroyed by summer's end. He questioned parking vehicles on grass potentially violating village code, noted ADA handicap spaces are on grass rather than required firm surfaces, questioned the origin of new food truck fire inspections, raised concerns about mobile food vendor regulations on public property, and criticized long-standing code enforcement cases showing no visible progress.
