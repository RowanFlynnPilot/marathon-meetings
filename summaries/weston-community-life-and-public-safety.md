## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Community Life and Public Safety Committee approved several ordinance amendments and addressed multiple community issues including ebike regulations, overnight parking restrictions, mobile food vendor fire inspections, and a demolition order. Key actions included recommending approval of nuisance ordinance amendments, an ebike ordinance with restrictions on emotos, and authorizing demolition of an abandoned structure on Shore Avenue.

## Key Discussions
### Department Reports
Roman/Audrey reported that 3020 Mountain View Avenue will be removed from the code enforcement list after the property sale was completed and the new owner submitted permits for repairs. Chief Hunt reported 2,394 police calls in March (77 per day), 410 traffic stops, and 281 citations issued. The department has hired its 40th officer, with the 38th starting last week, 39th starting in May, and 40th starting around January. Two SROs are assigned to zoning violations during summer. Motion to acknowledge reports 5, 6, and 7 made by Shawanka, seconded by Olsen, passed unanimously.

### Proposed amendments to Chapter 50 nuisances
Committee reviewed final version of nuisance ordinance amendments, including previously discussed changes to tree limb height requirements (14 and 10 feet switched) with compliance requirements for jurisdictions with authority. Motion to recommend approval to board of trustees made by Olsen, seconded by Kimi, passed unanimously.

### Ebike ordinance amendments
Committee discussed proposed ebike regulations. Chief Hunt strongly recommended prohibiting emotos (electric dirt bike-style vehicles) from roadways, sidewalks, trails, and public property due to safety concerns, noting they are essentially unlicensed dirt bikes. Discussion addressed whether electric scooters should be included in restrictions. Committee decided to allow officer discretion for children's play scooters. Motion by Olsen to recommend approval with emotos prohibited from roadways, sidewalks, trails, and public property, seconded by Shawanka, passed unanimously. Rothschild has not yet approved; Schofield indicated they don't feel it's necessary.

### Overnight parking regulations
Brent Tolson presented research showing Weston's 12-hour parking restriction window (4am-4pm) is significantly longer than other municipalities (most have 5-hour windows). Michael from public works explained the daytime restriction was implemented because plowing typically doesn't start until 4am and residential streets aren't reached until 7-8am. Chief Hunt expressed preference for overnight restrictions to give officers time to clear vehicles before plowing. Committee discussed reducing hours and identifying specific problem areas. Decision was made to table the item until July, with Michael tasked to identify approximately 6-8 specific problem areas for targeted signage.

### Crosswalk request on Alderson Street
Mount Olive Church requested a crosswalk between their property and DC Everest school where parishioners park. Michael estimated cost at approximately $2,000 for basic crosswalk, $25,000-30,000 for lighted system. Committee expressed reluctance to set precedent of paying for requested crosswalks. Chief Hunt noted no incidents in the area. Motion by Olsen that if Mount Olive agrees to cover installation costs, the committee recommends board approval, seconded by Shawanka, passed unanimously. Michael will contact Mount Olive.

### Mobile food vendor fire inspections
Chief Hunt explained a new regional fire inspection program involving Wisconsin Rapids, Marshfield, Stevens Point, Plover, Rib Mountain, and Weston. One inspection with a sticker is valid for all participating municipalities. Wausau opted out to do their own inspections. Fire Marshal Marty already inspected one truck that failed due to improper LP piping (galvanized steel) and unsecured propane cylinders. Inspections are free. Motion by Shawanka to approve Ordinance 26-8, seconded by Campkey, passed unanimously.

### Address corrections for E911 compliance
Jen presented three addressing corrections: 5915 Delicoski Street (duplex needing separate addresses, owners agreed); Ross Avenue properties out of sequence near existing apartment building; and 2211 Radkey Avenue for new duplex on private drive. Chief Hunt identified an additional issue at Weston Villa Apartments on Delicoski with nine separate addresses for one structure. Motion made and seconded, passed unanimously.

### Raze order for 4104 Shore Avenue
Structure has been abandoned since 2010-2011 and posted since 2021. Previous fire department training burn was cancelled because structure was too deteriorated for safe exercise. Property owners are current on taxes and have preferred the raze order route. Owners had previously housed chickens inside the structure. Demolition costs will be placed on the tax roll. Motion by Olsen, seconded by Campkey, passed unanimously.

## Public Comment
Jim Senault spoke regarding multiple agenda items. He criticized the new farmers market location at the park, predicting grass destruction by end of summer from vehicle parking. He questioned whether current village code prohibiting parking on grass would apply. He noted ADA handicap spaces are currently on grass rather than adjacent asphalt, which he argued doesn't meet ADA requirements for firm, level surfaces. He questioned the origin of new fire inspections for food trucks. He also expressed concern about accountability for long-standing code violations, stating some cases have been on the list for years with no visible progress.
