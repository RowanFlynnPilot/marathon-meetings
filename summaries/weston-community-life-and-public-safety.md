## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Community Life and Public Safety Committee approved amendments to Chapter 50 nuisances and recommended an e-bike ordinance that prohibits emotos on roadways, sidewalks, and trails. The committee also approved fire inspection requirements for mobile food vendors and authorized demolition proceedings for an abandoned structure at 4104 Shore Avenue.

## Key Discussions
### Public Comments
Jim Senalt spoke regarding several agenda items. He criticized the new farmers market location at the park, predicting the grass would be destroyed by summer's end. He questioned the legality of parking on grass in parks, noted ADA accessibility concerns with handicap parking on grass rather than asphalt, and questioned the need for fire inspections on food trucks. He also expressed concern about accountability on long-standing code violations.

### Department Reports
Code enforcement reported that 3020 Mountain View Avenue will be removed from the code enforcement roll after the property sale was completed and new owner submitted repair permits. Chief Hunt reported the police department had 2,394 calls in March (77 per day), 410 traffic stops, and 281 citations. The department has hired its 40th officer, with the 38th starting last week, 39th starting in May, and 40th around January. Two SROs work on zoning violations during summer for all three municipalities. Motion to acknowledge reports 5, 6, and 7 passed unanimously.

### Chapter 50 Nuisances Amendments
The committee reviewed final amendments including tree limb height requirements over streets and walkways (14 and 10 feet were switched as discussed in previous meeting) and language requiring compliance with jurisdiction authority. Motion by Olsen, seconded by Campkey, to recommend approval to the board of trustees passed unanimously.

### E-Bicycle Ordinance
Chief Hunt recommended prohibiting emotos (electric dirt bike-style vehicles) from roadways, sidewalks, and trails, comparing them to unlicensed dirt bikes. Discussion addressed electric scooters used by children, with the committee agreeing to leave officer discretion for those situations. The committee debated definitions and decided to add language specifically prohibiting emotos on roadways, matching the existing prohibition on sidewalks. Motion by Olsen to recommend approval with the emoto prohibition added, seconded by Sherwanka, passed unanimously.

### Overnight Parking Regulations
Trustee Tolson presented research showing Weston's 12-hour parking restriction (4 AM to 4 PM) is far outside what other municipalities have (most have 5-hour windows). Chief Hunt opposed eliminating overnight parking entirely, noting it gives officers time to clear vehicles before plowing. DPW Director Michael indicated residential streets aren't typically plowed until 7-8 AM anyway. The committee discussed potentially signing specific problem areas (6-8 locations) with even/odd restrictions while using shorter overnight restrictions elsewhere. Item was tabled for Michael to develop a proposal on specific exception areas for the July meeting.

### Crosswalk Request on Alderson Street
Mount Olive Church requested a crosswalk between the church and DC Everest parking lot. Staff estimated a basic crosswalk would cost about $2,000, while a lighted system would be $25,000-$30,000. The committee discussed that no accidents have occurred at the location. Motion by Olsen that if Mount Olive is willing to cover the cost, the committee recommends board approval, seconded by Sherwanka, passed unanimously. Michael will contact Mount Olive to determine their willingness to pay.

### Mobile Food Vendors Fire Inspection
Chief Hunt explained a regional program with Wisconsin Rapids, Marshfield, Stevens Point, Plover, Rib Mountain, and Weston where any participating department's inspection is valid for all jurisdictions. Fire Marshal Marty already inspected one truck that failed due to improper LP piping and unsecured propane cylinders. Wausau opted out to do their own inspections. Motion by Sherwanka to approve Ordinance 26-8, seconded by Campkey, passed unanimously.

### Address Corrections for E911 Compliance
Staff presented three addressing errors: 5915 Delicoski Street (duplex needing two addresses), Ross Avenue addresses out of sequence with a newly discovered apartment unit, and 2211 Radkey Avenue needing correction for a new duplex. Chief Hunt added another property on Delicoski (Weston Villa Apartments) with eight apartments having separate addresses plus a utility room address that needs review. Motion to recommend board approval passed unanimously.

### Raze Order for 4104 Shore Avenue
Staff requested authorization to begin demolition proceedings for an abandoned structure. The property has been abandoned since 2010-2011, was posted since 2021, and a planned fire department training burn was cancelled because the structure was too deteriorated to be safe. The current owners, who are up to date on taxes, previously housed chickens in the structure. Demolition costs will be placed on the tax roll. Motion by Olsen, seconded by Campkey, passed unanimously.

## Public Comment
Jim Senalt spoke for approximately 4 minutes criticizing the new farmers market location, questioning grass parking legality, raising ADA concerns about handicap parking placement on grass, questioning the need for food truck fire inspections, and expressing concern about lack of follow-up on long-standing code violations.
