## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Community Life and Public Safety Committee approved amendments to Chapter 50 nuisances, recommended an e-bike ordinance with emoto restrictions, and authorized razing of an abandoned structure. The committee also discussed overnight parking regulations and a crosswalk request, deferring both items for further staff work.

## Key Discussions
### Public Comments
Jim Senalt addressed the committee with multiple concerns about the new farmers market location at Kennedy Park, stating it was 'a terrible execution' and predicting the grassy areas would be destroyed by summer. He questioned ADA-compliant parking placement on grass rather than asphalt, asked about the origin of new fire inspections for food trucks, and expressed concern about accountability on longstanding code enforcement cases, noting some have been pending for years with no visible progress.

### Department Reports
Code Enforcement reported that 3020 Mountain View Avenue will be removed from the enforcement roll after the property sale and new owner submitted repair permits. Chief Hunt noted the police department had 2,394 calls in March (77 per day), 410 traffic stops, and 281 citations. The department has hired its 40th officer, with the 38th starting last week and others starting in May and January. Two SROs work summer zoning violations for all three municipalities and handle pet licensing compliance. Reports were acknowledged by motion from Sherwanka, seconded by Mosen, passed unanimously.

### Chapter 50 Nuisance Amendments
The committee reviewed final amendments including tree limb height requirements over streets and walkways (switched to 14 and 10 feet as discussed previously) with language about complying with jurisdiction authority. Motion to recommend approval to the board of trustees made by Olsen, seconded by Kimi, passed unanimously.

### E-Bicycle Ordinance
Extensive discussion occurred regarding emotos (electric mini-motorcycles). Chief Hunt strongly advocated prohibiting emotos on roadways and sidewalks, comparing them to unlicensed dirt bikes and citing safety concerns including complaints about people doing wheelies. Committee member Mike raised questions about electric scooters used by middle schoolers. The committee decided to add language prohibiting emotos on roadways, sidewalks, trails, and public property. Motion by Olsen to recommend approval with emoto restrictions, seconded by Sherwanka, passed unanimously. Rothschild has not yet approved the ordinance; Scofield indicated they didn't feel it was necessary.

### Overnight Parking Regulations
Trustee Tolson presented research showing Weston's 12-hour no-parking window (4am-4pm) is significantly longer than surrounding municipalities, which typically use 5-hour windows overnight. Chief Hunt opposed eliminating overnight parking, noting it gives officers time to clear cars before plows arrive. DPW Director Michael Wodalski suggested signing 6-8 specific problem areas rather than village-wide restrictions, noting most residential plowing doesn't begin until 7-8am. The committee deferred action, directing Michael to identify specific exception areas and return in July with recommendations.

### Crosswalk Request on Alderson Street
Mount Olive Church requested a crosswalk between their property and DC Everest school parking lot they use on Sundays. Staff estimated a basic crosswalk would cost approximately $2,000; a lighted system would be $25,000-$30,000. Committee members were reluctant to set precedent of paying for crosswalks on request. Motion by Olsen that if Mount Olive agrees to cover costs, the committee recommends board approval; seconded by Sherwanka, passed unanimously. Michael will contact Mount Olive about cost-sharing.

### Mobile Food Vendors Fire Inspection
Chief Hunt explained a regional fire inspection program involving Wisconsin Rapids, Marshfield, Stevens Point, Plover, Rib Mountain, and Safer where any participating department can inspect food trucks with a sticker valid for all municipalities. Fire inspector Marty already inspected one truck that failed due to galvanized steel LP piping and improperly secured propane containers. Wausau opted out to do their own inspections. Motion by Sherwanka to approve Ordinance 26-8, seconded by Campkey, passed unanimously.

### Address Corrections for E911 Compliance
Staff presented three addressing errors: 5915 Delicoski Street needs split to 5915/5917 as it's a duplex; Ross Avenue addresses need resequencing due to an existing apartment building addressed as 3909; and 2211 Radkey Avenue needs correction for a new duplex on a private drive. Chief Hunt also identified Weston Villa Apartments on Delicowski as having eight separate addresses for one structure. Motion passed unanimously to recommend address corrections to the board.

### Raze Order for 4104 Shore Avenue
Staff requested authorization to begin raze proceedings on an abandoned structure. The property has been abandoned since 2010-2011, posted for years, and was previously considered for a fire department training burn but was deemed too unsafe. Staff discovered the owners had been housing chickens inside during winter. The current owners are up to date on taxes and preferred the raze approach. Demolition costs will be placed on the tax roll. Motion by Olsen, seconded by Campkey, passed unanimously.

## Public Comment
Jim Senalt spoke during public comment, criticizing the new farmers market location at Kennedy Park as 'a terrible execution' that would destroy grassy areas. He raised concerns about ADA parking being placed on grass rather than asphalt, questioned the need for new fire inspections on food trucks, noted ambiguity in food truck regulations for public property, and criticized the lack of accountability on longstanding code enforcement cases.
