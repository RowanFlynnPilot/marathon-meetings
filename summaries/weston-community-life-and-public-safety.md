## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Community Life and Public Safety Committee met on May 4th, taking action on several ordinance amendments and property matters. Key decisions included recommending approval of nuisance code amendments, an e-bike ordinance with added emoto restrictions, mobile food vendor fire inspection requirements, address corrections, and a raze order for an abandoned structure on Shore Avenue.

## Key Discussions
### Public Comments
Jim Senalt spoke critically about the new farmers market location, expressing concern that parking vehicles on grass violates village code and will destroy the park. He questioned ADA compliance for handicap spaces on grass rather than asphalt, asked about the origin of fire inspections for food trucks, and criticized the age of pending code violations, stating some have been on the list for years with no visible progress.

### Department Reports
Code Enforcement reported 3020 Mountain View Avenue will be removed from the code enforcement roll after property sale and new owner submitted repair permits. Police Chief Hunt reported 2,394 calls for March (77 per day), 410 traffic stops, and 281 citations issued. The department has hired their 40th officer, with the 38th starting last week and 39th starting in May. Two SROs work summer zoning violations for Weston, Rothschild, and Schofield. Motion to acknowledge reports 5, 6, and 7 passed unanimously.

### Chapter 50 Nuisances Amendments
Committee discussed final copy of nuisance ordinance amendments including tree limb height requirements over streets and walkways (switched to 14 and 10 feet). Motion by Olsen, seconded by Kimpie to recommend approval to board of trustees passed unanimously.

### E-Bicycle Ordinance
Extensive discussion on proposed e-bike ordinance for Chapter 82. Chief Hunt strongly recommended prohibiting emotos (electric dirt bike-type vehicles) from roadways and sidewalks, comparing them to unlicensed dirt bikes. Committee debated electric scooters, with concerns about restricting kids' transportation. Chief noted officers need clear guidelines and currently have no ordinance to enforce. Motion by Olsen to recommend approval with addition prohibiting emotos from roadways, sidewalks, trails, and public property passed unanimously.

### Overnight Parking Regulations
Trustee Tolson initiated discussion noting Weston's 12-hour parking restriction window (4 AM to 4 PM) is far outside other municipalities' 5-hour windows. Public Works Director Michael indicated removing overnight parking entirely and signing only problem areas (6-8 spots) might work better. Chief Hunt preferred keeping overnight restrictions to give officers time to clear vehicles before plowing. Committee decided to table the item until July to allow Michael to identify specific problem areas needing signage.

### Crosswalk on Alderson Street
Mount Olive Church requested crosswalk between church and DC Everest high school parking lot. Staff estimated basic crosswalk cost at $2,000, lighted system at $25-30,000. Committee expressed reluctance to set precedent of paying for requested crosswalks. Motion by Olsen, seconded by Chwanka to recommend approval if Mount Olive covers installation costs passed unanimously.

### Mobile Food Vendor Fire Inspections
Chief Hunt presented regional fire inspection program involving Wisconsin Rapids, Marshfield, Stevens Point, Plover, Rib Mountain, and proposed Weston participation. Inspected trucks receive sticker valid across all participating municipalities. Fire Marshal Marty already conducted one inspection that failed due to improper LP piping. Wausau declined to participate. Motion by Chwanka, seconded by Kimpie to approve Ordinance 26-8 passed unanimously.

### Address Corrections
Staff presented three address corrections for E911 compliance: 5915 Delicoski Street (duplex needing separate addresses), Ross Avenue properties out of sequence, and 2211 Radkey Avenue new duplex on private drive. Chief Hunt mentioned additional issue at Weston Villa Apartments with eight separate addresses for one structure. Motion passed unanimously to recommend approval.

### Raze Request - 4104 Shore Avenue
Staff requested authorization to begin raze order on abandoned structure. Property abandoned since 2010-2011, with chickens previously housed inside. Fire department declined training burn due to unsafe conditions. Current owners prefer village proceed with demolition; costs will be placed on tax roll. Motion by Olsen, seconded by Kimpie passed unanimously.

## Public Comment
Jim Senalt provided extensive public comment criticizing the new farmers market location for parking vehicles on grass, questioning ADA compliance of handicap spaces on grass, asking about the origin of food truck fire inspections, and expressing concern about the age of pending code violations on the enforcement list.
