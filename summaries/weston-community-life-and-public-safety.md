## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Community Life and Public Safety Committee approved amendments to the nuisance ordinance and e-bike regulations, discussed overnight parking regulations without taking action, and authorized razing a condemned structure. The committee also approved fire inspection requirements for mobile food vendors and addressed several property addressing corrections.

## Key Discussions
### Department Reports
Code Enforcement Officer Audrey reported that 3020 Mountain View Avenue will be removed from the code enforcement roll after the property sale was completed and new owner submitted repair permits. Chief Hunt noted the police department had 2,394 calls in March (77 per day), 410 traffic stops, and 281 citations. The department has hired its 40th officer. Two SROs work with zoning violations during summer. Motion to acknowledge reports 5, 6, and 7 passed unanimously.

### Chapter 50 Nuisance Amendments
The committee reviewed final amendments to the nuisance ordinance, including previously discussed changes to tree limb height requirements (14 and 10 feet) and compliance with jurisdictional authority. Olsen made a motion to recommend approval to the board of trustees, seconded by Campkey. Motion passed unanimously.

### E-Bicycle Ordinance
Committee discussed the model e-bike ordinance for Chapter 82. Chief Hunt recommended prohibiting emotos (electric dirt bike-style vehicles) from roadways and sidewalks due to safety concerns, noting they are essentially unlicensed dirt bikes. Discussion included whether electric scooters used by children should be treated differently. Olsen motioned to recommend approval with the addition that emotos are prohibited from operating on roadways, sidewalks, trails, or any public property. Seconded by Chwanka. Motion passed unanimously.

### Overnight Parking Regulations
Trustee Tolson presented research showing Weston's 12-hour no parking window (4 AM to 4 PM, October 31-May 1) is significantly longer than surrounding municipalities which typically have 4-5 hour windows overnight. DPW Director Michael suggested possibly eliminating the broad restriction and instead signing only the 6-8 problem areas. Chief Hunt advocated keeping overnight restrictions to give officers time to clear vehicles before plowing. Committee directed Michael to develop specific exception areas for the July meeting; no action taken.

### Crosswalk Request on Alderson Street
Mount Olive Church requested a crosswalk between the church and DC Everest High School parking lot. Staff estimated cost at approximately $2,000-3,000 for a basic crosswalk or $25,000-30,000 for a lighted system. Committee members expressed reluctance to set precedent of paying for crosswalks on request. Olsen motioned that if Mount Olive is willing to cover the installation cost, the committee recommends board approval. Seconded by Chwanka. Motion passed unanimously. DPW Director will contact Mount Olive regarding cost sharing.

### Mobile Food Vendors Fire Inspection
Fire Chief explained a new regional fire inspection program with Wisconsin Rapids, Marshfield, Stevens Point, Plover, Rib Mountain, and SAFER. One inspection and sticker is valid across all participating municipalities. Chief noted a recent inspection found significant safety violations including improper LP gas piping. Wausau opted not to participate. Chwanka motioned to approve Ordinance 26-8; seconded by Campkey. Motion passed unanimously.

### Address Corrections
Staff presented three address corrections for E911 compliance: 5915 Delicowski Street (duplex needing split addresses), Ross Avenue properties out of sequence, and 2211 Radkey Avenue near a new duplex. Chief Hunt noted an additional issue at Weston Villa Apartments on Delicowski with eight separate addresses for one structure. Motion to recommend approval passed unanimously.

### Raze Request for 4104 Shore Avenue
Staff requested authorization to begin raze order on a structure abandoned since 2010-2011. The property was previously considered for fire department training burn but was deemed too deteriorated for safe exercise. Current owners have been housing chickens in the condemned structure. Owners prefer the village proceed with demolition. Costs will be placed on the tax roll. Olsen motioned, Campkey seconded. Motion passed unanimously.

## Public Comment
Jim Senalt spoke regarding multiple agenda items. He criticized the new farmers market location at Kennedy Park, predicting the grassy areas will be destroyed by vehicles parking on grass, which he believes violates village code. He questioned whether ADA-accessible parking spaces on grass comply with requirements for firm, level surfaces. He asked where the food truck fire inspection requirement originated. He also questioned the clarity of mobile food vendor regulations regarding farmers market days and expressed concern that some code enforcement cases have been pending for years without visible progress.
