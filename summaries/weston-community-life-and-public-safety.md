## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Community Life and Public Safety Committee approved amendments to Chapter 50 nuisances, recommended an e-bike ordinance with added emoto restrictions, and authorized a raze order for a condemned property at 4104 Shore Avenue. The committee also adopted fire inspection requirements for mobile food vendors and discussed but deferred action on overnight parking regulations.

## Key Discussions
### Public Comments
Jim Senalt spoke about concerns with the new farmers market location at the park, stating it was 'a terrible execution' and predicting grass damage from vehicle parking. He questioned whether parking on grass violates village code, noted ADA accessible spaces should be on asphalt rather than grass, questioned the need for new fire inspections on food trucks, and expressed concern about old code enforcement cases lacking accountability.

### Department Reports
Code Enforcement Officer Audrey reported that 3020 Mountain View Avenue will be removed from the code enforcement roll after the property sale was completed and new owner submitted repair permits. Chief Hunt reported the police department had 2,394 calls in March (77 per day) and 410 traffic stops with 281 citations issued. The department has hired its 40th officer. Motion to acknowledge reports 5, 6, and 7 was made by Chwanka and passed unanimously.

### Chapter 50 Nuisances Amendments
The committee reviewed final amendments including tree limb height requirements of 14 and 10 feet over streets and walkways, with language added requiring compliance with applicable jurisdiction authority. Motion to recommend approval to the board was made by Olsen, seconded by Campkey, and passed unanimously.

### E-Bicycle Ordinance
Committee discussed the model e-bike ordinance with significant debate about emotos (electric dirt bikes). Chief Hunt strongly advocated prohibiting emotos on roadways and sidewalks, stating they are 'exactly the same as a dirt bike except for electric' and pose safety hazards. Discussion also covered electric scooters used by middle schoolers. Motion by Olsen to recommend approval with amendment prohibiting emotos on roadways, sidewalks, trails, and public property was seconded by Chwanka and passed unanimously.

### Overnight Parking Regulations
Trustee Tolson presented research showing Weston's 12-hour parking window (4am-4pm) is far outside other municipalities' 3.5-5 hour windows. Public Works Director Michael Wodalski noted they rarely plow before 4-5am and suggested eliminating overnight parking restrictions except for signed problem areas. Chief Hunt opposed eliminating restrictions, preferring overnight enforcement to clear cars before morning plowing. Committee deferred action, asking Wodalski to identify specific problem areas for the July meeting.

### Crosswalk Request on Alderson Street
Mount Olive Church requested a crosswalk between the church and DC Everest High School. Staff estimated cost at $2,000 for basic crosswalk or $25,000-$30,000 for lighted system. Committee members were reluctant to pay for a crosswalk benefiting a private entity. Motion by Olsen that if Mount Olive is willing to cover installation costs, recommend board approval. Seconded by Chwanka and passed unanimously.

### Mobile Food Vendors Fire Inspection
Chief Hunt explained a new regional fire inspection program with Wisconsin Rapids, Marshfield, Stevens Point, Plover, Rib Mountain, and SAFER where any participating department can inspect food trucks and apply a sticker valid for all municipalities. He noted a recent inspection failed a vendor with improper LP piping and unsecured propane cylinders. Motion by Chwanka to approve Ordinance 26-8, seconded by Campkey, passed unanimously.

### Address Corrections
Staff presented three addressing errors for E911 compliance: 5915 Delicoski Street (duplex needing separate addresses), 3913/3917 Ross Avenue (out of sequence with apartment building), and 2211 Radkey Avenue (new duplex on private drive). Chief Hunt also identified Weston Villa Apartments on Delicoski with eight separate addresses for one structure. Motion to recommend approval passed unanimously.

### Raze Order for 4104 Shore Avenue
Staff requested authorization for raze order on a structure abandoned since 2010-2011, posted since 2021. A planned fire department training burn was cancelled because the structure was too deteriorated for safe exercise. Staff discovered chickens being housed inside during a previous investigation. Current owners prefer the raze order route with costs added to tax roll. Motion by Olsen, seconded by Campkey, passed unanimously.

## Public Comment
Jim Senalt spoke expressing concerns about the new farmers market location, stating it was a 'terrible execution' and predicting damage to park grass from vehicle parking. He questioned ADA compliance of accessible parking on grass, asked about the origin of new food truck fire inspections, raised questions about food truck permitting on public property, and criticized the length of time some code enforcement cases have remained unresolved.
