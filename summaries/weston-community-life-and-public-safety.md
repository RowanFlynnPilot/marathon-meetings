## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Community Life and Public Safety Committee met on May 4th, taking action on several ordinance amendments and receiving department reports. Key decisions included recommending approval of amendments to Chapter 50 nuisances, approving an e-bike ordinance with restrictions on e-motos, approving fire code inspections for mobile food vendors, and authorizing a raze order for an abandoned structure at 4104 Shore Avenue.

## Key Discussions
### Department Reports
Code enforcement officer Audrey reported that 3020 Mountain View Avenue will be removed from the code enforcement roll after the property sold at auction and the new owner submitted repair permits. She noted some enforcement-suspended cases may move back to pending status as construction season begins. Chief Hunt reported the police department had 2,394 calls in March (77 per day), 410 traffic stops, and 281 citations. The department has hired its 40th officer, with the 38th starting last week and others coming in May and January. Two SROs work on zoning violations and pet licensing during summer. Motion by Chwanka, second by Olsen to acknowledge reports 5, 6, and 7 passed unanimously.

### Chapter 50 Nuisances Amendments
Brent Tolson confirmed the amendments had been covered in past meetings and this was the final copy. The tree limb heights over streets and walkways were switched to 14 and 10 feet as previously discussed, with language added requiring compliance with the jurisdiction having authority. Motion by Olsen, second by Campkey to recommend approval to the board of trustees passed unanimously.

### E-bike Ordinance
Discussion focused heavily on e-motos (electric dirt bikes). Chief Hunt strongly advocated prohibiting e-motos on roadways and sidewalks, comparing them to unlicensed dirt bikes and citing safety concerns including people doing wheelies past pedestrians. Committee debated whether electric scooters used by middle schoolers should also be restricted. Olsen noted he preferred passing a starting ordinance rather than over-regulating. Motion by Olsen, second by Chwanka to recommend approval with the addition that e-motos are prohibited from operating on roadways, sidewalks, trails, or any public property passed unanimously.

### Overnight Parking Regulations
Trustee Tolson presented research showing Weston's 12-hour parking restriction (4am-4pm) is far longer than surrounding municipalities which typically have 5-hour windows overnight. Chief Hunt expressed opposition to eliminating overnight parking, saying it gives officers time to clear vehicles before plows arrive. Michael (Public Works) noted they rarely plow before 4-5am and suggested signing specific problem areas instead. Committee discussed uniformity with other municipalities and reducing hours. No action taken; Michael was asked to identify specific exception areas to bring back in July.

### Crosswalk Request on Alderson Street
Mount Olive Church requested a crosswalk between the church and DC Everest high school parking lot, which church members use for Sunday parking. Michael estimated basic crosswalk cost at $2,000, lighted system at $25-30,000. Committee expressed reluctance to set precedent of paying for private entity requests. Motion by Olsen, second by Chwanka to recommend board approval contingent on Mount Olive Church covering the installation cost passed unanimously.

### Mobile Food Vendors Fire Inspection
Fire Chief explained a regional consortium including Wisconsin Rapids, Marshfield, Stevens Point, Clover, Riverside, and Safer has formed to share food truck fire inspections - one inspection with sticker is valid for all participating municipalities. City of Wausau opted out to do their own. Chief noted one recent inspection failed due to improper LP piping and unsecured propane containers. Motion by Chwanka, second by Campkey to approve Ordinance 26-8 passed unanimously.

### Address Corrections
Staff presented three addressing errors needing correction: 5915 Delicoski Street (duplex needing second address), Ross Avenue properties out of sequence due to discovered apartment unit, and 2211 Radkey Avenue needing even number assignment. Chief Hunt added another issue at Weston Villa Apartments on Delicoski with eight units having separate addresses. Motion to recommend approval to board of trustees passed unanimously.

### Raze Request for 4104 Shore Avenue
Structure has been abandoned since 2010-2011 and was deemed too deteriorated for fire department training burn. Owners had housed chickens inside during winter. Current owners prefer the village proceed with demolition, with costs added to tax roll. Motion by Olsen, second by Campkey to recommend board authorization for raze order passed unanimously.

## Public Comment
Jim Senalt spoke regarding several agenda items. He criticized the new farmers market location at the park, predicting grass destruction from vehicle parking and questioning whether current village code allows parking on grass. He raised ADA concerns about handicap spaces signed on grass rather than asphalt, noting wheelchair operation is difficult on wet, sandy soil. He questioned the need for new fire inspections on food trucks when no trucks have burned down. He noted the farmers market moving to public property changes food truck permitting dynamics and the ordinance language is unclear about which days restrictions apply. Finally, he criticized accountability on long-standing code violations, stating some cases have been pending for years with no visible progress.
