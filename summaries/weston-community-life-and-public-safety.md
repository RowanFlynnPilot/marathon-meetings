## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Community Life and Public Safety Committee approved amendments to Chapter 50 nuisances, recommended an e-bike ordinance with added emoto restrictions, discussed overnight parking regulations without taking action, conditionally approved a crosswalk near Mount Olive Church, adopted fire inspection requirements for mobile food vendors, approved address corrections, and authorized demolition of an abandoned structure. A resident raised concerns about the new farmers market location and various code enforcement issues.

## Key Discussions
### Department Reports
Code Enforcement Officer Audrey noted that 3020 Mountain View Avenue will be removed from the enforcement list after the property sold at auction and the new owner submitted repair permits. Chief Hunt reported the police department had 2,394 calls in March (77 per day), issued 281 citations, and made 410 traffic stops. The department has hired its 40th officer and during summer, two SROs are assigned to zoning violations and pet licensing enforcement. Motion to acknowledge reports passed unanimously.

### Chapter 50 Nuisance Amendments
The committee finalized amendments that had been discussed in previous meetings, including switching tree limb height requirements to 14 and 10 feet over streets and walkways, and adding language that properties must comply with the jurisdiction that has authority. Motion by Olsen, seconded by Campkey, to recommend approval to the Board of Trustees passed unanimously.

### E-Bicycle Ordinance
The committee discussed the model e-bike ordinance for Chapter 82. Chief Hunt strongly recommended prohibiting emotos (electric dirt bike-style vehicles) from roadways, sidewalks, trails, and public property due to safety concerns, comparing them to unlicensed dirt bikes. The committee debated whether to include exemptions for children's electric scooters but decided to rely on officer discretion. Motion by Olsen, seconded by Chwanka, to recommend approval with the addition of emoto restrictions passed unanimously. Rothschild has not yet approved theirs, Schofield declined, and Wausau is making adjustments.

### Overnight Parking Regulations
Trustee Tolson presented research showing Weston's 12-hour no parking window (4am-4pm) is significantly longer than surrounding municipalities, which typically have 4-5 hour windows overnight. Michael Wodalski from Public Works suggested the even/odd restrictions help with daytime snow removal but acknowledged most plowing doesn't reach residential streets until 7-8am anyway. Chief Hunt preferred keeping overnight restrictions to give officers time to clear cars before morning plowing. The committee discussed potentially having six to eight problem areas signed specifically. No action was taken; Michael will prepare specific exception locations for the July meeting.

### Crosswalk on Alderson Street
Mount Olive Church requested a crosswalk between their property and DC Everest school district parking. The committee discussed that church services generate significant traffic but no accidents have been reported. Staff estimated a basic crosswalk would cost approximately $2,000, while a lighted system would be $25,000-$30,000. Motion by Olsen, seconded by Chwanka, to recommend approval to the board contingent on Mount Olive covering the installation costs passed unanimously. Michael Wodalski will contact Mount Olive to determine their willingness to pay.

### Mobile Food Vendor Fire Inspections
Fire Marshal Marty presented a regional fire inspection program involving Wisconsin Rapids, Marshfield, Stevens Point, Plover, Rib Mountain, and SAFER. Vendors would be inspected once annually and receive a sticker valid for all participating municipalities, eliminating duplicate inspections. Chief Hunt noted that Wausau declined to participate, preferring their own system. Fire Marshal Marty already performed one inspection that failed due to improper LP piping and unsecured propane cylinders. Motion by Chwanka, seconded by Campkey, to approve Ordinance 26-8 passed unanimously.

### Address Corrections for E911 Compliance
Staff presented three address correction requests: 5915 Delicoski Street (duplex needing separate addresses, owners already agreed), 3913/3917 Ross Avenue (out of sequence addresses with apartment discovered during sale), and 2211 Radkey Avenue (new duplex on private drive with incorrectly sequenced apartment complex). Chief Hunt also identified Weston Villa Apartments on Delicoski as having addressing issues with eight separate addresses for one structure. Motion to recommend approval passed unanimously.

### Raze Order for 4104 Shore Avenue
Staff requested authorization to begin razing an abandoned structure that has been vacant since 2010-2011 and posted since 2021. A previous fire department training burn was cancelled because the structure was too deteriorated to be safe. Staff discovered chickens were being housed inside during winter. The current owners prefer the raze order process, and demolition costs will be added to their tax roll. The property owners are current on taxes. Motion by Olsen, seconded by Campkey, to recommend authorization passed unanimously.

## Public Comment
Jim Senalt spoke expressing concerns about the new farmers market location, predicting the park's grassy areas will be destroyed by parking vehicles on grass. He questioned whether current village code prohibits grass parking and suggested ADA-compliant parking spaces should be on asphalt rather than grass. He also questioned the origin of new fire inspections for food trucks, asked about food truck permitting on public property, and criticized the age of some code enforcement cases, stating some have shown no visible progress after a year.
