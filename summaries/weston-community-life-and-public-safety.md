## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Community Life and Public Safety Committee met on May 4th, approving amendments to chapter 50 nuisances and an e-bike ordinance (with added emoto restrictions), recommending fire inspections for mobile food vendors, and authorizing a raze order for an abandoned structure at 4104 Shore Avenue. The committee also discussed overnight parking regulations but deferred action pending staff research.

## Key Discussions
### Public Comments
Jim Senalt raised concerns about the new farmers market location at a village park, stating parking vehicles on grass violates village code and ADA-compliant handicapped parking should be on asphalt, not grass. He questioned the new fire inspection requirement for food trucks and noted some code enforcement cases have been pending for years without visible progress.

### Department Reports
Code Enforcement reported 3020 Mountain View Avenue will be removed from the code enforcement roll after property sale and new owner submitted repair permits. Police Chief Hunt reported 2,394 calls in March (77 per day), 410 traffic stops, and 281 citations. The department has hired its 40th officer. Fire/EMS had no additions. Reports were acknowledged unanimously on motion by Chwanka, seconded by Olsen.

### Chapter 50 Nuisances Amendments
The committee completed review of nuisance ordinance amendments, including switching tree limb height requirements to 14 and 10 feet over streets and walkways. Motion by Olsen, seconded by Kimpel, to recommend approval to the board passed unanimously.

### E-Bicycle Ordinance
Extensive discussion on regulating e-bikes, e-motos, and electric scooters. Chief Hunt strongly advocated prohibiting e-motos on roadways and sidewalks, comparing them to unlicensed dirt bikes. Committee debated whether to also restrict electric scooters used by children. Motion by Olsen, seconded by Chwanka, to recommend approval with added language prohibiting e-motos on roadways, sidewalks, trails, and public property passed unanimously.

### Overnight Parking Regulations
Trustee Tolson presented research showing Weston's 12-hour parking restriction (4am-4pm) is significantly longer than surrounding municipalities (typically 4-5 hours). Public Works Director Michael suggested either eliminating overnight parking restrictions entirely or signing specific problem areas. Chief Hunt preferred maintaining overnight restrictions for snow removal efficiency. Committee deferred action, directing Michael to identify specific problem areas for the July meeting.

### Alderson Street Crosswalk Request
Mount Olive Church requested a crosswalk between the church and DC Everest parking lot. Staff estimated $2,000-3,000 for basic crosswalk, $25,000-30,000 for lighted system. Committee discussed church traffic patterns and safety. Motion by Olsen, seconded by Chwanka, to recommend board approval contingent on Mount Olive covering installation costs passed unanimously.

### Mobile Food Vendors Fire Inspection
Fire Chief explained new regional fire inspection program with Wisconsin Rapids, Marshfield, Stevens Point, Plover, Rib Mountain, and Safer. One inspection sticker is valid across all participating municipalities. Wausau opted out to maintain their own system. Chief noted a recent inspection failed due to improper propane piping and unsecured LP containers. Motion by Chwanka, seconded by Kimpel, to approve ordinance 26-8 passed unanimously.

### Address Corrections for E911
Staff presented three addressing corrections needed for duplexes and out-of-sequence addresses on Delicowski Street, Ross Avenue, and Radkey Avenue. Chief Hunt mentioned an additional issue at Weston Villa Apartments with nine separate addresses for one structure. Motion passed unanimously to recommend board approval.

### Raze Order for 4104 Shore Avenue
Structure has been abandoned since 2010-2011 and posted since 2021. Fire department previously declined to do a training burn due to unsafe conditions. Owners are current on taxes and had previously housed chickens inside the structure. Demolition costs will be added to tax roll. Motion by Olsen, seconded by Kimpel, to authorize raze order passed unanimously.

## Public Comment
Jim Senalt spoke during public comment raising multiple concerns: the new farmers market location will damage park grass, ADA parking should be on asphalt not grass, questioned the need for new food truck fire inspections, noted ambiguity in mobile food vendor ordinance language, and criticized slow progress on long-standing code enforcement cases.
