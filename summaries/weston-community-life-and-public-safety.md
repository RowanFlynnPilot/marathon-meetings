## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Community Life and Public Safety Committee approved amendments to the nuisance ordinance, recommended a new e-bike ordinance with restrictions on emotos, and authorized a raze order for a dilapidated structure at 4104 Shore Avenue. The committee also discussed overnight parking regulations and a crosswalk request, deferring both for further staff work.

## Key Discussions
### Code enforcement, Fire/EMS, and Police department reports
Staff presented departmental reports. Roman noted 3020 Mountain View Avenue will be removed from the code enforcement roll after the property sold at auction and the new owner submitted repair permits. Chief Hunt reported the police department had 2,394 calls in March (77 per day), issued 281 citations, and conducted 410 traffic stops. The department has hired its 40th officer. Motion to acknowledge reports passed unanimously.

### Proposed amendments to Chapter 50 nuisances
The committee reviewed final amendments to the nuisance ordinance, including changes to tree limb height requirements over streets and walkways (14 and 10 feet were switched per previous discussion). Olsen made a motion to recommend approval to the Board of Trustees, seconded by Campkey. Motion passed unanimously.

### E-bike ordinance in Chapter 82
The committee discussed a model e-bike ordinance for multiple municipalities. Chief Hunt strongly recommended prohibiting emotos (electric dirt bikes) on roadways and sidewalks due to safety concerns, comparing them to unlicensed dirt bikes. Discussion included whether to exempt children's electric scooters. The committee decided to add language prohibiting emotos on roadways, sidewalks, trails, and public property. Olsen motioned to recommend approval with the emoto prohibition added, seconded by Sherwanka. Motion passed unanimously.

### Overnight parking regulations October 31st to May 1st
Trustee Tolson presented research showing Weston's 12-hour overnight parking window (4am-4pm) is significantly longer than surrounding municipalities (typically 5 hours or less). Public Works Director Michael suggested either eliminating the overnight restriction entirely and signing specific problem areas, or matching Rothschild's approach. Chief Hunt preferred keeping overnight restrictions for enforcement purposes. The committee decided to defer to July, asking Michael to identify the 6-8 specific problem areas that need signage.

### Crosswalk request on Alderson Street between Mount Olive Church and DC Everest
The committee discussed a request for a crosswalk where church members cross from street parking to Mount Olive Church. A basic crosswalk would cost approximately $2,000-$3,000; a lighted system would cost $25,000-$30,000. The committee was reluctant to set precedent of paying for crosswalks on request. Olsen motioned to recommend approval to the board if Mount Olive agrees to cover the installation cost, seconded by Sherwanka. Motion passed unanimously. Michael will contact the church.

### Mobile food vendors fire inspection amendment
Fire Marshal Marty proposed joining a regional fire inspection program with Wisconsin Rapids, Marshfield, Stevens Point, Plover, Rib Mountain, and Weston. Food trucks inspected by any participating department receive a sticker valid for all jurisdictions. Chief noted one recent inspection failed due to improper LP gas piping and unsecured propane containers. Wausau opted out to do their own inspections. Sherwanka motioned to approve Ordinance 26-8, seconded by Campkey. Motion passed unanimously.

### Address corrections for E911 compliance
Staff presented three addressing errors: a duplex at 5915 Delicoski Street needing separate addresses, out-of-sequence addresses on Ross Avenue requiring renumbering, and a new duplex on Radkey Avenue. Chief Hunt also identified Weston Villa Apartments on Delicoski as having nine separate addresses for one eight-unit structure. Motion to recommend address corrections passed unanimously.

### Raze request for 4104 Shore Avenue
Staff requested authorization to begin raze proceedings on an abandoned structure from 2010-2011. A previous fire training burn was cancelled because the structure was too deteriorated for safe training. The property owners are current on taxes and have agreed to the demolition, with costs to be added to their tax roll. Olsen motioned to recommend authorization, seconded by Campkey. Motion passed unanimously.

## Public Comment
Jim Senalt spoke regarding several agenda items. He expressed concern that the new farmers market location at the park will destroy grassy areas by allowing vehicle parking on grass, and questioned whether this violates village code. He noted ADA-compliant handicap parking should be on firm surfaces rather than grass. He questioned the origin of the new fire inspection requirement for mobile food vendors. He also raised concerns about code enforcement cases, noting some have been pending for years with no visible progress, and requested periodic status updates to the committee.
