## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Plan Commission approved a site plan for the new Mountain Bay Metropolitan Police Department and Municipal Court facility at 3600 Community Drive, and adopted a new salt storage shed inspection program for wellhead protection areas. Both items passed unanimously with all commissioners present voting in favor.

## Key Discussions
### Mountain Bay Metro Police Department and Municipal Court facility site plan
Staff presented the site plan for the new police department and municipal court at 3600 Community Center Drive. The project meets all B2 district zoning requirements. Police Chief Jeremy explained the building was rotated to face east due to a gas pipeline easement running under the parking lot, which significantly reduced fill costs. The building will accommodate 50-60 officers (current staffing is 40 officers plus 4 clerical staff) with potential for future expansion to the north. An alternate storage shed is proposed but may be built later depending on bid costs. Commissioner Garant moved to recommend approval subject to staff conditions 2-5. Motion passed unanimously.

### Salt storage shed inspection program for Wellhead Protection District
Staff proposed a biannual inspection program for salt storage facilities within wellhead protection areas - one inspection in warm weather focusing on structural integrity and one in winter focusing on operational practices. The program uses a standardized inspection worksheet similar to DOT requirements. Staff noted one existing facility near the Sternberg Road well has never gone through the wellhead protection conditional use process and they will reach out to bring it into compliance. Motion to approve the program with reporting back to both Plan Commission and Public Works Committee passed unanimously.

### April 2026 meeting minutes
Motion to approve the April 13, 2026 meeting minutes as presented. Passed unanimously without discussion.

### Monthly project update and future items
Staff announced the first joint Plan Commission/Village Board steering committee meeting for the comprehensive plan is scheduled for Monday, June 1st at 5:00 p.m. Discussion arose about potential residential TIF districts for workforce housing, with staff noting Ehlers has prepared a presentation on qualifications that could be shared with commissioners or incorporated into comprehensive plan discussions.

## Public Comment
Jim Pensel of 5002 Arrow Street, Weston, spoke via remote connection. He urged the commission to exercise common sense regarding landscaping requirements for the police department project, asking that taxpayer dollars not be spent on excessive landscaping points similar to the Green Heck Turner building. He also requested that the salt shed inspection program include annual reporting on site visits and findings, noting some zoning complaints are years old and get 'lost in the sauce.'
