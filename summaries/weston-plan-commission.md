## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Plan Commission approved the site plan for the new Mountain Bay Metropolitan Police Department and Municipal Court facility at 3600 Community Center Drive, and adopted a new salt storage shed inspection program for wellhead protection areas. Both items passed unanimously with all members present voting in favor.

## Key Discussions
### Site plan review - Mountain Bay Metro Police Department and Municipal Court facility
Staff member Jen presented the site plan for the new police department and municipal court at 3600 Community Center Drive, noting it meets all B2 zoning requirements. Police Chief Jeremy explained challenges with a gas pipeline easement that required rotating the building to face east and utilizing higher ground to reduce fill costs. The building has capacity for 50-60 officers, up from current staffing of 40. Discussion included a potential storage shed alternate, landscaping compliance with current code, and future expansion possibilities to the north. A motion to recommend approval to the board of trustees with staff conditions 2-5 passed unanimously.

### Salt storage shed inspection program for wellhead protection district
Staff proposed a program with two annual inspections per facility - a warm weather inspection focused on structural integrity and drainage, and a winter operational inspection focused on material handling and containment. A standardized inspection worksheet similar to DOT standards was developed. Staff noted one existing facility near Sternberg has never gone through the wellhead protection conditional use process and is closest to the municipal well. Commission agreed staff should reach out to that facility about compliance. Motion to approve the program with reporting back to both Plan Commission and Public Works passed unanimously.

### April 2026 minutes and staff reports
Minutes from April 13, 2026 were approved unanimously. April 2026 staff approved certified survey maps, site plans, and building permits were all approved unanimously without discussion.

### Planning department monthly update
Staff announced the first joint Plan Commission and Village Board steering committee meeting for the comprehensive plan is scheduled for Monday, June 1st at 5:00 p.m. Staff will send calendar invites. Discussion also touched on potential residential TIF districts for workforce housing, with Ehlers having prepared a presentation that may be shared with the commission or incorporated into comprehensive plan discussions.

## Public Comment
Jim Pensel of 5002 Arrow Street, Weston, spoke via online connection. He urged the commission to exercise common sense regarding landscaping requirements for the police department project, referencing previous flexibility given to Green Heck Turner, to avoid unnecessary taxpayer expense. He also requested that the salt shed inspection program include annual reporting to track compliance visits at the limited number of sites in wellhead protection zones.
