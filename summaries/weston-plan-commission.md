## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Plan Commission approved the site plan for the new Mountain Bay Metropolitan Police Department and Municipal Court facility at 3600 Community Center Drive, and adopted a salt storage shed inspection program for wellhead protection areas. Both items passed unanimously with staff conditions.

## Key Discussions
### Mountain Bay Metro Police Department and Municipal Court facility site plan
Staff presented the site plan for the new police department and municipal court at 3600 Community Center Drive. Jen from staff explained the project meets all B2 zoning requirements and landscaping code. Chief Jeremy explained the building was rotated to face east due to a 36-inch gas pipeline running under the parking lot, which significantly reduced fill costs. The building will accommodate 50-60 officers compared to current staffing of 40, with expansion capability to the north. Discussion included the alternate storage shed placement to allow for future building expansion. Motion to approve with staff conditions 2-5 passed unanimously.

### Salt storage shed inspection program for wellhead protection district
Staff proposed a two-inspection-per-year program for salt storage facilities in wellhead protection areas - one warm weather inspection focusing on structural integrity and one winter operational inspection. The program includes a standardized inspection worksheet similar to DOT standards and a classification system for deficiencies. Staff noted one existing facility near the village well on Sternberg has never gone through the wellhead protection conditional use process and will be contacted. Joel Garant commended the program. Motion to approve with reporting to both Plan Commission and Public Works Committee passed unanimously.

### April 2026 staff approved certified survey map and site plan
Motion to approve passed unanimously with no discussion.

### April 2026 building permit report
Motion to approve passed unanimously with no discussion.

### Planning department monthly update
Staff announced the first meeting of the joint Plan Commission-Village Board steering committee for the comprehensive plan update is scheduled for Monday, June 1st at 5:00 p.m. Staff will send calendar invitations. Discussion touched on potential residential TIF districts for workforce housing, with Ehlers having prepared a presentation that may be shared with the commission or incorporated into comprehensive plan discussions.

## Public Comment
Jim Pensel of 5002 Arrow Street, Weston, spoke via online participation. He urged the commission to exercise common sense on landscaping requirements for the police department project to avoid excessive taxpayer costs, referencing relaxed standards applied to the Green Heck Turner building. He also requested the commission require annual reporting on the salt shed inspection program to ensure oversight doesn't get 'lost in the sauce' like some other zoning complaints.
