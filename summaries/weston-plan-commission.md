## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Plan Commission approved the site plan for the new Mountain Bay Metropolitan Police Department and Municipal Court facility at 3600 Community Center Drive, and adopted a salt storage shed inspection program for wellhead protection areas. Both items passed unanimously with all commissioners present voting in favor.

## Key Discussions
### Site plan review - Mountain Bay Metro Police Department and Municipal Court facility
Staff presented the site plan for the new police department and municipal court at 3600 Community Center Drive. The building was rotated to address challenges with a gas pipeline easement running through the property. Chief Jeremy explained the building is designed for future growth, with current capacity for 40 officers but room for 50-60 in the future. Discussion included the alternate storage shed placement, with commissioners suggesting it be positioned further north to allow for future expansion. The landscaping plan meets current relaxed code requirements. Motion to recommend approval to the Board of Trustees passed unanimously with conditions 2-5 as listed in staff report.

### Salt storage shed inspection program for wellhead protection district
Staff proposed a program with two annual inspections per facility - a warm weather inspection focusing on structural integrity and site drainage, and a winter operational inspection focusing on material handling and containment. A standardized inspection worksheet similar to DOT standards was developed. Staff noted one existing facility near the village well on Sternberg has never gone through the wellhead protection conditional use process. Commissioners agreed staff should reach out to bring that facility into compliance. Motion to approve the program passed unanimously, with an amendment to report back to both Plan Commission and Public Works Committee.

## Public Comment
Jim Pensel of 5002 Arrow Street, Weston, spoke remotely. He asked the commission to use common sense regarding landscaping requirements for the police department project, referencing previous relaxed standards for Green Heck Turner. He also requested that the salt shed inspection program include annual reporting on inspection results, noting some zoning complaints have gone unresolved for years.
