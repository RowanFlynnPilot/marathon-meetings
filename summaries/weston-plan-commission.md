## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Plan Commission approved the site plan for the new Mountain Bay Metropolitan Police Department and Municipal Court facility at 3600 Community Center Drive, and adopted a new salt storage shed inspection program for wellhead protection districts. Both items passed unanimously with the commission addressing technical details about gas pipeline easements, future building expansion, and environmental protection measures.

## Key Discussions
### Site plan review - Mountain Bay Metro Police Department and Municipal Court facility
Staff presented the site plan for the new police department and municipal court at 3600 Community Center Drive. The building was rotated 90 degrees and moved north to accommodate a 36-inch gas pipeline easement running under the parking lot. Police Chief Jeremy explained the building will accommodate 50-60 officers for future growth, up from current staffing of 40 officers and 4 clerical staff. Discussion included an alternate storage shed location, with commissioners recommending it be placed further north to allow for future building expansion. The landscaping plan meets current relaxed code requirements. Motion to recommend approval to the board of trustees with conditions 2-5 passed unanimously.

### Salt storage shed inspection program for Wellhead Protection District
Staff proposed a new inspection program with two annual inspections per facility - a warm weather inspection focusing on structural integrity and drainage, and a winter inspection focusing on material handling and containment. Staff noted one existing facility near Sternberg Avenue has never gone through the wellhead protection conditional use process and predates the ordinance. Commissioners agreed staff should reach out to bring that facility into compliance. Motion to approve the program with reporting back to both Plan Commission and Public Works Committee passed unanimously.

### April 2026 meeting minutes
Motion to approve the April 13, 2026 meeting minutes as presented passed unanimously without discussion.

### Planning department monthly update
Staff announced the first meeting of the joint plan commission/village board steering committee for the comprehensive plan update is scheduled for Monday, June 1st at 5:00 p.m. Staff will send calendar invites to commissioners.

### Future agenda items
Electric vehicle charging stations were noted for a future fall agenda. Commissioner inquired about potential residential TIF districts for workforce housing. Staff indicated Ehlers has prepared a presentation on residential TIF qualifications that could be shared with the commission, potentially as part of the comprehensive plan process.

## Public Comment
Jim Penson of 5002 Arrow Street, Weston, spoke via video call. He requested the commission urge staff to waive excessive landscaping requirements for the new police department project similar to accommodations made for the Greenheck Turner building, noting taxpayers will bear the costs. He also asked that the commission request annual reports on salt shed inspections in wellhead protection zones, expressing concern that some zoning complaints go unresolved for years.
