## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Plan Commission approved the site plan for the new Mountain Bay Metro Police Department and Municipal Court facility at 3600 Community Center Drive, and adopted a new salt storage shed inspection program for wellhead protection areas. Both items passed unanimously with the commission addressing technical details about gas line easements and future building expansion possibilities.

## Key Discussions
### Mountain Bay Metro Police Department and Municipal Court facility site plan
Staff presented the site plan for the new police department at 3600 Community Center Drive, noting it meets all B2 zoning requirements. Chief Jeremy explained the building was rotated to face east due to a gas pipeline underneath the parking lot, which significantly reduced fill costs. The building will accommodate 50-60 officers for future growth beyond the current 40 officers. Discussion included an alternate storage shed location and future expansion possibilities to the north. Motion to approve was made and passed unanimously with conditions 2 through 5 as listed by staff.

### Salt storage shed inspection program for wellhead protection district
Jennifer presented a new inspection program requiring two annual inspections per facility - one warm weather inspection focused on structural integrity and one winter operational inspection focused on material handling practices. The program includes a standardized inspection worksheet similar to DOT standards. Staff noted one existing facility near Sternberg has never gone through the wellhead protection conditional use process and agreed to reach out to them. Motion to approve passed unanimously with an amendment to report back to both the Plan Commission and Public Works Committee.

### April 2026 meeting minutes
Motion to approve the April 13, 2026 meeting minutes was made and seconded. Passed unanimously without discussion.

### April 2026 staff approved items and building permits
Both the certified survey map/site plan report and building permit report for April 2026 were approved unanimously without discussion.

### Planning department monthly update
Jennifer announced the first meeting of the joint Plan Commission/Village Board steering committee for the comprehensive plan is scheduled for Monday, June 1st at 5:00 p.m. Commissioner discussion followed regarding potential residential TIF districts for workforce housing, with staff noting Ehlers has prepared a presentation on qualifications that could be shared with the commission.

## Public Comment
Jim Pensson of 5002 Arrow Street, Weston, spoke via video call requesting the commission use common sense regarding landscaping requirements for the police department project to avoid excessive taxpayer costs similar to the Green Heck Turner building. He also requested annual reporting on the salt shed inspection program, noting some zoning complaints are years old and get 'lost in the sauce.'
