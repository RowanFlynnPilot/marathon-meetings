## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Plan Commission approved the site plan for the new Mountain Bay Metropolitan Police Department and Municipal Court facility at 3600 Community Center Drive, and adopted a new salt storage shed inspection program for wellhead protection districts. The commission also approved April 2026 minutes, staff-approved survey maps, and building permits.

## Key Discussions
### Mountain Bay Metro Police Department and Municipal Court Facility Site Plan
Staff presented the site plan for the new police department and court facility on Community Center Drive. Chief Jeremy explained the building was rotated to face east due to a gas pipeline on the property, which reduced fill costs significantly. The building meets all B2 zoning requirements and landscaping codes. Discussion included questions about future expansion capability (possible to the north), placement of an alternate storage shed, and coordination with ANR regarding the gas pipeline. The motion to recommend approval to the Board of Trustees with staff conditions 2-5 passed unanimously.

### Salt Storage Shed Inspection Program
Staff proposed a new inspection program with two annual inspections per facility in wellhead protection districts - one in warm weather focusing on structural integrity and one in winter focusing on operations. A standardized inspection worksheet similar to DOT standards was developed. Staff noted one existing facility near Sternberg has never gone through the wellhead protection conditional use process and commissioners agreed staff should reach out to bring them into compliance. Motion to approve the program with reporting to both Plan Commission and Public Works passed unanimously.

### April 2026 Meeting Minutes
Motion to approve the April 13, 2026 meeting minutes as presented passed unanimously without discussion.

### April 2026 Staff Approved Survey Maps and Site Plans
Motion to approve passed unanimously.

### April 2026 Building Permits
Motion to approve passed unanimously.

### Planning Department Update and Future Items
Staff announced the first joint Plan Commission/Village Board steering committee meeting for the comprehensive plan is scheduled for Monday, June 1st at 5:00 p.m. Discussion occurred about residential TIF districts for workforce housing, with commissioners requesting Ehlers' presentation on qualifications be shared, possibly incorporated into comprehensive plan discussions.

## Public Comment
Jim Pensel of 5002 Arrow Street, Weston, spoke via online connection. He urged the commission to exercise common sense on landscaping requirements for the police department project to avoid excessive taxpayer costs, similar to accommodations made for the Greenheck Turner building. He also requested the commission require annual reporting on the salt shed inspection program to ensure follow-through on compliance visits.
