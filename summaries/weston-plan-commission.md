## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Plan Commission approved the site plan for the new Mountain Bay Metropolitan Police Department and Municipal Court facility at 3600 Community Center Drive, and adopted a new salt storage shed inspection program for wellhead protection areas. Both items passed unanimously with minimal discussion.

## Key Discussions
### Mountain Bay Metro Police Department and Municipal Court Facility Site Plan
Staff presented the site plan for the new police department and municipal court at 3600 Community Center Drive, noting it meets all B2 zoning requirements. Chief Jeremy explained the building was rotated to face east due to a gas pipeline running through the property, which reduced fill costs significantly. The building will accommodate 50-60 officers for future growth beyond the current 40 officers. Discussion included future expansion possibilities to the north and placement of an alternate storage shed. The motion to recommend approval to the Board of Trustees with staff conditions 2-5 passed unanimously.

### Salt Storage Shed Inspection Program for Wellhead Protection District
Staff proposed a program with two annual inspections per facility - a warm weather inspection focused on structural integrity and site drainage, and a winter operational inspection focused on material handling and containment. A standardized inspection worksheet similar to DOT's was developed. Staff noted one existing facility near Sternberg has never gone through the wellhead protection conditional use process and is closest to the village well. Commission agreed staff should reach out to that facility about compliance. The motion to approve the program with reporting back to both Plan Commission and Public Works Committee passed unanimously.

### April 2026 Meeting Minutes
Motion to approve the April 13, 2026 meeting minutes as presented passed unanimously with no discussion.

### April 2026 Staff Approved Items and Building Permits
Both the certified survey maps/site plans and building permits for April 2026 were approved unanimously without discussion.

### Planning Department Update and Future Items
Staff announced the first joint Plan Commission and Village Board steering committee meeting for the comprehensive plan is scheduled for Monday, June 1st at 5:00 p.m. Discussion of potential residential TIF districts for workforce housing was raised by a commissioner, with staff noting Ehlers has prepared a presentation on qualifications that could be shared or incorporated into comprehensive plan discussions. Electric vehicle charging stations were noted as a future agenda item for fall.

## Public Comment
Jim Penson of 5002 Arrow Street, Weston, spoke via online participation. He urged the commission to exercise common sense regarding landscaping requirements for the new police department to avoid unnecessary taxpayer expense, referencing previous relaxed standards for the Green Heck Turner building. He also requested that the salt storage shed inspection program include annual reporting on inspection results, noting some zoning complaints are years old.
