## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Plan Commission approved the site plan for the new Mountain Bay Metropolitan Police Department and Municipal Court facility at 3600 Community Center Drive, and established a salt storage shed inspection program for wellhead protection areas. Both items passed unanimously with the commission taking steps to protect the village's drinking water supply.

## Key Discussions
### Mountain Bay Metro Police Department and Municipal Court facility site plan
Staff presented the site plan for the new police department and municipal court at 3600 Community Center Drive, noting it meets all B2 district zoning requirements. Chief Jeremy explained the building was rotated to face east due to a gas pipeline underneath the parking lot, which reduced fill costs significantly. The building is designed for 50-60 officer capacity versus current 40 officers. Discussion included an alternate storage shed placement and future expansion possibilities to the north. Motion to approve was made by a commissioner and passed unanimously, subject to staff conditions 2-5.

### Salt storage shed inspection program for wellhead protection district
Staff proposed a program with two annual inspections per facility - a warm weather inspection focusing on structural integrity and drainage, and a winter operational inspection focusing on material handling and containment. The program uses a standardized inspection worksheet similar to DOT standards. Staff noted one existing facility near Sternberg has never gone through the wellhead protection conditional use process and should be approached about compliance. Motion to approve the program passed unanimously, with an amendment to report back to both the Plan Commission and Public Works committee.

### April 2026 meeting minutes
Motion to approve the April 13, 2026 meeting minutes as presented. Passed unanimously without discussion.

### April 2026 staff approved items and building permits
Both the April 2026 staff approved certified survey maps/site plans and building permits were approved unanimously without discussion.

### Planning department monthly update
Staff announced the first joint Plan Commission/Village Board steering committee meeting for the comprehensive plan is scheduled for Monday, June 1st at 5:00 p.m. Commissioner asked about potential residential TIF districts for workforce housing, and staff indicated Ehlers has prepared a presentation on qualifications that could be shared with the commission or incorporated into comprehensive plan discussions.

## Public Comment
Jim Pensel of 5002 Arrow Street, Weston, spoke via online connection. He urged the commission to exercise common sense on landscaping requirements for the police department project, citing previous concerns about taxpayer costs for excessive landscaping points similar to the Green Heck Turner building. He also requested the commission require annual reporting on salt shed inspections within wellhead protection zones rather than letting complaints get 'lost in the sauce.'
