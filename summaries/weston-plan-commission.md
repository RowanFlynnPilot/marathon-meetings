## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Plan Commission approved a site plan for the new Mountain Bay Metropolitan Police Department and Municipal Court facility at 3600 Community Drive, and adopted a salt storage shed inspection program for wellhead protection areas. Both items passed unanimously with all members present voting in favor.

## Key Discussions
### Site plan review for Mountain Bay Metro Police Department and Municipal Court facility
Staff presented the site plan for the new police department and municipal court at 3600 Community Center Drive. The building was rotated to address challenges with a gas pipeline easement running through the property. Police Chief Jeremy explained the building is designed for future growth from current 40 officers to 50-60 capacity. Discussion included an alternate storage shed placement, with commissioners suggesting it be located further north to allow for future building expansion. The motion to recommend approval to the Board of Trustees with staff conditions 2-5 passed unanimously.

### Salt storage shed inspection program for Wellhead Protection District
Jennifer presented the proposed inspection program requiring two annual inspections per facility - a warm weather inspection focused on structural integrity and a winter operational inspection focused on material handling. Staff developed a standardized inspection worksheet similar to DOT standards. Discussion revealed one existing facility near Sternberg Road has never gone through the wellhead protection conditional use process despite being closest to the village well. Commissioners agreed staff should reach out to bring them into compliance. Motion to approve passed unanimously with addition that staff report back to both Plan Commission and Public Works committee.

## Public Comment
Jim Pensel of 5002 Arrow Street, Weston, spoke via remote connection. He urged the commission to exercise common sense regarding landscaping requirements for the police department project, referencing previous flexibility shown for the Greenheck Turner building. He also requested the commission require annual reports on the salt storage shed inspection program to ensure complaints don't get 'lost in the sauce.'
