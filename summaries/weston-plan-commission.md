## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Plan Commission approved the site plan for the new Mountain Bay Metropolitan Police Department and Municipal Court facility at 3600 Community Center Drive, and adopted a new salt storage shed inspection program for wellhead protection areas. Both items passed unanimously with all present commissioners voting in favor.

## Key Discussions
### Site plan review - Mountain Bay Metro Police Department and Municipal Court facility
Staff presented the site plan for the new police department and municipal court at 3600 Community Center Drive, noting it meets all B2 zoning requirements. Chief Jeremy explained the building was rotated to address challenges with a gas pipeline easement running through the property, which reduced fill costs significantly. The building will accommodate 50-60 officers for future growth beyond the current 40 officers. Discussion covered the alternate storage shed placement, landscaping compliance with current code, and potential future expansion to the north. A motion to recommend approval to the board of trustees with conditions 2 through 5 passed unanimously.

### Salt storage shed inspection program for wellhead protection district
Staff proposed a twice-annual inspection program for salt storage facilities within wellhead protection areas - one warm weather inspection focused on structural integrity and drainage, and one winter inspection focused on operational practices. A standardized inspection worksheet based on DOT practices was developed. Staff noted one existing facility near Sternberg has never gone through the wellhead protection conditional use process and should be contacted. Motion to approve the program with reporting back to both the Plan Commission and Public Works Committee passed unanimously.

## Public Comment
Jim Pensson of 5002 Arrow Street, Weston, spoke via online connection. He urged the commission to exercise common sense regarding landscaping requirements for the police department project, asking that taxpayers not be burdened with excessive landscaping costs similar to the Green Heck Turner building situation. He also requested that the salt shed inspection program include annual reporting on inspections conducted and results, noting some zoning complaints have gone unresolved for years.
