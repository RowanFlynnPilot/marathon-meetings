## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Plan Commission approved the site plan for the new Mountain Bay Metropolitan Police Department and Municipal Court facility at 3600 Community Center Drive, and adopted a new salt storage shed inspection program for wellhead protection areas. Both items passed unanimously with the commission addressing technical details about gas pipeline easements and future building expansion.

## Key Discussions
### Site plan review - Mountain Bay Metro Police Department and Municipal Court facility
Staff presented the site plan for the new police department and municipal court at 3600 Community Center Drive. Chief Jeremy explained challenges with building orientation due to a gas pipeline, noting the building was rotated to face east to avoid the easement while utilizing the higher elevation of the site. The building is designed for 50-60 officer capacity for future growth, with expansion possible to the north. An alternate storage shed was discussed for evidence overflow. Motion to approve with staff conditions 2-5 passed unanimously.

### Salt storage shed inspection program for wellhead protection district
Jennifer presented the new inspection program requiring two annual inspections per facility - a warm weather inspection for structural integrity and a winter operational inspection for material handling practices. A standardized inspection worksheet similar to DOT's was developed. Staff noted one existing facility near Sternberg has never gone through the wellhead protection conditional use process and will be contacted. Motion to approve with reporting to both Plan Commission and Public Works passed unanimously.

### April 2026 staff approved certified survey maps and site plans
Approved unanimously without discussion.

### April 2026 building permits
Approved unanimously without discussion.

### Planning and development department monthly project update
Jennifer announced the first joint Plan Commission and Village Board steering committee meeting for the comprehensive plan is scheduled for Monday, June 1st at 5:00 p.m. Discussion included potential residential TIF districts for workforce housing, with staff noting Ehlers has a presentation on qualifications they will share with the commission.

## Public Comment
Jim Pensel of 5002 Arrow Street, Weston, spoke via online connection. He urged the commission to exercise common sense regarding landscaping requirements for the police department project, referencing past issues with the Green Heck Turner building. He also requested annual reporting on the salt shed inspection program to ensure oversight doesn't get 'lost in the sauce' like some other zoning complaints.
