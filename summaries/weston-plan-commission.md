## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Plan Commission approved a site plan for the new Mountain Bay Metropolitan Police Department and Municipal Court facility at 3600 Community Center Drive, and adopted a salt storage shed inspection program for wellhead protection areas. Both items passed unanimously with all members present voting in favor.

## Key Discussions
### Site plan review - Mountain Bay Metro Police Department and Municipal Court facility
Staff presented the site plan for the new police department and municipal court at 3600 Community Center Drive, noting it meets all B2 zoning requirements. Chief Jeremy explained challenges with a gas pipeline requiring the building to be rotated and positioned on the higher part of the site to reduce fill costs. The building will accommodate 50-60 officers for future growth beyond the current 40 officers. Discussion addressed an alternate storage shed location, with commissioners suggesting moving it further north to allow for future building expansion. The motion to recommend approval to the Board of Trustees with staff conditions 2-5 passed unanimously.

### Salt storage shed inspection program for Wellhead Protection District
Staff proposed a twice-annual inspection program for salt storage facilities in wellhead protection areas - a warm weather inspection for structural integrity and a winter operational inspection. The program includes a standardized inspection worksheet similar to DOT requirements. Staff noted one existing facility near the Sternberg well has never gone through the wellhead protection conditional use process and commissioners agreed staff should reach out to bring them into compliance. The motion to approve the program with reporting back to both Plan Commission and Public Works Committee passed unanimously.

## Public Comment
Jim Penson of 5002 Arrow Street, Weston, spoke via online connection. He urged the commission to exercise common sense regarding landscaping requirements for the police department project, referencing previous relaxed standards for the Green Heck Turner building to avoid excessive taxpayer expense. He also requested the commission require annual reporting on the salt shed inspection program to ensure accountability, noting some zoning complaints are years old.
