## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Plan Commission approved the site plan for the new Mountain Bay Metropolitan Police Department and Municipal Court facility at 3600 Community Center Drive, and established a salt storage shed inspection program for wellhead protection areas. The commission also approved minutes and routine staff reports.

## Key Discussions
### Mountain Bay Metro Police Department and Municipal Court Facility Site Plan
Staff presented the site plan for the new police and court facility at 3600 Community Center Drive. The building meets all B2 zoning requirements. Chief Jeremy explained the building was rotated to face east due to a gas pipeline easement running under the parking lot area. The 36-inch deep gas line allows parking over it but required reorienting the building to higher ground, reducing fill costs. Discussion addressed future expansion capability to the north, the alternate storage shed placement, and landscaping meeting current relaxed code. Motion to approve with conditions 2-5 passed unanimously.

### Salt Storage Shed Inspection Program for Wellhead Protection District
Staff proposed two annual inspections per facility - a warm weather inspection focusing on structural integrity and drainage, and a winter operational inspection focusing on material handling and containment. A standardized inspection worksheet similar to DOT standards was developed. Staff noted one existing facility near Sternberg Road that never went through the wellhead protection conditional use process and is closest to the village well. The commission agreed to reach out to that facility. Motion to approve the program with reporting back to both Plan Commission and Public Works passed unanimously.

### April 2026 Meeting Minutes
Motion to approve the April 13, 2026 minutes as presented passed unanimously with no discussion.

### Planning Department Monthly Update
Staff announced the first joint Plan Commission/Village Board steering committee meeting for the comprehensive plan update is scheduled for Monday, June 1st at 5:00 p.m. Discussion included potential residential TIF districts for workforce housing, with staff noting Ehlers has a presentation on qualifications that could be shared with the commission or incorporated into comprehensive plan discussions.

## Public Comment
Jim Penson of 5002 Arrow Street, Weston, spoke via remote connection. He urged the commission to use common sense regarding landscaping requirements for the police department project, referencing previous Green Heck Turner building standards, to avoid unnecessary taxpayer expense. He also requested the commission require annual reporting on the salt storage shed inspection program to track compliance visits.
