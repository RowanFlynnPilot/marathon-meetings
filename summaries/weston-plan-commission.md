## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Plan Commission approved a site plan for the new Mountain Bay Metropolitan Police Department and Municipal Court facility at 3600 Community Drive, and adopted a new salt storage shed inspection program for wellhead protection districts. Both items passed unanimously with all commissioners present voting in favor.

## Key Discussions
### Mountain Bay Metro Police Department and Municipal Court facility site plan
Staff presented the site plan for the new police department and municipal court at 3600 Community Center Drive, noting it meets all B2 district zoning requirements. Police Chief Jeremy explained the building was rotated to face east due to a gas pipeline easement running under the parking lot, which significantly reduced fill costs. Commissioner Garant asked about future expansion capability, and the chief confirmed the building could expand north or south away from the gas line, with capacity for 50-60 officers versus current staffing of 40. Discussion covered an alternate storage shed placement, with suggestions to locate it further north to accommodate future expansion. The motion to recommend approval to the board of trustees with staff conditions 2-5 passed unanimously.

### Salt storage shed inspection program for wellhead protection district
Staff proposed a new inspection program with two annual inspections per facility - a warm weather inspection focusing on structural integrity and drainage, and a winter operational inspection focusing on material handling and containment. The program uses a standardized worksheet similar to DOT standards. Staff noted one existing facility near Sternberg has never gone through the wellhead protection conditional use process and predates the ordinance. Commissioners agreed staff should reach out to that facility about compliance. Commissioner Lumper praised the well-organized program. The motion to approve the program with reporting back to both Plan Commission and Public Works Committee passed unanimously.

### April 2026 staff approved items and building permits
Both the April 2026 staff approved certified survey maps/site plans and the April 2026 building permits were approved unanimously with no discussion.

### Planning department monthly update and future items
Staff announced the first meeting of the joint Plan Commission-Village Board steering committee for the comprehensive plan is scheduled for Monday, June 1st at 5:00 p.m. Discussion arose about residential TIF districts for workforce housing, with staff noting Ehlers has prepared a presentation on qualifications that could be shared with commissioners or incorporated into the comprehensive plan steering committee discussions.

## Public Comment
Jim Pensel of 5002 Arrow Street, Weston, spoke via online connection. He urged the commission to exercise common sense regarding landscaping requirements for the police department project, referencing previous relaxed standards for the Greenheck Turner building, so taxpayers don't have to spend excessive dollars on landscaping points. He also requested annual reporting on salt shed inspections in wellhead protection zones, noting some zoning complaints are years old and get 'lost in the sauce.'
