## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Plan Commission approved the site plan for the new Mountain Bay Metropolitan Police Department and Municipal Court facility at 3600 Community Center Drive, and adopted a new salt storage shed inspection program for wellhead protection districts. Both items passed unanimously with the commission also taking steps to address an existing salt storage facility that predates current ordinance requirements.

## Key Discussions
### Mountain Bay Metro Police Department and Municipal Court Facility Site Plan
Staff presented the site plan for the new police department and municipal court at 3600 Community Center Drive. Jen explained the building meets all B2 district zoning requirements and landscaping standards. Police Chief Jeremy described challenges with a gas pipeline on the property that required rotating the building orientation to face east, which also reduced fill costs. The building is designed for future expansion to the north, with current capacity for 50-60 officers compared to current staffing of 40 officers and 4 clerical staff. An alternate storage shed was discussed with suggestion to place it further north to accommodate future building expansion. Commissioner Lumper moved to approve with staff conditions 2-5. Motion passed unanimously.

### Salt Storage Shed Inspection Program - Wellhead Protection District
Staff proposed a program with two annual inspections per facility - a warm weather inspection focused on structural integrity and a winter operational inspection focused on material handling and containment. The program includes a standardized inspection worksheet similar to DOT standards and a classification system for deficiencies. Staff noted one existing salt storage facility near the municipal well on Sternberg Avenue that predates the wellhead protection ordinance and never went through the conditional use process. Commissioners agreed staff should reach out to bring that facility into compliance. Motion to approve the program with reporting back to both Plan Commission and Public Works Committee passed unanimously.

### April 2026 Meeting Minutes
Motion to approve the April 13, 2026 meeting minutes as presented passed unanimously with no discussion.

### Monthly Reports and Future Items
Staff announced the first joint Plan Commission/Village Board steering committee meeting for the comprehensive plan is scheduled for Monday, June 1st at 5:00 p.m. Commissioner Garant asked about potential residential TIF districts for workforce housing. Staff indicated Ehlers has put together a presentation on residential TIF qualifications that could be shared with the commission, possibly incorporated into the comprehensive plan process.

## Public Comment
Jim Pensel (5002 Arrow Street, Weston) spoke via video call. He asked the commission to exercise common sense regarding landscaping requirements for the police department project, referencing relaxed standards applied to the Green Heck Turner building. He also requested that the salt storage shed inspection program include annual reporting on site visits and findings, noting some zoning complaints are years old.
