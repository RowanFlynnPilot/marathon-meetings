## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Plan Commission approved the site plan for the new Mountain Bay Metropolitan Police Department and Municipal Court facility at 3600 Community Center Drive, and adopted a new salt storage shed inspection program for wellhead protection areas. Both items passed unanimously with the commission taking steps to protect municipal water supplies and advance the police facility project.

## Key Discussions
### Site plan review - Mountain Bay Metro Police Department and Municipal Court facility
The commission reviewed the site plan for the new police department and municipal court at 3600 Community Center Drive. Staff confirmed the project meets all B2 zoning requirements, setbacks, and building aesthetics. Chief Jeremy explained the building was rotated to accommodate a gas line easement, with the parking lot now positioned over the 36-inch deep pipeline (acceptable to ANR gas company). The building has capacity for 50-60 officers for future growth. Discussion included the alternate storage shed placement and potential for northern expansion. A motion to recommend approval to the board of trustees with staff conditions 2-5 passed unanimously.

### Salt storage shed inspection program for wellhead protection district
Staff presented a program for twice-annual inspections of salt storage facilities within wellhead protection areas - a warm weather inspection focusing on structural integrity and a winter operational inspection. The program includes a standardized inspection worksheet similar to DOT standards. Staff noted one existing facility near Sternberg Road has never gone through the wellhead protection conditional use process and commissioners agreed to reach out to bring it into compliance. The motion to approve the program with reporting to both Plan Commission and Public Works Committee passed unanimously.

## Public Comment
Jim Pensel of 5002 Arrow Street, Weston, spoke via Zoom. He asked the commission to use common sense on landscaping requirements for the new police department to avoid excessive taxpayer costs, referencing the Green Heck Turner building's landscaping standards. He also requested that the salt shed inspection program include annual reporting on site visits and compliance outcomes.
