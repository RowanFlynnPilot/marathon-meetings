## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Plan Commission approved the site plan for the new Mountain Bay Metropolitan Police Department and Municipal Court facility at 3600 Community Center Drive, and adopted a new salt storage shed inspection program for wellhead protection areas. Both items passed unanimously with conditions.

## Key Discussions
### Site plan review - Mountain Bay Metro Police Department and Municipal Court facility
Staff presented the site plan for the new police department and municipal court at 3600 Community Center Drive. The building was rotated to face east due to a gas pipeline running under the property, which reduced fill costs significantly. Chief Jeremy explained the building will accommodate 50-60 officers (up from current 40) for future growth. An alternate storage shed for evidence overflow was discussed, with commissioners suggesting it be placed further north to allow for future building expansion. The motion to recommend approval to the Board of Trustees passed unanimously with conditions 2-5 as listed by staff.

### Salt storage shed inspection program for wellhead protection district
Staff proposed a program with two annual inspections per facility - a warm weather inspection focusing on structural integrity and a winter operational inspection focusing on material handling. A standardized inspection worksheet similar to DOT standards was developed. Staff noted one existing facility near Sternberg has never gone through the wellhead protection conditional use process and commissioners agreed it should be contacted about compliance. The motion to approve the program passed unanimously, with an amendment to report back to both the Plan Commission and Public Works committee.

### April 2026 meeting minutes
Motion to approve the April 13, 2026 meeting minutes as presented passed unanimously without discussion.

### Planning department monthly update
Staff announced the first meeting of the joint Plan Commission/Village Board steering committee for the comprehensive plan is scheduled for Monday, June 1st at 5:00 p.m. Staff will send calendar invites and emails to commissioners.

### Future agenda items
Electric vehicle charging stations were noted for a future agenda, likely in the fall. Discussion arose about potential residential TIF districts for workforce housing. Staff indicated Ehlers has prepared a presentation on TIF qualifications that could be shared with commissioners or incorporated into comprehensive plan discussions.

## Public Comment
Jim Pensel of 5002 Arrow Street, Weston, spoke via online connection. He asked the commission to exercise common sense regarding landscaping requirements for the police department project, referencing the Green Heck Turner building as an example of excessive requirements. He also requested that the salt storage shed inspection program include annual reporting on actual site visits and findings.
