## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Plan Commission approved the site plan for the new Mountain Bay Metropolitan Police Department and Municipal Court facility at 3600 Community Center Drive, and adopted a new salt storage shed inspection program for wellhead protection districts. Both items passed unanimously with the commission addressing building orientation challenges related to a gas pipeline and establishing twice-yearly inspections for salt storage facilities near municipal wells.

## Key Discussions
### Mountain Bay Metro Police Department and Municipal Court facility site plan
Staff presented the site plan for the new police department and municipal court at 3600 Community Center Drive. The building was rotated to face east due to a gas pipeline running under the parking lot area, which reduced fill costs significantly. Chief Jeremy explained the building will accommodate 50-60 officers (current staffing is 40 officers plus 4 clerical). An alternate storage shed is proposed but may be built later depending on bid costs. Discussion included future expansion possibilities to the north and positioning the shed to avoid future relocation. Commissioner Zagami moved to recommend approval subject to staff conditions 2-5. Motion passed unanimously.

### Salt storage shed inspection program for wellhead protection district
Staff proposed a new inspection program with two annual inspections per facility - a warm weather inspection focused on structural integrity and a winter operational inspection focused on material handling and containment. A standardized inspection worksheet similar to DOT standards was developed. Staff noted one existing facility near Sternberg that predates the wellhead protection ordinance and has never gone through the conditional use process. The commission agreed staff should reach out to bring them into compliance. Commissioner Lumper praised the well-organized program. Motion to approve with reporting back to both Plan Commission and Public Works Committee passed unanimously.

### April 2026 staff approved certified survey maps and site plans
Motion to approve passed unanimously with no discussion.

### April 2026 building permits
Motion to approve passed unanimously with no discussion.

### Planning and development department monthly project update
Staff announced the first meeting of the joint plan commission and village board steering committee for the comprehensive plan is scheduled for Monday, June 1st at 5:00 p.m. Staff will send calendar invites. Discussion arose about residential TIF districts for workforce housing, with staff noting Ehlers has prepared a presentation on qualifications that could be shared with commissioners or incorporated into comprehensive plan discussions.

## Public Comment
Jim Pensel of 5002 Arrow Street, Weston, spoke remotely. He asked the commission to exercise common sense regarding landscaping requirements for the police department project, referencing previous relaxed standards for the Greenheck Turner building to avoid unnecessary taxpayer expense. He also requested the commission require annual reporting on the salt shed inspection program to ensure complaints don't get 'lost in the sauce' as with some other zoning complaints.
