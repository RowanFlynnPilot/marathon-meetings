## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Plan Commission approved the site plan for the new Mountain Bay Metropolitan Police Department and Municipal Court facility at 3600 Community Center Drive, and established a salt storage shed inspection program for wellhead protection areas. Both items passed unanimously with the commission addressing technical requirements and future expansion considerations.

## Key Discussions
### Site plan review - Mountain Bay Metropolitan Police Department and Municipal Court facility
Staff presented the site plan for the new police department and municipal court at 3600 Community Center Drive. The building was rotated to face east due to a 36-inch gas pipeline running through the property, which reduced fill costs significantly. Chief Jeremy explained the building will accommodate 50-60 officers for future growth beyond the current 40 officers. Discussion included an alternate storage shed location, with commissioners suggesting placing it further north to allow for future building expansion. The landscaping plan meets current relaxed code requirements. Motion to approve passed unanimously with conditions 2-5 as listed by staff.

### Salt storage shed inspection program for wellhead protection district
Jennifer presented the new inspection program requiring two annual inspections per facility - a warm weather inspection focusing on structural integrity and drainage, and a winter operational inspection focusing on material handling and containment. A standardized inspection worksheet similar to DOT standards was developed. Staff identified one existing facility near Sternberg that never went through the wellhead protection conditional use process and is closest to the village well. Commissioners directed staff to reach out to that facility about compliance. Motion to approve passed unanimously with the addition of reporting back to both Plan Commission and Public Works committee.

### April 2026 meeting minutes
Minutes from the April 13, 2026 meeting were approved unanimously without discussion.

### April 2026 staff approved items and building permits
Both the certified survey maps/site plans and building permits for April 2026 were approved unanimously without discussion.

### Planning and development department monthly update
Jennifer announced the first joint Plan Commission and Village Board steering committee meeting for the comprehensive plan update is scheduled for Monday, June 1st at 5:00 p.m. Discussion included potential residential TIF districts for workforce housing, with staff noting Ehlers has prepared a presentation on qualifications that could be shared with the commission or incorporated into the comprehensive plan process.

## Public Comment
Jim Pensel of 5002 Arrow Street, Weston, spoke via online connection. He urged the commission to exercise common sense regarding landscaping requirements for the police department project, requesting staff waive excessive requirements similar to what was done for the Greenheck Turner building. He also requested annual reporting on the salt storage shed inspection program to ensure complaints don't get lost and sites are actually visited.
