## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Plan Commission approved the site plan for the new Mountain Bay Metropolitan Police Department and Municipal Court facility at 3600 Community Center Drive, and adopted a salt storage shed inspection program for wellhead protection areas. Both items passed unanimously with all members present voting in favor.

## Key Discussions
### Site plan review - Mountain Bay Metro Police Department and Municipal Court facility
Staff presented the site plan for the new police department and municipal court at 3600 Community Center Drive. Jennifer explained the building meets all B2 district zoning requirements and setbacks. Police Chief Jeremy discussed challenges with a gas pipeline running under the parking lot, which required rotating the building orientation to the east and utilizing the higher elevation of the site. The building is designed to accommodate 50-60 officers for future growth beyond current staffing of 40 officers. Discussion included an alternate storage shed that may be built later depending on bid costs, with commissioners suggesting it be placed further north to accommodate future building expansion. Motion to recommend approval to the Board of Trustees with conditions 2-5 passed unanimously.

### Salt storage shed inspection program for Wellhead Protection District
Jennifer presented a new inspection program requiring two annual inspections per facility - a warm weather inspection focusing on structural integrity and drainage, and a winter operational inspection focusing on material handling and containment. The program includes a standardized inspection worksheet similar to DOT standards. Staff noted one existing facility near Sternberg has never gone through the wellhead protection conditional use process and commissioners agreed staff should reach out to bring them into compliance. Michael explained the village's wells are dispersed across four distinct areas making them more susceptible to contamination. Motion to approve the program with staff reporting back to both Plan Commission and Public Works passed unanimously.

### April 2026 meeting minutes
Motion to approve the April 13, 2026 meeting minutes as presented passed unanimously without discussion.

### Planning and development department monthly project update
Jennifer announced the first meeting of the joint Plan Commission and Village Board steering committee for the comprehensive plan is scheduled for Monday, June 1st at 5:00 p.m. She will send email notifications and calendar invites to members.

### Future agenda items
Electric vehicle charging stations were noted for a future agenda, likely in the fall. Discussion arose about potential new TIF districts for residential/workforce housing. Jennifer mentioned Ehlers has a presentation on residential TIF qualifications that could be shared with the commission or incorporated into comprehensive plan discussions.

## Public Comment
Jim Penson of 5002 Arrow Street, Weston, spoke via remote connection. He urged the commission to exercise common sense regarding landscaping requirements for the police department project, asking that staff waive excessive landscaping standards similar to accommodations made for the Greenheck Turner building to avoid unnecessary taxpayer expense. He also requested the commission require annual reporting on the salt shed inspection program to ensure accountability and prevent the issue from being 'lost in the sauce.'
