## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Plan Commission approved the site plan for the new Mountain Bay Metropolitan Police Department and Municipal Court facility at 3600 Community Center Drive, and adopted a new salt storage shed inspection program for wellhead protection areas. Both items passed unanimously with the commission addressing technical requirements including gas pipeline considerations and environmental protection measures.

## Key Discussions
### Mountain Bay Metro Police Department and Municipal Court Facility Site Plan
Staff presented the site plan for the new police department at 3600 Community Center Drive, which meets all B2 zoning requirements. Chief Jeremy explained that the building was rotated to face east due to a gas pipeline running under the parking lot, which significantly reduced fill costs. The facility is designed for 50-60 officer capacity (current staffing is 40 officers plus 4 clerical). Discussion included the alternate storage shed placement, with commissioners suggesting it be moved north to allow for future expansion. Commissioner Zagami made the motion to recommend approval subject to staff conditions 2-5, which passed unanimously.

### Salt Storage Shed Inspection Program
Staff proposed a new inspection program with two annual inspections per facility in wellhead protection areas - one warm weather inspection focused on structural integrity and one winter operational inspection focused on material handling practices. Jennifer noted one existing facility near Sternberg that predates the wellhead protection ordinance and has never gone through the conditional use process. Commissioners agreed staff should reach out to bring them into compliance. Commissioner Lumper praised the program as well put together. The motion to approve passed unanimously, with an amendment to report back to both the Plan Commission and Public Works Committee.

### April 2026 Minutes and Reports
The April 13, 2026 meeting minutes were approved unanimously. Staff approved certified survey maps, site plans, and building permits for April 2026 were also approved unanimously without discussion.

### Planning Department Update and Future Items
Staff announced the first joint Plan Commission-Village Board steering committee meeting for the comprehensive plan is scheduled for Monday, June 1st at 5:00 p.m. Commissioner inquired about residential TIF districts for workforce housing, and staff indicated Ehlers has prepared a presentation on qualifications that could be shared with the commission or incorporated into comprehensive plan discussions. Electric vehicle charging stations were noted as a future agenda item for fall.

## Public Comment
Jim Penson of 5002 Arrow Street, Weston, spoke via remote connection. He asked the commission to use common sense on landscaping requirements for the police department project to avoid excessive taxpayer costs similar to the Green Heck Turner building. He also requested that the salt shed inspection program include annual reporting on site visits and results, noting that some zoning complaints are years old.
