## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Plan Commission approved the site plan for the new Mountain Bay Metropolitan Police Department and Municipal Court facility at 3600 Community Center Drive, and adopted a new salt storage shed inspection program for wellhead protection areas. Both items passed unanimously with all commissioners present voting in favor.

## Key Discussions
### Site plan review - Mountain Bay Metro Police Department and Municipal Court facility
Staff presented the site plan for the new police department and municipal court at 3600 Community Center Drive. The building orientation was changed to face east due to a gas pipeline running under the parking lot area. Chief Jeremy explained the building has capacity for 50-60 officers (up from current 40) for future growth. An alternate storage shed is proposed but may be added later depending on bid costs. Commissioner Jordan asked about future expandability, which would be possible to the north or south. Discussion included ensuring the shed placement doesn't impede future expansion. Motion to approve passed unanimously with conditions 2-5 as listed by staff.

### Salt storage shed inspection program for wellhead protection district
Jennifer presented the proposed inspection program requiring two annual inspections per facility - one in warm weather focusing on structural integrity and one in winter focusing on operations. A standardized inspection worksheet similar to DOT standards was developed. Staff identified at least one existing facility near the Sternberg well that predates the wellhead protection ordinance and never went through the conditional use process. Commission agreed staff should reach out to bring them into compliance. Motion to approve the program with reporting back to both Plan Commission and Public Works passed unanimously.

## Public Comment
Jim Pensson of 5002 Arrow Street, Weston, spoke via remote connection. He asked the commission to exercise common sense regarding landscaping requirements for the police department project, referencing previous relaxed standards for the Greenheck Turner building. He also requested that the salt shed inspection program include annual reporting on site visits and findings, noting some zoning complaints are years old.
