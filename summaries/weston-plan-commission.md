## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Plan Commission approved a site plan for the new Mountain Bay Metropolitan Police Department and Municipal Court facility at 3600 Community Drive, and adopted a new salt storage shed inspection program for wellhead protection areas. Both items passed unanimously with the commission also scheduling the first comprehensive plan steering committee meeting for June 1st.

## Key Discussions
### Mountain Bay Metro Police Department and Municipal Court Facility Site Plan
Staff presented the site plan for the new police department and municipal court at 3600 Community Center Drive. The building was rotated to address challenges with a gas pipeline easement running through the property. Chief Jeremy explained the building will accommodate 50-60 officers for future growth, up from current staffing of 40 officers. Discussion included the alternate storage shed location and potential for future building expansion to the north. A motion to recommend approval to the board of trustees with staff conditions 2-5 passed unanimously.

### Salt Storage Shed Inspection Program for Wellhead Protection District
Jennifer presented a new inspection program requiring two annual inspections per facility - a warm weather inspection focusing on structural integrity and a winter operational inspection focusing on material handling practices. The program includes a standardized inspection worksheet similar to DOT standards. Staff noted one existing facility near Sternberg has never gone through the wellhead protection conditional use process. Commission directed staff to reach out to that facility about compliance. Motion to approve the program with reporting back to both Plan Commission and Public Works passed unanimously.

### April 2026 Minutes and Reports
Minutes from April 13, 2026 meeting were approved unanimously. April 2026 staff approved certified survey maps, site plans, and building permits were approved unanimously as consent items.

### Planning Department Monthly Update
Jennifer announced the first joint Plan Commission/Village Board steering committee meeting for the comprehensive plan is scheduled for Monday, June 1st at 5:00 p.m. Discussion also touched on potential residential TIF districts for workforce housing, with Ehlers having prepared a presentation on qualifications that may be shared with the commission or incorporated into comprehensive plan discussions.

## Public Comment
Jim Pensel (5002 Arrow Street, Weston) spoke via online connection. He urged the commission to exercise common sense regarding landscaping requirements for the police department project, referencing previous Green Heck Turner building standards. He also requested that the salt shed inspection program include annual reporting on site visits and findings.
