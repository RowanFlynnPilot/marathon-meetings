## Meeting Overview
The Finance and Human Resources Committee heard a detailed presentation on public works operations and budget, showing the department operates below average costs compared to central Wisconsin communities. The main action item was approving a modified employee clothing allowance policy after canceling the Cintas uniform contract, ultimately settling on $400 for 2026 and $500 annually starting 2027, plus a washer and dryer purchase, after three failed motions.

## Key Discussions
### Public Works Operations and Budget Presentation
Public Works Director Michael delivered an extensive presentation showing the department manages 119.5 centerline miles of road, 114 miles of water main, 103 miles of sanitary sewer, and 70 miles of storm sewer with about 50 pieces of equipment. He reported the 2026 public works budget decreased by $26,000 (1.1%) from 2025. Michael emphasized the village spends approximately $9,700 less per mile than the average central Wisconsin community, making them the third lowest spending community per mile in the region. He detailed the recent major snowstorm response where employees worked 16-17 hour shifts, with estimated costs around $50,000 for that single event. The county is applying for disaster relief funds which could reimburse some costs if the state certifies the disaster.

### Reimbursement for Clothing and Equipment Amendments
After canceling the Cintas uniform contract to save costs, staff proposed increasing the employee clothing allowance from $300 to $600 annually. Committee member Daniels objected, arguing savings shouldn't automatically mean spending and citing the upcoming fire department referendum as a higher priority. Director Michael defended the proposal, noting employees work in harsh conditions with chemicals and that providing benefits helps retention. The first motion for $600 failed on roll call (Daniels-yes, Love-no, Olsson-yes, My-no, Satai-no). A second motion for $400 also failed 3-3. A third motion for $500 with washer/dryer failed. The fourth motion for $400 in 2026, $500 annually starting 2027, plus a washer and dryer passed with one opposed.

### Financial Reports and Minutes
The committee unanimously approved the minutes from February 16, 2026, and acknowledged the February 2026 financial reports for all funds, T1 and T2 detail reports, and legal details. All motions passed with voice votes, no opposition.

## Public Comment
Lisa Beck of 1808 Cortez Lane spoke during public comment. She praised Public Works Director Michael for the department's work during the recent storm. She also questioned the proposed clothing allowance increase, suggesting the village consider not going to the highest proposed amount since Cintas was cancelled to save money, and questioned whether employees need all the apparel being provided.
