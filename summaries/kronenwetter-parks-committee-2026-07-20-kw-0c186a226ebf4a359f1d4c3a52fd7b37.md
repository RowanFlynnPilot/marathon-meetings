## Meeting Overview
The Kronenwetter Parks Committee met on July 20, 2026 to discuss a post-parks-tour review of village parks, two fundraising events for the Bushka Park renovation project, and future agenda planning. Key outcomes included approving a recommendation to the village board to hold a September 12 Bushka Park 5K/3K run-walk as an official village event, approving a motion to ask the board for a $5,000 matching grant, and declining a $2,000 funding request for a planned August 21 concert fundraiser, which was subsequently postponed to spring 2027.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of Minutes - June 29, 2026
A motion was made by committee member Patty to approve the June 29, 2026 Parks Committee meeting minutes, seconded by Elizabeth. The motion carried unanimously on a voice vote.

### Bushka Park Renovation Project Grant Update
Staff reported that three grants were submitted, including one to WASOW (outcome pending) and the Marathon County Parks and Recreation Foundation Fund grant for which the village received $5,000. A committee member asked for clarification on whether $100,000 was the total grant pool or the eligibility cap. Staff noted they had a call in with WASOW and expected to hear back. A motion was made by Patty and seconded by another member to recommend the village board approve a $5,000 match to the received grant; the motion carried 7-0.

### Post-Village Parks Tour Discussion
The committee conducted a lengthy park-by-park review of Municipal Park, Towering Pines, Friendship Park, Bushka Park, and Seville Park. Recurring themes across all parks included failing ADA accessibility (notably only one handicap swing in the village, which is itself inaccessible), aging playground equipment (15-20 years old), vandalism of bathrooms, poorly maintained Little Free Libraries, signage issues, and underuse of facilities. Staff member Greg confirmed budget constraints with only three part-time parks staff and five full-time streets staff covering seven parks. The committee directed staff to compile a prioritized list with rough cost estimates for the next meeting, and noted that a full assessment cannot be completed until all parks are reviewed. ADA compliance was identified as a high-priority cross-park issue.

### August 21, 2026 Concert at Towering Pines Fundraiser Event
Committee member Patty presented a plan for a fundraiser concert featuring donated band Living River on August 21, with proposed amenities including a bounce house, petting zoo, face painting, and free food funded by a $2,000 village board allocation from the community events budget. Trustees Mishka and Lesniak expressed concern about using village funds for a fundraising event with uncertain returns, and about event redundancy given National Night Out and the planned 5K. A motion by Patty for up to $2,000 from the village board was seconded for discussion purposes; the motion failed with only Patty voting yes. Following the failed vote, Patty indicated she would cancel the August 21 event and look at rescheduling to spring 2027.

### September 12, 2026 Bushka Park 5K/3K Run-Walk Fundraiser
Committee member Aaron presented a detailed plan for a 5K/3K run-walk starting at Bushka Park, passing through Towering Pines, and ending at Relocation Public Eatery, with music provided by a musician personally sponsored by Village President Dan Joling. Police Chief Terry McEwen confirmed police support for road closures. Relocation owner Jackie Fisher approved the event and proposed raffle baskets, 50/50 drawings, and potentially donating a percentage of food and drink sales. The committee discussed shirt pricing (roughly $9-$13 per shirt), registration fees ($25 per person or $50 per family discussed), and a shirt deadline for early registrants. A motion to recommend the village board approve this as an official village event passed unanimously 7-0.

### Creating a Friends of Kronenwetter Parks Support Group
Chair Mishka distributed a handout outlining steps to establish a Friends of Kronenwetter Parks nonprofit support group. The committee agreed by consensus to table detailed discussion and review the materials before the next meeting, with no vote taken.

### Future Agenda Items
Committee members proposed four future agenda items: Norm Plaza Park for a possible dog park, a disc golf course at the wellhead park (larger than Seville), water access points along the river for kayak and canoe landings, and placement of benches along walking trails. Additional future items suggested included further 5K planning details and a discussion of a 2027 village events calendar.
