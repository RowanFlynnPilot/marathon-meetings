## Meeting Overview
The Wausau Board of Public Works approved several construction bids and change orders, including awarding the 26th Street construction project to Switlick as the lowest bidder at $1,279,089.75, and approving a $14,436.50 change order for the Randolph Street/Cherry Street project due to unexpected underground conditions. All items passed unanimously.

## Key Discussions
### March 10th Board of Public Works Minutes
The minutes from the March 10th regular meeting were considered. A motion was made and seconded, and the minutes were approved unanimously with all members voting aye.

### 26th Street Construction Project Bid Opening
Seven bids were opened and read aloud. Switlick submitted the lowest bid at $1,279,089.75, with Haas coming in extremely close at $1,280,877.96. Other bidders included A1 ($1,374,600), Francis Melvin ($1,385,383), Steen ($1,489,126), James Peterson ($1,570,698.56), and Earth ($1,686,780.75). A motion to approve Switlick as the winning bidder passed unanimously. Board members noted how tight the top two bids were.

### North 8th Avenue Bid Opening
This item was postponed as the bid opening deadline was extended. It will return at a future meeting.

### Randolph Street/Cherry Street Change Order One
Staff presented Change Order One totaling $14,436.50 for four items: an inside drop on a manhole ($4,856) due to an undocumented large diameter sanitary service, water main tie-in modifications ($2,317.50) because existing pipe was 6-inch instead of recorded 8-inch, miscellaneous storm sewer tie-ins ($5,016) at $66 per linear foot, and geogrid installation ($2,247) for approximately 750 square yards near Thomas Jefferson Elementary due to unexpectedly poor soil conditions. Change Order Two regarding liquidated damages was deferred pending further discussions. The motion to approve passed unanimously.

### Randolph Street/Cherry Street Pay Estimate Nine
Pay estimate number nine from Haas Suns Incorporated for work completed through year end was presented in the amount of $535,114.20. The motion to approve passed unanimously.

### Portland Cement Concrete License - KSK Incorporated
A board member named Vinnie confirmed he reviewed the license application and everything was in order. A motion to approve the Portland cement concrete license for KSK, Inc. passed unanimously.
