## Meeting Overview
The Wausau City Council approved several key items including a development agreement for 11 Scott Street (6-3 vote), a 7-year solid waste service agreement with Harter's Fox Valley Disposal, and recognized city DPW crews for their response to a record 30.9-inch snowfall. The council also honored departing City Clerk Caitlyn and presented sustainability and mayoral citation awards.

## Key Discussions
### 11 Scott Street Development Agreement
The council approved a joint resolution for a development agreement and amended parking agreement with 11 Scott Street LLC for Waterside Place by a 6-3 vote. Developer Mark Craig and Raleigh Lray spoke during public comment, noting the $10 million project would create 52 mid-priced residential units in a 100,000 square foot building vacant for 3 years. Alder Rasmussen supported the project citing the need to replace downtown workers with downtown residents and return parking spaces to public use. Alder Neil highlighted the project would generate $55,000 annually in parking revenue and help close TID 8 sooner. Alder Larson dissented, arguing the city shouldn't discount its assets during budget cuts. Alder Tyranny questioned how the city could provide 150 alternative parking spaces within 300 yards if the ramp closed; Economic Development Director Randy Feifer explained this replaced an existing agreement that required 480 spaces, reducing obligations while generating new revenue.

### DPW Snow Removal Recognition
Mayor Denny presented a mayoral citation recognizing the Department of Public Works plow crews and municipal fleet staff for their response to a historic 30.9-inch snowfall from March 14-16, 2026. Kevin Kester, street supervisor, accepted the award and praised the plow operators and mechanics, stating 'you kicked its ass' to applause. The mayor noted four municipal fleet technicians worked 12-hour shifts providing 24-hour breakdown support, with two volunteering on Saturday for downtown snow removal. By Friday, these technicians will have worked 12 straight days without a day off.

### Sustainability Award to Kolbe and Kolbe
Christine Daniels from the Sustainability, Energy and Environment Committee presented the 2026 City of Wausau Sustainability Award to Kolbe and Kolbe Millwork. Mike Thompson and Keith Kaning accepted, describing their solar panel installation of over 2,000 panels on their manufacturing facility that became operational in July, generating enough power for about 120 homes and exceeding expectations. They also highlighted LED high bay lighting projects and recycling initiatives for wood, aluminum, glass, vinyl, cardboard, and other materials.

### Solid Waste and Recycling Service Agreement
The council approved a 7-year residential solid waste and recycling service agreement with Harter's Fox Valley Disposal by a 9-0 vote. Mayor Denny noted a previous mix-up on whether the term was 7 or 10 years, confirming the corrected 7-year term matched what Public Health and Safety Committee had forwarded.

### Settlement Resolution - David Holes vs City of Wausau
Assistant City Attorney Vincent Bonito explained a 2022 bus accident where Transit Mutual (city's insurer) paid the claim, then the individual who crashed into the bus filed a personal injury claim. The city filed a counter claim and third-party complaint. The insurer agreed to pay bus damages to the city's insurance company, seeking release of the city's counter claim. Alder Neil clarified this was separate from the individual's ongoing injury claim. Approved 8-1 without need for closed session.

### Sarah Rafi Day Proclamation
Mayor Denny summarized a proclamation read two weeks prior to approximately 50 people, honoring attorney Sarah Rafi who was diagnosed with stage 4 glioblastoma brain cancer in July. He noted Brad Gessel also recently passed from the same disease. The proclamation declares March 31st (Sarah's birthday) as Sarah Rafi Day in Wausau, recognizing her 30 years of law practice and her work empowering women through her 'Woman Warrior' podcast and book 'Be Happy in Both Worlds.'

## Public Comment
Two speakers addressed the council. Raleigh Lray spoke in support of the 11 Scott Street project, describing it as a green sustainable repurposing of a vacant building that would add mid-priced apartments downtown. Mark Craig of 3246 North 8th Street joined her, noting the $10 million project to revitalize a 100,000 square foot building and create 52 residential units, stating 'Without your help, it won't happen.'
