## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Board of Trustees approved multiple ordinances including rezoning requests and a speed limit change for Weston Avenue, though the board amended the original speed limit proposal to keep the Von Kennel to Highway J section at 45 mph rather than reducing it to 35 mph. A citizen delivered pointed criticism of the board's approach to funding the fire department through referendum rather than budget prioritization, and the finance director responded emotionally defending staff efforts and budget constraints.

## Key Discussions
### Public Comment on Fire Department Funding
Jim Pensel of 5002 Aerrol Street criticized the board's approach to fire department funding, stating the department is 'severely understaffed' and the board has offered only 'band-aids.' He argued the referendum has no sunset date and won't solve long-term funding problems. He urged the board to prioritize fire/EMS over 'wants' like artificial turf and the aquatic center. Finance Director Jessica responded emotionally, explaining the village cannot borrow for additional firefighters and defending staff efforts, noting the village is 'the cheapest' and 'most efficient' but cannot continue without more revenue.

### Ordinance 26-006 Speed Limits on Weston Avenue
The original ordinance to change speed limits failed on first vote with 4 nos (Maloney, Jordan, the presiding officer, and one other). Trustee Maloney argued the 35 mph limit from Von Kennel to Ryan made no sense given the road conditions compared to other village streets. A second motion was made by Maloney to keep Camp Phillips to Von Kennel at 35 mph but return Von Kennel to Highway J to 45 mph. This amended version passed with Jordan seconding and only Trustee Carne voting in favor of the original proposal.

### Intersection Signage at Community Center Drive and Birch Street
The board approved changing the stop sign to a yield sign, with a friendly amendment adding a stop sign for bicyclists coming off the pedestrian bridge. Trustee Hooang raised safety concerns about cyclists coming off the bridge at 15-20 mph without any signage. The amendment was accepted and the motion passed unanimously.

### Baseball/Softball Field Maintenance Agreement
The board approved a 10-year agreement with youth baseball and softball organizations for field maintenance at Kennedy Park. The Parks and Recreation committee recommended the longer term to protect the village's investment and provide stability despite leadership changes on both sides. Passed unanimously.

### Remote Meeting Attendance Policy
Trustee Lewis recommended postponing this item until the next meeting so the newly elected board could make the decision. The board unanimously agreed to defer to the April 21st meeting.

### Microsoft Teams for Communication
The board approved using Microsoft Teams for trustee communication starting with the next term. Staff will provide training at an upcoming meeting. Passed unanimously.

## Public Comment
Jim Pensel of 5002 Aerrol Street spoke during public comment, criticizing the board's approach to fire department funding. He attended the inaugural SAFER citizen academy and expressed appreciation for fire/EMS staff while arguing the board should fully fund the department through budget prioritization rather than a referendum. He specifically criticized spending on Kennedy Park turf and the aquatic center as 'wants' versus the 'need' of public safety funding.
