## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Tourism Commission approved four grant requests totaling $22,500 for local events including the Mountain Bay Cup soccer tournament ($5,000), GG's 5K Glow Fun Run ($2,500), Hmong Wausau Festival ($10,000), and Wisconsin Valley Fair ($5,000). The commission also scheduled a special meeting for May 18th to address a missed Taste and Glow Balloon Festival application.

## Key Discussions
### Public Comments
Jim Pensson of 500 Street Weston raised concerns about grant applications claiming up to 2,000 hotel room bookings, questioning whether that exceeds actual hotel capacity in Weston and the greater Wausau area. He also asked about coordination with Village of Weston restaurants and businesses as promised in after-action reports. The chair noted public comment would not receive immediate response but would be discussed during applications.

### CVB Reports
Tim White, Visit Wausau Executive Director, reported on upcoming events including the Wisconsin Bike Fed summit and Marathon County Parks film festival. He announced the World Horseshoe Tournament is progressing well with expected 800 participants (up from 644 in Salt Lake City last year), and ESPN coverage is being added. White introduced new Director of Operations Jamie Rice Hecondorf and acknowledged he missed the Taste and Glow Balloon Festival grant application, taking responsibility for the oversight. He noted there are approximately 2,400 hotel rooms in the greater Wausau area. Report acknowledged unanimously.

### Grant Request - Mountain Bay Cup
Dan Kubat and Betsy Kubat from MC United Soccer Club requested $5,000 for their spring soccer tournament May 1-3. Kubat reported 170 teams registered with 68.5% (116 teams) traveling more than 90 miles, estimating $700,000 to $1 million economic impact and 8,000-10,000 attendees. Games will be held at Peoples, Airport Park, and Greener Turner Center. This is a new spring tournament separate from the fall event previously funded. Motion by Renee, second provided, approved unanimously.

### Grant Request - GG's 5K Glow Fun Run Walk
Erica presented online for the sixth annual event at Weston YMCA. She reported securing WAOW advertising and noted participants come from Minnesota, Texas, Michigan, and Illinois. New this year is a cornhole tournament. The event includes a foam machine as a signature attraction. Renee moved to approve $2,500, seconded by Jackson, approved unanimously.

### Grant Request - Hmong Wausau Festival
Elang Jang, festival chair since 2017, requested $10,000 for the August 1-2 event. Last year drew 12,000-13,000 attendees; this year expects 14,000-15,000 with addition of under-18 soccer and volleyball tournaments bringing 500-800 additional athletes plus families. Jang noted hotels within an hour of the event are fully booked and emphasized using local vendors. Motion by Husane to approve $10,000, seconded by Renee, approved unanimously.

### Grant Request - Wisconsin Valley Fair
No representative present online or in person. Commission discussed that the fair requested $10,000 but received $5,000 last year. Commissioners questioned direct benefit to Weston, noting the application listed only 3-4 hotel rooms and hotels contacted were in Wausau and Rothschild, not Weston. One commissioner noted the event is largely self-contained with local attendance. Motion by Husane to approve $5,000, seconded by Renee, approved unanimously.

### Taste and Glow Balloon Festival Application
Staff noted the application was missed due to Tim White's oversight. The next regular meeting July 20th would be after the event. Commission decided to schedule a special meeting May 18th at 4:00 PM to consider the application so the event could include the village in advertising materials.

## Public Comment
Jim Pensson of 500 Street Weston commented online, questioning the accuracy of hotel room booking claims in grant applications (suggesting 2,000 rooms exceeds actual capacity) and asking how applicants coordinate with Village of Weston businesses as promised in after-action reports.
