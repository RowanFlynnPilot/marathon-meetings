## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Finance and Human Resources Committee approved previous meeting minutes and acknowledged April financial reports. The meeting featured an educational presentation by Municipal Clerk Pam on clerk office operations, elections, licensing, and budget. Committee member Evan announced his resignation as he is moving away from Weston.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of minutes from previous Finance and HR meeting
Motion to approve made by Ernling, seconded by Daniels. Motion carried with no discussion.

### April budget status report and TIFF reports
Jessica provided information on TIFF district closure timeline, anticipating October/November for final payoff. The CDA will need to pass a resolution to call the bonds due October 1st. A committee member confirmed the final numbers still look good for paying off the debt. Motion to acknowledge items 5, 6, and 7 made by Daniels, seconded by Soaper. Motion carried.

### Municipal Clerk's Office Educational Presentation
Clerk Pam presented comprehensive overview of clerk office operations. The office went from four full-time staff to currently two full-time and one part-time employee. She detailed elections administration noting they have over 700 absentee applications already on file for August election. Discussion included Badger Books replacement at $11,000 for four units, postage costs jumping from $6,000 to $15,000 due to fall elections, and pet licensing being brought back in-house from third-party Pet Data saving approximately $5,495 annually. Committee discussed the pet license fee increase from $10 to $20, which was required due to Marathon County raising fees to cover their $100,000 annual payment to the Humane Society. Newsletter costs of approximately $30,000 annually were discussed with suggestions to potentially survey residents again about digital alternatives.

### Staff remarks and committee business
Discussion of farmers market success at new location with staff arriving at 6 a.m. on Saturdays. Concerns raised about parking on Alderson Street during market hours, with clarification that only the west side allows parking. Committee member Evan announced his resignation as he and his wife are moving away from Weston. Chair noted open committee spots and encouraged outreach to potential new members.
