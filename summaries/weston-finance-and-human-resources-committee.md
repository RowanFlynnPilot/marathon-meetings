## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Finance and Human Resources Committee approved previous meeting minutes and acknowledged April budget and financial reports. The meeting featured an extensive educational presentation from Municipal Clerk Pam about the clerk's office operations, election administration, licensing, and budget considerations. Committee member Evan announced his resignation as he is moving out of Weston.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of minutes from previous Finance and HR meeting
Motion to approve made by Ernling, seconded by Daniels. No discussion. Motion carried unanimously with voice vote.

### TIFF one and TIFF two detail reports
A committee member asked about final numbers for TIF district closure. Staff explained they are anticipating October-November for bond payoff, noting the bonds are due October 1st. The CDA will need to pass a resolution to call the bonds. Staff confirmed the numbers still look good and they will be able to pay off the debt.

### Acknowledgment of work product transmittals (items 5, 6, and 7)
Motion to acknowledge the April budget status report, TIFF reports, and legal details report made by Daniels, seconded by Soaper. Motion carried unanimously.

### Municipal Clerk's Office Educational Presentation
Clerk Pam presented comprehensive overview of clerk's office operations including elections, licensing, tax collection, and pet licensing. She noted the office went from four full-time staff to currently two full-time and one part-time employee. Key points included: 84% of tax collection ($22 million) is done through walk-ins at clerk's office; they manage 51 liquor licenses; pet licensing was brought back in-house from third-party vendor Pet Data saving approximately $5,495 annually; and The Wire newsletter costs approximately $30,000 annually for postage and printing. Discussion included poll worker wages being average for the county but chief inspector pay being on the lower end at current rate.

### Farmers Market Update
Staff member Renee reported the farmers market at Kennedy Park has been going well. She has been present for 6-7 hours each Saturday since opening. Vendors are happy with the setup and location. Some Tuesday markets have had weather challenges. The market is full with food trucks, coffee vendors, craft vendors, and produce vendors with greenhouses. Committee members raised concerns about parking on both sides of Alderson and Newport streets causing traffic congestion.

### Committee Member Resignation
Evan announced he is stepping down from the committee as he and his wife are moving away from Weston, though staying in the metro area. This was his last meeting. The chair noted there are now open spots on this committee and encouraged members to reach out to citizens who may want to participate.

## Public Comment
No public comment was offered.
