## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Finance and Human Resources Committee approved previous meeting minutes and acknowledged financial reports including budget status and TIF district reports. The meeting featured an extensive educational presentation from Village Clerk Pam on the municipal clerk's office operations, covering elections, licensing, tax collection, and staffing challenges. Committee member Evan announced his resignation from the committee.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of minutes from previous meeting
Motion to approve minutes was made by Ernling and seconded by Daniels. The motion passed unanimously with no discussion.

### TIF one and TIF two detail reports
A committee member asked about the final numbers for TIF closure. Jessica explained they are anticipating October/November for bond payoff, noting the CDA will need to pass a resolution to call the bonds. She confirmed the village will be able to pay off the debt and described the process involving Ellers and DTC for notifying bond holders.

### Acknowledgment of work product transmittals (items 5, 6, and 7)
Motion to acknowledge the April budget status report, TIF detail reports, and legal details report was made by Daniels and seconded by Soaper. Motion carried unanimously.

### Municipal Clerk's Office educational presentation
Village Clerk Pam presented an overview of the clerk's office operations with three staff members (two full-time, one part-time). She detailed election administration including Badger Book electronic poll books ($11,000 for four units), explaining postage costs jumped from $6,000 to $15,000 due to fall elections with over 700 absentee applications already on file. She noted poll worker wages are average at $12/hour but chief inspector pay at $13/hour is below average and will likely be increased to $15/hour next term. Pet licensing was brought back in-house from Pet Data, saving approximately $5,495 annually. The clerk's office handles 84% of tax collection ($22 million) at the counter. Newsletter costs total approximately $30,000 annually for printing and postage to 5,089 postal patrons.

### Farmers market update
Staff member Renee reported the farmers market has been going well with full vendor capacity. She noted some Tuesday vendors had to leave due to wind damaging their plants and tents. The market has expanded to include coffee vendors, new food trucks, and craft vendors. Produce vendors will increase mid to late June. A committee member raised concerns about parking on Alderson Street, and staff clarified parking is only allowed on the west side while the east side remains no parking.

### Committee member resignation
Evan announced he is stepping down from the committee as he and his wife are moving away from Weston, though staying in the metro area. Committee members thanked him for his service.

## Public Comment
No public comment was offered.
