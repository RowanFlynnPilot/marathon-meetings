## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Finance and Human Resources Committee approved minutes and acknowledged budget reports before receiving an extensive educational presentation on the Municipal Clerk's Office operations, covering elections, licensing, tax collection, and staffing. Committee member Evan Easterling announced his resignation from the committee due to moving out of the village.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of minutes
Motion to approve minutes from the previous Finance and HR meeting was made by Ernling and seconded by Daniels. The motion carried unanimously with no discussion.

### TIF 1 and TIF 2 detail reports
Committee member asked about the final TIF numbers and bond payoff process. Staff member Jessica explained they are anticipating October/November for final action, that the CDA will need to pass a resolution to call the bonds (due October 1st), and confirmed the village will be able to pay off the debt. The process involves contacting Ehlers, who will notify US Associate Trust and DTC for official bondholder payment.

### Acknowledgment of work product transmittals (items 5, 6, 7)
The April budget status report, TIF 1 and TIF 2 detail reports, and legal details report were acknowledged together. Motion by Daniels, seconded by Soaper. Motion carried unanimously.

### Municipal Clerk's Office presentation
Clerk Pam presented extensively on clerk office operations. Key points included: staffing reduced from four full-time to two full-time plus one part-time (Leah); elections budget varies significantly between even years (presidential/gubernatorial) and odd years; Badger Books cost approximately $11,000 for four units; 84% of tax collection ($22 million) is handled at the clerk's office counter; pet licensing was brought in-house in 2025 after 12 years with third-party Pet Data, saving approximately $5,495 annually; and the quarterly newsletter costs approximately $30,000 annually for postage and printing to reach 5,089 postal patrons.

### Pet licensing fee increase discussion
Committee discussed the fee increase for neutered dogs from $10 to $20. Pam explained Marathon County requested the increase because they pay about $100,000 annually to the Humane Society but were only collecting $65,000 from municipalities. Discussion also covered the non-dog stray contract costing $175 per animal, including eight mice that were picked up. A committee member suggested requiring law enforcement to bring in strays rather than allowing residents to do so directly.

### Farmers market update
Staff member Renee reported the farmers market has been going well, with her present for 6-7 hours on Saturdays since opening. Tuesday markets have had some weather challenges. New additions include coffee vendors, food trucks, and craft vendors. Produce vendors expected mid to end of June. Full vendor capacity reported. Committee member noted vendors were 'very happy' with the setup and location. Discussion of parking concerns on Alderson (west side only now allows parking) and Newport streets.

### Committee member resignation
Evan Easterling announced he is stepping down from the committee as his last meeting, as he and his wife are moving away from Weston but staying in the metro area. He thanked the committee for his time serving.

## Public Comment
No public comment was offered.
