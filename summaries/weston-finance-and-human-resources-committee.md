## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Finance and Human Resources Committee held a routine meeting approving April budget and financial reports, and received an educational presentation from Municipal Clerk Pam on the clerk's office operations, elections, licensing, and budget. Committee member Evan announced his resignation as he is moving out of Weston.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of minutes
Motion to approve minutes from the previous Finance and HR Committee meeting was made by Ernling and seconded by Daniels. The motion passed unanimously with no discussion.

### TIFF one and TIFF two detail reports
A committee member asked about the final numbers for TIFF closure. Staff explained they anticipate calling the bonds in October/November, with bonds due October 1st. The CDA will need to pass a resolution to call the bonds, likely this summer. Staff confirmed the village will be able to pay off the debt as planned.

### Work product acknowledgment (items 5-7)
Motion to acknowledge the April budget status report, TIFF detail reports, and legal details report was made by Daniels and seconded by Soaper. The motion carried unanimously.

### Municipal Clerk's Office presentation
Clerk Pam presented a comprehensive overview of the clerk's office operations, staffing (now 2 full-time and 1 part-time employee, down from 4 full-time previously), elections administration including Badger Book equipment purchases ($11,000 for four units), licensing (51 liquor licenses, pet licensing brought back in-house saving approximately $55/year in third-party fees), and tax collection (84% or $22 million collected at the counter). Discussion included pet licensing fee increase from $10 to $20 driven by Marathon County's increased fees to the Humane Society, and newsletter costs of approximately $30,000 annually for printing and postage to 5,089 postal patrons.

### Farmers market update
Staff member Renee reported the farmers market at Kennedy Park has been going well over the past three Saturdays. Vendors are happy with the new setup and location. The market is full with food trucks, coffee vendors, craft vendors, and some produce vendors with greenhouses. Committee members discussed parking concerns on Alderson and Newport streets, with staff noting the west side of Alderson now allows parking while the east side remains no parking.

### Committee member resignation
Evan announced this would be his last meeting as he and his wife are moving away from Weston, though remaining in the metro area. He thanked the committee for the opportunity to serve. The chair noted there are now open spots on this committee and others, encouraging members to reach out to interested citizens.

## Public Comment
No public comment was offered.
