## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Finance and Human Resources Committee approved meeting minutes and acknowledged monthly financial reports. The meeting featured an extensive educational presentation by Municipal Clerk Pam on the clerk's office operations, covering elections, licensing, tax collection, and the quarterly newsletter. Committee member Evan Erling announced he would be stepping down from the committee.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of previous meeting minutes
Motion to approve minutes was made by Erling and seconded by Daniels. The motion carried with all in favor, no opposition.

### TIF one and TIF two detail reports
A committee member asked about the final numbers for TIF closure. Staff explained they are anticipating October/November for bond payoff, noting the bonds are due October 1st. The CDA will need to pass a resolution to call the bonds, likely this summer. Staff confirmed the numbers look good and they will be able to pay off the debt.

### Work product acknowledgment (items 5, 6, and 7)
Motion to acknowledge the April budget status report, TIF detail reports, and legal details report was made by Daniels and seconded by Soaper. Motion carried unanimously.

### Municipal Clerk's Office Educational Presentation
Clerk Pam presented an overview of the clerk's office operations with three staff members (two full-time, one part-time). She covered elections administration including Badger Book electronic poll books ($11,000 for four units), explaining 84% of tax collection ($22 million) is processed through the clerk's office walk-ins. Discussion included chief inspector pay potentially increasing from current rate to $15/hour, pet licensing fees doubling from $10 to $20 at Marathon County's request, and the quarterly newsletter costing approximately $30,000 annually for printing and postage to 5,089 postal patrons.

### Farmers Market Update
Staff member Renee reported the farmers market has been going well, with her arriving at 6 AM the last three Saturdays to help vendors. The market has expanded to include food trucks, coffee vendors, and craft people. Vendors expressed happiness with the new setup and location. Discussion addressed parking concerns on Alderson and Newport streets, with staff noting parking is now allowed on the west side of Alderson while the east side remains no parking.

### Committee Member Resignation
Evan Erling announced he would be stepping down from the committee as he and his wife are moving away from Weston, though staying in the metro area. He thanked the committee for his time serving. The chair noted there are now open spots on this and other committees and encouraged outreach to interested citizens.

## Public Comment
No public comment was offered.
