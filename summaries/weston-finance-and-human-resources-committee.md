## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Finance and Human Resources Committee approved previous meeting minutes and acknowledged April financial reports. The meeting featured an educational presentation by Municipal Clerk Pam on the clerk's office budget and operations, covering elections, licensing, tax collection, and the quarterly newsletter. Committee member Evan announced he is stepping down as this was his last meeting.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of minutes from previous Finance and HR meeting
Motion to approve made by Ernling, seconded by Daniels. No discussion. Motion carried unanimously with voice vote.

### TIFF one and TIFF two detail reports
A committee member asked about TIF closure timeline. Staff explained they are anticipating October/November for final closure, with bonds due October 1st. The CDA will need to pass a resolution to call the bonds, likely this summer. Staff confirmed the numbers look good and they will be able to pay off the debt.

### Work product transmittals (items 5, 6, and 7)
Motion to acknowledge the April budget status report, TIF detail reports, and legal details report made by Daniels, seconded by Soaper. Motion carried unanimously.

### Municipal Clerk's Office educational presentation
Clerk Pam presented comprehensive overview of the clerk's office operations. The office has reduced from four full-time staff to two full-time and one part-time employee. She noted 84% of tax collection ($22 million) is handled through walk-ins at the clerk's office. Pet licensing was brought back in-house in 2025 after 12 years with third-party Pet Data, saving approximately $5,495 annually. Committee discussed the pet license fee increase from $10 to $20, which was driven by Marathon County raising fees to cover their $100,000 annual payment to the Humane Society. The $175 per animal cost for non-dog strays was questioned, including $175 charged for picking up mice. Newsletter costs of approximately $30,000 annually for printing and postage were discussed, with staff noting previous attempts to eliminate mailing were reversed due to resident complaints.

### Farmers market update
Staff member Renee reported the farmers market at Kennedy Park has been going well for three weeks. Saturdays have been successful with full vendor capacity including food trucks, coffee vendors, craft people, and greenhouse vendors. Tuesdays have been challenging due to weather. Committee members raised concerns about parking on Alderson and Newport streets. Staff clarified parking is only allowed on the west side of Alderson, with no parking restrictions on Newport.

### Committee member resignation
Evan announced he is stepping down from the committee as he and his wife are moving away from Weston, though staying in the metro area. This was his last meeting. Committee thanked him for his service.
