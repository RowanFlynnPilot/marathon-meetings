## Meeting Overview
The Finance and Human Resources Committee debated clothing and equipment allowances for public works employees after canceling a Cintas uniform contract, ultimately recommending $400 for remainder of 2026 and $500 annually starting 2027, plus a washer/dryer purchase. The meeting also included a comprehensive presentation on public works operations and budget, showing the department operates below average costs compared to other central Wisconsin communities.

## Key Discussions
### Public Works Operations and Budget Presentation
Public Works Director Michael delivered an extensive presentation covering the department's management of 119.5 centerline miles of roads, 50+ pieces of equipment, and 114 miles of water main. He reported the 2026 public works budget decreased by $26,000 (1.1%) from 2025. Michael highlighted staff worked 16-17 hour shifts during a recent major snow event, with estimated costs of $50,000 for that single storm. The village is applying for disaster relief funds through Marathon County. He emphasized the village spends approximately $9,700 less per mile on streets than the average central Wisconsin community, and noted transportation aids are calculated based on a six-year spending average, warning that reducing street spending could eventually reduce state funding.

### Clothing and Equipment Allowance Amendments
Significant debate occurred over increasing the clothing stipend after canceling the Cintas uniform contract. Trustee Daniels argued against the full $600 increase, citing fiscal responsibility and the upcoming fire department referendum needs, suggesting $400 instead. Michael advocated strongly for maintaining employee benefits, noting staff had already lost benefits over the years and the village's lower-than-average compensation. Multiple motions failed: the original $600 motion failed 2-3 (Daniels and Love yes; Armain, My, and Sober no), a $400 motion failed 2-3, and a $500 with washer/dryer motion also failed. The committee ultimately approved 4-1 a motion for $400 for remainder of 2026, $500 annually starting 2027, plus a one-time washer/dryer purchase.

### February Financial Reports and Minutes
All routine items passed unanimously. Minutes from February 16, 2026 were approved with motion by Steve and second by Stephanie. February financial reports for all funds were acknowledged, as were T1 and T2 detail reports and legal details for February.

## Public Comment
Lisa Beck of 1808 Cortez Lane offered public comment. She praised Public Works Director Mike for the department's work during the recent storm. She also expressed concerns about the proposed clothing allowance increase, questioning whether employees needed the highest proposed amount given they already receive safety apparel, shirts, hats, and $300 for boots/pants, suggesting the village could save money by choosing a lesser amount.
