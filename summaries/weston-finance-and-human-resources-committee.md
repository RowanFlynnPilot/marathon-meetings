## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Finance and Human Resources Committee approved previous meeting minutes and acknowledged April budget and financial reports. The meeting featured an extensive educational presentation from Municipal Clerk Pam about the clerk's office operations, elections, licensing, and budget. Committee member Evan announced his resignation as he is moving away from Weston.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of minutes from previous meeting
Motion to approve was made by Ernling and seconded by Daniels. The motion carried with no opposition.

### TIF one and TIF two detail reports
A committee member asked about the timeline for TIF closure. Staff explained they are anticipating October-November for calling the bonds, which are due October 1st. The CDA will need to pass a resolution to call the bonds, likely this summer. Staff confirmed the numbers still look good and they will be able to pay off the debt.

### Acknowledgment of work product transmittals (items 5, 6, and 7)
Motion to acknowledge the April budget status report, TIF detail reports, and legal details report was made by Daniels and seconded by Soaper. Motion carried with no opposition.

### Municipal Clerk's Office Educational Presentation
Clerk Pam presented extensively on the clerk's office operations covering elections, licensing, tax collection, and staffing. She noted the office went from four full-time staff to currently two full-time and one part-time, with plans to add another part-time person. She highlighted that 84% of tax collection ($22 million) is processed through the clerk's office walk-ins. Discussion occurred about pet licensing fees increasing from $10 to $20 due to Marathon County raising fees to cover Humane Society costs. Committee members discussed the $30,000 annual cost of the quarterly newsletter 'The Wire' and whether alternatives like email distribution could reduce costs, but noted previous attempts to eliminate mailing were met with resident pushback.

### Farmers Market Update
Staff member Renee reported the farmers market has been going well, with her attending the last three Saturdays from 6 AM for 6-7 hours. The market has expanded to include coffee vendors, new food trucks, and craft vendors. Produce vendors will increase mid to late June. Committee members raised concerns about parking on Alderson and Newport streets near the market, with staff clarifying that parking is only allowed on the west side of Alderson.

### Committee Member Resignation
Evan announced he will be stepping down from the committee as he and his wife are moving away from Weston, though staying in the metro area. He thanked the committee for his time serving. The chair noted there are now open spots on this and other committees and encouraged members to reach out to citizens who may be interested in participating.

## Public Comment
No public comment was offered. The chair asked for public comments from the audience and online, and none were received.
