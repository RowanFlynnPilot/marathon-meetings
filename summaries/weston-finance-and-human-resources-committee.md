## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Finance and Human Resources Committee approved previous meeting minutes and acknowledged financial reports including April budget status and TIF district reports. The meeting featured an extensive educational presentation from Municipal Clerk Pam detailing clerk's office operations, election administration, licensing, and budget considerations. Committee member Evan announced his resignation as he is moving out of Weston.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of minutes from previous Finance and HR meeting
Motion to approve made by Ernling, seconded by Daniels. Passed unanimously with no discussion.

### TIF 1 and TIF 2 detail reports
Committee discussed TIF district closure timeline. Staff indicated they are anticipating October-November for final bond payoff, with bonds due October 1st. The CDA will need to pass a resolution to call the bonds, likely this summer. Staff confirmed final numbers still look good and they will be able to pay off the debt.

### Work product acknowledgment (items 5, 6, 7)
Motion to acknowledge the April budget status report, TIF detail reports, and legal details report was made by Daniels, seconded by Soaper. Passed unanimously.

### Municipal Clerk's Office Educational Presentation
Clerk Pam presented comprehensive overview of clerk's office operations. The office has reduced from four full-time staff to two full-time and one part-time employee. She noted they handled the entire 2024 presidential election with just two staff members. Key topics included: election administration costs varying between even and odd years, Badger Book electronic poll book replacement at approximately $11,000 for four units, postage budget increasing from $6,000 to $15,000 for fall elections due to absentee ballot volume, and pet licensing being brought back in-house after 12 years with Pet Data saving approximately $55 annually. The quarterly newsletter 'The Wire' costs approximately $30,000 annually for printing and postage to 5,089 postal patrons.

### Poll worker wages discussion
Clerk presented county survey data showing Weston's election inspectors are paid average ($12/hour) but chief inspectors are on the lower end. Pam indicated she will likely propose increasing chief inspector pay to $15/hour in the next election term due to their increased responsibilities.

### Pet licensing fee increase
Committee discussed recent increase in pet licensing fees from $10 to $20 for neutered animals. Pam explained Marathon County contacted municipalities because they pay approximately $100,000 annually to the Humane Society but were only collecting about $65,000 from municipalities. The non-dog stray contract was also discussed, with committee members noting costs of $175-200 per animal including mice, suggesting future review of requiring law enforcement to bring in strays.

### Farmers Market update
Staff member Renee reported the farmers market has been going well with good vendor turnout and customer response. New food trucks, coffee vendors, and craft people have been added. Produce vendors will start coming mid to late June. Committee member reported vendors were very happy with the setup and location. Discussion included concerns about parking on Alderson and Newport streets during busy periods.

### Committee member resignation
Committee member Evan announced he is stepping down as this is his last meeting. He and his wife are moving away from Weston but staying in the metro area. He thanked the committee for his time serving.
