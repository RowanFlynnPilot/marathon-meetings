## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Finance and Human Resources Committee approved minutes, acknowledged budget reports, and received an educational presentation on the Municipal Clerk's Office operations. Committee member Evan Erdling announced his resignation from the committee due to moving out of Weston.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of minutes
Motion to approve minutes was made by Ernling and seconded by Daniels. The motion passed unanimously with no discussion.

### Budget and TIF reports
The committee reviewed the April budget status report, TIF one and TIF two detail reports, and legal details report. Regarding TIF closure, Jessica explained the bonds are due October 1st and the CDA will need to pass a resolution to call the bonds, likely this summer. She confirmed the village will be able to pay off the debt. Motion to acknowledge items 5, 6, and 7 was made by Daniels and seconded by Soaper, passing unanimously.

### Municipal Clerk's Office presentation
Clerk Pam presented an overview of clerk office operations, staffing (herself and Sarah full-time, Leah part-time), and budgets. Key points included: elections budget varies significantly between even years (presidential/gubernatorial) and odd years; postage jumped from $6,000 to $15,000 due to fall elections; Badger Books cost about $11,000 for four units; pet licensing was brought back in-house saving $5,495 annually from the previous Pet Data contract; and the quarterly newsletter costs approximately $30,000 annually for printing and postage. Discussion occurred about potential cost savings on the newsletter, with staff noting previous attempts to eliminate paper copies were met with resident complaints requesting their return.

### Poll worker wages
Pam presented survey data from 52 Marathon County municipalities showing Weston's election inspector pay of $12/hour is average, but chief inspector pay is on the lower end. She indicated she would likely propose increasing chief inspector pay to $15/hour when the two-year term comes around due to the significant responsibilities involved.

### Pet licensing fee increase
Discussion addressed why pet licensing fees doubled from $10 to $20. Pam explained Marathon County raised fees charged to municipalities because they were only collecting $65,000 while paying $100,000 annually to the Humane Society. The fee increase was passed to municipalities. A non-dog stray contract with Mountain Bay Metro was discussed, with one committee member noting that eight mice cost $175 each to process, suggesting future policy review.

### Farmers market update
Staff member Renee reported the farmers market at Kennedy Park is going well, with full vendor slots including food trucks, coffee vendors, craft people, and greenhouse vendors. Some vendors have had to leave on windy Tuesdays. Produce vendors expected to increase mid to late June. Committee members discussed parking concerns on Alderson and Newport streets, with staff clarifying parking is now allowed on the west side of Alderson only.

### Committee member resignation
Evan Erdling announced this was his last meeting as he and his wife are moving away from Weston, though remaining in the metro area. He thanked the committee for the opportunity to serve. The chair noted there are now open spots on this and other committees and encouraged members to reach out to interested citizens.

## Public Comment
No public comment was offered.
