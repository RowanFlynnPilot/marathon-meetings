## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Finance and Human Resources Committee approved previous meeting minutes and acknowledged budget reports for April. The meeting featured an extensive educational presentation from Municipal Clerk Pam on the clerk's office operations, including elections, licensing, tax collection, and the newsletter budget. Committee member Evan announced he is stepping down as this was his last meeting.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of minutes from previous Finance and HR meeting
Motion to approve made by Ernling, seconded by Daniels. No discussion. Motion carried unanimously with voice vote.

### April budget status report and TIFF reports
Jessica presented the April budget status report and TIFF one and TIFF two detail reports. A committee member asked about the timeline for TIFF closure, and Jessica explained they are anticipating October-November for bond payoff. The CDA will need to pass a resolution to call the bonds, which are due October 1st. Jessica confirmed the numbers still look good and they will be able to pay off the debt. Motion to acknowledge items 5, 6, and 7 made by Daniels, seconded by Soaper. Motion carried unanimously.

### Municipal Clerk's Office educational presentation
Clerk Pam presented an extensive overview of the clerk's office operations. The office currently has two full-time employees (Pam and Sarah) and one part-time employee (Leah), down from four full-time staff previously. Pam explained that 84% of tax collection ($22 million) is handled at the clerk's office counter between mid-December and January 31st. Discussion covered election costs, noting postage increased from $6,000 to $15,000 due to fall elections and absentee ballots. The quarterly newsletter costs approximately $30,000 annually ($9,000 postage, $20,000 printing) for 5,089 postal patrons. Committee discussed potentially reducing newsletter costs, but staff noted previous attempts to eliminate mailing resulted in resident complaints requesting its return.

### Pet licensing fee increase
Pam explained that pet licensing fees increased from $10 to $20 because Marathon County raised fees charged to municipalities to help cover their $100,000 annual payment to the Humane Society. Discussion included the cost of non-dog stray contracts, with one committee member noting that eight mice cost $175 each to process through the Humane Society. A suggestion was made to require animals be brought in by law enforcement to be billed to the municipality.

### Farmers market update
Staff member Renee reported the farmers market has been going well. She has been present the last three Saturdays from 6 a.m. for 6-7 hours. The market is full with food trucks, coffee vendors, craft people, and some early produce. Concerns were raised about parking on Alderson and Newport streets. Staff clarified parking is only allowed on the west side of Alderson, though the east side is still signed no parking.

### Committee member resignation
Evan announced he is stepping down from the committee as he and his wife are moving away from Weston, though staying in the metro area. He thanked the committee for the opportunity to serve. The chair noted there are now multiple open spots on this and other committees and encouraged members to recruit interested citizens.

## Public Comment
No public comment was offered.
