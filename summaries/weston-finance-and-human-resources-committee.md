## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Finance and Human Resources Committee approved previous meeting minutes and acknowledged monthly financial reports. The meeting featured an extensive educational presentation by Municipal Clerk Pam on the clerk's office operations, elections, licensing, and budget. Committee member Evan announced his resignation as he is moving out of the village.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of minutes from previous Finance and HR meeting
Motion to approve made by Ernling, seconded by Daniels. Passed unanimously with no discussion.

### TIF 1 and TIF 2 detail reports for April
Jessica from finance discussed the timeline for TIF closure, anticipating October-November for final bond payoff. She explained the CDA will need to pass a resolution to call the bonds, which are due October 1st. A committee member asked about final numbers, and Jessica confirmed they will be able to pay off the debt.

### Work product acknowledgment (items 5, 6, and 7)
Motion to acknowledge the April budget status report, TIF detail reports, and legal details report made by Daniels, seconded by Soaper. Passed unanimously.

### Municipal Clerk's Office Educational Presentation
Municipal Clerk Pam delivered an extensive presentation on clerk's office operations. She noted the office went from four full-time staff to currently two full-time and one part-time employee, with plans to add another part-time worker. She covered election administration including Badger Book electronic poll books ($11,000 for four units), licensing operations (51 liquor licenses, bartender licenses), tax collection (84% or $22 million collected at the clerk's office counter), pet licensing brought back in-house saving approximately $5,495 annually, and the quarterly newsletter costing approximately $30,000 per year. Discussion included poll worker wages being average at $12/hour but chief inspectors being on the lower end at current rates. Pet licensing fees increased from $10 to $20 due to Marathon County raising fees to municipalities to cover Humane Society costs.

### Farmers Market Update
Staff member Renee reported the farmers market at Kennedy Park has been going well, with full vendor capacity including food trucks, coffee vendors, craft people, and some produce vendors with greenhouses. A committee member who spoke with vendors reported they were very happy with the setup and location. Discussion touched on parking concerns on Alderson and Newport streets, with staff noting east side of Alderson is signed no parking while west side now allows parking.

### Committee Member Resignation
Committee member Evan announced he is stepping down from the committee as he and his wife are moving away from Weston, though staying in the metro area. He thanked the committee for his time serving. The chair noted there are now open spots on this committee and others, asking members to reach out to citizens who may be interested in participating.
