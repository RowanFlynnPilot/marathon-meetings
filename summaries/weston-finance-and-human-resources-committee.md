## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Finance and Human Resources Committee approved previous meeting minutes and acknowledged budget status reports. The meeting featured an extensive educational presentation by Municipal Clerk Pam on clerk office operations, elections, licensing, and tax collection. Committee member Evan announced his resignation as he is moving away from the area.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of minutes from previous meeting
Motion to approve made by Ernling, seconded by Daniels. Passed unanimously with no discussion.

### April budget status report and TIFF reports
Committee reviewed budget status reports. Jessica confirmed that TIFF bond payoff is anticipated for October/November, with bonds due October 1st. The CDA will need to pass a resolution to call the bonds. Jessica confirmed the numbers look good and they will be able to pay off the debt. Motion to acknowledge items 5, 6, and 7 made by Daniels, seconded by Soaper. Motion carried unanimously.

### Municipal Clerk's office educational presentation
Clerk Pam presented comprehensive overview of clerk operations including elections, licensing, tax collection, and pet licensing. She noted the office went from four full-time staff to two-and-a-half, with her and Sarah handling the entire 2024 presidential election alone. Discussion covered election postage costs increasing from $6,000 to $15,000 for presidential election years, chief inspector pay potentially increasing from current rate to $15/hour, and pet licensing fee increase from $10 to $20 due to Marathon County requirements. Pam noted 84% of tax collection ($22 million) is handled at the clerk's office counter. Committee discussed the $30,000 annual cost of the village newsletter 'The Wire' and past attempts to reduce or eliminate mailing.

### Farmers market update
Staff member Renee reported the farmers market has been going well, with her present for 6-7 hours each Saturday. The market is full with food trucks, coffee vendors, craft people, and greenhouse vendors. Produce vendors expected to increase mid to late June. Committee member noted vendors expressed satisfaction with the new setup and location. Some discussion of parking concerns on Alderson and Newport streets, with staff confirming east side of Alderson is signed no parking while west side now allows parking.

### Committee member resignation
Evan announced he is stepping down from the committee as he and his wife are moving away from Weston, though staying in the metro area. He thanked the committee for the opportunity to serve. The chair noted there are now multiple open spots on this and other committees and encouraged outreach to interested citizens.

## Public Comment
No public comment was offered.
