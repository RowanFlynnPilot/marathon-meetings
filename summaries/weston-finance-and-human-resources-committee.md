## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Finance and Human Resources Committee approved previous meeting minutes and acknowledged April budget and financial reports. The meeting featured an extensive educational presentation from Municipal Clerk Pam on the clerk's office operations, elections, licensing, and budget. Committee member Evan Ewert announced his resignation as he is moving out of Weston.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of minutes from previous Finance and HR meeting
Motion to approve minutes was made by Ernling and seconded by Daniels. The motion passed unanimously with no discussion.

### TIF one and TIF two detail reports for April
A committee member asked about the final numbers for TIF closure. Finance Director Jessica explained they are anticipating October/November for bond payoff, with bonds due October 1st. The CDA will need to pass a resolution to call the bonds this summer. Jessica confirmed the village will be able to pay off the debt as planned.

### Acknowledgment of work product transmittals (items 5, 6, and 7)
Motion to acknowledge the April budget status report, TIF detail reports, and legal details report was made by Daniels and seconded by Soaper. The motion passed unanimously.

### Municipal Clerk's Office Educational Presentation
Clerk Pam presented an overview of her department's operations including elections, licensing, tax collection, and pet licensing. She noted the office went from four full-time staff to currently two full-time and one part-time employee. She highlighted that 84% ($22 million) of tax collection is handled through walk-ins at the clerk's office. Discussion included the pet licensing fee increase from $10 to $20 for neutered dogs, which was driven by Marathon County raising fees to cover their $100,000 annual payment to the Humane Society. The committee discussed the cost of the quarterly newsletter 'The Wire' at approximately $30,000 annually for 5,089 postal patrons, with some questioning whether alternatives should be explored.

### Farmers Market Update
Staff member Renee provided an update on the farmers market at Kennedy Park. She reported attending the last three Saturdays for 6-7 hours each. The market has expanded to include coffee vendors, new food trucks, and craft vendors. Vendors expressed satisfaction with the new setup and location. Committee members raised concerns about parking congestion on Alderson and Newport streets, with staff clarifying parking is restricted on the east side of Alderson but allowed on the west side and both sides of Newport.

### Committee Member Resignation
Committee member Evan Ewert announced he is stepping down from the committee as he and his wife are moving away from Weston, though staying in the metro area. This was his last meeting. The chair noted there are now open spots on this and other committees and encouraged members to recruit interested citizens.

## Public Comment
No public comment was offered.
