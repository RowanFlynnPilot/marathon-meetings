## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Finance and Human Resources Committee approved previous meeting minutes and acknowledged April financial reports showing TIF district bonds remain on track for October payoff. The Municipal Clerk presented an extensive budget overview covering elections, licensing, tax collection, and staffing, while committee member Evan Soaper announced his resignation from the committee due to relocation.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of minutes from previous Finance and HR meeting
Motion to approve made by Ernling, seconded by Daniels. Passed unanimously with no discussion.

### TIF one and TIF two detail reports for April
Jessica from finance reported the TIF district bonds are on track for October payoff. The process will require calling the CDA to order for a resolution to call the bonds, with Ehlers writing a letter to US Bank Trust. A committee member asked if final numbers still look good, and Jessica confirmed they will be able to pay off the debt.

### Acknowledgment of work product transmittals (items 5, 6, and 7)
Motion to acknowledge the April budget status report, TIF detail reports, and legal details report made by Daniels, seconded by Soaper. Passed unanimously.

### Municipal Clerk's budget presentation
Clerk Pam presented a comprehensive overview of the clerk's office operations. The office has reduced from four full-time staff to two full-time and one part-time (Leah), with plans to add another part-time position. She detailed election administration costs noting postage increased from $6,000 to $15,000 due to fall presidential election demands. Pet licensing was brought back in-house from Pet Data, saving approximately $5,495 annually. She noted the chief inspector wage of $13/hour is below average and will likely increase to $15/hour for the next two-year term. The quarterly newsletter costs approximately $30,000 annually ($9,000 postage, $20,000 printing), prompting discussion about potentially eliminating printed copies, though previous attempts met resident resistance.

### Pet licensing fee increase
Discussion revealed that neutered dog licensing fees doubled from $10 to $20 at Marathon County's request, as the county was only collecting $65,000 from municipalities while paying $100,000 annually to the Humane Society. A committee member noted the non-dog stray contract cost $175 per animal, including for eight mice picked up last year, prompting discussion about requiring law enforcement to bring in strays rather than allowing residents to do so directly.

### Farmers market update
Renee reported the farmers market at Kennedy Park has been going well over the first three Saturdays, with the market now full. Vendors expressed happiness with the setup and location. Committee members raised concerns about parking on Alderson and Newport streets, with staff clarifying parking is now allowed on the west side of Alderson. Discussion noted general parking congestion in the area during concurrent track meets and baseball games.

### Committee member resignation
Evan Soaper announced he is stepping down from the committee as he and his wife are moving away from Weston, though remaining in the metro area. He thanked the committee for his time serving. The chair noted there are now multiple open spots on this and other committees, encouraging outreach to interested citizens.
