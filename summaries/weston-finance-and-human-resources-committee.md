## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Finance and Human Resources Committee approved previous meeting minutes and acknowledged April financial reports including budget status and TIF district details. The meeting featured an extensive educational presentation from Municipal Clerk Pam on clerk's office operations, elections, licensing, and budget, with committee member Evan announcing his resignation due to relocation.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of minutes from previous Finance and HR meeting
Motion to approve made by Ernling, seconded by Daniels. Motion carried with unanimous voice vote.

### TIF District Reports
Jessica provided an update on TIF district closure timeline. The village anticipates finalizing TIF one in October/November, with bonds due October 1st. The CDA will need to pass a resolution to call the bonds, expected this summer. Jessica confirmed the district will have sufficient funds to pay off the debt. Committee members asked about final numbers, which staff confirmed look good.

### Work product transmittals (items 5, 6, and 7)
Motion to acknowledge the April budget status report, TIF one and TIF two detail reports, and legal details report made by Daniels, seconded by Soaper. Motion carried with unanimous voice vote.

### Municipal Clerk's Office Educational Presentation
Clerk Pam presented comprehensive overview of clerk's office operations, budget, and staffing. Key points included: office reduced from four full-time staff to two full-time and one part-time; 84% of tax collection ($22 million) handled at clerk's office counter; pet licensing brought back in-house saving approximately $5,500 annually; chief election inspector pay likely to increase from $13 to $15/hour in next term; newsletter printing costs approximately $30,000 annually with discussion of potential cost-saving alternatives. Committee discussed pet license fee increase from $10 to $20, which was driven by Marathon County raising fees to municipalities to cover Humane Society costs.

### Farmers Market Update
Staff member Renee reported the farmers market at Kennedy Park has been going well. She has been present for the last three Saturdays starting at 6 AM. The market is full with food trucks, coffee vendors, craft people, and some produce vendors. Produce vendors expected to increase mid to end of June. A trustee reported vendors were very happy with the setup and location. Committee discussed parking concerns on Alderson and Newport streets, with staff clarifying parking is restricted on the east side of Alderson but allowed on the west side.

### Committee Member Resignation
Committee member Evan announced he is stepping down from the committee as he and his wife are moving away from Weston, though remaining in the metro area. He thanked fellow committee members for the experience. The chair noted the committee now has open spots and encouraged members to reach out to citizens interested in participating.

## Public Comment
No public comment was offered.
