## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Finance and Human Resources Committee approved minutes and acknowledged budget reports before receiving an educational presentation on the Municipal Clerk's office operations, including elections, licensing, and tax collection. Committee member Evan announced he is stepping down and moving away from Weston.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of minutes
Motion to approve minutes from the previous Finance and HR meeting was made by Ernling and seconded by Daniels. The motion passed unanimously with no discussion.

### TIFF one and TIFF two detail reports
A committee member asked about the timeline for final bond payoff for TIFF districts. Staff explained they anticipate October-November for completion, with bonds due October 1st. The CDA will need to pass a resolution to call the bonds, likely this summer. Staff confirmed the numbers still look good and they will be able to pay off the debt.

### Acknowledgment of work product transmittals
Items five, six, and seven (April budget status report, TIFF detail reports, and legal details report) were acknowledged together. Motion by Daniels, seconded by Soaper. Motion carried unanimously.

### Municipal Clerk's office presentation
Clerk Pam presented on the clerk's office operations, staffing (two full-time and one part-time employee, down from four full-time plus two assistants previously), and budget. Key topics included election administration with Badger Books (electronic poll books at $11,000 for four units), licensing (51 liquor licenses), tax collection (84% collected at the counter totaling $22 million), and pet licensing brought back in-house saving approximately $5,500 annually. Discussion occurred about pet licensing fee increases from $10 to $20 for neutered dogs due to Marathon County raising fees to cover Humane Society costs. The quarterly newsletter 'The Wire' costs approximately $30,000 annually for printing and postage to 5,089 postal patrons.

### Farmers market update
Staff member Renee reported the farmers market at Kennedy Park has been going well over the past three Saturdays. The market has expanded to include coffee vendors, new food trucks, and craft vendors. Vendors reported being very happy with the setup and location. Committee members raised concerns about parking congestion on Alderson and Newport streets, with staff confirming parking is now allowed on the west side of Alderson only.

### Committee member resignation
Committee member Evan announced he is stepping down from the committee as he and his wife are moving away from Weston, though remaining in the metro area. He thanked the committee for his time serving. The chair noted the committee now has open spots and encouraged members to reach out to citizens interested in participating.

## Public Comment
No public comment was offered.
