## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Finance and Human Resources Committee held a routine meeting that included approval of budget reports and an educational presentation from the Municipal Clerk's office detailing election administration, licensing, and department operations. Committee member Evan Everson announced his resignation as he is moving away from Weston.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of minutes
Motion to approve minutes from previous Finance and HR meeting was made by Ernling and seconded by Daniels. Motion carried with no opposition.

### TIF 1 and TIF 2 reports
Jessica provided an update on TIF bond payoff timeline, indicating they are anticipating October/November for final payoff. The CDA will need to pass a resolution to call the bonds. A committee member asked about final numbers and was assured they will be able to pay off the debt as planned.

### Acknowledgment of work product transmittals
Items 5, 6, and 7 (April budget status report, TIF reports, and legal details report) were acknowledged together. Motion by Daniels, seconded by Soaper. Motion carried.

### Municipal Clerk's office presentation
Clerk Pam presented comprehensive overview of her department's operations including three staff members (herself and Sarah full-time, Leah part-time). She detailed election administration costs, noting postage increased from $6,000 to $15,000 for presidential election year. Discussion included Badger Book replacement strategy ($11,000 for four units), poll worker wages (currently $12/hour for inspectors, below average for chief inspectors at $13/hour). Pet licensing was brought in-house in 2025 after 12 years with Pet Data, collecting $17,480 through March with $8,067 retained by village. The quarterly newsletter costs approximately $30,000 annually for printing and postage to 5,089 postal patrons.

### Pet licensing fee increase
Committee discussed the increase in pet licensing fees from $10 to $20 for neutered animals. Pam explained Marathon County requested the increase because they pay about $100,000 annually to the Humane Society but were only collecting $65,000 from municipalities. Discussion also touched on the non-dog stray contract costing $175 per animal, including eight mice picked up last year.

### Farmers market update
Staff member Renee reported the farmers market at Kennedy Park has been going well with full vendor participation, new food trucks, and expanded offerings. Concerns were raised about parking on Alderson Street and Newport Avenue, with clarification that only the west side of Alderson now allows parking. Committee members noted congestion during concurrent baseball games and track meets.

### Committee member resignation
Evan Everson announced he is stepping down from the committee as he and his wife are moving away from Weston, though staying in the metro area. Committee members thanked him for his service.

## Public Comment
No public comment was offered.
