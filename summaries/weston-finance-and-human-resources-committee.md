## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Finance and Human Resources Committee approved minutes and acknowledged financial reports, then received a detailed educational presentation from the Municipal Clerk's office covering elections, licensing, tax collection, and staffing. Committee member Evan announced his resignation as he is moving out of the village.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of minutes
Motion to approve minutes from the previous Finance and HR Committee meeting was made by Ernling and seconded by Daniels. Motion carried unanimously with no discussion.

### TIF 1 and TIF 2 detail reports
A committee member asked about the final numbers for TIF closeout. Staff explained they are anticipating October/November for bond payoff, noting the CDA will need to pass a resolution to call the bonds which are due October 1st. Staff confirmed the numbers still look good and they will be able to pay off the debt.

### Work product transmittals (items 5, 6, and 7)
Motion to acknowledge the April budget status report, TIF detail reports, and legal details report was made by Daniels and seconded by Soaper. Motion carried unanimously.

### Municipal Clerk's Office presentation
Clerk Pam presented a comprehensive overview of the clerk's office operations, including staffing (now 2 full-time and 1 part-time employee, down from 4 full-time previously), elections administration, licensing, tax collection, and the village newsletter. Key points included: the office handled 84% of tax collection ($22 million) at the counter; they have 51 liquor licenses; pet licensing was brought back in-house saving the $5,495 previously paid to a third-party vendor; the quarterly newsletter costs approximately $30,000 annually in postage and printing. Discussion included the increase in pet licensing fees from $10 to $20 for neutered animals to help cover Marathon County Humane Society costs, and concerns about the cost of the non-dog stray contract including $175 per mouse for eight mice collected.

### Farmers market update
Staff member Renee reported the farmers market at Kennedy Park has been going well over the past three Saturdays, with full vendor capacity including food trucks, coffee vendors, and craft people. Vendors expressed they were very happy with the setup and location. Committee members raised concerns about parking congestion on Alderson and Newport streets, with staff clarifying parking is now allowed on the west side of Alderson while the east side remains no parking.

### Committee member resignation
Committee member Evan announced he is stepping down from the committee as he and his wife are moving away from Weston, though remaining in the metro area. He thanked the committee for the opportunity to serve. The chair noted there are now open spots on this and other committees and encouraged outreach to potential citizen participants.

## Public Comment
No public comment was offered.
