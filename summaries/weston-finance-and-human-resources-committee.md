## Meeting Overview
The Finance and Human Resources Committee approved previous meeting minutes and acknowledged budget reports before receiving an extensive educational presentation from Municipal Clerk Pam on the clerk's office operations, elections, and licensing. Committee member Evan Harling announced his resignation due to relocating outside the village.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of minutes
Motion to approve minutes from the previous Finance and HR meeting was made by Ernling and seconded by Daniels. Motion carried with no discussion.

### Budget and TIF reports acknowledgment
The committee reviewed the April budget status report, TIF 1 and TIF 2 detail reports, and legal details report. Regarding TIF closure, Jessica explained the village is anticipating October/November for final TIF closure, will need CDA resolution to call the bonds due October 1st, and confirmed the village will be able to pay off the debt. Motion to acknowledge items 5, 6, and 7 was made by Daniels, seconded by Soaper. Motion carried.

### Municipal Clerk's Office presentation
Clerk Pam presented extensively on the clerk's office operations, staffing (currently 2 full-time and 1 part-time employee, down from 4 full-time plus 2 assistants previously), elections administration, licensing, tax collection, and pet licensing. She noted the office collected 84% of tax payments ($22 million) at the counter. The committee discussed the recent pet licensing fee increase from $10 to $20 for neutered animals, which was prompted by Marathon County raising fees to municipalities to help cover their $100,000 annual payment to the Humane Society. Committee members questioned the $175 per animal cost for non-dog strays, including eight mice picked up last year. Newsletter costs of approximately $30,000 annually for printing and postage were discussed, with suggestions to explore cost savings through email distribution or coordinating with other municipalities.

### Farmers market update
Staff member Renee reported the Kennedy Park farmers market has been going well after three Saturdays of operation. Tuesdays have been challenging due to cool and windy conditions. The market has expanded to include coffee vendors, new food trucks, and craft vendors, with produce vendors expected to increase mid to late June. Committee member Daniels raised concerns about parking on Alderson Street, with staff clarifying parking is now allowed on the west side only, while Newport has never had parking restrictions.

### Committee member resignation
Evan Harling announced he is stepping down from the committee as he and his wife are moving away from Weston, though remaining in the metro area. Committee members thanked him for his service.

## Public Comment
No public comment was offered.
