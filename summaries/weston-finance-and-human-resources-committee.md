## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Finance and Human Resources Committee approved previous meeting minutes and acknowledged financial reports for April. The meeting featured an extensive educational presentation by Municipal Clerk Pam on the clerk's office operations, elections, licensing, and budget. Committee member Evan Erickson announced his resignation as he is moving out of Weston.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of minutes
Motion to approve minutes from previous Finance and HR meeting was made by Ernling and seconded by Daniels. Motion carried unanimously with no discussion.

### TIF 1 and TIF 2 detail reports
Jessica provided an update on TIF closure timeline, anticipating October-November for final bond payoff. The CDA will need to pass a resolution to call the bonds, which staff hopes to complete this summer. A committee member confirmed the final numbers still look good and the village will be able to pay off the debt.

### Acknowledgment of work product transmittals
Motion to acknowledge items 5, 6, and 7 (April budget status report, TIF reports, and legal details report) was made by Daniels and seconded by Soaper. Motion carried unanimously.

### Municipal Clerk's Office Educational Presentation
Clerk Pam presented extensively on clerk office operations including elections, licensing, tax collection, and the quarterly newsletter. Key points included: the office has reduced from 4 full-time staff to 2 full-time and 1 part-time; they handled 84% of tax collections ($22 million) at the counter; pet licensing was brought back in-house from Pet Data saving costs; and the newsletter costs approximately $30,000 annually for postage and printing. Discussion occurred about potentially increasing chief election inspector pay from $13 to $15/hour to match county averages. Committee members asked about the pet license fee increase from $10 to $20, which Pam explained was due to Marathon County raising fees to cover their Humane Society costs.

### Farmers Market Update
Staff member Renee reported the farmers market has been going well with full vendor spots. She has been present for 6-7 hours on Saturdays starting at 6 AM. Vendors expressed satisfaction with the new setup and location. Discussion addressed parking concerns on Alderson and Newport streets, with staff clarifying that only the west side of Alderson allows parking while Newport has never had parking restrictions.

### Committee Member Resignation
Evan Erickson announced he is stepping down from the committee as he and his wife are moving away from Weston, though staying in the metro area. He thanked committee members for the opportunity to serve. The chair noted there are now open spots on this and other committees and encouraged outreach to interested citizens.

## Public Comment
No public comment was offered.
