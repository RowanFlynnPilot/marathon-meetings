## Meeting Overview
The Village of Weston Finance and Human Resources Committee approved previous meeting minutes and acknowledged budget reports before receiving an extensive educational presentation on the Municipal Clerk's office operations, elections, licensing, and budget. Committee member Evan Arndt announced his resignation as he is moving out of the village.

## Key Discussions
### Approval of minutes from previous meeting
Motion to approve made by Ernling, seconded by Daniels. No discussion. Motion carried unanimously.

### TIFF one and TIFF two detail reports
Jessica from finance reported that TIFF bond payoff is anticipated for October-November. The CDA will need to pass a resolution to call the bonds, expected this summer. Staff confirmed the final numbers look good and the village will be able to pay off the debt. The bonds are due October 1st.

### Work product transmittals (items 5, 6, and 7)
Motion to acknowledge the April budget status report, TIFF reports, and legal details report made by Daniels, seconded by Soaper. Motion carried unanimously.

### Municipal Clerk's Office Educational Presentation
Clerk Pam presented an extensive overview of clerk's office operations. The office has reduced from four full-time staff to two full-time and one part-time employee. Key topics included: elections budget (higher in even years due to presidential/gubernatorial elections), Badger Book electronic poll book replacements at $11,000 for four units, 84% of tax collection ($22 million) handled at the clerk's counter, pet licensing brought back in-house saving $5,495 annually, and the quarterly newsletter 'The Wire' costing approximately $30,000 annually for 5,089 postal patrons. Discussion occurred about poll worker wages, with chief inspectors paid below average ($13/hour vs. $15 average). The pet licensing fee increase from $10 to $20 was explained as driven by Marathon County's request to cover Humane Society costs.

### Farmers Market Update
Staff member Renee reported the farmers market at Kennedy Park has been going well. She has been there the last three Saturdays helping vendors get situated. The market is full with food trucks, coffee vendors, craft people, and greenhouse vendors. Produce vendors expected mid to late June. Concerns were raised about parking on Alderson and Newport streets. Mountain Bay Metro has been monitoring the situation.

### Committee Member Resignation
Committee member Evan Arndt announced he is stepping down from the committee as he and his wife are moving away from Weston, though staying in the metro area. He thanked the committee for his time serving.

## Public Comment
No public comment was offered.
