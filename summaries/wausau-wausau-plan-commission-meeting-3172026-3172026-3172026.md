## Meeting Overview
The Wausau Plan Commission approved two major items: a conditional use permit for a 70-unit, 7-story apartment building at 731 North First Street for Beacon Resources LLC, and a transportation project plat for Grand Avenue signal replacements. A public hearing was held for a proposed personal storage facility at 218 South Fourth Street, though no action was taken on that item during this meeting.

## Key Discussions
### Minutes from February 18th
Motion to approve was made by Bugamman with a second from Balkan. The minutes passed unanimously with all members voting in favor.

### Public hearing on conditional use permit for 218 South Fourth Street storage facility
Jason Dunwy and Melinda Don Woody spoke in favor of allowing a personal storage facility in the light industrial district. They argued that with over 400 new apartment units approved downtown, including the 153-unit Foundry on Third and 102-unit Evergreen Landing, residents need nearby storage options rather than driving to surrounding areas like Kronenwetter. The public hearing was closed but no action was taken on this item.

### Conditional use permit for 731 North First Street apartment building
Bornman made the motion to approve with Bugamin seconding for the 70-unit, 7-story apartment building for Beacon Resources LLC. No questions or discussion were raised by commissioners. The motion passed unanimously.

### Transportation project plat for Grand Avenue signal replacements
Project 370-40-40 for signal replacements at Sturgeon and Townline Road was considered. Bugamin made the motion with Balkan seconding. The motion passed unanimously with no discussion.

## Public Comment
One written public comment was submitted by Linda Lawrence on March 12th supporting the Beacon Resources apartment building proposal, stating housing of this capacity will benefit downtown small businesses and expressing personal willingness to live at that location. Jason Dunwy and Melinda Don Woody provided oral public comment during the storage facility public hearing, advocating for downtown storage options to serve new apartment residents.

## Action Items
