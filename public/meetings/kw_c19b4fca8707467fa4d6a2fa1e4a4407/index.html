Village of Kronenwetter · Village Board

Village Board Meeting

May 11, 2026
RoadsZoningDevelopmentMarathon County Highway Shop Facility

The Kronenwetter Village Board voted unanimously 6-0 to approve a Quit Claim Deed transferring village property interests to Marathon County for a proposed Highway Shop Facility, a decision that drew sharp public criticism from a former trustee who argued the land was being given away before required zoning approvals were in place. The board also approved a model e-bike ordinance and a transportation funding resolution, both 6-0. Following a closed session on staff evaluations and compensation, the board approved staff recommendations 5-0 with Trustee Coyle abstaining.

Discussion

Consent Agenda - Meeting Minutes

The consent agenda, including minutes from the April 27 and April 30, 2026 Village Board meetings, was approved on a motion by Lesniak, seconded by Myszka. The motion carried by voice vote 6-0.

Vouchers, ACH Transactions, and Credit Card Activity Transactions - Month of March 2026

Finance Director John Jacobs presented March 2026 financial transactions totaling $699,821. Jacobs and Administrator Davel answered board questions regarding a bill for a lift station pump out. Trustee Stowell moved, Trustee Myszka seconded, and the motion carried by roll call vote 6-0.

Quit Claim Deed Proposed Marathon County Highway Shop Facility (CLIPP)

Trustee Myszka presented background on the proposal to transfer village property interests to Marathon County via Quit Claim Deed for a proposed Highway Shop Facility. County officials attended and answered board questions about plans for the land. Board members discussed the concept of being a 'good neighbor' to the county, the possibility that the county shop could spur a future interchange on Kowalski Road, and the disposition of a strip of land involved in the transfer. Trustee Coyle moved, Trustee Myszka seconded, and the motion carried by roll call vote 6-0.

Wausau Metropolitan Planning Organization Draft Model E-Bike Ordinance (CLIPP)

Trustee Myszka presented background on the draft model e-bike ordinance developed by the Wausau Metropolitan Planning Organization. Lt. Smart noted the police department had seen no issues with e-bikes to date. Discussion touched on overlapping ordinances in nearby communities, an influx of children riding e-bikes, and potential future safety concerns. Trustee Mortensen moved, Trustee Coyle seconded, and the motion carried by voice vote 6-0.

Resolution 2026-007 Transportation Funding Solution

Administrator Davel presented information on Resolution 2026-007 Transportation Funding Solution. The minutes do not record a named mover or seconder. The motion carried by roll call vote 6-0.

Closed Session - Staff Annual Evaluation and/or Pay Increases

Trustee Lesniak moved, Trustee Myszka seconded, to convene into closed session pursuant to Wis. Stat. 19.85(1)(c) for consideration of employment, promotion, compensation, or performance evaluation data of public employees — specifically staff annual evaluations and/or pay increases. The board entered closed session at 6:38 p.m. and reconvened in open session at 7:32 p.m. on a motion by Myszka, seconded by Coyle, carried 6-0 by roll call vote.

Action After Closed Session

Following the closed session on staff evaluations and compensation, Trustee Myszka moved, Trustee Stowell seconded, to accept the board's recommendations and direct the administrator to act on them. The motion carried by roll call vote 5-0-1, with Trustee Coyle abstaining.
