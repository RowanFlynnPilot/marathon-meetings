Discussion

Approval of minutes from April 27, 2026

A motion was made and seconded to approve the minutes from the previous meeting. The motion carried unanimously with no discussion.

Kennedy Park update

Staff presented a timeline document showing the Kennedy Park master plan process from 2023 to present, including the May 9th ribbon cutting for youth baseball fields. Sean Oster explained that the Friends of Kennedy Park are currently fundraising for lights on the upper field, with approximately $85,000 at the community foundation and a goal of $117,000. Committee member Scott asked clarifying questions about village capital funds, which are currently not earmarked pending fundraising progress. A committee member noted the Friends group plans a public check presentation at their June tournament. Discussion emphasized that master plans are guidance documents subject to change and that the 10-year timeline allows flexibility.

Yellow Banks disc golf course update

Dan presented photos and maps showing tree removal locations along the river and wood mat installation over gas pipelines to protect them during equipment access. The mats are now village property with extras available for other projects. Committee members asked clarifying questions about the 8x8 wood mats and installation process.

Dog Day at the aquatic center

The committee decided to defer this item to the next meeting because Chair Katrina, who requested the item, was absent due to an unforeseen issue. The resident who spoke during public comment against the proposal was informed his objection was noted. No formal motion was required for deferral.

Graffiti on the Eau Claire River Trail

Discussion focused on challenges removing graffiti from concrete and memorial benches on the trail. Staff explained that pressure washer access is limited due to bridge weight restrictions and terrain. Options discussed included anti-vandalism coatings, seal coating, and potentially organizing volunteer workdays. Committee members suggested community groups might help with maintenance. No formal action was taken; staff will explore cleaning options.

Yellow Banks kayak launch signs

Dan presented version 7 of signage for kayak launches along the Eau Claire River. Signs will be installed at three Weston locations (Highway J, Ross Avenue, and Yellow Banks) plus Schofield locations. The Marathon County Parks Foundation is funding the signs through state grants. Dan noted the Highway J launch may be closed all season due to bridge replacement. A motion to approve installation was made and seconded, passing unanimously.

Topics for future meetings

Staff proposed adding food allergy awareness signage at playgrounds after receiving a request from a community member. The committee agreed to bring it back for further discussion. Staff also announced the Mock Miller Park master plan process will begin with a kickoff meeting with Rettler, and noted the June 11th open house for the bike and pedestrian plan.
