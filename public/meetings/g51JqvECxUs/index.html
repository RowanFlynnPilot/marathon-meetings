Minutes from May 11, 2026 Meeting

A commissioner moved to approve the minutes as presented and another seconded. There was no discussion and the motion carried unanimously.

Public Hearing – Conditional Use Permit for ADU at 4804 Shore Avenue (Project 20260237)

Staff presented the application from Chad and Roxan Bucky to construct a 1,090 sq ft detached accessory dwelling unit on their 12.35-acre RR5-zoned property to house a family member (the applicant's mother, recently widowed). The applicant was not present. No public comment was offered. Commissioners discussed emergency access and fire numbering, the ADU's utilities (private well and septic required, its own meter), and whether the unit could be rented in the future; staff confirmed long-term rental would be permitted but short-term rental is prohibited under the draft conditions. The commission worked through the five required conditional use findings, answering 'yes' to each, then approved the CUP with conditions 1 through 7.

Final Plat – Bull Junior View Subdivision (Project 20250424)

Staff reported that the final plat for this 23-lot single-family subdivision by Rudy Builders is in substantial conformance with the preliminary plat approved by the village board in December 2025. The project engineer Dustin was present but no questions were directed to him. There was no notable debate; commissioners moved directly to a vote and the commission unanimously recommended approval to the village board subject to conditions in draft resolution 2026-019.

SIP Amendment No. 2 – Title Wave Auto Spa (Project 20260169)

Staff explained this item was placed on the agenda for acknowledgment only: a minor SIP amendment allowing a small shed had already been administratively approved the prior month and the building permit issued. Staff noted the ordinance allows minor amendments to be handled at the staff level. Commissioners acknowledged the item with a unanimous vote and one member commended staff for being proactive.

May and June 2026 Staff-Approved CSMs and Site Plans

Staff noted several projects are already under construction, including Burger King, and that Fabick Cat's long-pending site plan was finally submitted and approved after delays related to parking lot decisions. Commissioners acknowledged the list and praised the streamlined staff-approval process established in 2015.

Planning and Development Department Monthly Project Update Report

Staff highlighted work on a small office addition for Precision Body and Frame on Post Avenue and noted ongoing monitoring of two problem properties: Auto Select, where an unfinished parking area slab remains and staff has warned the owner his deadline is approaching, and a storage facility where construction stalled after materials were stolen from the site but the owner is expected to complete paving this year per his conditional use permit amendment. Commissioners noted the CUP contains binding deadlines and the owner may need to return to the commission.
