The Village of Weston Community Life and Public Safety Committee approved amendments to the nuisance ordinance, recommended an e-bike ordinance with emoto restrictions, and authorized demolition of an abandoned structure at 4104 Shore Avenue. The committee also discussed overnight parking regulations and a crosswalk request, deferring decisions on both to gather more information.

Discussion

Department Reports

The committee acknowledged reports from Code Enforcement, Fire/EMS, and Police departments. Roman reported that 3020 Mountain View Avenue will be removed from the code enforcement roll after the property sale was completed and the new owner submitted repair permits. Chief Hunt reported the police department had 2,394 calls in March (77 per day), 410 traffic stops, and 281 citations issued. The department has hired its 40th officer. Motion to acknowledge reports passed unanimously.

Chapter 50 Nuisances Amendments

The committee reviewed final amendments to Chapter 50, including previously discussed changes to tree limb height requirements over streets and walkways (14 and 10 feet). Trustee Olsen made a motion to recommend approval to the Board of Trustees, seconded by Campkey. Motion passed unanimously.

E-Bicycle Ordinance Amendments

The committee discussed the proposed e-bike ordinance at length, with Chief Hunt strongly advocating for prohibiting emotos (electric dirt bikes) on roadways, sidewalks, and trails due to safety concerns. Committee member Mike Compkey raised concerns about electric scooters used by children. The committee agreed to add language prohibiting emotos from roadways. Trustee Olsen moved to recommend approval with the emoto prohibition on roadways, sidewalks, trails, and public property. Motion seconded by Chwanka and passed unanimously.

Overnight Parking Regulations

Trustee Olsen presented research showing Weston's 12-hour overnight parking window (4 AM to 4 PM) is significantly longer than surrounding municipalities, which typically have 4-5 hour windows. Michael from DPW suggested eliminating the even-odd system and instead signing 6-8 specific problem areas. Chief Hunt preferred keeping overnight parking restrictions to give officers time to clear vehicles before plowing. The committee deferred action, directing Michael to identify specific exception areas to bring back in July.

Crosswalk Request on Alderson Street

Mount Olive Church requested a crosswalk between their property and DC Everest parking lot. Staff estimated a basic crosswalk would cost about $2,000, while a lighted system would cost $25,000-$30,000. Chief Hunt had no safety concerns. The committee was reluctant to set precedent of paying for requested crosswalks. Trustee Olsen moved that if Mount Olive is willing to cover the installation cost, the committee recommends board approval. Motion seconded by Chwanka and passed unanimously.

Mobile Food Vendors Fire Inspection

Fire Chief presented a regional fire inspection program for food trucks involving Marshfield, Stevens Point, Wisconsin Rapids, Plover, Rib Mountain, and potentially Weston. One inspection would be valid across all participating municipalities. Chief noted a recent inspection found a food truck with unsafe galvanized LP piping and improperly secured propane cylinders. Chwanka moved to approve Ordinance 26-8, seconded by Campkey. Motion passed unanimously.

Address Corrections for E911 Compliance

Staff presented three address corrections needed for E911 compliance: 5915 Delicoski Street (duplex needing two addresses), 3913 Ross Avenue (sequence correction and secondary unit), and 2211 Radkey Avenue (new duplex on private drive). Chief Hunt mentioned an additional addressing issue at Weston Villa Apartments on Delicoski with eight separate addresses for one structure. Motion to recommend approval passed unanimously.

Raze Request for 4104 Shore Avenue

Staff requested authorization to begin raze order proceedings on an abandoned structure at 4104 Shore Avenue, abandoned since approximately 2010-2014. A previous fire department training burn was deemed unsafe due to the structure's condition. The property owners are current on taxes and have agreed to the demolition approach, with costs to be placed on the tax roll. Motion by Olsen, seconded by Campkey, passed unanimously.
