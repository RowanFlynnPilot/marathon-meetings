Discussion

Public Comment

One member of the public, Jim, spoke on three topics: opposition to removing the legal detail from Finance and HR Committee packets, requesting to comment during the Cintas contract discussion given his two decades of experience with uniform contracts, and criticizing the exit interview report for including staff opinions rather than factual employee interview data. The chair allowed Jim to speak during the Cintas agenda item without objection from the committee.

June Budget Status Report and TIF Reports (Items 5-7)

A committee member asked Finance Director Jessica about the aquatic center revenue covering 50.42% of expenditures to date, and Jessica explained season pass sales in June account for a large portion of revenue. She noted the aquatic center receives $40,000 in property tax levy and $40,000 in room tax annually, and that balancing it is a year-to-year struggle. Items 5, 6, and 7 were acknowledged together by motion.

Semi-Annual Staff Transitions (Item 8)

Staff reported three transitions in the past six months. It was noted that a previously full-time deputy clerk position was split into two part-time positions successfully, reducing benefit costs. An engineering tech (RAS) was recently brought on, and two positions — deputy clerk/administrative assistant and utility operator/maintainer — were currently posted for recruitment.

Annual Exit Interview Report (Item 9)

HR staff Jamie presented the report, noting only one exit interview was completed and that for confidentiality reasons no specific recommendations were attributed to that individual. The report included commentary about increasing negativity and unconstructive criticism affecting staff morale, which generated significant discussion. A committee member argued the win of a 7.5% turnover rate — below public sector benchmarks — was being overshadowed by the opinion-laden portion of the report and suggested separating the factual turnover data from the morale discussion, making the latter a standing agenda item. Mark (identified in discussion as a village official present) stated he personally refuses to respond to criticism in outside media and expressed support for staff. A department director spoke about the difficulty staff face being identified in public while wearing village shirts and receiving negative comments. Jamie defended including the morale content as reflective of themes heard more broadly, not just from the one exit interview, noting it could be a leading indicator if turnover worsens. The committee agreed to add a standing item for August on how the committee can better support staff, and one member proposed adding encomiums as a standing agenda item at board meetings. The motion to acknowledge the report passed with one opposed vote.

Cintas Uniform Agreement (Item 10)

Public commenter Jim, who was invited to speak, shared detailed experience with uniform contracts, recommending the village negotiate seasonal garment rates to avoid makeup charges, standardize garment types to reduce costs (noting stretchy jeans cost four times standard pants), and consider an allowance model where employees pay the difference for premium options. DPW Director Michael explained the current contract uses Omnia Partners government pricing locking rates for 36 months, that Cintas would not go below a 36-month term, and that a minimum weekly charge of approximately $45 (50% of current ~$92/week bill) would still be met even if some employees opted out. He noted communication and weekly check-ins with the Cintas driver have improved. The committee discussed the opt-out hybrid model, employee satisfaction, female employees having difficulty finding well-fitting garments, and the January 1 start date for annual selections. The committee recommended approval of the Cintas uniform agreement renewal to the board of trustees.

Employee Personnel Policy Amendments (Item 11)

The committee discussed amendments to Chapter 5 (job classification and compensation, clothing and equipment loans) and Chapter 10 (appearance) of the personnel policies handbook to reflect the new hybrid uniform program — allowing employees to either use Cintas or receive a $500 clothing allowance, compared to the current $300 reimbursement. Staff confirmed this was essentially codifying the agreement approved in Item 10. The committee recommended approval of the policy amendments to the board of trustees.

Audit Proposal — Hawkins Ash (Item 12)

Finance Director Jessica presented a proposal from Hawkins Ash to continue as the village's auditor for the 2026 audit at a cost of $41,105. She noted this is lower than the $43,000–$46,000 proposals received from other firms during the last RFP process (for the 2025 audit), and that retaining Hawkins Ash is beneficial because of their familiarity with the village's TIF districts and existing work papers. The committee recommended approval of the one-year extension to the board of trustees.

2025 Financial Audit Report (Item 13)

Finance Director Jessica presented the final 2025 audit report from Hawkins Ash, noting she had included a memo summarizing the highlights to make the voluminous document more accessible. A committee member appreciated the memo. The committee acknowledged the final audit report without significant discussion.

2027 Budget Process and Tentative Calendar (Item 14)

Finance Director Amy (or Jessica) presented a proposed budget calendar, noting that the September 7 meeting date falls on Labor Day and should be moved to Tuesday, September 8. The committee discussed whether Finance and HR should hold joint meetings with the full board of trustees throughout the budget process or focus narrowly on wages and benefits as in prior years. Two committee members expressed preference for participating in the full budget process. The motion to approve the calendar and hold joint meetings with the board was approved unanimously.

Ethics Function Referral from Board of Trustees (Item 15)

The chair introduced this item, explaining that after raising the concept at the last board of trustees meeting, the board referred the task of developing a potential ethics process to the Finance and HR Committee. The chair noted the current ethics policy (page 226 of the packet) routes violations to a department director or administrator, and that the committee would explore whether to build upon, amend, or replace it. A committee member suggested a citizen-based body similar to the police and fire commission, with the chair agreeing that was a possibility worth considering. The chair noted the committee may grow from four to six members after that evening's board of trustees meeting and wanted to wait for that input before going deeper. Staff were asked to gather sample ethics policies from comparable municipalities for the August meeting discussion.
