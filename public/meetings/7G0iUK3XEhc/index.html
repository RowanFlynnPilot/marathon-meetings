Discussion

Approval of June 9, 2026 minutes

A board member moved to approve the minutes as drafted, seconded by another member. The motion passed unanimously on a voice vote.

2026 East Parking Ramp Project repairs - bid award

Six bids were received and read into the record. The low bid of $303,888 from Masonry Restorations Inc. was presented, with the next lowest being Building Restoration Corporation at $337,186. A board member noted the project budget was $300,000, making the low bid $3,888 over budget but within acceptable range. The board voted unanimously to award the contract to Masonry Restorations Inc. at $303,888.

Pay estimate #28 - Community Infrastructure Partners, lead service line replacement

A board member referenced an email from staff member Eric recommending approval, noting the work had been completed and inspected and that retainage was being withheld for any punch list items. The board voted unanimously to approve pay estimate #28 in the amount of $578,572.35.

2026 Downtown Utility Project - Haas Inc. Change Order #1

Staff member TJ explained the change order resulted from upgrading a water main from 8-inch to 12-inch diameter after the bid, determined to benefit the water system, and a larger sanitary manhole core required because the line was found to be 18-inch rather than the 15-inch shown in historical records. The net increase to the contract is $17,433.12 ($50,791.48 added minus $33,358.36 reduced). A board member confirmed the water department requested the upsizing and that the project is TID funded. The board voted unanimously to approve the change order.

Amendment 1 - Business Campus Trail East-West Connector contract (City of Wausau, WisDOT, MSA Professional Services)

TJ explained that a property owner along the planned trail route made an unreasonable purchase request and the city lacked eminent domain authority, requiring a trail redesign. The amendment totals $15,795.25, of which the city is responsible for only 20% due to 80/20 federal TAP program funding, though the full amount must be paid out before reimbursement. The board voted unanimously to approve Amendment 1.

Portland Cement Concrete License - Utility Traffic and Restoration LLC and B&D Plumbing and Heating Inc.

A board member moved to approve the license for both companies subject to their further personal review, noting they had not fully reviewed the materials before the meeting and had a question on the first applicant. The motion was seconded and passed unanimously.

Bituminous Concrete Paving License - Utility Traffic and Restoration LLC

A board member moved to approve the bituminous concrete paving license for Utility Traffic and Restoration LLC, also subject to their final review. The motion was seconded and passed unanimously.

Closed session - pre-qualification statements for 2026 North Fourth Street Lighting Project

The board voted unanimously to enter closed session pursuant to Wisconsin Statutes 19.85.1E to consider and deliberate on pre-qualification statements for the 2026 North Fourth Street Lighting Project. The motion was seconded and passed on a voice vote.
