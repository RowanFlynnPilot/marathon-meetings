Discussion

Public Comment - Kathy Flurry appointment dispute

Kathy Flurry, 915 North 2nd Avenue, addressed the commission disputing her removal from the Transit Commission by Mayor Doug Diny. She stated she signed an oath of office on October 22, 2025 with a term running through April 30, 2028, and was not informed of her removal until June 4, 2026, after a new community member (Christopher Weems) had already been appointed on June 2, 2026. She requested a written explanation and a copy of term limit policies, and asked for information on how to appeal the mayor's decision. A second speaker, Deborah Ryan of 702 Elm Street, corroborated parts of Flurry's account and alleged the city clerk had overstepped his boundaries in the appointment process. The chair thanked Flurry for her years of service but did not indicate any commission action would be taken.

Consideration of minutes

A motion to accept the minutes of the preceding meetings was made by Weems and seconded by Watson. The motion passed unanimously with no discussion.

WisGo Electronic Fare Contract

Transit Director Aaron (last name not stated) presented the WisGo electronic fare collection system, operated through Milwaukee Transit and used by multiple Wisconsin transit agencies, as a replacement for the current cash-only system. He outlined a one-time startup cost of $133,900 (to be covered by existing grant funds) and an annual fee of $24,260, and noted that fare increases would likely accompany the transition — adult fares from $1.75 to $2.00, senior fares from $0.85 to $1.00, adult monthly passes from $42 to $60, and senior monthly passes from $21 to $40. Commission members Alder Lukens and Alder Watson asked questions about card reloading, app integration, senior fare caps, and school ticket transitions. The director noted a driver was previously hospitalized after touching a fentanyl-laced transfer slip, citing driver safety as an additional benefit of going paperless. Weems moved to send the contract to finance for review; Alder Lukens seconded. The motion passed unanimously.

Wings Over Wausau Bus Barrier

The director requested commission approval to drive idle express route buses to the airport to serve as crowd barriers for the Wings Over Wausau airshow (June 26-27), noting there would be no cost to the transit system and that it would provide advertising exposure. Alder Lukens moved to approve; Weems seconded. The motion passed unanimously. Commission members briefly discussed the possibility of future shuttle services for community events and suggested creating promotional materials or signage to display alongside the buses.

Director's Report

The director reported that CAD/AVL system installation on current fleet buses will begin next month, but the full system will not go live until new buses arrive in October-December, at which point everything will launch simultaneously. Scheduling software is expected to go live sooner and will be used to redesign staffing. A feasibility study for a new transit facility is ongoing, with the current building facing stormwater challenges. The director also announced the hire of a new deputy director starting July 6. Community member Mr. Meany expressed enthusiasm about the changes, noting he is a long-time bus rider.

Future Agenda Items

Commission members proposed several future agenda items including a paratransit service overview for new members (requested by Alder Lukens), a presentation on e-bike compatibility with buses (the director confirmed current bike racks cannot support e-bike weight and there are battery safety concerns), marketing and outreach ideas including a bus presence at the August police and fire cookout (suggested by Mr. Wayne/Weems), and a potential resident fare discount tied to the new card system. The chair noted a council member had expressed interest in building a 'real system' in Wausau.
