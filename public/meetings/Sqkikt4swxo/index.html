Public Health & Safety Committee Meeting Pt.1

June 15, 2026 · 2h
Public SafetyBridge Street Mission

The Wausau Public Health & Safety Committee held its June 15, 2026 meeting covering a range of topics including a presentation on Bridge Street Mission's homeless shelter operations (119 unique guests since April 20 opening), a homelessness response framework from new county public health coordinator Megan Barnett, a detailed presentation on the police department's Flock Safety automated license plate reader program, and a rent abatement hearing for a tenant experiencing chronic window leaks for three years. The committee approved several routine items unanimously and directed staff on future reporting formats for homeless services data.

Discussion

Public Comment

One speaker, Deborah Ryan of 702 Elm Street, identified as a former alder and Westies neighborhood lead, addressed the committee. She raised concerns about gaps in homeless services on Sundays, the pending expiration of the city's ARPA-funded homeless coordinator position at year-end, the need for community surveys on services, and a specific case of a disabled individual receiving only $130/month and no housing assistance since November 2021. She also advocated for propane-powered municipal vehicles over electric given the climate.

Bridge Street Mission Shelter Operations Report

Craig Vincent and Director of Homeless Services Jim Torstensson reported that since opening April 20, the shelter served 119 unique guests (32% women), providing over 3,000 nights of shelter, with only five guests indefinitely dismissed for threatening behaviors. Community navigator staff helped three guests move to permanent housing and nine into recovery programming. Alder True Blood asked about denied service; Vincent confirmed the men's side had reached capacity and some were turned away, with overflow plans for cold weather. The women's recovery program also opened the prior week.

County Homelessness Response Framework - Megan Barnett

Megan Barnett, newly hired Marathon County public health coordinator, presented a foundational framework covering data collection, roles and leadership, strategic planning, and community engagement through year-end, with a goal of presenting a five-year strategic plan at a joint county board and city council meeting in December. She noted no one could quantify how many people are unhoused in the community due to lack of data, which she identified as a key barrier. Alder Lukans welcomed her warmly, saying the committee had 'been hoping for, wanting to get you for a long, long time.' No vote was taken; this was an informational presentation.

Item 2A & 2B: Approval of minutes

Regular minutes were moved by Alder Lukans, seconded by Alder Watson, and approved unanimously. Special meeting minutes were moved by Alder True Blood, seconded by Alder Watson, and approved unanimously. No discussion or corrections were raised.

Item 3A: Various licenses and applications

Alder Watson moved to approve all license applications as noted in the staff memo, with no denials reported. Seconded by Alder Solonsky. Approved unanimously.

Item 3B: True North Energy LLC 90-day liquor license extension

Representatives Michelle Knox and Josh Kasinski appeared on behalf of True North Energy LLC for the former Wagner Shell station at 4611 Stewart Avenue, requesting an extension while the property undergoes renovation. The chair confirmed the target reopening date of September 18. Alder Watson moved to grant the extension to September 18; seconded by Alder True Blood. Approved unanimously.

Items 3C & 3D: Municipal code chapter 1040 bicycles/e-bikes

Alder True Blood noted the ordinance had already been passed by the full city council on May 26 as part of the consent agenda. The chair struck these items from the agenda. A second item was similarly identified as already passed by council following discussion with the city attorney.

Item 3E: City of Wausau Emergency Operations Plan

Emergency Management Director Philip Brent Master presented the revised plan and identified several grammatical corrections needed, plus a substantive change on page 19: succession of authority was updated so that if the mayor is unavailable, the council president (an elected official) would take charge, replacing the previous plan which designated the police chief. Alder Watson moved to approve with the grammatical and content updates included; seconded by Alder Lukans. Approved unanimously.

Item 3F: Fox Valley Fire Department mobile food preparation vehicle inspection MOU

Chief explained the program allows food trucks to be inspected once by any participating department, with a sticker valid for one year, using NFBA national standards. Alder True Blood raised concerns about trucks passing inspection in one jurisdiction and later failing in another, and requested the division chief of prevention provide failure-rate data. Alder Watson suggested food trucks may warrant more frequent inspections than once annually given their mobile nature; Chief noted national NFBA standards address this. Moved by Alder Solonsky, seconded by Alder True Blood. Approved unanimously.

Item 3G: Urban Search and Rescue Response Agreement 2026-2028

Chief described this as the third renewal of the state contract for Task Force One urban search and rescue, covering training reimbursement and deployment costs for two department members trained in confined space, rope rescue, swiftwater rescue, and structural collapse response. Alder Solonsky confirmed training applies to both urban and rural structural collapse. Moved by Alder Solonsky, seconded by Alder Watson. Approved unanimously.

Item 3H: Downtown police staffing discussion

Captain Syler reported that the two downtown officer positions have continued flexing citywide since the shelter moved across the river, with no scheduling changes required. He noted a dramatic decrease in calls for service at Bridge Street Mission from April/May to June, crediting relationship-building with clients and staff. Alder Watson asked about neighborhood reception on the west side; Syler said early concerns from neighbors were addressed through proactive communication and that downtown business owners have been telling officers this spring and summer has been 'a breath of fresh air.' No vote taken; informational discussion only.

Item 4A: Flock Safety ALPR Program - Captain Ben Graham

Captain Ben Graham delivered a detailed presentation on the department's 20-camera automated license plate reader network, costing $50,000/year under a contract running through 2028. He highlighted 35 documented case successes, described privacy safeguards including 30-day data purging, monthly audit reviews, multi-factor authentication, and confirmed the department has opted out of nationwide data sharing and filters out federal immigration, ICE, DHS, FBI, and reproductive care search requests. He acknowledged three Wisconsin officers at other agencies were criminally charged for misuse of similar systems. Alder Watson noted the policy's human-review requirement before any enforcement action. Alder Slonsky pressed on whether partner agencies could submit false reasons for shared hot-list queries; Graham acknowledged the theoretical possibility but noted audit trails provide accountability. Alder True Blood, referencing other Wisconsin municipalities cutting ties with Flock, expressed appreciation for the presentation and guardrails. The committee requested Captain Graham return annually in January with an audit summary report; Graham agreed. No vote taken.

Item 4B: Tavern activities report and community outreach specialist report - May 2025

Captain Pauls reported all tavern businesses passed checks in May, with a couple of calls initiated by bartenders as hoped. Alder Watson asked whether Bridge Street Mission data could be incorporated into future monthly reports; Community Outreach Specialist Tracy confirmed it could. The chair indicated next month's agenda would include a discussion on future reporting format, potentially alternating monthly reports between Tracy and Bridge Street Mission, and possibly moving to quarterly reporting as suggested by Alder True Blood. Both reports placed on file.

Item 5: Rent Abatement Hearing - 1418 North First Street Apt. 105

Tenant Viviani de Gomez testified under oath that persistent window leaks have plagued her three-bedroom apartment since move-in in July 2023, requiring her to move furniture before every rain event, with water intrusion reaching outlets and causing mold on the ceiling. She pays $945/month and is current on rent. Property manager Joe Seagull of Paramark Real Estate testified multiple contractors had been engaged (Rip Mountain Glass, Ace Handyman, Quality Roofing, AA Seamless, Prestige Roofing), claimed repairs were attempted, and said he offered the tenant a no-transfer-fee move to an identical unit twice, which the tenant declined. City Inspector Adam Molzan testified he first inspected July 24, 2025 at the tenant's request, issued a municipal citation on August 13 for failure to properly repair leaking windows, and confirmed during a visit on June 10, 2026 water was still actively dripping inside the apartment with apparent mold in two ceiling locations. Molzan noted he received no repair documentation from the landlord. The transcript ends before the committee voted; the chair indicated the committee would adjourn to closed session to deliberate.
