Administrative Policy Committee Meeting

June 15, 2026

The Kronenwetter Village Administrative Policy Committee met on June 15, 2026, taking several key votes including recommending Associated Appraisal of Appleton for a $355,000 reassessment contract and approving budget amendments for a DPW truck purchase and a new special parks committee. The meeting also included a review of the village's 2024 audit results and a five-year financial comparison presented by Finance Director John, who highlighted improving general fund trends while noting ongoing concerns about the TID1 fund deficit and accounting software configuration issues.

Discussion

Assessment and Reevaluation Services Proposals (Item 5F)

Finance Director John presented three proposals received for reassessment and assessment services for July 2026 through December 2031: Accurate Appraisal at $240,900, Associated Appraisal (current vendor since 2017) at $350,000 for Option 2, and Municipal Group at $372,000 for an exterior evaluation option. John recommended Associated Appraisal Option 2 at $355,000, citing their 67 years of experience, 240 municipalities served, and satisfactory performance at Board of Review, and noted that if the village does nothing the state would intervene at roughly double the cost. He indicated there were unstated reasons the village switched from Accurate Appraisal in 2017. President Jolyne motioned to accept Associated Appraisal Option 2 for $355,000, seconded by Trustee Solheim; approved 3-0 as a recommendation to the Village Board.

2024 Audit Review (Item 4D)

Finance Director John summarized the 2024 audit, noting the village received an unmodified (clean) opinion, the best possible result, though auditors identified material weaknesses and significant deficiencies. John expressed frustration that auditors presented only the negative findings without discussing the financial numbers, and that a predecessor had been carrying an unreconciled ~$80,000 monthly adjustment that was rolled forward repeatedly. He also described discovering that the Counsil accounting software had been configured on a cash basis rather than accrual, causing mismatches between posted payments and actual bank disbursements; he and staff member Kim identified this issue and are working to correct it. No vote was taken; this was a discussion and informational item.

2024 Comparison Stats for Past Five Audits (Item 4E)

John presented a five-year financial snapshot to provide context the auditors omitted, showing the general fund balance at 16.44% for 2024 versus a 15% policy minimum, recovering from a low of roughly 3% in 2023. He highlighted $18 million in cash and investments (largely unspent borrowed funds), a declining debt service fund balance projected to reach ~$30,000 by end of 2026 due to prior under-levying, and a growing TID1 fund deficit advancing from the general fund now totaling ~$2.7 million, which John said would be resolved once the anticipated distribution center development generates property tax increment. No vote was taken; committee members expressed appreciation for the presentation.

Solis Sachs Cortette Musician Contract - National Night Out (Item 5G)

The committee considered a $750 contract with Solis Sachs Cortette for a patriotic musical performance at National Night Out on August 4, 2026, to celebrate the US 250th anniversary, an idea previously raised by Trustee Sarnson. Finance Director Jennifer was not present but relayed that the cost would be covered by sponsorships with no cost to the village. Trustee Solheim made the motion and President Jolyne seconded; approved 3-0 as a recommendation to the Village Board.

Budget Amendment 1 - DPW Truck Purchase (Item 5H)

Village Administrator Jim explained that a DPW truck suffered a transmission failure and was already slated for replacement next year; replacing the transmission at over $10,000 was deemed impractical. The board had previously approved the purchase; this item addressed how to fund it. John proposed moving $35,000 from the capital projects fund to the capital equipment replacement fund, with the net purchase cost after trade-in being $49,559, offset partly by savings from police squad car purchases. President Jolyne motioned and Trustee Solheim seconded the recommendation to concur with the finance director's budget adjustments; approved 3-0 by roll call vote.

Budget Amendment 2 - Special Parks Committee Funding (Item 5H continued)

Following the board's June 8, 2026 creation of a new special parks committee with five citizen members paid per meeting, John proposed a net-neutral budget realignment: reducing budgeted APC meetings from 18 to 13 and zoning board of appeals meetings from 4 to 2, redirecting those funds to cover up to five meetings for the five citizen members for the remainder of 2026 at $10 per attendee per meeting. Trustee Solheim motioned and President Jolyne seconded the recommendation; approved 3-0 by roll call vote as a recommendation to the Village Board.
