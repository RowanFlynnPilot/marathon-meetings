Approval of April Meeting Minutes

A motion was made and seconded to approve the minutes from the April meeting. The motion carried unanimously with no opposition or discussion.

UC Duties and Responsibilities - Village Administrator Presentation

Village Administrator Mark addressed the committee, emphasizing the need for better communication between committees and the village board on priorities, noting the village struggles with limited funding and lacks a clear unified vision. He highlighted the need for system standardization, particularly regarding pump inventory, noting the village had no spare pump for Lift Station 4. He also announced the recent departure of the community development director and previewed upcoming development proposals including one-acre lot subdivisions with private septics and townhome/zero lot line retirement-style communities, including a developer who paid $1.8 million for the old airfield. Committee member Jim Buck raised concerns about stormwater drainage, calling it a major unresolved issue and noting the lack of any enforceable drainage plan dating back to when sewer and water were installed in 1996.

Utility Director Report - Flooding Emergency and Emergency Pump Purchase

Utility Director Greg reported that recent flooding pushed daily system flow from a normal average of 345,000 gallons to a peak of 1.1 million gallons, primarily through Lift Station 4 which receives flow from Lift Stations 8 and 11. Country Pumpers was contracted for 10 days at a cost of $29,000, deploying two trucks simultaneously. Greg also reported that Village Administrator Jim authorized an emergency purchase of a replacement pump for Lift Station 4 at approximately $8,600-$8,700 to guard against pump failure under extreme strain, noting it arrived or was expected to arrive that day. The committee acknowledged this cost was within the administrator's approval authority. Greg noted Red Marble Metro billing will likely be one and a half to two times higher than projected due to elevated flows, and Finance Director John will present a financial update next month. Greg reiterated his ongoing push to purchase a vac truck and build a garage, noting the RFP for the garage design-build contract is due back May 20th.

Ductless Mini-Split System Purchase for Well Houses

Trustee Alex Fedvik had requested additional information at the prior meeting; Greg provided a vendor sheet and confirmed WPS approved the service connection. The system was budgeted in the adopted November budget. A motion was made to approve the contract to purchase and install the ductless heat pump for Well Numbers 1 and 2 at a cost not to exceed $9,560, with Melbourne as the contractor. The motion was seconded and passed unanimously.

Lift Station 8 Construction Progress Update

Greg reported the contractor is currently dewatering and preparing to dig the lift station pit, with pipe already run along Corona Motor Drive and some remaining work under the interstate near Kwasky. Completion is targeted for July. A significant concern was raised: the contractor cut power to Lift Station 8, requiring the village to station one of its two mobile generators there, which Greg said could be tied up for a month. Greg stated he told the contractor to obtain a rental generator from United Rental by end of week or the village would rent one and charge it back. Greg also challenged the engineering firm RPS on delays attributed to WPS, questioning when they actually contacted WPS given the contract was signed over a year ago.

Glacier Meadows Development - One-Acre Lots with Private Septics

In response to a question from committee member Jim Buck, Greg clarified that the Glacier Meadows developer informally asked whether the village would be open to one-acre lots with individual private septic systems rather than connecting to the municipal sewer system. No formal proposal or decision was made. Greg confirmed DNR involvement and percolation testing would be required before any approval, and the committee expressed openness to exploring the idea if testing supports it. Greg noted activity was observed at the site with a backhoe and trucks that day.
