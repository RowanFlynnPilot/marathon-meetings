Art Department Presentation - Three-Year Goals and Curriculum Alignment

The district's art coordinator, in her second year, presented the art department's three-year goals focused on amplifying arts reach, creating shared K-12 skill experiences, and meaningful vertical alignment between grade levels. She described the creation of an art continuum task force involving elementary, middle, and high school educators who identified priority standards, and a department-wide book study on teaching meaning in art making. Board member Lance Trupop expressed support, noting the value of consistent experiences as students move into middle school. The coordinator also announced an upcoming partnership with the Leigh Yawkey Woodson Art Museum to give art educators opportunities to make art themselves.

Consent Agenda - Personnel, Minutes, Bills, and Donations

The consent agenda included personnel actions, minutes from the May 11 and May 18, 2026 sessions, payment of bills, budget status, investment report, and donations from multiple community organizations including CW Fly Bags, Hub Central Collision, King Buffet of Wausau, Lee Insurance, Peace United Church of Christ, Safe Kids of Marathon County Coalition, Wausau Breakfast Optimist Club, ENT and Allergy Associates, and Cloverbeltt Credit Union. A motion was made, seconded, and approved unanimously with no discussion.

Rearview HVAC and Roofing Project - Myron Construction Corporation

Ryan Armansky, director of buildings and grounds, had presented this project at the May committee meeting. The board approved moving forward with Myron Construction Corporation as the general contractor unanimously with no discussion.

2026-27 Debt Defeasance Strategy Presentation and Adoption

Eric Cass from PMA Financial presented the annual debt defeasance strategy, explaining that the district would place excess levy funds into an irrevocable escrow trust targeting the longest-maturity, highest-coupon debt from a 2022 issuance, eliminating nearly three years of debt service through 2039-2040 and generating approximately $9.6 million in savings. Combined with prior years' defeasances dating back to 2017-18, total long-term interest savings would reach just over $29 million. Board member Nick Casher asked whether repeatedly prepaying debt affects the district's standing with bond investors; Cass explained that the callable feature is disclosed upfront and investors price in the possibility of early redemption. Another board member noted the strategy also helps smooth mill rate fluctuations and generates additional state equalization aid, projected at $5.7 million, though Cass cautioned that figure carries volatility. The motion was approved unanimously.

Phelps described the morning elementary program, structured as 'transition to' grade-level programs focusing on math, ELA, and encore rotations, and a middle school transition program combining school forest visits with academic intervention.
