The Wausau School Board's Committee of the Whole met on May 18, 2026, and advanced several items to the full board, including a preliminary 2026-27 budget, significant employee handbook changes affecting new hires starting July 1, 2026, updated meal prices, a revised athletics and activities code, and approval to move forward with Miron Construction for the Riverview Elementary HVAC and roofing project estimated at $5.6 million. The meeting also featured a brief Excellence in Action presentation from Thomas Jefferson Elementary before the committee adjourned into closed session for preliminary discussion of potential litigation.

Discussion

Approval of minutes and audit of bills

Both the minutes of the April 27, 2026 meeting and the audit of bills were approved by voice vote with no discussion or opposition noted.

Preliminary 2026-2027 Budget

Assistant Superintendent of Operations Ms. Channel presented the preliminary budget, noting it is based on January assumptions including a 2.63% wage increase for some employee groups, a 10% health insurance premium increase, and a 1.5% reduction to district building budgets. Board members asked about health insurance utilization trends nationally and whether the 10% assumption was still appropriate; Channel said she would follow up with M3 on current trend data. One board member, identified as Corey, asked what buildings were actually cutting under the 1.5% reduction, and the board requested that information be reported back. The motion to recommend the preliminary budget to the full board passed with one abstention.

Excellence in Action - Thomas Jefferson Elementary

Principal Megan Korak presented highlights from Thomas Jefferson Elementary, which was formed through consolidation of Grant and Jefferson schools. She noted both buildings received high impact scores from the state, that 37% of students with Personal Success Plans (PSPs) had been exited and 52% of second graders had exited PSPs, and that four special education students moved to 504 plans. She also shared community-building events and noted the school adopted two mascots, TJ and Oreo. A board member noted the high impact award had been discussed at both CESA 9 and CESA 8 meetings.

Employee Handbook Updates for 2026-27

HR staff presented three substantive changes applying only to new hires on or after July 1, 2026: (1) a consolidated vacation schedule replacing multiple differing schedules; (2) holiday alignment so employees working fewer than 230 days per year receive six paid holidays rather than nine, while those at 230 or more days retain ten; and (3) health and dental insurance standardized at 85% employer contribution for all employee groups, with eligibility threshold raised from 20 to 30 hours per week. Current employees are unaffected and will receive individualized letters with an option to voluntarily switch to the new benefit package. Two additional housekeeping changes addressed teacher retirement language dating to 2005 and a shift for new administrator hires to a defined contribution retirement benefit. The motion to recommend the handbook to the full board passed unanimously.

Proposed Changes to 2026-27 Paid Meal Prices

Nutrition Services Director Kieran Foos explained the district is required to complete the USDA paid lunch equity tool annually. To meet the required $3.19 weighted average pricing, the proposed change is a 10-cent increase for elementary, middle, and high school lunch prices and a 5-cent increase for breakfast. The motion to recommend the meal price changes to the full board passed unanimously with no significant debate.

Athletic and Activities Code Revisions

Dr. John Tomsky and B.J. Brant presented four to five changes including: clarifying the definition of full-time student by referencing board policies 5200 and 9720; updating habitual truancy language to align with Marathon County Truancy Court procedures; adding look-alike prohibitions for tobacco and drug products in addition to the existing alcohol look-alike clause. A board member raised questions about enforceability of the look-alike provision in off-campus situations, and Tomsky explained that ambiguous cases go to the activities council for interpretation. The board also discussed that middle school athletics now operate under fund 80, which allows participation by private, home school, and other non-public school students, prompting the policy 9270 update. The motion to recommend the revised activities code passed unanimously.

Policy 9270 and Policy 2431.01 Updates

Both policy changes were presented as companion updates to the athletic code revisions already discussed. Policy 9270 was amended to clarify that the WIAA participation restriction does not apply to middle school athletics funded through fund 80. Policy 2431.01 was updated to align official board policy language with the revised activities code document. Both motions to recommend approval to the full board passed unanimously without additional discussion.

Riverview Elementary HVAC and Roofing Project - Miron Construction

Buildings and Grounds Director Hermanski presented a recommendation to select Miron Construction as general contractor for the pre-construction phase (75% to 100% plans) of the Riverview Elementary HVAC and roofing project at a pre-construction fee of $5,000. Miron's total estimated project cost is $5.6 million, including $5.2 million in direct costs, a 2.25% construction fee of approximately $118,000, a 4% inflation clause, and bonding costs. Board members questioned the inflation clause and whether the 2.25% fee would increase if project costs rise; Hermanski confirmed the fee percentage is fixed but the dollar amount could increase with direct costs. The motion to recommend approval of Miron Construction as the general contractor passed unanimously.

Closed Session - Potential Litigation

Immediately following adjournment of the regular meeting, the board convened a special meeting and voted by roll call to enter closed session pursuant to Wisconsin Statute 19.85(1)(g) for preliminary discussion regarding potential litigation. All seven members present voted yes: Sarah, Charles, John, Pat, Corey, Lance, and Jim.
