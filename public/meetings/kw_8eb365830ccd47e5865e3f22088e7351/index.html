Police and Fire Commission Meeting

June 22, 2026
Public SafetyStaffingCitizen Complaint ProcessFire Department HiringElectric Scooters

The Village of Kronenwetter Police and Fire Commission voted 4-0 to delay action on revisions to its Citizen Complaint/Charge Form, pending incorporation of additional changes discussed at the meeting. The commission also reviewed but took no action on a proposed new selection process for hiring fire department members. A community member raised concerns about electric scooters on Village streets during public comment.

Discussion

Approval of 04/27/2026 and 06/01/2026 meeting minutes

Vice President Rick Smith moved, seconded by Commissioner Chet Rucinski, to approve the minutes of both prior meetings as submitted. The motion carried 3-0 by voice vote, with Commissioner Lucene Udulutch abstaining.

Selection Process for Hiring Fire Department Members

Chief O'Brien presented a proposal to have a fire department chief or fire officer meet with new applicants before their formal interview with the Police and Fire Commission. Commissioners discussed the concept but took no action.

Revision to Commission's Citizen Complaint/Charge Form

Commissioners reviewed attorney-proposed changes to the Citizen Complaint/Charge Form and discussed additional modifications they wished to incorporate. President Vedvik moved, seconded by Vice President Smith, to delay action pending incorporation of the changes discussed. The motion carried 4-0 by voice vote.

Action items

  • Staff to incorporate commissioner-discussed changes into the Citizen Complaint/Charge Form before the commission takes final action on the revised document.
  • No action taken on fire department hiring selection process; discussion to continue at a future meeting.
