Public Hearing: Alderson Street and Jelnick Avenue special assessment for driveway approach construction

Village Engineer Michael presented the project overview for the planned compact roundabout replacing the four-way stop at Alderson Street and Jelnick Avenue, with a total estimated project cost of approximately $1.08 million ($900K construction, $180K engineering). Only two private properties are subject to special assessment for driveway approaches, with an assessment rate of $8.61 per square foot — Legacy LLC at $1,747.83 for 203 sq ft and McDonald at $1,549.80 for 180 sq ft. Committee members engaged in extended discussion about the large cost discrepancy between the $8.61/sq ft rate on this project versus $5.40/sq ft on the Bladel Avenue project, attributed to the smaller number of driveway approaches (3 vs. approximately 40) and different contractors (Hos Construction vs. Melvin). The committee consensus, led by Chair Hushang, was to continue using actual bid prices rather than a standardized rate, as setting a fixed rate could shift costs unfairly to village taxpayers. No property owners attended the public hearing. The chair closed the hearing with no further public comment.

Item 10 - CIP Update

Engineer Michael provided a detailed update. On Weston Avenue, pavement settlement in sanitary sewer trenches from fall construction is being repaired — gravel patches have been placed and concrete curb and gutter repour was expected later in the week, with paving to follow. Committee member Hushang attributed the settlement to poor compaction by the contractor, which Michael partially attributed to the 20-foot depth and blasted rock conditions during mid-October construction. The Alderson/Jelnick intersection closed at 7 a.m. the morning of the meeting, with Hos Construction targeting mid-August completion and an incentive in place for early finish. Detours are in place via Park Ridge Avenue or Alterie/Newport. Bladel Avenue project is underway with tree clearing and temporary water setup. Concord and Bayberry project bids are opening June 17. Well 4 GAC tanks have been pumping since October; Well 3 iron/manganese treatment plant addition slab pour was scheduled for the following day. Bellwood Avenue water main project had a bid opening the following Wednesday. Hinter Springs subdivision had concrete deficiencies identified in a walk-through with Denyion that must be corrected before letter of credit sign-off.

Item 11 - Street operations update

Street operations supervisor reported on spring activities including street sweeping, curbside spring cleanup (requiring five staff across leaf bag and brush pickup operations), in-house crack sealing, fixing broken water stacks in the annexed Rothschild area, top soiling and hydro seeding at Yellow Banks, and milling and repaving around Sandy Lane. Shop repairs covered multiple units including bearings and seals on Unit 404, gearbox and driveshaft on Unit 1913, and carburetor replacement on Unit 22. Michael noted the wide range of funding sources — storm water utility, refuse/recycling fund, general fund, water and sewer funds, and parks — reflected in a single month's street crew work.

Item 12 - Utility operations update

Utility Manager Josh reported that Well 2 Carrie Well rehab is nearly complete, awaiting a new pump with zinc sleeves and epoxy coating to improve longevity. Unidirectional water main flushing began in May in the northeast portion of the village. A sewer lateral was bored through by what is believed to be Charter Communications, and discussions are ongoing. Messelix lift station cleaning was completed with crew starting at 4 a.m. on the previous Thursday to reduce flows. An INI (inflow and infiltration) study with InPipe is expected to be scheduled by end of June, with the goal of identifying sources of high wet-weather flows — total wastewater flows dropped from 53.8 million gallons in April to 37.5 million in May. GIS integration with IT Pipes televising data is nearing completion.

Item 13 - Resolution 2026-016: 2025 Compliance Maintenance Annual Report (CMAR) for Weston Sewer Utility

Josh noted the village typically earns top marks on the self-evaluation report due to no overflows or backups. A motion was made and seconded to adopt the resolution. During discussion, a typographical error was identified in the fourth 'whereas' clause — the word 'no' was missing, reading incorrectly as 'there are immediate critical problems.' A committee member moved to amend the resolution to correct the text to 'there are no immediate critical problems.' The amendment was seconded and approved unanimously. The original motion as amended then passed unanimously on a voice vote.

Item 14 - Resolution 2026-017: Bladel Avenue reconstruction special assessment authorizing resolution

Michael confirmed no changes from the May 28 public hearing report. The assessment rate for concrete driveway approaches is $5.40 per square foot. A motion was made and seconded to recommend the village board approve Resolution 2026-017. The motion passed unanimously on a voice vote.

Item 15 - Resolution 2026-018: Alderson Street and Jelnick Avenue intersection project special assessment authorizing resolution

The assessment rate of $8.61 per square foot, as discussed in the earlier public hearing, was affirmed with no recommended changes. Chair Hushang stated 'we should go with what is cost — it is what it is.' A motion was made and seconded to recommend the village board approve Resolution 2026-018. The motion passed unanimously on a voice vote.

Item 16 - Topsoil screening quotes

Two quotes were received: PGA Inc. at $5.50/cubic yard (total $38,500 for estimated 7,000 cubic yards) and Warden Enterprises at $6.50/cubic yard. Michael noted the village saves approximately $100,000 or more annually by screening and reusing its own topsoil versus purchasing contractor-supplied material at approximately $20/yard. The village has an estimated 25,000 cubic yards of unscreened topsoil stockpiled. A motion was made and seconded to award the project to PGA Inc. The motion passed unanimously on a voice vote.

Item 17 - Shirley Avenue paving quote

Two quotes were received for 3-inch asphalt paving: RC Pavers at $48,500 and American Asphalt at $56,465, against a budget estimate of $52,855. The overall 2026 surface maintenance budget remains under budget at $842,654 versus an initial estimate of $863,190, leaving approximately $57,000 contingency. Finance Director Troutman advised pausing additional spending to monitor fuel costs. In response to a question about quality, Michael stated RC Pavers has done good work in the village, citing a Kalen Avenue overlay from about 12 years ago that has far exceeded its expected lifespan. A motion was made and seconded to award the project to RC Pavers at $48,500. The motion passed unanimously on a voice vote.

Item 18 - MSA right-of-way services contract amendment number one for Alderson/Jelnick project

The original contract with MSA for right-of-way services was $28,425 based on three appraisals and three property acquisitions. During final design, additional property impacts were identified east of the roundabout, including a condo association with four separate property owners requiring individual title work and mortgage company approvals. The amendment adds $24,425, bringing the total contract to $52,850. Michael noted that completing the acquisition within a four-to-five month window — compared to over a year for the River Trail Estates condo acquisition for the Ryan Street lift station project — was a significant achievement. A motion was made and seconded to recommend village board approval. The motion passed unanimously, with one member noting they would abstain [noted after the vote].

Item 19 - Change order number one for Alderson Street and Jelnick Avenue intersection project

Michael explained that the bid documents inadvertently omitted a bid line item for 24-inch storm pipe that was shown on the plans, only including 12-inch pipe pricing. The change order adds $17,535 to correct this omission. Michael confirmed that even with this addition, the low bidder (Hos Construction) remains the lowest bidder by approximately $80,000 compared to the second-lowest bidder, and the total remains within the storm water utility budget. Unit pricing of $120–$130 per linear foot for 24-inch concrete pipe was validated against City of Wausau bid tabs. Hushang quipped the committee should tell the contractor 'we don't give him any more change orders.' A motion was made and seconded to recommend the board of trustees approve the change order increasing project cost by $17,535. The motion passed unanimously on a voice vote.

Items 20-21 - Future agenda topics: water main replacement and village lighting

Michael noted approximately 10 miles of water main replacement remain, even with ongoing projects, and the village is pursuing CDBG grant funding. On village intersection lighting, the committee discussed whether to proactively identify intersections needing lights. Hushang said he would take nighttime photos of intersections to evaluate. The consensus was that staff should act when complaints are received rather than proactively adding lights, as some residents prefer dark skies. The only recent complaint resulted in a light being added at Weston Avenue and County Road J. Michael will add intersection lighting back to the July agenda for further discussion, and Scoffield Avenue lighting was specifically mentioned as a continuation item.
